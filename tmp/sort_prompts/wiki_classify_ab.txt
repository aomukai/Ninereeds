Ninereeds is a small language model trained on short Q&A pairs. The wiki gives words context — connecting them to related concepts so the model can anchor meaning. This is not deep knowledge; it is basic grounding. Think: what would a bright child need to know to understand this word in context?

WIKI LEVELS:
L1 = 5 sentences. Simple "what is X?" answer. Almost any describable concept qualifies.
L2 = 10-15 sentences across several "how/why" questions. Worth it when the concept has real internal structure or common confusions to address.
L3 = multi-paragraph article. Only for concepts central to reasoning, emotion, or learning.
L4 = richest treatment. Currently only: emotions, evidence_and_justification, perspective_taking.
SKIP = junk, non-concept, already trivially obvious, or cannot be described in 5 honest sentences.
