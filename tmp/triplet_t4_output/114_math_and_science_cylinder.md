[Ninereeds]First, Owen sat at the kitchen table with a soup can, a piece of string, and a ruler. He wrapped the string around the can and marked where it met with a pencil. When he laid the string flat, he measured the length from the mark to the end. "This is the distance around the circle," he said to his mother.

"If I multiply this length by the can's height, then I will know the area of the curved side," Owen said. He wrote the numbers in his notebook and checked his work twice. He also traced a circle from the can's rim onto a sheet of paper.

After that, Owen added the area of the two circles and the rectangle together. He showed his mother that the total was the full surface area of the cylinder. "Every cylinder has two flat ends and one curved side," he said.

Finally, Owen checked the angle where the side of the can met each end. The seam formed a straight line at ninety degrees, and he saw that this angle kept the can strong. His mother nodded and said, "That is why the soup does not leak."
