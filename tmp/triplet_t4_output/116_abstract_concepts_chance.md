[Ninereeds]First, Will walked into the garden with the gardener. The gardener held a small bag of seeds in his hand. "If we plant these seeds in the good soil, then they will have a real chance to grow," he said. Will crouched down and touched the soft dirt by the fence.

The rich compost pile was the best spot in the whole garden. Will learned that compost gave the seeds a real opportunity to push out strong roots. It was far more likely for a seed to sprout in the warm compost than in the hard packed path. Will scooped a handful of the dark soil and smiled.

After that, Will watered the compost bed every morning before school. The warm sun and the daily water helped each tiny seed wake up and reach for the light. He spotted the first green sprout on the fifth day. "Look, it worked!" Will called out to the gardener.

Finally, Will understood that chance was not magic or pure luck. Every sprout in the garden had the same basic chance to grow. The ones in the best soil got the best opportunity, and that made all the difference.
