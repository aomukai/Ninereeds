After that, Will steps back and studies both trees together. He sees a strong similarity even though their leaves look so different. Both trees draw water from the same soil and stretch their branches toward the warm sun. Will understands that contrast does not mean one tree is better than the other.

Finally, Will touches a broad leaf and then a thin needle. The opposite shapes of the leaves teach a useful lesson.
