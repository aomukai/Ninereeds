[Ninereeds]First, Luke stood in his classroom looking at the atom poster on the wall. The drawing showed a cluster of protons and neutrons in the center. Tiny dots labeled as electrons circled around them. Luke raised his hand and asked, "What keeps the electrons moving around the nucleus?"

His teacher explained that the positive charge of the protons pulls on the negative charge of the electrons. "The opposite charges attract each other," she said, "so the electrons stay in constant motion." Luke nodded because he understood how attraction creates the orbit of each electron.

After that, Luke built a model of an atom at his desk with wires and beads. He remembered that if an atom has six protons, then it will always be carbon. Each element has its own unique number of protons, and that number defines the element.

Finally, Luke understood that every substance in the world is made from tiny atoms. Different elements have different properties because their atoms have different numbers of particles. He felt amazed that something so small creates so many different substances.
