[Ninereeds]First, the artist sits at the table by the window. Morning light fills the room and falls across the blank canvas. Nora knocks and steps in with a cup of coffee. "If you wait for the perfect idea," she says, "then you will never start." She sets the cup beside the paint tray and smiles.

The artist nods because Nora is often right. She picks up a brush and mixes color on a plate. She makes the first stroke, and the canvas is no longer blank. The work begins to teach her what the painting wants to become.

After that, the artist keeps working because the shapes begin to feel true. The creation grows with each layer of light and shadow. Nora watches from the chair as the painting takes form. The room feels full of quiet energy and inspiration.

Finally, the artist steps back and looks at the finished piece. She feels a steady sense of purpose and does not stop until the painting is done. If she had waited for a perfect idea, then nothing would have appeared on the canvas at all.
