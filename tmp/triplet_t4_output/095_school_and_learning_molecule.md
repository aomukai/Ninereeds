"If the atom is the smallest part of an element, how does a molecule form?" Jack asks. The teacher says that atoms from different elements bond together to make a molecule.

Jack writes the teacher's answer in his notebook. He thinks about how each element has its own properties and behaves in a specific way. The bonds that hold atoms together are not random, Jack sees. They follow the rules of chemistry.

After the board lesson, the teacher shows molecule models on the table. Jack watches how each model has a different shape depending on its elements and atoms. He sees that if the elements change, then the structure of the molecule will change too.

Finally, Jack looks at his notes and thinks about chemistry at its smallest scale. He sees that the water he drinks, the air he breathes, and the desk he touches are all made of atoms bonded into molecules. The lesson helps Jack understand the world around him.
