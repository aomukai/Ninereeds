Max thinks about this opposite set of facts. The pencil feels straight in his hand but looks bent through the glass. "If the light travels slower in water, then my eyes will follow a bent path," he says. He understands the concept of a paradox — a single true thing that appears as two opposite things at once.

After that, Max fills a bowl with oil and lowers the pencil in. The bend looks different because oil bends light more than water does. Different materials create different effects on the same object.

Finally, Max smiles. He has learned that careful thinking helps him see past the surface of things. He knows that a real paradox is not a trick but a chance to learn something new about the world.
