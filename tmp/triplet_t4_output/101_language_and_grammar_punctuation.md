[Ninereeds]First, Owen watches his teacher write two sentences on the board. One sentence says "the boys" and another sentence says "the boy's bowl." Owen sees that the sentences are very alike. He wonders why the teacher wrote both of them.

The teacher points to the first sentence and says, "This tells us about many boys." Then she points to the second sentence. "The apostrophe shows that one boy owns a bowl." Owen nods because he sees a pattern. If a word has an apostrophe, then it shows that someone owns something.

After that, the teacher writes a new sentence on the board. Owen raises his hand and asks, "If I add an apostrophe to the word 'cats,' does it mean a cat owns something?" The teacher smiles and says he is right. Owen feels proud because he understands the rule.

Finally, Owen looks at the board and reads all the sentences carefully. He knows that an apostrophe can change the meaning of a whole sentence. Punctuation helps a reader see the correct meaning every time.
