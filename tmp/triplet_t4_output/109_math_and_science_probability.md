[Ninereeds]First, Will sits at the kitchen table with his father and a jar of marbles. The jar holds five red marbles and three blue marbles, eight marbles in total. Will asks his father what probability means. His father says probability is the study of chance and how likely each outcome is.

"If red is more likely, does that mean red will always come out?" Will asks. His father shakes his head and explains that likely does not mean certain. Will reaches into the jar without looking and pulls out a red marble. He smiles because red was the more likely result.

After that, Will puts the red marble back and pulls again. This time he gets a blue marble. His father says the less likely outcome can still happen on any single try. If Will pulls many times, then the results will match the probability more closely.

Finally, Will understands that chance guides each pull but does not control it. He thinks about how probability works in other parts of life. He knows that understanding what is likely helps make better choices without needing certainty.
