[Ninereeds]First, Emma stayed after science class to ask more questions about dimension. She held the sphere in her cupped hands and turned it in the light. "If the sphere has a closed surface," she asked, "then how can anything touch infinity?" Her teacher answered by drawing a small circle on the board and letting the chalk line keep going past the edge of the frame.

Emma watched the line stretch across the board and beyond. The teacher said, "A sphere has a bounded surface, but dimension includes the space that goes beyond what we can measure." Emma touched the sphere again and thought about how its curved shape enclosed a small pocket of space inside a much bigger world. She liked that such a simple object made her think of such a big idea.

After that, Emma walked to the window and looked at the open sky. She pictured the chalk line continuing past the building and into the clouds. If she could follow that line, she thought, then it would go forward without ever stopping. The thought made her feel both small and curious at the same time.

Finally, Emma turned back to her desk. She understood that dimension was the way people measured solid things like the sphere. But infinity was different because it had no edges and no end. If the sphere could explain itself, she thought, then it would say that being finite inside a world of infinity made it special.
