[Ninereeds]First, Luke woke early because the morning sunlight streamed through his window. He remembered the gardener's permission from the day before and felt a rush of excitement. If he picked the best vegetables, then he would have good things to sell at the market. He pulled on his boots and hurried outside to the garden.

The ripe tomatoes hung heavy on the vines, and Luke filled his basket with care. "These are the best I have seen this season," the gardener said as Luke worked. Luke thanked him and packed the carrots carefully because he wanted every customer to receive good produce.

After that, Luke carried the heavy basket to the market and arranged each item on his table. He knew that if he offered fair prices, then people would trust his goods. The bright colors caught the eye of a woman, who stopped to inspect his vegetables. She bought three tomatoes and a handful of carrots, and her acceptance of his price made Luke smile.

Finally, another customer asked about the green beans, and Luke offered a fair price for those as well. By midday he had sold almost everything because people appreciated the freshness of his vegetables. Luke counted what he had earned and understood that opportunity was not just about permission but about the trust that acceptance built.
