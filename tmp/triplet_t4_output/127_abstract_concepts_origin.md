[Ninereeds]First, Owen watches his father measure flour into a large bowl before the sun rises. "If the water is too warm, then the yeast will not work," his father says. Owen sees the steam rise from the measuring cup and thinks about the origin of each loaf. His father adds the flour and water together and mixes the dough with his hands.

The creation of bread depends on the date on the paper label his father stamps each morning. Owen touches the soft dough and asks, "Does the yeast change with the seasons?" His father nods and points to the old book on the shelf. The recipe came from his teacher and has passed through the family for years.

After the dough rises on the counter, Owen helps his father shape it into loaves. He presses his fingers into the puffy dough and sees the small air bubbles inside. "If we bake it now, then the crust will be thin," his father says. Owen understands that careful timing is part of the origin of good bread.

Finally, the loaves come out of the oven with a golden crust. Owen breathes in the warm smell and thinks about the chain of steps that made this creation possible. He knows how the date on the label and the temperature of the water all matter. Every warm slice of bread carries its origin forward from the teacher's hands to his father's and now to Owen.
