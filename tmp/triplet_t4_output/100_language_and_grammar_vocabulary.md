[Ninereeds]First, Nora sat at her desk and watched her teacher hold up a picture of an apple. Nora knew the red fruit very well because she had eaten one before. She raised her hand and said the right word for the picture. The teacher wrote the word on the board for the whole class to see.

"Why does that word stand for the apple?" Nora asked. The teacher explained that the word was a label for the concept she already understood. Nora felt happy because she realized that understanding the idea first made learning the written name much easier. If she already knew the concept of an apple, then she could learn the word for it more quickly.

After that, the teacher showed more pictures to the class. Each new picture helped Nora build more knowledge about the world around her. She learned the names of a bird, a tree, and a house in the same way. Her knowledge continued to grow with every single lesson.

Finally, Nora looked back at all the words she had learned that day. She understood that vocabulary was the tool she used to describe everything she knew. If she kept learning new words, then her ability to share her thoughts would get stronger and stronger. Nora smiled and felt proud of her growing vocabulary.
