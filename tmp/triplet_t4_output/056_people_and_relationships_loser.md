Jack walked to the starting line with a racing heart.

The starting signal sounded and the children began to run. Jack's legs moved fast but he fell behind the other runners. Some children pointed at him and called out, "look at the loser go so slow." A friend called from the side of the track, "keep going Jack, you can do it."

After that, Jack kept his eyes on the finish line and did not stop. Each step brought burning pain to his lungs but his determination pushed his legs forward. He remembered all his practice and knew he could finish the race. Jack crossed the finish line last and fell to his knees, breathing hard.

Finally, Jack felt deep satisfaction because he finished the race. His parents cheered from the side and said, "we are so proud of you, Jack." The feeling of pushing through the pain was better than winning. Jack knew that trying so hard was what mattered most.
