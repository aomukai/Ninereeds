"If the cuts are not straight, then the box will not stay square," he told himself. He picked up his pencil and marked every board before setting the saw blade.

The sawdust piled up as he worked through each piece of wood. His helper watched from the doorway and asked, "Is every part ready for the production run today?" Will nodded and pointed to the neat stack of boards on the bench. He lined up the edges and checked that each piece matched the drawing.

After the glue dried, he clamped the walls together and wiped the extra glue from the seams. If the joints held tight, then the box would be strong enough for years of use. He let the clamps stay in place while he cleaned his tools and swept the dust from the floor.

Finally, he sanded every surface until the wood felt smooth under his fingers. He rubbed oil into the grain and watched the color deepen across the box. Will set his finished product on the shelf and stepped back. It stood sturdy and ready for its new home.
