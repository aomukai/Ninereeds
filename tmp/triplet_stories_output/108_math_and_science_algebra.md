"I will subtract the same number from both sides," Jack said. The teacher smiled and said, "That is correct, Jack. The inverse operation keeps the equation equal because both sides stay balanced." Jack picked up his pencil and wrote each step on paper.

Jack subtracted the number and saw the answer appear on his paper. He checked his work by putting the answer back into the original equation. The two sides matched, so his solution was correct. Jack felt proud because he had solved the algebra problem on his own.
