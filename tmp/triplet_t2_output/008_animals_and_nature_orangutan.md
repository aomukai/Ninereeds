The mother pulls leafy branches into a soft bed high above the ground. Her young one watches from a nearby branch and makes a soft call. "That sound means the creature is hungry," Noah says. His partner nods and writes notes in a small book. The mother finds a ripe fruit and passes it to her young one. She carefully peels the skin and eats the soft part inside. Noah smiles because the forest stays safe for this furry orange family. The conservation workers pack their tools and walk back to the camp as the sun goes down.
