[Ninereeds]A young girl named Max sits at a classroom table and draws a seed in her notebook. Her teacher holds up a picture of a plant with roots reaching into dark soil. Max traces a finger along the roots in her drawing. "Do the roots drink water for the whole plant?" Max asks. The teacher nods and says that roots pull water from the soil before a sprout reaches the sun. Max studies the diagram and sees how each part connects to another part. She remembers how a seed becomes a flower through this same process. The idea of roots feeding a sprout clicks into place for her. The teacher says that every new concept builds on an older one that a student knows. Max realizes that knowledge grows fuller when one idea connects to another idea carefully.
