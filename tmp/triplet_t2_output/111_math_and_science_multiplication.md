[Ninereeds]A teacher brings a tray of eggs to the front of Max's class. The tray has three rows with four eggs in each row. Max looks at the tray and thinks about the eggs. His teacher says, "Max, how many eggs do you see on this tray?" Max counts and says, "I see twelve eggs." His teacher smiles and asks how he got that number. Max explains that he used multiplication: three groups of four makes twelve. The teacher nods and shows the class that division is the opposite of multiplication. She slides the eggs apart to split the twelve into three equal groups of four. Then she picks up one group and says subtraction takes those eggs away. Max understands that both operations make amounts smaller. Subtraction removes a group, while division splits the whole into equal parts.
