[Ninereeds]First, Ella places a small seed in a pot of soil and gives it some water. She sets the pot by the window where the sun reaches it each day. "How long will the seed rest before it changes?" she asks. Her mother says, "If you give it patient care, then it will show you a sign of growth."

After a week of waiting, Ella checks the pot and sees the soil has stayed the same. She pays close attention to the pot but nothing is visible above the surface. Development takes time, and improvement is not always something the eye can catch right away. Beneath the soil the seed is opening and sending a root down for water.

After that, a small green sprout breaks through the surface and reaches toward the sun. Ella proves her patient care by watering the plant and moving it to catch the light. If the plant gets enough water and sun, then it will grow and open new leaves. This visible change feels like proof of improvement to Ella.

Finally the stem stands tall and a bright bloom opens at the top. The flower has reached its full size and shows the reward of Ella's patient care. Development transforms a single seed into a living thing that stands above the soil. Growth and improvement take time, but the result is worth the wait.
