[Ninereeds]Emma stayed after science class to ask her teacher more questions about dimension. She held the sphere in her hands because she wanted to understand how something finite could exist inside something infinite. The teacher explained that a sphere has three measurable dimensions, but the space around it stretches without limit.

"Does every dimension work the same way?" Emma asked. The teacher shook her head and said, "A line has only one dimension, so its infinity is just a straight reach. A sphere traps space inside its surface, but the space outside keeps going." Emma thought about the small sphere and the big idea of infinity together.

She looked out the window and tried to picture a line stretching forever across the sky. The idea made her feel small because infinity was so hard to grasp. Emma decided she liked learning about dimension best when it made her think about things she could not see.
