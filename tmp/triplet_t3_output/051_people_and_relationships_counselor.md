[Ninereeds]A school counselor met with Toby on a Monday morning because he needed extra support. Toby sat in the small chair and looked at the floor. His grief felt as heavy as a stone inside his chest. The counselor watched him with calm patience. She knew he needed time before he could speak.

"I still think about her kitchen every day," Toby said in a quiet voice. The counselor nodded and said, "What was your favorite part?" Toby described the warm smell and the flour on the counter because those details made him smile. The counselor listened very carefully so Toby felt safe sharing his feelings.

The counselor offered gentle guidance near the end of their time. She showed Toby how to write down his favorite memory and keep it in a small box. Toby chose the memory of his grandmother's smile during their last baking day. The grief did not disappear, but it grew a little lighter because someone helped him carry it.
