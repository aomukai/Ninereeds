[Ninereeds]Luke the ecologist stood beside a stream in the forest. Water flowed downstream between mossy stones and past the roots of an oak tree. The roots held the soil in place because they grew deep into the bank. The bank stayed firm, so the water remained clear. Every part of the forest was part of one ecosystem.

"Rain brings clean water to this stream," Luke said. "The water flows past the tree roots, and the roots hold the soil." He recorded the connections in his notebook because each one mattered for conservation. The frog and the bird and the tree all depended on one another because the ecosystem connected them all.

Conservation meant protecting the balance of the whole system. A healthy forest needed every creature and every plant to keep the water clean and the soil rich. Luke smiled and walked through the peaceful woods.
