[Ninereeds]Max fills a tall glass with water and lowers a pencil into it. He watches the straight pencil appear to bend at the water line. Max pulls it out and sees that it is still straight. He puts it back and the bend returns. Max wonders how one object can look like two different things at the same time.

"Mom, why does the pencil look like it bends?" Max asks. His mother explains that light travels slower in water because water is denser than air. The light bends when it crosses the surface, so the pencil image appears at a different angle. "Your eyes follow the bent light," she says, "because the light changes speed when it enters the water."

Max thinks about this opposite set of facts. The pencil feels straight in his hand but looks bent through the glass. He understands the concept of a paradox now — a single true thing that appears as two opposite things at once. Max learns that careful thinking helps him see past the surface of things.
