[Ninereeds]Jack came to the field early on race day and tied his shoes because his stomach felt tight from nerves. He had practiced running every morning for weeks so his legs would move faster. The other children did not know about the hours he spent running on the track. Jack walked to the starting line with a dry mouth and a racing heart.

The starting signal sounded and the children began to run. Jack's legs moved fast but he fell behind the other runners. Some children pointed at him and called out "look at the loser go so slow." A friend called from the side of the track, "keep going Jack, you can do it."

Jack kept his eyes on the finish line and he did not stop because he remembered all his practice. Each step brought burning pain to his lungs but his determination pushed his legs forward. He crossed the finish line last but he felt deep satisfaction because he finished the race. His parents cheered from the side and Jack knew that trying so hard was what mattered most.
