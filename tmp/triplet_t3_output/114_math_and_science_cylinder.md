[Ninereeds]Owen sat at the kitchen table and traced his finger around the rim of a soup can. He wanted to figure out the surface area of the curved side, so he wrapped a piece of string around the can and marked where it met. He cut the string and laid it flat beside a ruler to measure its length.

"The curved side is a rectangle when you flatten it out," Owen said. "You multiply the length of the string by the height of the can to get the surface area." His mother smiled because she liked how he explained the math. "The angle between the curved side and each flat end is ninety degrees, so the seams stay tight and the soup does not leak," he added.

Owen set the can on its side and gave it a gentle push. The cylinder rolled straight across the table because every point on the curved surface was the same distance from the center. He caught it before it fell and set it upright again.
