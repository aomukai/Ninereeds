[Ninereeds]Owen watches his father measure flour into a large bowl each morning. "Why does the bread come out the same every time?" Owen asks. His father says the origin of the bread is in the careful steps, because each ingredient and action builds on the last. He mixes the flour, water, and yeast together, so the dough can begin to form.

"The recipe came from my old teacher's book, so the creation follows the same process," his father says. Owen touches the soft dough on the counter. "Does the date matter?" he asks. His father nods and points to the paper label he stamps each morning, because the yeast and flour change with the seasons.

Owen watches the dough rise in the warm kitchen and begins to understand the chain of cause and effect. The creation of each loaf depends on the date of baking and the steps passed down through generations. Every warm loaf with its golden crust carries that origin forward, from the teacher's hands to his father's and now to Owen's own understanding.
