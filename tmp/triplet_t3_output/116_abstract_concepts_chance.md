[Ninereeds]Toby watched the gardener scatter seeds across the garden. Some seeds landed on soft soil by the fence, so they had a better chance to grow. The hard dirt near the path made growth much less likely because the packed ground blocked the tiny roots.

"I think this seed will grow," Toby said, pointing at the compost bed. The gardener nodded and explained that the rich compost was a real opportunity for strong roots. Toby watered that spot carefully because water helped the seed settle and wake up.

Each morning Toby checked the bed for small green sprouts. The warm sun and daily watering helped every seed push toward the light. Toby learned that chance was not pure luck but a mixture of good soil, careful work, and the right conditions.
