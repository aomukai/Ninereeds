[Ninereeds]Emma lived with her family in a small town in the nation. Because the town sat near the river, the fields stayed green and healthy every season. Emma's father grew wheat and corn on their farm, so the family always had enough food.

"Your wheat is the best in the district," said the baker one morning at the market. Emma smiled because she knew the baker would use it for fresh bread. The baker's shop was full of people from across the nation who came to trade goods.

The district connected many towns through roads and bridges, so goods could travel from farm to city. Emma helped her father load the cart each week because she liked to see the busy market. She felt proud that her small town supplied food for so many people in the nation.
