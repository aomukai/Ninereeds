[Ninereeds]Cody came to the carpenter's workshop because he wanted to see the origin of the chair. The carpenter lifted a rough block of oak onto the bench. The oak had come from a tree that took many seasons to grow. Cody traced the grain with his finger and studied the rings from each year.

"Why does the grain curve this way?" Cody asked. The carpenter pointed to the side of the block and said that the design followed the way the tree grew, so the curves were shaped by sunlight and rain. They measured the wood together and cut the legs on the saw.

They worked the wood with a plane and sandpaper until the surface felt smooth. Oil brought out the warm color of the grain. The finished chair stood beside the window because the carpenter wanted Cody to see it in the sunlight. Cody touched the seat and armrests and understood that the beauty of the chair came from the wood itself.
