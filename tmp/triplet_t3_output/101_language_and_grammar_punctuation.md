[Ninereeds]Owen sits in his classroom and watches his teacher write a sentence on the board. The sentence has the word "boy" in it, so Owen reads it carefully. The teacher points to the word and asks a question. Owen thinks about the meaning of the sentence.

"The word 'boy' can signal one boy or many boys," the teacher says. "Does this sentence mean one boy or many boys?" Owen raises his hand. "It means many boys because the word has an s at the end," he answers. The teacher nods and adds an apostrophe to the board. The class watches because they want to learn the difference.

Owen sees that the apostrophe changes the sentence. The new sentence says "the boy's bowl" and shows that one boy owns a bowl. The apostrophe makes a big difference because it changes the meaning of the whole sentence. Owen understands that punctuation helps a reader see the correct meaning.
