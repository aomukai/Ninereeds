[Ninereeds]Nora sat at her desk in the bright classroom. Her teacher held up a picture of an apple. Nora already understood the concept of an apple because she had eaten one before. So she raised her hand and said the right word. The teacher wrote the word on the board.

"Why does that word mean the red fruit?" Nora asked. The teacher explained that the word was a label for the concept Nora already knew. Because Nora understood the apple first, learning the written word came easily.

The teacher showed more pictures after that. With each lesson, Nora built more knowledge about the world. She felt proud because her vocabulary continued to grow every single day.
