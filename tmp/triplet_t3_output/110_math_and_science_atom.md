[Ninereeds]Luke stood in his classroom looking at a poster of an atom on the wall. The drawing showed a cluster of protons and neutrons in the center with small dots labeled as electrons circling around them. Luke understood that the number of protons in each atom defines which element it is because every element has a unique number of protons. Carbon has six protons, so it is always carbon no matter what.

"What keeps the electrons moving around the nucleus?" Luke asked his teacher. She explained that the positive charge of the protons pulls on the negative charge of the electrons, so they stay in constant motion. Luke understood because the attraction between opposite charges creates the orbit of each electron.

This knowledge helped Luke see why every element behaves in its own way. A gold atom and an iron atom have different properties because they have different numbers of protons and electrons. Everything in the world is made from these tiny particles, and Luke felt amazed that something so small creates so many different substances.
