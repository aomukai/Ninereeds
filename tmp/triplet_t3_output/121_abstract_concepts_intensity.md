[Ninereeds]Jack stands beside the stove and watches the covered pot. The flame grows hotter beneath it because he turned the knob higher. Steam gathers against the lid as the pressure builds inside. Jack shakes his head and leans closer to listen. The water is not ready yet so he waits.

"Why does it take so long?" Jack asks. The cook smiles and points at the small bubbles near the rim. "Every part needs the same heat, so the escalation must be steady because a sudden force will break the pot," the cook says. Jack settles his attention on the rising steam.

The bubbles come faster and the lid begins to shake. The cook reaches over and lowers the flame just a bit. Jack watches the intensity level off into a calm full boil. He understands that patience makes the heat work better.
