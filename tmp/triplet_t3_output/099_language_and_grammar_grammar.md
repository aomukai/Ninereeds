[Ninereeds]Noah sits at his desk in a bright classroom and looks at the board. The teacher writes a sentence with no punctuation at all. Noah squints because the words all run together without any stops or pauses. He cannot tell where one idea ends and the next begins. The teacher asks the class to read the sentence aloud.

"This sentence needs punctuation marks to make sense," Noah says. "Does it need a comma after the first part?" The teacher nods and adds a comma to the sentence. Then the teacher adds a period at the end. Now the words are clear because the punctuation shows where to pause and where to stop.

Noah understands why punctuation matters so much for good grammar. He writes his own sentence on paper with a comma and a period. The teacher reads his work and smiles because every mark is in the right place. Noah feels proud that his writing is clear and correct.
