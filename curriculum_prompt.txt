Write 4 short stories for a very young child about [concept].

Each story must have exactly 6 sentences.

[concept] = River - flowing water, banks, stones

---

General Rules (apply to ALL stories):
- Use only simple sentences.
- Use the exact word "[concept]" in every sentence. Do not use pronouns.
- In Stories 1–3, only "[concept]" may perform actions.
- In Story 4, simple external agents (e.g. "the girl", "the boy") may act on "[concept]".
- Other objects may appear only as places, locations, or things acted upon. They must not act.
- Use literal, concrete language only. No metaphors, no imagination.
- Each story must follow this structure:
  1. "This is a [concept]."
  2–4. Facts, properties, or actions (according to the story's focus)
  5. One movement or state change
  6. Category statement: "A [concept] is ..."

---

Story 1 — Properties:
- Sentences 2–4 must describe visible, physical properties only (colour, shape, size, material, weight).
- Sentence 5 must be a minimal physical action that reveals a property (e.g. "The cup is cold to touch.").
- Do not describe location, movement, or function.

Story 2 — Location and Movement:
- Sentences 2–3 establish where the [concept] is.
- Sentences 3–4 describe the [concept] in or near a specific place.
- Sentence 5 must show clear movement from one place to another.
- Do not focus on properties or function.

Story 3 — State Change:
- Sentences 2–3 describe the [concept] in its initial state.
- Sentence 4 sets up the change.
- Sentence 5 must show a clear, visible change (breaks, opens, gets wet, falls, fills, empties, etc.).
- Show a clear before → change → after pattern.
- Do not focus on properties or function.

Story 4 — Function:
- Sentences 2–3 briefly establish what the [concept] does or what it is used for.
- Sentences 4–5 must show a simple, concrete interaction with "[concept]" (e.g. holding, using, eating, wearing). Show it fulfilling its purpose.
- The category statement may use "A [concept] is for ..." instead of "A [concept] is a ...".
- Do not focus on properties or location.

---

Important:
- Each story must clearly follow its assigned focus.
- The four stories must be obviously different from each other.
- Do not repeat the same type of information across stories.

---

Formatting:
- Separate each story with a blank line.
- Output only the stories. No titles, no labels, no explanations.

---

Examples of the four focuses using "cup" (sentences 2–4 only, to show the contrast):

Story 1 — Properties:
  The cup is round. The cup is white. The cup is made of clay.

Story 2 — Location and Movement:
  The cup is on the table. The cup is near the window. The cup is on the shelf.

Story 3 — State Change:
  The cup is full. The cup falls. The cup breaks on the floor.

Story 4 — Function:
  The girl holds the cup. The girl drinks from the cup. The cup holds the water.
