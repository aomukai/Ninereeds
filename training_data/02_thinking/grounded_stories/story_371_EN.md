Gran led them across the field. The grass changed underfoot—long and soft near the oak, then shorter, springier, as the ground began to slope.

Emma put her hand down. The grass was cool and thick, like a brush.

Taro ran ahead. "It goes up and up!"

Biscuit ran past him, then stopped, then ran again, nose low.

Gran walked steadily, not fast. Emma stayed close. The hill got steeper. Her legs began to feel it.

At the top, the wind hit her face. It was stronger than she expected. She blinked.

Taro was already looking down. "Look! The cottage!"

Emma looked.

Gran's cottage sat far below. The roof was grey. The chimney was a thin line. The garden was a green patch beside it.

It looked very small. Small enough to fit in her palm.

She said nothing. She just looked.

The meadow path was a pale line. The oak was a dark spot at the field's edge. Beyond that, the village sat in the valley, roofs and trees and a thin thread of smoke from somewhere.

Biscuit had run ahead again. He stopped at a gate at the very top—wooden, grey, with a bar across it. He stood on his hind legs and put his front paws on the wood, looking through.

Taro went to see. "What's on the other side?"

Gran came up beside them. "Another valley," she said. "Another farm."

She did not open the gate.

The wind pulled at Emma's hair. She held the gatepost with one hand and looked down at the cottage again. It was still small. The hill was high.
