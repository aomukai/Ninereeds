The grass on the hill was short and dry. Emma stood still near the top. Biscuit sat beside her, ears up.

Far below, Owen’s sheep were moving. They came down the slope in a line. One behind another behind another. Their feet made no sound on the grass. A sheepdog ran along the side of the line, quick and low, then stopped.

Emma watched. The sheep did not hurry. They walked with their heads down, following the path the dog gave them. The sun was low and the light was golden. The grass on the hill caught the colour.

At the edge of the field, below the hill, the old oak stood. It was dark against the golden grass. The trunk was black. The branches spread wide. Emma looked at it, then back at the sheep.

They were still coming down.

Biscuit stood up. He did not bark. He watched the sheepdog move, and he watched the sheep move, and he did not move himself.

Owen was at the gate at the bottom of the hill. He lifted the latch. The first sheep went through. Then the next.

Emma stayed at the top until the last sheep passed through the gate. Then she turned. The oak was dark. The grass was still golden.
