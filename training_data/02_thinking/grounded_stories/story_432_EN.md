The flame was small. It flickered and smoked.

Gran lifted the lamp and looked at it.

“The oil is low,” she said. She set the lamp on the kitchen table.

She took a tin can from the shelf. Taro watched. She unscrewed the top of the lamp. There was a small metal cap over the wick. She lifted it.

“Watch,” she said.

She poured the oil from the can into the lamp. The oil rose slowly inside the glass. She stopped when it was near the top. She screwed the cap back on.

Then she took a pair of small scissors. She trimmed the wick. The black, burnt tip fell away.

Gran struck a match. She touched it to the wick.

The new flame was tall and bright. It did not flicker. It stood still and steady.

“Better,” Gran said.

She handed the lamp to Taro. He held it by the metal ring at the top. The light was warm on his hand.

He carried it to the door. He opened the heavy door that was set into the ground. He started down the stone steps. The light went before him, into the dark.
