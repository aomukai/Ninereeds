The rain had stopped, but the puddle was still there.

It lay in a dip in the meadow path, wide and shallow. The sky sat in it, grey and quiet.

Emma knelt beside it. The surface was smooth. Still.

Then a drop fell from the edge of a leaf above. It hit the puddle and a circle spread out — a perfect ring that grew and grew until it touched the edge and disappeared.

Emma watched. Another drop fell. Another circle. It met the first one where it had been. Where they touched, the pattern changed. The rings bent. They made a new shape.

"Look," she said.

Taro came to look. He held a leaf. He let it fall.

The leaf landed in the middle of the puddle. The ripples lifted it and carried it to the side.

"One ring," said Emma, "two rings, three rings—"

They overlapped. They crossed. The whole puddle was moving now, covered in circles that broke each other.

Bello stepped in.

One paw. Then the other. The surface shattered. The rings vanished. The sky broke into pieces and wobbled.

Bello looked down at the water, surprised.

Emma laughed. "You broke the pattern," she said.

The puddle was still again. The sky came back. A new drop fell.
