Gran lifted the rope of onions from the beam. The onions hung in a long braid, their skins papery and brown. She touched each one as it passed through her fingers.

The first few were firm. She pressed gently with her thumb. They went back on the beam.

The next one was soft. Gran did not say anything. She just pulled it loose and dropped it into her apron pocket.

Taro watched from the bottom of the steps. "Why do you check them?"

"Because one soft onion can spoil the row," Gran said, and reached for the next.

Emma held the lamp higher. The light moved across the onions, across Gran's hands, across the jars on the shelf behind her. The cellar smelled of earth and stored things and the sharp smell of onion skin.

Gran touched another. Firm. Back on the rope.

Another. Soft. Into the pocket.

"How many are soft?" Taro asked.

"Three so far." Gran did not look up.

The lamp flickered. The shadows on the wall moved. Emma watched Gran's hands go down the rope, one onion at a time, firm ones kept, soft ones gone.
