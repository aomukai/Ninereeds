The mill was quiet. The big wheel was still.

Mei stood by the wall. She put her hand on a long metal bar.

“This is the lever,” she said.

Emma looked at it. The bar was grey and thick. It pointed up.

Mei pushed it down.

A sound came from under the floor. A rumble. Then water moved — fast and brown — through a channel. It hit the wheel.

The wheel turned.

Slowly at first. Then faster. Water splashed. The wheel groaned. The floor shook a little under Emma’s feet.

“Down opens it,” Mei said. “The water comes. The wheel goes.”

She pulled the lever up.

The sound changed. The water slowed. The channel emptied. The wheel creaked and stopped.

Everything was quiet again.

Emma touched the lever. It was cold and still.

“A small push,” she said. “And something heavy moves.”

Mei nodded. “The water is heavy. The wheel is heavy. But the lever is long. It makes the force go where you want.”

Emma pushed the lever down.

The water came. The wheel turned.
