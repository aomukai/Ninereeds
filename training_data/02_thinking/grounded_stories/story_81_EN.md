The first drops fell heavy on Emma’s shoulders. She looked up. The sky had turned grey without her noticing.  

"Run!" Taro grabbed her wrist.  

Biscuit bolted ahead, tail low. The oak stood at the field’s edge, its leaves dark against the rain. They skidded under the canopy just as the downpour began in earnest.  

It was quieter here. The rain pattered high above, caught by the thick leaves. Only a few drops slipped through, landing cold on Emma’s nose. She wiped it with her sleeve.  

Taro edged forward. "Look." At the very edge of the canopy, the rain fell in a shimmering curtain. One step back—dry. One step forward—wet.  

Biscuit shook himself, spraying droplets. Gran would sigh at the muddy paws later.  

Emma crouched by the trunk, where the bark was driest. The squirrel’s hole between the roots had a tiny overhang. A single drop hung from it, swelling, then fell.  

Taro stretched his arm past the edge. His fingertips got wet first.  

The dry patch was exactly as wide as the oak’s reach. No farther.
