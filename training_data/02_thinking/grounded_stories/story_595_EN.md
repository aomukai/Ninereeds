The apple tree was full of blossom.

Emma stood underneath and looked up. The flowers were not all the same. At the tips of the branches the buds were tight and pink, closed up like small fists. Lower down the blossoms were open and white, with a little pink at the edges.

Mei reached up and touched one petal very lightly. It was soft. She did not pull it off.

A bee landed on a flower near her hand. It crawled inside the white part and came out again. Mei stayed very still. She watched it go to the next flower.

Emma looked down at the grass. A single blossom lay on the ground, still fresh. She picked it up. The petals were white with a crease in the middle. She turned it in her fingers.

Gran was digging near the vegetable patch. She straightened and looked at the tree.

"The bees are working," she said. "Every flower they visit will try to make an apple."

A gust of wind moved through the branches. A few petals came loose and drifted down. They landed on the grass, on Mei's sleeve, on Emma's hair. The ground was scattered with them — small and pale, like coins that had fallen and stayed where they landed.
