Emma held the egg basket as Gran lifted the latch on the henhouse. The three hens clucked and pecked at the ground, ignoring Biscuit as he ambled past. He sniffed the compost corner, yawned, and lay down in the sun.  

Bello trotted behind Emma, ears pricked. A hen scratched at the dirt, her feathers rustling. Bello froze. His tail stiffened. Then—*zip*—he dashed.  

The hens scattered in a flurry of wings and indignant squawks. Biscuit lifted his head, unimpressed. Gran caught Bello by the collar as he skidded past.  

"He doesn’t know yet," Emma said, picking up a stray feather.  

Gran rubbed Bello’s ears. "He’ll learn."  

Bello panted, eyes still fixed on the hens now regrouping under the apple tree. Biscuit sighed and flopped back onto his paws. The garden smelled of warm grass and damp earth. Somewhere, a bee buzzed.  

Gran let go. Bello sat very still, watching. The nearest hen pecked at a beetle, unconcerned.
