The apples lay in two baskets under the tree. One was piled high, the other only half full.  

"Which has more?" Gran asked, shaking a windfall from her apron into the smaller basket.  

Taro pointed. "That one!" The taller pile gleamed red in the sun.  

Emma crouched, tilting her head. "But the other's fuller at the bottom."  

Gran smiled. "Count and see."  

Taro scooped apples onto the grass. "One, two, three..." His heap grew. "Seven!"  

Emma lined hers up carefully. "Five."  

Taro blinked at the rows. "Oh. Seven's more."  

Gran lifted the emptier basket. "Light as a feather now." The last apple rolled against Emma’s knee, cool and smooth.  

Taro poked the larger pile. "They don’t look so big all spread out."  

A breeze shook the branches overhead. One more apple thudded into the grass, right between the two baskets.  

Gran picked it up. "Where shall we put this one?"  

Emma held out her hands. "Then it’ll be six and seven."  

Taro grinned. "Still more in mine!"  

The basket creaked as Gran dropped it in.
