The meadow path was wet. The grass on either side was bent and shining. In the middle, where the stones had sunk, a puddle lay still and grey.

Taro stopped. The puddle was smooth, like a small window into the ground.

He picked up a pebble. It was no bigger than his thumbnail, dark and round. He dropped it.

The pebble hit the water. A circle spread out — a ring of ripples, pale and quick. Then another ring inside it. Then another. They grew and grew until they reached the edge of the puddle and stopped.

Taro watched the water go still again.

He found another pebble. He dropped it from the same height. The same rings spread — first one, then the next, then the next. Same pattern. Same order.

Biscuit came up beside him. He put his nose to the puddle and drank. His tongue made the water wobble, but the ripples were not the same. They were messy and uneven.

Taro waited until Biscuit lifted his head and the puddle was still again.

He dropped a third pebble.

One ring. Two rings. Three rings. Four. He counted them as they spread.

Bello sat on the dry grass and watched the circles repeat.
