The hilltop grass was short and cool. Emma sat beside Gran, her hands flat on the ground.

“Look west,” Gran said.

Emma looked. At the far edge of the sky, a dark line sat low and still. It was not a cloud like the others. It was straight and heavy, the colour of slate.

“That will be here by afternoon,” Gran said.

Emma watched. The line did not move, but the air changed. The wind came across the hill in a single long push, bending the grass flat. Emma’s hair lifted and fell. She felt the coolness on her skin before the rain touched her.

Gran stood. “Time to go.”

Emma stood too. The dark line was closer now. The sky behind it had gone grey. She could feel the weight of it coming, the way you feel a door close in another room.

They walked down the hill. The wind followed them. The first drops landed on Emma’s arm, round and separate. She touched one with her finger.

“Not yet,” Gran said. “But soon.”

The line was still coming. The grass was still bending. Emma could feel the rain before it arrived.
