The market stall had a wooden table. On it sat baskets and a small brass scale.

Yun stood behind the table. She took an egg from a basket. She put it on one side of the scale. The side went down. She put another egg on the other side. That side went up.

Emma watched. The brass pans moved. They balanced for a moment. Then one side dipped again.

Mei stood beside Emma. She held a small basket with two hands.

“Like this,” Mei said. She showed Emma how to hold it. “Two hands. One on each side.”

Emma took the basket. She put both hands on the handles. The basket did not tip.

Yun put four eggs into Emma’s basket. Then she put a fifth. She looked at the scale. She added a sixth.

“That is six,” Yun said. She held out her hand.

Emma looked at the coin in her own hand. She put it into Yun’s hand.

Yun put the coin into a small wooden box. She did not count it again.

Emma held the basket with two hands. The eggs did not move. She looked at Mei.

Mei nodded.

They walked away from the stall together. Emma did not look back.
