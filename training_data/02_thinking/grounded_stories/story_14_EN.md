The rain tapped against the window. Taro pressed his finger to the glass, tracing a drop as it slid down. Outside, the garden was a blur of grey.  

Emma pulled the curtain back. "It's going all crooked," she said. The raindrops twisted paths as the wind pushed them.  

Gran turned a page in her book. Steam curled from her tea on the table. The fire crackled.  

Taro leaned closer, his breath fogging the glass. He drew a line through it. "Look, it’s a river."  

Emma wiped her sleeve across the fog. "Now it’s gone."  

Gran sipped her tea. "Best rivers stay outside," she said, without looking up.  

The wind rattled the window. The fire hissed. Inside, the room was warm and yellow. Outside, the oak tree stood dark against the sky, its branches shaking.  

Taro rested his chin on the sill. Another raindrop rolled down, slow and wobbling. He touched his fingertip to where it stopped.
