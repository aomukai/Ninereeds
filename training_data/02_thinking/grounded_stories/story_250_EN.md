The millstones were grinding. Emma stood on the stone floor and felt it through her boots. A low rumble came up through her soles, into her legs. She pressed her feet flat and stood still to feel it better.

Taro put his hand against the wall. The stone shook under his palm. He leaned closer and the rumble travelled up his arm into his chest.

"It's louder here," he said.

Mei was at the base of the wheel. She took Biscuit's head and guided his nose to a stone near the floor. He sniffed it once, then pressed his ear down and listened.

The sound was so deep that Emma felt it before she heard it. She walked a few steps. The vibration was weaker near the door. She stepped back beside Taro and the rumble grew strong again.

"The stones are very heavy," Mei said. "They go round and round. You feel it through everything."

Biscuit sneezed. The flour dust hung in the air, fine and white, and the low rumble went on through the floor, through the walls, through their feet.
