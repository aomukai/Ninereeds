Dou stopped at the edge of the pond.

Emma and Mei had walked down the meadow path together, and Dou had followed. She followed sometimes. Not always. But today she came, a small grey shape moving through the long grass, her white ear showing and then hidden.

Now she stood where the grass turned to mud. Her front paw was lifted. She did not put it down.

A frog jumped.

It came from somewhere near the reeds and landed in the water with a sound so small it was almost no sound at all — just a shift, a splash the size of a coin. The water shivered outward in rings.

Dou watched the rings. Her tail was very still. Her lifted paw stayed lifted.

Mei watched Dou.

“She won’t go in,” Mei said, not loudly.

Emma looked at the cat, then at the water. The rings were almost gone now. A second frog sat on a lily pad, very still, pretending not to be there.

Dou watched it. She did not step forward. She did not step back. She sat down where she was, one paw still raised, and waited.

The water smoothed. The frog did not move.

Dou waited.
