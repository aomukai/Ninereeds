Emma sat on the edge of her bed, toes brushing the cold floor. "How many days until Gran’s birthday?"  

Taro held up four fingers. "Four sleeps." He wiggled them. Emma folded her own fingers down one by one. Four.  

The next morning, Taro clattered down the stairs before Emma. When she reached the kitchen, he was already at the table, three fingers pressed flat against his toast. He didn’t say a word.  

Emma counted again. Three sleeps now.  

That afternoon, they helped Gran shell peas at the kitchen table. Taro dropped one, and it rolled under the stove. "After tomorrow," Emma said, picking it up, "it’ll be two sleeps."  

Gran smiled but kept shelling.  

At bedtime, Taro flopped onto his quilt. "Two," he said, holding up two fingers like a victory.  

Emma touched her own. Two more dark windows, two more mornings.  

The next day, the number on Taro’s hand was one.  

And then, at last, it was the morning with no fingers left to count.
