The hedge rustled. Biscuit’s nose was already buried in the leaves at one end, his tail wagging hard. Bello, quick and quiet, slipped to the other side.  

Taro crouched in the middle, trying to see. "What is it?"  

Something small scrabbled inside the hedge. Biscuit pushed forward, his shoulders squeezing between the branches. Bello wriggled in from the opposite side, ears pricked. The leaves trembled where they met.  

"Hey—" Taro reached, but the dogs were too far apart. Biscuit snorted. Bello gave a short, excited yip. The rustling moved lower, deeper into the thicket.  

Then—silence.  

Biscuit backed out, panting. Bello emerged a moment later, nose dusty. A flicker of movement in the grass beneath the hedge, and then nothing.  

Taro peered at the empty space between them. "It got out," he said.  

Biscuit shook himself, sending leaves flying. Bello trotted to the hedge’s end and sniffed the ground once, twice—then dashed off, chasing nothing at all.
