Taro ran at the slide. His shoes hit the gravel hard. He grabbed the rail with both hands. His feet skidded forward. Gravel scattered. He stopped just before the post.

Emma ran slower. Her feet landed flat. She stopped easily at the bottom. No skid. No scatter.

Taro looked at the ground where his feet had dragged. A long mark in the gravel.

"Again," he said.

He walked back to the start. Emma stayed at the bottom. He ran faster this time. His legs pumped. His arms swung. He grabbed the rail. His feet were too fast. They could not catch up. They skidded. They skidded longer. His body kept going. His hands held the rail. His chest hit the post. Soft. Not hard. But he had not stopped where he wanted.

He stood still. He looked at his feet. They were two steps past where Emma had stopped.

"Faster is harder to stop," Emma said.

Taro did not say anything. He put his foot on the gravel where his skid had ended. He pressed. The stones shifted under his shoe.

He walked back to the start again.
