The lane was still dark. The rest of the village was asleep.

But the bakery window was lit. A warm yellow glow fell onto the stones outside.

Emma pressed her face to the glass. Inside, Hana stood at the long wooden table. Her sleeves were rolled up. Her hands were deep in a bowl of dough. She folded it over and pushed it down. Folded and pushed. Her arms moved the same way every time.

On the counter beside her, four loaves sat on a cloth. Their tops were golden brown. Steam rose from them in thin ribbons.

Hana looked up and saw Emma at the window. She nodded once, then pointed at the loaves.

Emma went to the door. The handle was warm.

Inside, the air was thick and sweet. The heat from the oven pressed against her cheeks.

Hana lifted a loaf from the cloth. She turned it over and tapped the bottom. It made a hollow sound. She wrapped it in paper and set it on the counter.

A woman came in. She put coins on the wooden board. Hana pushed the wrapped loaf toward her. The woman carried it out into the cold morning.

Hana went back to the table. More dough. More folding. The oven ticked as it cooled.

Emma stood by the door and watched her work.
