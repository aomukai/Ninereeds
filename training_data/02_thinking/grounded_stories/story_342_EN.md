The market street was quiet now. Most of the stalls were already gone.

Yun stood behind her table. The apple basket was empty — just a few stems at the bottom and one bruised fruit that she set aside. The turnip basket was half full. She looked at the turnips for a moment. Then she took a sack from under the table and began to pack them.

Mei watched her grandmother's hands. Yun lifted each turnip, wiped the soil off with her thumb, and placed it in the sack.

"The ones that are left will keep," Yun said. She did not stop packing.

Mei pulled the cloth off the table. It was white with a faint blue edge. She folded it once, twice, a third time, and pressed the crease flat with the side of her hand.

Yun tied the sack. She lifted it onto her shoulder.

Mei picked up the basket and the folded cloth.

They walked together down the street. The baker's light was already off. The post box was a dark red shadow in the wall. The lane was cold and still, and the only sound was their footsteps and the small weight of what they carried.
