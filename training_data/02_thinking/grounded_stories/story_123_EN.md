The rain had stopped, but the meadow path was wet. Emma stepped carefully where the ground dipped. Her boot sank a little and came up with thick brown mud clinging to the sole.  

Taro poked at the soft earth with a stick. "Look," he said. He pressed the stick in deep and lifted it. The mud held the shape of the stick’s end, a neat round hole.  

Emma crouched beside him. She pressed her thumb into the mud. It gave way, cool and slick, then held the print when she pulled back. "It remembers," she said.  

Taro pressed his whole hand down. The mud squished between his fingers. When he lifted, his palm and fingers left deep marks. The edges crumbled slightly, but the shape stayed.  

A puddle at the path’s edge reflected the sky. Emma stepped closer, and her boot sank again. The water seeped into the soil slowly, leaving the ground soft where it had been firm yesterday.  

Taro dragged the stick through the mud, leaving a wobbly line. "It’s like writing," he said.  

The mud kept every mark.
