Emma pulled her mittens tighter and stepped into the cold garden. The woodpile stood against the shed, snow dusting the top logs. She picked one up—rough bark, heavy in her arms—and carried it inside.  

Gran knelt by the fireplace, stirring the embers. "One won’t last the evening," she said.  

Taro was already pulling on his boots. He came back with three logs stacked in his arms, cheeks pink from the cold. Gran took them one by one.  

"One," she said, laying Emma’s log by the hearth. "Two, three, four." She brushed bark from her apron. "One and three makes four. That will do."  

The fire crackled. Taro held his hands toward the flames. Emma watched as Gran arranged the logs—just so—and the room grew warmer. Outside, the snow kept falling, silent and steady.
