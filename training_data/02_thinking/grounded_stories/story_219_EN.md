The mill was quiet. The wheel was still.

Emma walked up close and put her hand on one of the paddles. The wood was damp and dark. Water slid over the top and dripped down, but the wheel did not turn.

She looked up. The low sun came through the gaps in the wall. Long stripes of light lay across the stone floor. Dust floated in them, very slow.

Mei was sitting on the step near the door. She was not doing anything. She was just listening.

Emma went and sat beside her. The water in the channel made a low sound — a trickle and a drip and a soft moving underneath. It was not loud like the mill in the morning. It was a different sound.

“The wheel is still,” Emma said.

“It is,” said Mei. “The sluice is closed.”

Emma looked at the water. It slid past the paddles without pushing them. The surface was dark and smooth. She could see the reflection of the sky in the channel — pale and flat and very still.

Inside the mill, the shadows were long. The stones did not move. The air was cool and damp.

They sat together on the step and listened to the water.
