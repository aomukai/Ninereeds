The schoolyard was hot. The sun sat on the stones and made them white. The climbing frame was empty. No one stood near it.

Emma sat on the bench under the ash tree. The shade was deep and cool. She leaned back against the trunk. The bark was rough through her shirt. She watched the leaves move above her. They were very small leaves, very many of them. They made a sound like soft water.

Mei stood at the edge of the shade. One foot was on the grass in the sun. The other was on the grass in the shadow. She looked at her feet.

“It is cooler there,” she said.

“Yes,” said Emma.

Mei stepped forward. The shade covered her. She sat at the other end of the bench. The shadow of the tree did not reach the whole yard. The far wall was still in the sun. The gate was still in the sun.

Taro came running across the hot stones. He stopped in the shade and breathed.

“Hot,” he said.

Clara came to the door of the classroom. She did not step out into the sun.

“Come in now,” she said. “From the shade.”
