The rain came down hard. It drummed on the mill roof like a hundred small fists.

Emma stood under the eaves with Mei. The stream beside the mill was not the quiet stream they knew. It was high and fast and brown. It pushed against the wheel.

The wheel turned fast. Faster than Emma had ever seen it. Water flew off the paddles in sheets. The sound was a deep, wet roar that shook the air.

Mei pulled the door open. Inside, it was dry. The stone floor was dusted with flour and the air was still. The sound of the wheel was different here—muffled and steady, like a heartbeat through the floor.

They sat down on the floor together. Biscuit lay beside them, chin on his paws, ears twitching at every new sound.

Rain on the roof. The wheel turning. The drip of something near the hopper.

They did not speak. There was nothing to say. The mill held all the sound there was, and they sat inside it, dry and still, and listened.

Mei watched a single drip fall from a beam. It landed in the flour dust and made a tiny dark circle. Another fell. Another.

The wheel kept turning. The rain kept falling. The mill held them both.
