Emma and Taro walked down the lane. The hedges were green with new leaves.

Ahead, a man stood by a gate. He leaned on the top bar with both hands. It was Owen, the farmer.

Behind him, the field was full of sheep. They moved slowly across the grass, heads down. A few lambs stayed close to their mothers.

Owen's sheepdog sat beside him. It did not move. It watched the children with bright eyes.

Taro stopped. Emma stopped.

Owen looked up at the sky. Then he looked at the children.

"Clouds coming from the west," he said. He lifted one big hand and pointed. "Rain by afternoon."

Emma looked where he pointed. The clouds were grey and low. They moved slowly across the sky.

Owen did not say anything else. He put his hand back on the gate. His hat had a dark patch where the brim was worn.

The sheepdog yawned. It looked at the sheep, then back at the children.

Taro looked at the clouds again. "Should we go back?"

Emma thought. "Not yet," she said. "But soon."

They stood for a moment longer, watching the sheep move across the field.
