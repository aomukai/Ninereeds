The light was going. The mill room was dim and cold.

Mei stood by the window. The wheel outside was almost still. Ice had built up on the paddles, thick and white. The water moved underneath, but the wheel did not turn.

“It is frozen,” Mei said.

Gran came over from the hopper. She carried a small lamp. She lit it with a match, and the flame grew. The shadows jumped on the stone walls.

Emma watched Mei. Mei touched the latch on the door once, checking it was closed. Then she crossed the floor and took the lamp from Gran.

“I will carry it,” she said.

Gran nodded. She pulled the door open. Cold air came in sharp.

They stepped out. The lane was dark. The sky above the mill was a deep grey-blue. No stars yet.

Mei latched the door behind them. The metal clicked.

They walked up the lane. The lamp made a small circle of light on the stones. The rest of the world was shadow.

Emma walked between Gran and Mei. No one spoke. The only sound was their feet on the cold ground.
