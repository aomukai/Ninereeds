The path up the hill was dry and dusty. Emma's legs were warm from the climb. Taro had run ahead and was waiting at the top, standing on the flat stone where the gate was.

Gran came up last, slower, and stopped to look.

Emma looked too. The village was below them, smaller than she had ever seen it. The roofs were grey and red and brown, fitted together like the pieces of a jigsaw puzzle Gran kept in a box in the sitting room.

"There," Mei said, and pointed.

Emma followed her finger. A thin line of smoke rose straight up from a chimney — no wind to bend it. The mill chimney.

"Smoke," Taro said.

"From the fire under the millstones," Mei said. "They grind today."

Emma could see the mill roof beside the chimney, dark and flat. And beside the mill, running away through the village, a silver line. The stream.

"It looks like a ribbon," Emma said.

"It turns the wheel," Mei said. "You can see where it goes."

Everything was laid out flat from here. The stream, the roofs, the mill chimney with its straight smoke. The village made a pattern that you could only see from the top.

Taro stepped off the stone. "Can we go down now?"

Gran smiled. "Not yet."
