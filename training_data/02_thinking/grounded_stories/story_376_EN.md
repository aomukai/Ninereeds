The sun was high and hot. The hill had no trees at all.

Emma walked with her head down. The grass was dry and yellow. It crackled under her shoes. Taro was ahead of her, already halfway up.

"Wait," Emma said. But Taro kept going.

Gran came last. She carried a cloth bag and walked slowly. Biscuit ran past her, then came back, tongue hanging out.

At the top, the grass was flattened in one spot. Taro sat down. Then he lay down. The ground was warm through his shirt.

Emma sat beside him. There was no shade anywhere. The sky was very blue. The village below looked small and still.

Gran sat down too. She took off her hat and fanned her face with it.

"Whew," she said.

A puff of wind came across the hilltop. It was not cool exactly, but it was less hot. Emma felt it on her face.

Biscuit flopped down beside them. His sides went in and out fast. He did not move.

"Better here than in the field," Gran said. She put her hat back on.

They sat in the small patch of flattened grass. The wind came again. It moved the dry grass in waves.

No one moved. The hill was quiet. The sun was still hot, but the wind was a little stronger now.
