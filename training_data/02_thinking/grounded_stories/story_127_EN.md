Emma stood on tiptoe by the sink. The taps shone in the morning light—one on the left, one on the right. She reached for them.  

"Feel first," Gran said, kneading dough at the table.  

Emma touched the left tap. Cold metal. She pressed her finger to it until the chill seeped into her skin. Then the right one. Warm. Not burning, just a quiet heat, like the side of a teacup.  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron and came over. She turned the cold tap. Water rushed out, clear and bright. Then the hot. Steam curled up in faint wisps.  

"Now both," Gran said.  

Emma watched as Gran twisted them together. The streams met in the basin, swirling. She held her hand under the flow. Not cold. Not hot. Something in between, like the pond in late summer.  

Gran left the water running and went back to her dough. Emma cupped her hands under the spout, feeling the temperature settle. Outside, a blackbird sang. The kitchen smelled of flour and warm metal.
