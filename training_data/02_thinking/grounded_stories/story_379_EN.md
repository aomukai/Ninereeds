Taro stood at the gate on the hill. The grass was short and warm under his hands.

Owen leaned on the gate beside him. He did not say anything. He was watching the slope.

The sheep dog was far out on the hill. A black-and-white shape moving fast. She ran wide, making a big circle around the flock. The sheep lifted their heads. They moved closer together.

Owen put two fingers in his mouth and whistled. One sharp note.

The dog stopped. Her head turned. She waited.

Owen nodded once. The dog moved again, cutting in from the side. The sheep turned. They began to move down the slope together, packed tight, all facing the same way.

Taro watched. The dog did not bark. She did not chase. She simply moved where the sheep were not, and the sheep filled the space she left.

Biscuit sat at Taro's feet. His ears were up. His nose worked. He whined once, very low.

Taro put his hand on Biscuit's head. "Stay," he said.

Biscuit stayed. But his tail wagged.

The dog brought the flock to the bottom of the hill. Owen pushed the gate open. The sheep filed through, one after another.

The dog sat. She looked up at Owen.

Owen said, "Good."

That was all.
