Emma led Mei across the field. Biscuit ran ahead, then circled back, then ran ahead again.

"Here," Emma said. "This is the oak."

Mei stopped. She looked up. Then she stepped closer and put her hand flat against the bark.

"It looks different," she said. "From the field it looked small. But it is not small at all."

Emma showed her the low branch. "You can sit on it. It is thick enough."

Mei touched the branch. The bark was rough. The leaves above it were new — very small, very green, just open.

"Look," Emma said. She knelt down between two roots. "There is a hole. A squirrel lives in there."

Mei knelt too. She leaned forward and looked into the dark space between the roots. The hole was deeper than she expected. She could not see the bottom.

She sat back and looked up again. The branches spread wide. The robin's nest was in the fork — she could see it only because she was underneath.

"The tree is different up close," she said.

Biscuit put his nose into the squirrel hole and sneezed.
