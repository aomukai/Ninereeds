The main street was full of people. Sun lay warm on the stones. A woman with a blue basket stepped around a man with a ladder. Voices came from every direction.

Emma held the basket Hana had given them. Inside was a loaf, still warm through the cloth. A small paper bag sat beside it. Taro walked ahead, then stopped, then walked again.

The market stall was busy. Yun stood behind the table. Her hands were fast — she weighed something, tied a bag, made change without looking up. Mei stood beside her. She was watching the people pass.

“Hello,” said Emma.

Mei looked up. “Hello.”

Taro leaned close to the table. There were apples in a wooden crate. A jar of honey. Small bundles of dried herbs.

“We got bread,” Taro said.

“I can see,” said Mei.

A man stopped at the table. Yun spoke to him. Mei straightened a jar that was slightly crooked.

Emma shifted the basket to her other arm. The street was loud. Doors stood open to let the air in. The shop beyond the stall had a striped awning and a cat sitting in the doorway.

“We should go,” Emma said.

Mei nodded. “I’m here until the afternoon.”

They walked back down the street. The sun was high. The basket was full.
