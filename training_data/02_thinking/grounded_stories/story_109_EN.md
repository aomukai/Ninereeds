The waiting room was quiet, the kind of quiet that made the clock louder. Taro swung his legs under the chair. The seat was hard, the plastic cold. Across the room, the fish tank glowed faintly. Three orange fish swam in slow circles. Taro counted them once, then again. One, two, three. One, two, three.  

The clock ticked. Taro glanced at it. The minute hand hadn’t moved—or maybe it had, just a little. He counted the fish a third time. One, two, three.  

Gran flipped through a magazine, her fingers moving slowly. The pages were thin and rustled softly. Taro leaned forward, resting his chin on his hands. The fish swam on. Their tails flicked, stirring the water.  

“How long?” he whispered.  
Gran looked up. “Not much longer,” she said.  

The clock kept ticking. The fish kept circling. Taro counted them again. One, two, three.  

At last, a voice broke the stillness. “Taro?”  
He slid off the chair. The appointment was quick—a jab, a word, a nod. Outside, the cold air hit his face. The wait had been longer than the jab. Much longer.
