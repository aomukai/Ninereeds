The market stall stood under the striped awning. Yun’s hands moved without pausing. She picked up a potato, turned it, set it aside. She picked up another and put it in the bag.

Emma watched from the front of the stall. Mei stood beside her grandmother, her eyes on the scale.

Yun set the bag of potatoes on the brass plate. The scale did not move. The needle stayed low.

She reached for a small brass weight. It was round and flat, with a number stamped into the top. She placed it on the other side.

The scale tipped. The needle swung past the middle.

Yun pulled the weight off. She picked up a smaller one. She placed it carefully.

The needle stayed low.

She added another. The needle rose a little.

She added one more. The needle sat in the middle, exactly between the two marks.

The scale balanced.

Yun looked at Emma. “That is three pounds,” she said.

Emma looked at the weights. They were all different sizes. The smallest one was no bigger than her thumb.

Mei pointed. “That one is half a pound. That one is a quarter.”

Emma did not touch them. She leaned closer. The brass was warm from Yun’s hand.

Yun lifted the bag of potatoes off the scale. The needle swung back and stopped.
