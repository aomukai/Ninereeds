Gran lifted the iron bar. The cellar door came up with a low scrape.

The smell rose. Earth and apples and something sharp and dark.

Bello stepped back. His ears went flat. His tail dropped.

Gran took the lamp from the hook. She struck a match. The flame caught, small and yellow. She turned the little wheel and the light grew.

She went down.

The steps were steep. The lamp went with her. The light sank, step by step, until only her hands and the top of her head were still visible.

Then she was gone.

Bello stood at the top of the steps. He did not follow. He put one paw forward and took it back. He put his nose out into the dark where she had been and pulled it in again.

The air from the cellar was cold on his face. Cold and still and full of the smell of the ground.

He waited.

The lamp came back. First a glow on the stone wall. Then Gran's hands. Then her whole face, lit from below.

She climbed the last step and set the lamp on the ground.

Bello did not go near the door. He watched it. He watched the dark place where it led.

Gran let the door fall. It hit the frame with a thick, solid sound. She slid the bar back into place.

Bello breathed.
