The hill was high. The grass was short and dry. The wind moved across the top without stopping.

Emma stood beside Gran. Taro was already looking down.

"See the chimney?" Gran said. She pointed.

Emma looked. Down in the village, the roofs were small and close together. Chimneys poked up like fingers. One was taller than the others. A thin line of smoke rose from it, straight up into the still air.

"That's the mill," Gran said.

Taro squinted. "I see it. The tall one."

Emma found it too. She traced the village lane with her finger. The lane went past the baker's, past the side street, past the green. She followed it up the hill, past the cottage, all the way to where she stood.

"That lane goes to the mill," she said.

"Everything is connected," Gran said. She was not looking at the children. She was looking at the smoke.

The smoke did not bend. It rose and disappeared into the blue.
