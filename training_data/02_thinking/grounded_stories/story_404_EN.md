Gran lifted the heavy door and propped it with the iron bar. The cold smell came up from the darkness—earth and apples and something sharp.

“Fetch me the pickled cabbage,” she said. “The back of the middle shelf.”

Taro took the lamp. The flame made the shadows lean and move. He went down the five stone steps one at a time, and the floor was hard earth under his feet.

The middle shelf was higher than his chin. He stood on his toes and looked. Jars lined the shelf in a row—dark shapes in the yellow light. He moved one aside. It clinked against the next. Behind it, a jar with a red thread tied around the neck.

He reached. His fingers touched the glass. He pulled it forward, careful, and lifted it with both hands. The jar was heavy and cold.

He carried it up the steps. Gran took it from him and set it on the kitchen table.

“That’s the one,” she said, and untied the red thread.
