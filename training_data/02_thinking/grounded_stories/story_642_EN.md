Mei carried Dou to the pond. The cat was light and warm in her arms, and her tail flicked once, twice, as they came through the long grass.

At the edge where the grass stopped and the mud began, Mei set her down. Dou's paws touched the ground. She did not move.

She sat.

Biscuit was already in the water. He had plunged straight in, front paws first, and now stood belly-deep, water dripping from his chin. He turned his head toward the reeds and sniffed loudly. A frog jumped. The splash was small and quick.

Dou's ear flicked. The white one. That was all.

Bello circled behind her, nose low to the ground, then trotted to the edge and put one paw in. Then the other. He stood at the rim, watching the ripples spread.

Mei sat down on the flat stone. The water was still again. The willow dipped its leaves into the far side. Dou sat at the edge of the grass, watching everything, not moving closer.

A second frog jumped. Biscuit sneezed. Dou blinked slowly.

She stayed where she was.
