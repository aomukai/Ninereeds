The mill was dark after the bright lane. Emma blinked. Her eyes did not hurry to adjust.

Gran stepped forward. The floorboard under her foot creaked and tipped.

Mei pulled her back. "That one," Mei said. She stepped over it to a dry spot near the wall.

Emma watched where Mei put her feet. The floor was stone in some places and wood in others. The wood was old. Some boards were dark with damp. Some were light and dusty.

"This is the path," Mei said. She pointed at a line of boards that looked no different from the rest. "Step here. Avoid there."

Emma followed. She put her foot where Mei had put hers. The board held.

Gran came behind, taking the same path. She did not ask why. She trusted Mei to know.

There was a spot near the wall where the floor was dry and the dust was thin. Mei stopped there. "This is where you stand," she said.

Emma stood beside her. The floor was solid under her shoes. The dust did not puff up around her feet.

Mei knew the mill. She knew which boards to trust and which to leave alone. Emma did not ask how. She watched and learned.
