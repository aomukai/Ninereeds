The sun was heavy on the pond.

Emma sat on the flat stone and looked down. The water was so still it barely moved. She could see the bottom — every stone, every dark leaf settled in the mud.

The fish were there, but they did not swim. They hung near the bottom, fins barely twitching. They looked deeper than before. Or maybe the water was shallower.

Taro knelt at the edge. He dipped his hand in.

"It's warm," he said.

He pushed his hand deeper. His fingers went down past his wrist.

"Now it's cold," he said. "Right at the bottom."

Emma put her hand in beside his. The top felt like bathwater. But when she pushed deeper, the water changed. Cool. Then cold.

Bello lay under the willow, flat on his side. His tongue hung out. He did not get up. He panted slow and did not move.

The willow hung down low. Its shadow was a dark patch on the grass. The bench sat in the sun, empty and hot.

"It's too hot for the bench," Emma said.

Taro pulled his hand out and shook it. Drops fell and the pond rippled. The fish did not notice.

Bello closed his eyes. The sun was very still.
