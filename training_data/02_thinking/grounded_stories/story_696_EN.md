The apple tree was pink and white. Emma stood under it with her face tilted up. Petals fell like soft snow. Bees moved from flower to flower, heavy and slow.

"This is the blossom," Gran said. She was on the stone bench with a bowl of beans in her lap. "In a few weeks, you will see something else."

Mei came through the garden gate on a warm morning. The tree was different now. No pink. No white. No bees.

Emma touched a branch. Small hard knobs hung where the petals had been. Green. Tight. Each one no bigger than her thumbnail.

She pressed one with her finger. It did not give.

"Are these apples?" Mei asked.

Gran came over and looked. She did not touch the fruit.

"They are green fruit," she said. "They will be apples. But not yet."

Emma picked one. It was hard and sour-smelling. She put it back on the branch. It did not belong anywhere yet.

Mei stood on her toes and looked at the others. All of them were the same. Small. Green. Waiting.

Gran went back to the bench. "Give them time," she said.

The two girls stood under the tree. The fruit did nothing. But the sun was warm, and the leaves above them were growing.
