Emma held an apple in each hand. She turned them over. One was round and fat. The other was thin and small.

“Look,” she said. “They are not the same.”

Taro came over with his arms full. Apples bumped against his chin. “These are all big,” he said.

Gran set three baskets on the grass. One was large. One was small. One was in between.

“Big apples in this basket,” Gran said. “Small ones in that one. The ones that are not big and not small go in the middle.”

Emma put her fat apple into the big basket. She put the thin one into the small basket.

Taro dropped his armful onto the grass. He picked them up one by one. He held each one beside another. “This one is bigger than this one,” he said. He put the bigger one in the big basket. He put the smaller one in the medium basket.

Emma found two apples that looked the same. She held them side by side. She turned them. She turned them again. “These are the same size,” she said.

She put them both in the medium basket.

The pile of apples on the grass got smaller. The baskets filled. The big basket was full first. The small basket had only three apples in it.

Taro picked up the last apple. It was very small. He put it with the other small ones.

“Done,” he said.
