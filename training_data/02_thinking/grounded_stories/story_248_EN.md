The mill stood at the edge of the village. The stream beside it was low.

Mei stood at the water's edge and looked at the channel. "Hardly any water," she said.

Taro came up beside her. Biscuit did not come. He found a patch of shade near the mill door and lay down, tongue out, panting.

Inside, the wheel was turning. But very slowly. One full turn. A pause. Another turn.

Taro put his hand on the wood. The wheel creaked. It moved a little, then stopped, then moved again.

"Why is it so slow?" he asked.

Mei pointed at the stream, then at the channel that fed the wheel. "Not enough water to push it. The wheel needs more water to go fast."

Taro looked at the dry ground, the low stream, the slow wheel. "So it cannot grind grain?"

"Not well," said Mei. "Not until the rain comes."

Biscuit panted in the shade. The dust in the air was fine and white and settled very slowly on everything it touched.
