The mill was quiet. The great stones had stopped turning.

Yun knelt beside a cloth sack. She put her hand inside and scooped.

“Feel,” she said.

Emma held out her hand. Yun let the flour fall into her palm. It was soft. It was fine. It was warm.

Emma touched it with her other hand. The warmth stayed. She turned it over in her fingers.

“Why is it warm?” she asked.

Yun pointed up at the millstones. “They did that. The stones grind. They rub together. They make heat.”

Emma closed her hand around the flour. It felt new. It felt fresh.

Mei came over. She put her own hand into the sack. “It is always warm,” she said. “Right after.”

Emma opened her hand. The flour sat in her palm, pale and fine and still carrying the heat from the stone.

She touched it again. She could feel it. The warmth, the softness, the way it had come from something hard and heavy turning against itself.

She did not want to brush it off yet.

Yun tied the sack closed. “Take some home,” she said. “Gran will know what to do with it.”
