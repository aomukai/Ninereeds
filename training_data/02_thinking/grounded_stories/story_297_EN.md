Owen came to the workshop door. He carried a wooden post over his shoulder.

He set it down on the ground. The bottom end was dark and soft.

"Rot," he said.

Vern came over. He took the post in his hands. He turned it and looked at the soft part.

He ran his thumb over the wood. It crumbled a little.

Vern did not say anything. He carried the post to the workbench and set it on the vise. He tightened the vise. Then he picked up the saw.

The saw teeth bit into the wood just above the rot. The sound was even and steady. Dust fell onto the workbench. The rotten end dropped off and hit the floor.

Vern looked at the post. The cut end was pale. The wood was clean.

He measured the post against a rule on the bench. He made a mark with a pencil.

Owen watched.

Vern loosened the vise and took the post to the door. He held it upright against the frame. He tipped it forward and back, looking at the fit.

"Good," he said.

Owen nodded. He picked up the post.

At the door he stopped. "Straight?"

Vern looked at him. "You set it straight, it will be straight."
