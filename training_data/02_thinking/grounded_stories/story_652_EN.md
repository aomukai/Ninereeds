The apple tree was heavy. The branches bent low, almost to the ground.

Emma stood underneath and looked up. Green apples and red apples. Hundreds of them.

A thump behind her. She turned. An apple had fallen into the grass. Biscuit trotted over and put his nose on it. He sniffed once and walked away.

Taro came running from the kitchen door. "Another one fell?"

"Look," Emma said. She picked it up. One side was green and hard. The other side was soft and brown where it had hit the ground.

"Windfall," said Gran from the garden bench. She was shelling peas into a bowl. "Gather them. They won't keep long."

Emma carried the basket. Taro walked beside her, scanning the grass. Every few steps he found another — under the tree, near the wall, one half-hidden in the parsley.

The basket filled. The apples bumped against each other. Some were split. Some were perfect except for one small bruise.

Emma lifted the basket and put her face close. The smell was sweet and soft, like nothing else in the garden.

"Tomorrow there will be more," Gran said. She did not look up from the peas.
