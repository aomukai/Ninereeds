Mei called.

Emma looked up from the vegetable patch. Mei stood at the garden gate, and Dou was already on the wall.

The cat had not jumped. She was simply there — grey, with one white ear and a tail that stood straight up like a question mark. She sat very still under the apple tree, her gaze fixed on something in the grass that Emma could not see.

At the far end of the garden, Bello had stopped moving. His ears were stiff. His front paw hung in the air.

He crept closer.

Dou did not run. She turned her head — just her head — and looked at him. Nothing else moved.

Bello stopped. He did not know what to do with a creature who did not run.

Emma watched. The lichen on the wall was grey-green and dry. Dou's tail did not flicker.

Mei stepped through the gate. "She does that," she said.

Bello sat down. He was still watching, but he was no longer creeping. Dou looked away again, back to whatever was in the grass.

Then she was gone — a flick of grey, a white ear, and the wall was empty.

Emma blinked.

Bello sniffed the stone where the cat had been.
