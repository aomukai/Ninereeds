Owen came up the hill with his dog. The dog ran wide and slow, and the sheep began to move.

"They are going down," said Owen. "Before the cold comes."

Taro ran to the gate. He pulled it open and stood against it. The iron was cold. The gate was heavy. He held it wide.

The flock moved through the opening. One by one. The sheep pushed together and then apart, their feet making small sounds on the grass. A lamb stopped in the middle of the gap. Owen's dog looked at it. The lamb moved on.

Emma stood beside the gatepost. She counted as each one passed. Seven. Eight. Nine. The wool was thick and dirty. One sheep had a torn ear.

Owen leaned on the gatepost on the other side. He was counting too. His lips moved. His eyes followed each sheep through.

When the last one passed, Emma said, "Sixteen."

Owen nodded. "Sixteen."

He pulled the gate closed. The iron latch clicked.

The sheep were already walking down the path, slow and steady, toward the lower field where the grass was still green.

Taro let go of the gate. His hands were cold.

"Winter is coming," said Owen. And he walked after the flock.
