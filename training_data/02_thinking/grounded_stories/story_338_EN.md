Riku stopped his bicycle at the edge of the market stall. The red frame was dusty from the lane. He leaned it against the corner of Yun's table and pulled a letter from his canvas bag.

The envelope was plain. Cream-coloured paper. Yun's name written across the front in dark ink.

He held it out. "Came this morning."

Yun took it. She turned it over once in her hands. Then she slid her thumb under the flap and opened it.

Mei stood beside the table. She watched her grandmother's face, not the paper.

Yun read the letter through. Her eyes moved across the lines. She did not hurry. When she reached the bottom, she turned the page over to check the back. It was blank.

She folded the letter along its creases. The paper made a neat, quiet sound. She slid it into the pocket of her apron and patted it once.

Then she picked up a loose carrot that had rolled to the edge of the stall and put it back in the row.

Mei waited.

Yun did not say what the letter said.

She straightened a twist of dried herbs. She nudged a jar into line with the others. Her hands moved the way they always moved.

Mei watched her hands for a moment. Then she picked up the second carrot and put it where it belonged.

Riku swung his leg over the bicycle and pushed off down the street. The bag leaned to one side as he went.
