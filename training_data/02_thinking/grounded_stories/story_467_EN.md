Dou sat on the stone bench in the afternoon sun. Her grey fur was warm. Her white ear turned like a leaf in the wind.

Biscuit came from behind the shed. His nose was in the air. He was sniffing something — something under the apple tree, or maybe in the grass by the path.

Then he saw the cat.

He stopped. His head came down. His ears went forward.

Dou did not move. She watched him with her yellow eyes.

Biscuit sniffed once. A short, quick sniff. Then he turned his head and walked past the bench. Not around it. Not close to it. Past it, on the grass side, as if the bench was empty.

He lay down under the apple tree. He put his nose on his paws.

Emma watched from the kitchen door. Biscuit had chosen. He did not care about the grey cat. The cat was not interesting today.

Bello came trotting around the corner. He saw Dou. He stopped. He looked at Biscuit. Biscuit did not lift his head.

Dou sat very still on the stone bench. She watched Bello. She watched the grass. She watched the sky.

She did not move.
