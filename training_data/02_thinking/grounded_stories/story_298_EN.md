Vern’s workshop smelled of sawdust and oil. Taro stood at the bench with a short saw in his hand. A piece of wood was clamped in the vise.

Taro pulled the saw toward himself. The blade skidded. He pulled harder. The saw jammed halfway through.

Vern came over. He did not take the saw away. He put his hand over Taro’s hand.

“Feel the teeth,” he said.

Taro ran his thumb very gently along the edge. The teeth pointed one way: forward.

“They cut on the push,” Vern said. “Not the pull.”

Taro pushed. The blade bit into the wood. Sawdust spilled from the cut in a fine line. He pushed again. The saw moved smooth and straight.

“Keep the angle,” Vern said. “Steady.”

Taro pushed again. The blade went through to the bottom. The piece fell onto the bench with a soft thump.

He looked at the cut. The edge was clean. The sawdust lay in a small heap beside his hand.

Vern nodded once. He picked up another piece of wood and set it in the vise.

Taro started the saw at the right angle. He pushed. The teeth cut.
