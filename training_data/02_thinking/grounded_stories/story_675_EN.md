Gran sat on the grey bench under the willow. The wood was old and warm from the sun.

Mei sat beside her. Dou, the grey cat, jumped up and settled on Mei's lap. She turned twice and tucked her paws in.

The willow leaves hung low over the water. Light came through them and fell on the bench in moving shapes. Small bright ovals. Dark gaps that shifted.

Mei watched the pattern travel across the wood. It moved when the leaves moved. It did not stay still.

Gran pointed at the pond. "Look at the water."

Mei looked. On the surface, the ripples made the same shapes. The same small bright ovals. The same dark gaps. The light through the leaves made one pattern on the bench. The light on the water made another. They were not the same thing, but they looked the same.

Mei looked from the bench to the water and back. Dou blinked slowly.

Gran did not say anything else. She sat still, hands resting on her knees. The willow made a soft sound above them. The water made a soft sound below.

Mei watched the patterns until she could not tell which was moving — the leaves, the ripples, or the light itself.
