The bell rang. A short, bright ring from outside the garden wall.

Emma looked up from the path. Riku was there, one foot on the ground, his red bicycle leaning against the low stones. His canvas bag sat heavy on the side, full of letters.

“Emma!” he called through the open gate. “I have something for you.”

She walked over. Biscuit followed, sniffing at the bicycle tyres.

Riku reached into his bag and pulled out a small package wrapped in brown paper. String was tied around it in a neat cross. A stamp sat in the corner.

“This came on the morning road,” he said. “It has your name on it.”

Emma held out her hands. He placed the package into them. It was light. The paper was smooth and the string was tight.

“Thank you,” she said.

Riku swung his leg back over the bicycle. He gave a short wave. “Tell Gran I said hello.”

He pushed off. The bicycle rolled down the lane, and he did not look back. Emma watched until he turned the corner and was gone.

She looked down at the package in her hands. The paper had a crease down the middle. Her name was written on the front in small, careful letters.
