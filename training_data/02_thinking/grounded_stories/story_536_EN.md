The bell rang when Emma pushed the door. A small, quick sound, then quiet.

The bakery was warm. The cold stayed on her cheeks for a moment, then began to fade. The air smelled of yeast and bread and something sweet underneath.

Hana stood behind the counter. Her apron was white with flour, and there was flour on her sleeve and a dusting of it on the wooden shelf behind her.

“Morning,” said Gran.

“Morning,” said Hana. She wiped her hands on her apron.

Gran pointed at the loaves on the shelf. The one at the front was round and brown, with a crack across the top.

Hana picked it up. She turned it in her hands, then laid it on a square of paper. She folded the paper neatly—once, twice, tucked the ends under.

Gran put the coins on the counter. Hana counted them into her palm, then opened the drawer and gave Gran a few smaller coins back. The change made a soft sound on the wood.

Hana handed the paper package across the counter. “Still warm,” she said.

Emma took it. The loaf was warm through the paper. She could feel it against her fingers.

She held it all the way to the door. The bell rang again when they left.
