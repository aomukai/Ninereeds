The market was quieter now. The morning rush had passed.

Hana stood at the baker’s stall, a loaf in her hands. It was still warm. She could feel it through the cloth.

Yun was at the stall beside her, straightening her bundles of herbs. She did not look up.

“The rosemary is good this week,” Yun said. “And the thyme.”

Hana held out the loaf. “For the herbs,” she said.

Yun looked at the loaf. Then she picked up a bundle of herbs tied with string. She handed it across the table.

Hana lifted it to her nose. She closed her eyes for a moment. The smell was sharp and green and clean.

Yun put the loaf into her basket. She tucked the cloth over the top.

No coins changed hands. No one counted anything.

Hana tied the herbs into her own cloth. She nodded once.

Yun nodded back.

Then Hana turned and walked down the street, the bundle swinging in her hand. Yun went back to straightening her table.

It was fair. That was all.
