The apple tree was full of leaves. Green and thick, like a roof.

Emma stood underneath and looked up. She could not see Dou anywhere.

“Where is she?” she said.

Mei pointed to a high fork where two branches met. “There.”

Emma squinted. At first she saw only leaves and the shape of the branch. Then Dou blinked. One eye, then the other. She was lying flat along the branch, her tail wrapped around her paws.

“She is half hidden,” Emma said.

“She likes it there,” Mei said. “No one can reach her.”

Dou looked down at them. She did not move. Her ears turned a little, but her body stayed still.

Emma watched for a long time. The leaves moved in the breeze. The shade shifted. Dou stayed exactly where she was.

“Can she see us?” Emma asked.

“Yes,” Mei said. “She sees everything.”

Dou blinked again, very slowly. Then she closed her eyes.

Emma looked at Mei. “She is not afraid up there.”

“No,” Mei said. “That is why she chose it.”
