Gran walked down to the pond. Emma followed.

The bench sat under the willow. The wood was grey. It looked older than it had in summer.

Gran stopped. She put her hand flat on the seat. It was damp.

She pulled her sleeve down over her palm and wiped the wood in a long, slow stroke. Then she wiped again.

“There,” she said. She sat down.

Emma sat beside her. The wood was still cool through her clothes. A dry brown leaf was stuck to the arm of the bench, curled at the edges.

The pond was still. The water held the sky and the dark shape of the oak on the far side. Nothing moved on the surface. Not a ripple. Not a duck.

Gran did not say anything. Emma watched the water. The air was cool and quiet.

A single leaf fell from the willow. It landed on the water and sat there without moving.

Emma looked at Gran. Gran was watching the leaf too. Her hands were folded in her lap. Her sleeve was dark where she had wiped the bench.

They sat for a long time. The light was low and yellow. The bench was damp and cold, but Emma did not want to leave yet.
