The pond was white at the edges. Frost covered every reed along the bank.

Taro knelt down. He touched a reed. It snapped in his fingers. The broken end was stiff and cold. He picked up another. That one snapped too. The reeds were frozen solid.

Biscuit pushed past him. He put one paw on the ice at the pond's edge. The ice cracked — a thin, sharp sound. Biscuit pulled his paw back. A line of white spread from where he had stepped.

Gran stood on the path. She did not come closer.

"The ice is not ready yet," she said.

Taro looked at the cracked ice. He looked at the broken reeds in his hand. The pond was still in the morning cold. Nothing moved on the water.

He dropped the reed pieces on the bank. They lay white against the dark ground. Biscuit sniffed them, then sneezed.
