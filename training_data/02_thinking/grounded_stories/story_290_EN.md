Taro held the wooden horse in both hands. The back leg had come off clean — a pale line of bare wood where the glue had let go.

Vern took the horse. He turned it over, touched the break with one finger, looked at it from the side. The workshop was quiet except for the hiss of the stove.

"It can be fixed," he said.

He set the horse on the bench. From a shelf he took a small brown bottle and a brush no wider than his thumb. He dipped the brush, touched it to the broken leg, touched it to the body. The glue smelled sharp and sweet.

Emma watched from the doorway. Biscuit had stayed outside. Bello had found a curl of wood shaving and was sniffing it.

Vern pressed the leg into place. A thin line of glue squeezed out. He wiped it with his finger.

"Crack," Taro said.

"Crack is fixed," Vern said. He picked up a clamp — two wooden jaws with a metal screw. He fitted it around the joint and turned the screw slowly. The clamp bit. The leg held.

"Tomorrow it will hold." He put the horse on the high shelf where nothing could knock it. "Come back then."

Taro looked at the horse on the shelf. Then he nodded.
