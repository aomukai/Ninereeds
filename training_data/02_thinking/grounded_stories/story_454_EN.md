The lane was warm and dusty. Riku’s red bicycle moved slowly down the hill. The front wheel turned. The back wheel left a thin line in the dust.

Dou crossed the lane.

She did not look left. She did not look right. She stepped from the wall on one side to the wall on the other side. One step. Two steps. Then she stopped.

Riku saw her. He swerved. The front wheel turned hard. The back wheel skidded. Dust puffed up from the stones.

Dou sat down.

She sat on the warm stone at the edge of the lane. Her tail was straight up. Her one white ear turned.

Riku stopped the bicycle. He put one foot down on the lane. He rang his bell once.

*Ring.*

The sound was quick and small. It went up the lane and faded.

Dou’s ear flicked.

She did not move. She sat on the stone and looked at the wheel. Then she looked at Riku. Then she looked past him, at something only she could see.

Riku waited.

Dou blinked.

Then she stood, turned, and walked back across the lane. She stepped over the dust line. She sat down on the other wall, in the shade, and began to wash her paw.

Riku pushed his bicycle forward. He did not ring the bell again.
