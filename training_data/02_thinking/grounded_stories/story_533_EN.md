The main street was full of people. Emma stepped to the side. Taro did the same. A woman with a pushchair came past, and they pressed themselves against the wall until she was through.

Mei pointed. "Hana's door is open."

Hana stood in the bakery doorway. She had a basket on her hip, full of bread. The loaves were brown and round. The smell of them reached across the street.

Stefan stood near the post box. He watched the street without moving much. His hat was dark. The sun made a small shadow under the brim.

A man stopped beside Stefan and said something. Stefan nodded. The man walked on.

Taro looked at the bread. "Can we buy one?"

"We don't have money," Emma said.

Mei was already moving. She crossed the street and stood in front of Hana. "My grandmother said to ask if you have the small loaves today."

Hana set the basket down. She lifted a cloth and took out a loaf no bigger than her hand. "This one."

Mei looked at it. She did not touch it.

The sun was high. The street was hot. The dust from the path rose in small clouds when people walked. Emma could feel the heat on her arms.

Hana put the small loaf back under the cloth. "Tell Yun I have six."

Mei nodded and came back across the street.
