Emma stood under the apple tree.

It was covered in pink and white blossom. The petals were soft, and some had fallen onto the grass like a thin layer of snow.

Gran came out of the kitchen with a basket for the herbs. She stopped beside Emma and looked up.

"Remember how it looked in winter?" Gran said.

Emma remembered. Bare branches against the grey sky. Nothing but dark wood and cold air.

"That was the same tree," Gran said. She was not looking at Emma. She was looking at the blossoms. "Now it is covered in flowers. Later there will be apples where the blossoms are now."

Emma touched a petal. It came off in her fingers. It was very light.

She thought of winter. The bare branches. The grey sky.

She thought of autumn. Apples falling. The smell of windfalls.

The same tree.

She looked at the bud beside her hand, tight and green at the base of the blossom. She did not pick it.

"I will wait," she said.

Gran did not answer. She was already bending to pick the herbs.
