Clara set a glass jar on the nature table. The water inside was green and still.

Emma leaned close. Tiny dark specks hung in the jar. Some were near the glass. Some were deep in the green.

One moved.

Emma held her breath. It flicked a small tail and went still again.

Taro came up beside her. "They look like commas," he said. "Commas with tails."

The jar sat in the light from the tall window. A bubble rose slowly from the bottom. One of the tadpoles drifted toward the glass, then vanished into the darker water.

Emma put her finger on the jar. The glass was cool. The tadpole did not come back.

Taro pressed his nose to the jar. "Where did it go?"

Clara did not answer. She was at the blackboard, writing the date in large careful letters.

Emma watched the green water. A tail flicked near the surface. Then still again.

She waited.
