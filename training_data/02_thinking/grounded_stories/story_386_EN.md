The hill was white. All the grass was gone.

Emma stopped at the bottom and looked up. The snow covered everything. There were no tracks anywhere. Not one.

“We are the first,” she said.

Taro was already walking. His boots made dark holes in the white. Behind him, a line of footprints.

Emma followed. She stepped exactly where he had stepped. The snow came over the top of her boots. Cold. Wet.

Biscuit ran past her. He did not step carefully. He bounded. Each jump punched a deep hole. The line of holes went up the hill in long leaps.

Bello came behind. His prints were smaller. Neater. He stopped and sniffed at Biscuit’s big holes. Then he ran on.

Near the top, the wind hit them. It blew the snow across the surface. The top of the hill was bare in patches. The snow had been swept away.

Emma turned and looked down. Their tracks were clear and dark against the white. Four sets of footprints. One big and leaping. One small and neat. Two walking.

The hill was very quiet. Even the wind made no sound. Just the snow moving.

Biscuit sneezed. The sound was loud in the stillness.

Taro wiped his nose on his sleeve. “Cold,” he said.

Emma looked at the sky. It was white too. Everything was white. Everything was still.
