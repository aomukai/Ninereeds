Vern pushed the plane along the board. A thin curl of wood lifted, curled over, and fell to the floor.

Taro watched it land. He bent down and picked it up.

The shaving was almost transparent. Light came through it. It felt smooth on one side and rough on the other. He turned it in his fingers.

Vern pushed again. Another curl lifted and dropped.

He did it again. And again.

The curls piled up on the floor. Some were long, some were short. Some broke when they fell. Taro picked up another. It was still warm.

He held it to his nose. It smelled of wood. Sharp and clean.

The plane made a sound like a long breath. Shush. Then the curl lifted. Shush. Another.

Taro laid the shaving on the bench. He picked up a longer one. It bent and stayed bent. He wrapped it around his finger. It held the shape.

Vern wiped the plane with his thumb and looked at the board. He did not say anything. He set the plane down and picked up a different tool.

The curls lay on the floor. The light made them look like thin paper. Taro picked up three and held them together. They were light. He put them back on the pile.
