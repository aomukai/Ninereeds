Vern’s workshop smelled of sawdust and oil. The long workbench was covered in shavings. Taro stood at one end, holding the two pieces of wood together.

Emma lined up the nail. She tapped it with the hammer.

It went in crooked.

The nail leaned to one side, half in the wood, half out. Taro looked at it. “It’s bent,” he said.

Vern reached over. He slid the claw of the hammer under the nail’s head and pulled. The nail came out with a small scrape. He held it up. The metal was curved.

“Start again,” he said.

Emma took the nail. She turned it in her fingers. The other side was straight. She put the point against the wood and held the hammer higher this time.

Tap.

The nail went in straight.

Taro held the pieces steady. Emma tapped again. The nail sank deeper. The joint held tight.

Vern picked up a second nail. “Same again,” he said.

Emma took it. She lined it up. She did not hurry.
