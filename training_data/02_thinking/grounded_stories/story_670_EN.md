The pond was very still. No wind touched the surface. It lay flat and bright, like a sheet of glass left in the sun.

Emma sat on the flat stone and looked down. The fish were deep — dark shapes far below, barely moving. They did not rise.

Taro knelt at the edge and dipped his hand in. “The top is warm,” he said. He pushed his fingers deeper. “But underneath it’s cold.”

The dogs lay under the willow. Biscuit panted with his tongue out, his sides heaving. Bello had found a patch of damp earth beside a root and pressed his belly flat against it.

A fly landed on the water. It sat without moving, its legs making tiny dents in the surface. Emma watched it. The fly did not go anywhere. The water did not ripple.

Taro pulled his hand out and let the drops fall. They hit the surface one by one, and each drop made a small ring that spread and vanished.

Biscuit got up slowly. He walked to the water, drank with long noisy laps, and lay down again in the shade.

The pond was quiet. The heat sat over everything.
