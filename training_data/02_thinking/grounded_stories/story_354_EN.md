The rain had stopped. The grass on the hill was wet and dark.

Mei walked up the slope with Yun. Dou followed a little behind, picking her way around the puddles.

At the top, Dou sat on the gate. She did not move. She watched.

Yun pointed down. "Look at the stream."

Mei looked. The stream ran fast. It was brown, not clear. It pushed against the banks and carried leaves and sticks with it.

Mei stepped closer to the edge. There was a cut in the hill — a small gully she had not seen before. The water had carved a line down the slope. The earth on either side was loose and crumbly.

"The rain did that," Yun said.

Mei crouched. She touched the edge of the gully. The soil was soft. It fell away in her fingers.

Below, the stream kept running fast.

Dou blinked from the gate. She did not seem interested in the gully. She was watching the water.

Mei looked at the cut in the hill. Then she looked at the stream. She did not say anything. She just watched.
