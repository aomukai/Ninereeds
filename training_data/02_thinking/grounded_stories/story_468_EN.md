Dou sat in the fork of the apple tree. Her grey tail hung down, still as a stone.

Bello saw her. He stopped at the trunk and looked up. Then he began to run.

He ran in circles around the apple tree, barking. His paws kicked up dead leaves. His ears bounced with every jump he tried.

Dou did not move. She blinked once.

Bello ran faster. He barked louder. He rose on his back legs and scratched at the bark. A piece of it came away. Dou watched it fall.

Taro came around the side of the cottage. He stood and watched. Biscuit came with him, sniffed once at the tree, and lay down in the grass. He knew this game.

Bello ran another circle. Then he stopped. He looked up at the fork where Dou sat. No branch came low enough. The trunk was too wide to climb. He dropped his ears.

Taro saw it on his face. The cat was out of reach. Bello knew it.

But he could not stop checking. He sat at the bottom of the trunk, looked up, and whined. Dou did not look down.

Biscuit yawned.
