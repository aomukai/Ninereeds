Mei’s hands were careful. She picked up a small brown paper bag and held it open. Emma dropped the plums in one by one. They were dark purple and soft at the ends.

The bag was full. Not too full.

Mei carried it to the brass scale on the stall table. She set the bag on the metal dish. The arm swung up, then dropped. Down. Too far down.

Mei watched the arm. She did not say anything.

She lifted the bag off the scale and opened it again. Her hand went in and came out with three plums. She set them on the wooden counter. One, two, three. They were the fattest ones in the bag.

She put the bag back on the scale. The arm rose. It hung level.

“That is the right weight,” she said.

She twisted the top of the bag closed and handed it to Emma. The paper crinkled. The plums were heavy at the bottom.

Taro looked at the three plums on the counter. “What happens to those?”

Mei picked them up. She dropped them into the basket on the ground. They landed with a soft thump.

“They go back,” she said.
