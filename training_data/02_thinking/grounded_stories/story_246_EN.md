Mei stood by the mill wall. Her hand rested on a metal lever.

"This is the sluice," she said.

Emma looked. The lever was grey and smooth. It stuck up from a slot in the stone.

Mei pulled it up.

Underneath the building, something creaked. Then water began to move — a sound like a long drink being taken. It rushed into the stone channel beneath the mill floor.

The great wheel outside began to turn.

Slow at first. A wooden groaning, a drip, a pause. Then faster. Water slapped against the paddles. The wheel gathered speed until it was a steady, rumbling blur. Emma felt the vibration through her shoes.

"It sounds like a storm," she said.

Mei nodded. She put her hand on the lever again. This time she pushed it down.

The sound changed. The water in the channel thinned, then stopped. The wheel slowed. One paddle at a time, it came to a halt. A last drip fell into the stream below.

The mill was quiet again.

Mei looked at Emma. "Pull it up," she said.

Emma put both hands on the lever. She pulled. The water answered.
