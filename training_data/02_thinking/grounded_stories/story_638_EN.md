The rain had stopped.

Emma stood at the garden door. The air was soft and wet. Every leaf was covered in drops.

She stepped out onto the path. The stones were dark and shiny. Water ran in a thin line along the edge of the vegetable patch.

Mei came through the gate. She stopped and looked at the ground.

"There," she said.

A worm lay on the path. It was long and pink and still. It did not move.

Mei bent down. She put her fingers under the worm, very gently. It curled a little, but it did not pull away.

She carried it to the flower bed and laid it on the damp soil. The worm sank into the earth and was gone.

Emma watched. "It went in," she said.

"It wanted to," said Mei.

Gran came out of the kitchen. She had a small cloth in her hands. She walked to the herb trough and shook the cloth over it. Drops flew off the rosemary and the thyme. The leaves were clean and bright.

She touched one of the bean leaves. A drop ran down her finger.

"Good rain," she said. "The garden needed it."

The earth smelled dark and full.
