Gran cracked the egg one-handed against the bowl’s edge. The yolk slid into Biscuit’s dish. He licked it up before the shell hit the counter.  

"One for Biscuit," Gran said, reaching for the next egg. "Three for the cake. Four take one is three."  

Taro peered into the mixing bowl. "One, two, three. Yes, still enough."  

Emma pressed her finger to the batter. It clung in sticky peaks. "Will it rise?"  

"Like Biscuit’s ears when he hears the gate," Gran said. The third egg slipped into the bowl.  

Biscuit nosed his empty dish across the floor. It clattered against the cupboard.  

"Not another," Taro said, but Gran was already scraping batter into the pan. The smell of warm sugar filled the kitchen. Biscuit sighed and flopped down by the hearth, one ear twitching at the sound of the spoon.
