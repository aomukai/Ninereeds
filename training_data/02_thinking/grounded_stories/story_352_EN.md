Gran stopped at the top of the hill. Emma stood beside her, catching her breath.

Owen was already at the gate. He leaned on it with both arms and nodded when they came close.

"You can see a long way from here," he said.

Emma looked down the slope. The field below was golden with autumn grass. At the far edge, near the trees, the oak stood dark and still. From the hill it was not a tree with branches and leaves — it was a shape, solid and black against the light.

"Like a shadow," Emma said.

Gran nodded. "From up here, everything looks different."

The sheep moved slowly across the slope below them. Their backs were grey in the afternoon light. One lifted its head and looked at the hilltop, then put its head down again.

Owen turned and looked at the same oak. "That tree has been there longer than any of us."

Emma watched the dark shape at the field's edge. It did not move. The grass around it moved, but the oak stayed still.

Gran put her hand on Emma's shoulder. "Time to go down," she said.

Owen lifted his hand in a small wave. Emma followed Gran back down the hill.
