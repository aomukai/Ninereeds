Taro walked along the meadow path. The grass was tall and brown. The air smelled of dry leaves.

Owen was at the gate. He leaned on the top bar with his arms crossed. His flat cap was pulled low. He did not turn when Taro came close.

Taro stopped. Biscuit sat down on the path beside him.

Owen lifted one hand and pointed. He did not say anything. His finger aimed at the far end of the field.

Taro looked. The sheep were bunched under a tree. They were very small from here — small as stones. One moved. Another followed.

Owen said, "They know rain is coming."

Taro looked at the sky. It was grey. He could not tell if rain was coming.

Biscuit looked where Owen had pointed. His ears were forward. He did not bark. He just sat and watched the small shapes in the distance.

Owen shifted his weight on the gate. The wood creaked. He did not say anything else.

Taro stood beside the fence post. He looked at the sheep. Then he looked back along the path toward Gran's chimney. The field was wide. The sheep were far. Biscuit sat very still.
