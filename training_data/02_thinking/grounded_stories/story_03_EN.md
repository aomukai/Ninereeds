The snow was fresh and white, muffling every sound. Emma’s boots crunched as she stepped closer to the oak. The branches were bare except for a ridge of frost along the low one, thick enough to sit on in summer.  

Taro pointed. "Look—tracks." Tiny footprints led to the base of the tree, then vanished near the squirrel’s hole. The opening was half-buried under snow.  

Biscuit circled the roots, nose pressed to the ground. His breath came out in little puffs. He sniffed at the hole, then sneezed.  

Emma crouched, brushing snow from the bark. It was rough under her mittens, colder than she expected. "Where’d he go?"  

Taro peered up. The higher branches were empty. The whole world felt quiet.  

Biscuit pawed at the snow, sending up a small cloud. He whined, then trotted off toward the meadow path, leaving his own tracks behind.
