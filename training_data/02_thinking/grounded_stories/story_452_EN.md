The apple fell.

It hit a branch on the way down, then another, then the ground. It landed in the grass a few feet from Biscuit.

Biscuit lifted his head. He walked over and put his nose to the brown spot on the side. He sniffed once. He sniffed again. The smell was sweet and sharp, the way apples get when they have been on the ground for a while.

He turned his head and walked away. He did not bite it.

Dou watched from the fork of the apple tree. She sat on a branch low enough to see everything, high enough to be left alone. Her tail hung down, still. She watched Biscuit pass beneath her. She watched him settle in the grass near the garden wall, ten feet from the fallen apple, and put his chin on his paws.

Dou did not climb down.

The wind moved the leaves. Another apple shifted in the branch above her. It did not fall.

Dou blinked once and stayed where she was.
