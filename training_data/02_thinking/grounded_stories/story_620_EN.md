The garden was very hot.

The sun sat high and white. The bean leaves hung down. They looked tired. Emma touched one. It was soft and warm, not crisp like a healthy leaf.

Gran came out of the kitchen. She carried a blue bucket. Water slopped over the edge and made dark spots on the stone path.

“The beans need a drink,” she said. She set the bucket down by the wigwam of canes.

Emma put her hand in. The water was cold. She cupped her palm and poured it at the base of the nearest cane. The dry earth drank it fast. The dark patch spread and then began to lighten at the edges as the sun pulled at it.

Mei took the other side. She poured slowly, watching the water sink. Steam rose from the soil in a thin, faint curl — the heat meeting the cold.

They took turns. Emma poured. Mei poured. The bucket grew lighter. The last of the water went in with a soft sound — a gasp from the earth.

Gran picked up the empty bucket. “It will take more than one,” she said. She went back inside.

The garden was still hot. But the bean leaves did not droop quite so low.
