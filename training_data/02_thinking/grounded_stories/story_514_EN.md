Clara put the paper on the table.

“Leaves,” she said. “Flat leaves. Dry leaves.”

She showed them how to press them first. Emma put a leaf inside a book and closed the cover. She put her hand on top and pushed. Taro used two books. He stacked them and pressed down hard.

Mei chose her leaf carefully. She laid it between the pages and left it.

On Tuesday, the leaves were flat and still.

Clara brought out the paste. She showed them how to spread it thin.

Emma glued her leaf onto the paper. It lay flat and stayed.

Taro put paste on the whole page and stuck everything at once. His fingers were white and sticky.

Mei held her leaf in place with one finger. She counted slowly. Then she let go. The leaf did not move.

On Wednesday they added stems and seeds. Small things. They glued them on top.

The paper sat on the corner of the nature table. It stayed there all day. And the next day. And the next.

On the fourth day Clara moved it to the windowsill. The light came through the paper where the paste was thin.

Mei looked at it from the side. The layers showed, one on top of another.

“It took four days,” she said.

Emma nodded. “You cannot hurry a leaf.”
