The rain fell straight and steady. It made a sound on the lane stones — not loud, but constant.

Emma held her umbrella low against the wind. Water dripped from the edge. Her boots were dark with wet. The ditch beside the lane was full — brown water running fast, carrying a stick and a dead leaf.

Owen was at the gate. He leaned on it with both arms. His cap was dark with rain. Water ran off the brim in a thin stream. He did not move. He stood and watched the field.

The sheep were under the hedge. They stood close together, backs to the rain. One shook itself. Another stood still as stone.

Emma stopped. Owen did not turn. He looked at the sheep and the wet grass and the sky.

She waited. The rain fell on her umbrella. It fell on Owen's cap. It fell into the ditch and the ditch carried it away.

Owen nodded. Just once. Not at her — at the field, at the rain, at the way things were.

Emma walked on. Behind her, the gate stayed where it was. Owen stayed where he was. The water ran.
