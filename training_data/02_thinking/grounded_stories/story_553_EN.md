The lane was still dark. Gran held Emma’s hand. Their breath made little clouds in the cold air.

A light was already on inside the bakery. It spilled through the window onto the street.

“She’s early,” Gran said.

Emma pressed her nose against the glass. Hana was inside. Her apron was white with flour. Her hands moved fast.

She lined the loaves up in the window. Big ones at the back. Small ones at the front. Each one in its place.

The smell reached the street before the door opened. Warm. Round. It made Emma’s stomach feel hollow.

Hana pushed the door. “Come in, come in. It’s cold out there.”

Inside, the air was thick and warm. Emma stood by the counter. The flour was on Hana’s sleeves and on her cheek and in her hair.

Hana picked up a loaf from the back row. She held it out.

“This one,” she said. “Still warm.”

Emma took it. The crust was crisp. The bottom was soft. She held it in both hands and the warmth went through her gloves.

Gran paid. Hana wiped her hands on her apron and smiled.

Outside, the street was still dark. But the bakery window glowed yellow behind them all the way home.
