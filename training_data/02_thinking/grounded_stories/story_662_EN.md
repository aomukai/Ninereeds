Gran pulled the door. The hinge made a sound — a long, slow creak that Taro tried to copy with his mouth.

Inside was dark. The air smelled of earth and metal and old wood, all mixed together. Emma blinked. Her eyes got used to the dark slowly.

Tools hung on hooks along the wall. A spade. A fork. A trowel. They were different sizes. They hung in order — big ones at the back, small ones near the door.

A cobweb stretched from the handle of the fork to the wall. It was thin and silver. It moved a little when Gran reached past.

She did not knock the cobweb down. Her hand went past it, past the spade, past the fork. She found the basket where it always was — on the hook nearest the door.

She lifted it down. The basket was old and the wood was smooth where hands had held it.

Taro leaned in and looked at the trowel. Its handle was dark. Its blade was narrow. He did not touch it.

Emma looked at the cobweb. One end was on the fork. The other end was on the wall. It connected them.
