The baker’s smelled of warm bread and sugar. Emma stood on tiptoe to see over the counter. A woman in a blue coat was first in line, handing coins to the baker. Behind her, an old man shifted his weight from foot to foot.  

Taro pointed. "One, two. We are third."  

Gran adjusted her basket. "First, then the old man, then us."  

Emma watched the woman take her loaf and step aside. The old man moved forward. "Now there’s two people before us," she said.  

The baker wrapped a roll in paper. The old man counted his change carefully.  

Taro rocked on his heels. "One now. We’re second."  

The bell jingled as the old man left. Gran nudged them forward.  

"First," Taro whispered.  

The baker wiped flour from his hands. "What’ll it be?"  

Gran ordered. The bread went into her basket, still warm.  

Outside, the village street was quiet. Emma looked back at the empty queue. The next customer was already stepping inside.
