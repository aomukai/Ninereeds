Clara set the basket on the nature table.

Six apples. Red and round.

She gave two to Emma. She gave two to Taro.

"Two are left," said Emma.

Mei counted on her fingers. "Six take away four. Two."

Clara picked up one of the remaining apples. She took a knife from the drawer and cut it straight through the middle.

Two halves. Pale flesh. Wet in the middle.

"How many pieces now?" Clara asked.

Taro counted. "Two apples and two halves."

"Three pieces," Mei said. She pointed. "The whole apple is gone. Now there are two halves."

Emma looked at the table. Two whole apples. Two half apples. She touched one half. The cut side was smooth and damp.

"That is still two apples," she said slowly. "But the pieces are different."

Clara nodded. She put the two halves together on the table. They made one apple again.

"Same amount," Mei said. "Just cut."

Taro picked up a half. "Can we eat them?"

Clara smiled. "After arithmetic."
