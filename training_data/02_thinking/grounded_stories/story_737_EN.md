Vern's workshop was warm. The stove ticked in the corner. Sawdust covered the floor like dry snow.

Mei stood at the workbench. Two pieces of wood lay on the flat surface. One was a square. One was a triangle.

Vern picked up the square. He picked up the triangle. He turned the triangle and slid it against the square's edge.

The triangle fit. There was no gap. The two pieces made one shape.

"See that," Vern said. Not a question.

Mei leaned closer. She traced the line where they met with her finger — straight, tight, no space between.

She picked up the pieces. She turned them in her hands. She slid the triangle against the square again. It fit the same way. The edge of the triangle matched the edge of the square exactly.

She set them down on the bench. Square and triangle together, one form.

"Turn them," Vern said.

She turned the whole shape. The line where they joined was still there, but the shape itself was new — bigger than either piece alone.

Mei looked at the two pieces. She looked at the shape they made together. She did not say anything. She fitted them apart and together one more time.
