The teacher tapped the chalk against the board. The sound made Emma look up. The board was dark and smooth, and the chalk left a white mark. The teacher wrote a number: 2. Then she wrote another: 2. She turned to the class.

“Emma,” she said. “What’s the answer?”

Emma hesitated. She looked at the numbers. “Two plus two is four,” she said.

The teacher nodded. “Correct,” she said. “Two plus two equals four.” She picked up a slate from the desk. The slate was small and grey, with a wooden frame. She wrote on it with chalk: 2+2=4. The numbers were neat and sharp.

Emma watched. The teacher held the slate up. “See?” she said. “It’s the same.”

Emma nodded. She looked at the slate, then at the board. The chalk smelled dusty. The teacher wiped the board clean with a cloth, and the numbers disappeared.

“Next,” the teacher said, and wrote another number. Emma leaned forward, her hands on the edge of her desk.
