They had been in the sun for an hour. The path to the mill was white and hard and the air did not move.

Mei pushed the mill door. It was heavy, and the cool came out like a hand.

Inside, the air was still and thick. Flour dust hung in the light from the window — fine, slow, never settling. The stones were silent. The wheel was stopped.

Mei sat down on the stone floor. She leaned her back against the wall and closed her eyes.

Taro wiped his forehead with his sleeve. His sleeve came away white. He looked at it, then sat down beside her.

Emma found the water jar on the shelf near the hopper. She lifted it with both hands. The water was not cold, but it was wet. She drank, then passed the jar.

Taro drank. Mei drank.

No one spoke.

On the stone floor, the heat of the walk began to leave them. The dust hung in the air. The light through the window was yellow and still.

Taro put the jar down between his feet. He did not get up.

Mei breathed slowly.

Outside, the sun was on the path and the field and the mill roof. But inside, the stone held the quiet.
