Dou climbed the apple tree.

She went up trunk by small jump, her grey body moving between branches. At the fork where the tree divided, she stopped. She turned once, twice, and settled. Her tail wrapped around her front paws. Her white ear caught the light.

Emma stood below. She picked up a leaf from the grass and held it up.

"Dou," she said.

Dou looked down. She did not move.

Bello came running. He stopped at the base of the tree and sniffed the bark where Dou had climbed. He circled the trunk once. He circled again. His nose went up the tree.

He looked up at Dou.

Dou looked back.

Bello sat. Then he lay down in the grass. He put his chin on his paws. His ears stayed up, pointing at the tree.

Emma stood still. The leaf was still in her hand.

Above, Dou settled deeper into the fork. Her eyes were half closed. The small green leaves moved around her.

Bello did not move.

Emma watched Dou. She watched Bello. No one spoke. The tree held them all.
