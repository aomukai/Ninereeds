The market was busy. Gran stopped at Yun’s stall.

Daffodils stood in a bucket. Their yellow was bright against the grey of the street. Gran picked a bunch up. She held them away from her face and looked at them. The stems were straight. The heads were just open.

“These,” she said.

Yun nodded. She took the bunch and laid it flat on the stall table. She folded a sheet of paper around the stems. The paper was brown and thin. She tied it with a piece of string. One loop, then another. A small knot.

Gran put a coin on the table. Yun picked it up and dropped it into her apron pocket without looking.

Gran handed the flowers to Emma.

“Carry them carefully,” she said.

Emma took them. The paper was cool. The stems were damp through the paper. She held the bunch against her chest with both hands.

They walked up the lane. The smell came with her — not strong, but clean and green. She lifted the bunch once and put her nose close. The yellow was very yellow.

At the garden gate, she looked down at her hands. The paper was dry now. But the smell was still there, on her fingers, like something she had kept.
