The nature table looked different. The jar was gone. In summer it had held tadpoles, small and dark, flicking through the water. Now there was nothing that moved.

Emma stood in front of the table. A bare branch lay on the cloth. It was grey and dry. Beside it sat a pine cone, its scales tight and closed. Next to the cone, a single white feather lay perfectly still.

She touched the branch with one finger. It was cold. It did not bend. It felt like something that had been dead a long time.

Mei came to stand beside her. She looked at the table without speaking.

The window above the table was covered in frost. It had drawn ferns and leaves on the glass — white shapes that did not grow anywhere. The light through them was thin and cold.

Emma picked up the feather. It was soft and very light. She put it down again. It did not move.

"Nothing is alive," Mei said quietly.

Emma nodded. She looked at the branch, the cone, the feather. They were all still. The frost ferns did not change. The winter light did not warm them.

She turned away from the table. Behind her, the things sat exactly as they had been.
