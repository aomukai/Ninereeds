Mei walked down the meadow path. Dou followed behind her, tail straight up, one white ear turned forward.

At the pond, Biscuit was already in. He splashed through the shallow water, sending ripples across the still surface. The ducks lifted from the reeds and settled further out.

Dou stopped at the edge. She sat down on the flat stone, very still.

She reached one paw out. The tip of it touched the water.

For a moment she held it there. Then she pulled it back. She shook it once, a quick flick. A single drop landed on the stone.

She did not put the paw in again. She sat where she was, dry, and watched the ripples spread.

Taro stood beside the willow bench. He watched Dou. He watched Biscuit, who was now chasing something in the reeds, splashing without care.

"The cat is smarter than the dog," Taro said.

Mei did not answer. She was watching Dou watch the water.

Dou blinked slowly. Then she stood, turned, and walked back along the path, her tail still straight up, her paw already dry.
