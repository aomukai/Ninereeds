The market street was quiet now. The other stalls had packed up. Only Yun's stall still had vegetables on the table.

A woman stopped in front of the stall. She pointed at the turnips.

Yun picked up a paper bag. She scooped turnips into it, one, two, three, four. Then she set the bag on the small brass scale. The arm moved. She added one more turnip. The arm balanced.

Mei stood beside the table. She held a cloth sack open with both hands.

Yun tipped the turnips into the sack. The woman reached into her pocket and pulled out coins. Three silver ones. One copper.

Yun took the coins. She turned them over in her palm. Then she reached into her apron pocket and brought out a small copper coin. She placed it in the woman's hand.

The woman nodded. She took the sack and walked away down the street.

Mei looked at the table. A few carrots. Two onions. A handful of herbs in a twist of paper.

"Nearly empty," she said.

Yun nodded. She began to fold the cloth that covered the table. Mei picked up the carrots and put them in the basket.

Gran came down the lane. She carried her own basket.

"Late?" she said.

"Last customer," said Yun. She tied the cloth into a bundle.

Gran looked at the table. "Turnips?"

"All gone," said Mei.

Yun lifted the basket. Mei picked up the folded cloth. They walked home together, the three of them, down the quiet street.
