Gran lifted the lamp. The flame sat still inside the glass.

“Fetch carrots,” she said. “The ones at the bottom of the box. You know where.”

Emma took the lamp. The metal handle was warm where Gran’s hand had been.

She pushed the cellar door open with her shoulder. It was heavy. She propped it with the iron bar the way Gran did. The five stone steps went down into dark.

She put her foot on the first one. Her weight settled. The stone was worn in the middle.

One.

The second step. The lamp cast her shadow long ahead of her.

Two.

Three. The air got cooler. It smelled of earth and apples and something sweet that was turning.

Four. She could see the floor now. Flat stone, swept clean.

Five. Her foot reached the bottom. She stood on the earth floor.

The carrot box was against the far wall. She held the lamp low and looked inside.

Nine carrots. She counted them with her finger. Nine.

She took two. The earth on them was damp. She put them in her pocket and climbed back up, one, two, three, four, five.
