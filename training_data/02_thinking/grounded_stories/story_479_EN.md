The shed door was open a crack. Dou sat on the top step, her back to the yard. Her tail curved up, very still.

Biscuit came around the corner. His nose was low, following a scent across the stones. He did not look up.

Emma watched from the garden gate. Biscuit walked right past the shed step. His tail wagged once, loosely, as if he had nothing to prove.

Dou did not turn her head. Her ears did not move. She sat exactly as she had been sitting, one paw tucked, looking out at the bare apple tree.

Bello trotted up behind Biscuit. He stopped when he saw Dou. His ears went up. He took one step toward the step.

Biscuit did not stop. He kept walking, nose down, past the shed, past the compost corner, toward the back wall.

Bello looked at Dou. Dou did not look at Bello.

He sniffed the air once, quickly. Then he turned and followed Biscuit.

Emma stood at the gate. The cat sat on the step. The dogs disappeared around the corner.

Dou blinked slowly. She did not turn around.
