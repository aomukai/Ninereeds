The climbing frame stood in the corner of the schoolyard. The bars were silver-grey and cold under their hands.

Taro went up first. He put his foot on the lowest bar, then the next, then the next. His hands found the top bar and he pulled himself up. He sat on the top, legs swinging.

Emma stopped at the middle bar. She wrapped her fingers around it and let her feet leave the ground. She hung. Her arms felt long and heavy. She swung her legs forward and back, forward and back. The bar was smooth and hard in her palms.

Mei stood at the bottom and looked up. She put one hand on the second rung and squeezed. She tested her grip. Then she climbed — not fast, not slow. She stopped at the fourth bar and held on.

Taro looked down. The mat was below him, thick and black. He let go. He dropped. His feet hit the mat and his knees bent. The ground shook. Emma felt it through her hands on the bar — a small thump that travelled up through the frame.

Mei held tighter. She did not drop.

Emma let herself down, one rung at a time. Her palms were red.
