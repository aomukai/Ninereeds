Taro patted his pockets. His scarf was gone.  

He had it at the gate—he remembered tying it tight before stepping outside. He ran back. The gate stood open, the scarf nowhere on the path or the latch.  

The bench, then. Gran had been sitting there earlier, watching him play. He hurried across the garden, kicking through frost-stiff grass. The bench was bare except for a few curled leaves.  

He stood still, breathing hard. The cold bit his neck. Then—oh! The meadow path. He’d taken the scarf off there, just past the gate, because the sun had warmed his back.  

He sprinted, skidding on frozen dirt. And there it was, looped over a low branch where he’d left it, the red wool bright against the winter grey. He grabbed it, still warm from the sun, and wound it back around his neck.  

Gran called from the garden. “Found it?”  

“Yeah,” he shouted, already running to tell her where.
