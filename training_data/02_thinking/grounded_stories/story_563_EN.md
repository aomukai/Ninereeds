The market stall was already set up when Gran and Taro arrived. A long wooden table with a green cloth over it, weighted at the corners with small stones. Vegetables were arranged in rows: beans in piles, carrots with their tops still on, tomatoes in a wooden bowl.

Yun stood behind the table. She put a handful of beans into a small cloth bag and set it on the brass scale. The little pan dipped and rose. She added one more bean. Then another. The scale balanced.

Mei stood beside her grandmother, watching the needle settle. She did not say anything. She reached under the table and brought up a paper bag folded flat.

Gran stopped in front of the stall.

"Morning, Yun."

"Morning, Gran." Yun picked up a bunch of carrots and held them out. "These are good today."

Gran took them. She turned them in her hands. The tops were fresh, the orange bright. She put them into her basket.

Taro looked at the tomatoes. They were red and round and sat in their bowl like something waiting.

Mei picked up a paper bag and held it open.

Yun scooped beans from the pile into the bag. She folded the top once, twice. She held out her other hand for the coins Gran was already counting out. She did not look at the money. She dropped it into the apron pocket at her waist.

Mei took the bag of beans and placed it carefully in Gran's basket, on top of the carrots.
