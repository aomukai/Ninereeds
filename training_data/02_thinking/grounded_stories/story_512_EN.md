Mei stood at the green gate. She did not open it yet. Her fingers rested on the wood, and she looked at the yard on the other side.

Emma was on the climbing frame. She saw Mei and stopped with one foot on the rung.

The yard was empty except for Emma. The climbing frame was metal and cold. The bench along the wall was empty. The big ash tree dropped leaves that skittered across the stones.

Mei did not move.

Then the school door opened. Clara came to the door and said Mei's name. She said it the way you say a name you already know.

Mei pushed the gate. It was stiff. She pushed harder and it swung open, and she stepped through into the yard.

Emma climbed down from the frame and stood on the ground. She looked at Mei. Mei looked back. No one said anything yet.

Clara held the door open. Mei crossed the yard, and her shoes made a different sound on the stones than they had on the lane.

She reached the door. She stepped over the threshold into the classroom.

The floorboard under her foot creaked. It was a loud creak, low and long, and it stopped when she put her weight on the next board.

Emma followed her inside. The door closed behind them both.
