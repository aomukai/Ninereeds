The kitchen was warm. Emma stood by the table. Her hands were warm. Her cheeks were warm.

Gran lifted the iron bar and pulled the cellar door open. It was heavy. It creaked.

A wave of cool air came up the steps. It hit Emma’s face. It was not like the air in the kitchen. It was still. It smelled of earth and apples.

“Feel that?” Gran said.

Emma stepped down. The stone steps were cold under her feet. The air on her skin was different — drier, quieter. She stood still. The cold settled around her like a slow thing.

She came back up. The kitchen air hit her. It felt hot now. Hotter than before.

Gran closed the door. The iron bar went back into place.

Emma touched her own arm. It was cool where the cellar air had touched it. The kitchen was warm around her.

She stood between the two feelings for a moment. Then she went to the table.
