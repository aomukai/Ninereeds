The bicycle came to a stop beside the stall. Riku leaned it against the table leg, careful not to tip the baskets.

He pulled a small parcel from his canvas bag. Brown paper, tied with string. A label on top with Yun's name written in neat letters.

"For you," he said.

Yun took it. She turned it over in her hands. The paper crackled. She set it on the table and reached for her scissors.

Mei watched. The scissors were small and silver. Yun slid them under the string and cut. Once. Twice. The string fell away in two pieces.

She unfolded the paper. Inside were bundles of dried herbs, tied with their own thin threads. The smell came out all at once — sharp and green and warm.

"From the miller," Yun said. "He grows them by the stream."

Mei picked up one bundle. The leaves were crumbly and light. She arranged them on the table in a row, between the jars of honey and the paper bags of flour.

Yun folded the brown paper flat and tucked it under the table. She handed the scissors back to Riku.

"Thank him for me," she said.

Riku nodded. He swung his leg over the bicycle and pushed off, the bag settling against his hip. The sound of the wheels faded down the street.
