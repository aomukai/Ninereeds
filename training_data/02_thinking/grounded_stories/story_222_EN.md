The wooden hopper stood at the top of the millstone. It was shaped like a funnel—wide at the top, narrow at the bottom.

Yun lifted the sack. Grain poured into the hopper with a sound like heavy rain on a roof.

"Watch now," she said.

Mei stood close to the hopper. Emma stood beside her. They both watched the grain fall.

At the bottom of the hopper, a narrow opening let the grain through. It fell between the two millstones—one that stayed still, one that turned. The turning stone was heavy and slow. It pressed against the grain and ground it.

Emma could feel the grinding through the floor, low and steady.

"It is coming," Mei said.

She pointed to the space below the stones. A fine dust began to fall. White and soft, it landed in the cloth sack that Yun had placed at the bottom.

The flour came faster now. It fell in a steady stream, warm to the touch when Emma put her hand near it.

Yun lifted the edge of the sack. "Feel," she said.

Emma put her hand in. The flour was warm and very fine. It coated her fingers in soft white.

Mei put her hand in too. She smiled without looking up.

The grain went in at the top. The flour came out at the bottom. And between them, the stones turned.
