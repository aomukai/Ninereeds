Taro stopped halfway up the hill. The slope stretched above him, pale green and dotted with white.

Owen's sheep moved across it like clouds — a few steps, then stop. Heads down, eating. Then a few steps more.

Owen's dog ran around them in a wide curve. He did not bark. He just ran, and the sheep shifted a little. The dog ran again, and the sheep shifted again. They did not hurry. They did not seem to notice the dog at all.

Biscuit sat beside Taro and watched. His ears were up. His tail did not wag. He was watching the way he watched the pond when something moved under the surface.

Bello did not sit. He stood with his front paws planted, his ears forward, his tail stiff. A small whine came out of him.

"They're not going anywhere fast," Taro said.

Bello whined again.

The sheep stopped. One of them lifted its head and looked at nothing. Then it put its head down and ate.

The dog ran again. The sheep moved a few steps. Then they stopped.

Taro watched. The grass was short and dry. The wind came over the top of the hill and moved through his hair.

"Come on," he said.

He started walking up the slope. Biscuit followed. Bello followed, still watching, still whining a little.

The sheep did not look up.
