Vern held up a flat piece of wood. It was a square. Four sides, four corners, all the same.

He put it on the workbench. Then he picked up a small saw. He cut the square from one corner to the other.

Now there were two triangles.

Taro looked at them. "You cut it."

Vern nodded. He put the triangles side by side, the long sides together. They made the same square again.

Then he picked up one triangle. He turned it around. He fitted the long side of one against the long side of the other, but not the same way.

The shape was different now. Long. Pointed at both ends. Like a diamond, but stretched.

"What shape is that?" Taro asked.

"Two triangles can make more than one thing," Vern said. He pushed the pieces together. They fit. The edges matched.

Taro put his finger on the join. "They still fit."

"They do," Vern said. "Same pieces. Different shape."

He left them on the bench. Taro moved one triangle a little, then pushed it back into place. The long diamond sat there, made of two triangles that used to be one square.
