The henhouse door was closed. Bello put his nose to the crack between the wood and the frame. He sniffed once, then again.

From inside, a hen clucked. It was a low sound, round and soft.

Bello jumped back. Not far — just one quick step — but his ears went up and his front paws landed wide.

He stood still. Nothing else happened. The hen did not cluck again.

Bello walked to the other side of the henhouse, where the compost pile steamed in the morning air. He sniffed there too — warm, dark, full of old things. A feather lay on the ground near the pile. He put his nose to it but did not pick it up.

Then he came back to the door. He sat down in front of it. His tail lay flat on the ground.

Gran came out of the kitchen with a bowl in her hands. She stopped when she saw him.

"You are watching the door," she said.

Bello did not turn around. He kept looking at the crack where the hen had spoken.

Gran went to the henhouse and lifted the latch. The door swung open. Inside, the straw was warm and the hens moved their feet.

Bello did not go in. He sat where he was and watched.
