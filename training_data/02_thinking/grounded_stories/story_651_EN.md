The apple tree was pink and white. Every branch, every twig, every place where a flower could be — it was full.

Emma stood underneath and looked up. The blossoms were so close together that she could not see the sky through them.

A bee moved from flower to flower. It went in, came out, went in again. Its legs were dusted yellow. The buzz was low and steady, like something humming to itself.

Mei stood a little apart. She held out her hand and a single petal fell into her palm. It was pale pink at the edge, white at the centre, soft as cloth. She turned it over once.

The ground below the tree was covered. Petals lay flat on the earth, scattered like small confetti. Some had landed on the grass. Some had drifted against the stone bench where Dou sat watching.

Gran came out of the kitchen door. She did not say anything. She stood beside the tree and touched a low branch very gently. A few petals came loose and fell straight down.

Emma picked one up from the ground. It smelled like nothing much — just a little bit green, a little bit damp.

The bee went on buzzing.
