The hill was long and the grass was short. Emma climbed until the cottage looked very small behind her. Taro was ahead, already at the top.

When she reached him, the air was still. The whole valley lay below — the village, the lane, the oak at the field's edge.

Emma heard it first. A faint sound, thin but clear.

"The school bell," she said.

Taro listened. "It sounds far away."

It was the same bell they heard from the meadow path on still mornings. Up here, it was softer. But every ring was separate and sharp.

Then another sound came up from the village. A dog barked. Biscuit pricked his ears. He turned his head, listening. He had heard it too.

Then a voice. Someone calling. Emma could not make out the words, only the shape of them rising.

"Everything sounds small up here," Taro said.

The wind was not blowing. The sounds travelled straight up the hill, each one arriving alone and clear.

Biscuit sat down and kept his ears forward. He was still listening when the next sound came.
