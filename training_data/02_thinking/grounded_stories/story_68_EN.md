The lane was cold. Emma held Gran’s hand and walked slowly, her boots scuffing the stones. The hedges were thin now, letting the wind through. Halfway down, the puddle dip had frozen at the edges.  

The waiting room was quiet. A fish tank stood on the shelf, its light glowing blue. Three orange fish drifted in circles, their mouths opening and closing. Emma sat in the nearest chair. Gran picked up a magazine but didn’t open it.  

“Emma?” called a voice from the corridor.  

The doctor’s hands were cold. He pressed the stethoscope to her back. “Hmm,” he said. Then, “Breathe in.” The paper on the table crinkled under her.  

Outside, the lane was just as cold, but the fish were still swimming. Gran buttoned Emma’s coat up to the top. “Home for soup,” she said. The wind pushed them up the hill.
