The mill wheel turned slowly. The sound was low and steady — a deep, wet grinding that you felt in your chest before you heard it with your ears.

Biscuit stood at the edge of the stone channel. The water ran past, clear and quick, from under the wheel. He put his nose close. He drank.

The wheel turned above him. Drops fell from the paddles. Some landed on Biscuit's back. He did not move. He drank again, tongue curling into the cold water.

Emma stood on the bank and watched. The mill was quiet except for the wheel. The stream made a different sound — softer, running away from the building.

Biscuit lifted his head. His ears changed position. One went forward. One went sideways. He listened to something Emma could not hear.

The wheel kept turning. Drops kept falling.

Biscuit stepped out of the channel onto the bank. He shook — ears flapping, water flying from his coat. Then he went to stand beside Emma's leg. He sat down, wet and warm against her knee.

She put her hand on his head. His ear was damp.

The wheel turned. The stream ran. Everything was slow and quiet.
