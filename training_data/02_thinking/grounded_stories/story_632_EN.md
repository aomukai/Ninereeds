Dou walked along the top of the garden wall. Her tail was very straight behind her. She did not look down.

She stopped where the sun touched the stone. The wall was warm there. Dou folded her feet under herself and sat very neatly. She looked into the garden.

Biscuit was sniffing at the base of the apple tree. He had found something interesting—a smell, maybe, or a place where a beetle had crossed. His tail wagged slowly.

Bello saw Dou first. He stopped in the middle of the path. His ears went up like two small flags. He barked once.

Dou did not move.

She blinked slowly. Then she lifted one paw and began to wash it. She touched the back of her ear, then the place beside her nose. She was very thorough.

Mei sat on the stone bench near the kitchen door. The sun was warm on her shoulders. She watched Dou watch the garden.

Bello barked again. Biscuit looked up from the apple tree, looked at Bello, looked at the wall, and went back to sniffing.

Dou finished washing her paw. She sat very still. She looked at the garden as if she owned it.

Mei did not call her down. She just sat on the bench and watched.
