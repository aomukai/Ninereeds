Emma stood on tiptoe to see the cake. Eight candles flickered in the kitchen’s dim light. Taro poked his finger toward them.  

"One, two—"  

Gran swatted his hand away. "Wait."  

The icing was thick and white, with tiny sugar flowers along the edge. A red ribbon curled around the base. Emma took a deep breath. She blew.  

Seven flames vanished. One wobbled but stayed lit.  

Gran chuckled. "Try again."  

Taro clapped. "Same wish!"  

Emma closed her eyes. The candle smelled like warm wax. She wished—hard—and blew.  

The flame disappeared.  

Gran lifted the cake knife. The ribbon slid off. "Now the surprise."  

Emma tugged the wrapping paper. It crackled. Inside was a small wooden box. She lifted the lid.  

Taro peered over her shoulder. "What is it?"  

Emma turned it in her hands. The wood was smooth. The hinges creaked.  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. "For your acorns," she said, and went back to slicing the cake.
