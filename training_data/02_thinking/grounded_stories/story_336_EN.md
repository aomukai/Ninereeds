Taro stood at Yun’s stall. The table was low, just above his waist. Carrots sat in a neat pile on the left. Apples sat in a smaller pile on the right.

He put his hand in his pocket. His fingers found the coin. One coin. He pulled it out and held it in his palm. It was warm from his pocket.

He looked at the carrots. He looked at the apples. He could not buy both.

Mei stood beside the table, watching. She did not say anything.

Taro pointed at an apple. It was red, with a small brown spot near the stem.

Mei picked it up and held it out to him.

He took it. The apple was cool and smooth. He put the coin on the table. It made a small clink against the wood.

Yun looked up from weighing a bag of flour. She nodded once and went back to her work.

Taro turned the apple in his hand. The brown spot was no bigger than his thumbnail. He could eat around it.

Mei went back to straightening the carrots.

Taro walked away from the stall, the apple in his hand, the coin gone from his pocket.
