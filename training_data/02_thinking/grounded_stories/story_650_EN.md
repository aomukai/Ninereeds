Emma sat with her back against the oak. Mei sat beside her, knees drawn up, looking at the trunk.

Mei leaned closer. She put her finger on the bark and traced a line from the low branch down toward the root.

“Did you see this?” she said.

Emma turned. The mark was long and straight, a scratch in the rough bark where something had cut through. It was not deep, but it was clear once you looked.

“No,” Emma said. She touched it. Her finger followed the same path Mei’s had taken. “I never saw it before.”

She looked up at the branch, down at the root, back at the scratch.

Mei sat back and looked at the tree from a little further away. “It must have been here all along,” she said.

Emma looked again. The mark was old. The edges were not sharp. It had been there through rain and sun and the winter snow. They had sat under the oak many times. They had climbed the low branch. They had leaned against the trunk. They had never looked at this part of the bark.

She put her palm flat over the scratch. It was longer than her hand.

“We missed it,” she said. “It was always there.”
