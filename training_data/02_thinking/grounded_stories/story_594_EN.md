The reeds at the pond edge were white. Each stem wore a coat of frost, thin and bright in the morning sun.

Emma reached out. She touched one reed with her finger. The frost came off in a cold dust. The crystal shapes melted on her skin.

Taro kicked a reed with his boot. The frost fell in a shower of tiny white pieces. They landed on the grass and disappeared.

The pond was still. Along the very edge, a thin skin of ice had formed. It was clear in some places, white in others. The water underneath was dark.

Biscuit walked onto the flat stone. He stood at the edge and looked at the ice. He put one paw forward, then stopped. He did not step on it.

Bello came up beside him. He sniffed at the ice, then pulled his nose back. The cold was sharp. He stood very still next to Biscuit, watching.

Emma touched another reed. The frost crackled under her finger. The stem was stiff and cold.

Taro bent down and put his hand near the ice. He did not touch it. He could feel the cold in the air above it.

The sun was still low. The frost on the reeds shone white.
