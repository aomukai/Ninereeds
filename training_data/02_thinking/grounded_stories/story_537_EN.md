The market stall stood in a patch of morning sun. Yun was at the table, arranging carrots in a row. She turned each one so the green tops pointed the same way.

Stefan came walking along the street. His hands were behind his back. He stopped in front of the stall.

He looked at the carrots. Then at the turnips beside them. Then at the small heap of beans tied in bundles. He did not pick anything up.

He nodded at Yun.

Yun nodded back.

Mei was behind the table, watching. She had a basket in her arms, empty. She did not move.

Stefan looked at the brass scale on the table. It was clean and still, the two pans empty. He looked at it for a long moment.

Then he moved on. His hands stayed behind his back. He walked slowly, head forward, not hurrying.

Yun straightened a carrot that was already straight. She did not say anything.

Mei put the basket down on the ground. She watched Stefan until he turned the corner.

The market was quiet. A fly settled on a bean bundle and walked across it.
