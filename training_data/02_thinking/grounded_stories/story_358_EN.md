The hill was green.

Emma climbed. The grass was short and soft under her hands. The wind was stronger up here. It pulled at her hair.

Biscuit ran past her, up the slope, then turned and ran back down. Bello followed him, then stopped, then ran again. They did not know which way to go first.

Taro came up behind Emma. “Where does the gate go?”

Emma kept climbing. The gate was at the very top. It was wood, grey, with a metal latch. It stood between two posts. Beyond it, the hill dropped away.

She reached the gate and put her hands on the top bar. It was rough. She stood on her toes and looked through.

There was another valley. A different one. A farmhouse with a red roof. A strip of trees along a stream. A road she had never walked on.

Taro reached the gate beside her. “What is it?”

“Another valley,” Emma said.

“Can we go there?”

She looked at the latch. It was closed. The gate was not locked, but it was shut.

“Maybe,” she said. “Not today.”

Biscuit pressed his nose against the bottom of the gate and sniffed the air on the other side.
