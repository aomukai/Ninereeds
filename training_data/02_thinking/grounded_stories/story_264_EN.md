Mei picked up a square of sandpaper. It was pale brown and stiff. She turned it over in her hand. The rough side felt like tiny stones pressed flat.

Vern was at the workbench. He did not tell her what to do. He just nodded at the board on the bench.

The board was a long piece of wood. Its edge felt sharp and scratchy under her thumb. Mei wrapped the sandpaper around her fingers and rubbed.

It made a dry, scraping sound. Dust came off the wood — fine and pale. It settled on her fingers and on the bench.

She rubbed again. The same spot. The same edge.

Her fingers were grey now with dust. She blew some of it away. It hung in the air for a moment and then fell.

She stopped and touched the edge with her bare finger. It was not sharp anymore. It was softer. Smoother.

Vern handed her another piece of paper. This one felt different — finer, gentler.

"Try this one," he said.

Mei wrapped it around her fingers. She rubbed again. The sound was quieter this time. The dust was finer. It felt like flour against her skin.

She touched the edge again. It was smooth now. Completely smooth.

She looked at her finger. Then she looked at the wood. The edge was the same colour but the feel had changed entirely.
