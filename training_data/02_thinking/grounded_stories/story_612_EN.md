Gran lifted the lid with the stick. Steam poured out into the cold air. It rose in a white cloud and disappeared.

Emma leaned closer. The inside was dark and warm. She could feel it on her face.

Taro put his hands near the steam. “It’s hot,” he said.

“Warm,” said Gran. She pushed the fork into the pile and turned it. The dark material rolled over. Steam came again.

Earthworms moved in the turned part. They were slow and pink and thick. They pulled themselves into the dark.

Emma watched one disappear. “They don’t like the cold,” she said.

“They don’t,” said Gran. “They like it in there. It’s warm.”

Taro breathed out. His breath made a small white cloud. He breathed in over the compost. That cloud was different — it was the steam from the pile.

“My breath is cold,” he said. “The compost breath is warm.”

Gran turned the pile again. The smell came up — wet earth and leaves and something deep. It was not a bad smell. It was the smell of things changing.

Gran put the lid back. The steam stopped. The compost corner was quiet again.
