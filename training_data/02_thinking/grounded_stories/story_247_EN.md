Gran carried the bucket of wheat into the mill. The grain was pale brown, heavy in the bucket, shifting when she set it down.

Mei stood by the hopper. She lifted the bucket and poured. The wheat ran into the wooden funnel like water, a steady stream, grain against grain.

The millstones began to turn. The sound came through the floor first, a low rumble Emma felt in her feet. Then the grinding—stone on stone, slow and deep.

Fine flour fell into the cloth sack below. White, soft, rising in a small cloud.

Gran lifted the sack. She held it in both hands, then set it on the stone floor.

“Lighter,” she said.

Mei looked at the empty bucket. Then at the sack.

Emma put her hand into the flour. It was warm. Soft. It clung to her fingers.

She scooped a little and let it fall. It drifted down like dust.

Gran closed the sack. “The stones make it warm,” she said. “And some of it goes into the air.”

Emma wiped her fingers on her apron. The flour was still on her skin, fine and white.
