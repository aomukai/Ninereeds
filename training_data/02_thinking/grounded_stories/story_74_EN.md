Emma lay very still. The wall beside her bed was cold against her ear. Through it came sounds—a clatter, a word, the scrape of a chair. Gran was in the kitchen, but the words were muffled, as if wrapped in wool.  

She pressed her ear to the floor. The clatter grew clearer—a spoon against a bowl, perhaps. A faint hum, then Gran’s voice, low and steady. Not the words, just the shape of them, rising and falling like the wind in the trees.  

Then—a creak. The kitchen door opening. The sounds sharpened all at once. The clatter was louder now, the voice closer. Footsteps on the stairs, the third step groaning as it always did.  

Emma sat up just as the bedroom door opened.  

“Still awake?” Gran said.  

The kitchen smells followed her in—bread, warmth, something sweet.  

Emma nodded.  

Gran smoothed the blanket. “Then listen,” she said.  

From below, the faint tick of the cooling stove. The settling of the house. The night, breathing.
