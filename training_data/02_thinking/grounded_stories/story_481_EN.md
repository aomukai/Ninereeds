The jar sat in the middle of the nature table.

Emma leaned close. The water was still. Then she saw them—small dark specks, no bigger than her smallest fingernail. They hung in the water without moving.

“What are they?” she asked.

Clara was writing on a card. She did not look up. “Look again in a minute.”

Taro came up beside Emma. He pressed his face close to the glass. His breath fogged the jar. The dark specks scattered—flick, flick, flick—to the far side of the jar and stopped.

“They moved,” Taro said.

Emma watched. The specks hung still again. She could see tiny tails, thin as threads.

Clara finished writing. She slid the card under the jar. It said: *Tadpoles. Pond. 14th April.*

“Where from?” Taro asked.

“The pond at the bottom of my field,” Clara said. “They were in the shallows near the reeds.”

Emma put her finger on the glass. One of the specks swam toward it, then stopped. It hung in the water, watching back.

“They’re alive,” she said.

Taro nodded. “They’re moving.”

They stood at the table, watching the jar. The specks did not move again. But Emma knew they would.
