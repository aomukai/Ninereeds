The mill was quiet now. The grinding was done.

Mei picked up the broom. It was taller than she was. She swept the floor in long strokes. The dust rose in small clouds and settled on her arms.

Gran stood at the millstones with a damp cloth in her hand. She wiped the flat faces in circles. The cloth turned grey. A paste came off — fine and sticky.

“It gets everywhere,” Mei said.

Gran nodded. She rinsed the cloth in a bucket and wrung it out. The water turned the colour of milk.

Mei took the bucket outside. The stream ran fast and clear beside the mill wall. She dipped the bucket in and carried it back, careful not to spill.

She poured water down the chute. The last of the flour washed away in a pale stream. She wiped the inside with a dry cloth.

Gran hung the damp cloth over the edge of the bucket. The millstones were clean now. The floor was clean. The chute was clean.

They stood at the door. The room was still and quiet. The dust had settled.

“Tomorrow,” Gran said, “it will all be dusty again.”

Mei looked at the stones. She knew it was true.
