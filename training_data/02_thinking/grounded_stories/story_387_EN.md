The snow lay deep on the hill. Emma’s boots sank with each step. Taro walked ahead, his breath puffing white.

The gate at the top stood against the sky. Taro reached for the latch. He pulled. The gate did not move. He pulled again, harder. Nothing.

“It’s stuck,” he said.

Gran came up behind him. She looked at the latch. Frost had sealed it in a thin skin of ice. She tapped the latch with the heel of her hand — once, twice. A sharp crack. The latch lifted.

Gran pushed. The gate swung open with a long, low creak.

Beyond the gate, the next valley spread out, white and still. Snow covered everything — the fields, the hedges, the far-off trees. There was no path. No track. No mark of any kind.

Emma stood beside Taro and looked. The world beyond the gate was empty and quiet. The wind moved across it, lifting a thin drift of snow.

“Can we go down?” Taro asked.

Gran shook her head. “No path. We would not find our way back.”

She pulled the gate closed. The latch clicked into place. They turned and walked down the hill, following their own footprints home.
