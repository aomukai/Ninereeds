Emma sat under the oak. The ground was covered in brown leaves. She picked up three things and laid them on her lap.

The first was a leaf. Dry and curled. She held it in her palm and closed her fingers. The leaf crumbled. Pieces fell into her lap like dust.

The second was a stone. Smooth and grey. She squeezed it as hard as she could. Nothing happened. The stone stayed exactly the same.

The third was an acorn. Hard and brown. She pressed her thumb against it. It did not break. But when she pulled at the cap, it came off in her fingers. The acorn underneath was smooth and pale.

Taro knelt beside her. He picked up a piece of the crumbled leaf.

"This was alive once," he said.

He picked up the stone.

"This was never alive."

He touched the acorn.

"But this one is still waiting."

Biscuit pushed his nose into Emma's hand. He sniffed the stone, the acorn, the leaf dust. Then he sneezed and walked away.

Emma put the acorn back on the ground. She left the stone where it was. The leaf dust was already gone, carried off by the wind.
