Bello stopped on the meadow path.

He did not run ahead. He did not circle back. He stood with one front paw lifted and his nose pointing into the hedge.

Emma came up beside him. "What is it?"

Bello sniffed again. His ears moved forward.

Something rustled in the leaves. A small rustle — not a bird, not a mouse. The kind of rustle that is almost nothing.

Emma pushed the leaves apart with two fingers.

A beetle sat on a stem.

It was small and green. Not the green of leaves. A different green — bright and hard, like a jewel. The sun caught it and it shone.

The beetle had long antennae. They curved out and forward, thin as thread, feeling the air.

Bello watched. He did not bark. He did not push his nose in. He just watched.

The beetle moved. It crawled down the stem, one leg at a time. Its shell caught the light again as it turned.

Emma held the leaves open. She did not move.

The beetle reached the bottom of the stem. It crossed a piece of bark. Then it dropped into the grass and was gone.

Emma let the leaves fall back.

Bello sniffed the spot once. Then he turned and trotted on down the path.
