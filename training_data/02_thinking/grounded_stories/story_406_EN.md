Gran lifted the wooden door. Cold air came up from the cellar steps.

She took the lamp and lit it with a match. The flame grew. Shadows jumped on the stone walls.

Emma followed her down. The cellar smelled of earth and apples and something sharp.

Gran reached into the sack. She pulled out a potato. It looked fine on the outside — brown skin, no mud.

She pressed her thumb into it.

The skin gave way. Inside, the flesh was brown and wet.

"Feel," Gran said.

Emma touched the soft place with her finger. It sank in like wet bread. She pulled her hand back.

Gran took a knife and cut around the spot. The brown ran deeper than the knife could reach.

"This one goes to the compost," she said. She dropped it into the bucket by the steps.

She picked up another potato. Emma watched her squeeze it. The skin was tight. It did not give.

"Firm," Gran said.

She put the firm one in the basket. Then she picked up another. She pressed. She waited. She put it in the basket too.

Emma reached into the sack. Her fingers found a potato. She squeezed. The skin held.

"Firm," she said.

Gran nodded. She blew out the lamp. The dark came back.
