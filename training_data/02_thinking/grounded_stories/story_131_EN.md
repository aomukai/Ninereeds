Bello stopped at the gate. His ears pricked forward. The meadow path stretched ahead, long grass swaying, dandelion clocks catching the breeze. Biscuit trotted past without pausing, his tail a metronome.  

A beetle crossed the path. Bello froze. He lowered his nose, sniffed, jerked back when it moved.  

"Come on," Taro said, tugging the lead gently.  

Bello took three steps. A bird burst from the grass. He leapt sideways, tangling the lead around Taro's legs. Biscuit was already at the bend, nose down, ploughing forward.  

The puddle stopped Bello completely. He stared at his reflection, then pawed at it. Water splashed his chest. He shook a front leg, startled.  

Emma crouched beside him. "It's just wet."  

Biscuit circled back, snorted at Bello, and lunged ahead again. Taro's arm jerked between them — one lead pulling forward, one lead rooted to the spot where Bello had found a feather.  

A bee hummed past. Bello's head swivelled. The feather dropped. The bee zigzagged into the clover, and Bello strained after it, suddenly all urgency, suddenly forgetting to be afraid.
