Emma reached for the apple. Her fingers were almost there.

Taro's hand came past her and took it.

He bit into it without looking at her. The crunch was loud in the garden.

Emma pulled her hand back. She crossed her arms tight across her chest. Her shoulders went up. She turned her face away from Taro and from the basket and from the apple tree.

She did not say anything.

Gran was sitting on the stone bench. She had beans in her lap and a bowl beside her. She looked at Emma's back.

"You are angry," Gran said.

Emma kept her face turned. Her mouth was pressed closed so hard that her lips felt thin. Her arms were tight against her body. She could feel the frown on her face without looking at it.

She wanted the apple. She had been reaching for it first. Taro had not even looked.

Gran did not say anything else. She snapped a bean and dropped it into the bowl.

Emma stood very still. Her arms stayed crossed. Her face stayed turned away. The frown stayed on her face like something she could not let go.
