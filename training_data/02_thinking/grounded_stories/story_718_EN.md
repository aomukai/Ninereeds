Emma and Mei knelt in the warm dirt. The strawberry plants were low and green, and the red berries hid under the leaves.

Emma parted a leaf with two fingers. There was a strawberry. She picked it and put it in the bowl.

Mei found one on the other side. Then another. They worked along the row without talking, each watching the leaves.

When they stood up, the bowl had eight strawberries.

Emma counted them. One, two, three, four. She put four into her own bowl. Then she put the other four into Mei's bowl.

Mei looked at her bowl. "That is the same," she said.

Taro came through the garden gate. He saw the strawberries. He stopped.

"Can I have some?" he said.

Emma looked at her four. Mei looked at her four. There were two bowls and three people.

Emma tipped her strawberries back into the big bowl. Mei did the same. Now there were eight again, all together.

Emma counted them out. One, two, three — into her bowl. One, two, three — into Mei's bowl. Then two left.

She gave them to Taro.

Taro looked at his two. Then at Emma's three. Then at Mei's three.

"That is not the same," he said.

Emma picked another leaf. There was one more strawberry, hiding. She put it in Taro's bowl.
