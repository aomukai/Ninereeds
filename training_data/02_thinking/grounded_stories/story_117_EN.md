Emma stood on tiptoe to peer into the pot. Steam curled up, carrying the warm smell of carrots and thyme.  

"Careful," Gran said, stirring. "The handle's hot."  

Emma stretched one finger toward the iron handle. A quick tap—then she snatched her hand back. "Ow!"  

"Not the soup," Gran said. "The handle. The heat travels through iron."  

Emma frowned. The pot sat black and heavy over the fire, its sides too hot to touch. But the soup inside only steamed, gentle as breath. She reached for the lid instead, brushing the knob with her fingertips. Hotter. She yelped.  

Gran lifted the ladle, letting broth drip back into the pot. "Fire heats the pot. Pot heats the soup. The iron holds it all."  

Emma blew on her fingers. The kitchen smelled of warmth and woodsmoke. Outside, the wind rattled the window, but here by the fire, the heat reached everywhere—even the handle she shouldn't have touched.
