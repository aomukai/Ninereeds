The ash tree stood tall at the edge of the schoolyard. Its leaves had turned — yellow and brown, dry and light.

They covered the ground like a carpet.

Clara came out with two rakes and a broom. She handed the broom to Taro.

"Start here," she said. "Make a pile."

Taro swept. The leaves rustled against the stone. He pushed them together into a heap — taller than his knees, wide as his arms.

Emma stood back and watched.

Then she ran.

She jumped into the pile with both feet. The leaves exploded upward and outward — yellow flakes spinning, brown scraps tumbling across the yard.

Taro stared at the empty space where the pile had been.

"Emma!"

The wind caught the loose leaves and carried them. Some landed near the wall. Some drifted back across the ground he had already swept.

Clara handed Taro the broom again.

He took it. He looked at the scattered leaves. He looked at Emma, who was brushing bits of leaf from her hair.

He started sweeping again.
