The fence ran along the meadow path. Emma put her hand on the first post. The wood was rough and grey.

Mei stood a little way ahead. She looked at the post, then at the next one, then at the one after that.

"They are the same," she said.

"What is the same?"

"The space. Between them." Mei pointed. "Every gap is the same distance."

Emma walked to the next post. She measured with her feet. One, two, three steps. Then she walked to the next one. One, two, three steps.

"You are right," she said.

She walked along the fence, counting the posts. One. Two. Three. Four. Five. Six. Seven. Eight. The same pattern, all the way. Post, gap, post, gap. Every step the same length.

Mei walked beside her, not counting, but watching how the posts came and went at the same pace.

"The fence knows how far apart to put things," Emma said.

"Yes," said Mei.

Ahead, the fence ended at a wooden gate. The last post stood beside it, exactly as far from the gate as the others were from each other.

Emma touched it. "Even the last one."

Mei nodded. She pushed the gate open. The pattern was complete.
