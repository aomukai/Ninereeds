Taro stood at the workbench. The piece of wood had glue on it — pale and sticky, running in a thin line along the edge.

Vern handed him the clamp. It was made of dark metal, with a handle on one side and two flat jaws.

"Turn it," Vern said.

Taro put the clamp around the wood. He turned the handle. The jaws moved closer. He turned again. The jaws touched the wood on both sides. He turned once more, carefully. The clamp held the pieces tight. A small bead of glue squeezed out at the seam.

Vern leaned down and looked along the joint. He did not touch it. He looked from one end to the other.

"Good," he said. "Not too tight. Just enough."

He straightened. "Leave it until tomorrow."

Taro let go of the handle. The clamp stayed where it was. It held the two pieces together, pressing them gently, keeping them still.

"The glue needs time," Vern said. "It dries slow. The clamp waits. You wait too."

Taro looked at the joint. The bead of glue had stopped moving. It was beginning to turn clear at the edges.

Tomorrow the pieces would be one piece.
