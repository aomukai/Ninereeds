Vern picked up the saw. He turned it in his hands so the teeth caught the light.

Taro stood close. Mei stood a little back, where she could see everything.

Vern placed the blade on the wood. He did not push. He pulled first — a short, careful pull. The teeth bit in. A thin line appeared.

“Why did you pull first?” Taro asked.

“To start it,” Vern said. “The teeth need to find their place.”

He pushed. The blade slid forward. He pulled. It came back. Sawdust fell onto the bench in a small pale heap.

Taro watched the blade. It stayed on the line Vern had drawn. It did not wander.

“Your hand is very still,” Mei said.

Vern did not answer. He was watching the cut.

Push. Pull. The saw moved evenly. The line of sawdust grew longer. The teeth made a sound — not sharp, but steady, like something being counted.

Vern finished. He lifted the saw. The two pieces of wood fell apart.

The cut was straight. You could run a finger along it without catching on anything.

Taro touched the edge. It was smooth.
