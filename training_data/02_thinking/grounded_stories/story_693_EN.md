Owen stood at the gate and leaned on it. His hands were big and rested on the top bar.

The sheep were at the far end of the field, white shapes against the hedge. One lifted its head, then put it down again.

Emma came up beside him. Gran was a few steps behind on the path.

Owen pointed at the hedge. "See that?"

Something green-brown clung to the twigs. It was round and rough, covered in tiny hooks.

Emma reached through the gap in the gate. She touched it. It stuck to her finger.

"It's a burr," Owen said. "It grows on that plant there. The hooks catch on anything fuzzy."

He looked at the field. "Sheep walk through the hedge. The burrs catch in their wool. They carry them all the way across the field. Drop the seeds somewhere new."

Emma pulled the burr off her finger. It left a small red mark.

Gran came up and looked at her sleeve. She picked something off the wool. Another burr.

"Got me too," she said. She dropped it on the path.

The sheep moved slowly across the grass. The burr was still in Emma's hand. She turned it over. The hooks were very small.
