Taro pushed the shovel into the compost heap. He lifted a load of dark, crumbly soil and dropped it into the wheelbarrow. Then another. And another.

The wheelbarrow sat low on its wheel.

He grabbed the handles and pushed. The wheelbarrow moved an inch. He pushed harder. It rolled forward a little way, then the left handle dipped. The whole thing tipped sideways. Soil spilled onto the path.

Gran looked up from the bean canes. She walked over and put her hands on the handles where Taro's were.

"Here," she said. "Not at the ends. Closer together. And keep the handles level."

Taro moved his hands. He lifted. The wheel came off the ground. He lowered it again until the wheelbarrow sat flat.

He pushed. The wheel turned. The load stayed level.

The path rose a little. He leaned into the handles. The muscles in his arms went tight. His feet pushed against the ground. The wheelbarrow crept forward.

Emma walked beside him. "It's heavy," she said.

Taro did not answer. He was using all his breath to push.
