Emma stood in front of Yun’s stall. The table was low, just above her waist.

She had a purse in her hand. It was small and brown, with a metal button that pressed shut. She pushed the button open.

Inside were four coins. Two silver. Two copper.

She tipped them into her palm and counted. One. Two. Three. Four.

She looked at the parsley. It was green and fresh, tied with a white string. A small card said the price. She looked at the honey. A tiny jar with a wax lid. It cost more.

She counted again. The coins were the same.

She put the honey back on the table.

She picked up the parsley.

Yun was weighing something for another customer. She did not look at Emma. She did not rush.

Gran stood a step behind. She did not say anything.

Emma held the parsley in one hand. She counted the coins in the other. She had enough for the parsley. She had less than she needed for the honey.

She put the coins back in the purse. She pressed the button shut.

Yun finished with the other customer. She turned. “The parsley,” she said. “Good choice.”

Emma put the purse in her pocket. She held the parsley. It was enough.
