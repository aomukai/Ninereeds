The table was bare. The morning light was still grey.

Yun lifted a cloth from the basket and wiped the table in long, slow strokes. She did not miss a corner.

Mei set the basket of apples on the table. She took them out one by one. Each apple went into a row. She lined them up by size. The biggest one at the front. The smallest at the back. She stepped back and looked at the row.

A basket was sitting slightly crooked on the left side.

Yun saw it. She did not say anything.

Mei saw it too. She walked around the table and turned the basket. A quarter turn. She pushed it until it sat straight. Then she went back and stood beside her grandmother.

The street was empty. No footsteps. No voices.

Yun pulled the cloth across the table one more time. Then she folded the cloth and put it away.

They stood together behind the stall. The apples waited in their row. The basket sat straight.

A door opened somewhere down the street. Footsteps came around the corner.

Yun did not turn her head. She waited.

The first customer was coming.
