The root cellar was cool. Mei held the lamp high. The light moved over the jars on the shelf.

“Plums — September,” she read. Gran’s writing was careful. Each letter stood straight.

Emma pointed. “That one says Pears — October.”

The jars were in a row. Some had dates. Some had names. Emma ran her finger along the line.

“This one has no label,” she said.

Mei handed her the lamp. She took the jar and turned it in her hands. The glass was cool. The contents were dark — plums or pears, she could not tell.

She held it close to the light. No writing. No date.

“It needs a name,” she said.

Emma looked along the shelf. “That one says Plums. That one says Pears. Maybe this one is apples.”

Mei turned the jar again. Then she put it back in the gap between two labelled jars — the right size for the space.

“Match,” she said.

Emma put the lamp on the floor. The shadows moved. The cellar was quiet. The unlabelled jar sat in its place.
