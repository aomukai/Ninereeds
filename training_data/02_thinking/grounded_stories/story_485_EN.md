Clara wrote on the board in large, careful letters. The chalk made a soft tapping sound.

“What is six plus seven?” she asked.

Taro’s answer came before she finished. “Thirteen!”

Clara did not say anything. She held the chalk in her hand and waited.

The other children turned to look at Taro. Emma looked at her own hands. Mei waited.

Taro’s face went warm. He put his hand up.

Clara nodded at him.

“Thirteen,” Taro said again. His voice was quieter this time.

“Yes,” said Clara. “That is the right answer. But you did not raise your hand first.”

She turned back to the board and wrote another number.

“Now we will wait before we speak,” she said. “It is a rule.”

She asked the next question. The room was quiet. Emma raised her hand. Clara called on her.

Emma gave her answer. Clara wrote it on the board.

Taro kept his hand down. He listened. When he knew the next answer, he raised his hand and waited until Clara called his name.
