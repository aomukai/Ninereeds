Biscuit stood in the doorway of Vern's workshop.

He had pushed the door open with his nose. It swung wide, letting in the cold air from the lane.

Inside, Vern was working at the long bench. The saw went back and forth. It made a sound Biscuit knew — a steady, even sound, like breathing.

Biscuit put one paw over the step.

The sawing stopped.

Vern looked up. His hand rested on the saw. The workshop was very quiet. Dust hung in the still air, catching the light from the window. Biscuit could smell sawdust and linseed oil and something resinous from the green wood stacked against the wall. A curl of shaving lay on the bench beside Vern's hand.

Biscuit stepped back. His paws found the cold stone of the step. He backed out into the lane, where his breath made little clouds in the cold.

The door swung shut behind him.

For a moment there was only the sound of the lane — nothing, then a bird, then nothing again.

Then, from inside the workshop, the sawing started again.

Biscuit sat down on the step and waited.
