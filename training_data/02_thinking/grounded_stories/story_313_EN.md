Stefan stopped at the stall. He stood still for a moment, looking at the baskets.

Yun was weighing something on the small brass scale. She did not look up.

Stefan reached into the basket nearest him. His hand moved over the apples without touching them. Then he picked one up.

It was red. Not the dark kind, not the striped kind. A clean, even red. He turned it in his hand. The skin was smooth. He turned it again and checked the stem. It was still there, small and brown, set square in the top.

He held the apple out.

Yun looked at it. She named a number.

Stefan reached into his pocket. His fingers found a coin and brought it out. He placed it on the edge of the stall table. Yun took it. She opened a small tin, counted something into her palm, and set three coins on the table beside his hand.

He picked them up. Dropped them into his pocket.

He walked away.

Halfway across the street he bit into the apple. The crunch carried. It was a clean bite, loud in the quiet morning.

He kept walking. His beat went on.
