Emma went to the apple tree every few days.

The first time, the branches were bare except for small tight buds. They were brown and hard. She touched one. It did not move.

A week later, the buds had opened. Pink showed through. Emma put her face close. The petals were soft and folded.

Another few days, and the tree was full of flowers. Pink and white. The bees came. They moved from flower to flower, heavy and loud. Emma stood still and watched. A bee landed on a flower near her hand. It crawled inside.

Gran came out with a basket for weeds. She stopped beside Emma.

“Look at this branch,” Gran said.

She bent a low branch down. Emma looked. Some of the flowers had dropped their petals. In their place were tiny green knobs, no bigger than a pea.

“That is the apple,” Gran said. “Where the flower was.”

Emma touched one. It was hard.

She counted the knobs on that branch. Fourteen.

She came back the next week. More petals had fallen. They lay on the grass like pale scraps. The green knobs were bigger. She counted again. Eleven. Some had fallen off.

Emma did not know why. But the ones that stayed were round and growing.
