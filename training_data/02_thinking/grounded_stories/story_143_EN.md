The meadow path stretched ahead, warm in the afternoon sun. Taro held two leads—one in each hand. Biscuit leaned left, nose twitching at something in the long grass. Bello strained right, ears pricked toward the flutter of wings in the hedge.  

Taro’s arms made a wide V. "Oi," he said, but neither dog listened. Biscuit gave a low whine and tugged. Bello danced on his toes, pulling the other way. The leads went tight. Taro wobbled, planted his feet, and frowned.  

Biscuit’s tail thumped once. Bello whined high in his throat. The path lay quiet between them.  

Taro glanced left, then right. "Fine," he muttered. He turned toward the hedge, stepping sideways to keep his balance. Bello bounded ahead, lead going slack. Biscuit huffed but followed, casting one last look over his shoulder.  

The wings in the hedge burst upward—a blackbird, gone in a flash. Bello yipped. Biscuit sniffed the air, then trotted on, resigned. Taro adjusted his grip and kept walking.
