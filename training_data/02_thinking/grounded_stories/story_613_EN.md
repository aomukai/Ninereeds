The snow had come overnight. It lay thick over the field, over the oak's roots, over the low branch that Emma usually sat on.

She walked up to the tree and stopped. The bark was dark against the white. No leaves. No acorns. The ground where she had picked them up in autumn was hidden.

Biscuit ran past her in a wide circle. His paws left dark prints in the snow. He came back and sniffed at the base of the trunk, where the raised roots made a hollow. Snow had drifted into the squirrel hole, covering half of it.

Emma knelt down. She brushed the snow away with her hand. The hole was still there, dark and dry underneath.

"Can you see anything?" Taro asked.

"No. Just dark."

He stood under the tree and looked up. The branches were bare and held snow in the forks. The robin's nest was gone or hidden. There was nothing moving in the tree.

"It looks different," he said.

Emma stood up and looked at the oak. It was just a shape now — a dark shape against the white field. No green. No sound. Only the quiet and the cold and Biscuit's paw prints going in circles.
