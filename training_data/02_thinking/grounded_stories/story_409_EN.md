Gran lifted the heavy door and propped it open with the iron bar. The cold smell of earth and apples came up the steps.

Emma went down first, one hand on the wall. The lamp threw a yellow circle on the stone floor.

The onions hung in ropes from the beam. Emma reached up and lifted one. Near the stem, a dark patch sat on the brown skin.

"Gran," she said.

Gran came down the steps. She took the onion and turned it in her hands. Her thumb pressed the dark spot.

"Small," she said. "We can see."

She peeled away the outer layer. The papery skin curled and fell. Beneath it, the spot was shallow, no bigger than a fingernail.

Gran cut around it with the knife from her apron pocket. The cut piece dropped into the bin she kept by the steps. The rest of the onion was clean and pale.

"This one," she said. "For tonight's soup."

Emma chose another from the rope. She turned it in her fingers, looking for spots. This one was clean. She held it up.

"Good," Gran said. "Bring both."

Emma carried them up the steps. Behind her, Gran lifted the lamp and checked the remaining onions on the rope, one by one, before she followed.
