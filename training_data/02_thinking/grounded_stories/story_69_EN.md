The waiting room smelled of disinfectant and dog. Taro sat on a plastic chair, his legs swinging. Biscuit leaned against Gran’s knee, his tail thumping softly on the floor. A woman with a cat carrier sat across from them, leafing through a magazine.

The vet called them in. She had short dark hair and quick hands. “Step up,” she said, pointing to the scale. Biscuit hesitated, then put one paw on the metal platform. He tried to step off immediately, but Gran held his collar. “Stay,” she said quietly.

The vet glanced at the numbers. “He’s heavier than last year,” she said, without surprise.

Biscuit’s ears drooped as she lifted him onto the cold metal table. He stood very still, his expression patient but resigned. The needle was quick. He didn’t flinch.

“Good boy,” the vet said, pulling a treat from her coat pocket. Biscuit swallowed it in one gulp, tail wagging again.

“All done,” Gran said, clipping on his lead. Taro patted Biscuit’s head as they walked out into the lane.
