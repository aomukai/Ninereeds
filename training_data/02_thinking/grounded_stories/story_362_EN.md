After heavy rain, Gran said, "Let's go up."

Emma and Taro followed her across the field. Biscuit ran ahead, then came back, then ran ahead again.

The path up the hill was muddy. Water ran in the ruts. Emma's shoes made a sucking sound with every step. Taro stepped in a puddle on purpose, then stepped in another.

At the top the wind was strong. Gran stopped and pointed down.

The stream at the bottom of the hill was not a stream anymore. It was brown and loud. It had eaten into the bank and made a wide gully. Water jumped over stones and crashed against the sides. Branches and leaves moved fast on top of it.

Emma could hear it from where she stood. It was louder than the wind.

"The field is draining," Gran said. "All the rain has to go somewhere."

Biscuit stood at the edge and watched the water go. He did not bark. He just watched.

Taro wanted to go closer. Gran put a hand on his shoulder and did not let him.

They stood together at the top, looking down at the brown water running fast. The sky was still grey. More rain was coming.
