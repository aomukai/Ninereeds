Clara stood at the front of the classroom. On her desk sat a tray of small pots and a bowl of dark soil.

"Today we begin something," she said. She gave each child a pot and a seed.

Emma took her pot to the nature table. She filled it with soil. The soil was cool and crumbly. It ran through her fingers and landed in the pot with a soft sound.

Taro held his seed up to the light. It was small and brown. He poked his finger into the soil. The hole was deep enough. He dropped the seed in and covered it.

Mei poured water over hers. The soil turned dark. A little water pooled on top.

Clara wrote on the blackboard: **Day 1. Soil. Day 2. Seed. Day 3. Water.**

Every morning the children looked. For a week, nothing changed. The pots sat on the windowsill. The soil stayed dark and still.

Then one morning Taro saw it.

A green sprout. Small and pale. Curved like a hook.

Emma touched it with one finger. Very gently.

Mei put her pot on the nature table where the sun came through the glass.

The sprout was very small. But it was there.
