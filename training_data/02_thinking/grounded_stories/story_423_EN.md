Gran lifted the cellar door and propped it with the iron bar. The steps were cold and steep. Emma held the lamp.

Inside, the room smelled of earth and old apples. Taro sneezed.

Gran took a jar from a shelf. The beans inside were brown and still. She held the jar toward the light.

“These are from last autumn,” she said.

She set the jar down and pulled a potato from the hessian sack. Her thumb pressed into the skin. It gave a little.

She cut it open.

Inside, the flesh was grey. Small dark spots ran through it like tiny threads.

Emma looked from the jar to the potato. “Why are the beans still good?”

“The jar keeps air out,” Gran said. She wiped the knife on her apron. “The potato sack lets air in. Air spoils things, slow but steady.”

Taro picked up another potato. He turned it over. It felt firm. He did not cut it.

Gran put the jar back on the shelf. She dropped the cut potato into the bucket by the stairs.

“Fresh is best,” she said. “But a good jar keeps the autumn alive.”

She took the lamp. The children followed her up the steps. The cellar door fell shut behind them.
