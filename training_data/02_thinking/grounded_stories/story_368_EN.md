The hill was long and green. Emma's legs were warm from the walk. Taro was already ahead, climbing fast.

Owen stood at the top. He leaned on the gate — a wooden gate, old and grey, with a latch that hung loose.

"This is the top," he said. "Look."

Emma reached him and looked. The hill dropped away on the other side. A different valley opened below — a farm she had never seen, a lane going down between hedges, a roof that caught the sun.

The gate was open.

Taro put his hand on the wood. "Where does it go?"

"The next village," Owen said. "A long walk. You can see it from here."

Emma looked through the gate. The lane curved and disappeared. The farm was quiet. She could see a dog in the yard, small and still.

She looked at the grass on her side. Then she looked through again.

It was the same hill. But the other side was different.

Taro pushed the gate a little. It swung on its hinge, then stopped.

Owen did not close it. He just stood there, hands on the top bar, looking out at the valley they had never walked into.
