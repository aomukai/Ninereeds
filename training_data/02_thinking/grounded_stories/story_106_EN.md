Emma knelt by the table. The candle from last night was gone. Only a dark disc of wax remained, with a stub of wick curled at the centre. She picked it up. Cold. The edges were smooth where the flame had melted them.  

Gran came in with the dustpan. She glanced at Emma’s hand.  

“Burned to nothing,” Emma said.  

Gran swept a crumb from the floor. “A candle does its work once,” she said. “Then it’s finished.”  

Emma turned the disc in her fingers. Before, it had been tall and white. Now it was this: a small dark thing that would never hold fire again.  

The morning light came through the window and touched the wax. It didn’t shine.  

Gran took the dustpan to the kitchen. The door creaked behind her.  

Emma set the disc back on the table. The wick stub pointed up, black and still.
