Emma put the coin on the table. It was big and silver. It felt heavy in her hand before she let it go.

Yun picked it up. She turned it over once. Then she opened the small tin box beside the brass scale and took out three smaller coins.

She held them out. “One,” she said.

She dropped the first coin into Emma’s open palm. It was cool and light.

“Two.”

The second coin landed beside the first. Emma felt the edge of it against her finger.

“Three.”

The third coin went on top. Emma closed her hand around them. The coins were warm now, from her own skin.

She looked down at her palm. Three coins. One, two, three.

“Thank you,” Emma said.

Yun nodded and straightened a jar of dried herbs on the stall.

Emma put the coins into her pocket. They clicked together once, then were still. She walked away from the stall. The bakery smell came across the street. She touched her pocket with her fingers. The coins were still there.
