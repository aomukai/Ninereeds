The sun was low over the hill. It sat on the edge of the world like a big orange coin.

Emma's shadow reached all the way down the slope. Taro's shadow was longer. He stretched his arms out and watched it grow.

"It's getting cool," Emma said.

The grass felt different now — not warm from the sun, but damp and soft. Biscuit sniffed the air. Bello sat beside Emma and did not move.

The sky changed. First it was orange. Then pink. Then purple at the top, where the first stars came out.

"Look," Taro said.

Down in the village, a light came on. Then another. Then more, one by one, like someone was lighting candles along the street.

Gran's voice came up the hill. Not loud. Just clear.

"Time to come down."

They turned. The cottage door was open. A square of warm light sat on the ground outside.

They walked back through the long grass. The shadows were gone now. The hill was dark. The sky was still purple above them.

Biscuit walked close. Bello walked in front.

The air was cool and quiet. The only sound was their feet in the grass.
