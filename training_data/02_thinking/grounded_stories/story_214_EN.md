The rain came hard and fast. It hammered the mill roof and ran off the eaves in sheets. Emma and Mei stood in the doorway, watching the stream below turn brown and rise.

The water wheel began to turn faster. Its usual slow, heavy rhythm changed to something quicker. The paddles slapped the surface and threw spray into the air — white and cold and sudden.

"Look at it," Mei said.

The wheel spun. Water flew off the rim in arcs. The noise was a low, wet roar that filled the building. Emma stepped back from the doorway. Biscuit did not step back. He stood at the edge of the stream, wagging, and got splashed full in the face. He sneezed and shook his head and stayed where he was.

They watched the wheel race for a long time. The spray reached further than it should. It dampened the stone floor inside the doorway. Biscuit ran through the wet grass in wide loops and came back dripping, his coat dark and heavy.

Then the rain eased. The stream slowed. The wheel settled back into its ordinary pace — slower, steadier, the sound dropping like something letting go.

Mei wiped her face with her sleeve. "It will be dry again soon."

They waited. The wheel turned and turned, and the water ran quieter.
