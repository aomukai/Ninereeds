Mei and Gran walked up the hill path. The grass was short and spring-green. The wind was stronger here, away from the trees.

At the top they stopped. Mei looked down at the village. The houses sat small and quiet. The mill chimney rose from the edge of the street — pale stone against the blue.

Mei pointed. “You can see the chimney.”

Gran stood beside her. She was not looking at the chimney. She was looking west.

“Yes,” Gran said. “That’s the best thing about this hill.”

Mei waited.

Gran lifted her chin towards the horizon. “You can see the weather coming, from the west. Before it reaches the village. Before you feel it in the lane.”

Mei looked west. The sky was clear. Far out, at the edge of the world, a line of grey sat very low and very still.

“That,” Gran said, “will be here by afternoon.”

Mei looked at the grey line. She looked at the chimney. She looked at the village below, where no one yet knew.

Gran started back down the path. Mei followed, turning once to look at the sky again.
