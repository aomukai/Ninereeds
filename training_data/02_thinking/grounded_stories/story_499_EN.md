The bell rang.

Emma stopped. Her feet went still on the cold ground. She was holding her scarf in one hand, and she held it, waiting.

Other children stopped too. Some were in the middle of a jump. Some had their hands in the air. They all went still, like the bell had frozen them where they stood.

Then the line started. Emma stepped into it. The girl in front of her had a red coat. The boy behind her was blowing on his fingers.

Taro did not stop. He ran.

He ran straight for the door, his boots loud on the hard ground. His coat was open. His hat was in his hand.

Clara stood in the doorway. She did not move. She looked at Taro.

"Taro," she said. "Wait."

He stopped. His feet skidded a little on the stones. He stood still, breathing hard.

"Walk," Clara said.

He walked. He came back to the line. He stood behind Emma, his breath making clouds in the cold air.

The line moved. One step. Then another. The door was open. Inside it was warm.
