Gran put four tomatoes on Yun’s stall.

They were round and red, with stems still attached. Yun picked them up one at a time and set them on the brass scale. The scale had two flat pans hanging from a beam. The tomatoes went on the left pan.

Yun placed a small weight on the right pan. The beam moved. She added another weight, smaller. The beam lifted slowly, then dipped again.

Mei stood beside her grandmother. She watched the brass arm. It moved a little, then settled. The arm was level. The scale was balanced.

“Good,” Yun said.

She poured the tomatoes into a paper bag. The stems stuck out at the top. She folded the edge once.

“That’s the weight,” she said to Gran. She said the price. Gran nodded and reached into her pocket.

Mei watched the brass scale. The arm was still level, even with no tomatoes on it now.

Yun took the weights off the pan and set them back in their row. The next customer came. She put apples on the left pan. Yun reached for the weights again.

Weigh. Settle. Bag.

Mei watched the arm each time. It always found the same place.
