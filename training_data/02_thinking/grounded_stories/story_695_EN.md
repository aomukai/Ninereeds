The grass was stiff under Emma’s boots. It did not bend. It broke.

She walked toward the pond. Her breath came out in puffs and hung in the air for a moment before it disappeared.

The reeds at the edge were white. Each stem wore a line of frost. The tips were silver. The leaves were stiff and pale.

Biscuit trotted ahead. He put his nose close to the ground and sniffed. The grass crunched under his paws.

At the edge of the pond, the water was still. Near the bank, a thin skin of ice covered the surface. It was clear and pale, like window glass left out in the cold.

Biscuit stepped forward. His front paw touched the ice.

It cracked.

He pulled his paw back and looked at the broken line. Then he looked at Emma.

Gran stood behind them on the path. She was holding her coat closed at the neck.

“The sun will melt it by midday,” she said.

Emma crouched. She looked at the cracked ice. The pieces had gone dark where the water had come through.

She touched a reed. The frost came off on her finger. It was cold and wet and gone.

The morning sun was low. It touched the reeds. The frost began to shine.
