Owen walked up the hill with his sheepdog beside him. Biscuit followed a few steps behind, then caught up, then fell behind again to sniff something in the grass.

The sheep moved slowly across the slope. They did not run. They took one step, then another, then stopped to pull at the grass. Their wool was thick and dirty. One looked up when Biscuit came near, then lowered its head again.

Owen stopped at the gate at the top. He rested his hands on the top bar and looked into the next valley. The ground dropped away on the other side. A different farm lay below, smaller, with a grey roof and a thin line of smoke.

Biscuit came up beside Owen and sniffed the gatepost. He put his nose to the wood, then to the latch, then to the ground where the gate had dragged. He sneezed.

The sheepdog sat at Owen's heel and did not move. She watched the sheep. Biscuit watched the gatepost.

Owen did not open the gate. He stood there a long moment, looking at the valley, then turned and walked back down the hill. The sheepdog rose and followed. Biscuit gave the gatepost one last sniff and trotted after them.
