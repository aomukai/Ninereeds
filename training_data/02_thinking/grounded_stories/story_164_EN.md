Emma stood on the stool beside the table. The flour bag crinkled as Gran opened it.  

"Here," Gran said, handing her a cup. "Fill it halfway."  

Emma scooped. Flour dust rose in a soft cloud. She pressed the back of the knife across the rim, just like Gran did. The excess fell away.  

"Half," Emma said, tipping it into the bowl.  

"Again," Gran said.  

Emma scooped a second time. The knife scraped. "And another half." She poured. The flour mound in the bowl grew.  

Gran touched the bowl's edge. "Two half-cups is the same as one full cup."  

Emma looked at the cup, then the bowl. "Two halves make one."  

"You could fill it twice, half way each time," Gran said, already turning back to the scales.  

Emma dipped the spoon into the flour. It made a soft thump against the side of the cup. The kitchen smelled warm, like yeast and woodsmoke. Outside, a blackbird sang from the apple tree.
