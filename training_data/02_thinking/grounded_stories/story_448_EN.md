Bello stepped into the water. One paw, then another. The reeds stood still around him.

The ducks did not stay still.

They lifted all at once — a rush of water and wings. The sound was loud and sudden. Bello stopped. His ears went up. One brown feather came loose and drifted down. It landed on the surface and sat there, very light, rocking a little.

The ducks circled. Their wings made a shape in the air. Then they came down again, further out, past the willow where the water was deeper. They settled. The ripples spread slow and thin.

Taro was standing on the flat stone. He had been counting on his fingers. Before the ducks lifted: seven. After they came down: seven. He counted again, just to be sure. Seven.

“Still seven,” he said.

Bello looked back at the ducks. Then he looked at the feather on the water. It was still rocking. He took one more step, and the water came up to his chest.
