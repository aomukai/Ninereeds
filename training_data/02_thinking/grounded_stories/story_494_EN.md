Emma stood under the ash tree in the schoolyard. She tipped her head back and looked up.

Last week the branches were bare. Dark lines against the sky. Nothing else.

Now there were buds.

Small green buds at the tips of every branch. Hundreds of them. Some were still tight, pointed, like tiny fists. Others had begun to split open.

Mei came and stood beside her. She reached up and touched one of the open buds with one finger.

"It's soft," she said.

Emma reached up too. The bud was soft. Not like a leaf yet — softer, almost damp, as if it had just come out of something.

Mei's finger slipped into the opening. The bud parted a little more. Inside was a fold of pale green, very small, very new.

"It was a leaf last year," Emma said.

Mei looked at her hand. Then at the branch. Then at all the other buds.

"But it wasn't this leaf," she said.

Emma looked at the bare branch below the bud. Grey and rough. She looked at the bud again. The green inside was so bright it seemed to make its own light.

Biscuit sniffed the trunk at the base of the tree. He found nothing interesting and sat down.

The bell rang.
