The basket sat on the ground beside Yun’s stall. It was full of turnips — dark purple at the top, white at the bottom, packed tight.

Yun bent and took the handles. She pulled. The basket did not move.

She pulled again. Harder. The basket shifted an inch. She let go and stood straight, breathing.

Mei was stacking empty crates. She stopped and looked at the basket. Then she looked at her grandmother’s hands.

Stefan came around the corner of the stall. He was walking his beat, hands behind his back. He saw Yun at the basket. He saw her straighten.

“That one needs two,” he said.

Yun nodded.

Stefan stepped in. He took one handle. Yun took the other. Together they lifted. The basket rose off the stone floor, heavy and even. They carried it to the table and set it down. The table legs took the weight. The wood creaked once, then held.

Mei pulled the empty basket out from under the table and moved it to the side.

Yun brushed her hands on her apron. She looked at Stefan. Stefan nodded. No words.

Then Yun said, “Thank you.”

Stefan touched the brim of his cap. He turned and walked on, hands behind his back again, slow and steady along the main street.
