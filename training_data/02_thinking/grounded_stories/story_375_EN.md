The path went on. Taro looked up the hill. Biscuit ran ahead, then stopped, then ran again.

At the top there was a gate. Wooden. A metal latch. Taro reached it first.

He pushed the latch. It moved.

Bello came up behind him, ears up, watching.

Taro pushed the gate. It swung open, slow and heavy. The grass on the other side was different. Greener. The path went on, down into the next valley.

Biscuit put his nose under the gate and sniffed the ground beyond. He did not go through.

Emma reached the top. She stood beside Taro and looked.

The valley beyond was quiet. No chimney. No lane she knew. The path went down between trees and then turned out of sight.

Biscuit came back and sat beside her.

Emma looked at the path. Then she looked at Taro.

"Should we go?" she said.

Taro did not answer. He held the gate open. The grass on the other side moved in the wind.

Bello circled once and came back to Emma's feet.

Emma looked down the path one more time. Then she reached out and pulled the gate closed. The latch clicked.

"Not today," she said.

Taro let his hand rest on the wood for a moment. Then he turned.

They walked back down the hill. Biscuit ran ahead. Bello followed close behind.
