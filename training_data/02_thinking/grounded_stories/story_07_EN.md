The garden hummed with bees. Emma crouched by the lavender, watching one hover over a purple bloom. Its wings blurred as it dipped into the flower.  

Taro stood very still. A butterfly, orange and black, had landed on his arm. Its wings opened and closed slowly. "Gran," he whispered.  

Gran snipped a rose stem. She pinched the brown petals off and dropped them into her apron pocket. "Painted lady," she said without turning.  

Biscuit pushed his nose into the peonies and sneezed. A bee buzzed past his ear. He shook his head and trotted to the vegetable patch instead.  

Emma touched a daisy. The white petals felt thin as paper. The smell of warm earth and flowers hung thick in the air.  

The butterfly lifted from Taro's arm. It fluttered over the roses, then disappeared behind the apple tree.  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. "Tea's in ten minutes," she said, and went inside.
