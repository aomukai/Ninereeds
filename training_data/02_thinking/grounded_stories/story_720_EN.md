The red bicycle came to a stop at Gran's gate.

Emma was at the window. She saw the postman swing his leg over the seat. His canvas bag hung heavy against his hip.

Riku walked up the path. The leaves crunched under his boots. He carried a flat parcel in his hand.

Gran opened the door before he knocked.

"Morning," Riku said.

"Morning," said Gran.

He held out the parcel. "For you."

Gran took it. She turned it over in her hands. The paper was brown and tied with string.

"Thank you," she said.

Riku nodded. He did not hurry away. He stood for a moment, looking at the garden. Then he turned and walked back down the path.

Emma came to the door. She watched him lift his bicycle and point it down the lane.

"He does this every day," Emma said.

"Yes," said Gran. "Every house in the village. Every day."

"He brings things."

"He brings letters. Parcels. Sometimes a card." Gran looked at the parcel in her hands. "Someone sent this to us."

Emma watched the red bicycle get smaller down the lane. The postman's bag leaned to one side. He turned the corner and was gone.
