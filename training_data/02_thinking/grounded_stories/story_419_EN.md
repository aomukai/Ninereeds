Gran lifted the iron bar from the ground. It was longer than her arm and dark at one end where it had sat in the earth.

"Watch," she said.

She set the bar against the wooden door. She pushed. The door swung up and stayed open, propped at an angle.

Emma stepped forward. She took the bar in both hands. She lifted. The bar was heavier than it looked. She set it against the door and pushed. The door rose a little, then the bar slipped. The door fell shut with a thump.

Mei tried. She set the bar carefully. She pushed. The door swung halfway and stopped. Mei pushed harder. The bar slid sideways. The door fell shut again.

Gran picked up the bar.

"The angle," she said. "Not too steep. Not too flat."

She set the bar once. One motion. The door stayed open.

Emma looked at the angle. She looked at Mei. Mei was already looking at the bar, measuring it with her eyes.
