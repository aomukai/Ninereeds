Mei stood on her toes. Her fingers walked along the high shelf in the root cellar. She felt jars, one after another—cool glass, smooth lids.

Then her hand touched something further back. Behind the other jars. Her fingers curled around it and pulled.

The jar came out into the lamplight. It was covered in dust. Grey and thick, like it had been sitting for a long time.

Emma took it from Mei. She breathed on the glass and wiped it with her sleeve. The dust smeared, then cleared.

A label was stuck to the side. Small paper, brown at the edges. Gran's writing, careful and slanted.

"Blackberry jam," Emma read aloud.

Mei leaned closer. "I didn't see it from the front. It was all the way at the back."

Emma turned the jar in her hands. The jam inside was dark and still. She wiped the lid clean with her thumb.

They carried it up the stone steps together. The heavy door stayed propped open behind them. In the kitchen, Gran looked up from the table and smiled.
