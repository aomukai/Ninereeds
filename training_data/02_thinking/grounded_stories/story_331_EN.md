The stall table was nearly empty. Just a few carrots, a small sack of potatoes, and a jar of dried herbs.

Yun straightened the cloth. She did not hurry.

Mei sat on the crate behind the stall. She watched the street. The baker's apron was still white with flour.

Hana came up the street with a basket over her arm. The basket had a cloth over it. She stopped at the stall.

She lifted the cloth. Inside was one loaf. It was round and dark. The top had a crack where the steam had come out.

"This one did not sell," Hana said.

Yun looked at it. She did not say anything.

Hana put the loaf on the stall table. It was still warm. The heat came through the cloth.

Yun reached under the stall. She brought up the sack of potatoes. She set it on the table beside the bread.

She did not count them.

Hana picked up the sack. It was heavy. She put it in her basket.

Yun picked up the loaf. She felt the warmth in her hands.

Hana nodded. She turned and walked back up the street.

Mei watched her go. Then she looked at the loaf on the table.

The bread was still warm.
