The hill was long and green. Emma climbed until the cottage was small behind her. Taro was ahead, already at the top, standing still.

When Emma reached him, she saw why.

Far below, across the field, a dog was moving. Black and white. It ran one way, then stopped. It turned and ran the other way. The sheep moved with it — a slow white shape that shifted across the grass.

Emma watched. The dog did not bark. It did not chase. It ran, and the sheep turned. It paused, and the sheep stopped.

Biscuit stood beside her. He was not sniffing. He was not wagging. His ears were forward. His tail hung low.

Taro looked down at him. "Biscuit is different," he said.

Emma looked too. Biscuit's whole body was still. His eyes followed the black-and-white dog.

"He is watching," Emma said.

The dog far below ran again. The sheep flowed around it like water around a stone.

Biscuit did not move. He watched until the dog stopped again. Then he watched some more.
