Emma knelt by the vegetable patch. The soil was damp from yesterday’s rain. She ran her finger along the row where the seeds had gone in—three weeks now, and nothing.  

Then she saw it. A pale thin shoot, bent like a hook, pushing through the dirt. It hadn’t been there yesterday. Or maybe it had, just under the surface, where she couldn’t see.  

She touched it. The shoot was cool and slightly rough. It didn’t straighten when she let go.  

Gran was weeding nearby. Emma didn’t call out. She watched the shoot instead, the way it leaned toward the light. By afternoon, the bend was less. By the next morning, it stood straighter.  

A week later, another shoot appeared in the same row. Then another. The first one had leaves now, small and pointed. Emma checked them every day. They were always there, always a little further along than before.  

Gran hummed while she worked. The shoots stretched toward the sound.
