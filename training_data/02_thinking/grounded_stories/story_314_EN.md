The carton sat on the stall table. It was grey cardboard, with twelve round holes inside.

Mei held it steady with one hand.

Emma reached into the basket. The eggs were warm. Some were brown and some were white. She picked up a brown one and fitted it into the first hole.

One.

The next egg was white. She turned it in her fingers before she put it in.

Two. Three.

Straw clung to the fourth egg. Emma brushed it off with her thumb and set it in its place.

Four. Five. Six.

Mei did not say anything. She just held the carton and watched.

Seven. Eight. Nine.

The eggs filled the holes one by one. The brown ones were darker than the white ones. They made a pattern if you looked at them together.

Ten. Eleven.

The last egg was brown. Emma picked it up carefully. It was the smallest of all.

Twelve.

The carton was full.

Mei lifted the lid and pressed it down until it clicked. Then she slid the carton across the table.

Emma picked it up with both hands. She held it flat and still, the way Gran had taught her.

"Good," said Yun, from behind the stall. She did not look up. She was weighing something on the brass scale.

Emma turned and began to walk back down the lane, the carton steady in her hands.
