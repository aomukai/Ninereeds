Emma took the bean seed from Gran’s hand. It was small and dry, with a pale line down one side. She pushed her finger into the soil, dropped the seed in, and covered it.

She brought water from the kitchen in a tin cup. She poured it over the spot. The soil went dark.

Every morning she looked. One day the soil was lifted. A small green thing had pushed through. It had two round leaves, very small.

Gran tied a cane into the soil beside it. “Watch it climb,” she said.

The plant grew a thin stem. It curled around the cane in a slow spiral. New leaves came, bigger than the first two. They were dark green and soft.

Emma came out one morning and saw a flower. It was small and pale pink, with a curl at the edge. She touched it with one finger.

The flower fell off. Behind it was a thin green bean, pointing down.

“Leave it,” Gran said.

Emma left it. It grew longer. It grew fatter.

One afternoon Gran said, “Pick it now.”

Emma held the pod and twisted. It came off in her hand. It was firm and cool. She pressed her thumb along the seam. The pod opened.

Inside were small pale seeds, lined up in a row. They were the same size as the one she had put in the soil.
