The main street was cold. Frost sat on the edges of the stone slabs.

Taro walked beside Gran. His boots made small sounds on the hard ground. His breath came out in white puffs.

Stefan was walking toward them. His hands were clasped behind his back. He did not hurry. His coat was dark and thick. The badge on his chest caught the pale winter light.

He drew closer. He did not slow down.

He nodded at Gran. Then he looked at Taro.

“Taro,” he said.

His breath made a small cloud when he spoke. Then it was gone.

He did not stop. He kept walking at the same slow pace past the baker’s window. Past the post box set into the wall. The frost on the ground did not change where he stepped.

Taro looked back. Stefan was still walking the same way, hands behind his back, not looking back.

Gran did not look back. She just kept walking.

Taro turned and walked beside her again.
