The last jar went onto the shelf. Emma wiped her hands on her trousers. The cellar smelled of earth and apples and something sweet that was just past its best.

Upstairs, Gran sat at the kitchen table. She was not doing anything. That was unusual.

Mei came through the garden door. "The cellar door is open," she said.

"Gran is telling a story," Taro said. He was already sitting at the table.

Emma sat down too. Mei sat beside her.

Gran looked at the three of them. She did not look at them to count them. She looked at them as if she was seeing something else.

"The first time I filled this cellar," she said, "I was not much older than you."

She pointed at the floor. Not at them. At the floor, where the cellar was below.

"Your mother was small," Gran said. "She sat on the top step and watched. I handed her apples one by one. She put them in the crate."

Emma looked at the floor too. She could see the cellar in her mind. The same shelves. The same jars. The same smell.

"We filled it together," Gran said. "Every autumn. Until she grew up and left."

She stood up. She went to the sink and turned on the tap.

The room was quiet. The clock ticked. The cellar waited under them, full of apples and jars and a past that nobody else remembered.
