The pond was white at the edges. Not snow — frost. It lay on the reeds like a thin, cold blanket.

Emma leaned close. Each reed wore a coat of tiny crystals. She touched one with the back of her finger. The frost crackled, and a flake of ice fell away.

“It sounds like paper,” she said.

Taro grabbed a reed near the middle and pulled. The stem did not bend. It snapped. The sound was sharp and dry — nothing like the wet pull of summer reeds.

Gran was on the bench, wrapped in her brown coat. She held a broken reed in her gloved hand.

“See how the frost changes the shape?” she said. She turned the stem slowly. The ice crystals followed every ridge, every line. “Cold makes the water freeze on the surface. The stems get stiff. Brittle.”

Emma picked up the piece Taro had dropped. The frost was already melting from her fingers. The reed was dark and wet underneath. She squeezed. It crumbled in her hand.

Taro pulled another reed. It snapped too. He held the broken end up to the grey sky.

“They all break now,” he said.

Their breath hung in the cold air. The frost on the reeds did not answer.
