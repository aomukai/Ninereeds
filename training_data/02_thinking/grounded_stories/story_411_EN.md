The lamp sat on the stone shelf. Emma struck the match. The flame caught, small and yellow, and she touched it to the wick.

The glass chimney went on. The flame steadied. It stood still and calm inside the warm glass.

Upstairs, the cellar door opened.

Mei’s voice came down the steps. “Emma? Gran said to—“

The flame tipped. It leaned sideways, long and thin, and the light slid across the wall. A draught came down the stone steps, cool and quick. Emma cupped her hand around the glass. The tip of the flame flickered. It wavered, bent almost flat, then straightened again.

Mei came down. The door stayed open above her. The flame leaned toward it, then back, then forward again.

“Close the door,” Emma said.

Mei went back up. The door shut with a soft weight. The draught stopped.

The flame settled. It stood straight, calm, the same steady yellow as before. The cellar was still. The light sat on the jars and the ropes of onions and the dark earth floor.

Emma lifted the lamp by its handle. The flame did not move.
