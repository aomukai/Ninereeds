Emma lifted the lamp. The flame inside sat small and still.

She turned. Mei’s shadow jumped across the stone wall.

It was big. Bigger than Mei. Much bigger.

Mei looked at it. She lifted her hand.

The shadow hand grew huge. It spread across the wall like a wing.

Emma took a step closer. The shadow shrank a little.

She took a step back. The shadow stretched again.

She set the lamp on a shelf. Now the shadows were different. Taller. Thinner. They slid up the wall and across the ceiling.

Mei moved her fingers. The shadow fingers opened and closed.

Emma moved her hand too. Her shadow hand met Mei’s shadow hand on the wall. They were giant hands now. Big enough to cover the whole wall.

The lamp flame flickered. The shadows flickered with it — big, small, big again.

They stood very still and watched the shadows move without them.
