The stream ran fast beside the mill. Taro knelt and put his hand in. The water pushed his hand downstream.

“It pushes,” he said.

Mei came and stood beside him. She pointed to the gap between the wheel and the chute. The water fell through the gap and hit the paddles. Each paddle caught the pressure and the wheel turned.

Taro watched. The wheel moved slowly, steadily. The water kept falling, kept pushing, kept the wheel in motion.

Gran came out of the mill door with flour dust on her apron. She stood and watched them watching the wheel.

“The water works,” she said. “Not us.”

Taro put his hand in the stream again. He felt the push against his palm. The stream was not fast. It did not rush or roar. But it pushed his hand and it pushed the wheel.

He looked at the gap. He looked at the water falling. He felt the force in his palm.

The wheel kept turning.
