The main street was empty. The bench under the horse chestnut was wet with frost. Emma could see her breath.

She walked beside Gran. Their footsteps echoed off the stone walls — one small step, one slow step. No other sound.

The bakery window had bread inside. Loaves lined up in rows. But no one was behind the counter. The room beyond the glass was dark.

Smoke came from the chimney above the baker's. It went straight up, thin and white, into the cold air.

"It's so quiet," Emma said.

Gran nodded. "Winter afternoons. Everyone is inside."

They passed the butcher's. The striped awning was rolled up tight. A cat sat on the step, not moving. It watched them go by.

At the end of the street, the lane began. The gate at the bottom stood open. Beyond it, the hedges were bare and thin.

Emma looked back. The main street was still empty. The frost on the bench had not melted. The smoke from the chimney rose without wind.

She turned and followed Gran through the gate. Their footsteps made the same sound on the lane — echo, echo, echo — all the way home.
