The sack was small. Emma could hold it with one hand.

Yun put it on the brass scale. The pointer swung. It settled to one side.

“Too light,” Mei said.

Yun opened the sack. She poured more grain from a larger sack into a tin cup. The grain ran like water. It hit the bottom of the cup with a thin, hard sound.

She tipped the cup into the small sack.

She put the sack back on the scale.

The pointer moved. It did not settle. It swung past the middle and stopped on the other side.

“Now too heavy,” Mei said.

Yun took a handful of grain out. She put it in the cup. She put the sack on the scale again.

The pointer swung. It slowed. It came to rest in the middle.

Yun leaned close. Her eyes followed the brass needle to the line on the face.

“Four pounds,” she said.

Emma looked at the sack. It did not look different. But the pointer said it was exactly enough.
