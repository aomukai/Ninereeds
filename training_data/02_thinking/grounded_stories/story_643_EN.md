Emma walked along the meadow path. The grass was tall and dry on both sides. A few brown leaves skittered across her feet.

Owen stood at the gate. He leaned on it with both arms, looking out across the field.

Emma stopped beside him. She did not say anything. She looked where he was looking.

The sheep were far away today. A white cluster against the far hedge, small and still.

Owen lifted one hand from the gate and pointed.

"Over there," he said.

Emma looked. She knew the field. She had walked past it many times.

"Last week," Owen said, "they were right here." He tapped the gate with his knuckle. "Near enough to touch."

Emma looked from the far sheep to the gate. The same field. The same grass. Just the sheep had moved.

A ewe lifted her head near the hedge, then put it down again.

Owen did not move. He just stood, leaning, looking across the grass.

Emma waited. Then she turned and walked on along the path. Behind her, the gate creaked once as Owen shifted his weight.
