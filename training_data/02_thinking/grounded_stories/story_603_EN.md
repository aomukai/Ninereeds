The hill was long and the grass was short. The wind came straight over the top without anything to stop it.

Taro walked up with Biscuit beside him. Biscuit’s ears blew back. He sneezed into the grass.

Owen stood at the top gate. His sheepdog lay at his feet, chin on its paws. The sheep were spread across the slope — some near, some far. They moved slow, heads down, cropping the short grass.

Owen did not say anything. He leaned on the gate and watched.

Taro leaned on the gate too. Biscuit sat down and let the wind push his fur sideways.

A sheep moved away from the flock. It walked alone, heading downhill, not grazing.

Owen pointed at it. He did not speak.

The dog got up. It went down the slope without hurrying. It curved wide and came around behind the sheep, then stopped. The sheep looked at it. The dog did not move.

The sheep turned and walked back to the flock.

The dog followed, then lay down again at Owen’s feet.

Owen nodded at the dog.

Taro watched the sheep gather close. He did not speak. The wind kept coming.
