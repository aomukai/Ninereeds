The hill was white today. Thin snow covered the grass. The sky was grey and flat.

Emma pulled her coat tighter. The wind came straight at them. There were no trees on the hilltop. Nothing to stop it.

Taro leaned forward. The wind pushed against him. He took a step. Then another. It was hard work.

Bello pressed close to Emma's legs. He was shivering. His ears were flat against his head.

Emma looked down toward the village. The houses were small and quiet. The mill chimney was a thin line. But she could not hear anything. No bell. No dog. No sound at all.

The wind took everything away.

Taro stopped walking. He turned his back to the wind and stood still. His face was red. His eyes were watery.

"Can you hear anything?" Emma asked.

Taro shook his head.

Bello pressed harder against Emma's leg. She crouched down and put her hand on his back. He was cold. The snow was thin, but the cold was thick.

Emma stood up again. She leaned into the wind.

They stood like that for a while. Three small shapes on the white hilltop.
