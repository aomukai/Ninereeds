The lane ran straight ahead. On one side, the fence.

Emma looked at the posts. They stood in a row. Each post, then a gap. Each post, then a gap.

She stopped walking.

Taro kept going. Biscuit kept going. Emma stayed where she was, looking.

The first post. The gap. The second post. The gap.

“One,” she said. “Two.”

She took a step. Another post. Another gap.

“One. Two.”

The posts were all the same. The gaps were all the same. Every post, then a gap, then a post, then a gap. The pattern did not change.

She walked beside the fence. She counted as she walked.

One. Two. One. Two.

The fence went all the way down the lane. It did not stop. It did not skip. One, two, one, two, all the way to the village.

Taro was far ahead now. Biscuit was far ahead too.

Emma did not hurry. She kept walking. She kept counting.

One. Two. One. Two.

The posts went past. The gaps went past. The pattern was even. It was the same each time.

She liked that.
