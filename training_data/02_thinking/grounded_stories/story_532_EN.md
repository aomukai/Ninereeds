The market was finished. The last stall was folded and tied. Yun lifted the basket onto her arm. Two empty jars inside. A cloth folded flat.

Mei walked beside her. Not ahead. Not behind. Beside.

The lane was long in the evening. The sun was low and the shadows stretched ahead of them, long and thin across the stones. Mei stepped over a crack where the stone had lifted. She did it without looking down.

Yun said, "The eggs went first today."

Mei nodded.

"Then the beans."

Mei nodded again. She knew. She had been at the stall since morning. She had watched the baskets empty one by one.

They walked slow. The basket was light now. The jars clinked against each other when Yun shifted the weight to her other arm.

A cat sat on a wall ahead. Grey, one white ear. Dou. She watched them approach, tail still.

Mei did not call her. The cat would come when she was ready.

The lane turned. The shadows turned with it. The sound of the village faded behind them. Quiet ahead. Quiet behind. Just footsteps and the clink of jars and the long light of evening.
