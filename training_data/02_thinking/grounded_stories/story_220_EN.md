The sluice lever did not move.

Mei pulled it with both hands. She pulled until her arms were straight and her feet slipped on the wet stone. The lever stayed where it was.

Gran came down the steps. She set her lamp on the floor and put both hands on the lever beside Mei’s. She pulled. Nothing.

Taro climbed up from the stream bank. “Let me try,” he said. He pushed the lever instead. He pushed hard, his face going red. The lever did not move.

Mei knelt and looked into the channel. The water was high but not moving. Something was in the way. A rock — wedged between the sluice gate and the stone wall.

“There,” she said, pointing.

Gran looked. She found a long stick on the bank, thick at one end. She pushed it into the water, past the rock, and worked it back and forth. The stick jammed. She pulled it out and tried again, lower down. The rock shifted. She pushed harder. The rock rolled free and the water found its gap.

Gran reached up and pulled the lever. It moved smoothly. The sluice opened.

The water rushed in under the mill. The wheel groaned, then turned. The grinding sound began again, low and steady, felt through the floor.

Mei wiped her hands on her coat. “Fixed,” she said.
