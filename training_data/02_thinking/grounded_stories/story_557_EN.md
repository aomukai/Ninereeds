The market was over. The stall was empty.

Yun lifted the last basket. It held vegetables—carrots with the earth still on them, three onions, a turnip. She settled the basket on her arm.

Mei walked beside her. The sun was high and hot.

They went along the main street. The cart was empty now, rolled back to the shed. Only the basket remained, and the walk home.

Mei's feet were tired. She did not say so. She watched the road in front of her feet. The stones were warm.

Yun stopped near the green. The old horse chestnut spread wide above them. The shade was sudden and cool.

Mei stopped too. She looked at the basket on Yun's arm. It was heavy. The handle was cutting into Yun's hand.

"Let me carry it," Mei said.

Yun did not argue. She handed the basket over.

Mei put the handle over her own arm. The basket was heavier than it looked. She straightened it, settling the weight. The carrots shifted inside.

They walked on together, slower now, under the shade of the trees along the lane. The sun was still high. The road was still warm. But the basket was lighter for Yun, and Mei did not let it drag.
