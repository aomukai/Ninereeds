Yun and Mei walked up the lane. The basket hung heavy between them.

Mei held one handle. Yun held the other. The basket swung as they walked.

The lane was covered in leaves. Brown and orange and yellow. Some were still damp. Others were dry and crunched under their feet.

The basket was full of vegetables. Carrots and turnips and a cabbage. A jar of honey wrapped in cloth. A bag of dried herbs sealed with a twist.

Mei's arm began to ache. She did not say anything. She shifted her grip and kept walking.

At the top of the lane, Gran was standing at the gate. She was wiping her hands on her apron.

"I thought you might be coming," she said.

She stepped through the gate and took the basket handle beside Mei. The weight changed. It became lighter.

They walked the rest of the lane together — Gran and Mei on one side, Yun on the other. The basket swung between them. The leaves crunched under three pairs of feet.

At Gran's gate they stopped. The basket was set down on the stone step.

"Thank you," said Yun.

Gran nodded. "Stay for tea."

Yun looked at Mei. Mei looked at the basket. The cabbage was showing.
