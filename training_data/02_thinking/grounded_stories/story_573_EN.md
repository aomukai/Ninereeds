Hana stood at the window inside the bakery. The glass was clean and the morning light came through it.

She placed the first loaf on the shelf. It was a big one, round and brown, with a dark crust. She put it at the back.

Then she placed another loaf beside it. Also big. Also at the back.

Emma pressed her nose against the glass from outside. Taro stood beside her, hands in his pockets.

Inside, Hana worked. She picked up a medium loaf. She put it in front of the big ones. Then another medium loaf. Then another. She was making a line.

The smallest loaves were last. They were round and neat, with lines cut across the top. Hana placed them at the front of the row.

She stepped back. Her hands were still. She looked at the line of loaves — big at the back, small at the front, all in a straight row.

Then she leaned forward. She moved one small loaf a little to the right. She stepped back again. She nodded.

Taro tapped the glass. Hana looked up and smiled.
