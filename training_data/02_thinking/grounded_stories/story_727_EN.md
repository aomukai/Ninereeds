The small pot sat by the kitchen door. Emma stopped.

The plant inside it had changed. The leaves hung down, soft and tired. They did not point up the way they had in the morning.

Emma touched one. It was thin. It was warm. The leaf did not push back against her finger.

“Gran,” she said.

Gran came to the door. She looked at the pot. She did not say anything. She went inside and filled a jug with water.

She poured it into the saucer beneath the pot. Not on the plant. Into the saucer. The water rose. The soil at the bottom of the pot turned dark. The water soaked up through the drainage hole.

Emma watched.

The leaves still hung soft. The plant did not change.

Emma went inside to eat lunch. When she came out again, the leaves had lifted. They were still green. They pointed up.

She touched one again. It was cool. It was firm.

By evening the plant looked the same as it had that morning. The leaves stood out from the stem. The pot sat by the door.

Gran came out to close the henhouse. She looked at the plant and nodded once.
