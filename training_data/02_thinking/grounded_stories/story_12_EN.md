Gran held the coats by the door. "Left arm first," she said, shaking out Emma's sleeves.  

Taro rummaged in the pocket of his coat. "My glove's gone."  

Emma pushed her foot into her boot. The zip stuck halfway. She wiggled it. "Gran—"  

"Pull the tongue up," Gran said, buttoning her own coat.  

Biscuit whined by the door, tail thumping.  

Taro found his glove under the bench. "Ha!" He pulled it on, fingers poking through the holes.  

Emma yanked her zip. It screeched up.  

They burst outside. The path was muddy from last night’s rain. Biscuit splashed ahead.  

By the time they came back, their socks were wet inside their boots. Emma’s scarf dripped. Taro’s coat sleeves were dark with mud.  

"Peel those off," Gran said, holding the door wide. They left boots by the mat, socks in a heap. Gran hung the coats to dry, clucking at the pockets full of acorns.  

Biscuit shook himself, spraying water across the floor.
