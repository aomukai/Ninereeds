The winter day was short.

Emma stood on the hilltop with her hands in her pockets. The sun was already low — a pale orange disc sinking behind the dark line of trees.

"It's going," Taro said.

Gran stood a few paces back, her scarf pulled up to her chin. She did not say anything. She just watched.

The light faded quickly. The field below turned from gold to grey. The cottage looked very small from up here. Smoke rose from the chimney in a straight white column. It did not drift. The air was still and cold.

Two windows in the cottage glowed orange. Warm light. Home light.

"Time to go down," Gran said. Her voice was quiet but it carried in the still air.

They started down the slope. Biscuit had already gone ahead — a small brown shape moving fast through the grass.

Emma looked back once. The last sliver of sun was gone. The sky was violet at the edge.

She turned and followed Taro down the hill. The cottage windows grew brighter as they came closer.
