Gran lifted the heavy wooden door near the kitchen wall. Cold air rose from the steps.

She lit the lamp and went down. Taro followed. The stone steps were worn in the middle. The cellar smelled of earth and apples and something else he could not name.

Gran put the lamp on a shelf. The light made long shadows on the walls. She pulled a hessian sack from against the stone.

"Potatoes," she said.

She untied the top and reached in. Her hand came out with a potato. She squeezed it. She put it on the floor. She reached in again. Squeezed. Another potato on the floor.

Taro watched.

Gran's hand stopped. She pulled out a potato that was darker than the others. She squeezed it. Her thumb went in a little way.

She held it up. The smell reached Taro before she said anything. Sharp. Sour. Not like the others.

"Soft," Gran said. She handed it to Taro. "The compost pile."

Taro took it. It gave a little in his hand. The skin was wrinkled near the soft spot. He carried it up the steps, across the cold garden, to the wooden frame behind the shed.

When he came back, Gran was still checking. The floor had a row of good potatoes. The sack was nearly empty.

She did not look up. "One more to check."
