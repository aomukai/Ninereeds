The hill was long and the grass was short. Emma’s legs ached before they reached the top. Taro ran ahead and waited, then ran again.

Gran stopped at the top and looked west.

Emma stopped too. The sky above them was blue, clear and wide. But low on the horizon, far away, there was a dark line. It sat flat against the edge of the world like a long grey wall.

“That cloud is coming this way,” Gran said. “We have time to get down before it arrives.”

Taro stared at the dark line. It did not look like it was moving. But while he watched, it grew. The edge of it lifted, just a little, and the blue in front of it turned pale.

“It is coming,” he said.

They started down. Emma looked back once. The cloud was bigger now, and the light on the hill had changed — not darker yet, but different, flatter, as if the sun had gone behind something that was not there.

They walked fast. Gran did not hurry, but she did not stop.

By the time they reached the bottom, the first spots of rain touched Emma’s arm.

She knew before she felt them. She had seen them coming.
