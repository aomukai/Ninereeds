The climbing frame stood in the corner of the schoolyard. The bars were cold and the paint was chipped.

Taro ran at it. He jumped, caught the top bar, and swung his legs up. The frame shook.

Emma climbed one rung at a time. She felt the metal in her hands. The bar was rough where the paint had gone. She stopped halfway and looked down.

Mei stood on the ground. She held the rail with one hand and watched.

Taro let go of the bar and dropped. His shoes hit the grass. The frame wobbled and the bars clanked.

"Push me," Mei said.

Emma climbed higher. She sat on the top bar and pushed the frame a little. The rail moved. Mei held tighter. The frame rocked.

Taro ran at it again. He grabbed the bar, swung, and tried to get his legs over. The frame tipped sideways. He dropped into the mud and landed on his back.

The grass was wet. His coat was brown down one side.

Emma looked down at him from the top bar.

Mei let go of the rail. She took one step back. The frame was still now.
