Emma knelt at the edge of the pond. The water was still and clear near the reeds.

Mei knelt beside her. She put her hands in the water, very slowly.

"There," she said.

A tadpole sat in her cupped hands. It was dark and wriggling. Emma leaned close to look.

"It has legs," Emma said.

Two tiny back legs stuck out from the side. They were small and pale. The tail was still there, long and thin.

Mei turned her hands. The tadpole wriggled again.

"It is changing," Mei said.

She put it back in the water. It swam away into the reeds.

Emma looked for another one. She found one that was smaller. It had no legs yet. Only a tail.

"Look," she said. "This one is different."

Mei looked. "Same kind," she said. "Not the same yet."

Biscuit pushed his nose into the water. The tadpoles scattered in all directions. The water went cloudy.

Biscuit sneezed.

Bello sat on the flat stone and watched. He did not put his nose in. He watched the ripples spread and settle.
