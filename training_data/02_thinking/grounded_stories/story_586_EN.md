The bakery door was heavy. Emma pushed it open. Taro slipped through beside her.

The heat hit them like a wall.

Inside was gold and brown and very, very warm. The cold on their coats began to melt. Emma's nose stopped feeling like ice.

Hana stood at the oven. She wore a white apron dusted with flour. Her face was red from the heat. She pulled a long tray out and set it on the wooden bench.

The smell of bread filled the whole room.

Emma stepped closer. The tray held four loaves. Steam rose from the crusts. The bread was dark brown and cracked on top.

"Just out," Hana said, without looking up. She wiped her forehead with the back of her wrist.

Taro stood very still. The warmth wrapped around him. He did not want to move.

Through the window, the lane was grey and cold. The stone walls looked hard. The sky was flat and white.

Here, inside, everything was soft and good.

Emma put her hands near the tray. The heat came off it in waves. She could feel it on her cheeks, on her chin, on the tips of her fingers.

They stood inside a moment longer.
