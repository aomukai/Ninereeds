The afternoon sun slanted across the market street. Most of the stalls were packing up. Hana walked quickly, her apron still dusted with flour.

She stopped at Yun's stall. Only one basket of eggs was left on the wooden table.

"How many?" Hana asked.

Yun leaned over the basket. She counted with her finger. "Six."

"I will take all six."

Mei reached for a cloth. She laid it flat on the table. Yun lifted each egg and placed it into Mei's hands. Mei wrapped the cloth around them, carefully, one at a time. The brown shells were warm from the sun.

When the last egg was wrapped, the basket sat empty on the stall.

Hana put a coin on the table. Yun nodded.

Mei handed the cloth bundle to Hana. The baker held it close to her chest, the way she held a loaf just out of the oven.

"Thank you," Hana said.

She turned and walked back up the street. The bakery smell drifted from her apron. Behind her, Yun folded the empty basket and set it under the table. The stall was nearly closed. The day was almost done.
