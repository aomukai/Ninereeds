The climbing frame stood in the corner of the schoolyard. Three bars up, a platform, and the slide slanted down.

Taro went first. He climbed fast, sat hard, and slid. His shoes scuffed the gravel at the bottom.

He looked up. Emma was on the platform.

"Come down," he said.

Emma shook her head. She held the bars.

Mei stood at the bottom of the ladder. She waited.

Taro ran around and put his foot on the first rung.

Mei said, "It is Emma's turn."

Taro stopped. He looked at Emma. She had not moved.

"You went already," Mei said. "Now Emma goes. Then me."

Taro took his foot off the rung. He walked to the back of the line. He stood behind Mei.

Emma pushed off. The slide carried her down. She landed on her feet and stepped away.

"Now you," she said to Mei.

Mei climbed. She did not rush. She reached the top, sat straight, and slid. Her hands stayed flat on her knees.

Taro watched. He counted the steps forward in his head. One. Two. Three. Then it would be his turn again.
