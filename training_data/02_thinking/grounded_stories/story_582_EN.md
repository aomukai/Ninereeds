The side street was cold. The sun did not reach it in winter.

Taro walked beside Gran. His boots made a hard sound on the stone. Hers made a softer one. That was all. No other footsteps. No voices.

The vet’s door was closed. The sign above it swung a little in the wind but made no noise. Taro looked at the low step. No one had sat on it today. The frost had not been broken.

The doctor’s gate was shut. The black latch had a line of white along it. Frost. Taro did not touch it.

Vern’s workshop door was closed too. No light came from the window. The sawdust smell was frozen out of the air. The street was bare.

“Everyone is inside,” Taro said.

Gran did not answer. She was looking ahead at the corner where the street met the lane. Her breath came out white and disappeared.

They walked past the closed doors. Past the step. Past the gate. Their shadows went with them, long and thin on the cold stone.

At the corner Gran stopped. She looked back down the side street. It was still and silent.

“In winter,” she said, “the street holds the cold.”

She turned and walked on. Taro followed. The street stayed quiet behind them.
