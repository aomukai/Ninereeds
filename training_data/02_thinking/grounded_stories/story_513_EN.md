Clara stood by the ash tree.

"Let us count the steps to the gate," she said.

Taro went first. He took long steps, stretching his legs. "One, two, three, four, five, six." He stopped at the gate. "Six."

Emma started from the same place. She took small steps, one foot just in front of the other. "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten, eleven." She looked back. "Eleven."

Clara walked to the tree. She stood where they had stood. She walked to the gate with her own steps. "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight."

Taro frowned. "Six for me."

"Eleven for me," said Emma.

"Eight for me," said Clara.

They stood at the gate and looked at the ash tree. The distance was the same. The grass was the same. The crack in the stone by the gate was the same.

Taro stepped back to the tree. He took very small steps, like Emma. "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten, eleven." He grinned. "Now eleven."

Emma tried long steps. "One, two, three, four, five, six." She looked at Taro. "Six."

Clara smiled. She did not say anything. She walked back to the classroom.
