The ash tree stood in the corner of the schoolyard. It had no leaves. Every branch was bare and black against the sky.

Emma stood under it and looked up. She could see the sky between every branch — little pieces of grey, like a broken plate. The branches made a pattern. It was like looking at a skeleton.

Taro came and stood beside her. "There is nothing left," he said.

Emma touched the trunk. It was dark and wet. The bark was rough under her fingers. Cold.

Mei walked over slowly. She put her hand flat against the trunk. She did not say anything at first. Then she said, "It feels different without leaves."

The frost was still on the ground near the wall. The air was cold and still. The tree did not move. In summer the leaves hid everything — the sky, the branches, the shape of the tree. Now there was nothing to hide.

Their shadows lay on the frost behind them — three short shadows and one long one from the tree.

Taro shivered. "It looks like it is waiting."

Mei kept her hand on the bark for another moment. Then she took it away. The tree did not change. It just stood there, bare and cold, with the sky showing through.
