The millstone turned. The floor hummed.

Mei stood at the hopper. Yun handed her a small wooden measure, full of grain.

"Pour it in," Yun said.

Mei tipped the measure. The grain rattled down into the dark. The millstone groaned. Flour began to fall into the cloth sack below.

Yun handed her a second measure.

Mei poured again. The sack grew a little fatter.

"One measure of grain does not make one measure of flour," Yun said.

Mei looked at the grain measure. Then she looked at the flour sack. The sack was not even half full.

"How much grain for one sack?" Mei asked.

Yun took a third measure and held it out. "Weigh one measure first."

They crossed to the brass scale. Yun set the empty measure on one side. She added weights to the other until it balanced.

"Now fill it," Yun said.

Mei filled the measure with grain. She set it on the scale. The weight side rose. Yun added another weight. The scale tipped again.

"Compare," Yun said.

Mei looked at the empty measure on one side and the full one on the other. The difference was clear.

"Now we count," Yun said. She poured the grain into the hopper. "We fill the measure. We weigh it. We pour. We count how many measures fill the sack."

The millstone turned. The flour fell.
