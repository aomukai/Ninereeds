Gran stood by the fence at the edge of the schoolyard. Her hands were in her coat pockets. Her breath made a small cloud in the cold air.

Biscuit sat beside her. His nose was wet. He watched the gate.

The gate opened. Children came out in twos and threes, buttoned up, scarves wrapped tight.

But Bello was not watching the gate. He was already moving.

He started at the fence post nearest Gran. He put his nose down to the ground. Then he walked. He followed the fence all the way along — past the climbing frame, past the ash tree, past the bench against the wall. His nose stayed low. His breath made little white puffs against the cold metal.

He did not stop. He did not look up. He followed the whole perimeter of the yard, one step at a time, until he reached the gate.

Then he stopped.

He sat down. He looked at the gate. Then he looked at Gran.

Gran smiled. "You went all the way around," she said.

Bello wagged his tail once.

Biscuit stood up. He had been watching the whole time. Now he walked over to Bello and sniffed the ground where Bello had sniffed. Then he sat down beside him.

They waited at the gate together.

The children were coming out now. Emma and Taro were at the back, walking slowly.
