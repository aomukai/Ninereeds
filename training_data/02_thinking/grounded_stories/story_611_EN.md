The garden was white.

Emma stood at the edge of the vegetable patch and looked at the rows. Every leaf had a thin coat of frost on it. The ground was hard under her boots.

She touched a cabbage. The leaf was stiff and cold. When she pressed it, the edge cracked. A small piece broke off in her hand and lay there like a piece of glass.

Mei came up behind her. She was holding something between her fingers — a frozen leaf she had picked up from the ground. Ice crystals ran along the edge in a thin white line. She turned it over and the light caught the crystals.

"It's like a feather," she said.

Gran came out of the kitchen door with a basket over her arm. She walked to the row of carrots and stopped.

"The frost will kill the last of these," she said. She bent down and pulled a carrot. The top was brown and limp, frozen stiff. But when she scraped the dirt off with her thumb, the root below was orange and firm.

She held it out. "The frost cannot reach underground."

Emma took the carrot. It was cold and heavy. She rubbed the dirt off with her fingers.
