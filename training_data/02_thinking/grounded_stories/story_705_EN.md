Clara set a basket on the nature table. The basket was full of things.

"Emma," she said. "Come and help me sort."

Emma came to the table. She looked into the basket. A leaf. A stone. A feather. An acorn. A shell. A small pot with a green seedling.

Clara placed three empty trays on the table. "Three piles," she said. "Things that are alive. Things that were alive. Things that were never alive."

Emma picked up the pot first. The seedling had small round leaves. It was green. It stood in soil. She put it in the first tray. Alive.

She picked up the feather. It was grey and soft. It had come from a bird. The bird was alive once. She put the feather in the second tray.

The shell was empty. Emma turned it over. No snail inside. She touched the spiral. It was light and dry. She put it beside the feather.

The stone was smooth and grey. Emma held it in her palm. It was cool. It had never been alive. She put it in the third tray.

The acorn was brown. Its cap was rough. Emma looked at it. It was still whole. She put it in the second tray.

The leaf was dry and brown. It had fallen. It was from a tree. She put it with the feather and the shell.

She looked at the three trays. The first tray had the seedling. The second tray had the feather, the shell, the acorn, the leaf. The third tray had the stone.

Clara nodded. "Good," she said.
