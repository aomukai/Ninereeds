Vern drew the plane along the wood one last time.

A thin curl lifted from the blade and fell to the floor. It lay on the sawdust like a pale ribbon.

Emma picked it up. She held it between her thumb and finger. The shaving was so thin she could almost see through it. It was still warm from the blade.

“Is it done?” she asked.

Vern ran his hand across the edge. He did not look at her. “It is.”

Taro came to look. The piece was a small box. The lid sat flush with the sides. The corners met exactly. Mei stood back and watched, her hands still.

Vern set the plane on its side on the shelf. He brushed the bench with his palm. A few stray shavings fell.

“What happens to the rest?” Taro said, looking at the pile on the floor.

“They go in the bin,” Vern said. “Or you take them.”

Emma held her shaving up to the window. The sun came through it, pale and golden.

She folded it carefully and put it in her pocket.

Taro scooped a handful from the pile. The curls were light and dry. He let them fall back.

Vern closed the vise. The workshop was quiet.
