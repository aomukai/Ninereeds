The snow lay thick over Gran’s field. Biscuit bounded out the door, kicking up white puffs behind him.  

Bello stopped at the doorstep. His ears twitched. He stretched his nose forward and sniffed. The snow was cold and smelled of nothing. He lifted a paw, set it down, and the white stuff gave way beneath him. He jerked back.  

Emma crouched beside him. “It’s just snow.”  

Bello sniffed again. He pressed one paw in, then another. The snow swallowed his feet. He took three careful steps, sinking a little deeper each time.  

Biscuit barreled past, sending snow flying. Bello flinched—then bolted after him. His paws skimmed the surface, then plunged, but he didn’t stop. The cold stung his nose. The field stretched ahead, white and endless.  

Taro whooped as Bello raced past, kicking up snow like Biscuit, like it was nothing new at all.
