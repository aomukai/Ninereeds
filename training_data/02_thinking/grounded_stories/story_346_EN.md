The path up the hill was steep. Taro went first, his feet finding the bare earth between the grass. Emma followed, slower, watching where she stepped.

Biscuit ran ahead. He stopped. He ran back. Then he ran ahead again, his tail high.

At the top, the wind was stronger. It pulled at Emma's hair. She turned and looked down.

The cottage was very small. The oak was a dark shape at the edge of the field. The lane was a thin grey line.

"It's tiny," Emma said.

Taro was looking the other way. "Listen."

She listened. She heard the wind. She heard her own breath. She heard Biscuit — he had run to the gate at the top of the hill and was barking at it, one sharp bark after another.

"I can't hear the village," she said.

"No," said Taro. "Just the dog."

The barking stopped. Biscuit came back, tongue out, tail wagging. He sat down between them and looked at the view as if he owned it.

Emma looked again at the cottage. A thread of smoke rose from the chimney. She could not see Gran. She could not hear anything from down there.

"Come on," said Taro, and started down.

Biscuit followed. Emma followed last, turning once to look at the gate at the top, then down at the cottage, then at her feet.
