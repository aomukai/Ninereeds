Yun took a scoop from the wall. It was made of dark wood, smooth on one side and rough on the other.

She dipped it into the grain sack. The scoop came up full. She ran her finger across the top, and the extra grain fell back.

"One," she said, and poured it into the hopper.

She dipped again. Leveled again. "Two."

The hopper was a wooden box with a narrow opening at the bottom. The grain went in and disappeared.

"Three."

Gran picked up another scoop. She did the same thing—dip, level, pour. One. Two. Three.

They each had a sack on the floor. The same number of scoops.

Gran lifted her sack. She set it on the scale. The needle settled.

Then she lifted Yun's sack. It was heavier.

Yun frowned. She turned the sack over and looked at the bottom. Something was caught in the seam.

She picked it out. A small stone. Then another.

She held them in her palm.

"Grain is grain," she said. "Stone is not."
