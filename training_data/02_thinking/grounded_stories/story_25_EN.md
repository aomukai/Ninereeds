Emma held the tent pole while Taro hammered the pegs into the ground. The tent leaned sideways. Gran didn’t fix it. She lit the lantern and hung it from a low branch of the oak. “Leave it,” she said, tossing a log onto the fire. “It’ll hold.”

They roasted sticks of bread over the flames until the edges were crisp. Biscuit sat close, watching every bite. The night grew darker. Shadows stretched long across the field. Stars blinked above the oak.

Inside the tent, the air smelled faintly of damp grass. Taro wiggled his toes against the canvas floor. “Listen,” Emma whispered. Something rustled outside — a soft, steady sound, like paws brushing through the grass. Biscuit’s ears twitched. He lay across the tent door, his nose pointing toward the noise.

The night sounds were louder than in the day — a distant hoot, the creak of branches, the chirp of crickets. Emma pulled the blanket up to her chin. The lantern flickered outside, casting faint light on the tent walls. The fire crackled softly. Biscuit sighed and closed his eyes.
