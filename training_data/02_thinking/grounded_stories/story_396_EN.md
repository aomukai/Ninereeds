Emma held the lamp. The flame was small and still.

The root cellar was cold. The air smelled of earth and apples. The shadows sat quiet on the stone walls.

Taro moved his hand in front of the lamp.

The shadow on the wall grew huge. It filled the space between the ceiling beams. Then he pulled his hand closer to the flame, and the shadow shrank to a dark spot, small as a coin.

"Look," he said.

Emma watched the shadow. It was his hand, but it was not his hand. It was bigger than Gran. It was bigger than the whole cottage.

Taro moved his fingers. The shadow fingers stretched and curled.

Behind them, the jars on the shelf cast their own shadows — long, thin shapes that leaned and straightened as the flame shifted.

Emma turned the lamp a little. The shadows slid across the wall. The jars stretched like tall dark bottles. Then they shrank to their own shape again.

Taro moved too fast. The flame flickered. The shadows jumped.

He stopped.

The flame settled. The shadows settled with it.

Emma held the lamp steady. The shadows stayed where they were.
