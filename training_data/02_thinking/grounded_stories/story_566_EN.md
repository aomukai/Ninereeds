Riku's bicycle leaned against the wall. The back wheel was flat.

Taro stopped on the lane. He watched.

Riku knelt down. He turned the wheel with one hand. His other hand found the puncture — a small hole, almost invisible. He did not say anything. He took a patch from his canvas bag.

The pump came out next. Riku fitted it to the valve and pushed. Air hissed in. The tyre grew round again. He checked it with his thumb, then bent over the wheel again.

His hands worked fast. The patch went on flat and smooth. He pressed it down with the edge of his thumb, working from the centre out. No air escaped.

Taro crouched beside the wheel. "Does it hold?"

Riku pumped again. The tyre swelled. He tapped it. "It holds."

He stood up and lifted the bicycle off the wall. The wheel was round and full. He swung his leg over the saddle and pushed off down the lane, the red bag swinging against his hip.

Taro watched until the bicycle reached the corner. Then he turned and walked back up toward Gran's gate.
