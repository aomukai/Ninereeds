The path was half dry, half damp. Emma stopped walking and looked up.

The sky was grey. Not cloudy grey, not storm grey. Just grey, low and flat, like a lid pressed down over the field.

“The wind stopped,” Taro said.

It had. The grass did not move. The leaves on the hedge hung still. Even Biscuit stopped sniffing and stood with his head up.

Emma felt the air on her face. It was still and cold and it smelled wet — not like rain falling, but like rain getting ready.

“It’s coming,” Gran said. She did not hurry. She kept walking at the same pace, her basket over her arm.

Emma walked beside her. The air pressed against her skin. The grey sky sat low over the meadow. The oak at the edge of the field was dark against it.

“I don’t see it yet,” Emma said.

“You feel it,” Gran said. “That’s the before.”

Biscuit sneezed. Taro pulled his coat tighter.

They walked. The path was quiet. The air was cold and still and full of something that had not arrived yet.
