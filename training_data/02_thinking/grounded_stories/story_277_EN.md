Gran lifted the cloth away. The chair leg lay on the bench in two pieces.

Vern picked them up. He turned the broken ends toward the light.

"See that?" He ran his thumb along the face of the split. "The grain runs straight here. The wood let go along the lines it was already made of."

Emma looked. The break was not rough and jagged. It was smooth, as if two pieces had been cut apart and put back together badly.

Vern set the larger piece in the vise. He worked glue into the split with the tip of a thin blade — slow, pressing it deep. Then he fitted the smaller piece against it. The edges met without a gap.

He reached for a clamp. The metal arms were cold. He turned the handle, and the clamp drew the two pieces together. A bead of glue squeezed out along the join.

"Leave it there," he said. "Tomorrow it will be one piece again."

Emma touched the clamp. It was tight. The wood could not move.

Gran picked up the cloth. "Tomorrow, then."

They left the leg clamped to Vern's bench, drying in the quiet workshop.
