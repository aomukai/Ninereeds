Owen stood at the gate where the meadow path met his field. He did not move. He leaned on the wood and looked west.

Emma stopped. Taro stopped too.

The sheep were moving. They walked slowly, all in one direction, down the slope toward the lower ground.

Owen pointed with his chin. "See those clouds?"

Emma looked. The clouds were coming over the hill. They were grey at the bottom and white on top, and they moved fast.

"The air smells different," Owen said. "Damp. You can smell it if you stop."

Taro stopped and sniffed. He smelled something — wet grass, maybe, or something else.

"The sheep know," Owen said. "They always go down before rain."

Emma looked at the clouds. They were closer now than they had been a moment ago. The hill was still far, but the clouds were not.

"Rain by evening," Owen said.

He did not say anything else. He pushed off the gate and walked on down the path.

Taro looked at the clouds. Emma looked at the sheep. The sheep kept moving. The clouds kept coming.

They walked home. The air was changing. They did not need to be told.
