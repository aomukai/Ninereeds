Emma stood on the flat stone, looking across the pond. The water was still enough to show the sky upside down.  

"Let's go all the way around," Taro said, already stepping off the stone.  

The near side was sun-warmed and open. The far side, under the willow, was different. The ground was soft, the air cool. Roots twisted into the water like fingers. Biscuit pushed through the reeds, his nose twitching.  

"Biscuit, come back!" Emma called. He ignored her, tail wagging.  

They circled slowly. The mud sucked at their shoes. Dragonflies darted over the surface, touching down and lifting again. From this side, the flat stone looked small and far away.  

Biscuit barked suddenly, his front paws deep in the reeds. Something rustled.  

"Biscuit—!"  

He came back reluctantly, shaking water from his fur. Taro crouched to peer into the reeds. "Just a frog," he said.  

Emma touched the willow’s hanging branches. The bark was rough, the leaves smooth. The same pond, but not the same at all.
