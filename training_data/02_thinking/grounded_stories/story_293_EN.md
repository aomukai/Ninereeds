The workshop smelled of sawdust and linseed oil. Mei stood at the workbench, watching.

Vern held a block of wood in his hands. Ash, pale and straight-grained. He turned it over once, then set it on the bench.

He picked up a wedge from the row of tools. Metal, dull grey. Mei looked at it. One end was thick. The other end was thin, almost a line.

Vern placed the thin end against the top of the block, right where a dark line ran through the wood. He lifted the mallet.

*Thump.*

The wedge went in a little way. The wood did not move.

*Thump.*

Deeper. A thin crack opened along the grain, following the dark line.

*Thump.*

The crack ran the length of the block. The two halves tilted apart and stopped, held open by the wedge.

Vern set down the mallet. He picked up the wedge and held it out to Mei.

"The wedge is thicker at one end," he said. "That is what makes it work. You drive it in. The taper forces the wood apart."

Mei took the wedge. It was heavier than it looked. She touched the thin end, then the thick end. The angle between them told her everything.
