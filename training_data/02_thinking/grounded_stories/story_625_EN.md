The flat stone at the pond was warm from the morning sun. Emma knelt at the edge and looked down into the shallows.

"Look," she said.

Clear jelly clung to the reeds. Inside each blob was a black dot, small as a full stop.

Mei leaned over her shoulder. "Frog eggs," she said. "Last week they weren't here."

A week later they came back. The dots had changed. Now they were tiny shapes with tails, wriggling in the water.

"Tadpoles," Emma said.

She put her finger in. The tadpoles scattered and then came back.

Taro waded in without taking his shoes off. He cupped his hands and scooped. Water ran through his fingers. The tadpole slipped away.

"It's too fast," he said.

Mei pointed. "That one has legs. Tiny ones. At the back."

Emma watched. The tadpole moved differently from the others. Its tail was shorter. Two small bumps stuck out where legs would be.

They came again the next week. The tadpole with legs had grown proper back legs. Its front legs were just starting.

"It's changing," Mei said.

She sat very still on the flat stone. The water was clear. The tadpoles drifted.
