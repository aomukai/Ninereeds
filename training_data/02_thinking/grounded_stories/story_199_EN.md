The rain stopped in the night, but the stream was still loud.

Mei stood at the mill door and listened. The sound was different from before — deeper, faster, a rumble that she felt in her chest.

She stepped inside. The stone floor was damp. The wheel was turning.

It did not turn slowly, the way it did on dry days when the sluice was half-closed. It turned fast. The paddles caught the water and threw it sideways in sheets. Drops hit the stone wall. Drops hit the floor. The whole room was full of noise and wet.

Biscuit came to the doorway. He put his nose in. Then he backed out, shook his head, and barked once.

Mei did not move. She stood where the flour dust did not reach her eyes, and she felt the rhythm of the wheel through the soles of her boots. The floor vibrated. The beat was steady and hard, like a heart under stone.

Biscuit barked again. He wanted her to come out.

Mei turned and ran. Biscuit ran beside her, splashing through puddles in the lane. His fur was dark with spray. He shook when they stopped, and water flew everywhere.
