Gran raked the leaves into a pile. The oak was almost bare now, its branches black against the sky. A few red and yellow leaves still clung, trembling in the wind.  

Emma picked up an acorn. It was smooth and brown, cool in her palm. Taro ran past, kicking up leaves. They crunched under his boots.  

"Jump!" he shouted.  

Emma dropped the acorn and leaped into the pile. The leaves rustled and crackled. They smelled like rain and earth.  

Gran leaned on her rake. "One left," she said, pointing up.  

A single yellow leaf twirled on its stem. The wind tugged. It spun down, landing softly on Emma’s knee.  

Taro grabbed it. "Got it!" He held it up to the light. The veins stood out like tiny roads.  

Gran swept the last leaves into the pile. The wind lifted a few, scattering them across the field.  

Emma lay back. The sky was pale blue through the oak’s bare branches.
