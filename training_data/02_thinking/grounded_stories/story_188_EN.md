The sofa was too small for two, but they made it work. Emma curled at one end, Taro at the other, their socked feet meeting in the middle like two islands in a blanket sea. The brown satin-edged blanket barely stretched across both of them.  

"Your feet are cold," Emma said.  

"Your nose is red," Taro countered, but he wiggled his toes away from her knees.  

Gran appeared with two steaming bowls. The smell of onion and thyme filled the room. She balanced them on the low table, then tugged the blanket’s edge where it dipped between them.  

"Different ends, same soup," she said.  

Emma’s throat hurt when she swallowed. Taro’s forehead was still too warm. The sofa cushions held the shape of a hundred afternoons, but never quite like this — two sets of sniffles, two spoons clinking, and the winter light pressing at the half-drawn curtains.  

Taro sneezed. The blanket slipped.  

Emma grabbed her corner before it could escape.
