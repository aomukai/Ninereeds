The crate sat on the stone floor of the root cellar. It was full of apples, the ones Gran had picked last week. The wood was dark and the sides bulged a little.

Taro wrapped his fingers under the edge. He bent his knees, the way Gran did when she lifted the heavy pot. He pulled.

The crate did not move.

He pulled again. His arms strained. His face went tight. The apples inside shifted a little, but the crate stayed where it was.

Gran’s hand came down on the corner of the crate, next to his.

“Push,” she said. “Not pull.”

Taro let go and moved to the other side. He put both hands flat against the wood. Gran kept her hand on her corner. Together, they pushed.

The crate slid an inch.

Then another inch.

It made a low scraping sound against the stone. Taro pushed harder. His feet braced against the floor. The crate moved again, slow and heavy, until it sat against the wall where Gran wanted it.

Gran straightened and wiped her hands on her apron.

“That’s it,” she said.

Taro looked at the crate. It had moved.
