The sluice lever was a long bar of dark metal. Mei took hold of it with both hands. She pulled.

Nothing happened at first. Then a sound came from somewhere below the floor—a deep, hollow rush. Water.

Emma stepped back. The wheel outside began to move. It turned slowly, a dark curve against the light. Then faster. Water fell from the paddles in a steady sheet.

“Now,” Mei said.

She lifted a wooden lever on the side of the hopper. Grain poured down into the dark space between the stones. For a moment there was only the sound of the wheel and the water.

Then the grinding started.

It was low. Steady. Emma could feel it through the stone floor before she heard it clearly. A hum that went into her feet and stayed there.

“That is the real start,” Mei said.

She stood beside the hopper with her hand still on the lever. Flour dust was already settling on her sleeves. The wheel kept turning. The grinding kept on, low and even, filling the room like something solid.

Emma watched the flour come out below, fine and pale, into the cloth sack. The sound did not change. It simply continued, steady as breath.
