Emma put the seeds into her palm. They were small and hard and pale green, with a dark line down one side.

“These?” she said.

“These,” Gran said, and she made a hole with her finger and dropped one in.

Mei came to watch. She knelt at the edge of the vegetable patch and put her finger in the soil the same way Gran had done.

---

The shoots came up in a week. Two leaves, then four. The stems were pale at first and then they turned green and strong.

Emma and Mei stood at the edge of the patch and measured with their eyes.

“It is taller than my knee,” Mei said.

“Mine too,” Emma said.

---

In summer the bean plants were tall. They had flowers — small, purple, like little closed mouths. Mei touched one. It did not open.

---

In early autumn the pods were fat. Gran picked a handful. They were green and smooth. She split one open with her thumb. Inside were seeds, pale and soft.

“Not ready,” she said.

---

In late autumn the pods were dry and brown. The leaves had fallen. The stems were bare.

Mei picked a pod. It was light. She squeezed it and it split open with a small crack.

The seeds fell into her hand. Hard. Brown. They rattled when she shook them.

She looked at Emma.

“Like the ones in spring,” she said.

Emma opened another pod. The seeds were the same.
