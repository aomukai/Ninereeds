The main street was almost empty. The trees along it had no leaves — just bare branches against a pale sky. Frost covered the bench on the green. It looked cold, even to look at.

Emma walked beside Gran. Taro walked on the other side. Their footsteps were loud on the stone. The street was very quiet.

A thin line of smoke rose from the bakery chimney. It went straight up into the white air and did not move sideways.

"Look," Emma said.

The bakery window was warm and lit. Light fell onto the pavement in a yellow square. Frost had not touched the glass.

They stopped. Emma put her hand near the window. The glass was warm on her palm.

Taro breathed out. His breath made a cloud and then it was gone.

Gran did not say anything. She stood behind them with her hands in her coat pockets.

The street was still. The smoke was still. The light in the bakery window did not move.

Then Gran turned and they walked on. Their footsteps were loud again in the quiet.
