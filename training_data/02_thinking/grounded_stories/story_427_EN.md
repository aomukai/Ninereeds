Gran lifted the cellar door. It was heavy. She propped it with the iron bar.

Taro went first. Mei followed. The stone steps were cold and worn.

At the bottom, Taro held the lamp up. The shelf was bare. Where the jars had stood all winter, there was only dust and a few circles where the bottoms had pressed.

“Time to wash them,” Gran said from the top of the steps.

Mei reached for the first jar. She turned it in her hands. The label said *tomatoes* in Gran’s careful writing. She peeled it off and put it aside.

Taro took the next one. *Beans*. He pulled the label away. The paper came off clean, but a corner of glue stayed. He rubbed it with his thumb.

Mei worked along the shelf. *Plums*. *Pears*. *Cabbage*. Each label came off and went into a small pile.

Taro stacked the jars by size. The big ones at the back. The small ones in front.

Gran came down the steps. She took the first stack and carried it up.

When they were all out, Taro looked at the shelf again. Empty. The dust was still there where the jars had been.

“Next year,” Gran said from above, “they will be full again.”
