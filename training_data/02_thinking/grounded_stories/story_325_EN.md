The market stall had a small brass scale. It sat on the wooden table between the jars of honey and the bundles of dried herbs.

Emma picked up a single dried bay leaf. It was light in her hand — so light she could barely feel it.

She placed it on the scale.

The arm did not move.

Mei leaned in. She picked another leaf from the bundle. She added it to the scale.

The arm trembled. A small shiver ran through it. It did not tip.

Yun watched from behind the table. Her hands were still — she had stopped straightening the jars.

She reached into the bundle. She took a third leaf. She set it on the scale.

The arm moved. It sank. It settled.

Emma watched the small movement. The leaf was nothing in her hand. Three leaves together were enough to make the arm tip.

Mei touched the edge of the scale with one finger.

"One is too light," she said.

"Two is almost," said Yun.

"Three is enough," said Emma.

She left the leaves on the scale. She picked up the bundle from the table. She felt its weight in her palm — heavier than she had expected.
