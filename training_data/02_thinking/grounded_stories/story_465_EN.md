Gran dug in the vegetable patch. The fork went into the earth with a dull sound. She turned it, lifted, and the soil broke open in dark clods.

Biscuit lay near her feet. His head was on his paws. His eyes followed her hands — down, lift, turn, down again. He did not move.

Bello sat by the gate. He watched the lane. Then he watched the garden. Then he watched Biscuit. He did not go anywhere.

Dou was in the apple tree fork. The white ear pointed down. She sat very still, her tail wrapped around her paws. She looked at everything and nothing.

Emma sat on the stone bench. Taro sat beside her. No one spoke. The sun came through the new leaves and made small moving shapes on the ground. A leaf fell onto the path. It did not make a sound.

Biscuit yawned. Bello put his head down between his paws. Dou blinked once, very slowly.

Gran lifted another forkful of earth. The light was warm. The shade from the apple tree reached the corner of the bench.

No one was doing anything. They were all there together, doing nothing at all.
