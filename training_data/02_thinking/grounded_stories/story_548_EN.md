The light was low. The sky above the field was pale and cold. Emma walked beside Gran along the meadow path.

Owen was at the gate. He was leaning on it with both arms. His sheep were far off, little grey shapes in the dusk. Almost invisible.

He did not turn when they came near. He just said, quiet, "It will be cold tonight."

Gran stopped. Emma stopped too. The grass was wet. The shadows were long.

Gran said, "Yes. Frost before morning."

Owen nodded. He looked out across the field for a moment longer. Then he pushed off the gate with his shoulder. It swung shut behind him. He walked toward the farm without looking back.

Gran stood still. Emma watched Owen go. His shape got smaller. The dusk swallowed him.

Then Gran said, "Come. Time to go home."

They turned. The lane was dark ahead. The cottage chimney was a thin shape above the trees. The light in the kitchen window was already on.

Gran walked slowly. Emma matched her step. The gate behind them clicked once in the quiet air.
