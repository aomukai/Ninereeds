The woodpile was high, the logs stacked neatly against the shed wall. Taro tugged his mittens tighter and picked up three logs, one after the other. They were rough against his hands, smelling faintly of pine. He carried them to the fireplace, careful not to drop any.

Gran stirred the pot on the fire. "One more," she said, without looking up.

Emma went out to the woodpile. The last log was smaller, smooth and cold. She carried it inside and set it by the hearth.

Taro crouched by the stack. "One, two, three — and Emma's makes four." He looked up. "Three and one — same as before. Four."

Gran nodded. "Enough for now."

The fire crackled, throwing warmth across the room. Emma held her hands out to the flames, fingers tingling as they warmed. Taro sat back on his heels, watching the logs catch. Outside, the wind whispered against the window, but inside, the fire burned steady and bright.
