The pond was different today.

Emma stood at the edge and looked. The water near the reeds had turned pale and hard. A thin white layer covered the dark underneath.

Taro knelt down. He touched it with one fingertip.

"Cold," he said.

The ice was smooth and still. It did not move when he pressed. He tapped it. A small sound, like a stone on glass.

Biscuit came to see. He sniffed the white edge, then put his front paw on it.

The ice cracked.

A line ran sideways from his paw. Then another. Water pushed up through the break, dark and wet. Biscuit pulled his paw back and looked at it.

Emma knelt beside Taro. She put her hand flat on the ice near the reeds. It was hard and cold against her palm. The ice did not break where she touched. But she could see through it, down into the dark water beneath.

"The same water," she said.

Taro looked at the crack. At the water rising through it. At the solid white further in.

"Hard," he said. "And soft."

Biscuit shook his paw and walked away. Bello came close, sniffed the broken edge, and stepped back.

The ice stayed where it was. The water stayed where it was. Two things from one thing, right there at the pond edge.
