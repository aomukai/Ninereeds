Emma woke to a white lace on the skylight. It spread in thin branches, like tiny roots across the glass. She sat up, her breath puffing in the cold air.  

She reached a finger toward the frost. The tip touched a single line—just for a second—and where her finger had been, the frost vanished. A clear spot shone through, dark and sharp against the white. She traced another line, slower this time. The frost melted under her touch, leaving a wet streak behind.  

Her breath fogged the glass. The patterns blurred, then sharpened again as the fog faded. Outside, the world was still and bright. The oak’s bare branches stood black against the morning sky.  

Emma pressed her whole hand against the pane. The cold bit her palm. When she pulled away, five clear fingers glistened in the frost. She leaned close, breathing softly. The fog spread, then shrank, then spread again.  

Downstairs, the fire crackled. A floorboard creaked. But up here, the frost held its patterns—except where her fingers had been.
