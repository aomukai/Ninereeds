Biscuit bounded down the bank and splashed straight into the pond, sending ripples across the still water. His paws stirred up little clouds of mud from the bottom. Bello trotted after him but stopped at the very edge, where the reeds met the water. He stretched one paw forward, touched the surface, and jerked it back.  

Emma crouched beside him. "It’s nice," she said, trailing her fingers in the water. Bello sniffed her wet hand but didn’t move. He sat down firmly, ears pricked, watching Biscuit paddle in circles.  

Taro waded in up to his knees. "Come on, Bello!" He slapped the water, sending droplets flying. Bello ducked his head but didn’t budge.  

Biscuit surged out, shaking himself so hard that water sprayed Emma’s legs. He bounded back in before the drops had even settled. Bello watched, tail twitching. He took one step forward—then stepped back again.  

Emma wiped her damp arm on her shorts. "Maybe next time," she said. Bello sat very straight, eyes fixed on Biscuit’s wagging tail in the pond. Not yet.
