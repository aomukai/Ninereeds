Riku stopped his bicycle beside Vern's workshop. He leaned it against the wall. The red paint was warm from the sun.

He lifted the bag. It was heavy with letters.

The workshop door stood open. Riku stepped inside. The floor was covered in shavings. The air smelled of sawdust and oil.

He handed the letter to Vern. Vern took it without looking up. He was holding a piece of wood in his hands. It was pale and smooth.

Riku came back out into the sun. He blinked. The side street was bright after the dark of the workshop.

Vern followed him to the door. He held the piece of wood up. It was long and straight.

Riku looked at it. He did not say anything. He looked at the wood, and then at the bicycle against the wall.

Vern turned the wood in his hands. The grain was even. The edges were clean.

Riku nodded once. He lifted his bag onto his shoulder. He took the bicycle by the handlebars and pushed it away from the wall.

The wheels turned on the stones. He rode down the side street, past the shade of the wall, into the sun at the corner.

Vern stood in the doorway a moment longer. Then he went back inside.
