Emma stood by the garden bush, her fingers stained purple. She held the berries carefully, one by one, and dropped them into the bowl. The bushes were nearly bare now, and the bowl was only half full.

“Not enough,” Gran said, kneading dough at the table. “The jam needs more.”

Taro ran up, his hands cupped. “I know where there’s more,” he said. “At the hedgerow by the gate.”

They hurried down the path. The hedgerow was thick with berries, hanging like tiny purple jewels. Taro reached in and grabbed a handful. Emma did the same. The juice seeped into her fingers, sticky and sweet.

Back at the kitchen, Gran tipped their berries into the bowl. “One, two, three, four, five,” she counted.

Emma looked at Taro’s handful and hers. “Two and three — that is five,” she said.

Gran poured the berries into the pot, and the smell of warm fruit filled the kitchen.
