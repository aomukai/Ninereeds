Vern picked up a piece of wood. He laid it flat on the workbench.

He took a ruler from the wall. It was long and yellow, with black lines and numbers along one edge.

He put the ruler against the wood. He held it still. With a pencil, he drew a line at the number.

Then he picked up a second piece. He put the ruler against it. He found the same number. He drew another line.

"Now we see," Vern said.

Taro picked up both pieces. He held them side by side. The pencil lines matched exactly. The ends were even. The pieces were the same length.

Mei leaned in to look. "They are the same," she said.

Vern nodded. "The ruler does not guess. It tells you. You just have to read it."

He picked up the saw. He lined the teeth against the first pencil mark. He pushed. The saw bit into the wood.

Taro watched the cut. It followed the line. It did not wander.

Vern measured the second piece the same way. The saw went through cleanly.

He put the two pieces together on the bench. They sat side by side, equal from end to end.

Mei touched the cut edge. It was smooth. "No gap," she said.

"No gap," said Vern. "That is what the ruler is for."
