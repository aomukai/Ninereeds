Vern's workshop was warm. The stove ticked. Sawdust lay thick on the floor.

Vern was carrying a small wooden box. His hand slipped. The box tipped.

Nails fell everywhere. They vanished into the sawdust. Gone.

Vern did not groan. He went to the shelf and took down a grey block. It was smooth. Dull. He held it over the sawdust.

Nothing happened at first.

Then a nail twitched. Then another. They rose through the sawdust like slow fish. They stuck to the grey block with a soft click. More came. Clusters of them. They jumped the last inch.

Taro watched.

Vern handed him the block. It was heavier than it looked. Cold.

"Hold it low," Vern said.

Taro held it near the floor. A nail stirred. Then another. They pulled toward the block. Taro felt the pull through his fingers — a soft, steady tug. The nails came faster as he lowered the block. They clicked against it. One stuck to the side. Taro picked it off and held it an inch away. The nail jumped back to the block.

Vern swept the remaining sawdust. He found three more nails by sound alone — the click of metal on the floor.

Taro carried the grey block to the bench. The nails stayed. They did not fall.
