Vern chose a plank from the stack near the door. He ran his thumb along the edge. "Good," he said.

He set it on the workbench. The wood was pale and clean-smelling. He took a ruler from the wall — metal, with numbers worn faint at one end — and laid it flat.

"Watch."

He drew a line with a pencil. Then another. Then he connected them. The rectangle was thin and long. He picked up a small metal tool — a square — and pressed it into each corner. The legs of the square fit exactly against the edges. He nodded.

He handed the saw to Taro. "Put the line on this side."

Taro took the saw. The handle was smooth. He looked at the line Vern had drawn. He put the saw's teeth against it.

"Check it," Vern said.

Taro looked. The line was still there, right beside the teeth. He looked up at Vern.

"Then cut," Vern said.

Taro pulled the saw. The teeth bit into the wood. The sound was rough and even. Emma watched. Biscuit lay by the door and watched too.

Taro pulled again. Then again. The saw moved along the line.

When the piece came free, Taro held it up. The line was still there, clean and straight, running along the top edge of the cut.

Vern picked up the square again. He pressed it into the new corner. He did not say anything. He did not need to.
