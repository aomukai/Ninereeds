The cellar door was propped open with the iron bar. Cold air breathed up from the stones.

Taro went first, holding the basket. Emma followed. Mei came last, her hand on the wall.

The lamp was already burning on the shelf. Its flame moved when they moved.

“Pick four potatoes,” Gran called down. “Three turnips. And two jars of beans from the back.”

Taro held the basket open. Emma knelt on the earth floor and felt for the potatoes. They were cool and dusty. She counted into the basket: one, two, three, four.

Mei knew the jars. She lifted two from the shelf — beans in dark liquid — and set them in the basket beside the potatoes.

Taro pulled three turnips from the wooden box. A small clod of earth fell from the last one.

“Anything else?” he said.

“Onions,” Emma said. “She always wants onions.”

Taro reached up and untied a rope of onions from the beam. Five came away in his hand.

The steps were steep going up. Taro carried the basket with both hands. Emma took one jar. Mei took the other.

Gran was at the table, peeling with a small knife. She looked at what they set down.

“Good,” she said. “That will do.”
