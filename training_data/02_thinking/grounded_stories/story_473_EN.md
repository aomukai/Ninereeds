The workshop door stood open. Warm air came in, carrying the smell of cut grass.

Mei stepped inside first. Dou followed at her heels, one paw at a time.

The long workbench ran along the back wall. Vern stood at the far end, a plane in his hands. He pushed it forward along the board. The blade bit. A thin curl of wood lifted and fell onto the bench.

Dou looked up. Her ears turned toward the sound.

Mei pointed to a low shelf near the window. Dou understood. She jumped up in one smooth motion, turned twice, and sat. Her tail wrapped around her paws. She watched the plane move.

The noise was steady. *Shave. Pause. Shave.* The shavings piled on the bench—pale, curled, thin at the edges.

Dou did not flinch. She blinked slowly. Her whiskers did not move.

Vern did not stop working. His hand found the plane handle again. He pushed. Another curl lifted.

“She knows where to sit,” he said.

He did not look up. His hand kept moving. The plane kept cutting.

Mei sat on the stool near the door and watched them both.
