Gran lifted the iron bar. The cellar door swung open, and a puff of cool air rose into the hot garden.

“Fetch six apples from the crate,” Gran said. “The ones near the front.”

Emma went first. The steps were steep and worn. She put her hand on the wall to steady herself.

Mei followed. Gran handed her the lamp. The flame was small and yellow.

At the bottom, the air changed. It was cool and still. It smelled of earth and stored things.

Mei held the lamp up. The light fell on crates and jars and sacks. Shadows moved when she turned.

“There,” Emma said.

The apple crate was against the far wall. The apples smelled sweet and a little bit like fermentation. Emma counted six into her skirt, one at a time.

Mei looked at the shelves. Jars of plums. Jars of beans. A rope of onions hanging from a beam. The light made the jars glow.

“It is very cool down here,” Mei said.

“Gran says it stays cool all summer.”

They climbed back up. The heat hit them at the top like a wall. Gran was at the kitchen door.

“Six?” she said.

Emma showed her.

Behind them, Mei set the lamp down. The flame steadied and the shadows went still.
