Gran wrote a list. Emma held it.

At the bakery, Hana was wiping the counter. She looked up and smiled.

“Warm today,” she said.

Gran agreed. They talked about the sun and the garden. Emma stood by the door and watched the light on the floor. It was bright and still.

Hana wrapped the bread in paper. Gran paid. They stepped outside.

Riku was leaning his red bicycle against the wall. He had a letter in his hand.

“For you,” he said to Gran. “Came this morning.”

Gran took it. She did not open it. She stood by the gate and talked to Riku about the lane and the dust and whether the weather would hold.

Emma waited. She shifted her weight from one foot to the other. The bread was warm through the paper.

The sun moved. The shadow of the bakery sign crept across the pavement.

Gran was still talking. Riku was still leaning on his bicycle. The letter was still unopened in Gran’s hand.

Emma looked at the bread. She looked at the shadow. She looked at the gate that led home.

The errand was taking longer than she had thought.
