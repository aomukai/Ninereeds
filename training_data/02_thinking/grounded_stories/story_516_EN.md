Emma sat at her desk. She knew the answer. She put her hand up.

Her hand stayed in the air. Clara looked her way. Then Clara looked past her.

Emma waited. Her arm began to feel heavy.

Clara looked at Mei. Mei sat very still. Her hands were on her desk. She had not raised her hand.

“Mei,” Clara said.

Mei looked at her desk. Then she looked at the board. The chalk marks were white and clear.

“Mei,” Clara said again. Her voice was soft. “What do you see?”

Mei opened her mouth. Her voice was very small. “The numbers. They go up.”

Emma leaned forward to hear. The words almost disappeared into the air.

Clara nodded. She wrote something on the board with the chalk. The chalk made a small tapping sound.

Mei looked at her desk again. Her hands were still.

Emma lowered her hand. She looked at Mei. Mei’s face was quiet. Her voice had been very small. But she had answered.
