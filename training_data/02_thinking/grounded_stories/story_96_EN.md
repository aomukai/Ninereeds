Emma knelt on the flat stone. The pond was smooth as glass. Small fish hung just below the surface, their shadows dark and sharp on the sandy bottom. The light touched everything the same way—no bright spots, no hiding places.  

"Look," she whispered.  

Taro crouched beside her. A fish flicked its tail and vanished in a swirl of silt.  

After lunch, they came back. The willow’s shadow cut the pond in two—one side bright, the other dark. The fish were gone. Or not gone, Emma realized, but deeper, shapes moving in the murk where the light didn’t reach.  

"It’s different," Taro said.  

Emma dipped her fingers in. The water was warmer now. A dragonfly skimmed the surface where the fish had been that morning.  

They sat until the shadow reached the stone. Then they stood, brushed off their knees, and walked back up the path without saying why.
