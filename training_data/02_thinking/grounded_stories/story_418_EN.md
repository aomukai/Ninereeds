Gran lifted the heavy door and propped it open with the iron bar. Cold air rose from the steps.

She lit the lamp. The flame wavered, then settled. Shadows climbed the walls.

"Go on down," she said. "We need to see what we have left."

Emma went first, one hand on the wall. The steps were worn and steep. Taro followed, and the lamp light swayed behind him.

The root cellar smelled of earth and apples. Emma held the lamp up. The shelves ran along the far wall.

"Read the labels," Gran said from the top of the stairs.

Emma leaned in. "Plums. Beans. Tomatoes."

Taro pointed at the middle shelf. "I can count those."

He touched each jar as he said the number. Seven bean jars. Four jars of tomatoes. Two jars of pickled cabbage.

One shelf was bare. Empty. The wood showed through.

Emma looked up at the ceiling. The onion rope hung from a beam. In autumn it had been thick and heavy. Now the onions were fewer. The rope was half as long, and what remained hung loose.

"Spring is coming," Gran said. She was not looking at them. She was already turning back up the stairs. "We will plant more soon."
