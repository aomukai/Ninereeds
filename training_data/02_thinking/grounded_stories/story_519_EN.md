There was one swing in the schoolyard. It hung from the big ash tree by two chains.

"Twenty pushes each," said Clara. "Then the next person."

Taro was first. He pushed hard. His legs pumped. The swing went high and fast. Up, down, up, down. In ten seconds he was done. He jumped off and the swing was still swinging.

Emma went next. She pushed slow. Each push took her higher. The chains creaked. Up she went, then back, then up again. She looked at the clouds. She looked at the ground far below. Thirty seconds passed before she stopped.

Mei went third. She counted aloud. "One, two, three." Her pushes were even. The swing rose and fell like a breath. At twenty she stopped and stood up straight.

"Both ways are fair," said Clara, when Taro frowned. "Taro had twenty pushes. Emma had twenty pushes. The rule is the number, not how fast."

Mei nodded. She knew.

Taro thought about it. Then he pushed the swing for the next person. It swung empty for a moment, chains clinking, before the next child sat down.
