The market street was quiet now. The morning crowds had gone home. Only a few stalls still had things on their tables.

Hana walked slowly along the street. Her apron still had flour on it from the morning bake. She stopped at Yun's stall.

Three loaves sat on the wooden board. They were the ones nobody had chosen. The smallest one was at the front.

Hana picked it up. It was still warm. She turned it over in her hands.

"How much for this one?" she said.

Yun looked at the loaf. Then she looked at the clock on the church tower.

"Half price," she said. "It is the end of the day."

Hana nodded. She reached into her pocket and took out a coin. She set it on the table. Yun picked it up and dropped it into her tin.

Hana wrapped the bread in a white cloth. She tucked the corners under. The warmth stayed inside.

She walked away down the street. The loaf was light against her side.

Behind her, Yun began to pack the other two loaves into a basket. The stall was almost empty now. The day was done.
