Gran lifted the first sack of grain onto her shoulder. It was heavy. She carried it to the hopper and tipped it in.

Yun brought the second sack. She tied the cloth opening with a quick twist of her wrist. “That is the last one,” she said.

Mei stood by the sluice lever. She waited.

Gran nodded.

Mei pulled. The water wheel groaned and began to turn. The floor hummed under their feet. The millstones started their low, grinding song.

The flour came out below, fine and pale, into a cloth sack. It filled slowly. Grain by grain.

Mei watched the sack grow fat. Dust rose from it and settled on her arms, her sleeves, her shoes. She did not wipe it off.

When the first sack was full, Gran tied the neck and set it aside. Yun refilled the hopper.

Mei worked the sluice again. The wheel turned faster. The stones ground.

The second sack filled.

The third.

Gran lifted one sack of flour and tested its weight in her hands. “That will last us until Saturday,” she said.

Mei looked down at her clothes. They were white from the dust. Even her eyelashes were white.

Yun smiled. She did not say anything. She just handed Mei a cloth to wipe her face.

Mei rubbed her cheeks. The flour came off in streaks.

The wheel slowed. The room went quiet.

They had enough.
