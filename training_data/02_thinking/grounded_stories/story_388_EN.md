Gran stood at the top of the hill with Emma beside her.

The sky to the west was grey. Low and heavy, like a lid pressing down on the valley.

Emma felt the cold on her cheeks. Her nose was cold too. She pulled her scarf up higher.

“It’s going to snow,” Gran said.

Emma looked at the sky. “When?”

“By evening.” Gran turned her face into the wind. “You can feel it coming.”

Emma felt the wind. It was sharp and dry. It pushed at her coat.

The hill was bare. The grass was stiff and brown. No sheep today — Owen had brought them down early.

Gran started walking back. Emma followed. The path was hard under her boots.

Halfway down, Emma stopped and looked back. The village was below. The lights were on in the windows. Smoke rose from the chimneys.

Gran waited. She did not call.

Emma turned and walked again. The cottage was ahead. The kitchen window was yellow.

They reached the garden gate. Gran pushed it open.

The wind pulled at Emma’s hair.

Inside, the kitchen was warm. The kettle was on the stove. Gran lit the lamp.

Emma sat at the table and watched the window.

The first snowflake landed on the glass.
