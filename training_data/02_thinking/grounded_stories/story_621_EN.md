Emma sat on the big oak root. The bark was rough under her hand.

She looked down. A line of ants moved up the side of the root, one after another, close together. She had never seen them here before.

She watched. The line went straight, then curved. It went up over a lump of bark and into a crack.

Emma leaned closer. The crack was narrow and dark. She had passed it a hundred times. She had never looked inside.

She put her finger on the ground behind the last ant. She followed the trail. Her finger moved slow. Past the lump. Up the bark. To the crack.

The ants went in, one by one. They disappeared.

Emma sat back. She looked at the crack again. It was just a crack. But the ants knew it was there.

Biscuit pushed his nose against the root. He sniffed hard. Then he sneezed.

The ants kept moving. The line did not stop.
