Mei pulled the heavy cellar door open. It creaked. She held the lamp high.

The root cellar was cool. It smelled of earth and apples and something sweet that was almost gone. Mei set the lamp on a crate. Then she looked at the jars.

They were on the middle shelf. Preserved plums. Pickled cabbage. Dried beans. Each one had a label with writing on it. Mei lifted a jar of plums and read the label. *September*, it said.

She put it down. Then she took all the jars off the shelf.

First she lined them up on the floor. September jars on the left. October in the middle. November on the right. She looked at the dates again. Then she put them back on the shelf in order: newest at the back, oldest at the front.

The line was straight.

Gran came down the steps. She stood at the bottom and looked at the shelf. She did not say anything. She picked up a jar from the front, turned it in her hands, and put it back exactly where it had been.

Then she turned and went back up the steps.

The lamp flickered. Mei straightened the line by a finger's width. Then she followed Gran.
