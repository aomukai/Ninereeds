The cellar door was heavy. Gran propped it open with the iron bar. The lamp flame flickered and steadied.

Emma went down first. The steps were cold through her socks. The smell of earth and apples and something sharp filled the air.

"Count the green beans," Gran said from the top of the stairs.

Emma found the shelf. The jars were lined up in two rows. She touched each one as she counted. "Nine."

Taro came down beside her. He stood on his toes and looked at the other shelf. "Pickled turnips. Twelve."

Gran's voice came down the stairs. "We use two jars a week. How long will they last?"

Emma looked at the jars. Nine. Two jars a week. She thought about the weeks. Two, four, six, eight. Nine was not eight. Nine was one more.

"Four weeks," she said. "And one jar left over. So maybe five weeks."

Taro counted on his fingers. "Twelve is six weeks."

"January is four weeks," Emma said. "February is four weeks. That is eight."

Gran nodded from the top of the stairs. "So we need to know what we have by February."

The lamp made the empty spaces on the shelf look darker than the jars.
