Taro stood at the base of the oak and looked up.

There was a branch higher than the low one. A good branch. He jumped and caught it. His fingers slipped. He landed hard on his feet.

He tried again. His hands touched the bark. He could not hold.

He jumped a third time. His chest hit the branch. For a moment he was there. Then his weight pulled him off. He landed on his side on the root.

Taro sat on the root. He did not get up. He looked at the ground.

Emma walked around the tree. She found an acorn cap from last year. Dry and brown. She picked it up and turned it in her fingers. The outside was rough. The inside was smooth.

She looked at Taro. He was still sitting. His hands were flat on his knees.

She did not say anything.

A robin moved in the branches above. Taro did not look up.

After a while he stood. He brushed his hands on his trousers. He walked past Emma without stopping. The path went toward the pond.

Emma watched him go. She put the acorn cap on the root and followed.
