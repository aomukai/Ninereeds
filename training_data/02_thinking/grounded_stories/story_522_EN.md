Clara held the collage up to the window.

The paper bent a little at the edges. The leaves were brown now. Some were so thin that the light came right through them.

Emma leaned in close. She pointed to a leaf near the top. "That one is mine," she said. "I pressed it under the book."

"It is still flat," Clara said.

Taro looked at the corner. "The stem came off that one," he said.

Mei touched it gently with one finger. "It was loose yesterday," she said. "I thought the glue would hold."

Clara tilted the paper. "That is how dried things are," she said. "They get brittle. They do not stay the way you first put them."

She turned the collage around so everyone could see the back. The stems that had fallen lay against the paper where she had moved them.

"Can we hang it now?" Emma asked.

Clara looked at the light through the leaves again. "By the window," she said. "So the light still comes through."

She carried it to the wall beside the nature table. Emma held the corner while Clara pinned it. The dry leaves rustled very softly.

Mei stepped back to look. "It is done," she said.

Taro looked at it from the side. Then he looked again from straight on. "It looks different from far away," he said.
