Vern took a piece of pine from the stack. It was pale and straight.

He laid it on the workbench. The wood made a hollow sound against the wood.

Taro stood at the edge of the bench. He watched.

Vern picked up the ruler. It was long and yellow, with black lines all the way down. He put one end against the edge of the pine. His thumb moved along the ruler. He stopped. He marked with the pencil. A small, clear line.

Then he lifted the ruler. He put it back. He measured again.

Taro leaned closer. Vern's thumb moved again. It stopped at the same place. Then it moved a little more. A thumb's width. Vern watched his own hand. He did not look away.

He moved the pencil mark. Just a little. The old mark was still there, fainter.

Then he picked up the saw.

Taro said, "You checked."

Vern nodded. "Once to know. Twice to be sure."

He set the saw on the line and pushed. The teeth bit into the pine. The cut was straight and clean.

On the floor, the offcut lay beside the shoe. Taro picked it up. It was short. A finger's length. The correct piece was longer. It was right.
