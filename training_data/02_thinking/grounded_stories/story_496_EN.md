Clara picked up the chalk. It made a small tap against the blackboard.

She wrote a number. Seven.

Then she drew a line. Minus.

Then another number. Three.

Then she drew a line underneath.

Taro looked at the board. His hand went up before Clara had turned around.

“Yes, Taro?”

“I can do it,” he said.

He held up his left hand. All five fingers. Then two more on his right hand. Seven.

He looked at his fingers. He put three of them down.

Now four fingers were still up.

Emma counted them. One, two, three, four.

“Four,” she said.

Clara wrote 4 on the board, under the line.

“Seven take away three,” she said, “is four. That is the answer.”

Taro looked at his four fingers. He put them down.

Emma looked at the board. The numbers were white on the dark green. The chalk dust had settled on the ledge below.

She counted again in her head. Seven. Take away three. Four.

It worked.
