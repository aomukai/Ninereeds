Biscuit was digging at the base of the wall. Mud flew between his back legs. His nose was brown to the tip.

Emma watched from the lavender patch. The earth came up in dark clumps. Biscuit's front legs worked fast.

When she looked up, Dou was there.

No sound. No warning. Just the small grey cat sitting on the top of the wall, one white ear tipped forward, tail wrapped around her paws.

Biscuit stopped digging. He looked up once. Sniffed. Then he put his nose back in the hole and kept going.

Bello came around the corner. He saw Dou on the wall. He took one step closer.

Dou did not move.

Bello stopped. His ears went up. His tail went still. He looked at Dou. He looked at Biscuit. He looked back at Dou.

Dou blinked once, very slowly.

Bello took a step back.

Emma watched. The sun was warm on the wall stones. The lavender smelled sharp and green. Dou sat like a small stone statue, grey against the grey stone, watching everything with her slow yellow eyes.

Then Dou stood. She turned. She walked along the top of the wall, one paw in front of the other, tail lifted high, and disappeared behind the apple tree.

Bello sat down where he was. Biscuit kept digging.
