Mei put her hand on the lever. It was cold and rough. She pulled it toward her.

It did not move.

She pulled harder. Her feet slid on the stone floor. Her fingers tightened. The lever gave a low groan and then it shifted. It came back, inch by inch, until it clicked into place.

The water slowed.

Taro watched it happen. The stream that had been rushing under the wheel grew thinner. The sound changed — from a roar to a pour to a trickle. The wheel turned slower. The spokes appeared one by one. Then the wheel stopped.

The silence was sudden and complete.

Taro heard his own breath. He heard the drip of water from the wheel into the channel below. He heard the soft scrape of Mei's shoes as she stepped back from the lever.

"That was hard," Mei said.

Taro nodded. He did not say anything. The mill was quiet now. The grinding sound was gone. The floor did not tremble. The air was still.

Mei touched the lever with one finger. "It takes all the water away," she said. "Down there it is just sitting. Waiting."
