The frost was white on the grass. Every blade stood stiff and separate.

Dou sat on the garden wall. The sun had found her patch of stone. She sat very still, her grey fur warm in the light, her white ear tipped forward.

Biscuit came down the path. His nose was low, following a smell along the base of the wall. He passed below the spot where Dou sat. He did not look up. He did not slow down. His paws left dark prints in the frost.

Bello stopped a few feet away. He sat down in the cold grass. His breath made small clouds. He looked at Dou across the white ground.

Dou did not look back at him. She blinked once, very slowly, at the sun.

Bello sat without moving. The frost held still around him. The distance between them was not far — a few steps of cold grass — but it was a distance Dou had chosen.

Bello waited. Biscuit had already gone around the corner.

Dou sat on the wall. Bello sat on the ground. The sun moved a little, and the frost began to shine.
