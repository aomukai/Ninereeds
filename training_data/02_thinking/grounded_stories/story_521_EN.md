The ash tree stood at the edge of the schoolyard. It had no leaves.

Emma stood under it and looked up. The branches made a net against the grey sky — thin at the ends like fingers, thick near the trunk where they joined. She could see every line, every fork, every place where one branch became two.

Taro put his hand flat on the bark. "It's cold," he said. "Really cold."

Emma touched it too. The bark was rough and hard. Her fingers found a crack that ran straight down like a dark line in the grey.

On the ground, the shadow of the tree was a tangle of thin shapes. No leaves to soften it. Just the black lines of branches crossing and crossing again.

The ground was hard. Frost had made the grass white and stiff. Where the shadow fell, the frost was thicker — the sun had not touched it all morning.

Taro walked around the trunk. He looked up from the other side. "It looks different from here."

Emma stayed where she was. The branches made a shape against the sky that she had never noticed before. Without the leaves, the tree was all structure — all line and fork and crack.

She traced the crack in the bark with her finger. It went all the way down to the root.
