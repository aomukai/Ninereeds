Emma pushed aside the leaves. The blackberries were still hard and green.  

"Not yet," Gran said, pinching one between her fingers.  

Every few days, Emma checked the same bush. The berries turned pink, then red. She touched one — firm, but not sharp anymore.  

"Not yet," Gran said, shaking her head.  

The red darkened. A cluster near the gate turned almost black, the fattest ones glossy in the sun. Emma held one carefully. It gave a little under her thumb.  

Gran reached past her. She didn’t say anything this time. She picked one, rolled it in her palm, then popped it into her mouth.  

Emma watched her face.  

Gran handed her a berry. It was warm from the sun. The juice burst sweet and deep, staining her fingers purple.  

Biscuit nosed at the lower branches. A ripe berry fell into the grass. He licked it up and sneezed.  

Emma reached for another. The thorns caught her sleeve. Gran freed her without a word and picked two more — one for each of them.
