Mei pushed open the low door to the mill. The air inside was cold, but not as cold as the lane.

"Here," she said, and led Emma past the heavy millstones, past the wooden hopper, to the far wall.

A gap in the floorboards showed the stream below, dark and fast. But the floor where they stood was dry. The wind did not reach this corner.

Mei pulled an old sack from a hook. She spread it on the boards.

"Sit here."

Emma sat. The wood was dry beneath her. She could hear the water moving under them, but she could not feel the cold from it. The sound filled the space — low and steady.

Through the gap, Emma could see the big wheel turning. Water fell from its blades in a long sheet. The light from the small window caught the drops and made them bright for a moment before they vanished.

"This is the best spot," Mei said. She sat down beside Emma, knees pulled up. "No draft. You can watch."

Emma watched. The wheel turned. The stream ran. The mill was warm in its own way — not from a fire, but from being dry and still and out of the wind.

Biscuit had not followed them in. He was outside, sniffing the wheel's wet track.
