The sack of grain sat on the mill floor.

Emma tried to lift it. She pulled at the rough cloth. The sack did not move. She pushed from underneath. It stayed where it was.

Mei tried. She set her feet wide and gripped the sides. She pulled hard. Her face went red. The sack did not lift.

Mei let go. She looked at Emma. Emma looked at the sack.

Mei walked to one end of the sack. "You take the other," she said.

Emma went to the other end. She bent her knees. Mei did the same.

"Now," Mei said.

They lifted together.

The sack came up off the floor. It was very heavy. Emma's arms shook. Mei breathed hard through her teeth. But the sack rose.

They took two steps. Three steps. They set it down on the low platform. It landed with a soft thump.

They stood still for a moment. Then Mei wiped her face with her sleeve.

"Alone," she said, "no."

Emma nodded. "Together," she said. "Yes."
