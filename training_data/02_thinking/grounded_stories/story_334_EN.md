Yun's hands moved over the eggs. One. Two. Three.

Each egg came from the crate beside her stall. She held it up, turned it once, and placed it into Mei's basket. The basket was lined with straw, soft and yellow.

Mei held the basket with both hands. She did not move. She watched each egg settle into its nest of straw.

Emma stood close. She watched too. The eggs were brown and warm-looking. One had a tiny crack near the top. Yun saw it and set it aside on the cloth.

"Four," said Yun. "Five."

The eggs filled the bottom of the basket. They touched each other. Mei shifted her grip when the weight changed.

"Six. Seven."

Emma counted in her head. She could see the basket was not full yet. There was space at the top.

Yun's hand went to the crate again. Eight. Nine. Ten.

She stopped and looked at the basket. Half full. The eggs sat in the straw, still and quiet.

Yun pulled a cloth from under the stall and spread it over the top. She tucked the edges under the basket rim.

"Half a dozen for the stall," she said. "Half a dozen for home."

Mei nodded. She carried the basket away from the table, careful with every step.
