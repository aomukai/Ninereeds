The morning sky was clear and blue. Emma crunched her toast and watched Gran at the window.  

"Rain by afternoon," Gran said, pointing west. "See those clouds coming?"  

Taro squinted. "I don’t see any."  

"You will." Gran wiped flour from her hands and went back to kneading.  

They took turns watching. First from the garden bench, then from under the apple tree. The breeze turned cool. By lunchtime, a line of grey smudged the horizon.  

"It’s happening!" Emma tugged Taro’s sleeve. The clouds thickened, rolling toward them like a slow wave.  

They ran inside just as the first drops hit the stones. The rain came fast then, pattering the garden path, drumming the cabbage leaves flat. Gran stood at the kitchen window, arms crossed.  

"Told you," she said.  

Taro pressed his nose to the glass. The west wind rattled the pear branches. Water streamed down the gutters and pooled by the gate.  

Emma cupped her hands to catch a drip from the eaves. It was cold. Just like Gran said it would be.
