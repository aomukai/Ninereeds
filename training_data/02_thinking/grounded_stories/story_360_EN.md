Gran walked up the hill with Biscuit and Bello. The grass was short and dry. The wind pulled at her coat.

Biscuit ran ahead, then stopped. His ears went up. Bello circled back and sat near Gran's feet.

A bicycle came up the lane. It was Riku. His red bag leaned to one side.

"Morning," he said. He stopped the bicycle beside them.

"Morning," said Gran.

Riku looked up the hill. "Going to the top?"

"Just to look," said Gran.

Riku wheeled the bicycle beside her. The tyres left a thin track in the dry grass. Biscuit sniffed the wheels. Bello stayed close.

At the top, the village was small below. The oak was a dark shape at the field's edge. The mill chimney stood thin against the sky.

Riku pointed west. "Look there."

Clouds were building. They sat low and thick, grey at the bottom and white at the top. They moved slowly, growing as they came.

Gran watched them. "The weather will change," she said.

Riku nodded. He did not look away from the clouds.

Biscuit sat down. Bello sat beside him. They all watched the clouds building in the west. The wind was cool now.
