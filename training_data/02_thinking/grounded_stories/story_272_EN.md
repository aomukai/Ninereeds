Mei stood at the workbench. Vern put a board on the flat surface.

“Hold it there,” he said.

Mei pressed her hands on the wood. The grain was smooth under her fingers. Vern picked up a pencil. He made two small marks near the ends of the board.

He lifted the saw. The teeth caught the edge. The sound was even — back and forth, back and forth. Dust fell in a thin line on the bench.

The cut end was rough. Vern ran his thumb across it once, then set the board aside.

He took two metal brackets from the peg above the bench. Each had three holes. He placed them against the wall and drove a nail through each hole. The hammer hit flat and solid. Mei felt the thud through the floor.

“Now the board,” Vern said.

She handed it to him. He set it on the brackets. It sat without wobbling.

Vern stepped back. Mei stepped back too.

The shelf was straight. The wall held it. The wood was still warm from the saw.
