Emma led Mei across the field. The grass was wet at the bottom and dry halfway up. Biscuit came part of the way, then turned back to lie in the sun.

"The oak," Emma said. She pointed up into the fork.

Mei tipped her head back. Her eyes followed the branches. "Where?"

"You have to stand on the branch," Emma said. She put her hand on the low branch. It was thick and rough. The bark felt like old stone.

Mei stepped up. She took her time finding her balance. Her fingers held the branch above her.

She looked into the fork. Her breath went quiet.

"Three," she said.

Emma nodded from the ground. "I counted."

Mei did not reach in. She stood still and looked. The nest was a bowl of dry grass and twigs. Mud held the bottom together. A piece of string hung over the edge — red, frayed.

Inside, three eggs. Very small. Blue. Dark specks scattered over each one like tiny freckles.

Mei climbed down. She placed each foot carefully. When she was on the ground, she looked up again.

They stood together, a little way back from the tree. The robin came back. It landed on the edge of the nest, settled, and was still.
