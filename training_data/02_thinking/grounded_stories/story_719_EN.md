The stone sat in the middle of the meadow path. It was grey and flat on top, and the grass had grown thin around its edges.

Taro put both hands on it and pushed. The stone did not move. He braced his feet and tried again. Nothing.

He called. "Emma. Mei. Come here."

They came running up the path. Biscuit came too, sniffed the stone once, and sat down to watch.

"It will not move," Taro said. "One person cannot."

Emma put her hands beside his. Mei found a place on the other side.

"Together," Mei said.

They counted. One. Two. Three.

They pushed. The stone shifted. It lifted from its bed of damp earth. They carried it together, three pairs of hands, and set it down at the edge of the path where the long grass began.

The stone was heavy. The place where it had sat was dark and wet, and small white roots ran through the soil.

Biscuit sniffed the hole. Emma wiped her hands on her dress. The stone sat at the edge of the path, and the path was clear.
