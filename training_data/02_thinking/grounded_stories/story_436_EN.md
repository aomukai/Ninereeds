Gran set a jar on the kitchen table. She turned the lid. It let go with a soft pop.

Something sharp came out. Emma stood on her toes to see. Inside the jar were pale ribbons of cabbage, packed tight, floating in cloudy liquid.

"That is from last autumn," Gran said. She pushed a fresh cabbage across the table, round and tight as a stone. She cut it open. The sound was crisp. The inside was pale green and clean.

Gran lifted a ribbon of pickle from the jar with a fork. It dripped. She set it on a plate. Beside it she set a slice of fresh cabbage.

Emma touched the pickle first. It was cool and soft. She put it in her mouth. Her face pinched. Sour. The cabbage crunched between her teeth. It tasted of nothing much, but it was clean and wet.

Taro pointed at the pickle. "Can I have that one?"

Gran gave him the rest of the ribbon. He ate it without making a face.

"Different," Emma said.

Gran nodded. She wiped the fork on her apron. "The jar keeps it through winter. But it changes."

Taro licked his fingers. "I like the change."
