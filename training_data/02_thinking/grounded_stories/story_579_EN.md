Stefan walked the side street. His hands were behind his back, one holding the other.

Taro sat on the bench with Biscuit beside him. The leaves were down — yellow and brown, scattered across the stones. Biscuit had his nose on his paws.

Stefan stopped. He did not look down at Taro at first. He looked at the leaves, at the wall, at the sky. Then he nodded — a small nod, just a dip of his chin.

Taro nodded back.

"Biscuit," Stefan said.

Biscuit's tail thumped once against the bench.

Stefan turned and walked to his bicycle, which was leaning against the wall. He pushed it slowly along the side street, one hand on the handlebar, the wheels clicking over the stones.

The sun came through the leaves above him. For a moment the badge on his chest caught the light — a flash of gold, then gone.

Taro watched him push the bicycle to the next corner. He did not hurry. He never hurried.

Biscuit put his head down again.

Taro sat on the bench. The leaves rustled when the wind moved them.
