Vern's workshop smelled of sawdust and something sharp and clean. He stood at the bench with a block of wood in his hand.

He handed it to Mei.

"Run your hand along it."

Mei took the block. It was pale yellow, lighter than she expected. She ran her palm down the length of it. The wood slid under her skin, smooth and even.

Then she turned her hand and ran it back the other way.

Her fingers caught. The surface was rough, like tiny teeth. She stopped and looked at her palm, then at the wood.

"One way is smooth," she said. "The other way is rough."

"That is the grain," Vern said. He picked up his plane — the blade was thin and sharp. He set the plane at the smooth end of the block and pushed it forward.

A curl of wood lifted away, thin and clean, like a single ribbon. It fell onto the bench and lay still.

Emma leaned closer. "It came off in one piece."

Vern turned the block around. He set the plane at the rough end and pushed it the other way.

The plane stuttered. The wood splintered. A short, broken shaving fell to the floor.

"With the grain," Vern said. "Or against it."
