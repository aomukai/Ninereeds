The field lay flat and pale under the wide sky. Where the wheat had swayed tall last week, only stubble remained—short and sharp under Emma’s sandals. She crouched to touch it. The cut stalks pricked her fingertips.  

Taro ran ahead, arms outstretched as if he could touch both ends of the field at once. "It’s *huge* now!" His voice carried farther without the wheat to swallow it. Biscuit bounded after him, kicking up dust.  

Gran stood at the edge, her basket hooked over one arm. Emma pointed to the oak across the field—its dark shape clear against the sky, no longer half-hidden by golden stalks. "It’s still there," she said.  

"Still there," Gran agreed. She scooped a handful of stubble and let it fall. "Different now."  

The wind moved over the bare earth, carrying the dry, warm smell of cut stems. Emma squinted at the horizon. Without the wheat, the world felt open, like a room with its walls taken down.  

Gran brushed her hands on her apron. "Green again in spring."  

Biscuit barked at nothing, his voice loud in the empty field.
