The hill got steeper near the top. Emma's legs ached. Taro was already ahead, his coat flapping.

"Wait," Emma said. But the wind took her voice.

They reached the top together. And the wind hit them.

It was not like the wind below. Down in the field, the hedge broke it. Up here, there was nothing. No tree. No wall. Just air moving fast and cold.

Biscuit's ears blew flat against his head. He squinted and turned his side to the wind.

Taro leaned into it, pushing forward like he was walking through water. His scarf flew out behind him.

Emma's face went cold very quickly. Her ears hurt. She pulled her hood up but the wind found the gap.

Gran reached the top last. She pulled her scarf tighter and held it with one hand.

"Hard up here," she said. The wind took her words but Emma saw her mouth move.

Emma turned her back to the wind. It pushed against her coat, hard and steady. She did not look up. She walked down, step after step, until the hedge came back and the air grew still.
