Clara stood at the nature table. She emptied her apron pocket.

A grey feather fell out. A smooth stone. A dry leaf. A snail shell, pale and curled.

Emma picked up the feather. She held it close to her mouth and blew. The tiny barbs lifted and separated. The quill was hollow and light.

"The bird is gone," she said. "But the feather is still here."

Mei touched the shell with one finger. She turned it over. The opening was empty. Inside, nothing moved.

"The snail is gone too," she said. "It was alive once."

Clara set a jar on the table. Water inside. Tadpoles moving — dark shapes that flicked and rested and flicked again.

"These are alive now," Clara said.

Emma looked at the stone. She picked it up. It was cold. It did not change.

"Was this alive?" she asked.

Mei shook her head. "It is a stone. It was never alive."

Emma put the stone down. She picked up the dry leaf. It crumbled at the edge where her fingers pressed.

"This was alive," she said. "On the tree. Before it fell."

Clara nodded. She did not say anything.

Emma touched the feather again. Then the shell. Then the leaf. She did not mix them up.
