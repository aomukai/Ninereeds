The workshop floor was covered in shavings. Curls of wood, pale and thin, lay scattered across the stone. Some were from pine—soft and pale yellow. Some were from oak—darker, tighter, with a rougher edge.

Mei held the broom. The handle was smooth from use. She swept the shavings into a pile near the workbench. The broom made a soft sound against the stone. The pile grew.

She stopped and picked up one of the curls. It was long and springy. She pressed it between her fingers. It sprang back. She let it fall onto the pile.

Vern was at the bench, running his hand along a board. He did not look up. "Leave the pile," he said. "I will bag it later."

The smell of sawdust was thick in the air. Mei leaned the broom against the wall. The curl she had picked up lay on top of the pile, separate from the others. It curled toward the ceiling, light and still.

She looked at the pile. Then she looked at the board Vern was holding. She did not pick up the broom again.
