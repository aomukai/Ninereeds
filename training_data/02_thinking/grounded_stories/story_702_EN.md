Emma poured water into the pot. The soil turned dark. A few drops ran out the bottom and onto the windowsill.

She put the jug down and went outside.

In the afternoon she came back to the kitchen. Gran was at the table, shelling peas. Emma looked at the windowsill.

The seedling was bent.

Its leaves hung down. They did not point up the way they had that morning. The stem leaned sideways, as if it was too tired to stand.

Emma touched the soil. It was dry again. Dry and light.

She filled the jug again. She poured slowly, watching the water sink in. The soil went dark. She waited.

Nothing happened.

She stood there, looking at the drooping leaves.

Gran did not say anything. She kept shelling peas. The shells fell into a bowl with a soft sound.

Emma waited.

After a while, the stem began to straighten. A little at a time. The leaves lifted. They pointed up again.

The seedling stood.

Emma touched the soil. It was damp. She touched a leaf. It was firm.

She walked away from the windowsill. The seedling was green and upright in the afternoon light.
