Vern picked up the piece of wood. It was the shape of a long finger, squared at the edges. He held it beside the dark hole in the other piece — the mortise.

He pushed the tenon in. It went partway, then stopped.

"Too tight," he said.

He pulled it out again. The tenon had bright scratches where the mortise had caught it. Vern ran his thumb along the side. He felt where the wood was thick.

He reached for the chisel. It was wide and flat. He set it against the tenon and pushed. A thin shaving curled away, pale and papery. Vern blew it off the bench.

He tried again.

The tenon went in to the same place. Still tight.

He took another shaving — thinner this time, almost nothing. He laid the chisel aside and picked up the mallet. One tap. The tenon slid deeper. Two taps. It sat flush with the surface.

Vern turned the joint over in his hands. He looked at the line where the two pieces met. Then he tapped the tenon back out. The wood was smooth now, with no bright scratches.

Mei watched. "You took it apart," she said.

"I did," said Vern. "Now I know it fits."
