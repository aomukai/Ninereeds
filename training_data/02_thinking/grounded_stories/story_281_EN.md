Vern held the piece of wood in his hand. The end was cut into a shape — a tenon, square and neat. He picked up the other piece. It had a hole cut into it — a mortise, the same shape but hollow.

He slid the tenon into the mortise. It went partway. Then it stopped.

Vern tapped it with the heel of his hand. It did not move.

"Hmm," he said.

He pulled the tenon out. He turned it in his fingers. The light from the window fell across it. One side was just a little too thick.

Vern picked up a small plane. He ran it along that side once. A thin curl of wood came off. He felt the surface with his thumb. He ran the plane again. Another curl.

He tried the fit again. The tenon went in. It was tight, but it went. Vern tapped it once with a wooden mallet. The joint closed all the way.

He held it up. The two pieces were one piece now.

Taro leaned closer. "No glue?"

"No glue," said Vern. "It is tight enough."

Emma touched the corner where the tenon had disappeared. She could not see the join.
