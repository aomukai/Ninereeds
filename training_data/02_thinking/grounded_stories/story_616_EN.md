Mei stopped at the edge of the field.

The oak stood ahead of her, wide and dark against the bright spring grass. She had seen it from the village lane, from the hill, from Gran's garden gate. But she had never stood this close.

She walked forward and put her hand flat against the bark.

It was rough. Deep furrows ran up and down the trunk, thick as her fingers. She pressed her palm into them and felt the hard surface beneath.

"It's different," she said.

Emma came up beside her. "Different from what?"

"The millstone." Mei kept her hand on the bark. "The millstone is smooth. This is not."

Emma crouched down near the roots. "Look here."

Mei knelt beside her. Between two large roots was a dark hole. The edges were worn smooth where something had gone in and out many times.

"A squirrel lives there," Emma said. "I saw it last week."

Mei did not say anything. She looked at the hole. Then she looked up at the branches. Small new leaves were opening, bright green, very small.

She touched one. It was soft and thin. The bark was hard and thick.

Same tree. Different parts.

She looked at the hole again. The squirrel was not there. But the hole was.
