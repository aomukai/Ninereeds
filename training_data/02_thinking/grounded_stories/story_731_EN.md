The pond looked different. White at the edges, dark in the middle.

Taro crouched at the bank. He tapped the white part with a stick. It made a thin, bright sound — *tock*.

He tapped harder. A crack ran through it like a skinny black line. He slid the stick underneath and lifted.

The piece of ice came away in his hand. It was clear in the middle, like glass. At the edges it was cloudy, like fog pressed flat.

He held it up. The light shone through the middle part. His fingers went cold.

Emma leaned over his shoulder. "It's dripping."

Water ran down Taro's wrist. The ice was getting smaller. It was thinner now, and the clear part was gone.

He turned it over. It shone for a moment, then broke in two. Both pieces fell into his palm, smaller still, and then they were just water.

He wiped his hand on his coat. "It was ice."

"Now it's water again," said Emma.

They looked at the pond. The surface was still and dark where the ice had been. Biscuit sniffed the wet edge. Bello stood behind him, watching.
