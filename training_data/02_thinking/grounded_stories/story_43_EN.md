Gran knelt by the fire, stacking the last log. "Five for the night," she said. "Best check the basket."  

Emma lifted the lid. Two logs lay inside, rough and knotted. "Only two," she said.  

Taro pulled his scarf up over his nose. "Two we have, three more I will get. Two plus three is five." He pushed the door open. Cold air rushed in.  

Emma watched from the window. Taro’s boots crunched through the frost to the woodpile. He dragged three logs free, arms wrapped tight around them. His breath made little clouds.  

Gran took the logs one by one as he came in. She stacked them by the fire. "One, two, three, four, five." She brushed bark from her hands. "That should last."  

The fire crackled. Emma held her palms to the warmth. Outside, the wind rattled the garden gate.
