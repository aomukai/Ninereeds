The hill was long and the grass was short. Emma walked behind Taro. Gran walked last, carrying a basket.

From the top, the field stretched out below. The pond was a flat grey circle. The lane was a pale line through the village.

Emma looked for the oak.

She saw it at the field edge. A dark shape. Small from here. She could fit it between her thumb and finger if she held her hand up.

"It looks tiny," she said.

"It has been there longer than the cottage," Gran said. She was not looking at the oak. She was looking at the clouds building in the west.

Emma remembered the acorn she had found in autumn. She had turned it in her fingers. The cap was rough. The bottom was smooth. She had put it in her pocket and forgotten it.

The leaves on the oak were brown now. From here they were just a dark smudge at the base of the shape.

Taro threw a stick. It landed somewhere in the long grass. Biscuit did not chase it.

"Can you see the mark?" Gran said.

"What mark?"

"Where the tree meets the field. The place you remember."

Emma looked. The oak was a dark shape. A fixed shape. It was easy to find because it was always there, the same place, small from the hill and big from the ground. She knew both.
