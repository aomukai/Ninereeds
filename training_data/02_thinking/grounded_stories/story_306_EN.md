Stefan came down the main street. His boots made a slow, steady sound on the stones. His badge caught the autumn light.

The market stall was ahead. Yun stood behind it. Mei stood beside her, holding a basket. The apples on the table were red and yellow, stacked in neat rows.

Stefan passed the stall. Then he stopped.

He nodded at Yun.

“Good morning,” he said.

“Good morning,” said Yun.

Stefan looked at the apples. He picked one up. It was red, with a small brown spot near the stem. He turned it in his hand. The skin was smooth. He put it back.

“These are from your tree?” he said.

“From the tree behind the house,” said Yun. “They came early this year.”

Stefan nodded again.

Emma and Taro stood a little way back. Taro watched Stefan’s boots. The leather was dark and scuffed at the toe. Stefan noticed him looking and said nothing.

Then Stefan straightened. He nodded a third time.

“Good apples,” he said.

He walked on. His steps went past the stall, past the baker’s window, down towards the side street. The stall was still tidy. The apples were still in their rows.
