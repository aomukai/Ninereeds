Vern held the board flat on the bench. It was long and pale, with a straight edge.

He put the ruler against one end. He looked at the numbers. Then he picked up the pencil and made a small dark line.

He did not put the ruler down.

Instead, he moved it to the other end of the board. He measured again. The pencil hovered. He made another mark.

The two marks were in the same place.

"Good," Vern said.

He put the ruler aside. He picked up the saw. The teeth were small and sharp. He set the blade against the first mark — right on the line.

Taro watched. Emma watched.

Vern did not look at them. He pulled the saw back once, gently. A thin groove appeared in the wood. Then he pushed forward, and the saw bit deeper.

The sound was even. Back and forward. Back and forward.

The board held still under his hand. The line stayed under the blade.

Dust fell onto the bench in a small pale pile.
