Gran carried the basket out into the garden. The sheets inside were white and wet. She pulled the line and it made a low sound.

Biscuit followed her to the middle of the grass. He watched the sheets go up one by one. When the last one was pinned, he turned twice and lay down in the shade underneath. The white cloth hung above him and moved a little.

Dou walked the top of the garden wall. She moved slowly, one paw in front of the other, tail straight up. At the corner where the lavender grew thick and grey-green, she stopped. She sat down in the middle of it. The lavender stems bent around her. She did not move.

Bello was near the shed door. He was on his side, legs out, head flat on the stone step. His eyes were half open. He watched the sheets and the cat and the other dog, but he did not change position.

Gran shook the last sheet open. It snapped once in the air before she pinned it.

The wind came and went. The sheets filled and fell. Dou sat in the lavender. Biscuit lay in the shade. Bello did not move.

Gran picked up the empty basket and went inside.
