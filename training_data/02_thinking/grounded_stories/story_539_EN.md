The main street was empty.

Emma pulled her scarf higher. The wool was cold against her chin. Her breath came out white and disappeared.

Taro stamped his feet. The frost on the stones crunched under his boots.

The horse chestnut stood bare. Every branch was dark and still. No leaves. No sound.

Gran walked between them. Her coat was long and grey. She did not hurry.

Smoke rose from Hana’s chimney. It went straight up, thin and pale, into a sky the colour of old paper.

The post box was covered in frost. Emma touched it. The metal was so cold it stung through her mitten.

Every shop had its door closed. The butcher’s shutters were down. The general shop had no light in the window.

Only the baker’s window was different. It was steamed up from the inside. A warm blur behind the glass. Emma could not see the loaves, but she could smell them — bread and yeast and something sweet.

“No one is out,” Taro said.

Gran shook her head. “It is too cold to stand still.”

They walked on. The street felt smaller in winter. The houses leaned closer. The sky pressed down.

Emma’s scarf was wet with her own breath. Taro’s nose was red.

They did not need to buy anything. They had come to walk, and now they were walking back.
