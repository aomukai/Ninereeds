The nature table stood by the window. Snow pressed against the glass on the other side.

On the table: a pine cone, a feather, a stone, a shell, and a small white bone.

Clara pointed at the row. "Which of these were once alive?"

Taro put his finger on the stone. "This."

Emma shook her head. She touched the feather. It was light. It bent a little when she pressed. "This," she said.

Clara did not say yes or no yet. She picked up the pine cone. It was dry. The scales were spread open. "Feel this," she said.

Emma took it. It was rough. Light. Hollow inside.

"It was part of a tree," Clara said. "The tree is still alive. But this part is not."

Taro looked at the stone again. He picked it up. It was cold. Heavy. It did not change when he squeezed it.

"Was the stone ever alive?" Clara asked.

"No," Taro said.

"What about the shell?"

Emma picked up the shell. It was curved. Smooth inside. "Something lived in it," she said.

Clara nodded. She picked up the bone. It was white and very light.

"And this?"

Taro did not touch it. "Something was inside it," he said.

Clara put the bone down gently. "Yes. Was."

The snow fell past the window. The things on the table were still.
