Gran lifted a bean leaf with the back of her hand. The canes were nearly hidden now. The plants had climbed past her shoulder.

“Look,” Mei said.

She touched a tendril. It curled around the cane in a tight spiral, pale green and thin as thread.

Emma crouched low. She pushed leaves aside. A pod hung near the bottom, long and smooth and dark green.

“This one feels full,” she said.

She bent it. The pod snapped open with a small, clean sound.

Inside were beans. Five of them. Pale green, smooth, each one the same size as the next. Emma tipped them into her palm. They were cool and round.

Mei found another pod on the same plant. She opened it the same way. The beans inside were the same size. Same pale green. Same shape.

She held her hand next to Emma’s.

“They match,” Mei said.

Emma turned a bean over in her fingers. It had a tiny line along one side, like a closed eye.

Gran had gone back to the watering can. The water hit the soil with a soft patter. She did not look up.

Emma put the bean back in her palm. Five of them. All the same.
