Emma was the first to see it. She pointed to the west.

The sky there was not blue anymore. It was dark grey, like the stone in Gran's wall. The grey was moving toward them.

"Look," she said.

Taro looked up. The wind came then. It pushed the grass flat and lifted Taro's hair off his forehead. Leaves skittered across the meadow path — dry brown ones, spinning past their feet.

Biscuit turned in a circle, nose high.

Gran stopped walking. She looked west the same way Emma had looked. Then she looked down the path toward home.

"We should head back," she said. Not loud. But the words were quick.

Emma smelled something new in the air. It was not the grass smell. It was not the dust smell. It was something sharp and cold, like wet stone. Like the ground before a storm.

The first drop landed on her hand.

She looked at it. A round dark spot on her skin.

Then another drop hit her cheek.

"Run," said Gran.

They ran. Biscuit ran beside them, ears flat. The path went past the hedge, past the gap where the pond showed, past the oak at the edge of the field.

They reached the garden gate. Emma's hand was on the latch when the rain arrived — all at once, heavy and cold, drumming on the leaves above them.

Gran pushed the gate open. They were home before the drops turned to a downpour.
