Vern picked up a piece of glass. It was flat and grey along one edge.

He held it in both hands and pulled it along the wood. The sound was soft—like a breath, not a scrape.

A fine dust curled off the surface. It settled on the bench in a pale line.

Mei leaned close. She did not touch. She watched.

“One more,” Vern said.

He pulled the glass again. The edge of the wood caught the light differently now. The grain showed through, clean and even.

Mei put her finger on the surface. She moved it slowly from one end to the other.

“It was rough before,” she said.

“It was,” Vern said.

He wiped the dust from the glass with the side of his thumb. Then he laid it down on the bench, edge away from the wood.

Taro picked up a curl of shaving from the floor. It was thin and dry. It crumbled when he squeezed it.

Mei touched the wood again. Her fingertip slid without stopping.

“That is the last cut,” Vern said.
