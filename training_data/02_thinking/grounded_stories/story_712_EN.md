Emma stood in front of Yun's stall. The honey was in a small jar. It was golden and thick.

She put her hand in her pocket. Her fingers found the coins.

She placed them on the wooden table one by one.

"One," said Yun.

The first coin clinked on the wood.

"Two."

The second coin lay next to the first.

"Three."

Emma's fingers were careful. She did not want to drop any.

"Four."

The fourth coin. Almost all of them.

"Five."

The last coin. She placed it down. Her hand was empty.

Yun touched the last coin. Her finger rested on it for a moment. "Five coins," she said. "That is enough."

Mei stood beside the stall. She watched the coins on the table.

"How many?" Emma asked.

Yun pointed. "One, two, three, four, five. The total is five."

Emma looked at the coins. She knew the number. She had counted them twice before she left Gran's house. But hearing Yun say it made it real.

"Five," Emma said. "That is the last one."

Yun picked up the jar. She put it into a small paper bag and twisted the top.

Emma took the bag. It was warm from the sun.
