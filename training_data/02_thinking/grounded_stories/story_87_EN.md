Emma crouched by the pond’s edge, crumbling bread between her fingers. The ducks floated halfway across, heads tilted. She tossed a piece.  

They turned as one, paddling toward her, smooth wakes fanning behind. She held still. Closer they came, necks outstretched, until she could see the dark beads of their eyes.  

Biscuit, lying beside her, twitched his tail.  

The ducks stopped. Then—without a splash, without a sound—they were suddenly far away, as if they had never moved closer at all. The water where they had been was already still.  

Emma blinked. "They’re fast," she murmured.  

Biscuit whuffed, ears pricked. She threw another crumb. The ducks drifted nearer again, cautious, until Biscuit shifted his paw. Away they went, gliding back to the reeds as if pulled by strings.  

Emma watched them float at a safe distance, the bread bobbing untouched between. The pond held them lightly, ready to carry them in or out, toward or away, with just a twitch of webbed feet beneath the surface.
