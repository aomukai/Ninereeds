Emma stood on the low branch of the oak. She held the trunk with one hand and looked up.

The nest was in the fork above her head. It had been full of noise for weeks — small beaks, quick movements, the robin flying in and out.

Now it was quiet.

Mei climbed up beside her. She balanced on the branch and looked too.

"It's empty," Emma said.

She reached up. Her fingers touched the rim of the nest. The twigs were woven tight. The mud was dry and hard.

Mei put her hand inside. The bowl was shallow. There was nothing in it.

"The fledglings flew," Mei said.

Emma remembered them — small and brown, hopping on the branch, then gone one morning. The robin had stopped coming back.

She looked at the nest again. It was still the same shape. The same twigs. The same mud.

"It's just old now," she said.

Mei pulled her hand out. She looked at the inside of her palm as if she expected to find something there.

They climbed down without speaking.

The oak stood in the sun. The nest stayed in the fork.
