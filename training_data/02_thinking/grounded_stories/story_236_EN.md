The mill was dark. No light in the windows. No sound from the village.

Mei led the way along the bank. The grass was wet. The stream was louder than it was in daytime.

Emma stopped when she saw the wheel.

It was turning. Slow and black against the sky. The water that fell from it caught the moonlight and turned silver.

They sat on the bank. The grass was damp through Emma's dress.

The wheel turned. Water dropped from each blade in a steady line. A soft sound. A sound you could listen to for a long time.

Emma watched the shape of it — round and dark, with the moon behind it. The wheel had no daytime now. No miller. No grain. Only the water and the night.

Mei pointed. A star sat low above the chimney. Bright and still.

Emma looked at the wheel again. It turned the same way. Steady. Quiet. A thing that kept going whether anyone was there to see it or not.

The water was silver. The wheel was black. The sound was soft.

Neither of them spoke. There was nothing to say.
