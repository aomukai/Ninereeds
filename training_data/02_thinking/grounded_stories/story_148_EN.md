Emma walked down the meadow path, her boots scuffing through fallen leaves. The air smelled of damp earth and dry grass. She turned to point at a crow in the field—and saw Biscuit far behind, his nose pressed to the ground, moving slower than the rustle of the hedgerow.  

She stopped. He lifted his head, ears flopping, and trotted toward her. Not the old dash, just a steady walk. When he reached her, his tail wagged once, then stilled.  

Emma bent to scratch his ear. His fur was warm and rough under her fingers. She straightened and walked on, shorter steps now. Biscuit kept beside her, his shoulder brushing her leg sometimes.  

The path curved toward the pond. Emma slowed where the mud was thick. Biscuit slowed too, his paws leaving soft prints next to hers. She didn’t say anything. Neither did he.  

They reached the flat stone by the water. Emma sat. Biscuit lay down, his chin on his paws, and sighed. The willow leaves drifted onto his back, and he didn’t shake them off.
