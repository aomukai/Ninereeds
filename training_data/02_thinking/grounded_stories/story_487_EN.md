The ash tree in the schoolyard had dropped its acorns. They lay on the ground among the leaves, brown and smooth and hard.

Taro scooped up a handful. "Look," he said. "Look how many."

Emma knelt and gathered hers one by one. She counted them into her hand. "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight."

She held out her hand. Eight acorns sat in her palm.

Taro set his on the ground in a pile. "One, two, three, four, five, six."

Mei looked from Taro's pile to Emma's hand. "You have two fewer," she said.

Taro looked at his pile. He looked at Emma's hand. He counted again.

"Two fewer," he said.

He looked at the ground under the ash tree. He found one acorn between two roots. He found another half-hidden in a crack. He picked them up and added them to his pile.

"Now I have eight," he said.

He counted again to be sure. "Eight."

Emma put hers down beside his. Two piles of eight. The same.

Mei nodded. "Now they match."
