Emma climbed the hill. The grass was short and dry. She put her feet where the grass was flat and pulled herself up with her hands.

She reached the top first. She turned around.

Down below, Gran's cottage was a grey square. The oak was a dark round shape at the edge of the field. The field was a green patch. The fence was a thin brown line.

"Taro," she called. "Come up. Everything fits in one hand."

Taro came up behind her, breathing hard. Biscuit came past him, tongue out, and sat down on the top.

Taro looked. He did not say anything for a moment.

"It is small," he said.

"It is not small," said Emma. "We are far away. The cottage is the same size. The view changes it."

Biscuit stood up and looked down the other side of the hill. A sheep moved across the slope. The wind came over the top and Emma's hair blew across her face.

She looked at the oak. It was a dark shape. She could see the line of the meadow path. She could see the pond, a blue-grey spot in the green.

She stood still and looked.
