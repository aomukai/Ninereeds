Taro pressed his chest against the ash tree's trunk. He stretched his arms around it as far as they would go. His fingers did not touch.

He tried again, higher up. Still no.

"Your turn," he said to Emma.

Emma shook her head. She had a piece of string from Gran's drawer. She wrapped it around the trunk, once, twice, and held the ends together where they met.

Mei knelt beside her. "How many hands?"

Emma let go of the string. Mei measured it against her own hand, finger to wrist. "Five," she said. "Five of my hands."

Taro came back and wrapped the string around his own middle. "Three," he said. "The tree is bigger than me."

Mei took the string and walked around the trunk, counting her steps. "Six feet," she said. "Almost."

Emma picked up a fallen twig and laid it beside the string on the ground. She wanted to see the difference. The twig was short and thin. The string was long and curved where the tree had pushed it.

Mei picked up another twig and laid it next to the first. "Now we have two," she said.

The ash tree stood above them, very still, its leaves high and quiet.
