Gran lifted the basket’s lid. "Nothing," she said. The inside was smooth and bare. "Emma, check the apple tree."  

Emma ran to the garden. The tree’s lowest branch bent under its own weight, apples round and red among the leaves. She picked one. It came away with a quiet snap. She picked another. And another.  

Back in the kitchen, she set them on the table. "One." The first apple rolled slightly. "Two." The second thumped beside it. "Three." The last one smelled sweet.  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. "Nothing in the basket, three from the tree—three." She took the apples and began to peel. "Zero plus three is three. That is what I need."  

Emma touched the empty basket. The wood was cool. Outside, the tree’s branches swayed in the wind.
