Gran walked up the hill first. Emma and Taro followed. The grass was dry and short, cropped close by Owen's sheep. Biscuit ran ahead and then came back and then ran ahead again.

Halfway up, Taro stopped. "It's hot."

"Keep going," Gran said. She did not turn around.

At the top, the wind was different. It moved without the trees to break it. Gran sat down on the grass. Emma sat beside her. Taro dropped onto the ground and lay flat on his back, looking up.

Gran pointed west. The sky was clear there, but low down, near the horizon, something pale and white was beginning to show.

"Clouds," Emma said.

"Watch them," Gran said.

They sat. The air was still. Warm. No birds called. The hilltop was quiet.

The clouds grew. They started small, like someone had drawn a faint line. Then they got bigger, pushing upward, turning grey at the bottom and white at the top.

"How long?" Taro asked.

Gran did not answer. She kept watching. The clouds kept growing. The wind shifted, just a little. The warmth did not change yet, but something in the air felt different.

Emma watched. The clouds were much larger now. They were coming from the west, just like Gran always said.
