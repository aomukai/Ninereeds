Vern held the nail between two fingers. He set it against the wood.

"Now," he said.

Emma kept her hands flat on the roof. She did not move. The hammer came down once, twice. The nail went in straight.

Taro fitted the perch into the hole Vern had made earlier. It slid in tight. He pushed until it stopped.

Mei stood at the front of the birdhouse. She put one eye close to the entrance hole. She looked through it into the dark inside. She looked at the hole from the outside again.

"It is round," she said.

"It is," said Vern.

He turned the birdhouse over in his hands. He ran a thumb along the edge of the roof. He set it on the bench.

"One more coat of oil," he said. "Then it is ready to hang."

He picked up a small cloth. He dipped it into a jar. The cloth came away dark and wet. He wiped it across the roof, the sides, the front. The wood went darker where the oil touched.

"Tomorrow," he said. "You can hang it in the oak."

Emma looked at the house. The roof was straight. The hole was round. The perch stuck out like a tiny step.

She touched the wood. It was wet and cool.
