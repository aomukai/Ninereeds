Vern's workshop smelled of sawdust and oil. Emma set the box on the bench. It was small, with straight sides and a lid that sat separate.

She put the lid on. It went down on the left side. On the right, it caught. It would not close.

Vern came over. He did not take the box. He looked at it sideways, then from above. He picked up a thin piece of chalk and marked the edge of the lid where the wood was high. A short, light line.

"Sand there," he said. "Not much. Just where the mark is."

Mei took the lid. She found a piece of sandpaper folded square in a jar on the bench. She rubbed the marked edge. Once, twice. She blew the dust off and looked at Vern.

He touched the wood with his thumb. "Again," he said.

She sanded once more. The dust was fine and pale.

Emma took the lid back. She held the box steady with one hand and lowered the lid with the other. It went down. It met the box on all sides. No gap. No tilt. The lid sat flush.

She ran her finger around the edge. It was smooth all the way.

Vern nodded once. "Correct," he said.
