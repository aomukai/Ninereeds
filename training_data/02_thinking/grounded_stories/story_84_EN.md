The hilltop was wide and open, the wind pulling at their hair. Below, the valley stretched out, patches of green and gold. Gran’s cottage was a small square in the distance, the oak a dark smudge in the field.  

Taro stepped forward, toes brushing the edge. He leaned out, squinting. “Everything looks tiny!”  

Emma stood back, her arms wrapped tight. The ground fell away sharply, the slope dotted with rocks and tufts of grass. She could see the meadow path winding down, the pond a faint glint.  

Gran stood between them, her hands resting on her hips. “Careful,” she said, not looking at Taro.  

The wind gusted, carrying the scent of warm earth. Taro crouched, peering over the edge. “I can see the pond! It’s so far down.”  

Emma took another step back. The drop made her stomach twist. She looked at Gran’s face, calm and steady.  

Gran pointed across the valley. “There’s the lane,” she said. “And the village. Can you see the baker’s chimney?”  

Emma squinted. The chimney was a thin line, barely visible. She felt small, standing there, the sky wide above her.  

Taro grinned, balancing on the edge. “I can see everything!”  

Gran reached out, her hand brushing his shoulder. “Look,” she said softly. “But don’t lean.”  

Emma stayed back, her feet firm on the ground. The wind swept past, carrying the sound of distant birds.
