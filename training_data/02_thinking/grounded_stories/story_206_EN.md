Emma followed Mei through the low door of the mill. The air was white and fine. It tasted of flour.

The wheel was turning. The sound filled the room.

Emma stopped just inside. The noise was not loud the way a shout is loud. It was deep. It came up through her shoes.

“Put your ear to the floor,” Mei said.

Emma knelt. She pressed her ear to the cold stone. The grinding was slow and heavy, like a big animal breathing underground. She felt it in her cheek and her hands.

Mei stood beside the millstones with her hand flat against the wooden housing. “You can feel it higher too,” she said. “It goes all the way up.”

Biscuit sat by the door. His ears were flat. He whined once, then again. He did not like the sound. It was too low and too big.

The wheel slowed. The grinding dropped. Then it stopped.

The silence was sudden and strange. It rang in Emma’s ears like a bell that had already stopped. She could hear the stream outside, and her own breathing.

Biscuit stood up and shook himself.
