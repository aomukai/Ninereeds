The snow came overnight. It lay thick and white over the field.

Emma stood at the kitchen window. “Can we go up the hill?”

Gran looked out. “If you wrap up warm. The path will be icy.”

Taro was already pulling on his boots. Biscuit scratched at the door.

They climbed the lane first. The stones were slippery. Taro put his feet down carefully, one after the other.

The hill path was worse. The snow had frozen hard on top. Emma took small steps. Taro slid sideways and caught himself.

Biscuit ran ahead. His paws slipped. He scrambled sideways, legs going too fast, and sat down hard. He looked back as if to say *that was on purpose*.

At the top the world was white. The fields were gone under snow. The village roofs were flat white shapes. The mill chimney stood up dark against the sky. A thin line of smoke rose from it and bent in the wind.

Taro pointed. “Look.”

A line of clouds was coming from the west. They were low and grey.

Gran pulled her scarf tighter. “That is more snow.”

Emma looked at the clouds and then at the white hill. Biscuit shook snow from his fur.

They stood a moment longer. The wind was cold. The clouds were coming.
