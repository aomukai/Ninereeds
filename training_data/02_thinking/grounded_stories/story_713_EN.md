Vern's workshop smelled of sawdust and wood. Emma stood at the bench and watched.

Vern picked up a piece of wood. Light wood. Straight. He laid it flat on the bench. Then he picked up another piece. Darker. A little longer.

He took his ruler from the wall. The ruler was yellow. Numbers ran along it in black.

He put the ruler against the first piece. He pointed at the number at the end.

"Three," he said.

Emma leaned closer. The number was small and black.

He moved the ruler to the second piece. He held it still. The wood went past the number three. Past four. The number at the end was five.

"This one is longer," Vern said. He put his finger on the wood at the number four. "This is where the first piece ends. See how much longer the second piece is?"

Emma looked. The wood went past Vern's finger by a whole space.

Vern picked up a pencil. He drew a straight line across the wood at the number four.

"Now I cut here," he said. "Then both pieces will be the same length."

He put the ruler back on its peg. He picked up a saw. The teeth were small and sharp.

Emma watched him put the saw against the line.
