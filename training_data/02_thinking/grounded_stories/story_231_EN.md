The mill was colder inside than outside.

Emma stood just past the door and watched her own breath. It came out in a white cloud. She did it again on purpose.

Mei stood beside her. She did not do it on purpose. Her breath was just there.

The window had frost on the inside. Emma touched it with one finger. The ice did not melt. It stayed hard and white.

The wheel was turning. It turned slowly. Where the water fell, there was slush — not clear water, but thick and white and slow. Ice hung at the edges of the stream. The wheel dragged through it. The sound was different than in summer. Lower. Slower. A crack and a drag and then a crack again.

Gran came in behind them. She did not say anything. She stood and looked at the wheel. Her breath made steam.

Emma shivered. The cold was not the same as outside cold. Outside cold moved. This cold sat still.

Mei walked to the window and looked at the frost. She did not touch it. She just looked.

The wheel turned. The ice crackled. The mill was very quiet.
