Snow had fallen in the night. It lay thick on the hill, on the field, on the branches of the oak.

Emma stepped out of the cottage and stopped. The air was still. She could not hear the lane or the village or anything at all. The whole world seemed to be holding its breath. Even Biscuit stood quiet at her heel, ears up.

She crunched forward. Every footstep made a sound. The snow packed under her boot with a hard, sharp crack. Taro came after her, stamping hard on purpose to hear it happen again.

They walked up the hill. The snow was deep enough to cover the grass and the stones and the shape of the path. Emma had to lift her feet high. Taro's legs were shorter and he puffed a little but he kept going.

At the top they stopped. The village was a grey smudge below. The oak was a dark shape at the edge of the field.

A bird called from somewhere down there. The sound was clear and very sharp, as if the snow had polished it.

Then the village bell began to ring. It was the same bell they heard on summer mornings. But now it sounded far away, small and thin, as if it had to push through the quiet to reach them.

Biscuit sat down in the snow and listened too.
