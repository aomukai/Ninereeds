The ash tree stood at the edge of the schoolyard. Its branches spread wide and thick, full of small green leaves.

Emma sat on the bench under the tree. The shade was deep and cool. She could feel the breeze on her arms, but the sun did not reach her.

Taro stood in the open yard. The sun was hot on his head and shoulders. He stepped forward into the shade.

It was cool. He stopped.

Then he stepped back into the sun. Hot again.

He did it again. Step in. Cool. Step out. Hot.

Clara was watching from the classroom door. She did not say anything.

Taro looked at the ground. The shadow of the tree was a dark shape on the grass. It did not stay still. It had moved since morning. Now it reached almost to the wall.

Emma watched the light on the leaves. The wind moved them, and the light moved with them — bright, then dark, then bright again.

Taro sat down on the grass at the edge of the shade. He put one hand in the sun and one hand in the cool.

The difference was there, on his skin, at the same time.
