Taro stopped walking. He stood still in the middle of the meadow path and looked at the fence post.

He had passed it before. A hundred times. Maybe more. The grass was long around it. The wood was grey and old.

But he had never seen the mark.

He crouched down. Emma came back and crouched beside him. Biscuit sniffed the post once and moved on, nose low to the ground.

There was a deep scratch in the wood. A long line, straight and narrow. At the top of it, a nail head sat flat against the post. It was brown with rust. The wood around the nail was darker than the rest.

Taro put his finger near it. He did not touch it. He just held his finger close.

Emma traced the scratch with her own finger, very lightly. The wood felt rough. The scratch went deep into the grain.

They both looked at it for a long time.

The nail was old. Older than them. Older than the post, maybe. It had been there before they ever walked this path. Before they ever noticed.

Biscuit came back and sat down in the grass, watching them.

Taro looked at Emma. She looked at him. Neither of them said anything.

Then they stood up and walked on.
