The hopper was empty. Mei looked up into the dark wooden box and saw nothing but the bottom. No grain slid down into the hole.

The millstones kept turning. They made their low, rumbling sound, the one Mei felt through the soles of her feet. But the sound was changing. It was getting thinner.

Then the grinding stopped.

The stones were still. The mill was quiet.

Yun looked up from the sack she was folding. "Time to fill it again," she said.

Taro stood at the door, blinking in the dim light. "It just stopped," he said.

"It stops when there is nothing to grind," said Mei.

She lifted the heavy sack of wheat. It was warm and smelled of dust and sun. She tipped it carefully into the hopper. The grain poured down, a soft rushing sound.

Yun pulled the lever. The stones began to turn again. The low rumble came back, and the floor hummed under their feet.

Mei watched the flour fall, fine and white, into the cloth sack below. The sound was full again.
