The window was open a crack. A sparrow fluttered in.  

It landed on the edge of the nature table, head tilted. The classroom was very still. Emma held her breath. Taro didn’t move.  

The bird hopped once, twice—then flew straight at the glass. *Tap.* It dropped to the windowsill, dazed.  

Emma’s fingers curled tight around her pencil. The sparrow fluttered up again, wings beating fast. *Tap.* Same spot.  

Taro shifted just his eyes toward the open window. The bird didn’t see it. It darted to the corner instead, perched on top of the cupboard. Small chest heaving.  

The teacher stayed at her desk. The clock ticked.  

Then—wings. A swoop past the blackboard, a turn at the wall. This time the sparrow veered left, toward the breeze. Out it went, a quick shadow against the sky.  

Emma let go of her pencil. Taro grinned.  

Outside, the sparrow landed on the schoolyard gate. It shook its feathers once and was gone.
