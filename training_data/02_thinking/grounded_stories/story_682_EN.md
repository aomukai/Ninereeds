The pond was gold.

The sun hung low behind the willow. The water held the light like a flat mirror. Emma sat on the flat stone with Mei. They did not speak. The fish came up from the bottom—slow, dark shapes that turned and caught the gold on their sides.

On the bench under the willow, Gran sat with her hands in her lap. She was watching the same thing.

Taro threw one last pebble. It hit the water with a soft *plink*. Rings spread out, widening, and each ring carried the gold light with it. The fish flicked away. The rings faded. The water went still again.

The colour was changing. The gold deepened, then softened. The sky above the willow turned darker—not black, not yet, but a blue that was closer to evening than afternoon.

Emma looked up. Above the far edge of the field, a point of light had appeared. Very small. Very steady.

"There," she said. Just that.

Mei nodded.

Gran rose from the bench. She did not hurry them. She stood a moment, looking at the pond, then turned toward the meadow path.

The dogs were already halfway home.
