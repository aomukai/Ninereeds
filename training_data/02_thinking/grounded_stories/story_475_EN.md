Dou sat on the far side of the gate. Her tail was wrapped around her paws. She was outside the garden, and she was not coming in.

Bello saw her first. His ears went up. His front legs went stiff. He stood very still and watched.

Emma was near the apple tree. She saw Bello stop. She saw Dou on the other side of the gate, small and grey, one white ear catching the autumn light.

Bello took a step closer. Then another. He stopped at the gate and pressed his nose to the gap between the bars.

Dou did not move. She lifted one paw and began to wash it, slow and careful, as if Bello were not there at all.

Bello lay down. His nose stayed at the gap. His tail tapped the ground once.

Emma watched. She thought something would happen. Bello thought something would happen.

Nothing happened.

Dou finished washing her paw. She stood up, turned, and walked away down the lane. Her tail went first, then her body, then last of all the white ear, disappearing around the corner.

Bello stayed at the gate. His nose was still pressed to the gap. The lane was empty.

He waited a little longer. Then he got up, shook himself, and walked back across the garden.
