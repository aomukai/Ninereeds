Mei and Vern climbed the hill. Vern carried a piece of wood in his hand — long and pale, with a grain that ran straight from end to end. He turned it over as he walked, feeling the weight, running his thumb along the edge.

At the top, the wind hit them. It came up the slope without anything to break it. Mei squinted. Her hair blew across her face.

Vern stopped and held the wood up flat. He looked along its length, then turned it and looked again. "Straight," he said. He tapped it with his knuckle. The sound was clear and quick.

Mei looked where Vern was looking. Down in the village, the mill chimney was a thin dark line. Beyond it, the water wheel was still. From here it looked like a small black circle, not moving.

"The wheel turns slow in summer," Vern said. "The stream is low. But it turns." He tapped the wood again. "This will make a good spoke."

Mei felt the wind on her face. It was warm and steady, coming up from the valley. It pushed against her like something solid.

Vern put the wood under his arm and started back down. Mei followed. The wind was at their backs now, helping them down the hill.
