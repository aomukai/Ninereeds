The bench on the green was cold under Taro. He sat alone. The leaves on the horse chestnut were turning yellow. One drifted down and landed on his shoe.

A shadow crossed the ground in front of him. Taro looked up.

Stefan walked past slowly. His hands were clasped behind his back. He nodded at Taro. Not a long nod, just a small one. Taro nodded back.

Stefan kept walking. His boots made a steady sound on the stone. Near the bakery he stopped.

Hana stood in the doorway. Her apron had flour on it. She was wiping her hands on a cloth.

Stefan said something Taro could not hear. Hana laughed. She pointed up at the sky. Stefan looked up too, then nodded again.

The autumn sun caught his badge. It flashed once, bright.

Taro watched them talk. They stood in the morning light. Leaves fell between them and landed on the street. Hana turned and went back inside. Stefan stood for a moment, then moved on, slow and steady, his hands behind his back again.
