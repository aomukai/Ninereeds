Biscuit trotted past the shed and vanished. Emma was counting beans into a basket. Taro waited, then frowned.  

"Where's he gone?"  

The back of the shed was a place they never went. It was all shadow and damp brick, cool even on hot days. Taro edged around the corner.  

Moss grew thick on the wall. An old bucket lay on its side, half-full of rainwater. Broken canes made a tangled pile. And there was Biscuit, sitting very straight in the middle of it all, his tail thumping the ground.  

Emma came up behind Taro. "What's back here?"  

Biscuit sneezed. A spider scuttled under the compost bin. The lid didn't fit right—Taro could see dark, wet stuff steaming inside.  

"It's like a secret room," Emma said.  

Biscuit stood up and shook himself, pleased. Then he trotted past them, back into the sun.
