Emma woke to the sound of the kitchen fire crackling below. She yawned and stretched her arms, then swung her feet down—the floor was cold. Taro still lay curled under his blanket, one arm flung over his face. She pulled on her socks standing up, hopping to keep her balance.  

Downstairs, the kitchen smelled of porridge. Sunlight slanted through the window over the sink, catching the steam from the pot. Gran stirred without turning. "Wash up," she said. The water from the tap was icy. Emma splashed her face, groped for the towel, and caught her reflection in the foggy mirror—her hair stuck up on one side.  

Taro thumped down the stairs, rubbing his eyes. "It's early," he mumbled, but he reached for his boots anyway. Emma handed him a shirt from the pile on the bench. The fabric was warm where it had lain near the hearth.  

Gran set two bowls on the table. The porridge had a skin on top, just how Emma liked it. She blew on a spoonful while Taro poked at his, making a hole in the middle. Outside, the garden gate creaked in the wind.
