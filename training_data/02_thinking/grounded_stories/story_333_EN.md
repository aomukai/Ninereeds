Mei put a potato on the scale.

It was a brown potato, dusty from the market stall. It sat in the brass dish on one side.

Emma picked up an apple from the wooden crate. Red, with a small green patch near the stem. She placed it in the other dish.

The potato side dropped. Down.

The apple side went up. Up into the air.

Emma looked at the scale. She looked at the apple. She picked another apple from the crate. She set it beside the first one, in the same dish.

The scale did not move right away. Then the apple side began to lower. Slow. The potato side lifted.

The dish stopped. The two sides were level. Even.

Yun was watching. She did not say anything. She was tying a paper bag closed with a twist of string.

Mei touched the potato with one finger. Then she touched the apples.

"Two apples," she said.

Emma nodded. She picked up the apples. They were heavy together.

The potato sat in its dish. It was heavy too. Just as heavy as the two apples.

Yun reached for the potato. She put it in Emma's hand.

"Feel it," she said.

Emma held the potato in one hand and the two apples in the other. The weight was the same. Different shapes. Same weight.
