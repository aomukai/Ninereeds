In spring, Emma stood under the apple tree.

The branches were covered in pink blossoms. Each one was small and soft. Emma reached up and touched a petal with her finger. It fell off and landed on her hand. She held it for a moment, then let it go.

The grass beneath the tree was spotted with fallen petals. They looked like confetti after a party.

---

In summer, Emma walked to the tree alone.

The blossoms were gone. In their place were small green apples, hard and tight against the branches. She touched one. It did not move. She tried to twist it off, but it would not come.

"Not yet," Gran said from the vegetable patch. She did not look up.

Emma left the apple where it was.

---

In autumn, Emma sat on the stone bench near the kitchen door. The apple tree was full of round fruit, heavy enough to bend the branches down.

A windfall dropped onto the grass. Then another. Emma walked over and picked one up. It was soft in her hand. She bit into it. The inside was sweet and pale.

She looked up at the tree. It looked different now. She remembered the pink petals and the small green apples that would not come off.

The tree had changed without her noticing.
