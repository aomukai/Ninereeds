Taro held the coin in his palm. It was silver and heavy. Bigger than his thumb.

Gran pointed to the stall. "Two carrots," she said. "And a turnip."

Yun stood behind the table. Her hands were busy — she was tying a paper bag with a quick twist. She looked at Taro's coin and nodded.

Taro put the coin on the table.

Yun picked it up. She turned it over once, then opened a small tin beside the scales. Inside were coins of different sizes — some copper, some silver, some very small.

She counted into her hand. One. Two. Three. Then a middle-sized one.

She held them out to Taro.

He cupped his hands. She dropped the coins into them. The small ones clinked. The medium one landed last, warm from her palm.

Taro looked at the coins in his hand. Three small. One medium. The big coin was gone.

He looked at Gran.

Gran nodded.

Taro put the coins into his pocket. They made a small lump against his leg.

Yun set the carrots and the turnip on the table. Taro picked them up. They were heavier than the coins had been.
