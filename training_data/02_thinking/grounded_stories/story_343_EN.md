The woman stopped at the stall. She looked at the vegetables.

Mei stood beside the table. She watched.

The woman pointed at the carrots. "Are these good ones?"

Mei nodded. She leaned over the crate and picked one up. She turned it in her hand. The carrot was straight and firm, with a tuft of green at the top.

"This one," Mei said. She put it on the edge of the table.

The woman picked it up. She felt the weight. She nodded.

Mei picked another. Then a third. She lined them up on the table. All straight. All firm.

The woman smiled. "These are good."

Mei took a paper bag from under the table. She opened it wide. She put the carrots in one at a time, careful not to bruise them. Then she folded the top of the bag over once.

The woman reached into her pocket. She brought out a small purse. She opened it and took out two coins. She held them out.

Mei took the coins. She looked at them. Then she put them into the tin box beside the scales.

"Thank you," Mei said.

The woman took the bag. "Thank you, Mei."

She turned and walked away down the street. Mei watched her go. Then she straightened the carrots in the crate.
