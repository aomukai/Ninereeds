Riku came down the village lane on his red bicycle. The bag over his shoulder was heavy today. It leaned to one side.

Owen was at the gate to his field. He leaned on it with both hands.

“Morning, Owen,” Riku said. He did not stop. He just lifted one hand from the handlebars.

“Morning,” Owen said back.

Riku pedalled on. The lane curved. The hedges were bright green with new leaves.

Emma and Taro were walking up from Gran’s gate. Biscuit ran ahead of them, nose down.

Riku slowed. His foot came down. The bicycle stopped.

“Letter for Gran,” he said. He reached into his bag and pulled out a white envelope. He handed it over.

Emma took it. The paper was warm from being in the bag.

“Thank you,” she said.

Biscuit trotted up to the bicycle. His tail wagged.

“That’s Biscuit,” Riku said. He smiled. “I know you, Biscuit.”

Biscuit’s tail wagged harder.

Then Riku pushed off. The bicycle rolled forward. He turned the corner at the bottom of the lane, and the red was gone.
