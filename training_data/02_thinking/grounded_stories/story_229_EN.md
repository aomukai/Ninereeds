Emma put her hand flat on the wall.

The stone was cold. Under her palm, a low rumble moved through it. She pressed her ear against the surface. The sound came through her cheek now—deep and steady, like something breathing far underground.

Mei stood by the hopper, watching. A fine dust of flour settled on her sleeve.

"It goes through the floor too," Emma said. Her voice sounded small next to the grinding.

She knelt down and placed her palm on the stone floor. The vibration pushed up into her hand. It was not loud, not the way the bell was loud. It was something she felt before she heard it.

Mei came closer. She knelt too.

"It feels different in winter," she said. "The stones are colder. The sound goes further."

Emma kept her hand on the floor. The rumble was there, constant, under everything. She closed her eyes. It was like standing next to something very large that did not know she was there.

"The wall feels it too," she said. She put her other hand back against the stone.

Mei nodded. She did not say anything else.

The millstones turned. The sound stayed low.
