Emma ran her fingers along the pear tree’s branch. It didn’t reach up like the apple tree’s. It lay flat against the wall, its twigs spread like fingers on stone.  

"Why is it like this?" she asked.  

Gran knelt by the east wall, where the sun had warmed the stones all morning. She touched a strip of cloth tied around a branch. "See how we guide it?" The branch followed the wall’s curve, held close by soft ties.  

Emma pressed her palm to the stone. It was warm, even in the shade now.  

"The wall keeps it," Gran said. "Warms it at dawn, holds heat past dusk." She traced a new shoot, barely thumb-thick, and looped a tie around it. "Grows toward what steadies it."  

A pear, small and hard, hung where the branch turned. Emma brushed its skin. The tree leaned into the wall as if listening. Above, the apple tree swayed free in the wind. Here, the air was still. The stones hummed with sun.
