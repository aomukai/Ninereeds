Mei put her hand on the lever. It was a long bar of dark iron, fixed to the wall beside the sluice gate.

"Watch," she said.

She pressed down with both hands. The lever did not move. She pushed harder, leaning in with her whole weight. Taro stepped close and pushed beside her. Together they forced it down.

A sound came from below — a rush, like something waking up.

Water poured into the channel. It hit the wheel hard. The wheel had been turning slow, almost lazy. Now it changed. It picked up speed. The spokes blurred. Water flew off the rim in bright arcs.

Taro felt it through his feet first. A low trembling came up from the stone floor, through his shoes, up into his knees.

"It shakes," he said.

"The floor," Mei said. "The whole building. When the wheel goes fast, it shakes the stones."

They stood still and felt it. The wheel turned. The water roared. The floor hummed beneath them like a living thing.
