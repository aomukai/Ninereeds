The workshop was quiet. Sawdust lay thick on the floor.

Vern knelt down. He held a small iron nail between his thumb and finger. Then he dropped it.

It vanished into the sawdust.

Emma watched. "You lost it."

Vern did not answer. He stood up and took a grey block from a shelf. It was smooth and heavy. He brought it down to the floor.

He passed the block through the sawdust in a slow, wide arc.

The nail jumped.

It stuck to the bottom of the block. It hung there, pulled tight against the grey surface, buried in sawdust that clung but did not hold.

"That is a magnet," Vern said. He turned the block over. The nail stayed. He shook it once. The sawdust fell away. The nail did not fall.

Emma leaned closer. "It pulled it."

"It pulled the metal," Vern said. "Not the sawdust. Not the wood. Only the iron."

He held the block out to her. She took it. The nail stuck to the bottom. She tipped the block sideways. The nail moved with it, clinging.

She picked the nail off with her other hand. It came away with a small click.

Then she held the nail near the block again. It jumped back. It stuck. She did not drop it this time — the magnet caught it first.
