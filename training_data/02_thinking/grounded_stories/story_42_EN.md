Gran wiped her hands on her apron. "Four bowls for the table," she said, and went back to stirring the pot.  

Emma stood on tiptoe by the shelf. Her fingers found one bowl—smooth, with a chip on the rim. "Only one here," she said.  

Taro yanked the cupboard door. It stuck, then gave with a creak. Inside, three bowls sat stacked like a tower. He grabbed them, warm from the sun through the window. "One from the shelf, three from the cupboard. One and three is four."  

Emma took them to the table. The wood was rough under her palms. She set a bowl at each seat: "One, two, three, four." The last one clinked against Gran's spoon.  

"Just right," Emma said.  

Gran nodded, her back to them, and lifted the ladle. Steam curled up to the ceiling.
