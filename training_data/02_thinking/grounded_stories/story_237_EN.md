The sack stood on the mill floor. It was taller than Taro's knees and wider than his shoulders.

He put both arms around it. He pulled. The sack did not move.

He set his feet wider and tried again. His face went red. A small sound came out of his throat. The sack stayed where it was.

Taro let go and stood back. He looked at the sack. He looked at his hands.

Mei stood by the hopper, watching. She did not say anything.

Taro tried again. This time he got the sack to tip. It rocked. He pulled it toward him. The sack slid across the stone floor a little way. Then it stopped and he could not make it move again.

Yun came in from the yard. She stood with her hands on her hips.

"It takes two hands and a good back," she said. "And sometimes that is not enough."

She looked at Mei.

Mei walked over. She stood on the other side of the sack. She looked at Taro.

"Together," she said.

They each took a handful of the cloth. They bent their knees. They lifted.

The sack came off the ground. It was still very heavy. But it was off the ground.

They carried it together, three steps, and set it down by the millstones.
