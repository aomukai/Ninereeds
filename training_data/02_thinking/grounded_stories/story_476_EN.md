The mill wheel turned. Water splashed from every spoke.

Mei stood at the edge of the stream and watched. The wheel dipped, lifted, dipped again. Each time it came down, a sheet of water slid off the wood and hit the stream with a slap.

Biscuit stood beside her. His ears were up. His nose worked.

A splash landed near his front paws. He stepped back. He put one paw forward again, testing. The wheel dipped. Water shot out sideways. Biscuit jumped back.

He circled the wheel slowly, keeping his distance. His nose touched the wet wood where the stream had run over it. He sniffed once, twice, then pulled his head away and sneezed.

The wheel turned. The water kept falling.

Biscuit sat down on the dry bank, a few paces from the splash zone. He watched the wheel go around. His head moved a little with each turn, left to right, left to right.

Mei sat down beside him. The wheel creaked. The water splashed. Biscuit did not move.
