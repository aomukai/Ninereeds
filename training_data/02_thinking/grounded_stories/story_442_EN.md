Gran lifted the iron bar and set it against the stone.

The root-cellar door swung open. A dark gap appeared in the ground near the kitchen wall.

Bello trotted over. He pushed his nose into the gap and stopped.

The smell came up thick and cold. Earth. Root. Damp. Something deep that did not move.

Bello sneezed.

Gran struck a match and lit the lamp on the top step. The flame grew small and yellow. It did not reach far into the dark below.

"Stay there," Gran said, and she took the first step down.

Bello stayed. He stood at the edge of the open door, ears up, watching the light sink into the cellar. The shadows moved. The lamp threw a circle on the stone wall, then another, then touched the crates and the hanging ropes of onions.

Bello did not follow.

He watched Gran's hand move along the shelf. He watched the light shift. He did not put one paw on the step.

The smell came up past him, cold and patient. He sneezed again.

Gran came back up. She set the lamp on the kitchen floor and lowered the door into place. The iron bar went back against the stone.

Bello shook himself.
