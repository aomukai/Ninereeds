Vern held the two pieces of wood up to the light. He fitted them together, one into the other. There was a gap.

He turned the joint in his hands. He looked at the gap from one side, then the other.

"Too much," he said.

He tapped the joint apart with the side of his hand. The pieces came loose. He picked up a plane and took a single thin shaving from the edge of one piece. The shaving curled and fell to the floor.

He fitted the pieces together again. No gap. The joint sat tight and straight.

He set the piece down on the bench. He put his palm flat on top and pressed. Nothing moved.

Emma watched. She had not said anything. The workshop smelled of sawdust and oil. The vise held a piece of wood that was waiting.

Vern picked up the glue bottle. He squeezed a thin line along the joint, fitted the pieces together a third time, and set them in the clamp. He turned the handle slowly. The wood creaked once and then was still.

"Dry fit first," he said. "Then glue."

He wiped his fingers on his apron.
