Emma stood on the chair, her elbows barely clearing the counter. Gran’s knife tapped the board—chop, chop, chop—until the onion fell into pale pieces.  

"Stir," Gran said, nudging the wooden spoon toward her.  

The pot bubbled. Emma dragged the spoon through, scraping bits from the bottom. Steam curled up, warm and smelling of garlic. It made her eyes sting.  

Gran tossed in the onions. They hissed. She wiped her hands on her apron, then pinched salt over the pot. "Taste."  

Emma blew on the spoonful. Hot. Rich. Something missing.  

Gran dropped a pat of butter in. It swirled and vanished. "Again."  

Outside, the oak’s bare branches clicked in the wind. Inside, the soup thickened. Biscuit sprawled by the hearth, nose twitching at the smell.  

"More salt?" Emma asked.  

Gran shook a little in. "Now it’s yours."  

Emma stirred until her arm ached. The spoon left trails in the golden surface.
