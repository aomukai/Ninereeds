The door was heavy. Emma knew that. She had to put the lamp down first, then pull with both hands. The iron bar held it open while she went down the stone steps.

Inside, the root cellar was cold. The lamp threw shadows across the jars. She found the plums on the third shelf, felt the jar cool and solid in her hands.

Then the door fell.

It made a sound like a stone dropping. A deep, flat thud. The iron bar clattered. The light from above went out.

Emma stood still. The lamp flame flickered. The shadows moved.

She looked up the steps. The door was shut. Dark wood against dark stone.

"Taro?" she called.

The cellar was quiet. The lamp hissed very softly.

Then footsteps crossed the kitchen floor. Quick, heavy. Taro's voice came down the steps.

"Emma? Are you down there?"

"The door fell shut," she said.

She heard him push. It did not open. He pushed harder. The wood groaned. Then there was a scrape, and light came through the crack.

Taro's face appeared. He was holding the edge of the door with both hands.

"Got it," he said.

Emma climbed up, the jar in one hand, the lamp in the other. Outside, the kitchen was bright and warm.
