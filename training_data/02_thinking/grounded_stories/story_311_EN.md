The market was busy. Hana’s bakery smell drifted down the street. Yun’s stall had jars in rows.

Emma stopped in front of the honey. Two jars sat side by side. One was taller. One was shorter.

She picked up the smaller jar. A card stood beside it. A number was written on the card: 3.

She put it down. She touched the bigger jar. Its card said 5.

“Three is less,” she said quietly. “Five is more.”

Taro was further along the stall. He pointed at a bag of nuts.

“How much?” he said.

Mei stood beside him. She looked at the card on the bag. It said 4.

She did not speak. She just looked at the number, then at Taro.

Taro counted on his fingers. “Four,” he said. “That is more than the honey.”

Emma was still at the honey jars. She held the smaller one again. It was light. She could lift it with one hand.

Mei walked over. She pointed at the bigger jar. She tapped the card. Then she pointed at the smaller jar and tapped the 3.

Emma looked at her.

“The big one costs more,” Emma said.

Mei nodded.

Emma put the small jar back. She picked up the big one. It was heavier. She held it with two hands.

“For Gran,” she said.

Taro came over with the nuts. “I want these.”

Mei looked at both. She pointed at the honey jar. Then she pointed at the nut bag. She held up five fingers. Then four. Then she waited.
