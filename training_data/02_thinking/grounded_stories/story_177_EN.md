Emma found the calendar under Gran’s sewing basket. She spread it on the kitchen table and pointed.  

"Today’s here. Gran’s birthday is—"  

"Three sleeps!" Taro bounced on his toes.  

Emma frowned. "No, it’s—" She counted squares with her finger. "Four."  

Taro leaned over her shoulder. "You skipped that one."  

They counted together, whispering. The squares were small. Emma’s finger covered two at once.  

"Four," she said finally.  

Taro sighed. "That’s longer to wait."  

Emma took the pencil from behind her ear. She wrote *LIST* at the top of a scrap of paper.  

"Flowers," she said, writing it down.  

"From the meadow?"  

"Too muddy after the rain. From the garden."  

Taro tapped the next line. "Eggs for cake."  

"We have three. Need five."  

He grinned. "I’ll sneak them from the nest tomorrow."  

Emma added *buy butter*. The list curled at the edges. She folded it small and tucked it into her pocket.  

Taro pressed a finger to his lips. "Secret."  

Outside, the hens clucked. The garden waited.
