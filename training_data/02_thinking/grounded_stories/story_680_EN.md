A puddle sat in the middle of the meadow path. It was wide and still, like a dark eye looking up at the sky.

Emma knelt beside it. She put her hands on her knees and watched.

A drop fell from a branch above. It hit the water with a soft *plink*.

Rings spread out from the place where the drop had landed. One ring. Two. Three. Four. Five. Then the water was still again.

Emma counted under her breath. "One, two, three, four, five."

Another drop fell. The same number. The same pattern. The rings opened and closed like a flower made of water.

Mei came up the path. She stopped beside Emma and looked down at the puddle.

"Five," Emma said. "Every time."

Mei picked a leaf from the hedge. It was small and green, with a stem still attached. She held it over the puddle and let it go.

The leaf landed in the centre. The rings spread out around it — one, two, three, four, five — but this time the leaf stayed. It floated in the middle, turning very slowly.

Emma watched the rings go around the leaf. The pattern was different now. The water moved around the leaf and met on the other side.

A drop fell. The rings spread. The leaf rocked.

"Six," Emma said. "Because of the leaf."
