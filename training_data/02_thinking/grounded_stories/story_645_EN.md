Emma and Mei stood under the oak. They looked up into the fork where the branches met.

"Three," Emma whispered.

Three blue eggs sat in the robin's nest. They were small and still. The mother robin had flown away when the girls came close. She watched from the hedge.

Every morning Emma and Mei came to look. They stood very quiet. Biscuit sat behind them and did not bark.

One morning the eggs were gone.

Three small mouths opened wide instead. They made thin sounds.

"They are hungry," Mei said.

The mother robin came with a worm. The mouths took it. Then another mouth. Then the last one.

The girls came every day. The mouths grew bigger. Feathers appeared. The nest began to look crowded.

Then one morning the nest was empty.

Emma looked around. A small bird sat on the low branch. It had a speckled chest. Not red yet.

"It is learning," Mei said.

The fledgling fluttered its wings. It did not fly. It sat very still.

Emma and Mei stood without moving. The speckled chest rose and fell. The robin's eyes were dark and round.

After a long time, the fledgling shook itself. It lifted from the branch — a short, clumsy flight to the hedge.

The nest fork was empty.

Emma looked at the blue shell she had found on the ground. She turned it in her fingers. It was very light.
