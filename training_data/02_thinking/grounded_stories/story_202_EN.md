Mei stopped walking. She put her foot to one side of a board in the mill floor. Then she stepped over it.

Emma watched. The board looked like all the others. Grey wood. Old. A crack ran along one edge.

"Why did you do that?" Emma said.

Mei pointed with her toe. "This one lifts."

She pressed the end of the board with her shoe. The far side rose a little. Emma saw the gap underneath. It was dark. It was deeper than she expected.

"Watch," Mei said.

She hooked her toe under the raised edge and lifted. The board came up. The hole below was black. A foot would go in there. A whole leg, maybe.

Emma took a step back.

Mei let the board fall. It settled with a soft thump.

"You have to know which ones," Mei said. She walked on. Her feet found the safe places without looking.

Emma followed. She watched the floor now. She watched where Mei put her feet. She watched the cracks and the edges and the places where the wood had worn thin.

She did not step on the board near the hopper. She stepped over it, the same way Mei did.
