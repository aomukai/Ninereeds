Taro held the wooden horse in both hands. One wheel was loose. It hung sideways, and the little axle that should have held it was snapped clean in two.

Vern took the horse and turned it over. He looked at the break without saying anything. Then he put the horse on the workbench and ran his finger along the broken edge.

He opened a drawer. Inside were short wooden dowels, all the same thickness. He picked one, cut it with a small saw, and held the piece up to the light. Then he chose a thin drill bit from a row on the wall.

The drill made a whirring sound. Vern pushed it gently into the centre of the wheel. A curl of wood dust fell onto the bench.

He fitted the new axle into the hole. Then he put the wheel against the horse's body and tapped the axle in with a small hammer. Three taps. No more.

He set the horse on the bench and gave the wheel a push. It turned.

Vern picked up a tiny pot of glue and touched the tip of a brush to the axle end. He pressed the wheel one more time, just gently, then set the horse in a clamp to hold it still.

"Let it sit," he said.

Taro touched the wheel. It was tight. It was straight. He turned it once, slowly. It spun.
