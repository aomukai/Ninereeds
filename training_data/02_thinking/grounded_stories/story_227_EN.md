The mill was cold. The wheel turned slow in winter water.

Mei stood beside the hopper. Gran poured grain into the top. Yun held the sack open below. The sound was low and even — grinding, turning, the stone against stone.

After an hour, everything was white.

Gran's sleeves were dusted with flour. The fine powder clung to the wool. Yun's hair had turned pale. She looked like someone made of snow. Mei looked down at her own hands. They were white. Her coat was white. Even her eyelashes were white.

She opened her mouth to say something and tasted flour on her tongue.

The air was thick with it. It hung in the still cold. It settled on the floor, on the beams, on the windowsill where the light came through grey and soft.

Mei shook her coat at the door. A small white cloud fell. It drifted down and landed on the step.

Gran brushed her sleeves. Yun shook her hair. The flour did not stop falling. It was everywhere.

"Come," said Yun. "Before we turn into loaves."

Mei shut the door. The dust still hung in the air behind them.
