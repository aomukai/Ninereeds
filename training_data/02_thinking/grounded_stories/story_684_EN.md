The bean canes were tied at the top. They leaned together like a tent. Green leaves covered the poles from bottom to top.

Emma stood at the opening. She pushed a leaf aside and looked in.

Mei knelt down and looked too. "It's different in there," she said.

Emma crawled inside. The leaves closed behind her. The air was cooler. Green light came through the leaves. The poles stood around her like thin tree trunks.

She touched a leaf. It was soft and wide. A stem curled around the cane, round and round, climbing up.

"Emma?" Mei's voice came through the leaves.

"Come in," Emma said.

Mei crawled in beside her. They sat very still. The wigwam was full of leaves and green light.

Emma saw something between two leaves. A bean pod. It hung down, thin and green, hidden.

She touched it. It was firm.

She pulled it carefully. It came away in her hand.

"Look," she said.

Mei looked. "Is there a bean inside?"

Emma held the pod out. Gran took it. She pressed her thumb along the seam. The pod opened.

Three small beans lay inside. Pale green. Soft.

Emma touched one. It was smooth and small.

"A seed," Gran said. "If you put it in the ground, it grows."

Emma looked at the three small beans in the open pod. Then she looked at the leaves all around her, climbing the canes, reaching up.
