Gran lifted the cellar door with the iron bar and propped it open. Emma went first, one hand on the wall. Mei followed.

The lamp Gran had lit sat on the top step. Emma picked it up and held it low. The light spread across the floor.

“It’s earth,” Emma said. She knelt and touched it. The ground was hard and a little damp. It did not give under her fingers.

Mei knelt too. She pressed her palm flat. “Not stone. Not wood.”

“Stone in the kitchen,” Emma said. “This is different.”

She moved the lamp toward the far wall. Near the corner, a pale root came through the stone and curved down into the earth. Emma touched it. It was smooth and cool.

“A tree root,” Mei said.

Emma stood and looked around. The floor was clean but dusty in places. A small broom leaned against the wall near the steps. She picked it up and swept the dust near the root, pushing it into a small pile.

Mei watched. “It stays hard,” she said.

“Yes,” Emma said. She swept a little more. The broom made a soft sound against the earth.
