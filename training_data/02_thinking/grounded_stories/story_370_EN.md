The rain came down hard on the hill.

It did not stay where it landed. The slope was too steep. The ground was already wet from yesterday.

Water ran in thin streams down the path. It gathered in the dips. It cut small channels through the mud.

Taro put his foot down on a patch that looked flat. The mud gave way. His foot slid sideways and he sat down hard.

"Careful," Gran said. She did not turn around. She was watching the water.

Emma watched too. The streams ran together. They grew wider and faster. They carved little lines in the earth, like fingers dragging through soft soil.

"Where does it all go?" Emma asked.

"Down," Gran said. "It finds its way down."

They followed the path to the bottom. The water from every little channel had reached the same place. It poured into the ditch beside the lane, brown and fast.

Taro's boot was full of mud. He pulled it off and tipped it sideways. Mud and water ran out onto the grass.

Gran looked at the sky. The rain was easing.

"The hill does the work for us," she said. "It sends the water where it needs to go."
