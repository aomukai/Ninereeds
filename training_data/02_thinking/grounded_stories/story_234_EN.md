The stream was different.

It did not sound the same. Usually Taro heard it from the path — a running, busy noise. Today it was quiet.

He stepped onto the stone beside the mill. Mei was already there, looking down.

The water was low. The bottom showed. Stones that were usually hidden lay in the sun, dry on top, dark underneath.

A small pool had formed where the stream bent. It was still. It did not move.

Taro crouched and put his hand in.

"It's warm," he said.

Mei nodded. She pointed at the pool. "There are fish."

Taro looked. Two dark shapes hung near the bottom, very still. They did not dart away. They barely moved.

Biscuit pushed past Taro's shoulder and put his nose in the water. He drank. His tongue made the only sound.

The stream ran in a thin trickle over the stones. It was so slow Taro could count the ripples. The mud at the edge had cracked into hard, dry plates. The sun sat on everything.

Mei sat back on her heels. "It will come back," she said. "When the rain comes."

Taro kept his hand in the warm water. The fish did not move. Biscuit sat down in the shade of the mill wall and waited.
