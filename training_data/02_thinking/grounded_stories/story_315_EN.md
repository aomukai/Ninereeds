Taro stood at the market stall. He pulled the purse from his pocket. It was a small cloth purse, blue, with a drawstring at the top.

He opened it. He tipped the coins into his hand. Four coins. He counted them again, one by one. Four.

The honey jar sat on the stall table. It was golden. The light went through it. A small card in front said: six.

Taro looked at the coins in his hand. He looked at the jar. He put the coins back in the purse.

He reached for the jar. His fingers touched the glass. Then he let go. He picked up the small bag beside it instead. Dried apples. The card said: four.

Yun watched him. She did not say anything. She took the bag from his hand and held it out to him.

Taro opened his purse again. He counted out four coins and placed them on the wooden board of the stall. The coins made a small sound.

Yun picked them up. She dropped them into her apron pocket. The change was a single coin left in Taro's purse. He looked inside. One.

He took the bag. It was light. He could feel the dried apple pieces through the paper.

He turned and walked away from the stall.
