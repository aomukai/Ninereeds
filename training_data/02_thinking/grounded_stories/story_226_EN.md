The stream was a trickle. It ran thin over the stones, barely covering the bottom.

Mei stood at the edge of the mill race and looked down. The water came slow. It did not fill the channel.

Taro sat on the bank where the grass was dry and brown. Biscuit lay beside him with his tongue out. The air was hot and still.

Mei walked to the sluice gate. She pulled the lever. The wheel groaned once, very slowly, and then stopped.

"Not enough," she said.

She sat down on the stone step. The dust from the mill floor was white on her shoes. She looked at the thin trickle of water and did not say anything else.

Taro waited. He watched the wheel. It was a large dark shape against the bright sky. It did not move.

Biscuit got up, walked to the stream, and put one paw in. The water came up to his ankle. He lapped at it once and went back to the shade.

Mei did not move. She was watching the trickle, waiting for it to become more.
