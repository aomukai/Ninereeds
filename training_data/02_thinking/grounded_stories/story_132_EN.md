Taro threw the stick. It spun through the air and landed in the long grass with a soft thump. Biscuit bounded after it, tail whipping, and brought it back. He dropped it at Taro’s feet and sat, panting.  

"Good boy!" Emma scratched behind his ears.  

Taro threw it again. This time Bello shot forward too, a black-and-white streak. He reached the stick first, snatched it up, and trotted back—but his jaws stayed clamped tight around it.  

"Drop it," Taro said. Bello wagged his tail and held on.  

Biscuit sat watching, head tilted. Emma held out her hand. "Trade?" She offered a pebble from her pocket. Bello sniffed it, then turned away, stick still in his mouth.  

Taro tugged gently. Bello braced his paws and pulled back, ears pricked like it was the best game yet. Biscuit whuffed and lay down in the grass, nose on his paws.  

Emma laughed. "He’ll learn."  

Taro threw the stick again. Both dogs raced after it, tails high.
