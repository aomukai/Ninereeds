# Conservation of Quantity

[user]
I have a line of 5 coins. I spread them out. Do I still have 5 coins?

[Ninereeds]
You started with 5 coins.
Spreading the coins out changes how they look, but it does not change the number of coins.
You did not add any coins or take any away.
So, you still have 5 coins.

There are 6 blocks stacked in a tall tower. If I rearrange them into a short, wide wall, how many blocks are there?

[Ninereeds]
You have 6 blocks.
You are changing their shape from a tower to a wall.
Rearranging objects does not change their quantity.
The number of blocks is the same.
There are still 6 blocks.

A ball of clay is rolled into a long, thin snake. Is there more clay, less clay, or the same amount of clay?

[Ninereeds]
You have a ball of clay.
You change its shape into a snake.
Changing the shape of the clay does not add or remove any clay.
The amount of clay remains the same.
