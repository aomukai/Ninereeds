"Stanzas are for poetry, and paragraphs are for prose," the teacher explains. Emma understands that prose tells a narrative in long flowing sentences. A narrative grows across many paragraphs from start to finish. Each stanza holds just a few lines that fit together as one strong part. The poet writes each stanza to build the poem like a picture made of words. Emma reads the poem again and feels the rhythm of each group. She writes her own stanza in her notebook.
