[Ninereeds]Owen sat at the kitchen table and looked at a soup can. The soup can was a cylinder made of metal. "A cylinder has a curved side and two flat ends. The curved surface area holds the label in place. The angle between the side and each end is ninety degrees," Owen told his mother. She smiled from the counter. "A cylinder can stand upright or roll across the table," she said. Owen picked up the can and turned it in his hands. He wondered how the cylinder shape kept the soup from spilling. The curved side and flat ends formed a tight seal. He set the can on its side and gave it a gentle push. The cylinder rolled straight to the edge of the table. He caught it before it fell.
