Nora found a container that held exactly one liter of water. She remembered that one liter of water also weighs one kilogram. If the container is full of water, then the scale will show the same number as the bag of flour. She tested this and watched the scale confirm her prediction.

After that, Nora poured the water out and filled the same container with oil. The scale showed a lower number this time. "Why is the oil lighter if the container is the same size?" she asked. Her mother explained that oil is less dense than water. Nora understood that the same volume can hold different amounts of mass.

Finally, Nora placed the empty container back on the scale and saw its own weight. She subtracted that number in her head to find the mass of the substance alone. She now knew that a kilogram is a fixed amount of mass, while a liter describes how much space something takes up. If she knows the density, then she can predict the weight of any substance.
