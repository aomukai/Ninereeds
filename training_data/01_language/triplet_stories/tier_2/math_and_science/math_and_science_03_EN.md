[Ninereeds]A boy named Luke stood in his classroom looking at a poster of an atom on the wall. "What keeps the electrons moving around the nucleus?" he asked. His teacher pointed to the poster and said the electrons stay in a constant dance around the center. Luke learned that the number of electrons in an atom defines which element it is. Carbon has six electrons, so it is always carbon. Oxygen has eight electrons, and that makes it oxygen. Hydrogen has just one electron, and Luke found that very simple. He saw that a gold atom is not the same as an iron atom because they have different numbers of electrons. Luke smiled and said, "Every element is special in its own way." Everything in the world is made from these tiny atoms, and Luke felt amazed that something so small could make up everything around him.
