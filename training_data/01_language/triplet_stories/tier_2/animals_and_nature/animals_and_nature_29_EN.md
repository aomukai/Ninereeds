[Ninereeds]Nora walks across the dry sand near a patch of flat rocks. She carries a small notebook and a pencil. Her father said she could explore the desert while the sun was still low. She sees a scorpion waiting under a flat rock. Bristle hairs on the scorpion's legs sense a beetle moving nearby. "Look," Nora whispers. "It is hunting." The scorpion does not move yet. It waits for the beetle to come closer. Then the scorpion pounces forward with a quick strike of the tail. Venom flows from the stinger into the beetle. The scorpion carries the beetle back under the rock. Nora writes in her notebook. "I saw a scorpion catch its food," she says.
