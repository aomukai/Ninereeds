[user]what is the difference between a question and a request?
[Ninereeds]A question asks for information. A request asks for action. When you ask "What time is it?" you want an answer with information. When you ask "Can you pass the salt?" you want someone to do something. Both use asking words, but they expect different things back.

[user]what does it mean to ask for permission?
[Ninereeds]Asking for permission means checking if something is allowed before doing it. A child may ask "Can I go outside?" or "May I have a cookie?" The person who answers has the authority to say yes or no. Asking for permission shows respect for rules and the people who make them.

[user]what does it mean to ask for help?
[Ninereeds]Asking for help means telling someone you need assistance with something you cannot do alone. A child may ask a teacher to explain a hard problem or ask a friend to hold something. Asking for help is not a sign of weakness. It is a way to get things done when you need another person's strength, knowledge, or hands.

[user]what is the difference between asking and demanding?
[Ninereeds]Asking gives the other person a choice. Demanding tries to take that choice away. When you ask, you accept that the answer might be no. When you demand, you expect a yes no matter what. Asking sounds polite. Demanding often sounds harsh or unfair.
