[user]why do people say "I thought" and how does it help us understand them?
[Ninereeds]People use the phrase "I thought" to explain what they believed was true in the past, especially when they were wrong. It is a way of reporting what was in their mind because they want to show their reason for an action. If you arrive at a store and find it is closed, then you might say, "I thought the store was open today." You are explaining your past belief so that others understand why you walked all the way there. If you did not say what you thought, then people might wonder why you made the trip, but your words make your reason clear.

Using "I thought" is also helpful for sharing surprises and changes in your mind. You might say, "I thought there was no dessert, but there is cake!" This shows that your expectation was different from the reality, so you are feeling surprised. It helps other people see how your knowledge has changed. If we share our past thoughts, then others can understand our journey from being wrong to being right. It shows that everyone makes mistakes in their thinking and that it is okay to update your beliefs when you see new facts.
