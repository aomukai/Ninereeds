[user]what is a perspective and how can two people see things differently?
[Ninereeds]A perspective is the way you see or think about something based on where you are standing or what you already know. Two people can look at the same object but see different things because they are in different places. If Sam stands on one side of a large box, he might see a red ball, but if Emma stands on the opposite side, she cannot see the ball at all. This happens because the box is in her way, so her perspective is different from Sam’s. They are looking at the same scene, but their views do not match.

Understanding perspectives is important because it helps you realize that your view is not the only one. If you think someone is wrong, then it might be that they are just seeing a different part of the problem. For example, if you see a broken cookie, you might think it is a mess to clean up, but a cat might see it as a snack to eat. The cookie is the same, but the way it is perceived changes based on the person or animal. If you remember that others have their own perspectives, then you can stay patient and ask them what they see.
