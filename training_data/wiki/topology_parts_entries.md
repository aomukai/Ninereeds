# Topology Parts Entries

[user]what is the neck of a bottle?
[assistant]The neck of a bottle is the narrow part near the top. The neck connects the wide body of the bottle to the opening. People pour liquid out through the neck of a bottle. A bottle may also be held near the neck. The neck of a bottle is not the bottom of a bottle.

[user]what is the rim of a cup?
[assistant]The rim of a cup is the thin edge around the top. The rim is where a drink reaches the mouth when someone sips. The rim helps show where the opening of the cup begins and ends. A chip in the rim can make a cup feel rough or unsafe. The rim of a cup is not the handle of a cup.

[user]what is the tip of a branch?
[assistant]The tip of a branch is the far end of the branch. The tip is smaller and thinner than the part near the trunk. New leaves, buds, or flowers may grow near the tip. The tip moves easily in the wind because it is so light. The tip of a branch is not the trunk of a tree.

[user]what is the top of a hill?
[assistant]The top of a hill is the highest part of the hill. From the top of a hill, people can often see far across the land. Wind may feel stronger at the top of a hill than below. The ground begins to slope down on all sides from the top. The top of a hill is not the valley.

[user]what is the edge of a road?
[assistant]The edge of a road is the outer side where the road ends. The edge marks the line between the road and the ground beside it. People and vehicles must be careful near the edge of a road. Grass, gravel, or dirt may begin at the edge. The edge of a road is not the middle of a road.

[user]what is the bank of a river?
[assistant]The bank of a river is the land along the side of the river. Grass, mud, stones, or trees may grow on a river bank. People may stand or walk on the bank beside the water. The shape of the bank helps guide the river as it flows. The bank of a river is not the middle of the river.

[user]what is the foot of a tree?
[assistant]The foot of a tree is the bottom part where the trunk meets the ground. Roots begin to spread out below the foot of a tree. Leaves, sticks, or shade may gather near the foot. Small animals and insects may hide there. The foot of a tree is not the top of a tree.

[user]what is the back of a hand?
[assistant]The back of a hand is the outer side opposite the palm. The back of a hand shows the knuckles and the nails. Skin on the back of a hand is often thinner than skin on the palm. People may feel rain, wind, or warmth on the back of a hand. The back of a hand is not the palm.
