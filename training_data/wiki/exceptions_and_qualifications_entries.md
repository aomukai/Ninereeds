# Exceptions and Qualifications Entries

[user]what does usually mean?
[assistant]Usually means something happens most of the time, but not every time. Birds usually fly, and children usually sleep at night. The word keeps a statement broad but not absolute. Usually leaves room for exceptions. Usually is not never.

[user]what does sometimes mean?
[assistant]Sometimes means something happens at some times but not at all times. It sometimes rains in the afternoon, and a child sometimes changes plans. The word shows that a thing can happen more than once without happening every time. Sometimes is weaker than usually. Sometimes is not never.

[user]what does except mean?
[assistant]Except points to the part that does not follow the main rule. A class may line up together except for one child who is absent. The word helps people name what is outside the main group or pattern. Except shows a clear exception. Except is not usually.

[user]what does not always mean?
[assistant]Not always means something is true at many times but not every time. A game is not always easy, and a day is not always sunny. The phrase helps people avoid making a rule too strong. Not always leaves room for change and exceptions. Not always is not most of the time.

[user]what does most of the time mean?
[assistant]Most of the time means in more cases than not. A dog may sleep most of the time during a lazy afternoon. The phrase is close to usually and points to what happens in many cases. It is useful when a statement is often true but not perfect. Most of the time is not every time.

[user]what does in this case mean?
[assistant]In this case means in this one situation or example. A child may say in this case, the plant needs more water. The phrase helps people talk about one special moment instead of the whole rule. In this case narrows the focus to one example. In this case is not every case.

[user]what does special case mean?
[assistant]A special case is one example that works differently from the usual pattern. Most birds fly, but a penguin can be a special case. People use special case when one thing needs its own note. A special case helps make a rule more accurate. A special case is not the main rule.

[user]what does it depends mean?
[assistant]It depends means the answer changes with the situation. A child may say it depends when asked if a coat is needed or if a game is fair. The phrase shows that one answer does not fit every case. It depends invites people to look at the details. It depends is not usually.

[user]what does unless mean?
[assistant]Unless means something will happen except in one special condition. A child may go outside unless it starts raining. The word adds one condition that changes the result. Unless helps make a rule more exact. Unless is not because.
