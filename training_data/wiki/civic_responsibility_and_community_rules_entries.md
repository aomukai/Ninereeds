# Civic Responsibility and Community Rules Entries

[user]what is a responsibility?
[assistant]A responsibility is something a person is expected to do or take care of. Children may have responsibilities at home, at school, and in the community. Responsibilities help groups stay orderly and safe. A responsibility is part of being a member of a group. A responsibility is not a right.

[user]what is authority?
[assistant]Authority is the power to make rules, lead, or decide in a certain place or role. A teacher, principal, or police officer may have authority in different situations. Authority works best with fairness and clear rules. People often follow authority because of the role, not because of fear alone. Authority is not a classmate.

[user]what are community rules?
[assistant]Community rules are rules people follow in shared places. Roads, parks, schools, and towns all use community rules. Community rules help people live together more safely and fairly. The rules matter because many people share the same spaces. Community rules are not personal wishes.

[user]what does follow the rules mean?
[assistant]Follow the rules means act in the way the rules say. Children follow rules in games, classrooms, and public places. Following rules can help keep things fair and orderly. People still need to understand the rule, not only hear it. Follow the rules is not break the rules.

[user]what does what's allowed mean?
[assistant]What's allowed means what a person may do under the rules. A child may ask what's allowed in a game, a classroom, or a park. The question helps people stay inside the rules. Knowing what is allowed can prevent mistakes and arguments. What's allowed is not what's forbidden.

[user]what is a citizen?
[assistant]A citizen is a person who belongs to a country and lives under its laws and systems. Even children learn about citizens as members of a community and country. Citizens share places, rules, and responsibilities with others. The idea helps connect the self to a larger group. A citizen is not a visitor.

[user]what does vote mean?
[assistant]Vote means make a choice in an organized way, often by raising a hand, marking a paper, or using a system. A class may vote on a game or a book. Voting helps a group make one decision together. Each vote counts as one choice in the group result. Vote is not authority.

[user]what are classroom jobs?
[assistant]Classroom jobs are small responsibilities given to students in a class. One child may pass papers, another may clean the board, and another may help line people up. Classroom jobs help the room run more smoothly. They teach responsibility and participation. Classroom jobs are not community rules.

[user]what is a right?
[assistant]A right is something a person should be allowed to have or do under fair rules. Children learn about rights such as safety, learning, and being treated fairly. Rights help protect people in families, schools, and communities. Rights often go together with responsibilities. A right is not a privilege earned by luck.

[user]what is a privilege?
[assistant]A privilege is something special that a person is allowed to do or have because of a rule, a role, or earned trust. A privilege is not something everyone is given by default. A child may earn the privilege of more free time by showing responsibility. Privileges can be given or taken away. A privilege is not the same as a right.
