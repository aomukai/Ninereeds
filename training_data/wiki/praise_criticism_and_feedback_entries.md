# Praise, Criticism, and Feedback Entries

[user]what is praise?
[assistant]Praise is kind language that notices something good. People give praise for effort, kindness, skill, or progress. Praise can make a person feel proud or encouraged. Praise is not criticism.

[user]what is criticism?
[assistant]Criticism is language that points out a problem or weakness. Criticism can be helpful when it is clear and respectful. Mean criticism can hurt feelings instead of helping. Criticism is not praise.

[user]what is feedback?
[assistant]Feedback is information about how something went and what could help next time. Teachers, parents, coaches, and friends give feedback. Feedback can include both praise and criticism. Feedback is not a grade.

[user]what does well done mean?
[assistant]Well done means something was done in a strong or careful way. People say these words after a success, a good try, or a finished job. The phrase is a kind of praise. Well done is not a correction.

[user]what does good job mean?
[assistant]Good job means the speaker is pleased with what someone did. Adults often say this after effort, learning, or helpful behavior. The words can encourage a person to keep going. Good job is not feedback about what to change.

[user]what does you can do better mean?
[assistant]You can do better means more improvement is possible. A teacher or parent may say this when effort or care needs to grow. The words point to a higher goal. You can do better is not praise.

[user]what does I like how you mean?
[assistant]I like how you means the speaker is about to name a specific good action. People use this start to give clear praise, such as I like how you shared. Specific praise helps a person know what worked well. I like how you is not a complaint.

[user]what does encourage mean?
[assistant]To encourage means to give support that helps someone keep trying. Encouragement can be words, gestures, or help. Encouragement often gives courage after a hard moment. To encourage is not to discourage.

[user]what does what could be different mean?
[assistant]What could be different is a question that asks how something could improve or change. Teachers and teammates may ask this during reflection. The question invites helpful feedback instead of blame. What could be different is not praise.

[user]what is a correction?
[assistant]A correction is a change made to fix a mistake. A teacher may make a correction on a paper or tell a student what the right answer is. A correction is meant to help by showing what was wrong and what is better. A correction is useful even when it feels a little uncomfortable to hear. A correction is not the same as praise.
