[user]what is group work?
[Ninereeds]Group work is when students work together on a task or project. Each person in the group may have a part to do. Group work teaches cooperation, listening, and sharing ideas. It can be harder than working alone because people must agree. Good group work means everyone participates and respects each other.

[user]what if one person in the group is not helping?
[Ninereeds]If one person in the group is not helping, the others can politely ask them to join in. They might say "We need your help with this part." If the person still does not help, the group can talk to the teacher. It is not fair for some people to do all the work while others do nothing.

[user]what if group members disagree?
[Ninereeds]If group members disagree, they should listen to each other's ideas first. Then they can try to find a solution everyone can accept. Voting is one way to decide. Compromise is another. If the disagreement is too big, the group can ask the teacher for help. Disagreement is normal in groups, and working through it is part of learning.

[user]how can a student be a good partner?
[Ninereeds]A good partner listens, shares ideas, and does their part of the work. They do not take over everything or leave everything to the other person. They speak kindly and accept that the other person may have different ideas. Being a good partner means working together, not working alone next to someone else.
