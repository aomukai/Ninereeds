[user]what does it mean to transition in school?
[Ninereeds]A transition in school is moving from one activity or place to another. It might be going from reading to math, or from the classroom to the gym. Transitions happen many times during the school day. Following the teacher's signals and staying calm during transitions helps the day go smoothly.

[user]how can a student handle transitions well?
[Ninereeds]A student can handle transitions well by listening for the teacher's signal, putting away materials neatly, and moving calmly to the next activity. Rushing or talking loudly makes transitions harder for everyone. Knowing what comes next helps. Some classrooms have visual schedules that show the order of the day.

[user]what if a student does not want to stop what they are doing?
[Ninereeds]Sometimes a student does not want to stop an activity they enjoy. This is normal. But the class schedule means everyone moves together. The student can finish later if possible, or do the activity at home. Learning to stop and switch is part of school. The next activity might be good too, even if the current one is hard to leave.
