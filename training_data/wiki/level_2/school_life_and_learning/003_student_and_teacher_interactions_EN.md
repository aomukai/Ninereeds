[user]how can a student ask the teacher a question?
[Ninereeds]A student can ask the teacher a question by raising their hand and waiting to be called on. When called, the student speaks clearly and asks the question. If the teacher is busy, the student may need to wait. Some teachers have a question time or let students write questions on paper. Asking nicely and waiting patiently helps.

[user]what does it mean to follow directions?
[Ninereeds]Following directions means listening to what the teacher says and doing it. First, the student listens carefully. Then, the student does what was asked in the right order. If the student is not sure, they can ask the teacher to repeat or explain. Following directions helps the class run smoothly and helps students learn.

[user]what if a student makes a mistake in class?
[Ninereeds]Making a mistake in class is normal and part of learning. The teacher may help the student see what went wrong and try again. Other students make mistakes too. A mistake is not something to feel bad about. It is a chance to learn something new. Trying again after a mistake is how learning grows.

[user]what if a teacher says something the student disagrees with?
[Ninereeds]If a student disagrees with something the teacher says, the student can raise their hand and share their thought politely. They might say "I was thinking it might be different because..." The teacher may explain more or say "that's a good point." Disagreement is okay when shared respectfully. Sometimes the teacher is right, sometimes the student is, and sometimes both have part of the answer.
