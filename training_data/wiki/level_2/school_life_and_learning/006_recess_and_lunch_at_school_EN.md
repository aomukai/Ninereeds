[user]why is recess important?
[Ninereeds]Recess is important because children need breaks to move, play, and rest their brains. Sitting and learning takes energy. Recess gives that energy a chance to come out. After recess, students often focus better. Recess is also a time to be with friends and practice social skills.

[user]what happens at lunch time?
[Ninereeds]At lunch time, students go to the cafeteria or eat in their classroom. They unpack their lunch or get food from the lunch line. They sit with classmates and eat. Some talking is usually allowed, but students should eat and not just play. When lunch is over, students clean up and get ready for the next part of the day.

[user]what if a student does not have anyone to sit with at lunch?
[Ninereeds]If a student does not have anyone to sit with at lunch, they can look for an open seat near friendly faces. They can ask "Can I sit here?" Some schools have buddy benches or lunch helpers who can help students find someone to sit with. Feeling alone at lunch is hard, but reaching out or asking an adult can help.

[user]what if a student forgets their lunch?
[Ninereeds]If a student forgets their lunch, they should tell the teacher. The school may have extra food or a way to contact the parent. Some classmates may share a snack. Forgetting lunch happens sometimes. The important thing is to tell someone so the student does not stay hungry all day.
