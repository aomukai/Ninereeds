# Data, Charts, and Graphs Entries

[user]what is data?
[assistant]Data is information people collect and look at. Data can come from counting, measuring, or asking questions. Children can collect data about favorite fruit, shoe color, or weather. Data helps people notice patterns and compare results. Data is not a chart.

[user]what is a chart?
[assistant]A chart is a way to show information in an organized form. A chart can use boxes, lines, words, or pictures. People read a chart to understand data more easily. A chart helps people compare parts of the same set of information. A chart is not a story.

[user]what is a graph?
[assistant]A graph is a kind of chart that shows data in a visual way. A graph can use bars, lines, or pictures to show amounts. People read a graph to see more, less, most, or least. A graph makes groups easy to compare at a glance. A graph is not a tally.

[user]what is a tally?
[assistant]A tally is a quick way to keep count while something is happening. People make a tally when they count votes, cars, or answers. A tally grows one mark at a time as each new thing is counted. Tally marks help people keep data neat and easy to check. A tally is not a survey.

[user]what is a tally mark?
[assistant]A tally mark is one line used in a tally. People add one tally mark for each thing they count. The first four tally marks are straight lines, and the fifth line crosses them. Tally marks help people count without forgetting. A tally mark is not a graph.

[user]what is a survey?
[assistant]A survey is a way to collect data by asking people questions. A class may do a survey about pets, snacks, or favorite colors. Each answer becomes part of the data. Surveys help people learn what a group thinks or chooses. A survey is not a tally.

[user]what is a result?
[assistant]A result is what people find after counting, measuring, or checking something. A result can show which choice got the most votes or how many objects were counted. People look at results after a survey, a tally, or a graph. Results help people talk about what happened. A result is not a survey.

[user]what does most mean?
[assistant]Most means the greatest amount in a group. If apples get more votes than pears or grapes, apples have the most votes. People use most when they compare data in a chart or graph. Most points to the biggest part. Most is not least.

[user]what does least mean?
[assistant]Least means the smallest amount in a group. If one snack gets fewer votes than the others, that snack has the least votes. People use least when they compare data in a chart or graph. Least points to the smallest part. Least is not most.

[user]what is a bar graph?
[assistant]A bar graph is a graph that uses bars to show amounts. Longer or taller bars show more, and shorter bars show less. People use bar graphs to compare groups clearly. A bar graph is easy to read when the bars are lined up. A bar graph is not a picture graph.

[user]what is a picture graph?
[assistant]A picture graph is a graph that uses pictures to show amounts. One picture may stand for one thing or for several things. People use picture graphs when they want data to be easy to see quickly. A picture graph can help children compare groups at a glance. A picture graph is not a bar graph.
