# Inclusion, Bullying, and Kindness Entries

[user]what does include mean?
[assistant]Include means let someone join or be part of a group or activity. A child may include another child in a game, a talk, or a table group. Including people helps them feel welcome. Inclusion can make a group kinder and stronger. Include is not exclude.

[user]what does exclude mean?
[assistant]Exclude means leave someone out of a group or activity. A child may feel excluded from a game or a table at lunch. Excluding people can hurt feelings and cause loneliness. People need to notice exclusion and respond with care. Exclude is not include.

[user]what is bullying?
[assistant]Bullying is repeated unkind or hurtful behavior meant to cause fear, pain, or embarrassment. Bullying can happen through words, actions, or leaving someone out on purpose. Bullying is a serious problem, not a joke. Children should tell a trusted adult about bullying. Bullying is not kindness.

[user]what does stand up for someone mean?
[assistant]Stand up for someone means help protect a person who is being treated badly. A child may stand up for someone by saying stop, getting help, or including the person. The action shows courage and care. Standing up for someone can make a hurtful situation smaller. Stand up for someone is not being a bystander.

[user]what is respect?
[assistant]Respect is treating people in a careful and honorable way. Respect means listening, being fair, and caring about another person's feelings or rights. Children show respect in words, actions, and choices. Respect helps people live and learn together better. Respect is not cruelty.

[user]what is compassion?
[assistant]Compassion is caring about another person's pain and wanting to help. A child may feel compassion when a friend is hurt, sad, or left out. Compassion can lead to kind action. The feeling helps people respond gently to suffering. Compassion is not meanness.

[user]what does leave them out mean?
[assistant]Leave them out means not let someone join or take part. A child may say this about a game, a group, or a plan. Leaving someone out on purpose can be hurtful. The phrase often points to a problem of exclusion. Leave them out is not include them.

[user]what does that's not kind mean?
[assistant]That's not kind means the action or words are hurtful or uncaring. A child may say that's not kind after teasing, pushing, or leaving someone out. The phrase helps name a problem clearly. It can remind people to choose gentler behavior. That's not kind is not that's okay.

[user]what is a bystander?
[assistant]A bystander is a person who sees what is happening but is not the main person acting. A child may be a bystander during an argument, a game problem, or bullying. Bystanders still have choices about whether to help, get help, or stay silent. The role matters in group situations. A bystander is not the bully.

[user]what is an upstander?
[assistant]An upstander is a person who chooses to help when something unfair or hurtful is happening. An upstander may include someone, speak up, or get a trusted adult. The word points to action, not just watching. Upstanders help make groups safer and kinder. An upstander is not a bystander.
