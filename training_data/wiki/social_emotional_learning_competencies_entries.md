# Social-Emotional Learning Competencies Entries

[user]what is self-management?
[assistant]Self-management is the skill of handling actions, feelings, and choices in a steady way. A person uses self-management to stay calm, follow a plan, or keep trying. Self-management helps in school, play, and daily life. Self-management is not acting without thinking.

[user]what is social awareness?
[assistant]Social awareness is noticing other people, other feelings, and what is happening around you. A socially aware person pays attention to words, faces, and situations. Social awareness helps people act with care and respect. Social awareness is not self-management.

[user]what is empathy?
[assistant]Empathy is understanding and caring about how another person feels. Empathy can help a person be gentle, helpful, and fair. People often show empathy by listening and responding kindly. Empathy is not the same as agreement.

[user]what is self-regulation?
[assistant]Self-regulation is controlling the body, feelings, and actions in a useful way. A person may pause, breathe, wait, or choose calmer words. Self-regulation helps with learning and relationships. Self-regulation is not losing control.

[user]what is responsible decision-making?
[assistant]Responsible decision-making means choosing in a careful and thoughtful way. A responsible choice looks at safety, fairness, and what may happen next. People use this skill when rules, feelings, and goals all matter. Responsible decision-making is not guessing.

[user]what is impulse control?
[assistant]Impulse control is stopping before doing the first thing that pops into the mind. Impulse control helps a person wait, think, and choose a better action. This skill matters when a person feels upset, excited, or tempted. Impulse control is not acting right away.

[user]what does how would you feel if mean?
[assistant]How would you feel if is a question that asks a person to imagine an emotion in a situation. Adults and children use this question to build empathy and reflection. The question helps a person think about effects on others. How would you feel if is not a command.

[user]what does that was a good choice mean?
[assistant]That was a good choice means an action was thoughtful, safe, or kind. People say this to notice good judgment and encourage more of it. The words point to a decision and its value. That was a good choice is not criticism.
