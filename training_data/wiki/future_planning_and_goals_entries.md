# Future Planning and Goals Entries

[user]what is a plan?
[assistant]A plan is an idea for what to do later and how to do it. A child may make a plan for homework, a game, or a trip. Plans help people think ahead instead of acting at random. A plan can have steps and a goal. A plan is not a future goal.

[user]what is a dream for the future?
[assistant]A dream for the future is a hope or wish about what life might become later. A child may dream about being a doctor, an artist, or a builder. Future dreams can guide learning and effort. A dream for the future is about later life, not sleep. A dream for the future is not a plan.

[user]what does when I'm older mean?
[assistant]When I'm older means at a later time in life after more growing. A child may say when I'm older, I will travel, work, or learn something new. The phrase points to the future self. It often appears in hopes and long plans. When I'm older is not right now.

[user]what does want to be mean?
[assistant]Want to be means hope to grow into a certain role or kind of person later. A child may want to be a teacher, a pilot, or a scientist. The phrase often shows a future goal. It connects identity to hope and learning. Want to be is not already am.

[user]what does someday I will mean?
[assistant]Someday I will means the speaker hopes or plans something for a later time. A child may say someday I will build a big house or help animals. The phrase is broad and future-facing. It gives room for a long path before the result. Someday I will is not today I did.

[user]what is a future goal?
[assistant]A future goal is something a person wants to reach later. A child may have a future goal to learn a skill, finish school, or do a certain kind of work one day. Goals help give direction to practice and choices. A future goal can be near or far away. A future goal is not a dream for the future.

[user]what does work toward mean?
[assistant]Work toward means take steps to move closer to a goal. A child may work toward reading better, playing an instrument, or joining a team. The phrase shows that goals often need time and effort. Working toward something may include practice and patience. Work toward is not ignore.

[user]what does prepare for mean?
[assistant]Prepare for means get ready ahead of time for something that will happen later. A child may prepare for a test, a trip, or a new grade. Preparation can include learning, packing, or practicing. Preparing helps a future event go better. Prepare for is not forget about.

[user]what does hope to mean?
[assistant]Hope to means want something good to happen in the future. A child may hope to visit a place, make a team, or learn something hard. The phrase shows a future wish that may need effort or luck. Hope to can be gentle and personal. Hope to is not prepare for.
