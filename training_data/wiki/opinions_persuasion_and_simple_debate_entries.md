# Opinions, Persuasion, and Simple Debate Entries

[user]what is an opinion?
[assistant]An opinion is what a person thinks or feels about something. Opinions can be different from person to person. People share opinions when they talk about favorites, choices, and ideas. An opinion is not the same as a fact.

[user]what does I think mean?
[assistant]I think is a way to tell your own idea or opinion. A person may say I think blue is the best color or I think this game is fun. I think shows that the speaker is giving a view, not a proven fact. I think is not the same as I know for sure.

[user]what does in my opinion mean?
[assistant]In my opinion is a phrase people use before sharing what they think. It helps show that the speaker is giving a personal view. People use the phrase when talking politely about choices or reasons. In my opinion is not the same as a fact.

[user]what does it mean to agree?
[assistant]To agree means to think the same thing as another person about an idea or choice. People agree when they say yes, that is right, or I think so too. Agreeing helps people work together or move on with a plan. To agree is the opposite of to disagree.

[user]what does it mean to disagree?
[assistant]To disagree means to think something different from another person. People disagree about games, rules, choices, or ideas. Disagreeing does not always mean being mean or fighting. To disagree is the opposite of to agree.

[user]what is persuasion?
[assistant]Persuasion is trying to help another person change a choice, action, or opinion by using words and reasons. A child may use persuasion to explain why one game is a better choice than another. Persuasion often uses reasons, examples, and calm speech. Persuasion is not the same as force.

[user]what does it mean to convince?
[assistant]To convince means to help another person believe something or choose something after hearing reasons. A person may be convinced by a clear explanation or a strong example. Convincing happens through words and ideas, not by grabbing or pushing. To convince is not the same as to force.

[user]what is a reason in an argument?
[assistant]A reason in an argument is something that explains why a person thinks an idea is good, true, or best. People use reasons when they want others to understand their opinion. Reasons help make opinions stronger and clearer. A reason is not the same as a guess.

[user]what is evidence?
[assistant]Evidence is information or an example that helps support an idea or claim. Evidence may come from seeing, testing, reading, or remembering something real. Evidence helps people explain why they think something is true. Evidence is not the same as a wish.

[user]what is a debate?
[assistant]A debate is a talk where people share different opinions and give reasons for them. In a debate, people listen, answer, and try to explain their side clearly. A debate can happen in class, at home, or during a group choice. A debate is not the same as a fight.

[user]what is a point of view?
[assistant]A point of view is the way a person sees or thinks about something based on their own ideas and experiences. Two people can look at the same thing and have different points of view. Sharing a point of view is one part of a debate or discussion. A point of view is not the same as a fact.

[user]what does it mean to see both sides?
[assistant]To see both sides means to think about the reasons for and against an idea. A person who sees both sides does not only think about their own view. Seeing both sides helps people understand a debate more fully. To see both sides is not the same as agreeing with everything.

[user]what does it mean to change your mind?
[assistant]To change your mind means to decide on something different from what you thought before. A person may change their mind after hearing a good reason or new information. Changing your mind after listening is a sign of careful thinking. To change your mind is not the same as giving up.

[user]what does it mean to support an idea?
[assistant]To support an idea means to give reasons or examples that help show why the idea is good or true. People support ideas in a debate by using facts, stories, or evidence. Supporting an idea helps others understand your point of view. To support an idea is not the same as proving it.
