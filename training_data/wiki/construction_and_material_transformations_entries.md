# Construction and Material Transformations Entries

[user]what is construction?
[assistant]Construction is the work of making or putting something together. People do construction when they make a wall, a house, or another structure. Construction often uses tools, parts, and careful steps. Construction is not the same as a repair.

[user]what is a repair?
[assistant]A repair is work that fixes something broken or damaged. A repair may help a toy, a chair, or a tool work again. Repairs help people use things instead of throwing them away. A repair is not the same as construction.

[user]what does it mean to glue something?
[assistant]To glue something means to join two things with glue so they stay together. People may glue paper, wood, or other light pieces in art or building work. Gluing helps parts stick in one place. To glue something is not the same as to tear it.

[user]what does it mean to fold something?
[assistant]To fold something means to bend it over so one part lies on another part. People may fold paper, cloth, or cardboard. Folding changes the shape without cutting the thing apart. To fold something is not the same as to flatten it.

[user]what does it mean to tear something?
[assistant]To tear something means to pull it apart into pieces. People may tear paper, cloth, or another soft material. Tearing changes one piece into smaller pieces. To tear something is not the same as to glue it.

[user]what does it mean to flatten something?
[assistant]To flatten something means to press it so it becomes flatter and wider. People may flatten dough, clay, or a soft piece of material. Flattening changes the shape by pushing it down. To flatten something is not the same as to fold it.

[user]what does it mean to mold something?
[assistant]To mold something means to shape a soft material into a new form. People may mold clay, dough, or playdough with hands or a mold. Molding lets a person turn one lump into a planned shape. To mold something is not the same as to crush it.

[user]what does it mean to shred something?
[assistant]To shred something means to turn it into many thin or small pieces. People may shred paper, cheese, or another material that can be cut apart. Shredding changes one larger piece into many smaller pieces. To shred something is not the same as to keep it whole.

[user]what does it mean to crush something?
[assistant]To crush something means to press it very hard so it breaks, bends, or loses its shape. People may crush a can, a leaf, or dry crumbs into smaller bits. Crushing changes a thing by strong pressure. To crush something is not the same as to repair it.

[user]what does it mean to assemble something?
[assistant]To assemble something means to put all its parts together in the right order. People assemble a toy, a piece of furniture, or a model from separate pieces. Assembling turns many parts into one finished thing. To assemble something is not the same as to take it apart.

[user]what does it mean to wrap something?
[assistant]To wrap something means to cover it with paper, cloth, or another material. People wrap a gift, a sandwich, or an object to protect or cover it. Wrapping keeps what is inside safe or hidden. To wrap something is not the same as to open it.

[user]what does it mean to stack?
[assistant]To stack means to put things one on top of another in a neat pile. Children stack blocks, books, or cups to build a tower. Stacking takes care and balance to keep the pile standing. To stack is not the same as to scatter.

[user]what does it mean to tie?
[assistant]To tie means to fasten two ends of string, rope, or cloth together by knotting. People tie shoes, bags, and bundles. Tying holds things in place or keeps them together. To tie is not the same as to untie.
