[user]why do people say "I thought" and how does it help us understand them?
[Ninereeds]People use the phrase "I thought" to explain what they believed was true in the past. This is very helpful when they were wrong. It is a way of reporting what was in their mind. They want to show their reason for an action. If you arrive at a store and find it closed, then you might speak. You might say, "I thought the store was open today." You are explaining your past belief so that others understand why you walked all the way there. If you did not say what you thought, then people might wonder why you made the trip.

Using "I thought" is also helpful for sharing surprises and changes in your knowledge. You might say, "I thought there was no dessert, but there is cake!" This shows that your expectation was different from the reality. Because of this, you are feeling surprised and happy. It helps other people see how your mind has updated itself. You can compare "I thought" to a label on an old map. The map shows where you used to think the path was. But now you have a new map that is more accurate. By sharing the old map, you show others the journey your mind has taken.

Using "I thought" is different from just saying "I was wrong." If you only say you were wrong, then people do not know why you made the mistake. But if you say what you thought, then you show the reason for your action. This helps others trust you because they see your logic. It proves that you had a plan, even if the plan did not work. Because we share our past thoughts, we can help each other understand how to think more clearly next time. This creates a safe space for everyone to learn and grow together. Because we cannot see into each other's minds, we must use words to explain our internal states.
