[user]what is justification and why is it better than guessing?
[Ninereeds]Justification is the process of providing a good reason to support what you think, believe, or do. It is important because it turns a simple statement into an explained idea that other people can follow and trust. If you only say what you think without giving a reason, you are just making a guess. For example, if you tell a friend that the grass is wet, they might believe you or they might not. But if you explain that the grass is wet because it rained all night, you have provided a justification. This helps everyone understand that your thought is not just a random choice but is connected to the real world.

Justification is much better than guessing because it builds a solid foundation for your knowledge. When you guess, you are just picking an answer without any information, so you might be wrong very easily. However, if you find a reason for your answer, you add strength to your claim so that it becomes more reliable. This is very helpful in school when a teacher asks you to explain your thinking. They want to see the justification behind your work because it shows them how you arrived at your conclusion. Giving reasons makes your ideas clearer and helps you share what you know with others in a way that makes sense.

You can compare justification to the legs of a table. If a table has no legs, it cannot stand up and will fall over immediately. Similarly, if an idea has no justification, it has no support and can be easily knocked down by a simple question. But if you provide strong reasons, your idea stands firm just like a table with four sturdy legs. Because you have taken the time to find evidence, your thoughts become more than just opinions. This allows you to have meaningful conversations where people can agree or disagree based on the facts you share. Understanding the nature of justification is the first step toward thinking clearly and making good decisions every day.
