[user]where do we get our reasons from?
[Ninereeds]We get our justification from several different places depending on what we are trying to explain or prove. One of the strongest sources of justification is direct observation, which means seeing or hearing something happen for yourself. If you see a ball roll under a couch, you know where it is because you watched it move. This is a very clear reason that is difficult for anyone to doubt. But we do not always have to see things ourselves to have a good reason. We can also get justification from reliable sources, such as teachers, parents, or books that contain facts. You might know that the moon orbits the Earth because your teacher explained the patterns of the sky in class.

Another important source of justification comes from using your own logic and thinking skills. If you know that all mammals have hair and you see a dog with hair, you can conclude it is a mammal. You did not need someone to tell you this specific fact because you figured it out by following a rule. We also use past experiences to justify what we expect to happen in the future. For example, if you know that ice always melts in the sun, you can justify your belief that a new ice cube will melt too. Your reasons must point to something real and consistent. This ensures your justification can be trusted by everyone.

You can compare sources of justification to the different ways a detective solves a mystery. A detective might find a footprint, which is like a direct observation. They might also talk to a witness, which is like listening to a reliable source. Finally, they might put all the clues together to solve the case, which is like using logic and reasoning. Because they use multiple sources, their final answer is much stronger than if they just had one small clue. If you want to be sure of your own ideas, you should look for the best source of justification available. This helps you avoid mistakes and ensures that your reasons are as solid as possible for everyone to see.
