# Garden and Planting Basics Entries

[user]what is gardening?
[assistant]Gardening is growing and caring for plants on purpose. People garden in yards, pots, garden beds, or school spaces. Gardening can include digging, planting, watering, and pulling weeds. Gardens can grow flowers, herbs, fruits, or vegetables. Gardening is not a jungle.

[user]what is soil?
[assistant]Soil is the loose ground where many plants grow. Soil holds roots, water, air, and tiny living things. Good soil helps plants stand up and take in what they need. Soil can be dark, sandy, rich, or dry. Soil is not sand.

[user]what is compost?
[assistant]Compost is old plant food scraps and other natural material that break down into rich dark plant food. People add compost to soil to help plants grow better. Compost can be made from leaves, fruit peels, and other plant waste. Compost helps feed the soil in a natural way. Compost is not plastic.

[user]what is a pot?
[assistant]A pot is a container used for growing a plant. A pot holds soil, roots, and water in one place. People use pots indoors, outdoors, and on windowsills. Pots can be made of clay, plastic, or other materials. A pot is not a garden bed.

[user]what is a garden bed?
[assistant]A garden bed is a prepared area of soil where plants grow together. People may grow flowers or vegetables in a garden bed. A garden bed helps keep plants in one planned space. Paths often go around a garden bed so people can reach the plants. A garden bed is not a pot.

[user]what does plant a seed mean?
[assistant]Plant a seed means put a seed into soil so it can start growing. People plant a seed at the right depth and then cover it lightly. Seeds need water, air, and warmth after planting. Planting a seed is often the first step in growing a new plant. Plant a seed is not pull a weed.

[user]what is a root?
[assistant]A root is the part of a plant that grows under the ground. Roots hold the plant in place and take in water from the soil. Some roots are thin and some are thick. Healthy roots help the whole plant grow well. A root is not a leaf.

[user]what is a sprout?
[assistant]A sprout is a very young plant just starting to grow. A sprout comes up after a seed begins to grow. Sprouts are small, soft, and easy to damage. People often watch for a sprout as a sign that planting worked. A sprout is not a flower.

[user]what does weed mean?
[assistant]A weed is a plant growing where a person does not want it to grow. Weeds can take water, light, and space from garden plants. People often pull weeds out to help wanted plants grow better. A weed is not always ugly, but it is in the wrong place for that garden. A weed is not a crop plant.

[user]what does give it water and sun mean?
[assistant]Give it water and sun means provide a plant with two things many plants need to grow. Water helps move through the plant, and sunlight helps the plant make food. Gardeners watch to make sure a plant gets enough of both. Too little water or too little sun can slow growth. Give it water and sun is not pull a weed.
