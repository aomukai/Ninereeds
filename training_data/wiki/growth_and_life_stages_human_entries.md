# Growth and Life Stages (Human) Entries

[user]what is a life stage?
[assistant]A life stage is one part of a person's growth over time. People change from one life stage to another as they get older. Life stages help people talk about age and development. A life stage is not the same as one single day.

[user]what is a baby?
[assistant]A baby is a very young child at the start of life. Babies need a lot of care, food, rest, and protection from adults. Babies are smaller and cannot do all the things older children can do. A baby is not an adult.

[user]what is a toddler?
[assistant]A toddler is a very young child who is older than a baby and is learning to walk, talk, and do more things alone. Toddlers are small, active, and still need a lot of help. A toddler is in an early life stage of childhood. A toddler is not a baby.

[user]what is a child?
[assistant]A child is a young person who is still growing. Children learn, play, ask questions, and gain new skills over time. A child is younger than a grown-up. A child is not an adult.

[user]what is a teenager?
[assistant]A teenager is a young person who is older than a child but not yet an adult. Teenagers are still growing and learning, but they can often do more things on their own. Teenage years are one stage between childhood and adulthood. A teenager is not a young child.

[user]what is an adult?
[assistant]An adult is a fully grown person. Adults often care for children, work, make choices, and take on responsibilities. Adults were once babies and children too. An adult is not a child.

[user]what is a grown-up?
[assistant]A grown-up is another word for an adult. Grown-ups are older people who have finished childhood. Children often go to grown-ups for help, care, and guidance. A grown-up is not a baby.

[user]what does it mean to grow up?
[assistant]To grow up means to change over time from being very young to being older and more able to do things. Growing up includes body changes, learning, and gaining skills. Growing up happens step by step over many years. To grow up is not the same as staying the same.

[user]what does when I was little mean?
[assistant]When I was little is a phrase people use to talk about an earlier time in childhood. A person may use the phrase when remembering old toys, old habits, or old skills. The phrase helps connect the present self to the past self. When I was little is not the same as right now.
