# Animals Insects and Arthropods Entries

[user]what is an insect?
[assistant]An insect is a small animal with six legs. Many insects also have wings and feelers. Ants, bees, butterflies, beetles, and flies are insects. Insects live in many different places. An insect is not a spider.

[user]what is an arthropod?
[assistant]An arthropod is a small animal with a hard outer body and jointed legs. Insects and spiders are kinds of arthropods. Arthropods come in many shapes and sizes. Many arthropods are very small, but some are larger. An arthropod is not a worm.

[user]what is an ant?
[assistant]An ant is a small insect that lives in a group. Ants work together to find food and build homes. Many ants walk in long lines and use smell to find the way. Ants may live underground, in wood, or in mounds of soil. An ant is not a bee.

[user]what is a bee?
[assistant]A bee is a flying insect with a fuzzy body. Bees visit flowers and collect nectar. Many bees live together and make honey in a hive. A bee can sting when it feels danger. A bee is not an ant.

[user]what is a beetle?
[assistant]A beetle is an insect with a hard cover on its back. The hard cover protects the wings underneath. Beetles come in many sizes and colors. Some beetles eat plants, and some eat other small animals. A beetle is not a butterfly.

[user]what is a butterfly?
[assistant]A butterfly is an insect with large colorful wings. Butterflies fly from flower to flower and drink nectar. A butterfly starts life as a caterpillar before it grows wings. Butterflies fly in the day. A butterfly is not a moth.

[user]what is a cricket?
[assistant]A cricket is a small insect with strong back legs for jumping. Crickets make a chirping sound. Many crickets are active at night. Crickets eat plants and other small bits of food. A cricket is not a grasshopper.

[user]what is a dragonfly?
[assistant]A dragonfly is an insect with a long thin body and four wings. Dragonflies are fast fliers and can catch other insects in the air. Many dragonflies live near ponds, lakes, and rivers. Young dragonflies grow in water before they get wings. A dragonfly is not a butterfly.

[user]what is a firefly?
[assistant]A firefly is a small insect that can glow in the dark. Fireflies use the light in their body to signal other fireflies. People often see fireflies on warm nights. Fireflies live in grassy places, woods, and places near water. A firefly is not a moth.

[user]what is a grasshopper?
[assistant]A grasshopper is an insect with long back legs for jumping. Grasshoppers eat grass and leaves. Many grasshoppers are active in the day. They often rest on plants in fields and meadows. A grasshopper is not a cricket.

[user]what is a ladybug?
[assistant]A ladybug is a small round beetle with spots on its back. Many ladybugs are red or orange with black spots. Ladybugs eat tiny insects on plants. People often see ladybugs on leaves and flowers. A ladybug is not a spider.

[user]what is a moth?
[assistant]A moth is an insect with wings that often flies at night. Many moths have soft bodies and wide wings. Moths are often seen near lights after dark. A moth begins life as a caterpillar before it grows wings. A moth is not a butterfly.

[user]what is a spider?
[assistant]A spider is a small arthropod with eight legs. Spiders make silk and many spiders spin webs. Spiders catch insects and other small animals for food. People find spiders in houses, gardens, and woods. A spider is not an insect.

[user]what is a worm?
[assistant]A worm is a small soft animal with a long thin body and no legs. Worms live in soil and help break down dead leaves and food scraps. Earthworms move through soil and help make it better for plants. Worms breathe through their skin and need to stay moist. A worm is not an arthropod.
