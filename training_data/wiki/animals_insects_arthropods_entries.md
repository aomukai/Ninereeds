[user]what is an ant?
[assistant]An ant is a small insect that lives with many others in a large group called a colony. Ants are very strong for their size and can carry things much heavier than themselves. They walk in long lines and find their way by using smell. Ants build their homes underground, in wood, or in mounds of soil called anthills. An ant is not a bee, though both live in groups and work together.

[user]what is a bee?
[assistant]A bee is a flying insect with a fuzzy body and two pairs of wings. Bees visit flowers to collect a sweet liquid called nectar, which they bring back to the hive and turn into honey. They live together in large groups and each bee has a job to do. A bee can sting when it feels danger. A bee is not a wasp, which has a smooth body and does not make honey.

[user]what is a beetle?
[assistant]A beetle is an insect with a hard shell on its back that protects its wings when it is not flying. When a beetle takes off, it opens the shell and spreads the wings hidden underneath. Beetles come in many shapes and colors and are found in forests, fields, and gardens all over the world. Some beetles are helpful in gardens, and some can damage crops. A beetle is not a bug, though both are insects.

[user]what is a butterfly?
[assistant]A butterfly is an insect with large, colorful wings and a thin body. It starts life as a caterpillar, wraps itself in a small case, and comes out with wings. Butterflies fly from flower to flower, drinking nectar and helping plants make seeds. They fly during the day and rest with their wings held upright. A butterfly is not a moth, which usually flies at night and rests with its wings spread flat.

[user]what is a cricket?
[assistant]A cricket is a small insect with strong back legs made for jumping. Crickets make a chirping sound by rubbing their wings together. You can often hear them calling at night in fields and gardens. They eat plants, small insects, and bits of food they find on the ground. A cricket is not a grasshopper, which is larger and mostly active during the day.

[user]what is a dragonfly?
[assistant]A dragonfly is an insect with a long, thin body and four large wings that it holds straight out to the sides. Dragonflies are fast fliers and can move in any direction, even backwards. They live near ponds and rivers and catch other insects while flying. A young dragonfly grows up underwater before it gets its wings. A dragonfly is not a damselfly, which folds its wings back along its body when resting.

[user]what is a firefly?
[assistant]A firefly is a small insect that can glow in the dark. It makes a soft light in its belly and uses it to signal other fireflies at night. Fireflies are most often seen on warm summer evenings, floating slowly through the air above grass and trees. They live in forests and meadows, often near water. A firefly is not a glowworm, which glows but cannot fly.

[user]what is a grasshopper?
[assistant]A grasshopper is an insect with long, powerful back legs that it uses to jump high and far. It has large eyes and eats mainly grass and leaves. Grasshoppers are active during the day and are often found sitting on plants in fields and meadows. When they fly, they sometimes make a snapping or crackling sound. A grasshopper is not a cricket, which is smaller and mostly active at night.

[user]what is an insect?
[assistant]An insect is a small animal with six legs and a body divided into three parts: a head, a middle, and a tail end. Most insects have two pairs of wings and two feelers on their heads that they use to smell and touch the world. Ants, bees, butterflies, beetles, and flies are all insects. Insects are the most common animals on Earth and live in almost every place. An insect is not a spider, which has eight legs and only two body parts.

[user]what is a ladybug?
[assistant]A ladybug is a small, round beetle with a bright red or orange back covered in black spots. It is a helpful insect because it eats tiny bugs that damage plants in gardens and fields. Ladybugs are found on flowers, leaves, and in grass across most of the world. In winter, they find a warm spot and sleep until spring. A ladybug is not a spider, though both are often found on plants and flowers.

[user]what is a moth?
[assistant]A moth is an insect that looks like a butterfly but usually flies at night. Most moths have thick, fuzzy bodies and wide wings that they spread flat when they rest. They are drawn to light and are often seen flying around lamps and windows after dark. Like a butterfly, a moth begins life as a caterpillar before growing its wings. A moth is not a butterfly, which flies during the day and rests with its wings held upright.

[user]what is a spider?
[assistant]A spider is a small animal with eight legs and a body made of two round parts. Spiders spin silk to build webs, which they use to catch insects for food. A spider bites its prey with small fangs to stop it from moving. Spiders are found in houses, forests, and gardens all over the world. A spider is not an insect, because insects have six legs and three body parts.
