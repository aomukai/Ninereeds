# Online Safety and Privacy Entries

[user]what does private mean?
[assistant]Private means meant only for certain people, not for everyone. A private message, picture, or fact should not be shared widely. Privacy helps protect people and personal information. Children need to know when something should stay private. Private is not public.

[user]what does public mean?
[assistant]Public means open for many people to see or know. A public post or video may be seen by many strangers. Public things are not kept for just one small group. People need to be careful with what they make public. Public is not private.

[user]what is a password?
[assistant]A password is a secret word or code used to protect an account or device. Passwords help keep other people out of private spaces online. Good passwords should be hard for others to guess. Children should keep passwords safe with trusted adults. A password is not a username.

[user]what is personal information?
[assistant]Personal information is private information about a person. A name, address, phone number, school, or password can be personal information. Children should be careful about sharing personal information online. Personal information can affect safety and privacy. Personal information is not public information.

[user]what is a stranger online?
[assistant]A stranger online is a person on the internet whom a child does not truly know in real life. Someone may seem friendly online and still be a stranger. Children should be careful with strangers online and tell a trusted adult if something feels wrong. Online strangers should not get personal information. A stranger online is not a trusted adult.

[user]what does report mean online?
[assistant]Report means tell a platform, app, or trusted adult about something unsafe or wrong online. A child may report mean messages, strange behavior, or unsafe pictures. Reporting helps adults or systems notice the problem. The action can help protect more than one person. Report is not block.

[user]what does block mean online?
[assistant]Block means stop a person or account from reaching you online. Blocking can stop messages, comments, or contact from someone unwanted. People block when they need more safety or peace online. Blocking is one tool for digital boundaries. Block is not invite.

[user]what does don't share that mean?
[assistant]Don't share that means the information, picture, or message should not be sent to others. A trusted adult may say this about private facts or unsafe pictures. The phrase warns that sharing could cause harm or remove privacy. Children should listen carefully when something is not safe to share. Don't share that is not post it.

[user]what is screen time?
[assistant]Screen time is time spent looking at or using screens such as phones, tablets, computers, or TVs. Screen time can be used for learning, games, videos, or talking to people. Too much screen time can make it harder to rest, move, or do other things. Families often make rules about screen time. Screen time is not outside time.

[user]what does ask a grown-up mean?
[assistant]Ask a grown-up means get help from a trusted adult before doing something. A child may ask a grown-up before sharing information, opening a link, or answering a strange message. The phrase points to safety and caution. Getting adult help is important online. Ask a grown-up is not stranger online.
