# Cooking and Food Preparation Entries

[user]what is cooking?
[assistant]Cooking is making food ready to eat by using heat, tools, or careful steps. People cook meals, soups, bread, and many other foods. Cooking often happens in a kitchen with help from kitchenware and ingredients. Cooking is not the same as eating.

[user]what is food preparation?
[assistant]Food preparation means getting food ready before or during cooking. Food preparation may include washing, cutting, mixing, or measuring. Food preparation helps people make meals in a safe and orderly way. Food preparation is not the same as eating.

[user]what is a recipe?
[assistant]A recipe is a set of steps that tells how to make a food or drink. A recipe often lists ingredients and tells what to do first, next, and last. A recipe helps people make the same dish in the right order. A recipe is not the same as a story.

[user]what is an ingredient?
[assistant]An ingredient is one food item that goes into a recipe. Flour, milk, eggs, salt, and sugar can all be ingredients. Ingredients are the parts people put together to make a dish. An ingredient is not the same as a recipe.

[user]what does it mean to chop food?
[assistant]To chop food means to cut it into smaller pieces. People may chop carrots, onions, or other food before cooking. Chopping helps make food easier to cook or eat. To chop food is not the same as to peel food.

[user]what does it mean to peel food?
[assistant]To peel food means to take the skin or outer layer off. People may peel an apple, a potato, or a banana. Peeling can help make food ready to cut, cook, or eat. To peel food is not the same as to chop food.

[user]what does it mean to stir food?
[assistant]To stir food means to move it around with a spoon or another tool. People stir soup, batter, or other food so it stays mixed and cooks evenly. Stirring helps stop food from sticking or staying in one place. To stir food is not the same as to pour.

[user]what does it mean to whisk?
[assistant]To whisk means to mix quickly with a whisk or another tool. People whisk eggs, cream, or batter to make it smooth or light. Whisking is a fast kind of mixing used in cooking and baking. To whisk is not the same as to stir food.

[user]what does it mean to bake?
[assistant]To bake means to cook food with dry heat, often in an oven. People bake bread, cookies, cakes, and other foods. Baking changes soft batter or dough into cooked food. To bake is not the same as to boil.

[user]what does it mean to simmer?
[assistant]To simmer means to cook a liquid gently with small bubbles. People simmer soup, sauce, or stew when they want slower cooking than a boil. Simmering helps flavors spread through the food. To simmer is not the same as to bake.

[user]what does it mean to season food?
[assistant]To season food means to add salt, herbs, spices, or other flavor to it. People season soup, rice, meat, or vegetables to change the taste. Seasoning helps food taste stronger or more balanced. To season food is not the same as eating.

[user]what does it mean to knead dough?
[assistant]To knead dough means to press and fold dough again and again with the hands. People knead dough when making bread or other baked food. Kneading changes the dough and helps it become smoother and stronger. To knead dough is not the same as to stir food.

[user]what does it mean to boil?
[assistant]To boil means to heat a liquid until it bubbles strongly and steam rises. People boil water, pasta, and vegetables in a pot on the stove. Boiling cooks food through or makes water safe to drink. To boil is not the same as to bake.

[user]what does it mean to pour?
[assistant]To pour means to let a liquid or loose material flow from one container into another. People pour juice into a glass, batter into a pan, or salt into a bowl. Pouring is a common action when cooking and preparing food. To pour is not the same as to stir.

[user]what does it mean to mix?
[assistant]To mix means to stir or combine different ingredients together until they are blended. People mix ingredients when making a cake, a salad, or a drink. Mixing helps things come together evenly. To mix is not the same as to separate.

[user]what does it mean to fry?
[assistant]To fry means to cook food in hot oil or butter in a pan. People fry eggs, potatoes, and other foods until they are cooked through. Frying is a fast way to cook some foods. To fry is not the same as to bake.

[user]what does it mean to spread?
[assistant]To spread means to move something soft across a surface to cover it. People spread butter on bread, jam on toast, or frosting on a cake. Spreading uses a knife or a flat tool to push the soft material out evenly. To spread is not the same as to pour.
