# Abstract Operators Entries

[user]what is a category?
[assistant]A category is a group of things that belong together in some way. People use categories to sort and compare things more easily. A category can be broad like animals or narrow like red toys. Categories help us see what things have in common. A category is not one single item.

[user]what is a feeling?
[assistant]A feeling is something a person or animal experiences inside the body or mind. Feelings can be calm or strong, pleasant or unpleasant. A feeling may show on the face, in the voice, or in the way a body moves. People talk about feelings to explain what is happening inside them. A feeling is not a thought.

[user]what is a material?
[assistant]A material is the stuff something is made from. Wood, metal, glass, cloth, and paper are all materials. Some materials are hard, and some are soft or bendable. People choose materials based on what they want to build or use. A material is not an object.

[user]what is a size?
[assistant]Size tells us how big or small something is. People compare size when they look at two things next to each other. Size helps us know if something will fit, fill a space, or be easy to carry. Words like tiny, small, big, and huge describe size. Size is not shape.
