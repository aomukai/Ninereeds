# Abstract Operators Entries (Batch 9)

[user]what is a bird?
[assistant]A bird is an animal with feathers and wings. Most birds can fly by flapping their wings. Birds have beaks instead of teeth and lay hard-shelled eggs. They come in many sizes, from tiny hummingbirds to big ostriches. Birds build nests to keep their eggs safe and warm. A bird is not a mammal, which gives live birth and has hair or fur.

[user]what is a category?
[assistant]A category is a group of things that are alike in some way. We put similar items together to make them easier to think about. Categories can be big like "animals" or small like "red toys". We use categories when we sort, compare or talk about groups of things. A category is not one single item, even though it contains items that belong to it.

[user]what is a color?
[assistant]Color is what we see when light hits our eyes. Different colors come from different types of light. We can describe colors by their name like red or blue. Colors can be bright or dull, light or dark. We see colors in nature, clothes, art and everything around us. Color is not shape, which is how something looks rather than what color it is.

[user]what is a feeling?
[assistant]A feeling is something we experience inside our body. Feelings include emotions like happy, sad, angry or scared. They also include body feelings like hungry, tired, pain or comfort. Feelings come from what happens inside us or around us. We show feelings through our face, body, voice and words. A feeling is not a thought, which is something we think with our mind.

[user]what is a fish?
[assistant]A fish is an animal that lives in water. It has gills to breathe underwater instead of lungs. Most fish have fins to help them swim and steer. Fish bodies are often covered with flat scales. They live in oceans, rivers, lakes and ponds everywhere. Fish eat tiny water plants, small animals or other fish. A fish is not a mammal, which breathes air and has live babies.

[user]what is a material?
[assistant]Material is the stuff that things are made from. Examples include wood, metal, plastic, glass, cloth and paper. Materials can be hard or soft, bendable or stiff. We pick materials based on what we need them to do. Materials can come from nature or be made by people. Material is not an object, which is a specific thing made from one or more materials.

[user]what is a number?
[assistant]A number tells us how many or how much of something we have. We use numbers to count, measure and label things. Numbers start with 1, 2, 3 and go on forever. We also use zero to mean none. We see numbers in math, clocks, prices and everyday life. A number is not a numeral, which is the written symbol like "5" or "五".

[user]what is a person?
[assistant]A person is a human being. Each person has their own body, mind and life story. People can think, feel and make choices about what to do. They grow from babies to adults over many years. People live in families, work together and form communities. A person is not an animal, which cannot think or talk like humans do.

[user]what is a shape?
[assistant]Shape is how something looks when we see its outline. Shapes can be flat like circles, squares and triangles. They can also be solid like spheres, cubes and pyramids. We notice shape by counting sides, corners or looking at symmetry. We see shapes in signs, buildings, leaves and many everyday things. Shape is not size, which is how big or small something is.

[user]what is a size?
[assistant]Size tells us how big or small something is. We describe size using words like tiny, small, big or huge. We can also measure size with rulers, scales or cups. Size helps us know if something will fit in a space. We compare size by looking at things next to each other. Size is not shape, which is how something looks rather than how big it is.

[user]what is a vehicle?
[assistant]A vehicle is something that moves people or things from place to place. Vehicles usually have wheels, tracks or other ways to move. They carry passengers or cargo in a special area. Vehicles include cars, bikes, buses, trains, planes and boats. They can be powered by gas, electricity, people or wind. A vehicle is not a building, which stays in one place and is not made to move.

[user]what is water?
[assistant]Water is a clear liquid that has no taste or smell. It is found in oceans, lakes, rivers and underground. Living things need water to stay alive and healthy. Water can be liquid, solid ice or gas steam. We use water for drinking, cleaning, cooking and many other needs. Water is not air, which is the invisible gas we breathe.

[user]what is weather?
[assistant]Weather is what the air outside is like right now. It includes how hot or cold, wet or dry, and windy it is. Weather changes from minute to minute and day to day. It is caused by the sun heating the Earth in different ways. We feel weather on our skin and measure it with tools. Weather is not climate, which is the usual weather pattern over many years.
