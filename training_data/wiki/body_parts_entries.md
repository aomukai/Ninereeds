# Body Parts Entries

[user]what is an arm?
[assistant]An arm is the part of the body that hangs from the shoulder and ends at the hand. You use your arms to reach, lift, carry, and hold things. Each arm has an upper part, an elbow in the middle, and a lower part called the forearm. People have two arms, one on each side of their body. An arm is not a leg, which grows from the hip and is used for walking.

[user]what is a backbone?
[assistant]The backbone is the long row of small bones that runs down the middle of your back. It holds your body upright and lets you bend and turn. The backbone also protects the soft cord inside it that carries signals between your brain and the rest of your body. Without a backbone, you could not stand or sit up straight. A backbone is not a rib, which is a curved bone that wraps around the front of your chest.

[user]what is a belly?
[assistant]The belly is the soft, round part of the body between the chest and the hips. It is where your stomach and the organs that digest food are kept. When you eat, your belly fills up and feels full. When you are very hungry, your belly might make a rumbling sound. A belly is not a chest, which is the upper part of the body where the heart and lungs are.

[user]what is a body part?
[assistant]A body part is any named piece of a person's or animal's body that has its own job to do. The head, the arms, the legs, the hands, and the feet are all body parts. Each body part works together with the others so the whole body can move, feel, and stay alive. Learning the names of body parts helps us describe where something hurts or where something is. A body part is not the whole body, which is all the parts together.

[user]what is a collarbone?
[assistant]The collarbone is a thin, hard bone that runs along the top of the chest, just below the neck. It connects the shoulder to the rest of the body and helps the arm move freely. You can feel your collarbone by pressing gently just below your throat on either side. The collarbone is one of the bones that breaks most easily when someone falls. A collarbone is not a shoulder blade, which is the flat bone on the back of the shoulder.

[user]what is an ear?
[assistant]An ear is the part of the head used for hearing. Sound travels into the ear and is sent as a signal to the brain, which tells you what you are hearing. You have two ears, one on each side of your head. Ears also help you keep your balance when you walk or run. An ear is not an earlobe, which is just the small, soft piece of skin at the bottom of the ear.

[user]what is an earlobe?
[assistant]The earlobe is the small, soft piece of skin that hangs at the bottom of the ear. It is the softest part of the ear because it has no hard cartilage inside it. Many people pierce their earlobes to wear earrings. The earlobe plays no part in hearing. An earlobe is not the ear itself, which is the whole part of the head used for hearing.

[user]what is an eye?
[assistant]An eye is the part of the face used for seeing. Light enters through the eye and a signal is sent to the brain, which turns it into a picture of the world. You have two eyes that work together to help you judge how far away things are. Eyes can be brown, blue, green, or grey. An eye is not an eyelid, which is the thin piece of skin that closes over the eye to protect it.

[user]what is an eyelid?
[assistant]An eyelid is the thin piece of skin that covers the eye and can open and close. When you blink, your eyelids move very quickly to clean and protect the eye. When you sleep, your eyelids stay closed to keep the light out. Eyelids also spread a thin layer of tears across the eye to keep it moist. An eyelid is not an eyebrow, which is the strip of hair above the eye.

[user]what is a face?
[assistant]The face is the front part of the head. It holds the eyes, the nose, the mouth, and the ears. The face is how other people recognize who you are. The muscles of the face let you smile, frown, look surprised, or show that you are thinking. A face is not a head, which includes the back and the top as well as the face.

[user]what is a fingertip?
[assistant]A fingertip is the very end of a finger, past the last knuckle. The skin of a fingertip is very sensitive and can feel small differences in texture, temperature, and pressure. Fingertips have a pattern of tiny ridges on them that is different for every person in the world. We use our fingertips for careful tasks like picking up a small coin or buttoning a shirt. A fingertip is not a knuckle, which is one of the bumpy joints along the finger.

[user]what is a foot?
[assistant]A foot is the flat part at the bottom of the leg that touches the ground when you stand or walk. Feet carry the weight of the whole body and push against the ground to move you forward. You have two feet, one at the end of each leg. The bottom of the foot has a soft pad and a small arch in the middle. A foot is not a hand, which is at the end of the arm and is used for gripping and holding.

[user]what is a forehead?
[assistant]The forehead is the flat area of the face above the eyes and below the hair. It is part of the front of the skull and protects the brain behind it. When you raise your eyebrows in surprise, you make lines on your forehead. Pressing your hand to someone's forehead is a way to check if they have a fever. A forehead is not a cheek, which is the softer, rounded area below the eye and beside the nose.

[user]what is a hand?
[assistant]A hand is the part of the body at the end of the arm. Each hand has four fingers and a thumb that can bend, grip, and feel. Hands are used for almost everything we do — eating, writing, building, touching, and waving. The inside of the hand is called the palm. A hand is not a foot, which is at the end of the leg and is used for walking.

[user]what is a head?
[assistant]The head is the round part of the body that sits on top of the neck. It holds the brain, which controls the whole body. The face is on the front of the head, and the ears are on the sides. The head can turn left and right and nod up and down. A head is not a face, which is only the front part of the head.

[user]what is a jawbone?
[assistant]The jawbone is the bone that forms the lower part of the mouth. It is the only bone in the face that moves, opening and closing so you can eat, speak, and yawn. You can feel your jawbone by pressing along the underside of your chin. The jawbone holds the lower teeth in place. A jawbone is not a tooth, which is the hard white piece inside the mouth used for biting and chewing.

[user]what is a leg?
[assistant]A leg is the part of the body that grows from the hip and ends at the foot. Legs support the weight of the body and are used for walking, running, jumping, and kicking. Each leg has an upper part called the thigh, a knee in the middle, and a lower part below the knee. People have two legs. A leg is not an arm, which grows from the shoulder and is used for reaching and holding.

[user]what is a mouth?
[assistant]The mouth is the opening in the face used for eating, drinking, and speaking. Inside the mouth are the teeth, the tongue, and the throat. Food enters the body through the mouth, where the teeth break it into small pieces. The mouth also shapes the sounds that come out when you speak or sing. A mouth is not a nose, which is used for breathing and smelling rather than eating and speaking.

[user]what is a nose?
[assistant]The nose is the part of the face used for breathing and smelling. Air goes in and out of the nose through two small openings called nostrils. The nose warms the air before it reaches the lungs and can detect many different smells. When you have a cold, the nose can become blocked and make it hard to breathe or smell. A nose is not a mouth, which is used for eating and speaking rather than breathing and smelling.

[user]what is a shinbone?
[assistant]The shinbone is the long, hard bone on the front of the lower leg, between the knee and the ankle. It is one of the main bones that holds you up when you stand. Because the shinbone is close to the skin with very little padding in front of it, it hurts a lot when you bump it. The shinbone runs from the knee all the way down to the ankle. A shinbone is not a kneecap, which is the small, round bone that covers the front of the knee.
