# Collections and Collecting Entries

[user]what is a collection?
[assistant]A collection is a group of similar things gathered and kept together. A child may have a collection of stickers, cards, rocks, or shells. Collections often grow one piece at a time. A collection can show a strong interest or hobby. A collection is not a duplicate.

[user]what is a sticker?
[assistant]A sticker is a small piece with a sticky back that can be placed on another surface. Children collect stickers in books, on papers, or on special pages. Stickers can show animals, letters, stars, or many other pictures. Some children trade stickers with friends. A sticker is not a card.

[user]what is a card?
[assistant]A card is a small flat piece used for games, collecting, or showing information. Children may collect picture cards, trading cards, or game cards. Cards often come in sets or series. A card can be kept in a box, binder, or album. A card is not a sticker.

[user]what is a series?
[assistant]A series is a group of things that belong together in order or by theme. A set of cards with matching style or numbers can be a series. People collect a series when they want all the related pieces. A series helps make a collection feel complete. A series is not one random item.

[user]what does organize mean?
[assistant]Organize means put things in order so they are easy to find and use. A child may organize cards by color, number, or type. Organizing helps a collection look neat and clear. Good organization makes missing parts easier to notice. Organize is not scatter.

[user]what does trade mean?
[assistant]Trade means give one thing and get another thing in return. Children may trade cards, stickers, or small objects with each other. A trade works best when both sides agree. Trading can help a collection grow in a new direction. Trade is not swap only in one direction.

[user]what is a duplicate?
[assistant]A duplicate is an extra copy of something already in a collection. A child may have a duplicate card or sticker. Duplicates are often traded, shared, or stored apart from the main set. A duplicate is the same kind of item as one already owned. A duplicate is not a missing piece.

[user]what does complete mean?
[assistant]Complete means nothing important is missing. A collection is complete when all the needed parts are there. Children may feel proud when a set or series is complete. The word can also describe one finished page or group inside a larger collection. Complete is not missing.

[user]what is an album?
[assistant]An album is a book or folder used to hold collected items. Children may keep stickers, cards, or pictures in an album. An album helps protect a collection and keep it in order. People can turn pages in an album to look through the items. An album is not a loose pile.

[user]what does swap mean?
[assistant]Swap means trade one thing for another. Children may swap cards, stickers, or small toys. A swap is often simple and direct between two people. Swapping can help both people get something they want. Swap is not collect.
