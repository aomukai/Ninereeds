# Learning, Memory, and Metacognition Entries

[user]what does learn mean?
[assistant]Learn means gain new knowledge or skill through experience, teaching, or practice. Children learn at school, at home, in play, and through mistakes. Learning can happen slowly or quickly. Learning changes what a person can understand or do. Learn is not forget.

[user]what does remember mean?
[assistant]Remember means bring something back into the mind. A child may remember a rule, a fact, or where a toy was placed. Remembering helps people use what they learned before. Memory supports learning over time. Remember is not forget.

[user]what does forget mean?
[assistant]Forget means not keep something in mind or not bring it back when needed. A child may forget homework, a name, or a step in a task. Forgetting happens to everyone sometimes. People often use reminders and practice to forget less. Forget is not remember.

[user]what does practice mean?
[assistant]Practice means do something again and again to get better at it. A child may practice reading, drawing, music, or a sport. Practice often helps skill grow over time. The work can feel hard at first and easier later. Practice is not give up.

[user]what does try again mean?
[assistant]Try again means make another attempt after a mistake or hard moment. A child may try again with a puzzle, a drawing, or a math problem. The phrase helps people keep going instead of stopping too soon. Another try can lead to learning. Try again is not all done.

[user]what does figure out mean?
[assistant]Figure out means come to understand something after thinking, looking, or trying. A child may figure out a pattern, a rule, or how to solve a problem. Figuring something out can take time and effort. The moment often feels satisfying. Figure out is not stay confused.

[user]what does I don't understand mean?
[assistant]I don't understand means the speaker does not see the meaning clearly yet. A child may say this during a lesson, a game, or directions. The phrase is honest and useful because it can lead to help. Saying it can be an important part of learning. I don't understand is not I know.

[user]what does I need to practice mean?
[assistant]I need to practice means the speaker knows a skill is not strong enough yet and needs more work. A child may say this about reading, music, sports, or writing. The phrase shows self-knowledge about learning. It often comes before more effort and repetition. I need to practice is not I already mastered it.

[user]what does I forgot mean?
[assistant]I forgot means the speaker did not keep something in mind or could not bring it back at that moment. A child may say I forgot my homework or I forgot the next step. The phrase often explains a problem without meaning harm. People may still need to fix what followed from forgetting. I forgot is not I remembered.
