# Evidence and Justification Entries

[user]what is justification?
[assistant]Justification is giving a good reason for a claim, choice, or action. A child may give justification for picking one answer or one game. Justification helps other people understand why something makes sense. Good justification uses clear support, not only strong feelings. Justification is not a guess.

[user]what is a reason why?
[assistant]A reason why is the explanation for something. A child may give a reason why a plant grew or a toy broke. Reasons help connect an answer to what happened in the world. A reason why makes an idea clearer. A reason why is not an example.

[user]what does because I saw it mean?
[assistant]Because I saw it means the speaker is giving direct seeing as support. A child may say because I saw it when explaining how something happened. This phrase points to something the eyes noticed. Seeing can be one kind of support for a claim. Because I saw it is not a guess.

[user]what does I know this because mean?
[assistant]I know this because is a phrase people use before giving support for an answer. The phrase tells the listener that a reason or example is coming next. Children use it in class when explaining thinking. It helps turn an answer into an explained answer. I know this because is not for instance.

[user]what is an example?
[assistant]An example is one case that helps show an idea clearly. A teacher may give an example to help children understand a rule or word. Examples make big ideas easier to see. One example does not show everything, but it can make the meaning clearer. An example is not justification.

[user]what does for instance mean?
[assistant]For instance is a phrase people use before giving an example. A child may say for instance, a seed needs water to grow. The phrase tells the listener that one clear case is coming. For instance helps connect a big idea to a small example. For instance is not that proves.

[user]what does that proves mean?
[assistant]That proves means something shows an idea is true in a strong way. A child may say that proves the ice melted when the water is seen on the table. The phrase points to a sign, example, or result that supports the claim. People use that proves when the support feels strong and clear. That proves is not a guess.

[user]what does I can show you mean?
[assistant]I can show you means the speaker can give support you can look at or check. A child may show a page, a mark, or the object itself. The phrase invites another person to see the reason directly. Showing can make an explanation stronger. I can show you is not a guess.

[user]what does back it up mean?
[assistant]Back it up means give support for what was said. A person may back it up with an example, a result, or something that was seen. Backing it up makes a claim stronger and clearer. Teachers may ask children to back up an answer in class. Back it up is not a guess.
