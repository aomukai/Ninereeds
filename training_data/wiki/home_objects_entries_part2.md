# Home Objects Entries - Part 2

[user]what is furniture?
[assistant]Furniture means things people can move and use in a room. Chairs, tables, beds, and shelves are all furniture. Furniture helps people sit, eat, sleep, work, and store things. Furniture can be made from wood, metal, plastic, or cloth. Furniture is not a wall fixture.

[user]what is a cup?
[assistant]A cup is a small container for holding drinks. A cup has an open top and usually a handle. People use cups for water, milk, tea, or other drinks. Cups can be plain or have colors or pictures on them. A cup is not a glass.

[user]what is a cupboard?
[assistant]A cupboard is a cabinet with shelves and doors. People keep dishes, food, or household things in a cupboard. Cupboards are often found in kitchens or dining rooms. A cupboard helps keep things clean and in order. A cupboard is not a closet.

[user]what is a desk?
[assistant]A desk is a piece of furniture with a flat surface for work. A desk may have drawers for paper, books, or supplies. People use desks for writing, homework, or computer work. A desk helps keep work in one place. A desk is not a table.

[user]what is a door?
[assistant]A door is a barrier that opens and closes so people can go in or out. A door can swing on hinges or slide in a track. Doors help with privacy, safety, and keeping out wind or cold. Most doors have handles, and some have locks. A door is not a window.
