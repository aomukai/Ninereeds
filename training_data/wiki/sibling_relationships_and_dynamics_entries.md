# Sibling Relationships and Dynamics Entries

[user]what is a sibling?
[assistant]A sibling is a brother or sister in the same family. Siblings may be older, younger, or the same age in the case of twins. Siblings often live together, play together, and argue sometimes. Sibling life can include closeness, sharing, and conflict. A sibling is not an only child.

[user]what is an older brother?
[assistant]An older brother is a brother who was born earlier than another child in the family. An older brother may be bigger, know more things already, or get some privileges first. Older brothers may help, tease, play, or argue. The role depends on family life and age. An older brother is not a younger brother.

[user]what is a younger sister?
[assistant]A younger sister is a sister who was born later than another child in the family. A younger sister may look up to older siblings or want to join what they are doing. Younger sisters may play, ask questions, and need help at times. The role is about family age order. A younger sister is not an older sister.

[user]what does bossing around mean?
[assistant]Bossing around means telling another person what to do in a pushy way. A sibling may feel bossed around by an older brother or sister. The behavior can cause arguments or hurt feelings. Bossing around is stronger than simple helping or suggesting. Bossing around is not sharing fairly.

[user]what does sharing a room mean?
[assistant]Sharing a room means two or more children sleep or keep things in the same room. Siblings who share a room may need to take turns, lower noise, and respect each other's space. Sharing a room can bring both closeness and arguments. Room sharing needs patience and clear rules. Sharing a room is not having a room alone.

[user]what is a tattletale?
[assistant]A tattletale is a person who tells on someone for small things in a way others may not like. Children may call a sibling a tattletale during an argument. The word is often used when one child thinks the telling was meant to get someone in trouble. Adults may still want important safety problems reported. A tattletale is not a sibling role.

[user]what does it's not fair mean?
[assistant]It's not fair means the speaker feels people are not being treated evenly. A child may say it's not fair when one sibling gets a bigger turn or a different rule. The phrase often appears in family arguments. It points to a problem about rules, turns, or treatment. It's not fair is not that's okay.

[user]what is an only child?
[assistant]An only child is a child with no brothers or sisters. An only child may play alone more often at home or spend more time with adults. Being an only child is one kind of family structure. An only child is not lonely by rule, because every family is different. An only child is not a sibling.

[user]what are twins?
[assistant]Twins are two children born at the same birth. Twins are the same age and grow through time together. Some twins look very alike, and some do not. People often notice twins because they share many life moments. Twins are not older and younger siblings.

[user]what does annoying mean?
[assistant]Annoying means making another person feel bothered again and again. A sibling may feel annoying when someone repeats a noise, grabs attention, or will not stop. The feeling is common in close family life. People often need calm words when something feels annoying. Annoying is not fair.
