[user]what happens when someone doesn't know something important?
[Ninereeds]When someone does not have all the information, they might act in a way that seems strange or silly to people who know the truth. This is called acting on missing information. If a bench has a "Wet Paint" sign, but Ben does not see the sign, then he might sit down and get paint on his pants. He didn't know the paint was wet, so he thought it was safe to sit. To Ben, his action was fine, but to someone else who saw the sign, it looked like a mistake. He was not being careless; he was just missing a piece of the world.

We can be more kind to others if we remember that they might not know what we know. If a friend spoils a surprise party by turning on the light, then your anger might go away because you realize they didn't know people were hiding. People can only make choices based on what is in their minds. If their information is incomplete, then their actions will follow that path. Understanding hidden knowledge helps us stay patient because it reminds us that everyone sees only part of the whole story.
