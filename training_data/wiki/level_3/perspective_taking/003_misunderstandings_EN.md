[user]what is a misunderstanding and how do we fix it?
[Ninereeds]A misunderstanding happens when someone gets the wrong idea about what another person said or did. This often occurs because words can have more than one meaning or because someone missed a small detail. If a father says he saw a bat, then he might mean the furry animal that flies at night. But if his daughter thinks he means a wooden bat for playing baseball, she has a misunderstanding. She has the wrong idea because the same word is used for two very different things, so she is confused about what her father saw.

Misunderstandings can also happen with plans and actions. If an invitation says a party starts at three, but a friend reads it too fast and thinks it says four, then they will arrive late. They did not mean to be late, but they misunderstood the time. If you realize there is a mix-up, then you can fix it by asking questions or explaining your thoughts again. You can say, "I thought you meant this," so the other person can help you understand the truth. Communication is the best tool for fixing these gaps because it allows two people to share their perspectives until they agree on the same idea.
