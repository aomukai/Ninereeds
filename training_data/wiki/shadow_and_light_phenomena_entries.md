# Shadow and Light Phenomena Entries

[user]what is a shadow?
[assistant]A shadow is a dark shape made when something blocks light. A shadow appears on the ground, a wall, or another surface. Shadows can be long or short, depending on where the light comes from. A person, tree, or toy can make a shadow. A shadow is not a reflection.

[user]what is a reflection?
[assistant]A reflection is an image made when light bounces off a surface. People often see reflections in a mirror, water, or shiny metal. A reflection can show a face, a lamp, or the sky. Reflections move when the object or the light changes. A reflection is not a shadow.

[user]what is a mirror?
[assistant]A mirror is a smooth surface that reflects light very well. People use mirrors to see faces, rooms, and other things. Many mirrors are made of glass with a special backing. A mirror makes reflections clear and easy to see. A mirror is not a reflection.

[user]what is a silhouette?
[assistant]A silhouette is a dark shape seen against a bright background. A person standing in front of a bright window may look like a silhouette. A silhouette shows the outer shape more than the inside details. Silhouettes often appear when the light is behind something. A silhouette is not a full reflection.

[user]what is a beam of light?
[assistant]A beam of light is a line or narrow path of light. Sunlight through a window or a flashlight in the dark can make a beam of light. A beam helps people see where the light is going. Dust in the air may make a beam easier to notice. A beam of light is not a shadow.

[user]what does shine mean?
[assistant]Shine means give off light or reflect light in a bright way. The sun can shine, and a polished spoon can shine too. Shining things catch the eye because they look bright. Shine can come from the object itself or from light bouncing off it. Shine is not glare.

[user]what does glow mean?
[assistant]Glow means give off a soft steady light. A night light, a firefly, or hot coals can glow. Glowing light is often gentler than a bright beam. Glow can make a place feel warm or calm. Glow is not glare.

[user]what is glare?
[assistant]Glare is very strong bright light that makes seeing hard. Sunlight in the eyes or light bouncing off glass can cause glare. Glare can make people squint or look away. Glare is bright in an uncomfortable way. Glare is not a glow.

[user]what does transparent mean?
[assistant]Transparent means light can pass through and people can see through it clearly. Clean glass and clear water can be transparent. Transparent things do not block much light. People use transparent materials for windows and some bottles. Transparent is not opaque.

[user]what does opaque mean?
[assistant]Opaque means light does not pass through, so people cannot see through it. Wood, stone, and many walls are opaque. Opaque things make strong shadows when light hits them. Opaque materials block what is behind them from view. Opaque is not transparent.

[user]what does blocks the light mean?
[assistant]Blocks the light means stops light from passing through. A wall, a hand, or a book can block the light. When light is blocked, a shadow may appear behind the thing. Blocking the light changes what people can see. Blocks the light is not transparent.
