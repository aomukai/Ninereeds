## Mixed feelings

[user]can someone feel two emotions at once?
[Ninereeds]Yes. A person can feel two emotions at the same time. You might feel happy about a gift but also sad that a friend could not come to your party. You might feel excited about starting something new but also nervous about what will happen. These are called mixed feelings. Mixed feelings are normal and do not mean something is wrong.

[user]what are mixed feelings?
[Ninereeds]Mixed feelings happen when you feel more than one emotion at the same time. You might feel proud of finishing a task but also tired from the hard work. You might feel relieved that a scary thing is over but also sad that someone got hurt. Mixed feelings can be confusing, but they show that life is complicated and our hearts notice many things at once.

[user]why do people get mixed feelings?
[Ninereeds]People get mixed feelings because most events have more than one part to notice. A goodbye can bring both sadness and hope. A win can bring both excitement and worry about the next game. Your brain and body respond to all the parts you care about. That is why one moment can hold more than one feeling.

## Feeling intensity

[user]can feelings be strong or weak?
[Ninereeds]Yes. Feelings come in different strengths. You can feel a little bit annoyed or very angry. You can feel slightly nervous or extremely scared. A strong feeling is harder to ignore and may change your body more. A weak feeling is quieter and easier to manage. Both strong and weak feelings are real.

[user]what does it mean to feel a little sad?
[Ninereeds]Feeling a little sad means the sadness is there but it is not very heavy. You might still play, talk, and do your activities. A little sadness may come from a small disappointment, like a canceled playdate. It usually passes quickly. A little sad is different from very sad.

[user]what does it mean to feel very angry?
[Ninereeds]Feeling very angry means the anger is strong and hard to ignore. Your body may feel hot, your heart may beat fast, and it may be hard to think clearly. Very angry often comes from something that feels very unfair or hurtful. When you feel very angry, you may need extra help to stay calm. Very angry is different from a little annoyed.

[user]what does it mean to feel a little nervous?
[Ninereeds]Feeling a little nervous means you notice some unease but it does not stop you. Your stomach might feel fluttery before a test, but you can still take the test. A little nervous is common before new things. It usually becomes smaller once you start. A little nervous is different from very scared.

## Feelings in the body

[user]how do feelings show in the body?
[Ninereeds]Feelings can change how your body feels and moves. When you are scared, your heart may beat fast and your muscles may tense. When you are happy, your face may smile and your body may feel light. When you are sad, your shoulders may droop and you may move slowly. Noticing your body can help you know what you are feeling.

[user]what happens in your body when you feel afraid?
[Ninereeds]When you feel afraid, your body gets ready to run or hide. Your heart beats faster to send blood to your legs. Your breathing becomes quicker. Your hands might shake or feel cold. Your stomach may feel tight. These body changes happen because fear tells your body something might be dangerous.

[user]what happens in your body when you feel excited?
[Ninereeds]When you feel excited, your body fills with energy. Your heart beats faster but in a good way. You may want to jump, run, or talk quickly. Your eyes may open wide and your voice may get louder. Excitement feels like happiness that cannot sit still.

[user]what happens in your body when you feel sad?
[Ninereeds]When you feel sad, your body often slows down. Your chest may feel heavy and your throat may feel tight. Tears may come to your eyes. You may want to curl up or sit quietly. Your voice may become softer. Sadness shows in the body as heaviness and slowness.

## Emotional regulation

[user]what can you do when you feel very angry?
[Ninereeds]When you feel very angry, you can pause before acting. Take a deep breath or count to ten. Walk away from the problem for a moment. Tell someone you trust how you feel. Squeeze a pillow or stomp your feet if you need to move the energy. Angry feelings are okay, but hurtful actions are not. Calming down helps you choose better actions.

[user]what can you do when you feel very sad?
[Ninereeds]When you feel very sad, it helps to let the sadness out safely. You can cry if you need to. You can talk to someone who cares about you. You can hold something soft or sit in a quiet place. Drawing or writing about your feelings can help too. Sad feelings usually become smaller when you give them attention and time.

[user]what can you do when you feel very scared?
[Ninereeds]When you feel very scared, try to slow your breathing. Take a deep breath in, hold it, then let it out slowly. Tell an adult you trust about what scares you. Remind yourself of things that are safe around you. Sometimes fear is a warning to be careful. Sometimes fear is too big for the real danger. Talking about it helps you tell the difference.

[user]what can you do when you feel very worried?
[Ninereeds]When you feel very worried, it helps to say your worry out loud or write it down. Ask yourself what is the worst that could happen and what is most likely to happen. Make a plan for what you can do. Talk to someone who can help. Worried thoughts can get stuck in circles, and action or talking can unstick them.

[user]what can you do when you feel lonely?
[Ninereeds]When you feel lonely, you can reach out to someone. You can call a friend, write a note, or ask to play. If no one is around, you can do something you enjoy or take care of yourself kindly. Loneliness is a signal that you want connection. Acting on that signal often makes the feeling smaller.

## Feelings in social situations

[user]how might you feel when you make a new friend?
[Ninereeds]When you make a new friend, you might feel excited, happy, and a little nervous all at once. Excitement comes from having someone new to play with. Happiness comes from being liked and wanted. Nervousness comes from not knowing the person well yet. These feelings usually settle into comfortable friendship over time.

[user]how might you feel when someone is mean to you?
[Ninereeds]When someone is mean to you, you might feel hurt, sad, or angry. Hurt comes from the unkind words or actions. Sad comes from wishing things were different. Angry comes from knowing the meanness was unfair. It is okay to feel all of these. Telling a trusted adult can help.

[user]how might you feel when you share something you made?
[Ninereeds]When you share something you made, you might feel proud, nervous, and hopeful. Proud comes from the work you put in. Nervous comes from wondering if others will like it. Hopeful comes from wanting good reactions. If people say kind things, you may feel relief and joy. If people say nothing or criticize, you may feel disappointed or embarrassed.

[user]how might you feel when you are left out?
[Ninereeds]When you are left out, you might feel sad, confused, or angry. Sad comes from missing the fun and the people. Confused comes from not understanding why. Angry comes from feeling it is unfair. Being left out hurts because belonging matters to people. Telling someone how you feel can help you decide what to do next.

[user]how might you feel when you help someone?
[Ninereeds]When you help someone, you might feel warm, proud, and connected. Warm comes from doing something kind. Proud comes from being useful. Connected comes from the moment you shared with the other person. Helping often makes both the helper and the helped feel better.

[user]how might you feel when you lose a game?
[Ninereeds]When you lose a game, you might feel disappointed, frustrated, or sad. Disappointed comes from wanting to win. Frustrated comes from feeling you could have done better. Sad comes from the loss itself. These feelings are normal. They usually become smaller after a rest and another chance to play.

[user]how might you feel when you win a game?
[Ninereeds]When you win a game, you might feel happy, proud, and relieved. Happy comes from the success. Proud comes from knowing your effort paid off. Relieved comes if you were worried about losing. Sometimes you might also feel bad for the person who lost, especially if they are sad. Winning feels best when everyone still had fun.

## Understanding other people's feelings

[user]how can you tell what someone else is feeling?
[Ninereeds]You can often tell what someone else is feeling by watching their face, body, and voice. A frown may show sadness or anger. Slumped shoulders may show tiredness or defeat. A shaky voice may show fear or nervousness. You can also listen to their words. If you are not sure, you can ask them how they feel.

[user]why is it hard to know what someone else feels?
[Ninereeds]It is hard to know exactly what someone else feels because feelings are inside a person and you cannot see inside. People do not always show their feelings on the outside. Some people hide their feelings. Some people show feelings differently than you do. That is why asking and listening are important.

[user]what should you do if you hurt someone's feelings?
[Ninereeds]If you hurt someone's feelings, you should stop and notice what happened. Say you are sorry and mean it. Ask if there is something you can do to help. Try not to do the same thing again. Apologizing does not erase the hurt, but it shows you care and want to make things better.

[user]what should you do if someone hurts your feelings?
[Ninereeds]If someone hurts your feelings, you can tell them how you feel using calm words. You can say "That made me feel sad" or "That was not kind." If they do not listen, you can walk away and talk to a trusted adult. You do not have to stay in a situation where you are being hurt.

## Emotions that are easily confused

[user]what is the difference between anger and frustration?
[Ninereeds]Anger is a hot feeling that comes when something seems unfair or wrong. Frustration is a stuck feeling that comes when something is hard to do or keeps failing. Frustration can turn into anger if it lasts too long. But frustration by itself is more like being blocked, while anger is more like wanting to push back.

[user]what is the difference between sadness and disappointment?
[Ninereeds]Sadness is a heavy feeling that comes from loss or hurt. Disappointment is a sinking feeling that comes when something you hoped for does not happen. Disappointment often leads to sadness, but they are not the same. You can feel disappointed without being very sad, especially if you can try again soon.

[user]what is the difference between fear and worry?
[Ninereeds]Fear is a strong immediate feeling that something dangerous is happening now. Worry is a restless feeling about something that might happen later. Fear comes fast and hits hard. Worry comes slowly and can last a long time. Fear tells you to act now. Worry tells you to prepare or think ahead.

[user]what is the difference between jealousy and envy?
[Ninereeds]Jealousy is the feeling of wanting what someone else has and wishing it were yours instead. Envy is the feeling of wanting what someone else has while they still have it. In everyday talk, people often use these words the same way. Both feelings can be uncomfortable, and both can be handled by noticing what you are grateful for.

[user]what is the difference between shame and guilt?
[Ninereeds]Shame is the feeling that there is something wrong with you as a person. Guilt is the feeling that you did something wrong. Guilt says "I made a mistake." Shame says "I am a mistake." Guilt can help you fix things. Shame can make you want to hide. It is healthier to feel guilt than to feel shame when you make a mistake.

[user]what is the difference between embarrassment and shame?
[Ninereeds]Embarrassment is an awkward feeling that comes from a social mistake or accident, like tripping in public. Shame is a heavier feeling that something is deeply wrong with you. Embarrassment is usually temporary and fades after the moment passes. Shame can stick around and hurt more. Embarrassment is lighter than shame.

## Common emotion scenarios

[user]why might a child feel nervous on the first day of school?
[Ninereeds]A child might feel nervous on the first day of school because everything is new. They may not know their teacher or classmates yet. They may not know where things are or what the rules will be. The unknown feels uncertain, and the body responds with nervousness. This is normal and usually fades as the child learns the new place.

[user]why might a child feel angry when a sibling takes their toy?
[Ninereeds]A child might feel angry when a sibling takes their toy because it feels unfair. The toy was theirs, and someone took it without asking. Anger is a natural response to feeling that something wrong happened. The child may need help using words to get the toy back instead of grabbing or hitting.

[user]why might a child feel sad when a pet dies?
[Ninereeds]A child might feel sad when a pet dies because they loved the pet and now the pet is gone. The child will miss playing with the pet, petting it, and having it nearby. This kind of sadness is called grief. It is a natural response to losing someone or something you loved. The sadness becomes smaller over time, but the love remains.

[user]why might a child feel excited before a birthday party?
[Ninereeds]A child might feel excited before a birthday party because something fun is about to happen. They may be looking forward to games, cake, presents, and friends. The brain notices that good things are coming and fills the body with energy. Excitement is the feeling of happy anticipation.

[user]why might a child feel embarrassed after making a mistake in front of the class?
[Ninereeds]A child might feel embarrassed after making a mistake in front of the class because they feel exposed. Everyone saw something they did not want others to see. They may worry that people will think less of them. This feeling usually fades quickly when they realize that everyone makes mistakes and most people forget soon.

[user]why might a child feel proud after learning to ride a bike?
[Ninereeds]A child might feel proud after learning to ride a bike because they worked hard and succeeded. They practiced, maybe fell, and kept trying until they could do it. Pride is the warm feeling of knowing you accomplished something difficult. It is a reward for effort and growth.

## When feelings need extra help

[user]when should you tell an adult about your feelings?
[Ninereeds]You should tell an adult about your feelings when the feelings are very strong and do not go away. You should tell an adult when you feel unsafe or when you need help that you cannot get on your own. You should tell an adult when you are thinking about hurting yourself or someone else. Adults can help you carry feelings that are too heavy to carry alone.

[user]what if you feel sad for a long time?
[Ninereeds]If you feel sad for a long time and it does not get better, you should tell a trusted adult. Sometimes sadness that lasts a long time needs extra help, like talking to a counselor or doctor. Feeling sad for a long time is not your fault and does not mean you are broken. It means you need support, and support is available.

[user]what if you feel angry all the time?
[Ninereeds]If you feel angry all the time, something may be bothering you that needs attention. Tell a trusted adult how you feel. Try to figure out what keeps making you angry. Sometimes anger that lasts a long time comes from hurt, fear, or unfairness that has not been addressed. Talking about it can help you understand it and find ways to feel better.

[user]what is courage?
[Ninereeds]Courage means a person does what a person knows is right even when the task is hard or frightening. A firefighter shows bravery by running into a burning house to save people inside. A child acts with bravery by telling the truth after a mistake. Courage is not the absence of fear.

[user]what is grief?
[Ninereeds]Grief is a natural human emotion that comes after loss or death. Grief can make a person feel sad and cry. A child may feel heavy in the body when a pet dies. A person may feel sadness when a friend is missing. Grief is not permanent.

[user]what does overwhelmed mean?
[Ninereeds]Overwhelmed describes a feeling that happens when too many things come at once. A child who has too much schoolwork and too many chores in one day may feel overwhelmed. The child's mind cannot think clearly or find a calm place. The person needs a break and sometimes needs help from a grown-up. Overwhelmed is not simple.
