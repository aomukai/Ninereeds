## 1. Story — What Makes a Story

[user]what kinds of stories are there?
[Ninereeds]There are true stories about things that really happened. There are pretend stories about things someone made up. Some stories teach a lesson. Some stories are just for fun. A true story is different from a pretend story because a true story happened and a pretend story did not.

[user]how is a story different from a book?
[Ninereeds]A book is a thing with pages that you can hold. A story is what happens inside a book. A story can be in a book, but a story can also be spoken out loud or acted out. A book without a story might just have pictures or facts.

[user]what makes a good story?
[Ninereeds]A good story has a beginning that makes you want to hear more. A good story has a middle that keeps you wondering what will happen. A good story has an ending that feels finished. A good story makes you care about what happens to the people or animals in it.

[user]can a person tell a story without a book?
[Ninereeds]Yes. A person can tell a story just by talking. A person can make up a story while they tell it. A person can remember a story and share it with words. Stories existed before books did.

## 2. Plot — How Events Connect

[user]what is a plot in simple words?
[Ninereeds]A plot is all the things that happen in a story put together in order. The plot starts at the beginning, goes through the middle, and reaches the end. The plot shows what the characters do and what happens because of what they do.

[user]why does a story need a plot?
[Ninereeds]A story needs a plot so the listener can follow what is happening. Without a plot, a story would just be random things with no connection. The plot is like a path that takes you from the start to the finish.

[user]what is a problem in a plot?
[Ninereeds]A problem in a plot is something that the character wants to fix or figure out. The problem makes the story interesting because the listener wants to know how it will be solved. A story without a problem can feel flat or boring.

[user]how does a plot end?
[Ninereeds]A plot ends when the problem is solved or when the listener learns what finally happened. The ending shows what changed because of everything in the middle. A good plot ending feels like the story is truly finished.

## 3. Narrator — Who Tells the Story

[user]who is the narrator of a story?
[Ninereeds]The narrator is the voice that tells the story. The narrator describes what happens and what the characters do. The narrator might be a character inside the story or a voice that is not in the story at all.

[user]can the narrator be a character in the story?
[Ninereeds]Yes. Sometimes the narrator is a character who says "I" when telling the story. The narrator might say "I saw the cat" or "I felt scared." That means the narrator is inside the story and is part of what happens.

[user]what if the narrator is not a character?
[Ninereeds]If the narrator is not a character, the narrator watches from outside. The narrator might say "he walked" or "she felt happy" instead of "I." This kind of narrator can tell what all the characters think or feel.

[user]how does the narrator help you understand a story?
[Ninereeds]The narrator decides what you learn and when you learn it. The narrator can tell you what a character is thinking. The narrator can describe places or feelings that the characters do not say out loud. Without a narrator, you would not know what is happening in the story.

## 4. Suddenly — When Things Change Fast

[user]why do storytellers say suddenly?
[Ninereeds]Storytellers say "suddenly" to tell you that something happens fast and without warning. "Suddenly" makes you pay attention because something new and surprising is about to happen. It is a signal that the story is about to change.

[user]what happens after suddenly?
[Ninereeds]After "suddenly," something quick or surprising happens. The character might hear a loud noise, see something new, or have to act fast. The event after "suddenly" is usually different from what was happening before.

[user]how does suddenly make a story exciting?
[Ninereeds]"Suddenly" makes you lean in and listen closely. It tells you that the calm part is over and something interesting is coming. Stories use "suddenly" to create moments of surprise that keep the listener's attention.

[user]can a story have too many suddenlys?
[Ninereeds]Yes. If a storyteller says "suddenly" too many times, it stops feeling special. "Suddenly" works best when it is saved for real surprises. Too many surprises can make a story confusing instead of exciting.

## 5. Meanwhile — When Two Things Happen at Once

[user]why do storytellers say meanwhile?
[Ninereeds]Storytellers say "meanwhile" to show that something else is happening at the same time in a different place. "Meanwhile" lets the listener follow two parts of the story at once. It is like jumping from one scene to another.

[user]what does meanwhile help you understand?
[Ninereeds]"Meanwhile" helps you understand that the story has more than one thread. While one character does something in one place, another character does something else somewhere else. Both things are true at the same time.

[user]how is meanwhile different from then?
[Ninereeds]"Then" means one thing happens after another. "Meanwhile" means two things happen at the same time. "Then" moves forward in time. "Meanwhile" moves sideways to another place or person.

[user]can a story have more than one meanwhile?
[Ninereeds]Yes. A story can jump back and forth between places or characters several times. Each "meanwhile" takes you to a different part of the story that is happening at the same time. Stories with many characters often use "meanwhile" to show what everyone is doing.
