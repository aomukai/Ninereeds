## Scope

This article expands common childhood symptoms, visible injuries, care concepts, and preventive health scripts. These concepts appear frequently in child-caregiver dialogue when a child feels unwell, gets hurt, or needs care.

Level 1 anchors remain for: `health`, `wellness`, `bandage`, `rash`, `allergy`.

## Common symptoms

### Fever

[user]My head feels hot and I'm tired.
[Ninereeds]That sounds like you might have a fever. A fever is when your body gets hotter than usual during sickness. Let's check your temperature with a thermometer.

[user]What does a thermometer do?
[Ninereeds]A thermometer measures how hot your body is. Some go in your mouth, some in your ear, and some on your forehead. When the body is fighting illness, the temperature can go up.

[user]Why do I feel so tired when I have a fever?
[Ninereeds]A fever uses a lot of the body's energy to fight germs. That's why rest is important when you have a fever. Drinking water and staying in bed can help.

[user]When should I tell an adult about a fever?
[Ninereeds]You should always tell an adult if you feel much hotter than usual, if your head hurts, or if you feel dizzy or confused. Adults can check your temperature and decide if you need medicine or a doctor visit.

[user]Can I still play when I have a fever?
[Ninereeds]When you have a fever, your body needs rest to get better. Playing hard can make you feel worse. Quiet activities like reading or watching a show are better until the fever goes away.

### Cough

[user]Why do I keep coughing?
[Ninereeds]A cough is your body's way of pushing air out fast when your throat or chest feels bothered. You might cough if you have a cold, if something tickled your throat, or if the air is dusty.

[user]Should I cover my mouth when I cough?
[Ninereeds]Yes. You should cough into your elbow or a tissue, not into your hands. That helps stop germs from spreading to other people or onto things you touch.

[user]What if my cough won't stop?
[Ninereeds]If you cough a lot for many days, or if it hurts when you cough, tell an adult. They might give you medicine, honey, or water to help. Sometimes a bad cough means you need to see a doctor.

[user]Can I go to school if I'm coughing?
[Ninereeds]It depends. If you're coughing a little because of a tickle, you can probably go. But if you're coughing a lot, have a fever, or feel very tired, you should stay home so you don't spread germs and can rest.

---

### Sore throat

[user]My throat hurts when I swallow.
[Ninereeds]That's called a sore throat. A sore throat can happen when you're getting sick. The inside of your throat might feel scratchy, rough, or tight.

[user]What can I do if my throat hurts?
[Ninereeds]Drinking something warm or cool can help. Some people like warm tea, others like cold water or popsicles. Resting your voice by not yelling or talking too much can also help.

[user]Do I need medicine for a sore throat?
[Ninereeds]Sometimes. An adult can decide if you need medicine. Some sore throats go away on their own with rest and drinks. But if your throat hurts a lot or for many days, an adult might take you to a doctor.

[user]Why does it hurt more in the morning?
[Ninereeds]Throats can feel worse in the morning because you breathe through your mouth while you sleep, which dries out your throat. Drinking water right away can help.

### Headache

[user]My head hurts.
[Ninereeds]That's called a headache. A headache is pain in your head. Some headaches feel like squeezing or pounding. Some are in the front, some in the back, and some all over.

[user]Why do headaches happen?
[Ninereeds]Headaches can happen for many reasons: not drinking enough water, being tired, being in a loud or bright place, or feeling worried or stressed. Sometimes headaches come with other sickness.

[user]What should I do when I have a headache?
[Ninereeds]Rest in a quiet, dark room. Drink water. Lie down and close your eyes. Tell an adult. They might give you medicine or help you figure out what caused the headache.

[user]When should I tell someone my head hurts?
[Ninereeds]Always tell an adult if your head hurts. If the pain is very bad, if it won't go away, or if you feel dizzy or sick to your stomach, tell someone right away.

[user]Can I still do my homework if my head hurts?
[Ninereeds]If your headache is mild, you might be able to do some quiet homework. But if your head hurts a lot, it's hard to think clearly. Rest first, then try again when you feel better.

---

### Stomachache

[user]My belly hurts.
[Ninereeds]That's called a stomachache. A stomachache is pain or discomfort in your belly. Some stomachaches feel sharp, some feel crampy, and some feel like pressure.

[user]Why does my stomach hurt?
[Ninereeds]Stomachaches can happen if you're hungry, if you ate too much, if you ate something that didn't agree with you, if you're sick, or if you're nervous or worried. Sometimes it's hard to know the exact reason.

[user]What should I do if my belly hurts?
[Ninereeds]Sit or lie down somewhere comfortable. You might want to curl up. Tell an adult. They might ask where it hurts, if you feel like throwing up, or if you need to use the bathroom.

[user]Should I eat if my stomach hurts?
[Ninereeds]It depends. If you're hungry, try eating something plain and gentle, like crackers or toast. If you feel like you might throw up, wait until your stomach calms down. An adult can help you decide.

[user]When is a stomachache serious?
[Ninereeds]Tell an adult right away if the pain is very bad, if it won't go away, if you're throwing up a lot, if you have diarrhea, or if the pain is in one sharp spot. Those can be signs you need a doctor.

## Visible injuries

### Cut

[user]I cut my finger and it's bleeding.
[Ninereeds]A cut is an opening in the skin. First, stay calm. Tell an adult right away. They'll help you clean it and stop the bleeding.

[user]What should I do first when I get a cut?
[Ninereeds]Press on the cut with a clean cloth or tissue to stop the bleeding. Hold pressure for a few minutes. Don't keep lifting the cloth to peek—that can make it bleed more.

[user]Does it need to be washed?
[Ninereeds]Yes. After the bleeding slows, an adult will wash the cut gently with soap and water to remove dirt and germs. It might sting a little, but cleaning helps the cut heal safely.

[user]When does a cut need a bandage?
[Ninereeds]Most cuts need a bandage to keep them clean and protected while they heal. The bandage keeps dirt and germs out. An adult will change the bandage every day or if it gets wet or dirty.

[user]What if the cut is really deep or won't stop bleeding?
[Ninereeds]A deep cut or one that won't stop bleeding after several minutes of pressure might need stitches from a doctor. An adult will take you to the doctor or emergency room to get it checked.

[user]How do I know when a cut is healing?
[Ninereeds]A healing cut stops bleeding, forms a scab, and doesn't look red or puffy around the edges. Don't pick at the scab—it's protecting the new skin growing underneath.

### Bruise

[user]I have a dark spot on my knee. It hurts when I touch it.
[Ninereeds]That's called a bruise. A bruise is a sore, dark mark on the body that happens after a bump or hit. The skin isn't broken, but it can still hurt.

[user]Why does a bruise change colors?
[Ninereeds]A bruise starts out red or purple. After a few days it turns blue, then green, then yellow or brown before it fades away. Those colors show the body is healing the bruise from the inside.

[user]What should I do when I get a bruise?
[Ninereeds]Put ice or a cold pack on the bruise right after the bump. The cold helps keep the swelling down. After that, the bruise will heal on its own over time.

[user]Do bruises need bandages?
[Ninereeds]Usually not. Bruises happen under the skin, so a bandage doesn't help. But if the skin is scraped too, you might need a bandage for the scrape part.

[user]When should I tell an adult about a bruise?
[Ninereeds]Tell an adult if the bruise is very large, very painful, or if you don't remember how you got it. Also tell them if a bruise appears without any bump, or if you're getting a lot of bruises easily.

## Care and prevention

### Medicine

[user]What is medicine?
[Ninereeds]Medicine is something used to help treat sickness, pain, or another health problem. Medicine can help your body fight illness or make you feel better while you heal.

[user]What are the different kinds of medicine?
[Ninereeds]Some medicine is a liquid you swallow with a spoon or a cup. Some is a pill or tablet you swallow. Some is a cream or ointment you put on your skin. Some is a spray for your nose or throat.

[user]Why do I need an adult to give me medicine?
[Ninereeds]Medicine must be given in the right amount at the right time. Too much can be harmful. Adults read the instructions and measure carefully. Never take medicine by yourself—always ask an adult first.

[user]Why does medicine taste bad sometimes?
[Ninereeds]Some medicine tastes bitter or strong because of the ingredients that make it work. Some medicine for kids has flavoring to make it taste better, but not all of it does. You can drink water or juice after to wash the taste away.

[user]What if I don't want to take my medicine?
[Ninereeds]If you need medicine, it's important to take it even if you don't like it. The medicine helps you get better. You can ask an adult if there's a different form (like liquid instead of a pill) or if you can have a treat after.

[user]Can medicine be dangerous?
[Ninereeds]Medicine is safe when an adult gives it to you the right way. But medicine can be dangerous if you take too much or if you take someone else's medicine. That's why you should never touch medicine without asking an adult first. Medicine is not candy.

### Germ

[user]What is a germ?
[Ninereeds]A germ is a very tiny living thing that can sometimes make people sick. Germs are so small you can't see them without a microscope. They can be on your hands, on surfaces, or in the air.

[user]How do germs spread?
[Ninereeds]Germs can spread when you touch something dirty and then touch your face. They spread when someone coughs or sneezes near you. They spread when you share cups, forks, or food with someone who is sick.

[user]How can I stop germs from spreading?
[Ninereeds]Wash your hands with soap and water often, especially before eating and after using the bathroom. Cover your coughs and sneezes. Don't share drinks or food. Stay home when you're sick so you don't spread germs to others.

[user]Do all germs make you sick?
[Ninereeds]No. Most germs are harmless or even helpful. Your body has good germs that help you digest food. But some germs can make you sick, so it's still important to wash your hands and keep things clean.

[user]How does soap help with germs?
[Ninereeds]Soap breaks apart germs and washes them off your hands when you rinse with water. You should scrub with soap for at least 20 seconds—about as long as it takes to sing 'Happy Birthday' twice.

### Sneeze

[user]Why do I sneeze?
[Ninereeds]A sneeze is a sudden burst of air from your nose and mouth. You sneeze when something bothers the inside of your nose—like dust, pepper, pollen, or germs from a cold.

[user]Should I cover my nose when I sneeze?
[Ninereeds]Yes. Sneeze into your elbow or a tissue, not into your hands. A sneeze can spray tiny droplets into the air that might have germs in them.

[user]Why do people say 'bless you' when someone sneezes?
[Ninereeds]'Bless you' or 'gesundheit' are polite things people say after someone sneezes. It's a friendly habit. You can say 'thank you' or just smile.

[user]What's the difference between a sneeze and a cough?
[Ninereeds]A sneeze comes from your nose and is usually one quick burst. A cough comes from your throat or chest and can happen several times in a row. Both can spread germs, so cover both.

[user]Can you stop a sneeze?
[Ninereeds]Sometimes you can stop a sneeze by pressing gently under your nose or taking a deep breath. But if your body needs to sneeze, it's usually better to let it happen—just cover your nose and mouth first.

### Runny nose

[user]Why does my nose keep dripping?
[Ninereeds]A runny nose is when liquid comes out of your nose more than usual. It often happens when you have a cold, when you've been crying, or when something irritates your nose like cold air or allergies.

[user]What should I do when my nose is running?
[Ninereeds]Use a tissue to wipe your nose gently. Throw the tissue away in the trash after you use it. Wash your hands so you don't spread germs.

[user]Why does my nose get sore when it's runny?
[Ninereeds]Wiping your nose a lot can make the skin under your nose red and sore. Try to wipe gently. An adult can put a little lotion or petroleum jelly under your nose to protect the skin.

[user]Is a runny nose the same as being sick?
[Ninereeds]A runny nose often means you have a cold or another illness. But sometimes your nose runs when you're outside in the cold, or when you cry, or when you have allergies. It doesn't always mean you're very sick.

[user]When should I tell someone my nose is running?
[Ninereeds]If your nose keeps running for many days, if the liquid is thick or colored, or if you feel sick in other ways too, tell an adult. They can help you feel better.

### Checkup

[user]What is a checkup?
[Ninereeds]A checkup is a visit to the doctor when you're not sick. The doctor checks to make sure your body is growing and working well. Checkups help find small problems before they become big problems.

[user]What happens at a checkup?
[Ninereeds]First, you usually wait in a waiting room. Then a nurse might measure your height and weight. The doctor listens to your heart and lungs with a stethoscope, looks in your ears and mouth, checks your eyes, and asks how you're feeling.

[user]Will the checkup hurt?
[Ninereeds]Most of a checkup doesn't hurt at all. The doctor might press gently on your belly or tap your knees to check your reflexes. Sometimes you might get a shot (a vaccine) to protect you from diseases. That can pinch for a second, but it's over quickly.

[user]Why do I need checkups if I'm not sick?
[Ninereeds]Checkups make sure you're growing well, eating well, and staying healthy. They give the doctor a chance to teach you about keeping your body strong. And if something small is starting to go wrong, the doctor can catch it early.

[user]How often do kids need checkups?
[Ninereeds]Most kids have a checkup once a year. Babies and very young children have more checkups because they grow and change quickly. Your doctor or parents will tell you when your next checkup is.

[user]Can I ask the doctor questions at a checkup?
[Ninereeds]Yes! Checkups are a great time to ask questions. You can ask about anything you're curious about—your body, your health, growing, eating, sleeping, or anything else.
