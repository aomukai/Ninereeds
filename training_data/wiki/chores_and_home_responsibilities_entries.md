# Chores and Home Responsibilities Entries

[user]what is a chore?
[assistant]A chore is a small job a person does to help at home. A chore may be cleaning, putting things away, or helping before a meal. Chores help keep a home clean, safe, and ready to use. A chore is not the same as homework.

[user]what is a responsibility?
[assistant]A responsibility is something a person is supposed to do and remember. A child may have the responsibility to feed a pet or put toys away. Responsibilities help a home work well for everyone. A responsibility is not the same as a toy.

[user]what does it mean to clean up?
[assistant]To clean up means to make a place neat by picking up, putting away, or wiping things. Children may clean up blocks, paper, or crumbs after an activity. Cleaning up helps a room become tidy and ready again. To clean up is not the same as to make a mess.

[user]what does it mean to put something away?
[assistant]To put something away means to place it back where it belongs. A child may put toys in a bin or clothes in a drawer. Putting things away helps people find them later. To put something away is not the same as to leave it on the floor.

[user]what does it mean to make the bed?
[assistant]To make the bed means to straighten the blanket, sheet, and pillow after sleeping. A child may pull up the blanket and smooth the bed in the morning. Making the bed helps a room look neat. To make the bed is not the same as to go to bed.

[user]what does it mean to set the table?
[assistant]To set the table means to put plates, cups, and spoons in the right places before a meal. A child may help set the table before dinner. Setting the table helps people get ready to eat together. To set the table is not the same as to wash dishes.

[user]what does it mean to sweep the floor?
[assistant]To sweep the floor means to use a broom to move dust and crumbs into a pile. A person may sweep the floor in a kitchen or a room. Sweeping helps make the floor clean. To sweep the floor is not the same as to mop it.

[user]what does it mean to water plants?
[assistant]To water plants means to pour water on plants so they can stay healthy and grow. A child may water plants with a cup or a watering can. Watering plants is one way to care for living things at home. To water plants is not the same as to pick flowers.

[user]what does it mean to feed a pet?
[assistant]To feed a pet means to give food to an animal that lives with people. A child may feed a pet dog, cat, fish, or bird. Feeding a pet is part of caring for the pet every day. To feed a pet is not the same as to eat dinner.

[user]what does it mean to take out the trash?
[assistant]To take out the trash means to carry garbage from inside the home to the outside bin. A person may take out the trash when the bag is full. Taking out the trash helps stop bad smells and helps keep a place clean. To take out the trash is not the same as to bring food in.

[user]what does it mean to tidy up?
[assistant]To tidy up means to make a space look neat and orderly. A child may tidy up a bedroom, a play area, or a table after work or play. Tidying up helps people move and work more easily in the space. To tidy up is not the same as to leave things everywhere.

[user]what does it mean to mop the floor?
[assistant]To mop the floor means to clean it with a damp mop to remove dirt and wet spots. People mop after sweeping to get the floor fully clean. Mopping uses water and sometimes soap. To mop the floor is not the same as to sweep it.

[user]what does it mean to wash the dishes?
[assistant]To wash the dishes means to clean plates, cups, bowls, and other dishes with soap and water. People wash dishes after a meal to keep them clean and ready for next time. Washing dishes is a common chore done at home. To wash the dishes is not the same as to set the table.

[user]what does it mean to do the laundry?
[assistant]To do the laundry means to wash clothes, towels, or bed sheets that have become dirty. People may put laundry in a machine or wash it by hand. Doing the laundry keeps clothing fresh and clean. To do the laundry is not the same as to wear clothes.

[user]what does it mean to vacuum?
[assistant]To vacuum means to use a machine called a vacuum cleaner to pick up dust and dirt from floors and carpets. The machine pulls in small pieces of dirt through a tube. Vacuuming helps keep floors cleaner than sweeping alone. To vacuum is not the same as to mop.
