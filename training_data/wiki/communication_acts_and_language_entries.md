# Communication Acts and Language Entries

[user]what is communication?
[assistant]Communication is sharing messages, ideas, or feelings with another person. People communicate by talking, writing, listening, and using signs. Communication helps people ask, answer, explain, and understand. Good communication helps people work and live together. Communication is not the same as silence.

[user]what does it mean to ask?
[assistant]To ask means to say words because you want an answer, help, or information. A child may ask a question in class or ask for a turn in a game. Asking helps people learn what they do not know. Asking often comes before an answer. To ask is not the same as to answer.

[user]what does it mean to answer?
[assistant]To answer means to reply to a question or message. A person may answer with words, a sign, or a short sound like yes. Answering helps keep communication going. A clear answer helps another person understand. To answer is not the same as to ask.

[user]what does it mean to whisper?
[assistant]To whisper means to speak very quietly. People whisper when they do not want others far away to hear. A whisper is softer than an ordinary voice. Whispering can be useful in quiet places. To whisper is not the same as to shout.

[user]what does it mean to shout?
[assistant]To shout means to speak very loudly. A person may shout to call someone from far away or during strong feelings. A shout is louder than an ordinary voice. Shouting can help a message travel farther, but it can also sound harsh. To shout is not the same as to whisper.

[user]what does it mean to explain?
[assistant]To explain means to help another person understand something. A person may explain a rule, an idea, or the reason for an action. Explaining often uses clear words and examples. A good explanation makes a confusing thing easier to follow. To explain is not the same as to hide.

[user]what is a promise?
[assistant]A promise is words that say a person really plans to do something or not do something. A child may promise to return a book or to help later. Promises matter because other people may trust those words. Keeping a promise helps trust grow. A promise is not the same as a guess.

[user]what does "what does that mean" mean?
[assistant]What does that mean is a question people ask when words are not clear yet. A child may ask this question during a lesson, a talk, or a story. The question asks for an explanation, not just a repeated sound. Asking what does that mean helps communication become clearer. What does that mean is not the same as I understand.

[user]what does "can you say it again" mean?
[assistant]Can you say it again is a question people use when they did not hear or understand something well enough the first time. A child may ask this in class, at home, or during play. The question asks for the same message one more time. It helps fix a small problem in communication. Can you say it again is not the same as keep going.

[user]what does "I meant" mean?
[assistant]I meant is something people say when they want to fix or clarify their own words. A child may say I meant the red one after saying the wrong color first. The phrase helps repair a message after a mistake or mix-up. I meant points back to what the speaker was trying to say. I meant is not the same as never mind.

[user]what is a complaint?
[assistant]A complaint is a message about something that feels wrong, unfair, or not working well. A child may make a complaint to a teacher when someone is being unkind. A complaint describes the problem so someone with the right role can help. A complaint is not the same as praise.
