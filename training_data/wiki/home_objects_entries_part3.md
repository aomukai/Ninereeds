# Home Objects Entries - Part 3

[user]what is a key?
[assistant]A key is a small object used to open a lock. Many keys are made of metal and have a special shape. People use keys for doors, boxes, cabinets, and some cars. A key works only with the lock it matches. A key is not a password.

[user]what is a lamp?
[assistant]A lamp is an object that gives light. People use lamps to read, work, or brighten a room. A lamp may stand on a table, on the floor, or hang above. Many lamps can be turned on and off with a switch. A lamp is not a flashlight.

[user]what is a plate?
[assistant]A plate is a flat dish for serving food. A plate is wider than it is deep. People use plates for meals, snacks, and slices of food. Plates can be made from clay, glass, metal, wood, or plastic. A plate is not a bowl.

[user]what is a shelf?
[assistant]A shelf is a flat surface for holding things. A shelf may attach to a wall or fit inside a cabinet. People put books, dishes, boxes, or toys on shelves. Shelves help keep things tidy and easy to reach. A shelf is not a drawer.

[user]what is a table?
[assistant]A table is a piece of furniture with a flat top and legs. People use tables for eating, working, games, or putting things down. Tables can be round, square, or long. Tables may be made from wood, metal, glass, or plastic. A table is not a desk.

[user]what is a window?
[assistant]A window is an opening in a wall that lets in light. A window usually has glass inside a frame. People look through windows to see outside. Some windows can open to let in air. A window is not a door.
