# Levels of Intensity and Gradation Entries

[user]what is level of intensity?
[assistant]A level of intensity is how strong or weak something is. People talk about levels of intensity when they describe sound, feeling, speed, light, heat, or effort. One thing can be a little strong, very strong, or too strong. Levels of intensity help people compare degrees, not just name things. A level of intensity is not a color.

[user]what does a little mean?
[assistant]A little means a small amount. A little rain, a little noise, or a little help is not very much. People use a little when something is present but not strong or large. A little points to a low level. A little is not a lot.

[user]what does a bit mean?
[assistant]A bit means a small amount, much like a little. People may say a bit tired, a bit cold, or a bit slow. The phrase helps soften a description and keep it small. A bit points to a low level, not a big one. A bit is not too much.

[user]what does a lot mean?
[assistant]A lot means a large amount. A lot of rain, a lot of noise, or a lot of work is more than a little. People use a lot when something is strong, big, or present in large amount. A lot points to a high level. A lot is not a little.

[user]what does very mean?
[assistant]Very is a word that makes another word stronger. A child may be very tired, a room may be very quiet, or a day may be very hot. Very helps people show that something is at a high level. The word makes a description stronger than plain tired, quiet, or hot. Very is not barely.

[user]what does really mean?
[assistant]Really is a word people often use to make a description stronger. A child may be really happy or really hungry. Really can sound more like everyday talk than very. The word helps show strong feeling or strong degree. Really is not barely.

[user]what does enough mean?
[assistant]Enough means as much as needed. If a cup has enough water, more water is not needed right now. People use enough for food, time, light, space, and many other things. Enough points to the right amount for a need or job. Enough is not too much.

[user]what does too much mean?
[assistant]Too much means more than needed or more than is good. Too much noise can hurt ears, and too much food can feel uncomfortable. People use too much when the amount has gone past a good level. Too much shows that the level is over the limit. Too much is not enough.

[user]what does barely mean?
[assistant]Barely means only a very small amount or only just enough. A person may barely hear a sound or barely finish on time. Barely points to a level close to nothing or close to not enough. People use barely when something almost did not happen or almost was not enough. Barely is not very.
