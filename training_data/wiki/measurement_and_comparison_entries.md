# Measurement and Comparison Entries

[user]what is measurement?
[assistant]Measurement is finding how much, how long, how tall, how heavy, or how hot something is. People use measurement to compare objects, food, time, and distance. Measurement helps people describe things in a careful way. Measurement is not the same as guessing.

[user]what is comparison?
[assistant]Comparison is looking at two or more things to see how they are the same or different. People compare size, weight, speed, height, and other features. Comparison helps people choose, sort, and understand things. Comparison is not the same as sameness.

[user]what does bigger mean?
[assistant]Bigger means larger in size. A bigger box takes up more space than a smaller box. People use bigger to compare things they can see or hold. Bigger is the opposite of smaller.

[user]what does smaller mean?
[assistant]Smaller means less large in size. A smaller toy takes up less space than a bigger toy. People use smaller to compare things of different sizes. Smaller is the opposite of bigger.

[user]what does taller mean?
[assistant]Taller means having more height. A taller person or tree reaches higher than a shorter one. People use taller when comparing how high things stand. Taller is the opposite of shorter.

[user]what does shorter mean?
[assistant]Shorter means having less length or height than something else. A shorter line or a shorter child does not reach as far or as high. People use shorter when comparing two things. Shorter is the opposite of taller.

[user]what does heavier mean?
[assistant]Heavier means having more weight than something else. A heavier bag is harder to lift than a lighter bag. People use heavier when comparing how much things weigh. Heavier is the opposite of lighter.

[user]what does lighter mean?
[assistant]Lighter means having less weight than something else. A lighter object is easier to carry than a heavier one. People use lighter when comparing weight. Lighter is the opposite of heavier.

[user]what is length?
[assistant]Length is how long something is from one end to the other. People measure the length of a rope, a table, or a road. Length helps compare how long things are. Length is not the same as height.

[user]what is height?
[assistant]Height is how tall something is from bottom to top. People measure the height of a child, a tree, or a building. Height helps compare how high things stand. Height is not the same as length.

[user]what is weight?
[assistant]Weight is how heavy something is. People compare the weight of bags, fruit, or animals. Weight helps tell how hard something may be to lift or carry. Weight is not the same as size.

[user]what is capacity?
[assistant]Capacity is how much a container can hold inside. A cup, bottle, or bucket has a certain capacity. Capacity helps people compare how much room containers have. Capacity is not the same as weight.

[user]what is distance?
[assistant]Distance is how far it is from one place to another. People talk about the distance from home to school or from one tree to another. Distance helps compare near and far places. Distance is not the same as time.

[user]what does estimate mean?
[assistant]To estimate means to make a careful guess that is close to the true amount. People estimate when they do not know the exact number or measurement yet. An estimate helps before counting or measuring exactly. Estimate is not the same as exact.

[user]what is temperature?
[assistant]Temperature is a measure of how hot or cold something is. People say the air is warm, hot, or cold based on its temperature. A thermometer shows temperature as a number. Temperature changes with the weather and with sources of heat or cold. Temperature is not the same as weather.

[user]what is speed?
[assistant]Speed is a measure of how fast something moves. A fast runner has a higher speed than a slow runner. People compare speed by saying faster or slower. Speed is not the same as distance.

[user]what does wider mean?
[assistant]Wider means having more space from one side to the other. A wider road lets more cars pass at once. People use wider when comparing how broad two things are. Wider is the opposite of narrower.

[user]what does narrower mean?
[assistant]Narrower means having less space from one side to the other. A narrower hallway has less room to walk through. People use narrower when comparing two things side by side. Narrower is the opposite of wider.
