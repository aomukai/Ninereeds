[user]what is a clam?
[assistant]A clam is a shellfish that lives in water. It has two shells that can open and close. Clams dig into sand or mud at the bottom of lakes and oceans. They eat tiny floating food by filtering water through their shells. A clam is not an oyster, which has a rougher shell and lives in different places.

[user]what is a coral?
[assistant]Coral is a ocean animal that makes hard rock-like homes. Many coral animals live together in groups called colonies. Coral gets food from tiny plants living inside its body. It grows in warm, shallow sunlight waters. Coral is not a rock, though its home becomes hard like limestone over time.

[user]what is a crab?
[assistant]A crab is a water animal with a hard outer shell. It has five pairs of legs including two claws. Crabs walk sideways on their legs. They eat both plants and animals they find in water. Crabs shed their shell to grow a bigger one underneath. A crab is not a lobster, which has a longer body and bigger claws.

[user]what is a fish?
[assistant]A fish is a water animal with back bones and gills. It uses fins to swim and steer in water. Most fish have flat scales covering their bodies. Fish live in oceans, rivers, lakes and ponds all over the world. They eat tiny plants, small animals or other fish. A fish is not a mammal, which breathes air with lungs and gives live birth.

[user]what is a jellyfish?
[assistant]A jellyfish is a water animal with a jelly-like bell body. It has long threads called tentacles that can sting. Jellyfish float and drift with ocean currents. They move by squeezing their bell body to push water. A jellyfish eats tiny floating food and small water animals. A jellyfish is not a fish, which has bones, gills and fins despite living in water.

[user]what is a octopus?
[assistant]An octopus is a soft water animal with eight arms. It can change color and shape to hide from enemies. An octopus has three hearts and blue-colored blood. It lives alone and hunts for food like crabs and fish. An octopus can open jars and use shells for hiding. An octopus is not a squid, which has a longer body and fins for swimming.

[user]what is a river?
[assistant]A river is a large natural flow of water moving downhill. It starts from rain, snow melt or springs in the ground. Rivers can be wide and deep enough for boats to ride on them. People use river water for drinking, farming and making electricity. Rivers cut valleys and create flat floodplains over many years. A river is not a canal, which is human-made and often dug straight.

[user]what is a salmon?
[assistant]A salmon is a fish that lives in both rivers and oceans. It is born in fresh water rivers and swims to the ocean. After years in the sea, it returns to its birth river to lay eggs. Salmon turn red and grow hooked jaws when ready to spawn. They jump up waterfalls to reach their spawning areas. A salmon is not a trout, which spends its whole life in fresh water rivers.

[user]what is a sea creature?
[assistant]A sea creature is any animal that lives in salt water oceans. It includes whales, dolphins, sharks and fish. It also includes corals, jellyfish, crabs and many small ocean animals. Sea creatures have special body parts for ocean life like gills or fins. They eat, hide and find mates in the ocean environment. A sea creature is not a land animal, which lives mainly on dry ground.

[user]what is a shark?
[assistant]A shark is a fish with bendable bones instead of hard ones. It has many rows of sharp teeth that replace when lost. Sharks can smell blood in water from very far away. They live in all oceans and hunt other sea animals for food. Sharks have existed for over 400 million years on Earth. A shark is not a dolphin, which is a mammal that breathes air and jumps in water.

[user]what is a starfish?
[assistant]A starfish is a ocean animal with a central body and arms. It usually has five arms shaped like stars pointing out. Starfish have bumpy skin and move using tiny tube feet underneath. They can grow back lost arms and sometimes make a whole new body. Starfish eat clams and oysters by opening their shells. A starfish is not a fish, which has bones, gills and fins despite living in water.

[user]what is a wave?
[assistant]A wave is water moving up and down in a repeating pattern. It is usually made by wind blowing across water surfaces. Waves can also come from underwater earthquakes or landslides. They have height from bottom to top and length between crests. Waves travel far while water mostly moves in circles underneath. A wave breaks when it reaches shallow shore and bottom slows the top. A wave is not a current, which is a steady flow of water in one direction.
