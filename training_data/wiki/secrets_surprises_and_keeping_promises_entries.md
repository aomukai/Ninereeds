# Secrets, Surprises, and Keeping Promises Entries

[user]what is a secret?
[assistant]A secret is something that is kept hidden from some people. Some secrets are playful and short, like a birthday surprise. Other secrets can feel heavy or unsafe. A secret is not a surprise.

[user]what is a surprise?
[assistant]A surprise is something hidden for a short time so the reveal feels exciting. People plan surprises for gifts, visits, or parties. A surprise is usually meant to bring joy, not fear. A surprise is not an unsafe secret.

[user]what does keep a promise mean?
[assistant]To keep a promise means to do what was said would be done. Keeping a promise helps build trust between people. People keep promises by remembering and following through. To keep a promise is not to break a promise.

[user]what does break a promise mean?
[assistant]To break a promise means not doing what was promised. Broken promises can hurt trust and feelings. Sometimes a promise is broken because of forgetting, changing plans, or making a bad choice. To break a promise is not to keep a promise.

[user]what does I promised I wouldn't tell mean?
[assistant]I promised I wouldn't tell means the speaker gave a promise to keep information private. These words often come up with secrets and trust. A person may need help from a trusted adult if the hidden thing is unsafe. I promised I wouldn't tell is not the same as I forgot.

[user]what is a surprise party?
[assistant]A surprise party is a party planned without telling the person who will be celebrated. Guests, food, and decorations are often prepared ahead of time. The fun comes from the happy reveal. A surprise party is not a secret kept forever.

[user]what is a pinky promise?
[assistant]A pinky promise is a playful promise made while hooking little fingers together. Many children use a pinky promise to show that the promise feels special. The finger action is a symbol of trust. A pinky promise is not a written rule.

[user]what is an unsafe secret?
[assistant]An unsafe secret is a hidden thing that makes a person feel scared, hurt, or trapped. Unsafe secrets should be told to a trusted adult for help. Safety matters more than keeping an unsafe secret. An unsafe secret is not a surprise.
