# Intentions and Plans in Action Entries

[user]what is an intention?
[assistant]An intention is what a person means or plans to do. An intention comes before the action happens. Children show intentions when they choose, plan, or get ready to act. Intention helps explain the purpose behind an action. An intention is not an accident.

[user]what does I'm going to mean?
[assistant]I'm going to means the speaker plans to do something soon. A child may say I'm going to draw, clean up, or go outside. The phrase points to an upcoming action. It helps people talk about the next step. I'm going to is not I already did.

[user]what does I plan to mean?
[assistant]I plan to means the speaker has thought ahead about an action. A child may say I plan to build a tall tower or finish homework first. The phrase sounds a little more organized than just saying want. It helps show a clear plan. I plan to is not I forgot.

[user]what does next I will mean?
[assistant]Next I will means the speaker is naming the next step in order. A child may say next I will wash my hands or next I will color the sky. The phrase helps show sequence in a plan or task. It keeps actions in order. Next I will is not I'm about to.

[user]what does I'm about to mean?
[assistant]I'm about to means the action is going to start very soon. A child may say I'm about to jump, eat, or open the box. The phrase points to the moment right before an action. It helps others know what is coming next. I'm about to is not later.

[user]what does let's mean?
[assistant]Let's means the speaker wants to do something together with another person or group. A child may say let's play, let's read, or let's try again. The word invites shared action. Let's helps start a joint plan. Let's is not go away.

[user]what does shall we mean?
[assistant]Shall we is a polite way to suggest doing something together. A child may say shall we start now or shall we clean this up. The phrase sounds softer than a command. It invites agreement before the action starts. Shall we is not do it now with no choice.

[user]what does I decided to mean?
[assistant]I decided to means the speaker made a choice and settled on it. A child may say I decided to use blue or I decided to sit here. The phrase shows the choosing part is finished. It often comes before the action begins. I decided to is not I am still choosing.

[user]what does I changed my mind mean?
[assistant]I changed my mind means the speaker had one plan or choice and then picked a different one. A child may change a game, a color, or a seat. The phrase helps explain a new choice after an old one. It shows that plans can change. I changed my mind is not I decided to keep the same plan.
