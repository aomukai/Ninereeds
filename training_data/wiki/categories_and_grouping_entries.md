# Categories and Grouping Entries

[user]what is a category?
[assistant]A category is a group of things that belong together in an important way. Animals, foods, and colors can each be categories. Categories help people sort and understand the world. A category gives a name to a whole group, not just one thing. A category is not a set.

[user]what does types of mean?
[assistant]Types of means different kinds inside a bigger group. Dogs and cats are types of animals, and apples and pears are types of fruit. People use types of when they want to talk about smaller groups inside a larger category. The phrase helps show how things are related. Types of is not the same as one single thing.

[user]what does belongs to mean?
[assistant]Belongs to can mean fits into a group or category. A robin belongs to the bird group, and a carrot belongs to the vegetable group. People use belongs to when they sort things by shared features. The phrase helps show where something fits. Belongs to is not does not belong.

[user]what does does not belong mean?
[assistant]Does not belong means something does not fit with the other things in a group. A shoe does not belong in a group of fruits. People use does not belong when they sort and compare items. The phrase helps people notice important differences. Does not belong is not belongs to.

[user]what does sort mean?
[assistant]Sort means put things into groups by a rule or feature. Children sort by color, size, shape, or type. Sorting helps make patterns and groups easier to see. People sort objects when they want order and clear groups. Sort is not classify.

[user]what does group mean?
[assistant]Group means put things together because they belong in the same set. People group objects that match in some clear way. A group can be small or large. Grouping helps people compare and organize things. Group is not separate.

[user]what is a set?
[assistant]A set is a collection of things looked at together as one group. A set of blocks or a set of cards belongs together in one collection. People talk about sets in math, play, and sorting. A set helps people count and compare a whole group at once. A set is not one piece.

[user]what does in the same group mean?
[assistant]In the same group means two or more things were sorted together. A spoon and a fork may be in the same group because both are kitchenware. People use this phrase when comparing what fits together. It shows that the things share an important feature. In the same group is not in different groups.

[user]what does classify mean?
[assistant]Classify means sort something into a category or group. People classify animals, rocks, foods, and many other things. Classifying uses careful looking and shared features. The word is often used when sorting in a more careful way. Classify is not does not belong.

[user]what does which one fits mean?
[assistant]Which one fits means which thing belongs with the others. Children ask this when sorting pictures, objects, or ideas. The question helps people look for the shared rule in a group. It is common in classroom sorting tasks. Which one fits is not which one does not belong.
