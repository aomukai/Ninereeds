[user]what is hot?

[assistant]Cold is a word used to describe a low temperature. When something is cold, it takes away heat and can make you shiver. Ice, snow, and the air in winter are all cold. People wear coats and gloves to stay warm when it is cold outside. Cold is the opposite of hot.

[user]what is light?
[assistant]Light describes something that has very little weight and is easy to lift or move. A feather, a leaf, and a piece of paper are all light. Even a small child can pick up something that is light. Light objects often float on top of water or blow away in the wind. Light is the opposite of heavy.

[user]what is hard?

[user]what is soft?
[assistant]Soft describes an object that changes shape easily when you touch or press it. A pillow, a blanket, and a piece of cotton are all soft. Soft things feel gentle against the skin and are often used for comfort or sleeping. You can squeeze or fold something that is soft. Soft is the opposite of hard.

[user]what is a solid?
[assistant]A solid is a state of matter that has a firm shape and does not flow like a liquid. The particles in a solid are packed very tightly together, which makes it hard or strong. Wood, stone, and ice are all examples of solids. A solid keeps its shape even if you move it from one place to another. You can hold a solid in your hand easily. A solid is not a liquid.

[user]what is a liquid?
[assistant]A liquid is a state of matter that flows and takes the shape of the container it is in. It does not have a shape of its own, but it has a consistent volume. Water, milk, and oil are common liquids that we see every day. If you pour a liquid onto the floor, it will spread out into a puddle. A liquid can be poured from a bottle into a cup. A liquid is not a gas.

[user]what is a gas?
[assistant]A gas is a state of matter that has no fixed shape and can expand to fill any space. Most gases are invisible, like the air that we breathe every day. The particles in a gas move very quickly and are spread far apart from each other. Steam rising from a hot pot of water is a gas. You can feel a gas moving when the wind blows against your face. A gas is not a solid.

[user]what is steam?

Steam can be very powerful and was once used to move large trains and machines. If steam touches something cold, it turns back into tiny drops of water. You must be careful because steam can burn your skin just like hot water. Steam is not ice.

[user]what is ice?

When the temperature goes above freezing, ice begins to melt and turn back into liquid water. Ice can be found in a cold drink or as snow and frozen ponds in the winter. It is often clear or white in color. Ice is not steam.
