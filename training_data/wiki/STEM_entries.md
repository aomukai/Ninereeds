# STEM Entries

[user]what is hot?
[assistant]Hot means having a high temperature. The sun, a fire, and a stove can be hot. Hot things can warm other things or burn skin. Hot is the opposite of cold.

[user]what is cold?
[assistant]Cold means having a low temperature. Ice, snow, and winter air can be cold. Cold things can make your body shiver or your hands feel stiff. Cold is the opposite of hot.

[user]what is warm?
[assistant]Warm means between cold and hot. Soup, sunlight, and a blanket can feel warm. Warm often feels pleasant to the body. Warm is not the same as cold.

[user]what is heavy?
[assistant]Heavy means hard to lift or move because of weight. A big stone or a full bag can be heavy. Heavy things often need more strength to carry. Heavy is the opposite of light.

[user]what is light?
[assistant]Light means easy to lift or move because of low weight. A feather, a leaf, and a sheet of paper can be light. Light things may blow in the wind or float on water. Light is the opposite of heavy.

[user]what is hard?
[assistant]Hard means not easy to press, bend, or squeeze. A stone, a coin, and a piece of wood can be hard. Hard things keep their shape when you push on them. Hard is the opposite of soft.

[user]what is soft?
[assistant]Soft means easy to press, bend, or squeeze. A pillow, a blanket, and cotton can be soft. Soft things often feel gentle on the skin. Soft is the opposite of hard.

[user]what is a solid?
[assistant]A solid is something with a shape of its own. A rock, a spoon, and a block of ice are solids. A solid does not pour like water. A solid is not a liquid.

[user]what is a liquid?
[assistant]A liquid is something that flows. Water, milk, and oil are liquids. A liquid takes the shape of the cup, bottle, or bowl it is in. A liquid is not a gas.

[user]what is a gas?
[assistant]A gas is something that spreads through the space around it. Air is a gas, and steam is a gas. Most gases are hard to see, but people can often feel them move. A gas is not a solid.

[user]what is steam?
[assistant]Steam is hot water in gas form. Steam rises from very hot water. Steam can look like a white mist in the air. Steam is not ice.

[user]what is ice?
[assistant]Ice is water that has become solid from cold. Ice is hard, cold, and slippery. Ice can melt and turn back into water when it gets warm. Ice is not steam.

[user]what is wet?
[assistant]Wet means covered with water or another liquid. Wet clothes, wet grass, and wet hair all have water on them. Wet things may drip or feel cool. Wet is the opposite of dry.

[user]what is dry?
[assistant]Dry means without water on it. A dry towel, dry sand, and dry paper do not feel wet. Things can become dry after time in the sun or air. Dry is the opposite of wet.

[user]what is rough?
[assistant]Rough means not smooth to the touch. Bark, gravel, and some stone walls feel rough. Rough things have bumps or sharp little parts on the surface. Rough is the opposite of smooth.

[user]what is smooth?
[assistant]Smooth means even and not rough to the touch. Glass, calm water, and polished stone can feel smooth. Smooth things are easy to slide a hand across. Smooth is the opposite of rough.

[user]what is a push?
[assistant]A push is a force that moves something away. You push a door to open it, or push a cart to move it. A push can be gentle or strong. A push is the opposite of a pull.

[user]what is a pull?
[assistant]A pull is a force that brings something closer. You pull a rope, a drawer, or a wagon toward you. A pull can be gentle or strong. A pull is the opposite of a push.

[user]what does it mean to float?
[assistant]To float means to stay on top of a liquid instead of going to the bottom. A leaf, a duck, and many boats float on water. Floating means the water holds the thing up. Float is the opposite of sink.

[user]what does it mean to sink?
[assistant]To sink means to go down in a liquid. A stone or a coin sinks in water. Sinking means the liquid does not hold the thing up. Sink is the opposite of float.

[user]what does it mean to melt?
[assistant]To melt means to change from solid to liquid because of heat. Ice melts into water, and butter melts in a hot pan. Melting happens when something gets warm enough. Melt is the opposite of freeze.

[user]what does it mean to freeze?
[assistant]To freeze means to change from liquid to solid because of cold. Water freezes into ice. Things freeze when they get cold enough. Freeze is the opposite of melt.

[user]what does it mean to grow?
[assistant]To grow means to become bigger over time. Plants grow from seeds, and animals grow from babies into adults. Growing needs time, and living things often need food or water too. A rock does not grow.

[user]what does it mean to move?
[assistant]To move means to change place or position. People, animals, water, clouds, and cars can all move. Moving can be fast or slow. Move is the opposite of stay still.

[user]what does it mean to fall?
[assistant]To fall means to go down from a higher place. A leaf falls from a tree, and a ball falls when you let go of it. Falling goes downward. Fall is the opposite of rise.

[user]what does it mean to rise?
[assistant]To rise means to go up from a lower place. Smoke rises, and a person rises from a chair when standing up. Rising goes upward. Rise is the opposite of fall.

[user]what does it mean to break?
[assistant]To break means to come apart or stop working because of damage. A glass may break into pieces, and a toy may break and stop working. Broken things may need help before they can be used again. Break is the opposite of fix.

[user]what does it mean to fix?
[assistant]To fix means to make something work again or put it back together. People fix toys, doors, bikes, and other things. Fixing often uses tools, care, and time. Fix is the opposite of break.

[user]what is fast?
[assistant]Fast means moving quickly. A car, a rabbit, or a bird can be fast. Fast things take less time to get somewhere. Fast is the opposite of slow.

[user]what is slow?
[assistant]Slow means moving without much speed. A snail and a turtle can be slow. Slow things take more time to get somewhere. Slow is the opposite of fast.

[user]what does it mean to boil?
[assistant]To boil means to heat a liquid until it bubbles strongly. Water boils when it gets very hot. Boiling can make steam rise. Boil is not the same as melt.

[user]what does it mean to burn?
[assistant]To burn means to be damaged by fire or strong heat. Paper, wood, and dry leaves can burn. Burning can make smoke, ash, heat, and light. Burn is not the same as melt.

[user]what does it mean to pour?
[assistant]To pour means to make a liquid flow from one place into another. You can pour water from a jug into a cup. Pouring follows the shape and path of the liquid. You do not pour a solid block.

[user]what does it mean to mix?
[assistant]To mix means to put things together so they join. People mix water and flour, or paint and paint. Mixing can happen with a spoon, hands, or a machine. Mix is not the same as pour.

[user]what does it mean to bend?
[assistant]To bend means to curve or fold without breaking. A branch, a wire, or a straw may bend. Some things bend easily, and some hardly bend at all. Bend is not the same as break.

[user]what does it mean to be full?
[assistant]Full means having no more room inside. A full cup or a full bag cannot hold much more. Full things may spill if you add too much. Full is the opposite of empty.

[user]what does it mean to be empty?
[assistant]Empty means having nothing inside. An empty cup has no drink in it, and an empty bag has nothing in it. Empty things have room to be filled. Empty is the opposite of full.

[user]what does it mean to be alive?

[user]what does it mean to eat?

[user]what does it mean to drink?

[user]what does it mean to sleep?

[user]what does it mean to be awake?

[user]what does it mean to live?

[user]what does it mean to hear?
