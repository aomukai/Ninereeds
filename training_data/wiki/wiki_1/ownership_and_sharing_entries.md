# Ownership and Sharing Entries

[user]what is ownership?
[Ninereeds]Ownership is the relation between a person and something that belongs to that person. A toy, book, or coat may be under one person's ownership. Ownership helps people know whose thing something is. Ownership matters in sharing, borrowing, and returning. Ownership is not the same as borrowing.

[user]what does mine mean?
[Ninereeds]Mine means something belongs to the speaker. A child may say this book is mine or that ball is mine. Mine shows ownership in a short way. The word helps people talk about what belongs to them. Mine is the opposite of yours.

[user]what does yours mean?
[Ninereeds]Yours means something belongs to the other person. A child may say this pencil is yours or that coat is yours. Yours shows ownership in a short way. The word helps people return or sort things correctly. Yours is the opposite of mine.

[user]what does it mean to borrow?
[Ninereeds]To borrow means to use something that belongs to another person for a time. A child may borrow a pencil, a book, or a toy. Borrowing usually means the thing should be returned later. Borrowing depends on another person's permission. To borrow is not the same as to keep.

[user]what does it mean to return?
[Ninereeds]To return means to give something back to the person or place it belongs to. A child may return a library book or a borrowed marker. Returning helps fix the ownership link after borrowing. Returning shows care and responsibility. To return is not the same as to take.

[user]what is sharing?
[Ninereeds]Sharing is letting another person use, have, or enjoy part of something. Children share toys, food, space, and time. Sharing can help people play, learn, and work together. Sharing does not always change who owns the thing. Sharing is not the same as grabbing.

[user]what does can I use that mean?
[Ninereeds]Can I use that is a question people ask before touching or borrowing something. A child may ask this about a toy, a crayon, or a book. The question shows respect for ownership and choice. Asking first can prevent arguments. Can I use that is not the same as taking it without asking.

[user]what does that's mine mean?

The phrase can help identify ownership quickly. People often need calm words when more than one person wants the same thing. That's mine is not the same as that's yours.

[Ninereeds]Inheritance is the process of receiving items from a family member after that person dies. A parent can leave belongings to children. An owner can pass property to a younger family member. The things received become the new owner's possessions. Inheritance is not earning.
