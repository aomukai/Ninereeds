# Simple Physics: Energy and Power Entries

[user]what is energy?
[Ninereeds]Energy is what makes things move, shine, heat up, or work. People use energy every day at home and at school. Food, sunlight, fuel, and batteries can give energy. Energy is not a material.

[user]what is power?
[Ninereeds]Power is energy being used to do something. A strong motor uses power to turn or lift. A bright lamp uses power to make light. Power is not the same as size.

[user]what is electricity?
[Ninereeds]Electricity is a kind of energy that can travel through wires. Electricity can make lights turn on and machines run. Many homes and schools use electricity every day. Electricity is not fuel.

[user]what is a battery?
[Ninereeds]A battery stores energy for later use. A battery can make a toy, clock, or flashlight work. Some batteries are small and some batteries are large. A battery is not a plug.

[user]what is fuel?
[Ninereeds]Fuel is something that gives energy when it is burned or used up. Gasoline, wood, and gas are kinds of fuel. Cars, stoves, and fires can use fuel. Fuel is not a battery.

[user]what is a plug?
[Ninereeds]A plug is the part at the end of a cord that goes into a wall outlet. A plug lets electricity move from the wall into a machine. Many lamps and chargers have plugs. A plug is not a switch.

[user]what is a switch?
[Ninereeds]A switch is something that turns power on or off. A light switch can make a room bright or dark. A machine may have a switch to start or stop it. A switch is not a plug.

[user]what is a flashlight?
[Ninereeds]A flashlight is a small light that often uses batteries. People use a flashlight in the dark or when the power is out. A flashlight can be carried in one hand. A flashlight is not a candle.

[user]what is solar power?
[Ninereeds]Solar power is energy that comes from sunlight. Solar panels can collect sunlight and turn it into electricity. Solar power can help run lights, homes, and machines. Solar power is not fuel.

[user]what is impact?
[Ninereeds]Impact describes what happens when one thing hits another. A falling rock can make a hole in the ground. A drop of rain makes a splash in a pool of water. Each event causes changes that move from one thing to other things. Some effects are big and some are small. Impact is not stillness.

[user]what is pressure?
[Ninereeds]Pressure is a push that spreads over an area. When a person sits on a chair, the body pushes down on the chair surface. When wind blows against a wall, the air pushes against the wall. Both a hand and wind can make a push over an area. Pressure is not a weight.

[user]what is a vibration?
[Ninereeds]A vibration is a rapid back-and-forth movement of matter. This movement travels through solid objects and through air. A pulled guitar string shakes and makes a sound. A hand tapping a drum feels the drum surface move. Vibration is not stillness.
