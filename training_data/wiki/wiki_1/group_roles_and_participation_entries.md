# Group Roles and Participation Entries

[user]what is a group role?
[Ninereeds]A group role is the part a person has in a group activity. Different people may lead, help, watch, or keep track of time. Group roles help people know what to do. Clear roles can make group work and play smoother. A group role is not a goal.

[user]what is a leader?
[Ninereeds]A leader is a person who helps guide a group. A leader may start the activity, explain the plan, or help people work together. Good leaders listen as well as speak. A leader helps the group move toward the goal. A leader is not a follower.

[user]what is a follower?
[Ninereeds]A follower is a person who goes along with the plan or direction in a group. A follower may listen, watch, and do the next step after the leader or group decides it. Following can help a group stay organized. A follower still matters to the group. A follower is not a leader.

[user]what is a helper?
[Ninereeds]A helper is a person who gives support during a task or activity. A helper may carry things, clean up, explain, or share materials. Helpers make group work easier for everyone. Many groups work better when someone helps quietly and steadily. A helper is not an audience member.

[user]what is a partner?
[Ninereeds]A partner is one of two people working or playing together. Partners often share turns, ideas, and jobs. A partner listens and responds to the other person closely. Many classroom tasks use partners instead of large groups. A partner is not a whole team.

[user]what is a team member?
[Ninereeds]A team member is a person who belongs to a team. Team members work toward the same goal with other people. A team member may pass, build, listen, or take a turn when needed. Each team member has a part in the team effort. A team member is not an opponent.

[user]what is an audience?
[Ninereeds]An audience is the group of people who watch or listen to something. An audience may watch a play, hear a song, or listen to a speaker. Audience members are not the main performers in that moment. An audience can clap, listen, and pay attention. An audience is not a volunteer.

[user]what is a volunteer?
[Ninereeds]A volunteer is a person who chooses to help or take part without being forced. A volunteer may raise a hand to go first or help with a job. Volunteering shows willingness. Teachers often ask for a volunteer during class activities. A volunteer is not a person who was picked without asking.

[user]what is a captain?
[Ninereeds]A captain is a leader of a team. A captain may speak for the team, help the team stay organized, or encourage other team members. Captains are common in sports and games. A captain has a role inside the team, not outside it. A captain is not just an audience member.

[user]what is a timekeeper?
[Ninereeds]A timekeeper is the person who watches the time during an activity. A timekeeper may say when to start, stop, switch, or finish. This role helps a group stay on schedule. A timekeeper does not have to lead everything else. A timekeeper is not the captain.

[user]what does whose turn is it mean?
[Ninereeds]Whose turn is it means which person should go next. Children ask this in games, lines, and group activities. The question helps people share fairly and keep order. Knowing whose turn it is can stop arguments. Whose turn is it is not order.

[user]what is management?
[Ninereeds]Management is the act of organizing people and resources toward a goal. A store manager uses management to set schedules and guide workers. A school principal uses management to plan lessons and run the school. Leaders help teams work together. Management is not control.

[user]what is membership?
[Ninereeds]Membership is a way for a person to become part of a group. A person can join a library and get a card. A person can sign up for a sports team and play games. Membership often gives special access to events or spaces. Membership is not ownership.

[user]what is an organization?
[Ninereeds]Organization is a group of people who work together for a common purpose. a school is one kind of group. teachers and students each have different roles in the school. the group helps people reach goals that are larger than one person can reach alone. organization is not chaos.

[user]what is participation?
[Ninereeds]Participation means taking part in an activity with other people. a child can show taking part by joining a game at recess. a student can show taking part by raising a hand to answer a question in class. everyone in a class can take part together. participation is not standing alone.

[user]what is unity?
[Ninereeds]Unity is a condition of different parts working together as a single whole. A team shows unity when all members pull in the same direction toward a shared goal. A family meal brings people closer in togetherness. Unity is not separation.
