[user]what is money?
[assistant]Money is something people use to buy things. People earn money by doing work or by selling something. Money can help people get food, clothes, tools, and other things they need. Some money is made as coins, and some money is made as paper money. Money is not food.

[user]what is a coin?
[assistant]A coin is a small piece of money made of metal. Coins are often round and flat. People can keep coins in a pocket or a jar. A coin can be used to pay for something small or as part of a bigger price. A coin is not paper.

[user]what is a dollar?
[assistant]A dollar is one kind of money. People use dollars to talk about how much something costs. Some things cost one dollar, and some cost many dollars. A dollar may be one bill or a group of coins that add up to one dollar. A dollar is not a coin.

[user]what is a store?
[assistant]A store is a place where people buy things. A store may sell food, clothes, books, tools, or toys. People go to a store when they need or want something. Stores often have shelves, prices, and workers who help people. A store is not a house.

[user]what does it mean to buy?
[assistant]To buy means to get something by giving money for it. People buy food, clothes, books, and many other things. You can buy something in a store or marketplace. Buying means one person gives money and gets a thing. To buy is not the same as to sell.

[user]what does it mean to sell?
[assistant]To sell means to give something to another person for money. A store may sell food, clothes, or toys. A person can also sell something made, grown, or owned. Selling means one person gives the thing and gets the money. To sell is not the same as to buy.

[user]what does it mean to pay?
[assistant]To pay means to give money for something. People pay when they buy food, a toy, or a book. You may pay with coins, bills, or other kinds of money. Paying finishes the exchange when something has a price. To pay is not the same as to earn.

[user]what is a cost?
[assistant]A cost is the amount of money needed to get something. A small toy may have a low cost, and a big machine may have a high cost. The cost helps people know if they have enough money. People often ask about the cost before they buy. A cost is not the same as a price.

[user]what is a price?
[assistant]A price is the amount of money asked for something. A store puts a price on food, clothes, books, and other things for sale. A price may be written on a tag, a sign, or a shelf. People look at the price to decide whether to buy. A price is not the same as a cost.

[user]what does it mean to save?
[assistant]To save means to keep money instead of using it right away. A child may save money for a toy, a book, or a gift. Saving takes waiting and planning. People save when they want to use money later. To save is not the same as to spend.

[user]what does it mean to spend?
[assistant]To spend means to use money to buy something. People spend money on food, clothes, books, and many other things. After you spend money, you have less of it left. Spending happens when you pay for something. To spend is not the same as to save.
