# Fractions and Sharing Quantities Entries

[user]what is a fraction?
[assistant]A fraction is a way to name part of a whole. Fractions help people talk about sharing and equal pieces. One half and one quarter are fractions. A fraction is not a whole.

[user]what are equal parts?
[assistant]Equal parts are pieces that are the same size. A sandwich cut into two matching pieces has equal parts. Equal parts help sharing feel fair. Equal parts are not uneven parts.

[user]what is a half?
[assistant]A half is one of two equal parts of a whole. Two halves make one whole thing again. A half can be one side of a cut sandwich or one side of an apple. A half is not a quarter.

[user]what is a quarter?
[assistant]A quarter is one of four equal parts of a whole. Four quarters make one whole thing. A pizza cut into four same-size slices has quarters. A quarter is not a third.

[user]what is a third?
[assistant]A third is one of three equal parts of a whole. Three thirds make one whole thing. A chocolate bar can be split into three thirds. A third is not a half.

[user]what does it mean to share evenly?
[assistant]To share evenly means to give the same amount to each person. Sharing evenly helps each person get a fair part. Equal parts are often used to share evenly. To share evenly is not to take more.

[user]what does it mean to cut in half?
[assistant]To cut in half means to divide something into two equal parts. People cut food in half when two people will share it. A half should match the other half in size. To cut in half is not to tear at random.

[user]what does one out of three mean?
[assistant]One out of three means one part is chosen from a group of three equal parts or three things. If one cookie out of three is chocolate, one cookie has that kind. The number one tells how many are chosen. One out of three is not two out of three.

[user]what does divide into mean?
[assistant]To divide into means to split something into parts. People divide into groups, teams, or pieces. The parts can be equal when sharing fairly. To divide into is not to keep together.
