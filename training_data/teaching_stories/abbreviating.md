A child and an old woman walked along the dusty path to the market one afternoon. The old woman carried a long list of things to buy. “We need Doctor Brown’s medicine, a dozen eggs, milk, bread, apples, and so on,” she said.

The child looked at the list. “That is a lot of writing,” the child said.

The old woman smiled. “You can shorten it. Instead of ‘Doctor’, write ‘Dr.’ And instead of ‘and so on’, write ‘etc.’” She pointed to a stall. “Also, for the long name of the farmer at the end of the lane, just write ‘Mr. Tom’ — skip the middle part.”

The child tried it. The list became much shorter. The old woman gave the child the responsibility of holding the list. “Accountability is important,” she said. “Now, let’s see if we can finish quickly.”

They bought the medicine from Dr. Brown’s stall. The old woman gave advice about affording the items — they had just enough coins. The child felt proud of accomplishing the task so fast. They even had time to look at a toy airplane at the next stall. “We saved time by abbreviating,” the old woman said. “Now we can enjoy the rest of the afternoon.”

The child smiled, amassing a small pile of goods in the basket. “I will use shortcuts from now on,” the child said.
