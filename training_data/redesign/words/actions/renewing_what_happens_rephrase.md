[Ninereeds]When something is renewed, it becomes fresh again. When something is renewed, it is restored to a good state. When something is renewed, its life is extended. When something is renewed, it can continue or function again. After renewing, the thing is no longer expired, broken, or faded. Renewing changes the condition of a thing from worn to usable. Renewing changes the status of a thing from expired to valid. Renewing changes the state of a thing from inactive to active. Without renewing, things expire, decay, or end. With renewing, things persist instead. Renewing does not make something new in the sense of never existing before. It makes something feel or function new again. The result of renewing is that something can keep going. The result is continuation. The result is restoration. The result is a fresh start. Something that is renewed is not identical to what it was before it aged or faded. It is similar. It is good again. But it carries its history. Renewing adds time and care, not a new beginning from nothing.
