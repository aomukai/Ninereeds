The lamp glowed dim on the dresser. Emma curled up under the blanket, her pillow cool against her cheek. The moon shone through the gap in the curtains, casting a thin silver line across the floor.  

Gran bent down and tucked the edges tight. "Sleep, little wren," she whispered, so quiet Emma almost didn’t hear. Then the door clicked shut, and the room grew darker.  

Emma listened. The house creaked softly—the old wood settling, the wind brushing the apple tree outside. Biscuit sighed in his sleep by the kitchen hearth, a faint thump of his tail. Shadows stretched long on the walls, shifting as the lamp flickered.  

She yawned and pulled the blanket higher. The moon’s light touched the edge of her bed, pale and quiet. Somewhere far off, an owl called. Emma closed her eyes and let the dark wrap around her like another blanket.
