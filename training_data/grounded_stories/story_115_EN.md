Emma pressed the stick into the dirt beside the gatestone. The morning shadow stretched long and thin, its tip just brushing the mark she’d made. She ran her finger along the cool edge of the stone. It hadn’t moved.  

By afternoon, the sun had climbed high. Emma crouched again. The shadow had shrunk, curled like a cat by the stone’s base. She laid another stick where the tip ended now—left of the first, not right.  

Gran knelt by the bean wigwams, pinching off yellow leaves. Emma held out both sticks. “It moved.”  

Gran squinted at the sky. “Sun walks,” she said, and dropped a leaf into her apron pocket.  

Emma traced the shadow’s new path with her toe. The stone hadn’t shifted. The sticks hadn’t either. But the dark line between them had turned, slow as the hands on the kitchen clock. She watched it stretch longer as the sun dipped behind the apple tree.
