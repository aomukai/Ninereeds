Gran set two flour bags on the kitchen table. One was fat and squat. The other was tall and narrow.  

"Which feels heavier?" she said, wiping her hands on her apron. "Try both."  

Emma lifted the fat bag in her left hand, the tall one in her right. The left arm sank a little. She switched. The right arm stayed up.  

"This one pulls more," said Taro, grabbing the fat bag. It thumped back onto the table.  

Emma held them out again, arms wavering. The fat bag dragged her left hand down like a stone in a pocket. The tall one sat light as a loaf of bread.  

Gran watched, kneading dough. "Well?"  

"The short one," Emma said.  

"Mm." Gran flicked flour from her fingers. "Heavier."  

Taro poked the bags. "But it's smaller!"  

Gran smiled and turned the dough. "Not how it looks. How it feels."  

Outside, the apple tree dropped a windfall. Thud.
