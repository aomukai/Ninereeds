Taro burst through the doorway, his cheeks pink from the cold. Gran was at the table, shelling walnuts into a bowl.

"Any on the path?" she asked, not looking up.

Taro patted his coat pockets. The left one bulged with leaves. The right was flat and empty. He turned it inside out. Nothing.

Gran glanced at the bowl. "One, two, three. Same as before." She tapped the edge of the bowl with her thumb. "Three and nothing is still three. It is enough."

Taro peered over the table. The walnuts lay in a small pile, their shells cracked open like tiny mouths. He could smell the earthy scent of them, strong and sweet.

"Still?" he asked.

Gran nodded. "Still." She picked up another walnut, pressed it between her fingers until the shell gave way with a soft snap. The kernel fell into the bowl.

Taro stood still for a moment, watching. Then he shrugged off his coat and hung it on the hook by the door. The empty pocket swayed gently in the draft.
