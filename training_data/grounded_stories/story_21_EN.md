Emma pumped her legs hard, higher and higher. The swing creaked. The wind rushed past her ears. At the very top, she let go.  

For a second, she flew. Then her knees hit the sand, her hands scraped the ground. She sat up, blinking. Her palms stung.  

Taro watched from the top of the climbing frame. He didn’t move.  

Emma brushed the sand off her skirt. "That was fun," she said.  

Taro swung down from the rope. "You landed like a sack of potatoes."  

Emma scowled. "You try it."  

Taro ran to the swing. He kicked off, leaning back as it soared. At the highest point, he jumped—and stumbled forward, nearly falling. He caught himself on the bench.  

Emma laughed. "Like a sack of turnips!"  

Taro’s ears turned pink. He grabbed a handful of sand and threw it at her. Emma shrieked and ran.  

Biscuit barked and chased after them, his tail wagging. The school bell rang, but neither of them stopped.
