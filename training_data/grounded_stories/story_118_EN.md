Biscuit stopped mid-step. His ears pricked up, nose twitching toward the hedge.  

Emma tugged Taro’s sleeve. "Look."  

Biscuit’s tail stiffened. He took one slow step forward, then another, muscles taut under his brown fur.  

Taro crouched. "What is it?"  

Then Emma heard it—a rustle, light and quick, deep in the brambles. Biscuit’s head tilted. He already knew.  

Something moved again. A flicker of shadow between leaves. Biscuit’s nose led him closer, sniffing the air.  

"There," Emma whispered.  

A small shape darted—a mouse, perhaps, or a vole—gone before they could see it properly. Biscuit whuffed, pawing at the spot where the sound had been.  

Taro grinned. "He knew before we did."  

Biscuit circled the hedge, nose to the ground, ears alert. The children waited, but whatever it was had vanished into the thicket.  

Emma brushed a leaf from Biscuit’s back. "Next time," she said, "we’ll watch where you look first."  

Biscuit shook himself and trotted ahead, already scenting the next thing.
