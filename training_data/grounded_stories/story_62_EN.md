The light woke Emma first. It came in sharp and early, slanting across the ceiling. She stretched her arm up. The skylight was just out of reach. 

She sat up and found the stick by the bed. It was smooth from use. She pushed it against the latch once, twice. The skylight clicked open. Cool air slipped in, smelling of grass and something she couldn’t name — fresh, like dew. 

She leaned back on her elbows. A strip of blue sky filled the frame. At the edge, the apple tree’s tip swayed slightly. Leaves caught the light, turning it green and gold. 

“Emma?” Taro mumbled from the other bed. 

“Shh,” she said. “Listen.” 

He turned over. Outside, the garden was quiet except for the soft rustle of branches. The air was cool on her arms. She breathed in again. Morning was just beginning.
