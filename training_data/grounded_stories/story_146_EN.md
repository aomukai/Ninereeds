The fire crackled low in the hearth. On the rug before it, Biscuit lay on his side, legs stretched flat as if he’d melted into the wool. Bello was a tight curl beside him, nose tucked under his tail. Their ribs rose and fell in the warm, quiet room.  

Biscuit’s hind leg twitched—once, twice—paddling against nothing. A soft whuff escaped his muzzle. Gran glanced up from her mending. "Chasing rabbits," she murmured.  

Bello’s ear flicked. He opened one eye, dark and alert, and peered at Biscuit. The big dog’s paws stilled. Bello sighed, tucked his nose deeper into his fur, and closed his eye again.  

The fire settled. The clock ticked. Outside, the winter wind brushed the window, but inside, the room held its warmth like a cupped hand. Biscuit’s breathing deepened. Bello’s tail gave one tiny twitch of his own, then stilled.  

Gran threaded her needle and bent back to her work. The dogs slept on, together and apart, in their own soft worlds.
