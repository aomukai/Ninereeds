Emma stretched her arms wide against the edge of the kitchen table. "One span," she said, pressing her fingertips to the wood. She shuffled sideways. "Two spans—and a bit."  

Taro tried next. His arms barely reached. "Almost three of mine!"  

Gran looked up from kneading dough. Flour dusted her sleeves. "About two of yours," she said to Emma, "or a little more."  

Taro stretched again, wobbling. "Why’s yours two and mine’s almost three?"  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. "Your arms are shorter. Like measuring with different sticks."  

Emma walked around the table, trailing her fingers. "Does it change if you grow?"  

"Only until your arms stop growing." Gran pinched a corner of dough. "Then it’s always your span."  

Taro flopped onto the bench. "I want long arms."  

"Plenty of time," said Gran. The dough sighed under her palms.  

Outside, a blackbird sang. The table held the warm, quiet weight of their measuring.
