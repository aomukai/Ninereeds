Gran walked ahead, her basket swinging. The lane dipped and turned, and then she was gone—just like that, around the corner. Emma stopped. The stones under her feet were uneven, and the hedges pressed close on either side.  

"Wait," Taro said, but Emma was already stepping forward.  

One more step, and the corner opened like a door. There was Gran, right there, as if she’d been waiting all along. And beyond her—the village street, sudden and bright. The baker’s window full of loaves. The red post box, moss creeping up its side.  

"It’s here," Emma said.  

Taro came up behind her. "It was hidden," he said.  

Gran didn’t look back. She walked on, past the butcher’s striped awning, her shoes tapping on the stones. The lane was quiet again, just the sound of their steps and the hedges brushing close. But the village was ahead now, around the corner, where it had been all along.
