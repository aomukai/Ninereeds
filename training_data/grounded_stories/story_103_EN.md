Emma stood by the door with one boot on. The other was gone. She wiggled her bare toes against the cold stone.  

"Where did you last have both?" Gran called from the kitchen, her hands busy with dough.  

Emma thought. At the gate—she’d stopped to pull them on. Before that—the bench. She’d sat to tie the laces.  

She stepped outside, the gravel sharp under her sock. The garden was quiet, the apple tree dropping its last leaves. She walked past the vegetable patch, past the henhouse where the hens clucked softly. The bench stood against the cottage wall, its stone grey in the autumn light.  

She crouched. There, just under the edge where the wood met the wall, was the boot. It lay on its side, the laces trailing. She must have kicked it off without noticing.  

Gran appeared at the door, flour dusting her apron. "Found it?"  

Emma held up the boot. The leather was cool in her hands. She sat on the bench to put it on, the laces stiff between her fingers. The hens scratched in the dirt. Somewhere down the lane, a blackbird sang.
