Gran looked out the kitchen window while stirring the porridge. "Storm by afternoon," she said. The sky was still blue.  

Emma and Taro ran to check after breakfast. The first clouds were small and dark, crowding from the west. The wind lifted Biscuit's ears as he sniffed the lane.  

By lunchtime, the trees swayed. Gran brought in the washing. "Inside now," she said, latching the garden gate.  

The first drops hit the windows like pebbles. Then the rain came sideways, rattling the glass. Biscuit whined and pressed against Emma's legs. Upstairs, a loose tile clattered. Gran lit the lamp though it was only three o'clock.  

When the storm passed, the garden smelled of wet earth. A pear tree branch lay across the path, leaves still shaking. Puddles filled the dips in the lane, reflecting the clearing sky. Biscuit stepped in the deepest one and shook water everywhere.  

Gran picked up a broken twig. "Good kindling," she said, and went to put the kettle on.
