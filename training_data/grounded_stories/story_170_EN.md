The apples lay in a row on the kitchen table—seven, small and golden from the tree outside. Gran wiped her hands on her apron and looked at Emma and Taro.  

"Seven apples," she said. "Two each for three of us—that is six. One left."  

She picked them up one by one, pressing them into pairs. "Two for Emma." The first two thumped onto the table in front of her. "Two for Taro." Another pair slid across the wood. "Two for me." She set them beside her mixing bowl.  

"Six given out," she said. "One left."  

The last apple sat alone in the middle. Taro poked it. "One spare," he said.  

Gran turned back to the dough she was kneading. "Decide between you," she said, flour dusting her sleeves.  

Emma cupped the apple in her hands. It was warm from the sun, the skin smooth where the stem had pulled free. She held it out to Taro.  

He shook his head. "You keep it."  

Gran smiled but didn’t look up. The dough folded under her fingers, again and again, as the extra apple settled into Emma’s pocket.
