The meadow path was warm underfoot. Bello darted ahead, his black-and-white tail flicking through the tall grass like a flag. Biscuit followed, his nose low to the ground, pausing every few steps to sniff at a clump of clover or a turned-up stone.  

Emma walked slowly beside him. She noticed the grey around his muzzle, like frost creeping in at the edges. He was the same dog—still eager, still lifting his head when a bird startled from the grass—but his legs moved at a different pace now.  

Bello circled back, panting, and nudged Biscuit’s shoulder as if to hurry him along. Biscuit wagged his tail but didn’t speed up. He trotted a few steps, then stopped to sniff a patch of earth where the ants were busy.  

Emma crouched beside him. "You don’t have to rush," she said, scratching behind his ears. The sun pressed down on her back.  

Bello dashed ahead again, vanishing into the golden field. Biscuit watched him go, then turned his attention to a dandelion gone to seed. He blew it gently with his nose, and the seeds scattered like little parachutes.  

Emma stood, brushing off her knees. Biscuit fell into step beside her, his tail swaying like a slow metronome.
