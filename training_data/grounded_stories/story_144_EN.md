Emma unclipped Biscuit’s lead. Taro let go of Bello’s.  

For a breath, nothing happened. Then Biscuit lunged forward, a straight brown streak across the field. Bello shot after him, then veered left, right, doubling back to sniff a tuft of grass before darting ahead again.  

Biscuit ran like a rolling stone—powerful, unstoppable, his paws thudding the earth in a steady rhythm. Bello was all quick turns and sudden angles, his black-and-white body flickering like a shadow between the tall summer grass.  

Emma watched Biscuit reach the far fence first. Bello arrived a moment later, having traced a wild, looping path. He skidded to a stop beside Biscuit, panting, tongue lolling. Biscuit shook himself and trotted off toward the pond, already forgetting the race.  

"Bello ran farther," Taro said.  

Emma nodded. "But Biscuit ran straight."  

The dogs didn’t care. Bello was busy digging at a molehill. Biscuit had found a stick. The field smelled of warm grass and damp earth, and the sun pressed down on their shoulders like a heavy hand.
