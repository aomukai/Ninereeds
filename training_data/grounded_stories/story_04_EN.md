Emma stood on tiptoe under the oak. "Gran, I see twigs!"  

Gran shaded her eyes. "Higher up, in the fork."  

Taro scrambled onto the low branch. "There’s a nest! And—" He wobbled. "Eggs! Blue like the sky!"  

Three pale eggs nestled in the woven twigs. A feather stuck out from the side.  

"Too high to touch," Gran said, steadying Taro’s ankle. "But we can watch."  

Days later, chirps came from the oak. Emma ran barefoot across the field. A tiny beak gaped over the nest’s edge. Then another.  

"Gran, they’re *open*!"  

Gran knelt by the vegetable patch. "Parents are busy now. Worms all day."  

Taro crouched under the tree. A robin landed on a branch with a wriggling worm. The nest erupted in chirps.  

"Careful," Emma whispered, though the bird couldn’t hear. "Don’t fall."  

The robin tipped its head. The worm disappeared into a yellow beak.  

Biscuit sniffed at the oak’s roots. The children didn’t move.
