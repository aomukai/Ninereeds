Emma stood on tiptoe, fingers stretching toward the highest cluster. The blackberries glistened, fat and dark. Yesterday they’d been just out of reach. Today—  

"Quickly," Gran called from further down the hedgerow. "The birds know they’re ripe."  

Taro was already tugging at a low branch, popping berries straight into his mouth. Juice stained his chin purple. Biscuit nosed through the grass beneath, snuffling for fallen ones.  

Emma plucked a berry. It came away soft, leaving a smudge on her thumb. Sweetness burst on her tongue. She reached again, filling her palm before dropping them into the bowl at her feet. The ones at the top were the best—warm from the sun, almost sticky.  

Gran’s basket rustled as she moved down the line. "That’s half," she said, glancing at Emma’s bowl. "Leave some for tomorrow."  

But Emma knew better. Tomorrow the birds would have found them, or the berries would be overripe, sinking into mush at the slightest touch. She stretched higher, staining her fingers deeper purple. Now—or never.
