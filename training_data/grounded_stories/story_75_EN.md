The meadow path was quiet in the early morning. Emma stopped to pick up a feather from the grass.  

"Do you hear that?" Taro tilted his head.  

A sound hung in the air—thin, far away. The school bell.  

Emma dropped the feather. "We have to run."  

The bell came again, faint as a breath. They hurried, feet thudding on the damp earth. Biscuit bounded ahead, stirring up the scent of crushed leaves. The sound grew clearer with every step—no longer a whisper, but a proper ringing now, sharp and impatient.  

By the time they reached the gate, the bell was loud enough to make Taro wince. He shoved the stiff green wood with his shoulder. Emma slipped through after him, the sound suddenly close and clanging right above them.  

"It’s the same one," she said, panting.  

Taro grinned. "Just louder."  

The last note hung in the air as they dashed across the yard.
