Gran stood at the kitchen counter, rolling dough. "We need four apples for the cake," she said, dusting flour from her hands. Emma dashed outside to the garden. The apple tree stood heavy with fruit, its branches bending low. She reached up and plucked two apples, their skin cool and smooth. She carried them back inside and laid them on the counter.

"Only two," Gran said, glancing at them. "Not enough."

Taro grabbed the basket from the table. "Two plus two equals four," he said, swinging it over his arm. "Biscuit, come on!" The dog bounded after him, tail wagging, as they headed down the lane to the market.

The market was busy. Taro waited in line, holding the basket tight. The seller handed him two more apples, shiny and red. Biscuit sniffed them as they walked back.

When Taro returned, Emma lined the apples up on the counter. She touched each one. "One, two, three, four." She looked at Taro. "Two and two is four. You were right."

Gran picked up the apples. "Now for the cake," she said, slicing one open.
