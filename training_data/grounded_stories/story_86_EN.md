The grass was tall and warm under the sun. Biscuit ran first, his big paws thudding against the ground. Bello darted after him, quick as a shadow.  

For three strides they matched perfectly—shoulder to shoulder, ears flat back, legs stretching the same way. Their paws hit the ground at the same time. Emma held her breath.  

Then Bello veered left, chasing something only he could see. The moment broke. Biscuit kept running straight, his tongue lolling.  

Taro crouched in the grass, watching. He didn’t say anything. The dogs circled back, panting. Bello’s nose was dusty. Biscuit shook himself, sending flecks of dry earth into the air.  

"Again," Emma said softly.  

They ran. This time Bello was faster, his small legs a blur. Biscuit lumbered beside him for a step or two before falling behind. The grass swayed where they’d passed.  

Taro picked a stalk and chewed the sweet end. The dogs collapsed in the shade of the oak, side by side, their sides heaving. Close, but not quite parallel anymore.
