Emma stood in the kitchen doorway. The floorboards were yellow—not just brown, but gold where the light touched them. She hadn’t noticed that before. The air smelled of onions frying, sharp and sweet at once. Her stomach growled.  

Gran turned from the stove. She didn’t say *You’re better* or *How do you feel?* She slid a plate onto the table—bread, butter, a slice of cheese curling at the edges. Emma sat. The bread crackled under her fingers.  

Outside, the frost had melted from the windowpane. The world looked clearer, as if someone had wiped the glass with a sleeve. She could see the apple tree’s bare branches, the way they crossed like fingers against the sky.  

Gran put a cup beside the plate. Steam rose in a thin line. Emma wrapped her hands around it. The warmth seeped into her palms, up her wrists. She took a bite. The cheese was salty. The bread gave way.  

Gran sat down with her own cup. She blew across the top. The steam swirled and vanished. Emma watched it. She ate until the plate was empty.
