Emma stood on the oak’s thick root, her fingers brushing the rough bark. Above, the branches wove a net of new leaves.  

"Look," Gran said, pointing up.  

Emma tilted her head. High in the fork, where two branches split, a nest sat snug. A robin’s beak flashed as it tucked something inside.  

Taro crouched. "Down here!" Between the roots, where the earth dipped, a small hole gaped. Biscuit had sniffed at it last week.  

Gran nodded. "Squirrel’s larder."  

Emma pressed her palm to the trunk. The bark was cool and ridged. Up high, the nest. Down low, the hole. And here—her feet on the root, the tree solid between her hands—the middle.  

Taro scrambled onto the low branch, stretching. "I can almost see the eggs!"  

The robin darted away with a chirp.  

Gran chuckled. "Best leave her to it."  

Emma jumped down, landing in the soft moss beside the roots. Above, the nest. Below, the hole. And all around, the oak holding them up.
