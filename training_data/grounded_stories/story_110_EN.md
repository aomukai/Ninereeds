The oak stood bare against the grey sky, its branches black with winter damp. Emma’s boots crunched on the frozen ground as she followed Gran past the tree.  

Gran stopped. She reached up and touched a low branch. "Look here."  

Emma stood on tiptoe. At first she saw only the dark bark, the same as always. Then—tiny bumps, pale as a fingernail’s edge, dotted along the branch.  

"Bud scales," Gran said. Her thumb brushed one. "They’ll open when the light stays longer."  

Emma cupped her hands around a bud. It was no bigger than a ladybird, hard and green beneath its fringe of brown. She could snap it off with a pinch. But it clung fast, waiting.  

A wind shook the higher branches. The buds held on.  

Gran’s sleeve brushed Emma’s shoulder as she turned toward the lane. "They know," she said, and left the rest unsaid.  

Emma glanced back once. The oak stood quiet, its small green signals just beginning.
