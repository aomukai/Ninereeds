Emma and Taro stood at the edge of the field, toes brushing the first row of wheat. The stalks were tall and golden, heavy with grain. A warm wind sent ripples through them, like water.  

The cutter rumbled far off, moving in slow, straight lines.  

"Watch the middle," Gran said, wiping her forehead with her sleeve. She held a bundle of cut wheat under one arm. "Everything hiding in there will have to run when it gets close."  

Emma squinted. The field was dusty, the air thick with chaff. It tickled her nose.  

A rabbit burst from the wheat, then another. A flock of sparrows lifted all at once, wings beating. Taro pointed as a fox slipped along the edge, low and quick.  

"Help me carry this," Gran said, handing Emma a smaller bundle. The stalks scratched her arms. They smelled warm and dry.  

Biscuit barked at the fleeing animals but didn’t chase. The cutter turned again, and the wheat fell in neat rows behind it.
