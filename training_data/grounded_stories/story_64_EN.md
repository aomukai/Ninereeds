The radiator clicked and hissed in the corner. Emma pressed her palm against it—warm iron, almost too hot to touch. Outside, the schoolyard was grey with frost. A wind shook the bare branches of the ash tree.  

Taro blew on his fingers, then rubbed them together. "Miss Jenkins forgot to close the window again," he muttered. The latch never quite caught. A draft crept along the floor.  

Emma ran her finger over the desk. Someone had carved **J.M.** deep into the wood, the edges worn smooth. She wondered who J.M. was. If they’d sat here on a winter morning like this, listening to the same chalk squeak on the board.  

The teacher wrote **12 + 15 =** in large, careful numbers. Dust drifted from the ledge. The room smelled of old wood and wool coats drying—warm and close, like a cupboard full of mittens.  

In the corridor, coat hooks lined the wall. Emma’s card had a smudged **E** where she’d pressed her pencil too hard. Taro’s swung slightly, knocked by someone rushing past.  

The bell rang. Boots clattered in the yard. But inside, the radiator hummed, and the chalk kept writing.
