The sitting room was cold. Emma pulled the brown blanket up to her chin, but her toes still felt the winter air. Gran came in with an armful of firewood. She glanced at Emma, then set the wood down and took the other end of the blanket.  

"Same blanket—folded in half," Gran said. She doubled it over Emma's legs. "Two thicknesses now."  

Emma pressed her hands into the layers. One side was heavy and warm; the other, thin where it had been before. "It is heavier on this side," she said.  

Gran tucked the edges under. "Half the size, twice as warm."  

Outside, the wind rattled the window. The fire popped in the grate. Emma curled into the folded blanket, the weight of it holding the heat close. Gran brushed a hand over her forehead, then went back to stacking wood. The room smelled of pine and wool.  

Emma wiggled her toes. No cold air reached them now.
