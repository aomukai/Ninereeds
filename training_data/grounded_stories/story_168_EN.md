Emma held up both hands, fingers spread. "Ten," she said, pressing one thumb down. The grass tickled her ankles.  

Taro crouched low, one foot forward. "Hurry up," he muttered, but Emma kept going.  

"Nine." Another finger folded. The oak tree stood at the far end of the field, its leaves rustling.  

"Eight." A breeze carried the smell of warm earth.  

Taro shifted, ready to spring. Biscuit panted beside him, tail wagging.  

"Seven." Emma’s voice was steady. "Six."  

"Come *on*," Taro said, bouncing on his toes.  

"Five." She closed her fist halfway. "Four." A ladybug landed on her wrist and stayed.  

"Three." Taro leaned forward.  

"Two." The ladybug flew off.  

"One—"  

Taro was already moving before she finished. "Go!" Emma shouted, and he shot across the field, Biscuit barking at his heels. The numbers hung in the air behind them—ten, nine, eight—all the way down to zero, the signal dissolving into the sound of running feet and laughter.
