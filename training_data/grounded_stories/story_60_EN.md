The afternoon sun warmed the stone bench by the kitchen door. Gran sat with a bowl in her lap, her fingers working through a tangle of green beans. She pinched the stem end between her nails—snap—then the tip—snap—and dropped the trimmed bean into the bowl. The sound was crisp and regular, like a slow clock.  

Biscuit lay sprawled at her feet, his belly pressed into a patch of warm earth. His paws twitched once, chasing something in a dream, then stilled. A bee hummed past, heavy with pollen, and disappeared into the bean wigwams.  

Gran didn’t hurry. The bowl filled slowly, bean by bean. The shadow of the apple tree stretched toward the bench but hadn’t reached it yet. Biscuit sighed in his sleep, his tail thumping once against the ground.  

Somewhere beyond the garden wall, a bird called, high and clear. Gran didn’t look up. She snapped another bean, then another, and the bowl grew heavier in her lap.
