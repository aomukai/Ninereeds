The lane was narrow, the hedges brushing their sleeves as they walked. Emma looked back. Gran’s chimney rose above the cottage roof, and beyond it, the oak stood dark against the field.  

"Come on," said Taro, already ahead.  

The stones underfoot were smooth from years of feet. Halfway down, the lane dipped. Taro stepped right into the puddle without looking. Water splashed his shoes.  

"Taro!" Emma said.  

He shook his foot. "It’s not deep."  

Gran didn’t turn. "Keep walking. They’ll dry."  

Around the last corner, the smell reached them first—warm bread, sweet and yeasty. The baker’s shop appeared suddenly, the lane opening onto the street as if someone had lifted a curtain.  

Emma breathed in. "We’re here."  

Taro’s wet shoe made a soft sound on the stones. The oak was far behind them now, just a shape at the edge of the world.
