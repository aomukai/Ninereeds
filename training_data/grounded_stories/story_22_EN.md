Emma’s pencil rolled off the bench and clattered through the grate. She knelt, fingers pressing against the cold metal. The pencil lay just out of reach.  

Taro dug in his bag. "Here," he said, holding out a stubby blue one. "You can borrow it."  

Emma took it. The wood was smooth where his teeth had gnawed it. The bell rang before she could thank him properly.  

At lunch, she found an orange pencil in her pocket—the one Taro had lent her last week. She’d forgotten to give it back. The gate creaked as she pushed through to the yard. Taro was drawing lines in the dirt with a stick.  

She held out the orange pencil. "I found it."  

He blinked, then grinned. "Oh. Yeah." He tucked it behind his ear.  

The bell rang again. Emma’s borrowed blue pencil poked out of her bag. Taro was already running ahead.
