Emma heard the bark first — sharp and quick, not Biscuit’s deep woof. She ran to the gate. A small dog stood there, panting. His fur was black and white, his ears perked up like little flags. Biscuit trotted over, nose twitching. He circled the new dog slowly, sniffing. The little dog wagged his tail but stayed still, watching.

"Gran, look!" Emma called. "There’s a new dog!"

Gran came out, wiping her hands on her apron. "Well, hello," she said. "Where did you come from?"

Biscuit sniffed again, then gave a short bark. The little dog barked back, higher and faster. Suddenly, Biscuit dashed across the garden. The small dog darted after him, weaving between Gran’s feet.

Emma laughed. "Biscuit’s running with him!"

Gran smiled. "One dog, two dogs." She counted on her fingers. "We had one dog. Now we have two."

The dogs raced around the apple tree, tails wagging. Biscuit’s tongue flopped out. The little dog’s paws kicked up tufts of grass. Emma watched them chase each other across the garden, laughing as they disappeared behind the shed. "One and one," she said. "Twice the trouble!"
