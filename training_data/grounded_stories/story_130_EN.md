Taro leaned across the table, his elbow nudging the jar. It wobbled once, twice—  

Emma’s hand shot out, fingers stretching. Too late. The jar tipped.  

Water hit the floor with a slap. It spread fast, thin fingers reaching under the table, over the tiles, toward Gran’s shoes.  

Gran looked up from the dough. Her hands kept kneading.  

Taro froze. Emma’s palm still hovered where the jar had been.  

The water kept moving. It found the cracks between the tiles, the dip near the hearth, the edge of the rug. A little river, already shrinking into the wood.  

Gran reached for the cloth by the sink. “Next time,” she said, wringing it over the bowl, “move the jar first.”  

She tossed the damp cloth to Emma. Taro scrambled for the mop in the corner. The dough waited, patient, on the board.
