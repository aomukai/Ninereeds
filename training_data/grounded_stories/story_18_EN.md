Emma knelt in the tall grass, her fingers brushing the fuzzy head of a dandelion gone to seed. She lifted it, blew gently, and the white tufts floated away like tiny parachutes. Taro had already run ahead, but now he crouched near the path’s edge, his nose almost touching a broad green leaf.  

"Look," he whispered.  

Emma crept closer. A fat green caterpillar inched along the leaf, its body rippling as it moved. Taro held out his hand, letting it crawl onto his palm.  

"Too sunny here," he said. He carried it carefully to a shady clump of milkweed, where the leaves were bigger and softer. The caterpillar curled for a moment, then stretched forward, nibbling at the edge.  

A butterfly drifted past—orange wings bright against the blue sky. It landed on a purple flower, probed for nectar, then lifted again, floating over the meadow. Emma watched until it vanished into the warm, swaying grass.  

Biscuit barked from farther down the path. The pond was still waiting.
