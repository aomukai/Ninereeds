Emma picked up the apple. It was warm from the sun, smooth except for one brown spot. She turned it over in her hands.  

Gran had said last week, when the first windfalls came: *The ones with brown spots are already going soft inside.*  

Emma pressed her thumb gently against the spot. It gave a little, like dough. She sniffed. Not sweet like the good ones—a faint sourness, like the time the milk turned.  

She dropped it into the compost bucket. The next apple was perfect, round and red with no marks at all. That one went into the basket.  

Gran came down the path with the fork over her shoulder. She glanced at the buckets. "Got the hang of it now?"  

Emma nodded. "The spot was right where you said."  

Gran lifted the lid of the compost bin. The apple lay on top of the dark heap, already sinking a little. "Turns out quick," she said, and let the lid fall.
