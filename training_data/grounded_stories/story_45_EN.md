Emma stood on tiptoe under the pear tree. The branches spread wide and empty above her. She checked the low ones first, running her fingers along the rough bark where fruit might hide. Nothing. She climbed onto the stone bench to see higher. A spider scuttled away. A leaf brushed her cheek.  

Gran’s voice came from the kitchen window. "How many?"  

Emma scanned the top branches. "None."  

"None is zero," Gran said. She was kneading dough; flour dusted her elbows. "Zero is the answer. Zero is a number."  

A breeze shook the leaves. Emma jumped down and wiped her hands on her skirt. The grass under the tree was dry and prickly. No fallen pears, not even green ones.  

She came back to the kitchen. Gran’s hands moved in the dough, pushing and folding.  

"Zero pears," Emma said.  

Gran nodded. "Next year, maybe." The dough made a soft thump against the bowl. Outside, the empty branches swayed.
