The fire crackled in the hearth. Gran sat in her chair, knitting needles clicking. Biscuit lay across her lap, a warm brown weight, his chin propped on her knee.  

Bello trotted in. He stopped. His ears twitched.  

He put one paw on Gran’s slipper. Then another. He tried to climb up, but Biscuit’s tail was in the way. He nudged it aside with his nose.  

Gran didn’t look up. “There’s only one lap,” she said.  

Bello tried half-on, his front legs draped over Gran’s thigh. Biscuit sighed and shifted, pressing closer to Gran’s middle. Bello’s back legs slid off.  

He tried lying across both their legs, but Biscuit’s elbow dug into his ribs. He tried wedging his head between Gran’s arm and the chair, but the knitting needles tickled his ear.  

Finally he flopped onto the rug at Gran’s feet with a huff. The fire warmed his back. Gran’s slipper was soft under his cheek.  

Biscuit yawned. The needles clicked. Bello closed his eyes.
