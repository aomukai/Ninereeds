The morning pond was still as glass. Emma knelt on the flat stone, her shadow barely touching the water. Below, dark shapes hovered near the surface—five, no, six—their tails flicking lazily in the clear light.  

"See?" she whispered. "They're right there."  

Taro leaned over, squinting. "Same fish as yesterday."  

A cloud passed. The fish vanished.  

After lunch, the pond had changed. The sun stood higher; the water held the sky at a sharper angle. Emma pressed her palms to the warm stone. "Where are they?"  

Taro pointed to a deeper patch. "Down there. Just harder to see."  

She waited. A ripple—maybe a fin—but nothing clear. The fish were grey smudges now, half-hidden in the wavering light.  

"They were closer this morning," she said.  

Taro shrugged. "Same fish."  

A breeze crossed the pond. The reflections shattered. The fish, if they were there at all, were gone.
