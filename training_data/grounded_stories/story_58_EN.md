The bell jingled as Gran pushed the door open. The smell rushed out to meet them — warm and yeasty, with a crusty edge that made Emma’s mouth water. Inside, golden loaves lined the counter, their tops split and shiny. Smaller rolls sat in baskets, some dusted with flour.  

The baker wiped his hands on his apron. "Morning, Mrs. Hale."  

"One of the brown, please," Gran said.  

He reached for a loaf, still warm from the oven, and wrapped it in crisp white paper. The ends twisted tight under his fingers.  

"Here you go, miss." He held it out to Emma.  

She took it with both hands. The weight settled into her arms, the warmth seeping through the paper. She could feel the crust through the wrapping — rough in some places, smooth in others.  

Gran paid, and the coins clinked into the till.  

"Mind the step," Gran said as they turned to go.  

Emma hugged the loaf close. The bell rang again behind them, and the smell of bread followed them all the way down the lane.
