The trail up the hill was steep and full of loose rocks. Emma stepped carefully, holding onto tufts of grass when the ground slid under her feet. Taro scrambled ahead, sending small stones clattering down. Biscuit bounded between them, panting.  

"Wait here," Gran said when they reached a flat rock. She pulled a water flask from her bag. The wind tugged at Emma's hair. She could see the whole valley from here — the fields like green and gold squares, the thin line of the meadow path.  

"Higher!" Taro said, already moving.  

The last stretch was the hardest. Emma's legs felt tired, but then they were at the top. The wind was stronger here. Gran's cottage looked very small below. Emma squinted and found the oak tree, a dark smudge near the edge of the field.  

Biscuit flopped onto his side, tongue lolling. Taro picked up a stick and tossed it downhill. It bounced twice and disappeared into the grass.  

"Coming?" he called, already starting down.
