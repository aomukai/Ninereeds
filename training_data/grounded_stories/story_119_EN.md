Emma pressed her fingers into the dough. It was soft and cool, like the clay by the pond. Gran folded the edges over, tucked them under, and set the lump in the same bowl they'd mixed it in.  

"Now we wait," she said, draping a cloth over the top. "And watch."  

Taro leaned on the table. "Watch what?"  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron and didn't answer.  

An hour later, the cloth had lifted. The dough pushed against it, round and puffy. Emma touched it lightly. The dent filled in slowly, like Biscuit’s fur when she smoothed it the wrong way.  

"It’s bigger," Taro said.  

Gran nodded. "Yeast does that. Warmth wakes it up." She poked the dough herself. It sighed under her finger.  

"Like breathing," Emma said.  

"In a manner of speaking." Gran lifted the cloth higher. The dough nearly reached the bowl’s rim now, soft and alive and waiting.  

Taro blew on it, just to see.
