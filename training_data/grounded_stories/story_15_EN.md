The fire had burned low. Only embers glowed in the dark. Emma watched the last log crumble into ash.  

Gran knelt by the hearth. "One more," she said, and laid a new log across the coals. It caught with a crackle. Flame licked up the bark.  

Taro lay on his stomach, chin in hands. The firelight flickered on his face. "It's like a little house burning," he said.  

Shadows danced on the ceiling. The room smelled of woodsmoke and warm wool. Emma curled into Gran's lap. Outside, the wind pushed at the windows.  

The candle on the table burned lower. Its flame wavered, making the shadows stretch and fade. Gran hummed something quiet.  

Taro poked at the edge of the rug with one finger. "Will it last till morning?"  

"Long enough," said Gran. She brushed a bit of bark from his hair. The fire popped, sending up a shower of sparks.
