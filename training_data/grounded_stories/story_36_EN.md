Taro ran to the hedgerow first. He pushed through the gate, his hands already reaching. The berry bushes were thick with purple clusters. He grabbed a handful, careful not to squash them. The juice stained his fingers.

“Three!” he called, dropping them into Gran’s bowl.

Emma waited by the garden gate. She looked at the bushes Taro had left. Two ripe berries hung low, almost hidden. She picked them gently. “Two more,” she said, adding them to the bowl.

Gran tipped the berries onto the table. She spread them out with her fingers. “One, two, three, four, five. Yes.” She looked up. “Three and two, five. Same as the other way.”

The berries sat in a small heap, their purple skins shiny in the sunlight. Gran took the bowl and poured them into the pot. The kitchen smelled sweet already.

Taro leaned on the gate, watching. “Can we pick more?”

“Not yet,” Gran said, stirring. “These will do for now.”
