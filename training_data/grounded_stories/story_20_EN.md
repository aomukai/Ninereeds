Taro ran ahead, his feet kicking up dust from the meadow path. The grass swayed tall on either side, hiding the flutter of butterflies. Then—thud. He sprawled forward, hands out, knee scraping the rough dirt.  

Emma crouched beside him. "Let me see."  

Taro held out his palms. Pink scrapes dotted them, grains of dirt pressed into the skin. His knee was worse—a raw patch, edges flecked with tiny stones.  

"It stings," he muttered, flexing his fingers.  

Emma touched his elbow. "There’s the stream by the pond."  

They pushed through the grass to the water’s edge. Taro knelt on the flat stone and plunged his hands in. The cold made him gasp. He pressed his palms to the smooth pebbles below, letting the current rinse the dirt away. Emma dipped her fingers too, then cupped a little in her palm and dabbed it gently on his knee.  

Biscuit sniffed at the mud on Taro’s shoe, tail wagging. Overhead, a dragonfly skimmed the water.
