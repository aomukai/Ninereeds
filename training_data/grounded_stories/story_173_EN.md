Emma stood with her head tilted back. The shed roof stretched above her, covered in rows of red-brown tiles.  

"Let's count them," she said.  

Taro squinted. "Count the rows and how many in each row. Then you multiply."  

Emma pointed. "One, two, three..." Her finger wavered where the roof met the wall. "Seven." She tried counting the rows next, but the tiles blurred together. "...I think eight rows."  

Taro frowned. "Seven rows of seven... that is a lot."  

Emma lost track halfway through the second row. She started over, then gave up. "Too many."  

Biscuit nosed at her knee, impatient. The tiles baked warm in the sun, their edges rough where the moss grew.  

Taro crouched to pick up a stick. "Maybe if we—"  

But Emma was already walking toward the apple tree, the big number left behind on the roof.
