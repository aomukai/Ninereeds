The kitchen smelled of warm butter. Emma stood on her toes to peer into the bowl where Taro stirred flour and sugar. His nose was dusted white.  

"Your turn," Gran said, and Emma reached for the dough. It was soft and cool under her fingers. She pinched off little lumps and rolled them into balls—some round, some lopsided. Taro poked one.  

"Stop opening the oven," Gran said without turning from the sink.  

But they couldn’t help it. They crouched by the door, watching through the glass. The cookies puffed up, golden at the edges. The smell grew sweeter.  

At last Gran pulled them out. The tray hissed.  

"Too hot," she warned, but Taro reached anyway. He yanked his hand back and blew on his fingers. Emma laughed.  

They waited, shifting from foot to foot, until Gran nodded. The first bite crumbled. Taro’s cheeks bulged. Emma licked sugar from her thumb. Outside, the apple tree rustled in the breeze.
