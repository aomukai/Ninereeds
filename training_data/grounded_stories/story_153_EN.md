Emma held the bowl carefully. The berries shone dark red against the white china. Five.  

"One each," said Taro, reaching.  

Gran shook her head. "Count them."  

Taro poked the berries with a finger. "One, two, three, four, five." He frowned. "Five between two—two each and one left over."  

Emma touched the smallest berry. "Someone gets three."  

Gran tipped the bowl so the berries rolled. "One, two, three, four, five. One extra."  

The sun warmed Emma's neck. The berries smelled sweet and sharp.  

"Not quite fair," said Taro.  

Gran picked up the bowl. "Who gets the remainder?"  

Emma looked at Taro. Taro looked at the grass.  

Gran set the bowl between them. "Eat two. Then decide."  

They ate slowly. One berry left.  

Biscuit nosed Emma's knee.  

Gran smiled. "There's always one extra somewhere."  

The last berry went to the dog.
