The cookies sat cooling on the rack, their edges crisp and golden. Gran lifted them one by one onto the blue plate. Emma leaned in close, catching the warm smell of sugar. Taro bounced on his toes.  

"One for Emma," Gran said, placing the first cookie before her. "One for Taro." The second landed in front of him. She reached for the third. "One for Emma." Then the last. "One for Taro."  

Taro looked at his two, then at Emma’s two. "Two each," he said.  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. "Four cookies, two children — two each. That is fair."  

Emma picked hers up carefully. It was still warm at the bottom. Taro took a bite at once, scattering crumbs. Outside, a bird called from the apple tree. Gran turned back to the sink, and the tap ran cold over the empty baking tray.
