The stone was warm under Emma’s legs. Taro squeezed in beside her, his shoulder touching hers. The edge was just wide enough for them both if they sat very still.  

Emma’s toes dipped into the water. Tiny ripples spread out and disappeared. Taro stretched his legs, but his feet hovered just above the surface.  

A fish darted under the stone, quick and dark. Neither of them spoke. The quiet was the kind that felt close, like the air before rain.  

Biscuit splashed at the reeds, then shook himself off nearby. Drops landed on Emma’s ankles. Taro shifted, his elbow bumping hers.  

The sun moved lower. The water turned gold where it touched the sky. Emma wiggled her toes again. Taro leaned forward, watching the same small waves.  

Gran called from the bench under the willow, but neither of them got up right away. The stone held them a little longer.
