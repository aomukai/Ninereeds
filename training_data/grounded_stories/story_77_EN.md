The lane dipped steeply at the bottom, the stones worn smooth by rain. Emma gripped Gran’s hand as they reached the corner where the lane met the street.  

"Left for the baker," Gran said, pointing. "Right for the post box."  

Emma looked left—the warm smell of bread curled toward them. Right was the red post box, half-hidden by ivy. She tugged Gran’s sleeve. "Left."  

The baker’s window gleamed with golden loaves. Gran bought one, still warm, and tucked it into her basket. On the way back, Emma held up her hands, studying them. She turned them over. Which was left? The one with the thumb this way? She wiggled her fingers, testing.  

Gran didn’t say anything. She just waited while Emma frowned at her palms, then at the lane ahead. The post box was on the... other side now. Emma clenched her right hand into a fist, then her left. The bread smell lingered behind them.  

"Ready?" Gran asked.  

Emma nodded and reached for her hand again. She kept her left thumb tucked tight all the way home.
