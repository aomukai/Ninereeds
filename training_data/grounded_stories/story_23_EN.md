Gran spread the blanket by the pond. The grass was warm under Emma’s knees. Taro plopped down and kicked off his sandals. Biscuit circled, nose twitching at the basket.  

Gran lifted out the bread first—a round loaf with a crackly crust. Then came the cheese, wrapped in wax paper, and a little bottle of apple juice. Emma unpacked the cups while Taro poked at a blackberry stain on his sleeve.  

A tiny ant crawled over the cheese before Gran saw it. She flicked it away with her thumb. "Quick hands," she said, breaking the bread in half.  

Biscuit sat so close his whiskers brushed Gran’s elbow. He didn’t whine. He didn’t have to. Gran sighed and gave him the heel of the loaf.  

Taro spilled his juice reaching for cheese. The drops darkened the blanket in little circles. Emma licked berry juice from her fingers. The willow leaves trailed in the pond, and the ducks watched from the reeds. Biscuit crunched his prize loudly, tail thumping the ground.
