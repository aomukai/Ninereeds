The bowl sat on the kitchen table, four apples shining in the afternoon light. Emma picked one up. It was cool and smooth, with a little dent near the stem. She took a bite. Juice ran down her chin.  

Taro reached past her and grabbed another. His teeth crunched into it. "Gran said not to eat them all," he mumbled, mouth full.  

Gran came in from the garden, her hands dusty. She looked at the bowl.  

Taro wiped his sleeve across his mouth. "One, two." He poked the remaining apples. "Only two."  

Gran lifted the bowl. "There were four."  

"Four, take away two—two left." Taro scuffed his shoe against the floor. "We ate too many."  

Gran set the bowl down. The two apples rolled to one side. She sighed and reached for her basket by the door. "Best pick more, then." The screen door creaked shut behind her.  

Emma licked her sticky fingers. The kitchen smelled of earth and apples. Outside, the tree branches rustled.
