The jar was heavy with pond water. Emma held it steady while Taro scooped the tadpoles in, one by one. They were small and dark, their tails flicking as they darted between his fingers.  

"Careful," Emma said.  

Taro cradled the jar against his chest all the way down the meadow path. He walked slowly, watching the water slosh against the glass. The tadpoles swirled in little circles, their gills pulsing.  

The classroom smelled of chalk and old wood. Emma cleared a space on the nature table by the window. Sunlight spilled through the glass, turning the water gold.  

"Look," someone whispered.  

The whole class gathered around. The tadpoles drifted near the surface, tails curling, then darted down again.  

"Can we feed them?" asked a boy.  

The teacher smiled. "Tomorrow."  

Emma pressed her nose close to the jar. One tadpole paused, its tiny body still except for the quick beat of its gills. Then it flicked away, vanishing into the shadow at the bottom.
