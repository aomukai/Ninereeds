Emma carried the basket to the henhouse. The door creaked. Inside smelled warm—straw and feathers and something dry.  

Two hens clucked near the feed tray. The third still sat in the corner nest, her feathers puffed up. Emma crouched. "Shoo," she whispered. The hen blinked but didn’t move.  

Gran’s voice came from the garden. "Reach under slow."  

Emma slid her fingers through the straw. The hen’s belly was warm. One egg—smooth, still damp. Another beside it. She tucked them into the basket.  

Something white glinted in the corner. A third egg, cold against the dirt floor. Emma picked it up. No cracks. She added it to the others.  

The hen settled back into the hollow of her nest. Outside, Gran’s footsteps crunched on the gravel path. Emma held the basket carefully, feeling the weight shift—one, two, three.
