The kitchen smelled of warm bread and yeast. Emma stood on tiptoe to watch Gran knead the dough, her hands pressing and turning.  

"Will you bake again tomorrow?" Emma asked.  

Gran shook her head. "Not tomorrow. The day after market day."  

Emma frowned. "Which day is that?"  

"Seven days in a week," Gran said, dusting flour from her fingers. "I bake on the same one every week."  

Emma counted on her fingers. "Today is... then tomorrow is... that is five days." She looked up. "Five sleeps."  

Gran nodded and folded the dough again. "Five sleeps, and then the flour comes out, just like today."  

Emma leaned against the table, watching the dough rise under its cloth. The kitchen clock ticked. Outside, a bird called the same call it always did in the afternoons.  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. "Same day, same bread," she said. "Every week."  

Emma pressed a finger into the flour left on the table. It made a small, soft mark.
