Gran tipped the apples onto the kitchen table. They rolled and settled — one, two, three — until seven lay in a loose circle. She wiped her hands on her apron.  

"Seven on the table," she said. "How many more to make ten?"  

Emma touched each apple with her finger. "Eight, nine, ten—" She held up three fingers. "Three more."  

Taro leaned over the table. "We’re three short of ten." He reached for the basket. "I’ll get them."  

Gran nodded. "Mind the step."  

The back door banged. Emma lined up the apples by size. The smallest had a brown spot near the stem. She turned it to hide the mark.  

Taro returned, his arms full. Three apples tumbled onto the table. One hit the edge and spun.  

Gran caught it before it fell. "Ten," she said, and set it with the others. The last one made the circle complete.  

The oven timer rang. Gran lifted the pie from the shelf, golden and steaming. The apples waited in their tidy row.
