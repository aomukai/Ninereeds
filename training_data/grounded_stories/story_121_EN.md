The grass tickled Emma’s ankles as she stood at the edge of Gran’s field. Taro was a small shape at the far end, waving his arms. His voice reached her in pieces—a shout, then nothing, then another shout, like a bird calling from the trees.  

“What?” she called back.  

His answer was faint, just the shape of a word without the sound. She took a step forward. The wind rustled the grass between them.  

“Emma!” Closer now, but still not clear.  

She walked faster. His voice grew louder with every step—not all at once, but like a tune coming into focus. By the time she could make out the words, she was close enough to see the dirt on his knees.  

“Found a frog!” he said, grinning.  

She crouched beside him. The frog sat still in his cupped hands, its throat pulsing. Taro’s voice was loud now, right in her ear.  

“See?”  

She nodded. The field stretched behind them, quiet except for the wind.
