The pond lay still as glass. Emma crouched by the flat stone and saw the sky in the water—a blue upside-down bowl with clouds floating across it. A dragonfly skimmed the surface, leaving tiny ripples where it touched.  

Taro pulled off his shoes. "Watch this." He stepped in, sending mud swirling up from the bottom. The water reached his knees. A frog plopped off a lily pad before he could get close.  

Biscuit bounded past them and waded in up to his belly. He stood very still, tail wagging, as the ducks flapped away into the reeds.  

Emma dipped her fingers in. The water was warm at the top, cooler underneath. She cupped her hands and tried to catch a tadpole, but it slipped through.  

"Look," Taro whispered. A frog sat on the edge of a lily pad, its throat pulsing. Emma held her breath. Biscuit shook himself, spraying drops everywhere, and the frog vanished with a splash.
