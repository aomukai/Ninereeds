Emma was pulling weeds in the vegetable patch when Biscuit went still. His nose twitched. His tail stiffened.  

She looked up.  

Under the bean canes, half-hidden by leaves, sat a rabbit. Its ears were up. Its sides moved fast.  

Biscuit didn’t bark. He didn’t lunge. He stood frozen, one paw lifted, as if the air had turned to stone.  

The rabbit’s nose quivered. It took one hop toward the cabbages.  

Then—  

A bird called from the apple tree. The rabbit’s head jerked. Its back legs tensed.  

Gone.  

Just like that. A brown streak under the gate, quick as a breath. The gap was barely wider than Emma’s hand.  

Biscuit blinked. He sniffed the ground where the rabbit had been. He looked at Emma, then back at the gate, as if waiting for an explanation.  

Gran’s voice came from the bench. “Visitor,” she said, and snapped a bean in two.  

The garden was still again. Only the bean leaves trembled where the rabbit had passed.
