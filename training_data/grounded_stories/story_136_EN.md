Biscuit bounded ahead, paws kicking up dust from the path. The pond lay still ahead, the willow’s shadow stretching across it. Without slowing, he launched himself off the flat stone—a great, arcing leap—and hit the water with a splash that sent ripples to the reeds.  

Bello skidded to a stop at the edge. His ears pricked. Biscuit was already paddling, sending up little waves with his nose.  

"Go on," said Emma.  

Bello took two quick steps back, then forward—not a jump so much as a scramble, all legs and surprise. He hit the water with a smaller splash, surfaced blinking, and began to dog-paddle furiously toward Biscuit.  

Taro laughed. "He didn’t even think!"  

The two dogs circled each other, stirring up the pondweed. Bello’s ears were slicked back, his tail a frantic rudder. He looked startled, as if the water had grabbed him first.  

When they clambered out, Biscuit shook hard, spraying drops everywhere. Bello copied him, wobbling on his paws. They stood side by side on the grass, dripping and panting, the same shape but different sizes.  

Emma wrung out her sleeve. "Next time," she said, "maybe don’t stand so close."
