The apple tree bent low with fruit. Red apples hung heavy on every branch.  

Gran held the ladder steady. "Twist, don’t pull," she said, her hands firm on the rungs.  

Taro stretched high. His fingers brushed the biggest apple. He gave it a twist. It came free with a soft snap.  

Below, Emma gathered windfalls from the grass. Each one made a quiet thud as she dropped it into the basket. Some were bruised. Some still warm from the sun.  

Gran reached up and plucked one from a low branch. She turned it in her palm. The skin was smooth, the color deep red where the sun had touched it.  

Taro climbed down, his pockets bulging. "Look," he said, holding out an apple with a single leaf still clinging to the stem.  

Emma bit into one. Juice ran down her chin. The core, when she turned it over, showed five tiny seeds nestled tight in the white.  

Biscuit nosed at the fallen leaves, sniffing for the ones that smelled sweetest.
