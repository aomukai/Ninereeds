Emma opened her eyes. The room was bright—too bright—but not the yellow, thick brightness of the sick days. This light was sharp, clear. The blanket lay heavy on her legs, but her head felt light.  

Downstairs, the kitchen smelled of toast. Her stomach growled.  

Gran looked up when Emma reached the bottom step. "Hungry?"  

Emma nodded. She ate slowly, the bread warm in her hands.  

By afternoon, she stood at the kitchen window. The garden was winter-bare, the apple tree’s branches black against the sky. A cold wind shook the last leaves from the hedge. Yesterday, she hadn’t noticed the wind. Yesterday, the world had been muffled.  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. "Not yet," she said, without turning. "Tomorrow."  

Emma pressed her palm to the glass. It was cold. Outside, the light was fading. The garden waited.
