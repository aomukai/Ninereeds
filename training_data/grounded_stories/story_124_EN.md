Emma stood under the oak, arms outstretched. Taro climbed onto the low branch above her, an acorn in one hand, a dry leaf in the other.  

"Ready?" he called.  

The acorn fell straight down—*thock*—into the grass. The leaf spun sideways, caught a breeze, and landed near the roots.  

Emma picked up the acorn. It was smooth and heavy. She found a feather by the squirrel’s hole, grey with a white tip. "Try this."  

Taro dropped it from the branch. The feather drifted slower than the leaf, swaying side to side, and settled on Emma’s sleeve.  

Biscuit nosed the acorn, then sneezed at the feather.  

"Again," Emma said.  

Taro threw the leaf high. It spun. The feather fluttered. The acorn fell straight every time.  

The wind gusted. The feather lifted, then dropped behind them. Biscuit bounded after it, paws crushing dry leaves.  

Emma held out her hand. "Once more."
