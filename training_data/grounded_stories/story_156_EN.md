Emma stood at the garden gate. "Let's count steps to the pond."  

Taro bounced on his toes. "Go!"  

They started together. "One, two, three—"  

By ten, Taro was ahead. His legs swung wide. "Twenty-three," he called, already at the hedge gap.  

Emma took smaller steps. Her toes touched each heel-mark in the dust. "Thirty-one," she said, reaching where Taro had been.  

The pond glinted ahead. Taro stretched his stride. "Forty-eight!" He slapped the flat stone.  

Emma kept counting. "Sixty-three." She stopped beside him.  

Gran looked up from the bench. "Different numbers?"  

"Sixty-three steps," Emma said.  

"Forty-eight," said Taro.  

Gran pinched a willow leaf between her fingers. "You have different legs."  

Taro looked down. His toes stuck out past Emma's.  

"Bigger steps cover more ground," Gran said.  

Emma touched the stone. It was warm. The same distance, two numbers. She wiggled her smaller foot next to Taro's and smiled.
