Taro pushed open the kitchen door, his hands empty. "The market’s closed," he said, kicking off his muddy boots. Biscuit trotted in behind him, sniffing at Taro’s pockets.

Gran stood at the table, kneading dough. A bowl of apples sat beside her — red and smooth, their skins shining in the morning light. Emma reached for one, turning it in her hands.

"How many do we have?" Taro asked, leaning over the table.

Emma set the apple back and counted. "One, two, three. Same as before."

Taro frowned. "Three and nothing is still three." He glanced at Gran, who was shaping the dough into a round loaf.

"Enough for a pie," she said, dusting flour from her hands. "Go pick some blackberries from the meadow. Biscuit will keep you company."

Emma grabbed the bowl, and Taro followed her out the gate. The meadow path was damp from last night’s rain. Biscuit bounded ahead, shaking water from his fur. The bowl felt light in Emma’s hands, but the apples clinked softly as they walked.
