The sky darkened as Emma and Taro walked down the meadow path. A fat drop landed on Emma’s nose. Then another.  

“Run!” Taro grabbed her hand.  

Biscuit lifted his snout and let the rain patter his tongue. He shook his ears but didn’t hurry.  

The drops came faster. The path turned slick. Emma’s boots splashed through puddles, spraying mud up her socks. Taro slipped, caught himself, and ran faster.  

The oak’s branches stretched wide. They ducked under, panting. Water dripped from Emma’s hair. Taro wrung out his shirt.  

Biscuit trotted up, soaked. He shook, showering them both.  

“Look.” Emma pointed. A tiny stream trickled between the oak’s roots, carrying leaves away.  

Taro crouched. “It’s going to the pond.”  

Biscuit sniffed the current, then plopped down in it. Mud streaked his fur.  

The rain drummed on the leaves above. Some drips found their way through, cold on Emma’s neck.  

Taro grinned. “We’re still getting wet.”  

Biscuit sneezed.
