Biscuit stood by the kitchen door. His tail was still, his ears up. He looked at Emma. Then he looked at the door.  

Emma turned the page of her book. Biscuit circled once, twice, and trotted to the hook where his lead hung. He sat beneath it and stared.  

"You want to go out," Emma said.  

Biscuit’s nose pointed to the lead, then back to her. His paws shifted on the tiles.  

Taro looked up from his drawing. "He didn’t even bark."  

Emma closed her book. She knew the routine: the door, the circle, the sitting just so. Biscuit never whined. He asked without words.  

She took the lead from the hook. Biscuit stood at once, his tail sweeping the floor.  

"You read him like a signpost," Taro said.  

Emma clipped the lead to Biscuit’s collar. He nudged the door with his nose, impatient now that she’d understood.  

The garden air rushed in as she opened it—warm, green, full of things to sniff. Biscuit pulled, not hard, just enough to say *this way*. Emma followed.
