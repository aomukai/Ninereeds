The rain had left the meadow path flooded. Where the dip usually held puddles, a shallow stream now ran, swift and brown with stirred-up earth. Two flat stones broke the surface—slick, uneven, but solid.  

Taro took a running start. He leaped, arms wheeling, and landed with a skid on the far bank. "Easy!" He grinned, brushing damp leaves from his knees.  

Emma crouched by the nearer stone. She pressed a palm against its wet surface, then stepped onto it. The stone wobbled slightly. She shifted her weight, reached for the next one, and stepped again. Her shoe grazed the water, but she was across.  

Biscuit trotted to the edge, sniffed the current, and walked straight in. The water reached his belly. He paused midstream, lapped at it, then shook himself vigorously on the other side, spraying droplets everywhere.  

The stream burbled on. The stones stayed where they were. The gap was short, but all three had crossed it differently, and none looked back.
