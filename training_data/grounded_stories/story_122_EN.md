Gran bent down and opened the oven door.  

A rush of heat came out. Emma felt it hit her face first—dry and sudden, like stepping too close to the fire. Then the smell followed: warm bread, golden and thick, filling the kitchen all at once.  

Taro leaned in. "Is it done?"  

Gran shook her head. She closed the door again, and the heat stopped pouring out. The warmth stayed in the air a little longer, pressing against their cheeks, their arms, the front of their shirts. Then it thinned, fading into the room like steam from a cup.  

The smell lingered. Emma breathed in. It was different now—not the immediate punch of heat, but something slower, settling into the corners of the kitchen.  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. "Ten more minutes."  

The oven ticked softly as it cooled. Outside, a breeze moved through the garden, but inside, the air still held the memory of warmth.
