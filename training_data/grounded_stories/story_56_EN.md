Emma stood by the herb trough, watching Gran’s hands move between the plants. The rosemary was dark green and spiky. The thyme sprawled in little mounds. Then Gran’s fingers closed on something grey-green, pinched a sprig, and held it under Emma’s nose before she could speak.  

The smell hit sharp and clean, like the air after rain. Emma blinked.  

Gran rubbed the leaves between her fingers. "Strong, isn’t it?" She took the kitchen scissors and snipped a few stems, then another. The cut ends smelled even sharper.  

A piece of string appeared from Gran’s pocket. She tied the stems together in a small bunch, knotted it tight, and held it up. "We’ll hang this in the kitchen. By the window."  

Emma reached out. The leaves were rough, but when she pressed one, it left a scent on her skin.  

Gran was already turning back to the trough. "Next time, you cut." She handed Emma the scissors, their blades cool and slightly sticky with sap.  

The bunch swung from her fingers as they went inside, trailing that sharp, clean smell all the way to the hook by the window.
