Emma picked up a smooth stone from the bank. "This one will sink," she said, and dropped it into the pond. It vanished at once, leaving only ripples.  

Taro snapped a twig from the willow. "This won't." He laid it gently on the water. It bobbed, turning slowly with the current.  

Biscuit sniffed at the edge, then sat back, ears cocked.  

Emma found a broad leaf, still green from the tree. "This is heavy. It'll sink." She placed it on the surface. For a moment it floated, flat as a plate. Then the edges darkened. Water crept up the veins. The leaf tilted, filled, and slid under.  

Taro grinned. "You were wrong first."  

Emma watched the leaf drift downward, pale beneath the surface. "Only for a bit."  

Biscuit yawned and trotted off to nose at the reeds. The children kept testing—a feather, a acorn cap, a flat chip of bark—while the pond swallowed each one in its own time.
