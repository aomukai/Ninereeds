Emma stood on tiptoe to peer into the henhouse. The straw prickled her fingers. One brown egg sat warm in the nest.  

"Found one!" She cupped it in both hands.  

Gran kneaded dough at the kitchen table. "We need three."  

Taro kicked the shed door open. Dust floated in the sunlight. "There were eggs here yesterday." He reached for the high shelf. Two eggs rested in the basket, smooth and cool.  

Gran cracked the first into the bowl. The yolk wobbled. "One."  

Emma set hers down. "Two."  

Taro added the last. "One and two is three. That is enough."  

Gran counted them on the table, tapping each shell. "One. Two. Three."  

The pan hissed. Emma watched the whites curl at the edges. Taro leaned over the bowl to sniff. Biscuit whined by the door, nose twitching at the smell.
