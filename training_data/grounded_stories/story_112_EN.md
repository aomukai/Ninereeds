The ice at the pond’s edge was thin and clear. Emma crouched to look. Beneath it, the reeds stood frozen mid-sway, trapped in their own reflection.  

Taro tapped the ice with his boot. It held. He pressed harder. A crack shot out like a branch snapping. Biscuit sniffed at the line, then set one paw on the ice. It creaked. He stepped back.  

The middle of the pond was still open water, dark where the ice hadn’t reached. Emma picked up a pebble and threw it. It skittered across the frozen shelf before vanishing into the open with a plink.  

Taro found a stick and poked at the edge. The ice broke into jagged pieces. He lifted one. It was cold and smooth, thinner at the edges where the water had frozen first.  

Biscuit barked at the broken pieces. His breath came out in little clouds.  

Emma held her hand over the open water. The air above it felt colder.  

"Gran says it freezes from the sides in," she said.  

Taro tossed his stick into the middle. It floated, slow and black, on the water that wasn’t frozen yet.
