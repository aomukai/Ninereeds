Emma knelt by the wigwam of poles. The bean plant had climbed all the way to the top, twining tight around the rough sticks. Its leaves were wide and cool under her fingers.  

"Look," Gran said, pointing to where the stems curved. A green pod hung there, fat as her thumb.  

Emma pinched the stem above it. The pod came away with a soft snap. Inside, four pale beans sat in a row, smooth as pebbles from the pond.  

"Same as the seed we put in," Gran said. She was digging carrots nearby, the fork lifting clods of dark earth.  

Emma turned a bean in her palm. It was hard to believe this little thing had sent up shoots, pushed through soil, climbed all summer to make more of itself.  

"Try one," Gran said without looking up.  

The bean tasted green and crisp. Somewhere under her feet, in the warm earth, other seeds were doing exactly what this one had done.
