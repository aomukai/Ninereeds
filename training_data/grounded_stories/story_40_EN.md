The morning light was pale and cold. Emma stood in the sitting room, her breath making little clouds in the air. The candles from the night before sat on the table — five of them, tall and white. Three were nothing but pools of wax now, smooth and hard. The others still had their wicks, blackened and short.

Emma leaned closer. "One, two," she counted softly. "That is all."

Gran came in with a basket of kindling. She glanced at the table. "Five candles," she said, setting the basket down. "Three burned out — two left."

Emma touched the edge of the wax where it had dripped and cooled. It felt slick and cool under her finger. The wick of the burned-out candle was almost buried in the wax, like a tiny stick in a frozen pond.

Gran picked up the basket again. "Best fetch the broom," she said. "The wax will need scraping."

Emma nodded, her eyes still on the candles. The two that were left stood straight and quiet, waiting for the evening.
