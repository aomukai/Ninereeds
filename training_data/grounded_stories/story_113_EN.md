Emma crouched by the pond’s edge. The ice had been a wide white shelf that morning, crisp under her fingers. Now it shrank back from the bank, its edge soft and crumbling. Water dripped from the underside where the sun touched it.  

Gran settled on the bench behind her. "Afternoon sun," she said, pulling her shawl tighter.  

Emma poked at the slush with a stick. The ice was thinner where it met the water, full of little holes like lace. A piece broke off and floated free, rocking in the open patch. By the willow, the whole shelf had pulled back, leaving dark water where Biscuit had skidded that morning.  

"It was solid here," Emma said.  

Gran nodded. "Same pond."  

The sun slid lower. The drip-line crept inward. By the time they stood to go, the ice was only a pale rim at the far side, and the stick Emma had left on it earlier floated loose, its shadow stretching long across the water.
