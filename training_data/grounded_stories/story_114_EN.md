The rain came down hard all afternoon. By the time they reached the cottage, Emma’s boots were full of water. Taro’s squelched when he walked.  

Gran took them at the door. "Off with these," she said, turning each one upside down. A little puddle spread on the stone floor.  

She set them by the fire, not too close—just near enough that the heat could reach. The leather darkened where the water soaked in.  

Morning light through the kitchen window. Emma picked up her boot. It was stiff now, the leather warm all through. She pressed her nose to it—smoke and earth and something deep and dry.  

Taro shoved his foot into his. "They’re all crinkly."  

Gran stirred the porridge. "Fire does that."  

The smell hung in the room, like the last trace of a storm.
