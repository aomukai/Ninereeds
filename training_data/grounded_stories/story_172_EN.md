Emma knelt under the oak. Acorns crunched under her knees. She lined them up one by one along a root.  

"Twenty-eight, twenty-nine—" A breeze shook the branches. More acorns pattered down. "Oh! Now I've lost count."  

Taro scooped a handful from the pile. "There's more than a hundred under this tree." He tossed them. Biscuit bounded after the rolling ones, sniffed each, left them where they lay.  

Emma started again. She made little groups of five. The bark was rough against her arm as she leaned to reach more. A squirrel chattered overhead.  

"Thirty-something," she said at last. "I keep losing my place."  

Taro kicked at the pile. Acorns scattered into the grass. "Too many to count."  

Biscuit nosed one into Emma's lap. She held it up—smooth at the tip, rough where the cap had been. The oak's shadow stretched long across the field.  

Gran called from the garden gate. Biscuit's ears pricked. He bounded toward the sound, paws rustling through the drifts.  

"More than you can count in an afternoon," Gran said as they reached her. The last acorns fell behind them, quiet in the grass.
