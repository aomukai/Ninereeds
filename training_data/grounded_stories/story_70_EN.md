Bello stopped at the surgery door. His ears, usually like little flags, pressed flat. He trembled.  

Emma crouched. "It's all right," she said, and lifted him. He was lighter than Biscuit, his paws tucked tight against her chest.  

Inside, the room smelled strange—sharp and clean. A woman with short dark hair knelt. "Hello, small one," she said, her voice soft. She touched Bello's nose before she stood to speak to Gran.  

The metal table was cold. Bello shivered when the vet set him on it, but he stayed still. Her hands moved quickly: ears, teeth, paws. A rustle as she opened a drawer.  

Then—a treat from her pocket. Bello sniffed, then took it. His tail gave one small wag.  

By the second treat, his trembling had stopped. He licked the vet's fingers.  

"All done," she said.  

Emma carried him back into the lane. The sun was out now. Bello turned his face up to it, ears lifting again.
