Emma sat on the flat stone, her knees tucked under her chin. The ducks bobbed at the edge of the reeds, their heads dipping for weeds. Biscuit lay beside her, his chin on his paws, watching a beetle crawl through the grass. He didn’t even lift his head when one of the ducks flapped its wings.  

Bello saw them first. His ears shot up. He took one step, then another, his whole body quivering.  

"Bello—" Emma started.  

Too late. He bolted.  

The ducks scattered in a flurry of wings and splashes. Bello skidded to a stop at the water’s edge, paws sliding in the mud. He barked once, high and surprised, as the ducks glided to the far side of the pond.  

Biscuit sighed through his nose and closed his eyes.  

Bello turned back, tail wagging, as if expecting praise. Emma scratched behind his ears. "They do that every time," she said.  

Bello looked at the ducks, then at Biscuit, then at the ducks again. His head tilted.  

Biscuit yawned.
