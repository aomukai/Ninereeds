Emma carried the basket from the henhouse, warm eggs nestled in straw. Gran stood at the kitchen table, a bowl waiting.  

"Let’s count," Gran said. She took the eggs one by one, placing them side by side. Two here, two there, until all stood in neat pairs.  

"Two," Gran touched the first pair. "Four," her finger moved to the next. "Six—six eggs." She glanced at Emma. "Count by twos: it is quicker."  

Emma studied the arrangement. The eggs gleamed in the morning light, each pair a little team. She pointed. "Three pairs."  

Gran nodded. "And three pairs make…?"  

Emma hesitated, then brightened. "Six!"  

Biscuit nosed the door open, sniffing at the table leg. Gran shooed him gently. "Out, you. These aren’t for you."  

Emma reached for the bowl. "Can I put them in?"  

"Go on."  

She lifted them two by two, careful not to clink them too hard. The last pair fit snugly beside the others. Gran covered the bowl with a cloth. "Enough for cakes tomorrow."  

Outside, a hen clucked, as if agreeing.
