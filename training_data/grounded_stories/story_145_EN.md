Gran was kneading dough at the kitchen table. The loaf sat near the edge, warm from the oven. Emma watched as Taro reached for the butter knife.  

Biscuit lay by the door, nose on his paws. Bello circled the table. His ears twitched.  

Gran turned to the sink. A quick shadow darted—Bello’s muzzle flashed up. He snatched the bread.  

Gran turned back. Biscuit hadn’t moved. His eyes were politely fixed on the ceiling. Bello chewed, tail wagging, crumbs on his chin.  

“Bello,” Gran said. She pointed at the floor.  

Bello swallowed. He dropped the last crust and slunk under the table. Biscuit sighed through his nose, as if he’d known better all along.  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. The dough waited. The kitchen smelled of yeast and dog.
