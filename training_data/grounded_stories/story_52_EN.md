Gran sat on the bench by the pond, her hands busy with the bread. She tore off the crust and broke it into small pieces, crisp and crumbly. Emma stood a little way off, watching. The ducks paddled in the middle of the water, their feathers sleek and dark.

"Here," Gran said, handing her a handful of crumbs. Emma tossed the first piece. It landed with a ripple, and the ducks turned their heads. One dipped its bill, swallowing the bread in a quick motion. Emma threw another piece, closer this time. The ducks swam toward it, their movements smooth and deliberate.

With each throw, they came nearer. Emma crumbled the next piece between her fingers, scattering it just at the water’s edge. The ducks waddled onto the bank, their webbed feet scrambling over the wet stones. They were close now, so close Emma could see the fine edges of their feathers, the way their eyes flicked toward her hand. She reached out, fingers brushing the air.

"Not to," Gran said softly, her gaze still on the ducks. Emma pulled her hand back and let the last crumb fall. The ducks dipped their bills, then turned and glided away, leaving small ripples behind.
