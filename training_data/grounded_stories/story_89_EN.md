Emma stopped on the path. "Here," she said.  

Taro stepped back. "Where?"  

She took his sleeve and pulled him sideways. "Here."  

The hedge was thick with leaves, but there was one narrow gap where the stems bent apart. Through it, just beyond, the pond lay still and bright. The willow trailed in the water. A duck floated where the reeds opened.  

Biscuit pushed between them, sniffing at the gap. His tail wagged.  

"See?" Emma said.  

Taro leaned. The pond vanished. He straightened, and it was there again, framed by the hedge. "It’s like a window," he said.  

Emma nodded. She knew the spot by the way the path dipped and the smell of warm grass rose. One step too far and the hedge closed. One step back and the gap showed only field.  

Biscuit sneezed and trotted ahead. The children followed, but Emma glanced back once. The gap was just a tangle of green now, the pond hidden until next time.
