Emma watched as Gran took the apple from the bowl. It was round and smooth, with one side redder than the other. Gran turned it in her hands, then pressed the knife through the middle.  

"One apple," Gran said, "two halves." She held them apart, then pushed them back together. The cut edges matched exactly.  

Emma took both pieces. The flesh was pale where the knife had been. She pressed the halves together—one, two—and the apple was whole again, except for the thin line between them.  

"It’s still one apple," she said.  

Gran nodded. "Cut in two or split in half, it’s the same. The pieces fit."  

Emma pulled them apart. Juice dampened her fingers. She gave one half to Gran and kept the other. The seeds, she noticed, were all on Gran’s side.  

They ate in quiet. The kitchen smelled of bread and the sharp, sweet scent of apple. Outside, a bird called from the garden. The two halves were gone, but the apple had been whole when they began.
