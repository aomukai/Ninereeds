Emma wiped her hands on her apron. The flour stuck between her fingers.  

"The pond walk takes longer," she said.  

Taro shook his head. "No, the village does."  

Gran kneaded the dough without looking up. "Count your steps next time."  

Emma frowned. The pond path wound through the meadow, past the oak. The village lane went straight down the hill. But the baker’s smell reached you before you saw the door. The pond was quiet until you were right at the water.  

Taro tapped the table. "The village is farther."  

"You’re wrong," Emma said.  

Gran pressed her thumb into the dough. "Guess all you like. Actually walk it after."  

Outside, the lane was dry. The pond rippled under the willow. Emma tried to picture her feet on each path—the long grass brushing her ankles, the stones hard under her shoes.  

Taro kicked a pebble. "We could measure."  

"Not now," Gran called from the kitchen.  

The clock ticked. The dough rose. The question hung between them, light as flour in the air.
