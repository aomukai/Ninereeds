Emma pressed the last seed into the soil with her thumb. The earth was cool and crumbly under her fingers. She brushed soil over it, patting gently until the row looked smooth and blank again.  

"It's already changing," Gran said, shaking a watering can over the beans.  

Emma crouched closer. She saw only dirt—dark, quiet, the same as before. "But there’s nothing."  

Gran tipped the can sideways. Water glistened on the soil, then sank in. "Below," she said.  

A breeze moved the apple blossoms overhead. Emma touched the ground where the seeds lay hidden. The soil gave nothing back—no sound, no shift, no sign. Yet Gran spoke as if something were happening, right now, in the secret dark.  

Emma sat back on her heels. The garden smelled of wet earth and green shoots. Somewhere, unseen, the seeds were listening to the rain.
