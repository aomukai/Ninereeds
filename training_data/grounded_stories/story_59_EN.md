The post box was set into the wall, red and slightly mossy at the base. Gran held out the envelope. "Lick the stamp," she said.  

Emma pressed the stamp to her tongue. It tasted of glue and nothing. She stuck it in the corner, just like Gran had shown her.  

"Now push," said Gran.  

The slot was a dark line in the metal. Emma slid the envelope in until it tipped—then it was gone. A soft thump came from below.  

"Where does it go?" Emma pressed her ear to the box.  

Gran straightened her hat. "Someone will come and collect it. All the letters. Then they'll go to the people we wrote on the front."  

Emma peered into the slot again. Nothing but black.  

"Will they come now?"  

"Later." Gran took her hand. "The post goes even when we're not watching."  

They walked back up the lane. Emma kept turning to look at the red box, small against the stone.
