Biscuit tugged at the lead. His nose was buried in the hedge, sniffing furiously. Taro waited, shifting his weight from one foot to the other. The meadow path was muddy from last night’s rain, and the air smelled of wet grass.

“Come on, Biscuit,” Taro said, giving the lead a gentle pull. But Biscuit didn’t budge. His tail wagged as he pawed at something under the hedge. Taro crouched to see. “What is it?” he muttered.

Then, with a sudden bark, Biscuit bounded ahead. The lead went taut, jerking Taro forward. He stumbled, then ran to keep up. Biscuit’s ears flapped as he raced down the path, puddles splashing under his paws. He stopped abruptly, nose to the ground again. Taro skidded to a halt, panting.

Biscuit found a stick—thick and knobby, too big for him to carry. He rolled it with his nose, then tried to fetch it, clamping it awkwardly in his jaws. He looked up at Taro, tail wagging.

“You’re ridiculous,” Taro said, laughing. Biscuit dropped the stick and bounded ahead again, ready to run.
