Gran set three small cloth bags on the kitchen table. The apple basket was full — round red ones, still warm from the sun.  

"Two in each bag," she said, handing one to Emma.  

Emma pressed the first apple into the bag. It made a soft thump. "One." The second apple filled the space beside it. "Two."  

Taro peered into his bag. "Three lots of two," he said, stacking his pair stem-up.  

Gran nodded. "Two and two and two." She tucked the last apples into the third bag. The table smelled sweet and sharp where the stems had rubbed.  

Emma pointed to each bag in turn. "One, two — three, four — five, six."  

"That is six," said Gran. She lifted the bags by their strings. They swung, all the same weight, all the same amount.  

Outside, the apple tree stood empty where the windfalls had been. The bags bumped against Gran’s skirt as she carried them to the shelf.
