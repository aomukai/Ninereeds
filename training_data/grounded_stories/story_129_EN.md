The rain had stopped, but the garden bed was soft underfoot. Emma crouched by the vegetable patch, her fingers hovering over the mud.  

"Look," she said.  

Taro squatted beside her. Small, round prints dotted the soil—three forward toes, sharp at the tips. Not the blunt press of boots, not the messy scatter of dog paws.  

"Who made those?" Taro asked.  

Gran knelt, her sleeve brushing a cabbage leaf. "Not us," she said. "Not Biscuit."  

Emma traced a print with her finger. "A bird?"  

Gran nodded. "From there." She pointed toward the fence, where the tracks began, faint at first, then clear.  

Taro stood, following the line with his eyes. "It hopped."  

The prints led to the fence post, then vanished. Emma touched the last one, the edges still sharp where the mud had held its shape.  

Gran dusted her hands. "Something passed before the rain dried."  

The garden was quiet. Overhead, a blackbird called.
