First the coats. Emma’s was thick and blue, with buttons that slipped into woolly holes. Taro’s had a zip that caught halfway up. He tugged. It stuck. He tugged again.  

Then the boots. Emma’s laces were stiff with cold. She bent over them, breath fogging. Taro stamped his feet in. His zip was still undone.  

Gran knelt. “Here.” She pulled the zip up slow and steady. The teeth closed. She wrapped scarves around their necks — Emma’s tucked in, Taro’s loose.  

Finally, the door. Cold air rushed in. The meadow path was white with frost.  

Emma stepped out first. The grass crunched. Taro ran past her, then stopped. “It took ages,” he said.  

Gran smiled. “Only five minutes.”  

The sun was bright. Their boots left dark prints in the frost.
