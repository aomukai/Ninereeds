The garden was still bright. Emma sat on the stone bench, watching the light catch the apple leaves. Taro lay on his back in the grass, squinting up at the sky.  

Gran came to the door, wiping her hands on her apron. She didn’t call them in. Instead, she leaned against the frame and said, "Today’s the longest day."  

Emma turned. "But it’s late."  

"Not yet." Gran pointed west. The sun hung just above the trees, pale gold. No shadows stretched yet.  

Taro sat up. "Usually it’s dark by now."  

They stayed. The light didn’t fade the way it usually did—no sudden dimming, no hurry. The sky stayed soft, the air warm. Bees still hummed in the lavender.  

Gran brought out three glasses of lemonade. They drank slowly, watching the sun sink without sinking, the blue of the sky deepening but never quite turning black.  

At last, the first star appeared. The air cooled. Gran stood. "There it goes," she said.  

Inside, the kitchen lamp was already lit.
