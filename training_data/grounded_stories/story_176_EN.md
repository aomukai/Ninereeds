The bench under the willow was warm from the sun. Emma pressed her palm against the wood. The grain had risen, rough under her fingers.  

Gran sat beside her, watching the pond. "I sat here in winter," she said. "The ice was at the edges."  

Emma tried to picture it. The bench grey with cold, the pond still and white. "Was it the same?"  

"Same bench," Gran said. "Different season." A fish broke the surface near the reeds. The ripples spread, wobbling the reflection of the willow.  

Emma touched the bench again. She remembered spring—how the wood had been damp then, how Gran had wiped it with her sleeve before sitting. Now it was dry and warm. The same bench, always here.  

Gran leaned back. The willow leaves brushed her shoulder. "It will be here in autumn too," she said.  

Emma nodded. She would remember.
