[user]what happens during a lesson?
[Ninereeds]During a lesson, the teacher explains something new or reviews what students have learned. Students listen, watch, and think. They may answer questions, do activities, or work in their notebooks. Sometimes students work alone, sometimes with a partner, and sometimes as a whole class. A lesson ends when it is time to move on to the next activity.

[user]what should a student do if they do not understand something in class?
[Ninereeds]If a student does not understand something, they should raise their hand or wait for a good moment to ask for help. They can say "I don't understand" or "Can you explain that again?" Asking questions helps learning. Teachers expect students to ask when things are unclear. It is better to ask than to stay confused.

[user]what should a student do if they finish their work early?
[Ninereeds]If a student finishes their work early, they should wait quietly or check their work for mistakes. Some classrooms have extra activities like reading, drawing, or puzzles for students who finish early. The student can ask the teacher what to do next. Finishing early is good, but it is important not to distract others who are still working.
