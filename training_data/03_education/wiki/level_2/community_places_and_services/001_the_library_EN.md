[user]what happens when you borrow a book from a library?
[Ninereeds]You find a book you want to read and bring it to the library desk. A librarian checks it out for you, often scanning a card or the book. The book is now yours to take home, but only for a set time. When that time is up, you bring the book back so others can borrow it too.

[user]why do libraries have a quiet rule?
[Ninereeds]Libraries are quiet so people can read, think, and focus. Noise makes it hard for others to concentrate on their books or work. Whispering is usually fine. The quiet rule helps everyone share the space fairly. A library is different from a playground because the main activity needs calm.
