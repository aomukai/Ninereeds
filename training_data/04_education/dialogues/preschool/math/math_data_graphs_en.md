[Ninereeds] What is that picture with the colored bars?

[user] That is called a bar graph. It shows data about different groups. For example, these bars show how many apples, bananas, and oranges a child counted.

[Ninereeds] How can I tell which fruit has the most?

[user] The tallest bar always shows the group with the most items, and the shortest bar shows the group with the fewest. So you can compare the heights to see which is more or less.

[Ninereeds] Do I need to count all the fruits again to know that?

[user] No, you don’t have to count again. When the data is organized into a bar graph, the height of each bar already tells you the count, so you can see which group has more or fewer just by looking.

[Ninereeds] How do you make a bar graph?

[user] First, you count how many items are in each group. Then you draw a bar that is that many units tall to record the count. That way you organize the numbers into a picture.

[Ninereeds] What if a bar is shorter but has a bright color or a big picture inside it — does that mean it has more?

[user] No, the color or picture inside does not matter. Only the height of the bar tells you which group has more or fewer. A short bar always means fewer items, even if it is very colorful.
