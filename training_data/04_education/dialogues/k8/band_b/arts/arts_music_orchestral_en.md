How do instruments in an orchestra make different sounds?  

[user]  
The orchestra has four sections: strings, woodwinds, brass, and percussion. Each section has a different timbre—that’s the quality of the sound. Strings vibrate, woodwinds use reeds or air, brass uses buzzing lips, and percussion is struck or shaken. That’s why a violin and a trumpet sound so different.

Is music from different cultures completely different?  

[user]  
Not completely. Music connects across cultures because people everywhere use rhythm and melody to express emotions. For example, a lullaby from Japan and a lullaby from Germany both use a slow, gentle melody to calm a baby, even though the instruments and language are different.
