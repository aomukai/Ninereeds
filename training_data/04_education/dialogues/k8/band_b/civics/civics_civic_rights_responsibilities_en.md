Does the 14th Amendment give everyone the right to vote?

[user]  
No, the 14th Amendment gave citizenship to all people born or naturalized in the United States and guaranteed equal protection under the law, but it did not give anyone the right to vote. Voting rights came later—through the 15th, 19th, and 26th Amendments.

Why do citizens have to serve on a jury?

[user]  
Jury duty is a responsibility of citizenship. It helps make sure that someone accused of a crime gets a fair trial, which is part of their due process right. A group of ordinary people—their peers—listen to the evidence and decide the verdict. That way the government can't just lock someone up without a fair hearing.

[user]  
Voting is both a right and a responsibility. When citizens don't vote, the people elected might not represent what most citizens want. That weakens democracy and hurts the common good. So voting is a way to take part in civic participation and make sure your voice is heard.
