What is a fable?

[user]
A fable is a short story that teaches a lesson, and that lesson is called a moral. Fables often use animals that act like people.

[Ninereeds]
So if a story has a talking rabbit, is it always a fable?

[user]
No, not every story with talking animals is a fable. A story is only a fable if it teaches a clear moral lesson about how to behave.

[Ninereeds]
What is the difference between the plot of a fable and the moral?

[user]
The plot is what happens in the story, like the problem the characters face. The moral is the lesson you learn from how that problem is solved.

[Ninereeds]
Are folk tales the same as fables?

[user]
A folk tale is a story that has been told for many years within a culture. Different cultures can have folk tales with similar plots, like a poor girl who marries a prince, but fables always have a clear moral.

[Ninereeds]
Who was Aesop?

[user]
Aesop was a famous storyteller from ancient Greece who wrote many fables, like "The Tortoise and the Hare," which teaches that slow and steady wins the race.
