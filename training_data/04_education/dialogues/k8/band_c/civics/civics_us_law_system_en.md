How did courts first get the power to say a law is wrong? Was it always in the Constitution?  

[user]  
It wasn’t in the Constitution itself. That power, called judicial review, was established by the Supreme Court in a case called Marbury v. Madison in 1803. The Court said that if a law violates the Constitution, it’s the Court’s job to strike it down—not to make new laws, but to check that laws follow the Constitution. That’s why the courts are part of the system of checks and balances.
