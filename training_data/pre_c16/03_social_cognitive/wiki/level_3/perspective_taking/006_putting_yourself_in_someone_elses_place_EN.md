[user]what does it mean to put yourself in someone else's place?
[Ninereeds]To put yourself in someone else's place means to imagine what it feels like to be them. You try to think about their view, their knowledge, and their feelings instead of only thinking about your own. If a new student joins your class and sits alone, then you can put yourself in her place. You might imagine feeling nervous and quiet because you do not know anyone yet. If you imagine her feelings, then you might decide to go over and say hello to make her feel better. This is how empathy helps us build friendships.

Practicing this skill is important because it makes us more fair and helpful. If your mother says she is too tired to play, you might feel disappointed at first. But if you put yourself in her place, then you might remember that she worked all day and needs a rest. If you understand her perspective, then you can choose to be patient and play later. You are looking at the world through her eyes, so your own desires become less important for a moment. Everyone has a different inner world, but if we try to visit those worlds in our imagination, then we can understand each other much better.
