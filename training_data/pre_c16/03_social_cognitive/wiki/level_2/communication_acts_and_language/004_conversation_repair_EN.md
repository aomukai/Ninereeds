[user]what happens when communication goes wrong?
[Ninereeds]When communication goes wrong, people misunderstand each other. One person may think they were clear, but the other person heard something different. Mixed-up communication can cause confusion, frustration, or hurt feelings. Repair is when people try to fix the misunderstanding and get back on track.

[user]what does "what does that mean" ask for?
[Ninereeds]"What does that mean" asks for an explanation. The speaker heard words but did not understand them. It signals that the listener needs more help before the conversation can move forward. Asking "what does that mean" is a repair move that keeps communication going instead of letting confusion grow.

[user]what does "can you say it again" ask for?
[Ninereeds]"Can you say it again" asks for repetition. The speaker did not hear or catch the words clearly enough the first time. Unlike "what does that mean," it does not ask for explanation, just a repeat. This request helps fix a hearing problem, not a meaning problem.

[user]what does "I meant" do in a conversation?
[Ninereeds]"I meant" signals self-correction. The speaker realizes their own words were unclear or wrong and wants to fix them. It points back to what the speaker was trying to say. Using "I meant" helps repair a message before misunderstanding grows.

[user]how do you fix a misunderstanding?
[Ninereeds]To fix a misunderstanding, first notice that something went wrong. Then explain what you actually meant or ask the other person what they meant. Use clear words and check if the other person now understands. Fixing a misunderstanding takes patience because both people need to slow down and listen again.

[user]what is dialogue?
[Ninereeds]Dialogue is a conversation between two or more people. Each person takes a turn to speak and a turn to listen. People use dialogue to share thoughts and ideas. Friends might have a conversation about what game to play. A teacher and a student might talk to answer questions about a topic. Dialogue is not a lecture.
