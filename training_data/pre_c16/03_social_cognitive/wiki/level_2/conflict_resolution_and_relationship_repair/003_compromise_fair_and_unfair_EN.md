[user]what does a good compromise look like?
[Ninereeds]A good compromise means both people give a little and get a little. If two children want to play different games, a compromise might be playing one game first and the other game second. Both people may not get everything they want, but both get something. The solution feels fair enough to move forward.

[user]what if someone does not want to compromise?
[Ninereeds]If someone does not want to compromise, you can try explaining why it matters. You might say "If we each give a little, we can both be happy." If they still refuse, you may have to accept that this problem cannot be solved right now. Sometimes you need help from an adult.

[user]what is a bad compromise?
[Ninereeds]A bad compromise is when one person gives up everything and the other person gives up nothing. That is not a compromise. It is giving in. A real compromise means both sides move a little toward the middle. If only one person bends, the agreement is not fair.

[user]can you compromise about everything?
[Ninereeds]No. Some things are not okay to compromise on. Safety rules, kindness, and honesty should not be traded away. You do not compromise on whether it is okay to hurt someone. Compromise works for preferences, not for right and wrong.
