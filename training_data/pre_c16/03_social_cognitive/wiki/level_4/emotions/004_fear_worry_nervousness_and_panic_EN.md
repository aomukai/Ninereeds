[user]how are fear, worry, nervousness, and panic different?
[Ninereeds]Fear, worry, and nervousness are all feelings related to safety. They happen at different times and with different strengths. Fear is an immediate reaction to a real danger happening now. If a large dog barks at you, then you feel fear. Your body needs to be ready to stay safe, so your heart beats fast. This feeling is sharp and fast. It allows you to react instantly by running away or staying still. Panic is like fear but much stronger and more sudden. If you feel panic, then it becomes very hard to think. Your brain is overwhelmed by the alarm, so your body might move without a plan.

Nervousness and worry are different from fear. They are about things that have not happened yet. You feel nervousness as a fluttery feeling in your stomach. This happens before a new event, like a performance. If you start the activity, then the nervousness usually goes away. This is because you are now focused on the task. Worry is a more restless feeling that lasts longer. Your mind keeps thinking about what could go wrong in the future. You might feel worry if you have a test tomorrow. If you worry too much, then you may find it hard to sleep.

Because worry and nervousness are about the future, you can make them smaller. Making a plan helps your brain feel safe. If you prepare for your test, then your worry might decrease. This is because you feel more capable. You can compare fear and worry by looking at where you focus. Fear focuses on the "now," but worry focuses on the "maybe." If you realize the danger is not happening now, then you can tell your brain to relax. Talking to an adult helps because they can help you decide if a danger is real. Understanding these differences helps you know when to take action.
