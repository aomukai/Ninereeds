The bicycle made a sound like a dry stick dragged over stone. Riku pulled it up beside the stall and put one foot down.

He reached into his canvas bag. The bag was heavy today. He pulled out a parcel wrapped in brown paper and tied with string. The string was crossed in the middle and knotted tight.

"Morning," Riku said.

Yun looked up from the brass scale. She set down a handful of beans and took the parcel. She turned it over. A label was stuck to the paper with small, neat writing.

She took a pencil from behind her ear and signed on the line he held out.

Mei stood at the corner of the stall. She watched the string. It was not ordinary string. It was thicker than thread, and it had a twist in it that went one way and then another.

"Is it for us?" Mei asked.

"It is," said Riku.

Yun did not open it. She set it on the stall table beside the piles of carrots. The string caught the morning light.

Riku put the paper back in his bag. He lifted his foot onto the pedal and pushed off. The bicycle wobbled once and then straightened. He rode down the street without looking back.

Mei put one finger on the parcel. The paper was warm from being in the bag.
