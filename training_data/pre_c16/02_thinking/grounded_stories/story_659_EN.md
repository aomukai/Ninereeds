Emma sat on the flat stone at the edge of the pond. The water was still and green. She looked down. No fish.

She leaned forward. Her reflection looked back at her. Nothing else.

Mei stood on the bank behind her. “Are they there?”

“No,” Emma said.

She moved to the other side, where the willow dipped close to the water. She sat on the grass and looked again. The water was still. No fish.

She stood up. She walked around the pond. She tried the bench by the willow. She tried the reeds. She sat in three different places.

No fish.

Mei came and stood beside her. “The light is wrong,” she said.

“What does that mean?”

“They can see you before you see them. If the light is in your eyes, you miss them.”

Emma sat back down on the flat stone. She made her hands still on her knees. She waited. The water did not move. The light sat on the surface like a lid.

Nothing.

She stood up quickly. Her hands were tight at her sides.

“I’m going,” she said.

She walked up the meadow path without looking back.
