The door was low. Emma had to duck her head to step through.

The room smelled of sawdust. Not just sawdust—something sharp and clean underneath, like the air after Gran split wood.

Vern stood at the workbench with his back to her. He did not look up.

Emma stood still. She watched his hand move along a piece of wood. His finger ran from one end to the other, slow. He put that piece down and picked up another.

The floor was covered in pale shavings, curled like the peel from an apple. Tools hung above the bench on wooden pegs, each in its own place. A plane. A chisel. A wooden mallet with a dark handle.

Vern ran his finger along the second piece. He turned it over. Ran his finger again.

Emma took one step closer. The shavings shifted under her shoe.

Vern set the second piece down. He picked up the first one again. He held it up to the light from the window.

He still had not looked at her. But he said, "This one is ready."

Emma waited. He set it on the bench and reached for a tool from its peg.
