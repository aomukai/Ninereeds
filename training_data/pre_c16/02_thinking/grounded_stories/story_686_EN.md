Emma stood on the raised root and reached up. The nest was still in the fork of the oak, where it had been all spring and summer.

She touched the edge. The nest felt different now — dry and loose, not firm like before.

Mei stood beside her and looked up. "It looks old," she said.

"It is old," Emma said. "The robin made it a long time ago."

She lifted her hand higher and felt inside. Nothing moved. No eggs. No chicks. No robin.

Inside the nest there were two brown leaves and one small feather. The feather was grey and thin, bent at the tip. Emma pulled it out and held it in her palm.

"The robin is gone," Mei said.

Emma nodded. She looked at the nest again. The edges were frayed. The twigs stuck out at odd angles. The whole thing looked looser than it had in summer, as if the wind had pulled at it for weeks.

She put the feather back inside.

"It looks different now," she said.

Mei looked up at the bare branches above them. "The nest is still here, though."

Emma touched the side one more time. It was rough and cold. The robin was not coming back this season. The nest sat in the fork like something left behind.
