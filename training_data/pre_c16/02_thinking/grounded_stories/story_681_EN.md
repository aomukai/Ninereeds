The wind blew all night. In the morning, the grass under the apple tree was covered.

Apples lay everywhere. Some were red and round. Some were brown and soft.

Emma picked one up. It was firm in her hand. The skin was smooth and clean.

Taro picked one up. His fingers went into the side. The apple was brown all the way through.

"That one is not good," Gran said. She was already on her knees, sorting them into two piles.

She showed them how. Squeeze gently. Look for holes. Smell it.

Emma squeezed. The apple was hard. It smelled sweet and fresh. She put it in the basket.

Taro squeezed. His apple was soft. It smelled sharp and strange. He put it on the compost pile.

Biscuit sniffed a soft apple. His nose went close. Then he sneezed — a big, wet sneeze that shook his whole head.

Taro laughed. "Biscuit does not like that one either."

Gran picked up a very brown apple. A bee crawled out of a hole in the side. A wasp sat on the top.

"Not that one," Gran said, and dropped it into the compost.

The basket filled with good apples. The compost pile grew soft and brown.
