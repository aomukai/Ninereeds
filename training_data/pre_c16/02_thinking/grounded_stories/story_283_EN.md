Vern took the trowel from Gran. The wooden handle was split from end to end.

He turned it over in his hands. "Old," he said.

Gran nodded. "Too old to fix."

Vern set the trowel on his workbench. He walked to the stack of wood along the wall and ran his hand across a pale piece. Ash. He lifted it, felt the weight.

He set it on the bench and marked the length with a pencil. Then he cut. The saw moved in long, even strokes. Sawdust fell onto the floor.

He clamped the piece in the vise. He picked up a spokeshave and began to work. The blade took off thin curls of wood. The ash turned from square to eight-sided, then round.

He tested it in his hand. Too thick in one place. He shaved a little more.

Then he held it up. He picked up a piece of sandpaper and rubbed along the length. Once. Twice. He rubbed until the wood was smooth and warm.

He fitted the new handle into the trowel's metal socket. A tap with a wooden mallet. It sat tight.

He handed it back to Gran. She closed her hand around it. Her fingers fit exactly.

"New handle," she said.

Vern nodded.
