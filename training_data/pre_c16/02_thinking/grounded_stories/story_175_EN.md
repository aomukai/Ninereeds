The teacher tapped the board with her chalk. "Eight apples here," she said, writing the number. "Five apples here." The chalk squeaked. "How many more in the first group?"  

Emma watched the numbers. She held up three fingers. "Three more."  

"Correct." The teacher pointed. "Eight minus five is three. The difference."  

Outside, a blackbird sang. The radiator clicked. Emma rubbed her finger along the groove of her desk where someone had carved a wobbly E.  

"Now you try." The teacher wiped the board clean. "Seven and four. How many more?"  

Emma looked at her fingers. The chalk dust smelled sharp. The numbers waited, white on grey.  

At the nature table, the tadpoles flicked their tails in the jar. The teacher's sleeve had a smudge of chalk where she'd leaned on the board.  

Emma counted again in her head. The difference. Take away. She raised her hand.
