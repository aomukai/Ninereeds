Vern pulled a folded piece of paper from his apron pocket. He smoothed it flat on the workbench. Emma leaned in close.

He drew two lines with a short pencil. A notch between them. He drew another line that crossed the first at an angle. Then he stopped.

"See that?" he said.

Emma looked at the paper. Then she looked at the piece of wood on the bench. The paper shape was small. The wood was bigger.

Vern picked up the wood and held it beside the drawing. "The notch goes here," he said, pointing at the paper, then at the wood. "Same shape. Same angle. I draw it first so I can see it before I cut it."

He put the wood down. He drew the same two lines on the wood with the same pencil. The line was light, thin, easy to miss. But it was there.

Emma watched the line. It was just a mark. But she could already see the notch in her mind. The saw would follow the pencil. The shape on the paper would become the shape in the wood.

Vern reached for the saw. He did not lift it yet. He looked at the line, then at Emma, and waited.
