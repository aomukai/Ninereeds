Emma sat on the flat stone, her knees tucked under her. The pond was still. Above her, the sky stretched blue and endless. Below her, the same sky lay trapped in the water, broken only by the occasional ripple.  

A dragonfly darted past, its wings a blur. It skimmed the surface, touching down so lightly it left no mark. Emma held her breath.  

Just beneath, where the reflection wavered, fish moved in slow, dark shapes. They hovered near the bottom, where the water was deep and cool. One flicked its tail, stirring the silt.  

"Look," Emma whispered.  

Taro crouched beside her, squinting. "Where?"  

"Everywhere."  

Above, the dragonfly. At the surface, the sky doubled. Below, the fish drifting like shadows. Three layers, all at once.  

The dragonfly veered away. The fish scattered. The water stilled again, holding the sky as if it had always been there.  

Emma dipped a finger in. The reflection shattered into rings. For a moment, nothing was where it should be. Then the pond remembered itself, and the world settled back into place.
