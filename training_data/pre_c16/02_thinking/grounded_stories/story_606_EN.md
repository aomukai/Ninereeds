Gran stood on her toes and touched the pear. It gave a little.

She turned it in her hand. The stem was still firm, but the fruit near it was soft.

She picked it and held it out to Emma. “Try.”

Emma pressed her thumb against the side. The skin gave way just a little. She bit into it. The flesh was soft and sweet, and juice ran down her chin.

Gran picked another and gave it to Mei. Mei held it in both hands. She pressed it the way Gran had pressed hers. Then she bit. Her eyes widened.

“It’s ready,” she said.

Yun appeared at the garden gate with a basket over her arm. She set it down on the stone wall.

“Are they?” she asked.

Gran nodded. “This week. After that they go mealy.”

Yun reached up and turned a pear on its branch. She did not pick it. She just felt it. Then she nodded too.

They picked the rest together. Emma reached the low ones. Gran stood on a stool for the high ones. Mei held the basket steady. Yun checked each pear before it went in — soft at the stem, not too hard, not too soft.

The basket filled. The tree was lighter.

“Tomorrow they will be perfect,” Gran said. “Today they are just right.”
