The rain came down hard all morning. It drummed on the root cellar door.

Gran lifted the heavy door with the iron bar and propped it open. The steps were dark and damp.

"Fetch me some onions," she said. "The ones hanging on the left."

Taro went first. Mei followed close behind. The lamp threw their shadows large against the stone walls.

At the bottom, the floor was cold under their feet. Taro stopped.

"There is water," he said.

A small puddle sat near the far wall, spreading slow and dark across the earth floor. A thin trickle ran down the stone, one drop at a time.

Mei knelt and touched the edge of the puddle with her finger. "Where does it come from?"

Gran's voice came down the steps. "The stone lets a little water in when the rain is this heavy."

She came down with an old cloth in her hand. She folded it and laid it over the puddle. The cloth darkened at once.

Taro reached up and felt the onions. The rope was dry. He untied one and handed it to Mei.

The rain kept falling. The cloth soaked up the water, slow and steady.
