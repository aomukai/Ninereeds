The cellar door was heavy. Gran propped it open with the iron bar. The stone steps went down into the dark.

Emma held the lamp. The flame made the shadows jump.

The turnip box was against the far wall. The top was full enough, but Gran reached past them. She lifted a turnip from the bottom.

She turned it in her hand. The skin was firm. The root end was still tight.

"Good," she said. She set it on the step.

Emma reached into the box. Her fingers found the others. Some were hard and round. Some gave a little when she pressed.

"These ones," she said.

Gran took them from her. She held each one close to the lamp. Two were soft at the top. She set them aside. The rest were good.

They counted together. Nine good turnips.

The soft ones Gran carried up first. Emma followed with the lamp. The box was half full now. Less than half, maybe. But nine turnips would keep.
