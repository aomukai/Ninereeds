Yun's stall had small paper bags lined up in rows. Each one was folded at the top and tied with a twist of string.

Gran picked up a bag of dried herbs. She held it close to her face and sniffed.

"Thyme," she said.

Yun nodded. She was weighing something on the brass scale, her fingers quick and sure.

Gran reached into her pocket and brought out a coin. It was silver and larger than the ones Emma usually saw. She put it on the wooden table.

Yun picked it up. She opened a small tin box and took out a handful of smaller coins.

"One," she said.

She placed a coin in Gran's open palm.

"Two."

Another coin landed on top.

"Three."

The last one was the smallest. It made a light clink against the others.

Gran closed her hand. She dropped the coins into her pocket without looking at them.

Mei was standing beside the stall. She had a bag of dried herbs in her own hand — smaller than Gran's, tied with a blue string.

Emma watched Gran's pocket. She had not counted the coins again. She had just trusted them.

"Ready?" said Gran.

Emma nodded. They turned and walked back up the lane.
