Gran lifted the lamp from the hook by the kitchen door. She struck a match and lit the wick. The flame grew small and yellow.

"Apples for a pie," she said. "From the bottom of the crate, Emma. The ones at the top are for eating."

Emma took the lamp. The metal handle was warm already. She pushed the root cellar door open with her shoulder and held it with her foot while she found the first step. Five stone steps, worn in the middle. The air got cooler with each one.

The apple crate sat against the far wall. She set the lamp on a shelf above it. The light fell on the top layer — red apples, firm, with stems still on. She picked one up and turned it in her hand. It smelled sweet and clean.

She put it down and reached deeper. Her fingers touched something different. Softer. She pulled out an apple with a brown patch near the stem. Another had a dent that gave way when she pressed it.

She checked each apple from the bottom. Some were good. Some were not.

She filled her hands with the firm ones and carried them up the steps. The soft ones stayed behind in the crate.

Gran took the apples at the door. "Good," she said. "That's enough."
