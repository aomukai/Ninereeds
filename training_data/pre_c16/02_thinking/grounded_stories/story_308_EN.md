The customer took her bag and walked away.

Yun looked at the stall table. The basket of beans had shifted. One jar of jam stood a little apart.

She reached out and straightened the basket. She turned it until the edge was even with the edge of the table. Then she picked up the jar and moved it two inches to the left. It lined up with the other jars now. There was no gap.

Mei watched. A small spot of something sticky sat on the wood between two baskets. She wiped it with her sleeve. The cloth came away dark.

The table was tidy again. The rows were even. Nothing was out of place.

Yun ran her hand along the edge of the table, feeling for dust. She found none. She nodded once, very slightly.

Mei put her hand flat on the clean wood. It was smooth and warm from the summer sun. She could see her own reflection in the lid of the nearest jar, small and upside-down.

A woman stopped in front of the stall. Yun smiled and lifted the basket of beans.

Mei stepped back and let her grandmother work.
