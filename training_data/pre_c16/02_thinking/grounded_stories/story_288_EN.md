Vern held the chisel against the wood. The blade was sharp and silver.

"Watch the grain," he said.

Mei stood close to the workbench. She watched his hands.

He tapped the chisel with the mallet. *Tap.* The blade bit into the wood. A thin chip curled away, clean and smooth, along the line of the grain.

"That is paring," Vern said.

He turned the chisel the other way. He tapped again. *Tap.*

The wood did not split clean. It tore. A rough edge lifted. A splinter ran sideways into the piece, and the chip came away broken and ragged.

Vern stopped. He looked at the torn place.

"Wrong direction," he said. "The grain decides where the wood wants to split. You cannot force it."

He set the chisel back the right way. *Tap.* The blade slid. The chip came away clean again.

Mei reached out and touched the smooth place with her fingertip. Then she touched the torn place. The difference was plain.

Vern turned the piece over in his hands.

"No waste," he said. "The torn side will be the back. No one sees the back."
