The door to the schoolyard was heavy. Emma pushed it with her shoulder and stepped inside.

Along the wall were the coat hooks. Low enough for children. Each one had a small card above it, held by a single drawing pin. Clara had written the names in her careful letters.

Emma found her hook. Third from the left. The card said EMMA in black ink. She pulled off her coat and hung it by the loop at the collar. The sleeves hung down. She touched the card once with her finger.

Mei came in behind her. She did not look at the names. She looked at the row and found her place — two hooks down from Emma. She ran her finger over the card before she hung her scarf. The scarf was blue and thick. It swung a little when she let go.

The door opened again. Riku came in with a letter in his hand. His breath was cold. He stopped and watched the children at the hooks.

"Your coats know where they live," he said.

Emma looked at her hook. Then at Mei's. The pegs were the same. The cards were the same. But the place was different.
