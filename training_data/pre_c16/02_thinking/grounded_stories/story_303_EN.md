Hana came early. The bakery light was on before the sun.

She carried the last tray of bread to the market stall. Yun was already there, lifting apples from a crate and setting them in a row.

“Morning,” Hana said.

Yun nodded. She placed the apples so the red side faced out.

The first customer came before Hana had finished unwrapping the cloth from her loaves. A woman in a green coat. She bought two loaves and a small bag of apples.

By mid-morning the apple basket was half empty. Yun straightened the ones that were left. She turned each one until it sat right.

A queue formed at the bread end of the stall. Three people. Then four. Hana handed loaves over the table and made change without counting.

At the end of the day, only the small apples were left in the basket. The ones no one had chosen.

Yun looked at them. She did not put them back in the crate.

“They will go in a pie,” she said.

Hana folded her cloth. The stall was quiet now. The sun was low over the village lane.
