Vern turned the handle on the vise. The two metal jaws moved closer together. They touched the piece of wood. Vern turned again. The jaws pressed in. The wood held still.

Vern let go of the handle. The wood did not move. It stayed where the vise held it.

Taro watched. Vern picked up a file. He ran it along the edge of the wood. The wood did not shift. It did not wobble. It sat still in the vise.

Taro reached out. He touched the wood with one finger. It was solid. It did not move at all.

“How does it stay?” Taro said.

Vern kept working. “The vise holds it,” he said. “Turn the handle. The jaws close. The wood cannot go anywhere.”

Taro looked at the handle. He put his hand on it. He turned it a little, the other way. The jaws opened. The wood was loose now. He turned the handle back. The jaws closed again. The wood was still.

Vern stopped filing. He looked at what Taro had done. He nodded once. Then he picked up the file again and went back to work, his hands free to move because the wood did not.
