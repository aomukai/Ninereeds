The sack of potatoes slumped against the shed wall, heavy and stubborn. Emma pushed from the bottom. It barely budged.  

"Maybe if we—" She tried tipping it sideways, gripping the corners. The sack groaned but stayed put.  

Taro crouched, squinting. Then he grabbed one end and twisted, lifting at a sharp angle. The sack lurched up, sudden and light, and he staggered back with it.  

Emma blinked. "You just—"  

Gran passed them with a bucket, glancing at the sack now clear of the doorway. "Hose needs winding," she said, and kept walking.  

Taro grinned, adjusting his grip. "It didn’t like the straight way."  

Emma touched the sack where it had resisted her. The rough weave was warm from the sun. She pressed a hand to the side and pushed experimentally—stuck again.  

Taro nudged past her, tilting the sack onto his shoulder. It came loose like it had been waiting for him.  

Emma watched it go. The path to the kitchen was scattered with potato dirt, small and dry, already blowing away.
