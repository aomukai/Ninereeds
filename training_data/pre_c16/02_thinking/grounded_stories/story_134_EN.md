Bello stopped at the base of the oak, nose twitching. He pressed forward, sniffing hard at the gap between the roots. His tail stiffened.  

Emma crouched beside him. "What is it?"  

Bello scraped at the dirt with one paw. He shoved his muzzle deeper into the hole, ears flat, shoulders tense. A small cloud of dry leaves puffed out as he sneezed.  

Biscuit ambled over. He gave the hole a single, slow sniff. Then he turned away and flopped down in the grass, chin on his paws.  

Taro laughed. "Biscuit's done with that."  

Bello dug again, claws scuffing against bark. A beetle scuttled out, and he jerked back, startled.  

"It's old news," Emma said, brushing dirt from her knees. "Biscuit found that ages ago."  

Bello whined, circling the hole once more. He nosed at it, hopeful, as if this time might be different. Biscuit sighed and closed his eyes.  

The hole stayed empty. The oak leaves rustled overhead. After a minute, Bello trotted off, nose already low to the ground, searching for something new.
