Emma lifted an apple from the basket. The skin was cool and smooth. She lined them up on the garden table, one by one.  

"One, two, three—" A breeze shook the tree. A leaf landed on the fourth apple. She lost her place. "Five? Six. Seven."  

Gran pinched thyme between her fingers. "Count again."  

Emma frowned. She started over. The basket wobbled as she lifted each apple. "One. Two. Three." The fourth had a bruise, soft under her thumb. "Four. Five. Six. Seven. Eight."  

Gran nodded toward the table. "Count a third time."  

This time Emma touched each one as she said it. "One." Her finger left a faint shine on the skin. "Two. Three." The smallest apple barely filled her palm. "Four. Five. Six. Seven. Eight."  

Gran dropped the thyme into the pot. "Eight," she said.  

Emma looked at the row. The first count had been wrong. The second count was the one that matched. She picked up the bruised apple and turned it in her hands. The sweet, sharp smell filled her nose.
