Mei stood at the edge of the stall. Her grandmother Yun placed a potato on one side of the brass scale.

The side dropped. *Down.*

Yun picked up an apple from the basket. She put it on the other side.

Mei watched. The apple side went down. The potato side went up.

“The apple is heavier,” Mei said.

Yun nodded. She took the apple off. She put another potato on the scale, next to the first one.

Now both sides sat level. The brass arm stayed straight, not tipping.

“Two potatoes,” Yun said, “equal one apple.”

Mei reached out. She touched the potato on the left. It was rough and dusty. She touched the apple on the right. Smooth and cool.

“Can I try?”

Yun moved aside. Mei picked up a small potato. She put it on the empty side. The scale tipped. The potato side went up. The other side stayed down.

“Too light,” Mei said.

She picked up a bigger potato. She put it on. The scale tipped the other way. Down on her side. Up on the other.

“Too heavy.”

She picked up a third potato. She held it in her hand. She looked at the potato on the other side. They looked the same size.

She set it down beside the first one. The brass arm moved slowly. It stopped. Level.

“Equal,” Mei said.

Yun smiled and straightened a bag of dried herbs.
