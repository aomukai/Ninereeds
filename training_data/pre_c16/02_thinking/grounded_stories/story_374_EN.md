The hill was high. Emma climbed until the cottage looked small. Taro was already at the top.

“Listen,” Emma said.

She stood still. The morning was quiet. No wind.

Then the village bell rang. It was a long way down the lane, past the meadow, past the school. But on the hill it sounded close — as if it were ringing just behind the hedge.

“It sounds like it’s next to me,” Taro said.

Biscuit came up beside them, tongue out. He sat and pointed his nose toward the village.

The bell rang again. Clear and still in the air.

Then Biscuit barked. A short, sharp bark.

The sound went across the valley. It hit the far side and came back — softer, thinner, but still a bark.

Biscuit turned his head. He barked again. The echo came back again.

“The sound carries,” Emma said. “Up here, everything carries.”

Taro listened. The air was so still that he could hear a gate close somewhere down in the village. He could hear a dog answer Biscuit, far away.

They stood on the hilltop. The bell was done. The air was quiet again. Every sound had room to travel.
