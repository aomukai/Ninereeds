The sun was lower now. The last customer walked away down the street.

Mei stood at the stall table. She looked at what was left.

Half a basket of apples. Two jars of jam. A small pile of herbs tied with string.

“Not much,” she said.

Yun nodded. She took the cloth from the edge of the table and laid it over the jars. She folded the corners under. The cloth was blue. It had been white once.

Mei picked up the herbs. She held them to her nose. They smelled dry and green.

Yun lifted the basket. The apples shifted inside. Some were small. Some had bruises. The ones that were left.

“We will carry it home,” Yun said.

Mei put the herbs on top of the apples. She looked at the empty table. The stall was bare now except for a few crumbs and a single leaf that had blown in.

She picked up the leaf and put it in her pocket.

Yun waited. The basket was heavy on her arm.

Mei took one handle. Yun took the other. They lifted together.

The street was quiet. The day was over.
