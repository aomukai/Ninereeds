The lever was a long bar of dark metal, fixed to a post beside the sluice.

"This is the one," Mei said.

Emma put both hands on it. She pulled. Nothing. She pushed. The lever did not move.

"It is heavy," Mei said.

Emma pushed again. Her arms shook. The lever stayed still.

Mei stepped beside her. They put their hands on the bar together, one above the other. Mei counted.

"One. Two. THREE."

They leaned into it. Their feet slid on the stone floor. The lever shifted. A little. Then more. Emma felt something give under her hands.

Below them, water rushed. The sound changed — louder, faster, deeper.

The wheel began to turn. Emma felt it through the floor, through her feet, through her whole body. A slow, grinding weight.

She looked at her hands. She had pushed something heavy, and it had moved.

Mei pointed at the water streaming under the wheel.

"Open," she said. "Now the wheel turns."

Emma nodded. She did not say anything. She could still feel the force in her arms.
