Biscuit raced along the pond bank, his paws drumming the ground. Emma stood by the flat stone, watching the reeds ripple in the breeze. She glanced up just as Biscuit barreled past, ears flapping, tongue lolling. “Biscuit!” she called, stepping back just in time.  

He didn’t slow down. He leaped, paws outstretched, and hit the water with a loud splash. For a moment, he vanished under the spray, then his head popped up, ears flattened, eyes bright. He paddled in a circle, tail wagging, sending ripples across the pond.  

Taro laughed. “He’s like a fish!”  

Biscuit swam to the shore and hauled himself out, water streaming from his fur. He shook, hard, sending droplets everywhere. Emma ducked behind Taro, but it was no use. The spray hit them both, soaking their clothes and hair.  

Biscuit stood there, dripping and proud, his tongue hanging out. He wagged his tail and looked at them as if to say, *Your turn.*  

Emma wiped her face. “He’s soaked us!”  

Taro grinned. “Let’s wade in. It’s hot anyway.”  

Biscuit barked, bouncing on his paws, ready to go again.
