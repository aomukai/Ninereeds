Emma stood on tiptoe by the pear tree. She reached up and pressed her thumb gently into the side of a pear. It was firm.  

"Like this," she said to Taro. He stretched and pressed another one.  

"Still hard," he said.  

Emma nodded. "Gran said you have to check every day. When it gives a little, that’s the right day."  

Taro pressed again, frowning. "Not yet?"  

"Not yet. Maybe two days. Maybe three." She plucked a windfall from the grass and turned it in her fingers. The skin was smooth, but the flesh underneath was unyielding.  

Taro crouched to inspect the fallen ones. "These aren’t good?"  

Emma shook her head. "Too soon. Gran says they go mealy if you pick them early."  

A breeze rustled the leaves. Somewhere in the cottage, a door clicked shut.  

Taro stood and brushed grass from his knees. "We’ll check tomorrow?"  

Emma smiled. "Tomorrow."
