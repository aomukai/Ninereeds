The thick log lay at the edge of the yard. It was grey and worn, and the bark had come off in patches.

Taro put one foot on it. Then the other. He stretched his arms out wide.

"Look," he said. He walked three steps, wobbled, steadied himself. Walked again.

Emma watched his feet. She put one hand out and stepped onto the log. One slow step. Then another. Her arms were out too. She felt the roundness under her shoe.

Mei climbed on at the other end. She took two steps. On the third step her foot slipped sideways and she dropped off onto the grass. Her hands hit the ground first.

She stood up. She brushed her palms on her shorts.

"Again," she said.

She stepped back onto the log. Her arms went out. Her eyes were on the bark ahead of her feet.

Taro reached the far end and jumped down. He turned around. Emma was still on it, moving slowly, one careful step at a time.

Mei wobbled in the middle. Her arms waved. She did not fall.

She took another step.
