The workshop smelled different today. Not just sawdust — something deeper, something that sat in the back of the throat.

Vern stood at the bench with a small wooden box in his hands. It was pale, almost white. The grain ran across the lid in long, even lines.

He dipped a rag into a tin. The rag turned dark. The smell came stronger — sharp and warm at the same time.

"Linseed oil," he said, not looking up. "Watch."

He rubbed the rag across the wood. Where the oil touched, the pale wood turned gold. Then deeper. Then brown.

Emma leaned closer. The wood drank the oil like the ground drinks rain. The grain stood out darker, clearer.

"It changes," she said.

"It soaks," said Vern. "That's the first coat. It goes in. Then it dries. Then I do it again."

Taro reached out and touched the wet part. His finger came away shiny.

"Now your finger has a finish," Vern said. He almost smiled.

Emma watched the oil spread, slow and steady, darkening everything it touched. The smell hung around them, thick and patient.

The wood was not done changing. She could see that.
