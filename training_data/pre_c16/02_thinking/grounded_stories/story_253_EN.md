Vern held out a piece of wood. "Keep your eyes closed," he said.

Emma shut her eyes tight. He put the wood into her hands. It was cool. She ran her fingers along the surface. One side was smooth. The other had lines, like tiny rivers.

She turned it over. Then she lifted it to her nose.

She smelled dust. She smelled something sharp and green, even in winter.

"Oak," she said.

Vern did not say anything. He put another piece into Taro's hands.

Taro held it. He scratched the surface with his thumbnail. Then he sniffed it.

"Pine," he said.

Vern nodded. He took the pieces back and laid them on the bench.

Taro opened his eyes. "How did you know?" he said.

Emma looked at her fingers. "Oak feels different," she said. "The grain is deep."

"And it smells old," Taro said. "Pine smells bright."

Vern picked up a third piece. He held it out without speaking.

Emma closed her eyes again.
