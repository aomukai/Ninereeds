The lane was dry. The dust rose in small puffs where Emma walked.

Gran led them to the mill. Mei was already there, standing by the stream.

The water was low. Very low. Emma could see the stones at the bottom. They were brown and cracked. The stream did not hurry. It barely moved.

Mei pointed. "The wheel stopped."

It was true. The great wooden wheel stood still. Water dripped from one blade, slow and thin. A single drop. Another. Then nothing.

Gran put her hand on the wheel. It did not turn.

"Too dry," she said. "The stream cannot push it."

Emma knelt by the water. It was warm on her fingers. There was no sound to it. Just a trickle between the stones. A small wet line. Nothing more.

"The grain?" Mei asked.

Gran shook her head. "We wait for rain. The mill cannot grind without the wheel. The wheel cannot turn without the stream."

Emma stayed by the water. She watched the trickle. It moved over one stone, around another, then disappeared into the mud.

The wheel made a small sound. A tick. Like something settling.

The mill was silent. The stream was dry. There was nothing to do but wait.
