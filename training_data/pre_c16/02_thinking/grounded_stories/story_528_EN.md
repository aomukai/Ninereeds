The lane was still dark. Gran walked with her basket over her arm. The village was quiet. No one else was about.

Then she saw the light. It came from Hana’s bakery window — a warm yellow glow against the cold street.

Gran stopped and looked in. Inside, Hana stood at the shelf. She was lining up loaves. Big ones at the back. Small ones at the front. Her apron was white with flour. A fine dust of it clung to her sleeves.

Gran tapped on the glass. Hana looked up and smiled. She came to the door and opened it.

The warm smell came out into the cold street. Bread. Yeast. Something sweet underneath.

“You are early,” Hana said.

“I saw the light,” said Gran.

Hana stepped aside. The bakery was full of loaves. They sat in rows on the shelf, brown and round. The heat from the oven pressed against Gran’s face.

“I will take one,” Gran said.

Hana reached for the biggest loaf. She set it in Gran’s basket. The basket felt warm through the cloth.

Gran stood in the doorway a moment longer, holding the warmth.
