Emma stood in front of Yun's stall. The wooden table was low, just above her waist. She had five coins in her pocket. They were warm from her hand.

Gran had said: bread from the market.

Yun was weighing something on her small brass scale. She did not look up, but she knew Emma was there.

The bread was on the left. A round loaf with a dark crust. It smelled of the bakery and of Hana's hands.

Next to the bread was the honey.

A jar with a metal lid. The honey inside was the colour of late sunlight. It was thick and slow. Emma could see it through the glass. She wanted it. She wanted to hold the jar and feel how heavy it was. She wanted to taste it on the tip of her finger.

She took the coins out of her pocket.

The bread cost four coins.

The honey cost four coins.

She had five.

She could buy one. Not both.

Emma picked up the honey. The glass was cool. She held it in both hands. She looked at the light coming through it.

Then she put it down.

She picked up the bread. It was heavier than it looked. It was still slightly warm.

She put four coins on the table.

Yun nodded once.

Emma walked away with the bread. She did not look back at the honey.
