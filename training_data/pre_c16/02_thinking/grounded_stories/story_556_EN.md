The side street was quieter than the main street. The sound of the baker and the market was far away, like something under water.

Emma and Taro walked past the vet’s door and the doctor’s gate. Then they stopped.

Vern’s workshop door was open.

Inside, a lamp burned against the grey afternoon light. Vern stood at the bench with his back to the street. He was planing a board. The plane slid forward and a thin curl of wood lifted from the iron mouth, pale and smooth, and fell onto the floor.

The children stood in the doorway and watched.

The smell came out to meet them — sawdust and oil and something sharp and clean. The floor was covered in shavings. Some were long and curly. Some were short and broken. They lay in layers, pale yellow and white.

Vern did not look up. He pushed the plane again. The sound was a long, soft shaving sound, like something being unwrapped very carefully.

Taro leaned forward. Emma put her hand on the door frame. The wood there was smooth, worn by years of hands.

On the bench beside the board lay a row of tools. Each one had its own peg on the wall above. The vise held the board still. The shavings kept falling.

No one said anything. The sound and the smell and the quiet filled the doorway.
