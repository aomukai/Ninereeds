The cellar door was propped open with the iron bar. The five stone steps went down into cool air.

Emma held the lamp. Mei followed close behind. The shadows moved when the flame moved.

On the shelf, at the very back, stood one jar. Emma reached for it. The glass was cold. Inside were pale yellow pears in clear liquid.

The label said, *Pears — Early Autumn*.

Mei leaned in. “When was that?”

Gran’s voice came from the top of the stairs. “The week they were right. We put them away for the cold months.”

Emma turned the jar in her hands. The pears shifted inside. They looked soft. They looked like something from a long time ago.

“This is the last one,” she said.

The shelf beside it was empty. The other jars were gone — eaten in winter, one by one, when the trees were bare and the ground was hard.

Mei touched the glass. “What happens now?”

Emma held the jar. It felt heavy in her hands. She did not know the answer. But the pears were still there, sealed and waiting, in the cool dark of the cellar.
