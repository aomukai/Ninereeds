The lane was quiet. Emma walked alone. The sun was not high yet — it came low over the rooftops, long and yellow.

At the bottom of the lane, the smell hit her. Warm. Sweet. The smell of Hana's bakery, early in the morning.

The window was full of loaves. They stood in rows — big ones at the back, small ones at the front. The glass was steamed at the edges. Inside, the light was golden.

The door opened before Emma reached it.

Hana stood there. Her apron was white with flour. A fine dust of it sat on her sleeves and across the front where she had wiped her hands.

"Early today," Hana said.

Emma nodded. "Gran sent me."

Hana stepped back. The light from inside spilled past her onto the street — a warm square on the stone. The smell came with it, full and soft.

Emma stepped through the door.

The bakery was warm. The loaves sat on the counter in a row, still giving off heat. Hana picked one up and turned it in her hand. She held it out.

"Still warm," she said.

Emma took it. The loaf was heavy. The crust was firm. Through the paper, she could feel the heat against her palm.
