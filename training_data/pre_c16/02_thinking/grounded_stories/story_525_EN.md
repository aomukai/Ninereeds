Clara stood at the front of the room. She picked up the eraser.

The board was full of numbers — rows and rows of them, white against the dark grey. Clara pressed the eraser to the top corner and pulled it across.

The numbers disappeared.

Emma watched. The chalk dust fell in a fine white cloud. It drifted down onto the ledge, onto Clara’s sleeve, onto the floor by her feet. The dust settled. The board was blank.

Clara wiped again. And again. Her hand moved in long, straight lines. The white cloud grew thicker, then thinned, then was gone.

Taro sneezed. The sound echoed in the quiet room.

Clara put the eraser down. She picked up a piece of chalk. It was new — long and white, unbroken. She wrote. The chalk made a soft scraping sound. A new sum appeared on the clean board.

Emma watched the numbers grow. First one, then another, then a line between them.

The board was full again. Then empty again. Then full.

Clara wiped her hand on her apron. She turned to face the class. The chalk dust was still on her fingers.
