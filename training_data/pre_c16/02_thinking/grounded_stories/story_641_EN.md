Emma walked ahead of Mei on the meadow path. Biscuit ran past them both and disappeared around the bend.

"The oak is just there," Emma said.

Mei stopped. She did not walk straight up to the tree. She stood back, on the grass, and looked at the whole thing first — the wide trunk, the low branch, the way the green leaves were small and new and bright against the dark bark.

Then she came closer. She put her hand flat on the bark. She did not tap it. She pressed, then lifted her palm and looked at the pattern it had left on her skin.

"There is a hole," she said.

Emma nodded. "A squirrel lives there."

Mei knelt. She put her hand into the hole up to her wrist. Her fingers touched the dry dark inside. She pulled them out and looked at the dust on them.

Biscuit pushed his nose into the roots at the base of the tree. He sniffed, sneezed, and walked in a circle.

Dou sat on the wall at the edge of the field. She watched. She did not come down.

Mei picked up a new leaf from the ground. She turned it over. She held it next to a leaf on the branch.

"Same," she said.

Emma nodded. "It fell off."

Mei placed the leaf back on the ground exactly where she had found it.
