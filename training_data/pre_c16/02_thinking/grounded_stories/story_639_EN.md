The lane was grey. The grass was wet. Emma’s shoes left dark prints on the stone.

Gran walked ahead. She did not speak. Taro followed, still pulling his jumper over his head. Biscuit trotted beside him, but his tail was low. He did not sniff anything.

The pond lay flat and still. Mist hung over it like a white cloth. The willow dipped into the water without a ripple. No wind. No sound.

Emma stopped at the edge. The surface was a mirror — pale sky, dark trees, the shape of the willow upside down.

A fish rose. A circle spread from the middle. It grew and grew and stopped.

A bird called once from the reeds. Then nothing.

Biscuit sat down. He did not bark. He did not splash. He watched the pond with his ears up.

Taro whispered, “Is it still morning?”

Gran nodded. “The first morning light. Before the day wakes up.”

Emma did not move. She watched the mist shift. She watched the circle on the water fade.

Everything was waiting.
