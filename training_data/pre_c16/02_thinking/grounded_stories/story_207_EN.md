The mill stood quiet. The wheel did not turn.

Gran stood on the stone path and looked at the water. The stream was pale and hard. Ice had grown across it in the night, thick at the edges, thin in the middle, still as glass.

Mei pressed her boot against the edge. The ice did not crack. She picked up a stick and tapped. A small break opened at the rim. Dark water moved underneath, slow and silent.

“Not enough,” Gran said. She touched the wheel. It did not shift. The wood was cold and damp and still.

They carried the grain sacks into the shed and left them there. The sacks were heavy. Mei set hers down on the dry stone floor and straightened the corner where it had folded.

Gran closed the shed door. The latch clicked. She stood for a moment looking at the mill.

“Tomorrow morning,” she said.

They walked back along the lane. The ground was hard underfoot. The wheel did not turn behind them. The mill was patient. So was the grain in the sacks. But the morning was cold and there was nothing else to do but wait.
