The side street was quiet.

Leaves lay on the stones — brown and yellow, curled at the edges. The vet’s door was closed. The doctor’s gate was latched. Vern’s workshop door was shut. No smoke came from the chimney.

Emma and Taro walked down the middle of the street. Their footsteps tapped against the stones and bounced back from the walls. Tap. Tap. Then nothing.

Biscuit walked ahead. His claws clicked. Then he stopped and pushed his nose into a pile of leaves. The leaves rustled. He sneezed.

Taro stood still and listened. “No one is here,” he said.

Emma looked up the street. Then down. The bench against the wall was empty. The lane beyond the houses was empty too.

She stepped onto a dry leaf. It cracked. The sound was loud in the quiet.

Biscuit circled the leaf pile once, then twice. He put his front paws on it and pushed. The leaves scattered. A few drifted down again.

Taro walked to the doctor’s gate. He put his hand on the latch but did not lift it.

“Should we go back?” Emma said.

Taro looked at the closed door. Then at the empty street. Then at Biscuit, who had found another leaf pile.

“Yes,” he said.

They turned. Their footsteps echoed again, and the side street went still behind them.
