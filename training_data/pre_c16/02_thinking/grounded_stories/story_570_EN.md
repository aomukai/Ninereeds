Taro stood in the doorway of Vern’s workshop. The air smelled of sawdust and something sharp and clean.

Vern was at the workbench, running his hand along a length of wood. He looked up.

Taro pointed at the wall. Rows of tools hung from wooden pegs. Each one had its own place.

“That one,” Taro said.

Vern put down the wood and crossed the room. He took the tool from its peg. It had a wooden handle, smooth and dark where hands had held it.

“Spokeshave,” Vern said. He turned it over and put it in Taro’s hand.

The handle was warm. The metal blade was cool. Taro ran his thumb along the wood. It felt polished, almost soft.

Vern pointed to another tool on the wall. A wider blade. A handle that sat low.

Taro looked at it. He remembered the shape.

“Plane,” he said.

Vern nodded. He took it down and set it on the bench, flat side down.

Taro pointed again. A thin blade with a sharp end.

“Chisel,” he said.

Vern did not take it down. He just nodded again, once.
