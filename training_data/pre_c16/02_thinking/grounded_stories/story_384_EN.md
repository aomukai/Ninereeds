The grass was white.

Emma stood at the bottom of the hill and looked up. Every blade was stiff and pale. The ground crunched when she put her foot down.

She took another step. Crunch.

Biscuit ran past her. His paws left dark prints in the white grass. He stopped, sniffed the air, and sneezed.

Taro came up beside her. "It's cold," he said. His breath made a small cloud.

Emma kept walking. The frost crackled under her boots. The hill was very quiet. No birds. No wind. Only the sound of their feet and Biscuit's heavy breathing.

They reached the top. Bello was already there, waiting. His ears stood up like flags. His paws were wet.

The sun was coming over the far edge of the hill. It looked pale and thin, but it touched the grass.

Emma watched. Where the light fell, the frost began to change. The white turned to wet. The stiff blades softened. A line of melt crept down the hill like a slow tide.

Taro put his hand on a patch of wet grass. "It's going away," he said.

Biscuit sat down. His dark footprints were already fading.
