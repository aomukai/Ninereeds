The oak was different now.

Last month the branches were bare. Now the leaves were out — small and bright green, so new they looked wet.

Emma stood under the tree and looked up. The leaves moved in the light. Some were still very small, no bigger than her thumbnail. Others had already grown wide.

Mei pointed to a branch low down. “That one is bigger.”

Emma looked where she pointed. Then she looked at a branch above it. “That one is smaller.”

She reached up and touched the small leaf. It was soft and thin. The big leaf on the lower branch was darker and firmer.

They stood side by side and looked.

Emma touched one leaf. Mei touched another. They did not say anything for a moment.

Then Mei pointed again. “The same tree. But the leaves are not the same size.”

Emma looked at the branch, then at the trunk, then at the ground where the roots came up. She did not know why some leaves grew faster than others.

She touched the small leaf again. It would get bigger. She could see that already.
