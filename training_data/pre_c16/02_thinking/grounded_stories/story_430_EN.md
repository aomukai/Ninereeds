Gran lifted the onion rope from the beam in the root cellar. It was heavy and brown, and the onions swung as she carried it up the stone steps.

In the kitchen, she laid it flat on the wooden table. Emma leaned close. Taro stood on his toes to see.

“Some are not good anymore,” Gran said. She untied the rope and the onions rolled apart.

Emma picked one up. The bottom was soft when she squeezed it. A green shoot poked out of the top.

“This one is sprouting,” she said.

“And this one is soft,” said Taro, holding another. He pressed his thumb into the side. It gave way easily.

Gran put a basket on the table. “Good ones in here. Sprouted ones on the board. Soft ones on the floor for the compost.”

Emma sorted carefully. She turned each onion in her hands before she decided. Taro worked faster, grabbing and dropping. Gran did not tell him to slow down.

The good pile grew in the basket. The sprouted ones lay on the cutting board. The soft ones made a small heap on the floor.

“That is all of them,” Emma said.

Gran looked at the piles. She nodded once. Then she swept the soft onions into her hands and carried them to the garden door.
