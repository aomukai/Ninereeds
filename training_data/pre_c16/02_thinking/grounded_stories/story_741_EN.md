Emma set her apples on the stone bench in two piles. She counted them. Seven in one. Seven in the other.

Taro came over with his basket. He looked at her piles. He looked at his own apples.

"Why two piles?" he said.

"One for Mei," Emma said. "She likes apples."

Taro put his basket down. He picked up his apples one by one and set them on Emma's pile. Six. Then seven. Then he stopped.

Emma watched him. "Those are yours."

"I know," Taro said.

He reached past her and picked up a slice of apple from the bowl on the bench. It was pale and wet at the edges where Gran had cut it. He bit into it. The crunch was loud in the quiet garden.

Biscuit sat down and watched the pile. Bello circled the bench once and lay down in a patch of sun.

Gran came out of the kitchen with another bowl in her hands. She looked at the two piles on the bench, then at Taro, who was still chewing.

"Equal?" she said.

"Almost," Emma said.

Gran set the new bowl down beside the others. "Then we'll share the rest," she said, and turned back inside.
