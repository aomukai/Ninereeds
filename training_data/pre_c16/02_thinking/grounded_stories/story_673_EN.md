Gran stood still and pointed up into the oak tree. Emma and Taro stopped where they were and looked.

A robin sat in the fork of a high branch. She was very still. Her head turned once, then again.

Then she flew.

Emma saw the nest. It was tucked into the fork, made of moss and dry grass and a few pale feathers.

She stepped closer. Taro stepped closer too.

Inside the nest were three eggs. They were very small. They were blue — a soft blue, like the sky early in the morning — and they had brown speckles on them, tiny and even.

Taro reached up.

"No," Gran said, quiet but firm. "Do not touch."

"But I want to see," Taro whispered.

"You can see from here," Gran said. "If you touch, the mother might not come back. Step back. Be quiet. Watch."

Emma stepped back. Taro stepped back too. They stood on the path and watched the nest.

The robin came back. She landed on the edge of the nest. She settled over the eggs.

Emma counted in her head. Three. Small and blue and speckled.

They watched without moving.
