Emma sat on the flat stone. She leaned forward and looked into the water.

The pond was different today. The water was not clear. It was grey and cloudy, like thin mud stirred through it.

She waited. She watched.

No fish.

Taro came up beside her. He had a stick. He pushed it into the water and stirred. Brown mud rose in a slow cloud. The water turned thicker, darker.

"Stop," Emma said.

Taro stopped. They both looked.

Nothing moved.

"Where are they?" Emma said.

Gran was on the bench under the willow. She did not look up. "They are still there," she said. "Deeper."

Emma stayed on the stone. She did not move. She watched the water settle. The mud sank. The cloud thinned.

The bottom of the pond came back — soft brown, a few old leaves. But no dark shapes. No flicker.

The water was cold. The air was cold. The sky above the pond was flat and grey.

Emma kept watching. She did not stop.

Taro dropped his stick into the water. It floated. He sat down on the grass and waited with her.

No fish came.
