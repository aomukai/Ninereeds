The cellar door was heavy. Emma held it open with the iron bar while Mei went down first.

The stone steps were cold through Emma's shoes. The lamp threw shadows that moved when she moved.

Mei stopped at the bottom. She pressed her nose close to the apple crate.

"It smells sweet," she said. "And sharp."

Emma came beside her. The smell was thick and warm. It made her think of something that had been sitting a long time.

She picked an apple from the top. It was still firm in her hand, but the skin felt a little sticky.

"Gran said these are turning," Emma said.

Mei took it from her and held it close to her nose. She closed her eyes.

"I can smell the inside," she said. "It's not the same as the outside."

Emma picked another apple. This one was smooth and dry. She put it back.

The lamp flickered. Their shadows grew big on the stone wall.

Mei opened her eyes. She put the sticky apple back in the crate.

"Gran will know which ones to use first," Emma said.

They went back up the steps. The cellar door fell shut behind them with a soft, heavy sound.
