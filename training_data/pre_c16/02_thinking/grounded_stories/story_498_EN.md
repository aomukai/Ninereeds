Mei put something on the nature table.

It was a shell. Curled and pale, with a hole in the middle where the spiral stopped.

Emma leaned close. “Where did you find it?”

“In my garden,” Mei said. “Under the big leaf. It was just sitting there.”

Emma picked it up. It was light in her hand. Almost nothing. She turned it over. The inside was smooth and empty. She held it to her ear.

“I can’t hear anything,” she said.

Taro came over. He poked a finger into the hole. “The snail is gone.”

“It was alive,” Clara said from her desk. She did not look up. She was writing something on a card. “A snail lives in a shell. When it dies, the shell stays.”

Emma put the shell back on the table. She touched the edge with one finger. It was thin. It could break.

Mei moved the shell to the front of the table, where the light from the window caught it.

Emma looked at it for a long time. Then she went back to her desk.
