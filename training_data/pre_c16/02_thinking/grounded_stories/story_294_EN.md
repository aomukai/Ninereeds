The workshop was quiet. Sawdust lay in curls on the floor. Emma stood at the long bench and ran her finger across a plank. The wood felt smooth one way, rough the other. She stopped at a small dent. Something had pressed in and left its mark.

Vern came to the bench. He picked up a scrap of pale wood and held it out. “Press your nail in.”

Emma pushed her thumbnail into the surface. It sank. A small crescent stayed behind.

“Pine,” Vern said. “Soft.”

He put a darker piece beside it. Darker and heavier. “Try this one.”

Emma pressed again. Her nail slid across the top. Nothing. No dent at all.

“Oak,” he said. “Hard.”

She picked up both pieces. One light in her hand, the other solid. She pressed each with her finger again. Pine gave. Oak did not.

On the bench lay a box of nails. She touched one. Cold and hard. Hard like the oak. She tapped it against the darker plank. A small click. A small mark.

Vern was planing a long edge. The shaving curled away in one piece. Emma watched the grain run through the wood. Smooth and rough. Hard and soft. She ran her finger across the oak again. There was no dent. There was nothing but the grain, running straight and even.
