Gran walked first. Mei followed.

The lane was still dark. Mist sat low over the ground, grey and cool. Mei could feel it on her face.

They reached the mill. The building was a dark shape against the sky. No light inside. No sound except water.

Gran lifted the latch. The door swung open.

Inside, the air was cold and still. The wheel stood dark above the stream. It was not turning.

Gran went to the sluice lever. She put her hand on it. She did not push it yet.

Mei stood in the doorway. The floor was damp. The stream below moved slow and quiet. A bird called once, somewhere in the mist. Then silence again.

Gran pushed the lever. Just a little.

The wheel creaked. It turned once. Then again. Water ran into the channel, dark and cold.

Mei watched. The wheel turned slowly. Drops fell from the paddles into the stream below.

Gran said nothing. She stood beside the wheel and watched it turn.

The light was coming now. Pale and thin. The mist began to lift.

Mei put her hand on the cold stone wall. The wheel turned. The morning had started.
