Owen stood at the gate. His hand rested on the top bar. His sheep were spread across the field.

Emma came up the lane. She stopped beside him and watched.

Owen raised one finger. He pointed at the sheep on the left side of the slope. His lips moved. He counted without making a sound. His head turned slowly as his finger moved across the field.

"Twenty," Owen said. Then he looked again. The sheep had moved while he counted. Three had walked behind the hedge. Two more had joined them.

Owen put his finger down. He pressed his lips together.

"How many?" Emma asked.

Owen looked at the field again. He counted with his eyes this time. "Might be fifteen," he said. "Might be twenty."

"You don't know?"

"Not certain," Owen said. "They keep moving. Hard to count something that does not stand still."

The sheep shifted again. One bleated. The sound carried across the grass.

Emma watched them. She tried to count. But they moved, and she lost her place.
