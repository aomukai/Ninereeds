The hill was wet. The grass bent flat where the rain had pushed it.

Emma’s shoes slipped on the slope. She put a hand down. The ground was soft.

“Look,” Taro said.

He pointed down. The stream at the bottom was not the same as before. It was wider. It was fast. The water was brown.

Owen stood by the gate. He did not say anything. He just nodded at the stream.

Emma walked closer. The bank had changed. A new cut ran down the slope—a narrow track where the water had pushed through. The mud was smooth and dark, like something had polished it.

“The rain did that,” Owen said.

Taro crouched. He put one finger into the cut. The soil came away easily.

“It carried the dirt down,” Emma said.

Owen pointed to the meadow below the stream. A fan of brown mud spread out across the grass, thin and flat, like a tongue.

“That’s where it stopped,” he said.

The stream kept running. Fast. Brown. The sheep on the far slope did not look up.
