Yun lifted the bag of grain from the cart. She set it on the mill floor with a solid thump.

Then she lifted the bag of flour from the same grain. She carried it to the table. It came up easier.

Taro watched her arms. "That one is lighter," he said.

Yun nodded. "Same grain. Different weight."

Taro found the scale in the corner. It had a metal pan and a row of brass weights. He dragged it closer. First the grain bag. He had to use both hands. The scale tipped. The brass weight on the other side lifted and he added another.

"Eight," he said.

Then the flour bag. He lifted it onto the pan. One brass weight was enough. The scale stayed still.

"Five," he said.

Mei stood at the hopper. She brushed flour dust from her sleeve. "The weight goes into the dust and the air," she said. "The millstone pushes it out. The grain is whole. The flour is not."

Taro looked at the two bags. The grain bag was full and tight. The flour bag was soft and loose. He lifted each one again, one in each hand. The difference was clear.
