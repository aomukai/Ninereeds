The hill was wet. Two days of rain had soaked into the grass. The ground felt soft under Emma's boots.

Taro ran. He took three steps down the slope and his feet went out from under him. He sat down hard. The mud was dark and wet. It spread across the back of his coat.

"Oof," he said.

Emma did not run. She turned sideways and put one foot down. Then the other. She kept her knees bent. Each step was slow and careful.

Taro tried to stand. His boots slipped again. He sat down again. Biscuit came running past him, legs going fast, and slid all the way to the bottom on his belly. At the bottom he stood up and shook. Mud flew everywhere. Some of it hit Taro's cheek.

Taro wiped his face with his sleeve. The sleeve was already muddy.

Emma kept walking sideways. One step. Then another. Her boots held.

Taro stood up more carefully. He put his feet flat. He bent his knees the way Emma had. He took one step. Then another. His boots held.

At the bottom, Biscuit shook again. Taro looked at his coat. Emma looked at the hill.

"It's steeper when it's wet," she said.

Taro nodded. They walked back to the cottage. Biscuit left muddy pawprints all the way up the lane.
