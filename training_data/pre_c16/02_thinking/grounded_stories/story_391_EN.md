Gran lifted the heavy wooden door. It was wider than her shoulders and thicker than her arm. She propped it open with the iron bar.

"Come on," she said, and went down.

Emma went first. The stone steps were worn in the middle. They were steeper than the stairs in the house. She counted them. Five.

The air changed. It was cool on her face. It smelled of earth and something sweet and something sharp all at once.

Gran lit the lamp. The flame was small at first, then it grew. The shadows jumped on the wall.

Taro came down behind Emma. He stopped on the bottom step.

"It's dark," he said.

"Not for long," said Gran.

She held the lamp up. The light found the shelves. Jars stood in rows. Onions hung from the ceiling beam in a long rope. Crates of apples lined the wall. The floor was earth, not stone.

Emma touched one of the apples. It was cool and firm.

Gran lifted an onion from the rope and turned it in her hand.

"Good," she said. "Still sound."

The lamp flickered. The shadows moved across the wall. The room was still and cool and full of things that would last through winter.
