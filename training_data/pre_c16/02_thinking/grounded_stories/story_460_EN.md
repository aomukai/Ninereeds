The fence ran along the meadow path. On the other side, Owen’s sheep stood in the grass.

Bello stopped. He put his head through the gap between two rails. His ears stood up. His tail went still.

The sheep did not move. They looked at him with their yellow eyes and went on chewing.

Bello took a step closer. A sheep took a step back. Their hooves made soft sounds on the grass. Their wool was thick and dirty.

Bello watched. He did not bark. He did not run.

Biscuit came up behind him. Biscuit looked at the sheep once. Then he put his nose to the ground and kept walking down the path. He had smelled something more interesting.

Bello stayed. One sheep turned its head. It had a bell on its neck that gave a small clink. Bello’s ear flicked. He backed up a step. Then another.

He did not leave. He sat down just out of reach and watched the sheep watch him.

Owen leaned on the gate further along the field. He did not call out. He just watched too.
