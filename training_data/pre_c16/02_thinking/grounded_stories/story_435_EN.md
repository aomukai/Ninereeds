Gran said to fetch a jar of pickled cabbage from the root cellar. Taro went down the stone steps first. Mei followed. The lamp threw a moving shadow on the wall.

The jar was on the low shelf. Taro reached for it with both hands. He pulled it toward his chest. The jar was heavier than he was ready for. His fingers slipped. The jar tilted.

It hit the floor.

The sound was sharp and then wet. Glass scattered across the earth floor. Liquid spread out in a dark circle. Cabbage lay in pieces among the glass. The pieces were pale and glossy. The liquid made the earth dark.

Mei stepped back onto the bottom step. She did not say anything. Taro looked at the floor. He did not move.

Gran's footsteps came down the stairs. She carried a cloth and a bucket. She looked at the floor. She did not shout.

"Step back," she said. "Both of you. I will move the glass. You bring the cloth where I point."

Taro picked up the pieces she pointed at. He put them in the bucket. Mei held the lamp steady. The pieces were sharp. The cloth soaked up the liquid. The earth floor was darker where the liquid had been. The smell of pickled cabbage filled the root cellar.

Gran swept the last fragments into the bucket. She checked the floor with her fingers. She found one more piece, very small, and put it in the bucket.

"Careful," she said. "That is the thing about glass. It goes everywhere."
