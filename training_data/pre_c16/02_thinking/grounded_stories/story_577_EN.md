Riku stopped his red bicycle outside the post box. The bag on his shoulder leaned to one side. It was full.

Emma and Taro stood at the corner of the lane. They watched.

Riku pulled letters out of his bag. One. Two. Three. He slid each one into the slot of the red post box. The letters landed inside with a soft sound.

He saw them watching. He nodded once. Then he got back on his bicycle.

The bicycle moved slowly at first. Riku's feet pushed the pedals. His bag swayed. Then he went faster down the main street. The red bicycle got smaller and smaller.

Emma looked at Taro.

"His bag was full," she said.

Taro nodded. "Where do the letters go?"

Emma did not know. She walked to the post box. She put her hand on the side. It was warm from the sun.

She could not see inside. But she could hear nothing now. The letters were already gone, somewhere down the street with Riku and his red bicycle.
