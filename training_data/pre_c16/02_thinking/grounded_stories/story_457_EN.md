The path was not the same.

Branches lay across it. Leaves were pressed flat into the mud by the rain. The storm had come in the night and gone again, and this was what it left behind.

Biscuit pushed through first. He put his nose into the wet leaves and shoved. His shoulders worked. He did not wait to see if the path was clear—he made it clear.

Bello followed. But he checked left before he stepped. Then right. Then left again. He stepped over a branch instead of through it. He looked where Biscuit had already gone, and then he looked somewhere else.

At the top of Gran's gate, Dou sat.

She was clean. Her fur was dry. She had not walked through anything wet or muddy. She sat with her tail wrapped around her front paws, and she watched them pass.

Biscuit did not look up. Bello did. He stopped. He looked at Dou on the gate. Dou did not move. She blinked once, slowly.

Bello turned and followed Biscuit.

Dou stayed where she was, clean and dry, watching the path.
