The hedge along the meadow path was full of berries. Red ones and black ones, tucked between the leaves.

Taro stopped first. He reached into the hedge, careful of the thorns, and picked a red berry. He put it in his mouth.

His face went tight. His eyes squeezed shut.

"Sour," he said, and spat it out onto the grass.

Emma looked closer. The red berries were bright and easy to see. The black ones hung lower, half-hidden under the leaves. She picked one. It was soft and warm from the sun.

She put it in her mouth.

It was sweet. Not as sweet as sugar, but sweet in a different way — a taste that spread slowly.

"Try a black one," she said.

Taro picked one. He chewed. He nodded.

Biscuit pushed his whole nose into the hedge. Leaves rustled. He pulled back with a leaf stuck flat to his nose, right between his eyes.

He sneezed. The leaf flew off and landed on the ground.

Taro laughed. Emma picked another black berry. From the path, the hedge looked like one green wall. But if you stopped and looked closely, it was full of different things.
