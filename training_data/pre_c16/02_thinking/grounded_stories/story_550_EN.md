The snow came in the night. Emma woke and the light in the room was different — soft and still. She pressed her face to the cold glass.

The world was white.

Taro was already pulling on his boots by the time she came down. Gran had the kettle on and a scarf in her hand.

“We’ll go to the village,” she said. “Hana will have the bread warm.”

The lane was muffled. Their footsteps made a sound Emma had never heard — a soft creak, then silence. The hedges wore thick white caps. Every twig was outlined.

The main street was empty. Untouched.

Stefan’s footprints went from the post box to the baker’s door, one clean line. No other marks. The horse chestnut stood black against the white, its branches holding snow in perfect shapes.

Hana’s chimney smoked straight up into the cold air.

Emma stepped off the path and put her foot down in the fresh snow. It came up to her ankle. Taro followed, making a second track beside hers.

Gran walked where Stefan had walked, her boots fitting into the prints that were already there.

The baker’s door was closed. A curl of steam rose from the edge of the roof.
