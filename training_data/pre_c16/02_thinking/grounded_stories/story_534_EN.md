The bicycle bell rang at the garden gate. *Ring-ring.*

Emma looked up from the grass. Riku was there, one foot on the ground, the other on the pedal. His red bicycle leaned against the gatepost.

"Package for Gran," he said.

Gran came out of the cottage, wiping her hands on her apron. Riku handed her a small box tied with brown string. It was not much bigger than Emma's hand.

"Sign here," Riku said. He held out a small paper and a pen. The pen was blue and had a clip.

Gran took the pen. She wrote her name on the paper. The pen made a scratching sound.

She handed the paper back. Riku tucked it into his bag.

"It's warm," Emma said. She touched the package. The cardboard was soft with heat.

Gran turned the box in her hands. "It will keep," she said.

Riku pushed off from the gate. The bicycle wobbled once, then straightened. He rode down the lane. The leaves on the stones shifted behind him — brown and dry, lifting and settling.

Emma watched until the red bicycle was gone.

Gran carried the warm package into the house.
