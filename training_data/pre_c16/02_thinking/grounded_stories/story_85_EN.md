Taro ran ahead down the meadow path, his shoes kicking up little puffs of dust. Biscuit trotted behind Emma for a while, nose busy in the grass, then dashed past her to catch up with Taro. Emma walked slowly, stopping sometimes to touch the feathery tops of the long grass at the path’s edge.  

A gap opened between them. Taro was far ahead now, almost at the bend where the path dipped toward the pond. Biscuit looped back, circled Emma once, then bounded forward again.  

Then Taro stopped. He crouched by a patch of clover, his back to Emma. Biscuit reached him first, sniffing at whatever had caught his eye. Emma kept walking, her pace steady. By the time she reached them, Taro was poking a stick at a beetle making its way across the path.  

"Look," he said, without turning.  

Emma crouched beside him. Biscuit lost interest and trotted ahead again, his tail waving like a flag.  

After a minute, Taro stood. He walked on, but slower now, letting Emma stay beside him. The gap closed. Biscuit glanced back once, then disappeared around the bend alone.
