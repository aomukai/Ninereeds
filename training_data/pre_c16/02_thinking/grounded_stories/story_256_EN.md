Vern’s workshop was warm. Sunlight fell through the open door and landed on the workbench in a bright square. Sawdust floated in the light.

Emma stood at the edge of the bench. She watched Vern’s hands. He was marking a line on a piece of wood. The pencil made a small scratch as he drew it along the edge of a metal square.

He straightened. He did not look at her. He held out his hand, palm up.

“Chisel,” he said.

Emma turned. The tools hung on pegs above the bench, all in a row. She knew the chisel. It had a wooden handle worn smooth and dark at the end. She lifted it from its peg and put the handle into his open palm.

His fingers closed around it. He took it and turned toward the wood. The blade found the line he had marked. He pushed. A thin curl of wood lifted away from the cut.

He did not say thank you. He did not look at her. He worked the chisel along the line, and the wood opened where he guided it.

Emma watched his hands. They did not hurry. They did not pause. They knew what came next before the cut was finished.
