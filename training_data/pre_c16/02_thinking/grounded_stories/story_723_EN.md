Emma pushed up her sleeves. The flour sack was open on the table. The big wooden bowl was waiting.

“Wash your hands first,” Gran said.

Emma stopped. She looked at her hands. They looked clean.

“They’re clean,” she said.

“Are they?” Gran did not look up. She was measuring salt into a small cup.

Emma went to the sink. She turned the tap. Cold water ran over her fingers.

She took the soap. It was a pale block, smooth on one side from use. She rubbed it between her palms. White foam appeared.

She rubbed between each finger. She rubbed around her thumbs. She rubbed her wrists, where the skin was thin and the soap felt different.

The water turned grey.

She rinsed. She held her hands out, dripping.

Gran looked. She nodded once.

“Now you can touch the dough.”

Emma dried her hands on the cloth by the sink. The flour sack was still open. The bowl was still waiting.

She put her hands into the flour. It was soft and cool. It sifted through her fingers like fine dust.
