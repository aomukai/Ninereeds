The dip in the lane was full. After the night of rain, the puddle stretched from one hedge to the other. The stones underneath were gone. The water was brown and still.

Taro stopped at the edge. He looked at the water. He looked at his boots. He stepped in.

The water came over the top of his boot. Cold. He felt it through his sock.

Emma stayed on the dry stones. She watched the water creep toward the toe of her boot. She took a step back. Then another. She went around, where the grass was wet but the ground was solid.

Biscuit did not go around. He went straight through. The water splashed high. His legs went dark and wet.

Bello followed. He went in deep. The water came up to his belly. He sneezed and kept going.

A sound came from the lane — a slow creak of wheels.

Riku came around the corner on his red bicycle. He saw the puddle. He slowed down. He steered to the edge, where the water was shallow. He rode through carefully. The front wheel sent a small wave. The back wheel sent another.

He passed the children and lifted one hand from the handlebars.

"Wet enough for you?"

Then he was gone, the bicycle wobbling slightly on the dry stones beyond the puddle.

Taro took another step. The water was deeper here. He felt it climb up his calf.

He pulled a stick from the hedge and dropped it in the puddle. It floated. He measured the deep part with the stick. The stick went straight down.
