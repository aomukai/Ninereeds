Emma stood under the oak tree and looked up.

High in the fork of the branches, something moved. A robin flew away.

"She left something," Emma said.

Taro climbed onto the low branch. He pulled himself higher and looked into the fork.

"Eggs," he whispered. "Three of them. They are blue."

Emma climbed up beside him. She held the branch and looked.

The nest was round and neat. Inside, three small eggs lay close together. They were blue, pale blue, like a piece of sky.

Gran came walking across the field. She stood below them and looked up.

"Don't touch," she said. "The robin will come back."

For several days, the robin sat on the nest. Emma and Taro checked from the ground. The robin watched them with her bright eye but did not move.

Then one morning, the eggs had cracks.

Small cracks. Thin lines across the blue shells.

The next day, the cracks were wider. A piece of shell fell down.

Then the chicks came out. They were small and bare. They opened their mouths very wide.

The parent robin came with food. She put it into the open mouths. Then she flew away and came back with more.

For weeks, the robins brought food. The chicks grew bigger. Their mouths were always open.

Then one day, the nest was empty.
