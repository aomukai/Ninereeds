The stream was not the same stream.

After three days of rain, it was high and brown and fast. It pushed against the bank where Mei and Emma stood. It made a sound like a constant low growl.

The wheel turned faster than Emma had ever seen it turn. Water hit the blades and flew off in white sheets. The spray was so thick it made a froth on the surface of the stream below. The wheel did not creak and groan the way it did on dry days. It hummed. A wet, spinning hum that Emma felt in her chest.

Biscuit stood at the edge of the bank, front paws planted, and barked. The wheel did not stop. The water did not slow. He barked again, louder, and the wheel spun on.

Mei pulled Emma back a step. "Stand here," she said. "You do not want to get wet."

But the spray reached them anyway. Fine cold drops landed on Emma's arms, her face, the back of her neck. She did not move. She watched the wheel turn and turn, faster than she had ever seen it, and felt the noise in her bones.
