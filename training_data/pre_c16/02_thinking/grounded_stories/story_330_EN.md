Stefan walked his slow beat along the main street. His hands were clasped behind his back. The sun caught his badge and made it flash.

He passed the baker's. He passed the post box. Then he reached Yun's market stall.

Yun was straightening a row of onions. She saw him coming and gave a small nod. Stefan nodded back.

He stopped. He looked at the vegetables on the table — the cabbages, the beans, the carrots in a wooden box.

Emma was standing at the front of the stall. She was holding a carrot. It was long and straight and still had a little earth on it.

Stefan looked at the carrot. Then he looked at Emma.

"That is a good carrot," he said.

Emma looked at the carrot. Then she looked at it again. It was a good carrot.

Mei was behind the stall, beside Yun. She was watching.

Stefan did not buy anything. He did not take the carrot. He just stood there for a moment, looking at the vegetables. Then he put his hands back behind his back and walked on.

His walk was slow. His pace did not change. He passed the stall and kept going down the main street.

Yun did not watch him go. She was already tidying the onions again. But Mei watched. The sun was bright. Stefan's badge was small and far away now, and then it was gone.
