Emma knelt in the warm earth. The bean plants were tall now, taller than her, climbing the sticks Gran had pushed in months ago. She touched a leaf. It was broad and soft and very green.

She found a pod hanging low. It was fat and pale green, smooth in her hand. She held it up to the light. Inside, something made a small shadow.

She pressed her thumb against the seam. The pod snapped open with a clean sound. Inside, five beans lay in a row. Each one was the same shape. Each one was pale and plump.

"Gran," she said.

Gran was at the herb trough, pinching leaves. She looked over.

"I found the seeds."

Gran came and stood beside her. She looked at the open pod in Emma's hand.

"Those are the seeds."

Emma looked at the beans. Then she looked at the plant, and the stick, and the ground where the seed had gone in.

"The seed went in the ground," she said. "And the plant grew. And the plant made the pod. And the pod holds new seeds."

Gran nodded. "That is what beans do."

Emma closed the pod again. It did not snap shut. It stayed open, showing the row inside. She laid it in her palm and carried it toward the kitchen.
