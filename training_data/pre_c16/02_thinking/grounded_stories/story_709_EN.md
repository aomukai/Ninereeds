Emma knelt by the vegetable patch. Gran pushed a finger into the soil and dropped a seed into the hole.

"It looks like nothing," Emma said.

Gran covered it and pressed the earth flat.

Every morning Emma checked. For five days the soil stayed the same. Then on the sixth morning a tiny loop of green pushed through.

Taro came to look. "That's it?"

"It's a sprout," Emma said.

She watered it. The sun came and went. The sprout grew taller. It put out small leaves. Then it reached for the cane and began to climb.

The vine grew fast now. One morning Emma found a flower. Small and pale yellow.

"Look," she said.

Gran looked up from the herbs. "Something to watch."

The flower fell off. Where it had been, a thin green shape appeared. It got longer every day.

Emma touched it. Soft. Smooth.

She remembered the dry seed in her hand. Hard. Brown. Nothing like this.

"It's a bean," she said.

Taro came over. He touched the pod too. "Before it was a seed. Now it's food."

Emma looked at the vine. The pod hung where the flower had been. Weeks of sun and water and waiting. And here it was.
