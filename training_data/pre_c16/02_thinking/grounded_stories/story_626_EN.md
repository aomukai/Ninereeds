Gran wiped the bench with her sleeve before she sat. The wood was damp. A flat grey sky hung over the pond.

Mei sat beside her. She did not speak. She looked at the water.

The willow had dropped its leaves. Yellow leaves lay on the surface of the pond, still and flat. They did not drift. The water was so still that the leaves looked painted on.

Mei watched a single leaf. It had a brown edge and a curl at the tip. It did not move.

Gran put her hands on her knees. She did not say anything.

The air was cool. It smelled of damp wood and wet grass and something quiet that Mei could not name.

No fish today. The water was too still, too cold. The fish were somewhere else, deep down where the leaves did not reach.

Mei looked at the bench beside her. The wood was grey and the grain had risen. Gran had wiped it with her sleeve, but the damp was still there.

She looked back at the water.

The yellow leaves floated without moving. The willow hung over the edge of the pond. The air was still and cool.

Mei sat. She watched.

Gran sat beside her. She did not wipe the bench again.
