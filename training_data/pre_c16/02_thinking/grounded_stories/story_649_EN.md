Taro walked to the pond fast. Biscuit ran ahead, then circled back, then ran ahead again.

Taro pushed the reeds apart with his hands. He looked under the broad leaves. He crouched and looked into the dark spaces where the stems grew thick.

No frogs.

He walked to the flat stone and knelt. The water was still. A leaf floated on it, yellow at the edges, very still.

He waited. The water did not move. No frog appeared.

He stood and walked to the willow. He checked the edge where the roots went into the mud. Nothing.

Gran was on the bench. She did not say anything. She was looking at the water.

Biscuit sniffed the mud. He sneezed.

Taro came back to the flat stone and sat down. He put his hands on his knees. The pond was quiet. In summer it was full of splashes and croaks and movement. Now it was still. The reeds were brown at the tips. The leaves on the willow were thin and yellow.

He missed the frogs. He missed the sound of them.

"I wanted to find one," he said.

Gran nodded. She did not say anything else.

Taro looked at the water. A fish moved somewhere deep, a shadow, gone. A leaf fell from the willow and landed on the surface without a sound.

The pond was different now. The season had changed.
