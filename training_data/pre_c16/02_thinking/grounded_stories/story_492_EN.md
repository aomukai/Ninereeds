Clara stood at the front of the room. She wrote a number on the blackboard with white chalk.

“What comes after seven?” she asked.

A few hands went up. Taro’s hand was up. Mei’s hand was up. Emma kept her hands on her desk. She knew the answer. Eight came after seven. She could see it in her head, clear as the chalk line on the board.

Clara looked across the rows. Her eyes stopped.

“Emma,” she said.

Emma’s stomach went tight. She looked at her desk. Then she looked up.

“Eight,” she said. Her voice was small. It did not sound like her voice. It sounded like a mouse voice, thin and quick.

Clara nodded. She turned and wrote an eight on the board, right after the seven. The chalk made a soft sound against the board.

“Good,” Clara said.

Emma let her breath out. Beside her, Mei did not look at her. But Mei’s hand was down now. The room went quiet again. Clara asked another question.
