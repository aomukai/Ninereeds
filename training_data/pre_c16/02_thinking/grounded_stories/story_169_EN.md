Taro stood under the apple tree, hands on his hips. "Eleven," he said.  

Emma frowned. She reached up and touched the nearest branch. "Count again."  

"I did." He pointed at each apple in turn, moving his finger through the air. "One, two, three—"  

Emma walked around the tree. She counted silently, lips moving. The wind shook the branches. A leaf drifted down.  

"Eleven," she said.  

Taro blinked. "Same as me?"  

Emma nodded. "Same as Taro."  

He crouched and picked up a windfall from the grass. "This makes twelve."  

"No." Emma took it from him. The apple was warm from the sun, slightly soft on one side. "This one fell. It doesn’t count."  

Taro peered up into the branches. "So eleven’s right?"  

Emma counted once more, quick this time. "Eleven."  

They looked at each other. The tree rustled. Somewhere beyond the wall, Biscuit barked.  

"Good," Taro said.
