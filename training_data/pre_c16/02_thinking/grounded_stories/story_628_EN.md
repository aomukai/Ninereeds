Taro stood very still at the edge of the pond.

A frog sat on a lily pad. Green and still. Its throat pulsed now and then.

Taro bent his knees. He put his hands out, fingers spread, ready to cup.

He took one step. Then another. The water sucked at his boots.

The frog jumped.

It did not jump away. It jumped straight up and came down in the same spot with a flat *plop*.

Taro's hands closed on nothing. Air. He looked at his empty palms.

Gran sat on the bench under the willow. She did not say anything.

Taro moved to the next lily pad. This frog was smaller. It sat on the edge where the pad curled up. He crept slower this time. His hands were ready.

The frog jumped before he was close. A long jump into the reeds. A splash. Gone.

Taro stood in the water. His hands hung at his sides.

Biscuit waded in beside him, nose working, tail wagging. He did not understand the problem.

Taro looked down at the water. At his hands. At the empty lily pad.

He took a breath. He moved to the third lily pad.

The frog there did not move. Taro leaned. He reached.

The frog jumped.
