The gate at the top of the field stood open. It was not supposed to be open.

Owen stood beside it, holding a piece of wire. He turned the wire in his fingers, looking at the latch.

Emma and Taro stopped on the lane. They stood where the grass met the stones and watched.

Owen bent the wire around the broken part of the latch. He pulled it tight. He pressed it with his thumb. Then he pulled again. The wire held.

The sheep watched from the field. They stood in a loose group, heads up, ears forward. One of them chewed slowly.

Owen stepped back. He lifted the gate and swung it closed. The latch caught. He pushed. It held. He pushed harder. It held.

He nodded once. Then he saw the children.

"Hot work," he said.

Emma looked at the gate. The wire was silver against the black metal. It was bent in a neat loop.

Taro pointed. "Is it fixed?"

"For now," Owen said. He picked up a stick from the grass and laid it across the top of the gate. "That will hold it until I find a proper tool."

The sun was high. The grass was dry and pale. The sheep had stopped watching and were grazing again.

Owen walked back down the field. The gate stayed closed behind him.
