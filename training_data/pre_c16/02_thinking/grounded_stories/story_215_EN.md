The mill wheel turned. Taro stood on the stone floor and watched the paddles go past. One. Two. Three. Mei had her hand on the sluice lever.

“Count how many turns in one minute,” she said.

Taro counted. The wheel was slow. The paddles passed like big wooden hands. When the minute ended, he had counted twenty-three.

“Half open,” Mei said. She pushed the lever down. The sluice opened all the way.

The water came faster. The wheel picked up. The paddles slapped the stream. Taro started counting again. This time the numbers came quicker. Forty-one turns in one minute.

“Same water,” Mei said. “More of it.”

Taro looked at the lever. “What happens if you close it all the way?”

Mei pulled the lever up. The sluice clicked shut. The stream noise dropped. The wheel slowed. Stopped.

“Count now,” Mei said.

Taro watched the still wheel. The paddles hung over the water. Nothing moved. “Zero,” he said.

Mei nodded. “No water. No turning.”

The wheel waited. The stream ran on underneath, quiet and dark.
