Riku's bicycle leaned against the mill wall. The red paint was dusty with flour.

He bent and lifted a sack onto the carrier. His arms went tight. His breath came out in a quick puff.

"That one is full," Mei said.

Riku nodded. He strapped it down with a leather belt. The bicycle shifted under the weight.

He reached for the second sack. He lifted it easily. Too easily.

"This one is half full," he said. He held it out to Mei.

She put her hands under it. The cloth was rough. The flour inside was soft and settled. She pushed up. The sack came off the ground without trouble.

"Feel the difference?" Riku asked.

She nodded. She carried it to the bicycle and set it beside the first sack. He strapped it on. The carrier was full now — one side heavy, one side light.

Riku stood back. He looked at the load. He pulled the strap tighter on the light side so nothing would slide.

Yun came to the mill door. "Two sacks today?"

"One and a half," said Riku. He swung his leg over the bicycle and pushed off. The front wheel wobbled once, then held.
