Gran set out two baskets on the grass under the apple tree.

One was large. One was small.

Emma picked an apple from the pile. She turned it over in her hand. It filled her whole palm — her fingers could not reach around it. She put it in the big basket.

Taro picked one up. His fist closed around it, but his thumb and middle finger did not touch. He looked at it. Then he put it in the small basket.

They worked side by side. Emma held each apple in her hand before she decided. Taro held each one in his fist. Big ones went to one side. Small ones went to the other.

Biscuit sat between the baskets and watched. He sniffed an apple that had rolled onto the grass. It was small. He pushed it with his nose and it rolled again.

When the pile was gone, both baskets were full. One basket held big apples. One basket held small apples.

Gran came over and looked into the big basket. She picked one up and turned it in her hand, the same way Emma had done.

"These," she said, "go into the pie."

She looked at the small basket.

"These I will cook down for sauce."

Emma looked at her own hands. They had done the sorting.
