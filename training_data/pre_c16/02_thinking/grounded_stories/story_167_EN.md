Gran set three cookies on the kitchen table. They were round, golden at the edges, and still warm from the oven. Biscuit sat very straight, his nose twitching.  

Emma touched the nearest one. "They're all the same size."  

"One, one, one," Gran said, sliding a cookie toward each of them. The last one stopped just short of Biscuit’s paw. He licked his chops but didn’t move.  

Taro grinned. "Three cookies, three of us. One each—it works out exactly."  

Emma picked hers up. It fit perfectly in her palm. "Just right," she said, the way Gran did when the soup wasn’t too hot.  

Gran broke hers in half. The crumbs scattered. Biscuit got the bigger piece before it even hit the table.  

Taro took a bite. "Fair," he said with his mouth full.  

Outside, the wind rattled the window. Inside, the kitchen smelled of sugar and warm dog.
