The compost corner was quiet. The sun was high and the garden felt still.

Bello came around the corner first. His ears stood up. His nose twitched.

Dou was already there. She sat on the warm earth near the compost frame. Her tail was wrapped around her front paws. One white ear pointed toward Bello.

Bello stopped. He did not step forward. He did not step back.

Dou did not move either. She blinked once, slowly.

Emma sat on the stone bench in the shade of the cottage wall. The stone was cool through her dress. She watched.

A leaf fell from the apple tree. It landed between them.

Bello looked at the leaf. Then he looked at Dou again.

Dou looked at the leaf. Then she looked at Bello.

Neither one moved.

The garden was very quiet. A bee went past. The compost smelled warm and dark.

Emma stayed on the bench. She did not call Bello. She did not call Dou.

Bello lowered his head a little. His tail did not wag. He was waiting.

Dou sat very still. Her eyes were half closed.

Emma counted her own breaths. One. Two. Three.

Then Dou stood up. She turned. She walked along the wall and out of sight.

Bello watched her go. He sniffed the leaf. Then he walked back toward the cottage.
