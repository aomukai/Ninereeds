The frost was on everything.

Emma followed Gran down the meadow path. The grass was stiff under her boots. It crackled when she stepped.

At the pond, the reeds stood white along the edge. They were not brown and bending like yesterday. They were stiff and pale, each blade coated in frost.

Emma reached out and touched one.

The frost crumbled off in her fingers. The reed underneath was dark and cold.

Gran stopped at the willow bench. She wiped the damp from the wood with her sleeve and sat.

The pond was still. A thin skin of ice lay over the water near the edge — not thick ice, just a layer, smooth and grey-white.

Emma found a small stone. She dropped it.

The ice cracked. The sound was sharp and short, like snapping a twig. The stone sank through and disappeared.

The crack spread in thin lines across the surface. Then the water was still again.

Emma looked at the reeds. At the crack in the ice. At her fingers, where the frost had melted into tiny wet dots.

The sun was just above the trees now. The frost on the reeds began to glisten at the tips.
