Emma threw the stick as far as she could. It spun once in the air and landed with a soft thump in the long grass. Biscuit bounded after it, ears flapping. Bello darted ahead, quick as a shadow.  

They reached it at the same time.  

Biscuit clamped his jaws around one end. Bello seized the other. Both dug their paws into the ground and pulled. The stick didn’t budge.  

Taro laughed. “They’ve got the same idea.”  

Biscuit growled low in his throat. Bello’s tail stiffened. They tugged in opposite directions—Biscuit toward the oak, Bello toward the pond—but the stick stayed between them. Then, without letting go, they began to run.  

It was an awkward dance. Biscuit’s heavy strides dragged left; Bello’s quick steps veered right. The stick swung in a slow arc across the field, carving a wobbly line through the grass. Emma and Taro trailed behind, watching as the dogs circled the apple tree, still locked in their tug-of-war.  

Near the garden gate, Bello’s grip slipped. The stick dropped. Biscuit pounced—but Bello was already on it again. Together, jaws tight, they carried it between them, bumping shoulders as they trotted back to the children.
