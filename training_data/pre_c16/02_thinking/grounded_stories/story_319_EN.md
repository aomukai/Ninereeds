Riku's bicycle leaned against the wall behind the market stall. The red paint was wet with mist.

He held out a package tied with brown string. Square. Heavy.

"For you," he said to Yun.

Yun took it. She turned it over in her hands. The label had her name on it in small, careful letters.

"Thank you," she said.

Riku did not leave. He stood beside the stall and looked up at the sky.

"Road is bad," he said. "Mud on the lane. Deep by the bend."

Yun nodded. She put the package under the table, beside the sack of potatoes.

Stefan came down the street. His hands were behind his back. He stopped beside the bicycle.

"More rain tonight," he said.

"Tomorrow too," said Riku.

Mei stood at the corner of the stall. She watched the package under the table. She did not ask what was inside.

Stefan looked at the sky. Riku looked at the mud on his tyres. Yun straightened the jars on her table.

"Best get on," said Riku.

He lifted his bicycle off the wall and swung one leg over. The basket was empty now. He rode slowly down the street, past Stefan, past the baker's window, towards the lane.

Stefan walked on.

Mei looked at the package under the table.

Yun did not open it.
