Clara wrote on the blackboard. The chalk made a soft tap-tap sound. The numbers were large and white.

Emma looked at the first sum. She put one hand under the desk. She counted on her fingers. Thumb. Finger. Finger. She pressed them down one at a time. The answer was three.

Taro looked at his paper. He counted in his head. His eyes went to the window. A leaf fell past the glass. He wrote his answer without looking down.

Mei did not count on her fingers. She drew the answer in the air first. A small circle. A line. Her finger moved. Then she picked up her pencil and wrote.

Clara walked between the desks. She stopped at Emma's row. She looked at the fingers still folded under the desk. Emma opened them. The number was right.

At the next desk, Taro had finished two sums already. His numbers were quick and crooked. Clara touched the page with her eraser. "Check this one," she said.

Mei wrote her answers in a straight row. The numbers were small and even. She did not need to count again.

The window was open a crack. The air smelled of leaves and damp. Clara erased the board with a long sweep. The chalk dust floated in the light.
