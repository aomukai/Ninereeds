Taro picked up a thin strip of wood from Vern's bench. It was pale and smooth, lighter than it looked.

He held it at both ends. He bent it.

The wood bowed. It bent a long way, curving like a hoop. Taro bent it further. The wood did not break. When he let go, it sprang back straight.

"That's ash," Vern said. He was planing a thicker piece at the bench. He did not look up.

Vern reached to the side without stopping his work. He handed Taro another strip, the same size, darker.

"Try that one."

Taro took it. He bent it. The wood resisted. He pushed harder. The wood gave a short, sharp crack and snapped in two.

The sound was quick and final. Taro held a piece in each hand.

Mei came closer. She looked at the broken ends. They were rough and pale inside.

"Same size," she said.

"Different wood," Vern said. He set the plane down and wiped his hand on his apron. "The ash is green, still springy. That pine was dry. Dry wood breaks."

Taro looked at the two pieces in his hands. One bent. One snapped. Both felt like wood.

He did not say anything. He put the broken pieces on the bench.
