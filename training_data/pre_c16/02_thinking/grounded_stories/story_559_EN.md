The side street was quiet. Stefan walked his beat with his hands behind his back. His boots made a slow, even sound on the stones.

Emma and Taro came around the corner. Biscuit trotted ahead of them, nose to the ground.

A dog ran past. It was fast and low to the ground. It did not stop. It did not look back.

Biscuit stopped. He lifted his head and watched the dog go.

Stefan stopped too. He bent down, one hand on his knee. The loose dog had slowed near the vet's door. Stefan read the tag on its collar. He read it twice.

He nodded.

Then he straightened and walked toward the dog. The dog let him come close. Stefan took the collar gently and led it down the side street, toward the low door with the sign.

Emma said, "He knows whose dog it is."

Taro said, "He read the name."

Biscuit sniffed the ground where the dog had been. Then he looked up the street and wagged his tail once, as if the story was over.
