The bottom of the lane was warm. The sun fell on the stone.

Emma heard a sound. A rattle. A creak.

Riku came around the corner on his red bicycle. His bag was full. It leaned to one side.

He saw them and slowed. The bicycle wobbled. He put one foot down on the stone.

“Emma. Taro.”

He reached into his bag. His hand came out with a letter.

The envelope was pale. On the front, in dark ink: Gran’s name. In the corner, a small stamp — bright, square, with a picture on it.

“For your grandmother,” Riku said.

He held it out. Taro took it. The paper was stiff. The flap was sealed.

Riku nodded. He pushed off with his foot. The bicycle rolled forward. He did not look back.

Emma watched him ride down the street. His bag flapped against his leg.

Taro turned the envelope over in his hands.

“Should we take it to Gran?” he said.

The bicycle was small now, far down the street. Red. Still moving.
