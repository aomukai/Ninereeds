It was a hot day. The kind where the air sat still and the grass felt dry underfoot.

Gran packed a bottle of water into a cloth bag. "Up to the hill," she said. "There might be a breeze."

They walked through the field. The grass was tall and pale. Biscuit pushed through it with his nose low. Bello stayed close, his ears up, watching.

The hill was long and not steep. By the time they reached the top, Emma was warm. She sat down and drank water from the bottle. Taro lay flat on his back in the grass and looked up at the sky.

The wind was light. Just enough to move the tips of the grass. Emma lay down too. The grass was dry and prickly under her arms.

For a while no one spoke.

Then Emma heard it. A sound. Faint but clear. *Thud.* Like a door closing. It came from the village below.

"Listen," she said.

Taro was quiet. They both listened.

Another sound. *Ding-ding-ding-ding.* Thin and distant.

"That's the school bell," said Taro.

Emma turned her head. The sound came up the hill as if the air carried it straight to her ear. She could hear everything. The village was far away but it was not silent.
