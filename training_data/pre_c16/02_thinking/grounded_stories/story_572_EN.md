Riku pushed his bicycle along the main street. The canvas bag hung from the handlebars and leaned to one side, heavy with letters and parcels. His breath made small clouds in the cold air. He walked slowly, one hand on the bag to keep it steady, and stopped at the red post box set into the wall.

He pulled the handle. The box was almost empty. He slid a bundle of letters inside and let the lid fall shut with a clank.

Footsteps on the stone. Stefan came up the street with his hands behind his back. His badge caught the pale winter light. He stopped beside Riku and looked at the bag.

"That is a heavy one," Stefan said.

Riku nodded. His hand rested on the canvas. "A heavy day. Two parcels for the lane. A stack of letters for the far end."

Stefan looked at the sky. Grey. More cold coming. He said nothing.

They stood together for a moment on the empty street. Their breath hung in the air and drifted apart. Then Stefan nodded once and walked on, hands behind his back.

Riku turned his bicycle and pushed it toward the lane.
