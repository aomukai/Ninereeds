Mei stood at the green gate. She held the bar with both hands. She did not push it open.

Emma was on the climbing frame. She saw the girl at the gate. She dropped down and walked across the yard.

Mei looked at the climbing frame. She looked at the bench along the wall. She looked at the ash tree in the corner. Her hands stayed on the bar.

Emma stopped on the other side of the gate. "Are you coming in?"

Mei did not answer. She looked at the yard again.

The classroom door opened. Clara came out and stood on the step. She said, "Mei."

Mei turned her head.

"Come in," Clara said. "Your grandmother told me you were starting today."

Mei pushed the gate. It was stiff. She pushed harder and it swung open.

Emma stood beside her. "I can show you the yard."

Mei looked at her. Then she looked past her at the climbing frame, the bench, the big tree.

"All right," she said.

They walked across the yard together. Taro was already hanging upside down from the climbing frame by his knees. He waved. Biscuit was not there. Bello was not there. But the yard was warm and the ash tree had new leaves.

Mei stood beside the climbing frame. She did not climb. She watched.
