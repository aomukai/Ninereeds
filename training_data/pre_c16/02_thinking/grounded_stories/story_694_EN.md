The air was thick and still. Even the reeds did not move.

Taro walked to the edge of the pond. The water was very low. The flat stone, usually wet, was dry and warm under his hand.

“No fish,” he said.

Mei sat on the bench under the willow. The shade was deep and cool. She did not answer. She was watching the surface.

A fly landed on a lily pad. The pad did not move. The fly sat there, cleaning its legs. No frog came.

Biscuit walked into the water. He did not splash. He walked until the water reached his chin, and then he stood still. Only his head showed above the surface. His ears floated.

Bello stayed on the bank. He put one paw in, then pulled it out. He shook it. Then he lay down in the grass, panting.

Taro sat on the flat stone. It was warm through his shorts. He looked into the water. Nothing moved. The frogs were hidden somewhere he could not see.

Mei shifted on the bench. The willow branches hung low around her, almost touching her shoulders.

“It is too hot for frogs,” she said.

Biscuit did not move. Only his eyes blinked, slow and steady, above the still water.
