Vern held the piece of pine. It was pale and straight. He turned it in his hands. The lines in the wood ran from one end to the other.

Mei watched from the stool near the window. The workshop smelled of sawdust and something sharp.

Vern placed a nail near the edge of the board. He tapped once. The wood split. A thin crack ran along the grain, following the line like a river finding its way.

"Wrong place," he said.

He set the split piece aside. He picked another board and laid the nail further in, away from the edge. He tapped again. The nail went in straight. The wood did not crack.

Mei leaned forward. "Why did the first one split?"

Vern put his hand flat on the board. "The nail pushes the wood apart. If the nail is too close to the edge, the wood has nowhere to go. So it follows the grain. Splits."

He picked up the hammer. "But if you put the nail here—" he tapped the head gently— "the wood holds."

Mei looked at the split piece. Then at the uncracked one. The grain in the good piece ran the same way. Only the nail had moved.
