The beam lay along the edge of the yard. Low, wooden, worn smooth in the middle where feet had rubbed.

Taro ran at it and jumped up. He walked fast, arms loose, feet finding the wood without looking. At the end he stepped off, turned, and grinned.

Emma put one foot on the beam. Then the other. She held her arms out. The wood felt narrow under her shoe. She took a step. Her arm dipped. She wobbled, stopped, waited until she was still, then took another step.

Mei stood at the start of the beam. She watched Taro. She watched Emma. Then she put one foot on the wood and lifted the other.

She did not walk fast. She did not walk slow. She walked halfway, and stopped.

The beam was still. Mei was still. Her arms were at her sides. She stood on the wood and did not move.

Taro said, "Go on."

Mei looked at the end of the beam. Then she turned. One foot. Then the other. She walked back to the start and stepped off onto the ground.

Emma reached the end. She jumped down and looked at the beam.

It was the same width all the way along.
