Emma and Mei walked across the field. Biscuit ran ahead, then circled back.

“This is it,” Emma said.

The oak stood at the edge where the grass met the trees. Its trunk was wide. The bark was rough and deeply furrowed, like lines drawn in thick brown.

Emma crouched near the base. She pointed.

“Look. Between the roots.”

Mei crouched too. She put her hands on her knees and leaned forward. Her eyes moved slowly.

Between two large roots there was a dark hole. The edges were worn smooth, as if something passed through often.

Biscuit pushed his nose into the hole. He sniffed hard. Then he sneezed — a loud, quick sneeze that made his whole head jerk.

Mei did not laugh. She pointed at the ground beside the hole.

“What are those?”

Emma looked. Old acorn shells lay pushed to one side. They were brown and split open, their caps missing.

“The squirrel leaves them,” Emma said. “She eats the inside and pushes the shells out.”

Mei touched one with her finger. It was dry and light.

She looked at the hole again. She listened. Inside, something moved — a small rustle, very soft.

Biscuit’s ears went up. He put his nose back to the hole and stayed very still.
