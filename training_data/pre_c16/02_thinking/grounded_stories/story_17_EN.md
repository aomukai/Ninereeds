Gran pressed the worm onto the hook. It wriggled. Taro wrinkled his nose but held the rod steady.  

"Now cast," Gran said.  

He swung it back. The line flew out with a soft *plop* and sank. Ripples spread.  

They waited. The pond was still. A dragonfly skimmed the surface. Taro shifted on the flat stone.  

"Patience," Gran murmured, her hands busy mending a net.  

Then—a tug. Taro jumped up. The rod bent. "Gran!"  

She caught his shoulder just as his foot slipped on the wet bank. "Pull slow."  

He yanked. The line thrummed. A flash of silver broke the surface—a fish, twisting. Gran scooped it into the bucket.  

Taro crouched, breathless. The fish's gills flared. Its scales shone.  

Gran lifted the bucket. "Ready?"  

He nodded. The fish slipped back into the water and vanished with a flick.  

Taro picked up the rod. The hook was bare. Gran handed him another worm.
