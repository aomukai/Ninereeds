The sky turned dark very fast. One moment it was bright. The next, the light was gone.

"Come on," Taro said. He was already running toward the oak.

Emma ran after him. The wind pushed at her back. The first drops landed on her arms — big, cold drops that left dark spots on the stone.

They reached the oak just as the rain opened. It hit the leaves above them with a sound like stones falling. Emma pressed her back against the trunk. The bark was rough through her shirt.

Taro sat beside her. He was breathing hard. "We made it."

The leaves above were thick. Most of the rain stayed up there, hitting and running and dripping off the edges. A few drops came through. One landed on Emma's knee. Another hit her cheek.

Biscuit pushed in between them and lay down at the roots. He put his nose on his paws. Bello circled once, twice, then curled up close to Biscuit's side. His ears were flat.

The rain was very loud. It filled the air. Everything beyond the oak was grey and wet.

But under the branches, the ground was almost dry. The roots made a wall around them. The trunk held the wind away.

Emma looked up. The leaves shook and rattled. Water ran off them in streams.

She pulled her knees up and waited.
