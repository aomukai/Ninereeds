Gran took the sack down from the shelf. The dogs sat, tails thumping the flagstones. She scooped kibble into Biscuit’s bowl — the old chipped one with the blue stripe. Then she reached for Bello’s, the new black one. The scoop clattered twice.  

"One for Biscuit," she said, setting it down. "One for Bello. Two bowls."  

Bello darted in. Biscuit waited, polite, until Gran nodded. The crunching filled the kitchen. Gran watched them eat. She remembered when the scoop only clattered once.  

"Two dogs, two bowls," she said to the empty sack. "Twice what I gave before Bello came."  

Bello licked his bowl clean and nudged Biscuit’s. Biscuit growled, low in his throat. Gran tapped the counter. "Enough."  

The bowls shone in the lamplight, one slightly tipped into the other. Twice as much as before. She folded the sack small and put it away.
