Gran lifted the heavy door of the root cellar. Cold air and the smell of earth and apples came up the stone steps.

"Let us see what is left," she said.

She lit the old metal lamp. The flame was small. It made the shadows move against the stone walls.

Emma went down first. She held the lamp up. The top shelf had jars of plums. Dark red, close together.

"Seven," she said. She touched each one as she counted. The glass was cool.

Taro looked at the middle shelf. Jars of pickled cabbage. Green and brown, packed tight.

"Five," he said. "And one more. No. Five."

Gran handed him a small piece of paper and a pencil stub.

"Write it," she said. "Plums, seven. Pickles, five."

Taro wrote the words in careful letters. The pencil was soft and the numbers came out thick.

"February," Gran said. "Still a month of winter. We need to make these last."

Emma looked along the row of jars. The shelves held onions, dried beans, a jar of something dark she could not name. The lamp flickered. She put her hand on the plum jars again, counting once more.
