Emma stood under the apple tree, her hands cupped around a windfall. The skin was warm from the sun, the curve of it fitting neatly against her palm.  

A soft *thud* in the grass beside her. Another apple, fallen without warning. No wind, no branch shaking—just the quiet sound of something letting go.  

Gran bent to pick it up. A bruise bloomed where it had struck the ground. "It was ready," she said, turning it over. "See how the stem’s clean? No tear."  

Emma touched the broken end. Smooth, not ragged. She looked up at the branch, still heavy with fruit. Some hung low, their stems thin and dry. Others clung tight, green still at the join.  

"The ripe ones get heavy," Gran said. "The stem can’t hold forever."  

Emma pressed her thumb to the apple’s skin. It gave just slightly, like the earth after rain. Above them, a leaf drifted down, spinning. No hurry. Just ready.
