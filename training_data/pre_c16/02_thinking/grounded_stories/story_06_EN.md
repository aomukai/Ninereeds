The morning sun was still low over the garden wall. Gran knelt by the vegetable patch, her trowel biting into the dark soil. Emma crouched beside her. The earth smelled rich and cool, like the bottom of the rain barrel.  

"Here," Gran said, pressing a seed into Emma's palm. It was small and hard. "Make a row like this." She drew a line in the dirt with her finger.  

Emma dug little holes with the trowel, one after another. The soil crumbled under her nails. A worm wriggled away from the blade. Biscuit sniffed at the edge of the patch, then pawed at a spot where the dirt was soft.  

"Not there," Emma said. She covered the seeds gently, pressing the earth flat. Dew still clung to the grass at the edge of the patch. A fat slug glistened near the lettuce.  

Gran moved down the row, dropping seeds without looking. "Water them after," she said. The sun climbed higher. Biscuit sneezed into the dirt.
