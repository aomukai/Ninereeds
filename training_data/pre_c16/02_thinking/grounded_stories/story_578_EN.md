The market was quiet now. The other stalls had packed up. Only Yun’s table was still out.

Gran stood in front of it. Yun lifted the last carrots from the crate. She laid them on the brass scale and watched the needle settle. Then she twisted the paper bag closed—once, twice—and held it out.

Gran took it. She put the bag into her basket.

Mei stood beside the table. She was not helping. She was watching. Her hands were still. She watched Yun straighten the empty crate, then the cloth under the scale.

Yun looked at the basket. “That is all today.”

Gran nodded. She picked up the basket by one handle. Mei stepped forward and took the other handle. The basket hung between them, low and even.

They walked down the main street together. The baker’s light was off. The post box was red in the late sun. At the corner they turned into the lane.

The lane was narrow. The basket bumped against Gran’s knee, then Mei’s. Neither of them said anything. The paper bag rustled with each step. The lane went uphill, then flat, then the cottage came into view.
