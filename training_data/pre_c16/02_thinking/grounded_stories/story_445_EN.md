Biscuit stopped.

Not the slow stop, where he puts his nose down and sniffs. A sudden stop, front paws planted, ears up.

Emma stopped too.

They were halfway up the hill. Owen's field spread out below them, long and green in the autumn light. And at the far edge, something was moving.

A dog.

Not Biscuit's kind of dog. This one was black and white, low to the ground, running fast. It ran one way. The sheep moved. It turned. The sheep turned with it.

Biscuit did not bark.

He stood very still. His tail did not wag. His eyes followed the other dog all the way across the field.

Emma watched Biscuit. His whole body was quiet. He did not pull at the lead. He did not whine.

"He's working," said Owen.

Emma had not heard him come up. He stood beside her, hands in his pockets, looking out across the field.

"That's what they do," he said.

The dog ran again. The flock curved at the edge of the hill like water turning.

Biscuit watched.

Then the dog stopped. The sheep stopped. Everything was still.

Biscuit sat down.

He did not look at Emma. He looked at the other dog, across all that distance.

Owen said nothing more.
