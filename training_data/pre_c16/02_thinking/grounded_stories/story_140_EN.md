Emma crouched by the apple tree, fingers brushing the rough bark. Biscuit snuffled beside her, his broad nose pushing into the leaf litter. He always checked here—always the same way, always missing the gap.  

Bello trotted up, ears pricked. He circled once, nose twitching, then darted forward. Where Biscuit had pressed and given up, Bello wriggled—quick, narrow—and vanished into the dark space beneath the roots.  

Emma held her breath. A rustle. A satisfied snort. Then Bello backed out, something small and round clamped in his jaws. He dropped it at her feet: a walnut, still in its green husk.  

Biscuit huffed. He nosed at the gap again, but his shoulders were too wide. Emma scratched his ears. "Different sizes," she murmured. Bello shook himself, sending leaves flying, and trotted off to sniff the compost corner. The walnut rolled a little way and stopped, half-hidden in the grass.
