The rain began after the lights were out. At first it was just a whisper against the skylight, but soon it drummed hard on the tiles—so close overhead that Emma could almost feel it through the ceiling. The sound filled the room, loud and steady, not like the muffled rain heard from downstairs.  

She lay still under the blanket, listening. The room was dark except for the faint grey outline of the skylight. Taro’s breathing was slow and even beside her. The third stair creaked—Gran coming up to check—but the door didn’t open. She must have seen they were asleep.  

The rain went on, sharp and quick. Emma stretched her fingers toward the ceiling. The tiles were right there, just above her. Inside, the room was warm, the blankets dry. Outside, the water ran down the roof, over the gutters, onto the ground far below.  

Taro turned in his sleep, pulling the blanket tighter. Emma curled onto her side, listening until the sound wrapped around her, and then she wasn’t listening anymore.
