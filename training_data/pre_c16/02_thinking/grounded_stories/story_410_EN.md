Gran lifted the iron bar and propped the cellar door open. The smell of earth and apples came up the steps.

Emma went down first. The lamp threw a yellow circle on the stone walls. She counted the sacks along the wall.

"Four," she said.

Taro came down behind her. He lifted the corner of one sack. It was heavy but not full. He could feel the potatoes shift inside, loose in the bottom.

"Half," he said.

Gran came down the steps. She held up two fingers.

"We planted fifty potatoes in April. Now we have half a sack left."

Emma looked at the other three sacks. They were flat and empty. Their mouths hung open against the wall.

"So the half sack has to last," Taro said.

"Through spring," Gran said. "One month. Maybe two."

She lifted the half sack onto her shoulder. Emma counted the empty sacks again. Three empty. One half full. Forty-seven potatoes gone since autumn.

They went back up the steps. Gran left the cellar door open to air. The empty sacks stayed below in the dark.
