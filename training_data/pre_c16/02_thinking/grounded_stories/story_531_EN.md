Emma and Mei walked down the side street. It was quiet here. The vet's door was closed. A small sign hung above it with a picture of a paw.

The doctor's gate across the way was latched. Emma could see the fish tank through the window — three orange shapes moving slow circles.

But between them, Vern's workshop door stood open. The smell of sawdust came out. Tools hung on the wall inside. The vise was empty.

Mei stopped walking. She pointed at the ground in front of the vet's door.

"Look," she said.

Emma looked. The stone step was worn. The middle of it dipped down, smooth and grey. The edges were rough, but the centre was polished like a river stone.

"All the feet," Emma said.

Mei nodded. "People coming. Dogs going in. Dogs coming out. People going home."

Emma put her foot on the worn place. It fit exactly. She imagined all those feet — boots and shoes and paws — going up and down, up and down, until the stone gave way.

She stepped off. The step was still warm where the sun had touched it.

The side street was quiet again.
