The grass under the apple tree was full of fallen apples. Some were red and firm. Some were brown on one side and soft.

Emma picked one up. The good side was smooth and cold. She turned it over. The other side was brown and gave way under her thumb.

"Good ones in the basket," Gran said. She handed Emma a wicker basket. "The soft ones stay on the ground."

A wasp crawled over a windfall near the trunk. It walked slowly across the brown patch. Emma stepped around it.

Mei crouched on the far side of the tree. She picked up an apple that looked perfect — round and red all over. She turned it. The bottom was soft and brown. The skin had split.

"This one is good on top and bad underneath," she said.

She put it back on the grass.

Biscuit pushed his nose into a windfall. He sniffed once. Then he sneezed and walked away.

Emma picked up the firm red ones. She put them in the basket. The soft ones stayed where they were. The wasp stayed on its apple.

Gran looked at the basket. "That is enough," she said.

Emma carried the good apples inside.
