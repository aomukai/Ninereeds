The lane was damp underfoot. Brown leaves lay in flat piles against the walls.

Emma walked on one side. Taro walked on the other. Biscuit trotted ahead, then back, then ahead again.

A bicycle bell rang softly behind them.

Emma stepped to the side. Taro did the same.

Stefan passed them on his bicycle. He did not ring again. He slowed, one foot dropping to the ground, and walked the bike beside them.

"Emma," he said. "Taro."

He said it like a greeting, not a question.

"Off to school?" he asked.

"Yes," said Emma.

Stefan nodded. He did not stop walking. His boots made a steady sound on the stones.

They passed the bench at the edge of the street. Leaves had collected on the seat and under it — brown and curled, some still wet. The bench looked as if no one had sat there in days.

Stefan looked at the leaves. Then he looked at the children.

"Good day for it," he said.

He pushed off with his foot and rode on, slow and steady, hands loose on the handlebars.

Emma watched him go. The bicycle moved straight down the street. The leaves did not lift behind it.
