Gran put a small paper bag on the stall. The bag was white and had a twist at the top.

“Dried herbs,” she said.

Yun nodded. She lifted the bag and weighed it on the brass scale. The scale tipped and settled. Yun looked at the numbers and nodded again.

Gran took a coin from her pocket. It was silver and round. She handed it to Yun.

Yun took the coin. She opened a small tin on the stall table and dropped the coin inside. It made a soft clink. Then she put her hand into the tin and took out three smaller coins. She counted them into Gran’s open palm.

One. Two. Three.

Emma watched the coins land. She counted them in her head. One, two, three. That was the right number.

Gran closed her fingers around the coins and put them back in her pocket.

“Thank you,” she said.

Yun straightened the edge of the stall table. “Next week the mint will be ready.”

Gran picked up the bag of herbs. She turned to Emma.

“Ready?”

Emma looked at the tin on the stall. It was closed now. The coin was inside.

She nodded and followed Gran.
