Gran put the flour on the table. It made a soft thump when the bag landed. She began to scoop it out with her hand.

Emma reached for the white pile.

Gran caught her wrist. Not hard. Just enough to stop her.

"First your hands," Gran said.

Emma went to the sink. She turned the tap. The water ran cold over her fingers. She picked up the soap — pale yellow, smooth from use. She rubbed it between her palms. White bubbles appeared. She worked the soap under her nails, around each finger, over the backs of her hands, up to her wrists. The soap smelled sharp and clean.

She rinsed. The water ran cloudy, then clear.

The towel hung on the hook by the window. She dried each finger separately, then the palms, then the backs. The towel was rough and smelled of sun.

She went back to the table. She put her hand flat on the flour. It was soft and cool. It clung to her skin.

Taro was already at the sink. He did not wait to be told. He rubbed the soap hard between his hands, splashed water everywhere, dried on the same towel.

Gran nodded. She pushed the flour towards them.
