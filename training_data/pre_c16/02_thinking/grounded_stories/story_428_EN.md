Gran lifted the iron bar. The cellar door came open with a low scrape. Cold air rose from below—not sharp like the lane, but damp and still.

Emma went first. The stone steps were worn in the middle. She put her hand flat against the wall. It was cold, but not the hard cold of the frozen ground outside. It was a different cold. A steady cold.

Taro came behind her. He stood at the bottom and breathed out. Nothing happened. No white cloud.

"It doesn't show," he said.

"Too warm," said Emma.

She touched the wall again. The stone was smooth and old. It had been here longer than the frost outside.

The cellar smelled of apples and earth. The lamp threw a yellow circle onto the crates. The shadows on the far wall did not move.

Taro reached into the apple crate. The apples were cool in his hands. Not frozen. Not warm. Just cool, and still, and waiting.

Upstairs the lane was hard as iron. Down here the air held its own mind. The frost could not reach this deep.
