Emma held the tray with both hands. The bowl of soup sat in the middle, steaming.  

"Slow now," Gran said.  

The stairs were steep. Emma watched the bowl, not her feet. The third step creaked. The soup wobbled, just a little. She stopped. Breathed. Then went on.  

Up was heavier than down. Her arms knew it first. The tray pressed into her palms. The landing seemed farther now than it had from the bottom.  

One step. Another. The soup smelled of onions, warmth rising past her chin.  

At the top, the tray was a weight she had to set down carefully, not let go of all at once. The bowl settled. Nothing spilled.  

Gran was already at the bedroom door. She didn’t say *well done*. She didn’t need to. Emma’s hands still felt the shape of the tray, even after she let go.
