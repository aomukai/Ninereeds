Gran and Yun stood at the edge of the vegetable patch. The rows were nearly empty. A few cabbages sat tight and round. The last of the leeks stood in a single row, thin and green.

Emma looked at the bare ground. She remembered when the same patch was full of bean canes in summer. The canes had made a wigwam. She had crawled inside and looked up at the leaves.

Now the canes were gone. The ground was flat and dark.

"The ground looks different now," Mei said.

Gran nodded. "It will rest until spring."

Yun knelt and pressed her palm flat against the earth. "It is not empty," she said. "It is waiting."

Emma looked again. The soil was dark and crumbly. She remembered how tall the beans had grown. Now there was nothing but earth.

But the earth was still there. The same earth. She touched it. It was cool and soft.

"Next year," Gran said, "we will plant again."

Mei knelt beside Emma and touched the soil too. Then she looked up at the leeks, then at the cabbages, then at the bare row between them.

"The cabbages are still here," she said.

"They are," said Yun. "And the leeks. The garden is not finished yet."
