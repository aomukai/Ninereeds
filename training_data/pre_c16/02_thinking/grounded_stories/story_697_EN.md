Emma sat on the stone bench. The garden was quiet. Gran was at the vegetable patch, pulling the last of the beans. Mei had come through the gate a few minutes ago and was standing near the apple tree, looking up at something.

Emma looked at the wall. She had looked at it before. But she had never looked at it like this.

One side was green. Green and soft. Moss growing in a thick patch, damp to the touch. She leaned forward and pressed her finger against it. It was cool. It was wet.

She turned her head to the other side of the wall. That side was different. Orange. Bright orange in places, pale orange in others. Small scaly patches that looked dry. Lichen.

Gran came over, wiping her hands on her apron. Mei followed.

"Gran," Emma said. "The wall is not the same on both sides."

Gran looked. She did not say anything at first. She just looked.

"That side," Emma said, pointing at the green, "is damp. And that side," she pointed at the orange, "is dry."

"North side," Gran said. "South side."

Mei came closer. She touched the orange patch. She touched the green patch.

"Same wall," she said.

"Different," Emma said.

She sat back on the bench. She looked at the wall again. She had seen it every day. She had never seen it before.
