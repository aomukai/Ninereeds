Owen leaned on the gate. His cap cast a shadow over his eyes. The field behind him was dry and brown. The sheep were scattered across it, far apart, moving slowly.

Taro came down the lane. The sun was hot on his neck. Dust puffed up at his feet with every step.

He stopped at the gate. Owen did not move. His big hands rested on the top bar. His sheepdog sat in the shade of a fence post, tongue out, watching nothing in particular.

Taro waited.

The sheep moved. Not much — a step here, a step there. One bent its head to nibble at the dry grass. Another stood still and looked at nothing.

The dog did not move either. It blinked slowly.

Taro put his hand on the gate beside Owen’s. The wood was warm. The metal latch was hot.

Owen said nothing. He did not look at Taro. He looked across the field.

The sheep took another step. Then another. The sun moved a little. The shadow of the fence post shifted.

Taro stood at the gate. Owen leaned. The dog sat. The sheep moved.

No one was in a hurry.
