The kitchen smelled of warm yeast and flour. Emma pressed her nose to the oven door. "Is it done yet?"  

"Not yet," said Gran, kneading the next loaf on the floured table.  

Taro bounced on his toes. "But it’s been ages!" He reached for the oven handle.  

Gran didn’t look up. "Leave it be."  

The smell grew richer, golden at the edges. Emma’s stomach growled. She traced the oven’s warm metal with her fingertips.  

Taro cracked the door open—just a peek—and heat rushed out, thick with the scent of crust. Gran’s hand closed it again, gentle but firm.  

"Almost," she said.  

Emma leaned against the table, watching Gran shape the dough. It was soft and alive under her hands, rising even as they waited. The oven hummed. The smell deepened, like sunlight caught in flour.  

Taro sighed and flopped onto the bench. "How much longer?"  

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. "It stays till it’s ready."  

The kitchen held its breath. The bread knew when.
