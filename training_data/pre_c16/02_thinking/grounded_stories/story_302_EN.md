Gran handed Emma a small coin. It was silver and cool in her palm.

The market stall had eggs in cartons. Each carton had six eggs. The price was on a small card stuck into the stack: six eggs, one coin.

Emma counted the eggs. One, two, three, four, five, six. She looked at the coin in her hand. Then she looked at the card again.

Taro had his own coin. He turned it over. He looked at the carton. He looked at the card. He counted on his fingers. Then he opened his palm and counted the coins in it. One, two, three. He counted them again.

He was short by one.

Mei stood beside her grandmother's stall. She watched Taro count. She did not say anything. She just watched.

Yun reached for Emma's coin. She dropped it into a small metal box. Then she lifted a carton and handed it to Emma. The eggs shifted inside. Emma held the carton with both hands.

Taro put his coins back in his pocket.

Gran looked at him. "Next time," she said.
