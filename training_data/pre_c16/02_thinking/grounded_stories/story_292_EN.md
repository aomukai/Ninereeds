Vern's workshop was cool even in summer. Shavings lay curled on the floor like pale ribbons.

He set two blocks of wood on the bench. They were the same size. Same shape. Both smooth.

"Lift them," he said.

Emma put out both hands. She took one block in her left hand and one in her right.

Her left hand dropped an inch before she caught it.

She looked at the block in her right hand. It was light. She could hold it up easily. She moved her fingers and the block did not pull.

The one in her left hand was heavy. She had to grip it harder. The weight sat in her palm and did not pretend to be anywhere else.

"That one," she said, lifting the heavy one. "What is it?"

"Oak," said Vern. He was not watching her. He was running his thumb along the edge of a chisel. "The other is pine. Same size. Not the same wood."

Taro picked up the pine block. He tossed it from hand to hand. "This one feels like nothing."

"Try the oak," said Vern.

Taro took it. His arm dropped. He did not toss it. He held it still and looked at it.

Emma put the pine block back on the bench. It made a light sound. The oak block landed heavier. The bench felt it.
