Riku's bicycle made a soft ticking sound as he stopped beside the market stall.

He swung his leg over the seat. The canvas bag was heavy today. He reached inside and pulled out a thick envelope.

The paper was cream-coloured. In the corner, a small stamp showed a bird with its wings spread.

"Letter for you," he said to Yun.

Yun put down the brass scale. She wiped her hands on her apron. Then she took the envelope.

She held it flat in her palm. She looked at the name written on the front. Her thumb moved once across the edge of the stamp.

She did not open it.

Instead, she folded it once, carefully, and slid it into the pocket of her apron. The pocket was deep. The envelope disappeared.

Mei watched from beside the stall. She had been arranging beans in a row. Now her hands were still.

Yun picked up the scale again. She adjusted the small brass weights. She did not say what the letter was.

Riku climbed back onto his bicycle. The bag settled against his hip. He nodded once and pushed off down the street.

The bicycle ticked again, quieter, going away.

Mei looked at her grandmother's apron pocket. The edge of the envelope showed just a little above the cloth. White against blue.
