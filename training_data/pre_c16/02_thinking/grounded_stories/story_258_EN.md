Vern poured a small amount of oil onto a cloth. The oil was pale and thin. It moved slowly across the rag.

He folded the cloth and began to rub. His hand moved in circles on the wood. The wood changed colour as he worked — from light to deeper, from dry to rich.

The smell filled the workshop. It was not the sharp smell of sawdust. It was something else — warm and plant-like. Emma breathed in. Mei leaned closer.

The wood darkened under the rag. The surface began to shine. It was not a bright shine. It was a deep shine, like water in a shadow.

Vern turned the piece in his hands. He rubbed the edges. He rubbed the corners. He did not stop until every part had been touched.

Emma watched his hands. They moved the same way every time. Around and around. Slow and steady.

Vern set the piece on the bench. He put the rag aside. The wood was still. The oil sat on the surface, waiting.

"It will dry by morning," he said.

Mei touched the edge of the bench, very lightly. The wood was cool. The smell was still there, settling into the air like something that had decided to stay.
