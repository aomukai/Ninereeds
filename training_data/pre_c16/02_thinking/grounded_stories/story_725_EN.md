Vern stood at his workbench. Two planks of wood lay side by side.

Emma watched. Mei watched.

Vern put his hand on the first plank. Then the second. One was longer by a thumb's width.

"Here," he said. He handed Emma the ruler.

She knelt on the sawdust floor. She put the ruler against the first plank. The numbers went up to forty. She moved to the second plank. It stopped at thirty-eight.

"Shorter," she said.

"Longer," said Mei, and pointed at the first one.

Vern nodded. He took a pencil from behind his ear. On the longer plank he made a mark. Then he measured again, from the end to the line. He ran his finger along the ruler. He looked at the mark. He looked at the shorter plank beside it.

He picked up the saw.

"The line," he said. "Keep your eye on the line."

He set the teeth of the saw against the wood. The blade touched the pencil mark. He pulled once, gently. A thin cut opened along the line.

Emma did not blink.

Mei did not move.

Vern pushed the saw forward. The teeth bit into the wood. Sawdust curled up, fine and pale, and fell onto the bench.
