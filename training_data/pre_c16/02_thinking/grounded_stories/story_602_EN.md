Emma sat on the flat stone and looked at the water.

Last week, the shallows were full of frog spawn. Clear blobs with black dots in the middle. Emma had touched one with a stick. It was soft and wobbly.

Now the spawn was gone.

“Look,” Mei said. She pointed at the reeds.

Tiny black shapes moved in the water. Tadpoles. They swam in a group, close to the stems.

Emma leaned forward. “They have tails.”

“Last week they did not have tails,” Mei said.

“No,” Emma said. “Last week they were still in the blobs.”

Biscuit came to the edge and put his nose down. He drank, lapping with his tongue. He did not look at the tadpoles. He did not care about any of it.

Emma watched the tadpoles swim. They wriggled all together, then scattered, then came back.

“Next week,” Mei said, “they will have legs.”

Emma thought about that. A tail and then legs. She would come back next week to see.
