The bakery window was bright against the dark street. Taro pressed his nose close to the glass. Inside, Hana was lining up the loaves in a row — big ones at the back, small ones at the front.

Gran pushed the door. The bell rang. Warm air and the smell of bread came out together.

"End-of-day," Hana said. She pointed at a tray by the counter. "Half-price now. Still warm."

Gran nodded. She chose a round loaf with a dark, floury top. Hana put it in a paper bag and handed it to Taro.

It was heavy. It was warm. The warmth came through the paper and into his fingers.

Gran counted out coins and put them on the counter. Hana gave her the change. The bell rang again as they stepped back onto the street.

The cold hit his face. But his hands were still warm where he held the bag. He held it against his coat as they walked up the lane.

The bakery light shrank behind them. The dark came down. But the warmth stayed in Taro's hands all the way home.
