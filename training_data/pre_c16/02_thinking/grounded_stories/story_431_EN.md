The root cellar door was propped open with the iron bar. Cold air came up the steps.

Gran handed Emma the lamp. "Count what is left," she said.

Emma went down first. Mei followed. The lamp made the shadows move.

The top shelf had two jars. Plums. Dark purple. The light went through the glass.

The middle shelf had five jars. Beans. Grey-green. Emma set the lamp on a crate.

"Two and five," Mei said. She counted on her fingers. "Seven."

The bottom shelf was empty. Nothing there at all.

Emma looked at the seven jars. Plums and beans. She counted again. Two. Five. Seven.

"Seven jars," Emma said when they came back up.

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. "That will not last until summer."

Emma thought about summer. The garden full of beans. The apple tree. The warm kitchen.

"How many weeks until summer?" Mei asked.

Gran didn't answer. She looked at the seven jars. Two plums. Five beans. Fewer than she had hoped.

Emma went back down. She touched the empty shelf. The wood was cold.

Seven jars. That was not enough.
