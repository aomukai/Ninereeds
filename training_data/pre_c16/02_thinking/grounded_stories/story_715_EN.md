Emma woke up.

The light was low and yellow. It came through the kitchen window and fell across the floor in a long, quiet shape.

She pushed her feet out of bed. The floor was cold. She padded down the stairs.

Gran was at the table. The sun came from the east, through the window over the sink. It touched the wooden tabletop. It touched the bread Gran had set down.

“Morning,” Gran said.

Emma sat. The room was still cool. The sun was bright on the table, but the air had not yet warmed.

She watched the light. It made a shadow of the bread knife on the wood. It made a shadow of the cup.

“The sun comes from that side,” Emma said.

“Yes,” said Gran. She was cutting bread. She did not look up.

Emma touched the patch of sun on the table. It was warm. The rest of the table was not. She put her hand in the light again.

The day was beginning.
