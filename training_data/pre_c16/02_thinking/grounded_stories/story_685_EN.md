The grass on the meadow path was white. Every blade had a thin coat of frost. It looked like sugar, but it was cold.

Emma took a step. The grass under her shoe went *crunch*. She lifted her foot and looked down. Her footprint was dark green. The frost had broken where she stepped. All around it, the frost was still white.

She took another step. Another crunch. Another dark green mark.

Taro ran past her. *Crunch crunch crunch crunch* — a whole line of footprints, close together, running away down the path.

Biscuit pushed past Emma’s legs. His paws left four round dark marks, then four more, then four more. Bello bounced behind him, leaving smaller prints that were close together.

Emma walked slowly. She looked back. The path was covered in dark marks — big ones, small ones, close ones, far apart. The white frost was broken everywhere.

Then the sun came over the hedge. It touched the edge of the path first. The frost at the edge began to shine. A drip fell from a blade of grass. A patch of green appeared where the frost had been.

Emma watched. The sun moved. The white began to melt. The dark footprints were already fading.
