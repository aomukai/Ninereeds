Clara brought the wood to the classroom. She laid it on the desk. Small planks, a flat piece for the roof, nails in a tin.

“This will take more than one day,” she said. “Each day is one step.”

Day one. Taro held the hammer. He put a nail against the wood and hit it. The nail bent sideways. He pulled it out with the claw and tried again. The second nail went in straight.

Day two. Emma held the pieces together. Taro hammered. The wood did not move. Biscuit would have watched, but Biscuit was not here. Emma’s arms got tired before the nail was all the way in.

Day three. Mei took the glue. She spread it along the roof seam. She held it until the glue began to set. “Do not touch it now,” she said. “It needs to dry.”

Day four. Clara hung the feeder outside the window. The wood was pale and new. The roof was straight. The nail heads caught the light.

“It is finished,” said Mei.

The feeder swung a little in the spring air. No bird came yet. That was not the last step.
