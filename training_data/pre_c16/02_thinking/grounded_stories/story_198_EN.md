Emma brushed the table inside the mill. Her hand came back white.

She looked at her palm. The lines were gone. Just white.

She touched the table again. The flour was fine and soft. It clung to her skin like dust on a window.

The air was full of it. Tiny specks floated in the light from the door. They moved when she breathed.

Mei stood beside her. She shook her sleeve. A puff of white came off and hung in the air.

"See?" Mei said. "Everything gets a coat."

Emma wiped her hand on her trousers. The flour smeared. It did not come off. It just spread.

She looked at the floor, the window ledge, the top of the grain sack. Everything had a thin white coat. The iron latch on the door was grey with it. The rope hanging from the hopper was stiff with it.

Emma opened her mouth to say something. Instead she sneezed.

The flour rose off the table in a small cloud. It settled again slowly, the way dust settles after footsteps on a dry path.

Mei laughed. She wiped her own face with the back of her hand. Her hair was white at the ends.

The mill wheel turned somewhere below them. The grinding sound went on. The air did not clear.
