Gran set six apples in a tight line on the table. She took six more and spread them out, each one a hand’s width apart.  

Taro leaned in. "That row looks like more."  

"Count them," Gran said, wiping her hands on her apron. "They look different, but let us see."  

Emma touched each apple in the first row. "One, two, three, four, five, six." She moved to the spread-out row, pointing. "One, two, three—" Biscuit nosed her elbow. She pushed him gently away. "Four, five, six." She looked up. "Six and six. The same."  

Taro frowned. He lined them up again, side by side. The tight row was short and neat. The spread-out row stretched nearly to the table’s edge. "But this one’s longer."  

Gran lifted an apple from the tight row and set it with the others. Now both rows were spread the same. "Count again."  

Emma did. "Still six."  

Taro scratched his head. Then he grinned. "Same number!"  

Gran picked up an apple and polished it on her sleeve. Biscuit wagged his tail, hopeful.
