The bell rang.

Emma and Taro walked out of the schoolyard. The green gate was stiff. Emma pushed it with both hands.

Mei was waiting on the other side.

They walked together down the meadow path. The grass on either side was tall and green. It brushed against Emma's legs. Taro kicked at a clump of clover.

Near the hedgerow, Mei stopped.

"I go this way," she said. She pointed toward the village lane.

Emma nodded. "See you tomorrow."

Mei turned and walked. Her steps were quick and even. She did not look back.

Emma and Taro walked on. The path narrowed. Biscuit came running from somewhere ahead. He had a stick in his mouth. He dropped it at Emma's feet.

Taro picked it up and threw it. Biscuit chased.

The meadow opened around them. The oak was a dark shape at the far edge of the field. Gran's chimney was just visible above the hedge.

Emma walked. The grass hummed with insects. The sun was warm on her neck.

Taro ran ahead. Then he stopped and waited. Then he ran again.

They walked the rest of the way together.
