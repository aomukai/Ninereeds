The cellar door was propped open with the iron bar. Emma held the lamp. The light moved as she stepped down.

Mei stood at the bottom. She did not move. She looked at the crates on the left. She looked at the jars on the right. She looked up at the onions hanging from the beam.

"Apples on the floor," she said. "Onions in the air."

Emma held the lamp higher. The light touched the jars. The labels faced outward. The crates were lined up, square to the wall. The onions hung in a row, even spaces between them.

"You arranged this," Mei said.

"Gran did," Emma said.

Mei stepped closer to the shelves. She looked at the jars on the left shelf. She looked at the ones on the right. She did not touch anything.

"There is a pattern," she said.

Emma came down the last step. The lamp swayed. The shadows swung across the jars.

"The big jars are on the left," Emma said. "The small ones are on the right."

Mei nodded. She looked at the apples in the crate. They were all stem up, all facing the same way.

She did not say anything. She looked at the pattern.
