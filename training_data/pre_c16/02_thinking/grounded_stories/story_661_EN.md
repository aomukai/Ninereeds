The pond was still. The water near the edge was shallow and warm.

Emma knelt on the flat stone. She looked down.

“Mei,” she said. “Look.”

The water was full of them. Small and black. Each one had a head and a tail. They moved together in a cluster, close to the edge where the sun had been all afternoon.

Mei knelt beside her. She did not speak. She just looked.

Some of the tadpoles had tiny back legs, no bigger than a pin. Others had none. They were different, but they stayed together, a dark crowd that shifted when a cloud passed over the sun.

Biscuit came to the edge. He put his nose down and lapped.

A tadpole near the front wriggled sideways, fast, and disappeared into the deeper water.

Emma watched the place where it had been. Then she looked at the ones that stayed. They were so small. Their tails curved and straightened, curved and straightened, holding them in the warm water.

Mei pointed. “That one has legs.”

“I see it,” Emma said.

She did not lift her hand. She just watched them move, together, at the edge of the pond.
