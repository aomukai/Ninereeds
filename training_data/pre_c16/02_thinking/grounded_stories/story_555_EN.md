Gran gave Emma the bread money and the letter.

"To the baker first," she said. "Then the post box. Then straight back."

Emma put the money in her pocket. Taro put the letter in his.

The lane was warm. The sun was high. By the time they reached the village street, the baker's smell was already there.

They passed Riku's red bicycle leaning against the wall near the post box. Riku himself was not there.

They stopped at Hana's window. The loaves were lined up in rows — big ones at the back, small ones at the front. Taro pressed his nose to the glass. Emma pulled him away.

Inside, Hana handed over the bread. It was still warm in the paper.

Outside again, Stefan was walking his beat. He nodded at them. "Long list today?" he said.

Emma showed him the letter. "Just bread and the post," she said.

But Stefan stopped to talk. He asked about Gran. He asked about Biscuit. He asked if they had seen Riku's bicycle.

By the time they reached the post box, the sun had moved.

The letter went in. The bread was still warm.

Taro looked up at Emma. "Gran said straight back," he said.

Emma looked down the long street.

"It's a long way," she said.
