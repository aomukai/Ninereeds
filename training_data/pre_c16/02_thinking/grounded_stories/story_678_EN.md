Emma walked ahead on the path. Mei followed, quiet.

"The nest is in the oak," Emma said. "The robin's nest."

They stopped under the low branch. Emma pointed up. Two small birds sat on the edge of the nest. Their feathers were brown and soft, but not all of them. A gap showed on one bird's wing where the feathers had not grown yet.

The first bird hopped onto a branch. It wobbled. Its wings opened and closed, opened and closed.

The second bird stayed by the nest. It opened its mouth wide.

A sound came from above. The mother robin landed on a higher branch. She had something in her beak — small and dark. She dropped down to the first bird. The bird took it.

Then she turned to the second bird. It opened its mouth again. She fed it too.

Mei did not speak. She watched.

The first bird hopped again. Higher this time. One hop. Then another. Its wings opened and closed.

The mother called. A short sharp sound.

The second bird stayed on the edge of the nest. It did not hop yet.

Emma looked at Mei. Mei was still watching.

They stood under the oak until the first bird was gone into the leaves. The second bird sat alone on the edge of the nest, its small beak closed.
