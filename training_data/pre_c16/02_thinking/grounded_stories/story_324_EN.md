Emma put her hand in her pocket. The coins were round and cool. She counted them on her palm. Three.

Taro pulled his out too. He laid them on the stall table in a row. Two.

“That’s five,” said Mei. She stood behind the stall next to her grandmother’s baskets. “Five coins.”

Emma looked at the jars on the table. Small ones. Big ones. One with honey so clear the light went straight through it.

“That one,” she said.

Taro picked it up. The lid was tight. The honey inside was pale gold. He could smell it through the glass.

Mei’s grandmother weighed it on the brass scale. “Four coins,” she said.

Taro put his two coins on the table. Emma put her three next to them. Together they made four.

Taro pushed the coins forward. “We pay,” he said.

Mei’s grandmother took them and put the jar in a small paper bag. Mei handed it across the stall.

Emma held the bag. The jar was warm from standing in the sun. She and Taro walked back down the lane. Taro carried it next. Then Emma again. They did not open it yet. They were sharing it, and that meant waiting until they were both ready.
