The stall was full. Vegetables in crates. Eggs in a box. Bunches of herbs tied with string.

Yun packed a basket. She put carrots in first, then turnips. She tucked a bag of flour in the middle. The basket was full before she finished.

She lifted it. She set it down again.

“Two baskets today,” she said.

Taro looked at the basket. Then he looked at the other one, still empty.

He picked up the full one.

It was heavy. The handles pressed into his hands. He shifted his grip and walked.

Mei walked beside him. She did not say anything. She just walked.

The lane was quiet. The sun was warm. Taro’s arms began to ache, but he kept walking.

Mei reached the gate first. She pushed it open and held it. Taro passed through. The basket was heavier now than at the start.

He walked to the step. He bent his knees. He set the basket down.

The weight left his hands.

Yun came behind him. She carried the second basket. She set it down beside the first.

“Thank you,” she said.

Mei nodded at Taro. She did not say anything. She did not need to.
