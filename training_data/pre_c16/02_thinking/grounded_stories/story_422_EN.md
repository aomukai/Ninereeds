The cellar door was heavy. Emma held it open with her shoulder while Mei went down first.

The stone steps were worn in the middle. Mei's feet found each one carefully.

"It smells different down here," Mei said.

Emma came down behind her. The air was cool and still. She breathed in. Earth. Old apples. Something sharp that made her nose feel tight.

"It smells of the ground," Emma said. "And something else."

Mei touched a jar on the shelf. "This one says plums."

The lamp on the step threw a yellow circle. Beyond it the cellar was dark. The onion rope hung from a beam near the ceiling. Emma could smell the onions separately — stronger, drier, closer to the top of her head.

She stepped toward the apple crate. The smell changed. It was sweeter there, with a soft edge like something turning.

Mei breathed in again. "The onions smell near the ceiling. The apples smell near the floor."

Emma nodded. The damp smell came from the walls. The mould smell came from the corner where the earth was darker.

They stood still in the cellar. The lamp flickered. The smells stayed where they were.
