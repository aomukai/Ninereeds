Riku’s bicycle bell rang once at the gate. Emma looked up from the kitchen table. Gran wiped her hands on her apron and went to the door.

Riku stood there with his red bicycle leaning against the wall. He held out a letter. “For you,” he said.

Gran took it. “Thank you, Riku.”

He was already on his bicycle, already pedalling. The bell faded down the lane.

Gran came back to the table. She set the letter down flat. It was cream-coloured paper in a cream-coloured envelope. Emma watched.

Gran took a knife from the drawer. She slid it under the flap. The paper made a thin tearing sound.

She pulled out the letter and unfolded it. Her eyes moved across it once. Then again, slower.

Emma could see the postmark on the envelope — a blurred circle with a word she could not read.

Gran folded the letter back along its creases. She laid it on the table beside the envelope.

She did not say what it said.

Emma reached out and touched the corner of the paper. It was smooth and cool.

The letter stayed on the table. Gran went back to the sink.
