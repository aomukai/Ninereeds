The grass on the meadow path was dry. It was not green like summer. It was yellow and pale, and it bent where Biscuit pushed through it.

Emma took a step. Her foot came down and the grass made a sound — *crunch*.

She stopped and stepped again. *Crunch.*

"It sounds different," she said.

Taro was ahead. He kicked a pile of leaves. Brown and curled, they flew up and scattered across the path. Some landed on Biscuit's back. He did not notice. He was sniffing the dry stems along the edge.

Emma walked slowly. Each step made its own small sound. The leaves under her feet were thin and brittle. They broke into pieces when she pressed down.

Bello pushed his nose into a clump of dry grass and sneezed. He shook his head and sneezed again.

Taro walked backwards, looking at the path. "Everything is brown," he said.

Emma picked up a leaf. It was curled tight, like a small fist. She pressed it and it cracked in her hand.

Biscuit ran through the long grass. The sound of him was a dry rustle, fast and loud, and then he was gone around the bend.

The path was the same path. But it did not sound the same at all.
