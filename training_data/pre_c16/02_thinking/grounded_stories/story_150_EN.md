The rain came down hard and sudden. Taro felt it first on his neck, then everywhere. Biscuit stopped mid-step, lifted his nose straight up into the wet, and stood perfectly still. Rain dripped off his whiskers.  

Bello whined once, low in his throat. He pressed his whole side against Biscuit’s leg, ears flat, as if the bigger dog could block the rain. His black fur darkened to slick streaks.  

“Come on,” Taro said, tugging both leads. The oak’s thick branches waited ahead. Biscuit didn’t move. He blinked rain from his eyes but kept his nose up, drinking the sky. Bello shivered against him.  

Taro crouched between them, one hand on each wet back. Biscuit’s fur was warm underneath the rain. Bello’s ribs moved fast under his fingers.  

“Under the tree,” Taro told them, and this time Biscuit followed, shaking his head as he walked. Bello stayed glued to his side, stepping where Biscuit stepped, until they reached the oak. The three of them stood close under the lowest branch, listening to the rain hit the leaves above. Bello licked water off Biscuit’s ear.
