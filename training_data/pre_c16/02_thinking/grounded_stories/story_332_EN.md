The stall was full in the morning. Emma stood on her toes to see over the edge.

Yun was weighing potatoes on her brass scale. The weights clinked as she set them down. Mei was arranging the egg basket—brown eggs in neat rows, each one clean.

“The eggs go first,” Mei said. “Every Saturday.”

Emma watched. A woman came and took three eggs. Another woman took four. The basket grew emptier.

Taro looked at the turnips. They were still piled high. No one had bought any.

“Why does no one take the turnips?” he asked.

Yun straightened a paper bag without looking up. “Turnips keep. You can wait with a turnip. An egg does not wait.”

By midday the egg basket was empty. Only a few small ones were left at the bottom, the ones Mei had set aside.

Emma touched the empty basket. It was light. It had been heavy that morning.

“The turnips are still here,” she said.

Yun nodded. “They will be here next week too. That is what turnips do.”

Taro picked one up. It was firm and dusty. He put it back.
