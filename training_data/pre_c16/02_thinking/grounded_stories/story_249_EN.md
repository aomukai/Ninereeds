The mill was cold. Emma could see her breath.

Mei walked ahead. She stepped over a board near the hopper. The board moved under her foot. It did not crack, but it could.

“Stand here,” Mei said.

She pointed to a post. It was thick and wooden. Emma stood behind it. The air was still. But the flour dust hung in it anyway — small white specks that drifted without moving.

Mei stood near the millstone. The air there was thicker. White settled on her coat.

Gran came in behind them. She brushed her sleeve. White came off in a small cloud.

“You are clean,” Gran said to Emma. “Mei is not.”

Mei looked down at her coat. The flour dust was on the shoulders and the front and the cuffs.

“It comes off,” Mei said.

Emma stayed behind the post. She watched the dust hang in the cold air. It did not go anywhere. It just stayed, very fine, very white.

Gran brushed her other sleeve. Then she stepped over the loose board without looking down. Mei had shown her where it was.
