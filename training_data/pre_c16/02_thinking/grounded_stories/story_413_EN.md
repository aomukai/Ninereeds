The kitchen table was covered in small things.

Gran sat with a jar of ink in front of her. The ink was black and still. She dipped a thin brush into it.

Emma held the jar with both hands so it would not tip.

Gran touched the brush to a square of paper. Her hand moved slowly. A line went down. A curve. Another line.

She lifted the brush.

“Apple Chutney — October,” she read aloud.

The ink shone wet on the paper. It smelled sharp and a little like the shed.

Gran set the square aside and picked up another piece. She dipped the brush again. This time she wrote “Pear Jam — September.”

Emma watched the letters appear. The brush made a soft sound against the paper. Not quite a scratch. A whisper.

Gran finished and blew gently across the paper. Then she took a jar from the shelf. She held the label against the glass, turned it straight, and pressed it down with the flat of her thumb.

Emma picked up the brush. The handle was thin and smooth.

“Shall I write one?” she said.

Gran looked at the ink. “The paper is already cut. Choose a jar.”

Emma looked along the row. She picked the one with the dark red inside.

Gran handed her a clean square.
