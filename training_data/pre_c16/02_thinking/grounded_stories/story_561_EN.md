Emma’s throat hurt when she swallowed. It felt like something rough was stuck there.

Gran put on her own coat and held Emma’s coat out. “Come on, then.”

They went out the garden gate and turned onto the lane. The stones underfoot were pale with frost. Emma’s breath made a small white cloud in front of her face. Gran’s breath made a bigger one.

The lane was quiet. No one else was walking. The hedges on either side were thin and bare.

At the bottom, they turned onto the side street. Gran took Emma’s hand and they crossed. The wooden gate with the black latch was ahead.

Gran lifted the latch. The gate swung open.

The waiting room was warm. It smelled of polish and something clean. Six chairs stood against the wall. A low table held old magazines. Beside the reception hatch, a fish tank bubbled softly. Three orange fish circled slowly in the water.

Emma sat on a chair. Her coat was still buttoned. Gran sat beside her and did not say anything about taking it off.

The fish went around and around. The bubbles rose. The room was very quiet.
