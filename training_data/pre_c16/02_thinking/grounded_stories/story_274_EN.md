Vern took a clean rag from the shelf. He folded it once, then twice.

On the workbench lay a piece of pine. It was pale and dry. The grain ran straight from end to end.

He tipped the oil can. A dark puddle spread on the rag. He wiped it across the wood.

The oil vanished. The wood was pale again.

He wiped again. Slower this time. The oil stayed on the surface for a moment, then sank in.

"The wood drinks it," Mei said.

Vern nodded. He pressed the rag into a corner where the grain was tight. The oil pooled there and did not move.

Taro touched the wet spot with one finger. "It's cold."

"Oil is cold," Vern said. "Until it dries."

He rubbed the last spots in small circles. The wood darkened under his hand. The grain came out — darker lines where the oil had soaked deepest.

Mei leaned close. "It looks wet."

"Not wet," Vern said. "Finished."

He set the rag aside. The piece of pine was smooth and dark and the oil had gone into it and stopped.
