Owen stood by the gate. He put two fingers in his mouth and whistled.

The sound was sharp and clean. It cut across the field.

His dog was already moving. It ran wide, away from the sheep, then curved back. The sheep lifted their heads. One took a step. Then another. They began to move together, flowing away from the far hedge.

Taro stopped on the meadow path and watched. Biscuit stopped too. His ears went up. His nose pointed at the field.

The dog ran behind the sheep. It did not bark. It did not chase. It just ran, and the sheep turned and kept moving. They were all going the same way now, toward the gate.

The dog slowed. It dropped to a walk. The sheep came through the gateway one by one, close together, their hooves clicking on the stones.

The dog stopped at Owen's foot. Sat down. Waited.

Owen put a hand on its head. He did not look down. He was watching the last sheep pass through the gate.

Taro let out a breath he had not known he was holding. Biscuit's tail wagged once, then stopped.
