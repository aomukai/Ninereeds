Emma wanted to go out.

She stood at the garden gate. The wooden latch was old and stiff. She put her hand on it and lifted.

It slipped.

The gate did not move.

She tried again. She lifted the latch higher. Her fingers slid off. The latch fell back with a small hard clack.

Emma pushed the gate with her shoulder. It stayed shut. She pushed harder. Nothing.

Her face went tight. She pulled the latch. She pushed the gate. She tried it fast and then slow. Wrong. Wrong. Wrong.

She stepped back. She wanted to be on the other side. She could not get there.

Gran was beside the apple tree. She had been watching.

“Try again,” she said. “But this time, push and lift at the same time.”

Emma put her hand on the latch. She lifted. She pushed. The gate did not open.

“Harder,” Gran said. “Your hand needs the right angle.”

Emma moved her hand higher on the latch. She lifted and pushed together.

The latch clicked. The gate swung open.

Emma stood in the open doorway. She looked back at Gran.

Gran was already pulling a weed from between the cabbages.
