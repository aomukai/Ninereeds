The red bicycle was against the gate. It leaned on the metal bars as if it belonged there.

Riku stood beside it. His canvas bag was full — letters and parcels and things that fit in envelopes. He saw Emma first.

"Emma," he called. "A letter for Gran."

He held it out. White envelope. Small handwriting on the front. Emma took it and turned it over in her fingers. It was light. It had a stamp in the corner.

"Thank you," she said.

Riku nodded. He swung his leg over the bicycle and sat. The bag swung against his leg. He pushed off and pedalled up the street, past the baker's window, past the post box set into the wall. The bicycle moved smoothly. The red frame caught the light.

Taro came up behind Emma. "Who is it from?"

"I don't know," she said. "It's for Gran."

They stood at the gate and watched Riku reach the corner. He did not look back. He turned and the red bicycle was gone.

Emma held the letter against her chest and started walking up the lane. Taro followed.
