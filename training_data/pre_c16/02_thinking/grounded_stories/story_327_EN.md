Taro stood at the edge of the market stall. The apples were in a wooden crate — red ones, small and round, with stems still attached.

He put his hand in his pocket. The coins were warm.

Yun was weighing something on her brass scale. She did not look at him, but she knew he was there.

Taro tipped the coins onto the edge of the stall table. He counted them with his finger. One. Two. Three. Four.

He looked at the price written on the little card beside the crate. Five coins.

He counted again. Four.

Mei stood beside Yun, watching. She did not say anything. She looked at Yun. Yun said nothing.

Taro picked up a coin and put it back in his pocket. Then he picked up another and put it back too.

Three coins now. He counted them again. Three.

He looked at the crate. He took one apple out and set it on the table. Then another. He counted the coins again. Three coins. Three apples.

He picked them up, one in each hand, and the third under his arm. They were heavy together.

Mei watched him walk away. Yun straightened a jar on the stall. Neither of them spoke.
