The waiting room was quiet. Emma sat on the plastic chair. Her feet did not reach the floor.

Biscuit lay under the chair. He was very still. His nose rested on his front paws. His eyes were half closed.

Across the room, another dog sat beside a woman. It was a thin dog with long legs. It whined low and steady. The sound went on and on, like a kettle that had not yet boiled.

Biscuit did not raise his head. But one ear turned. It swivelled toward the sound and stayed there.

Emma watched his ear. She thought about how it listened without the rest of him moving.

The thin dog whined again. Its tail was tucked. Its ears were flat. It did not look at Biscuit. It looked at the door.

Biscuit's ear turned back. He sighed. His breath made a small cloud on the cold floor.

Emma put her hand on his side. She could feel him breathe. Slow and steady, like the clock on the wall.

The door opened. The thin dog stood up. Its whine stopped. The woman said, "Come on, then."

They went through the door. The room was quiet again.

Biscuit did not move. His ear did not turn.
