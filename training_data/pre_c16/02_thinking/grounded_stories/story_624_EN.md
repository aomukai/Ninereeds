The garden fence ran along the path. Post, gap, post, gap.

Mei stood still and counted. “Twelve posts,” she said. She pointed at each one as she said the number.

Emma looked at the fence. Then she looked at the bean canes. Three canes tied together at the top. Then three more. Then three more. They made a row of little wigwams all down the vegetable patch.

“It is the same,” Emma said.

Mei turned. “What is the same?”

“The fence has a pattern. Post, gap, post, gap.” Emma pointed. “The canes have a pattern too. Three, then three, then three.”

Mei looked from the fence to the canes and back again. She nodded slowly. “The pattern repeats.”

Emma walked along the row of bean canes. She counted the groups. One, two, three, four, five. Five wigwams. The same shape. The same gap between each one. The same all the way down.

She stopped at the last one and touched the string that tied the canes together. The knot was tight and even.

“Who made them the same?” she asked.

“Gran,” said Mei. “She does everything in rows.”

Emma looked back at the fence. Post, gap, post, gap. All the way to the corner.
