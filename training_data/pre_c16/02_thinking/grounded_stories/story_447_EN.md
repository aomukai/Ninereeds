Dou stepped along the windowsill in Vern's workshop. One paw, then the other, then a pause. The sill was narrow. She placed each foot with care.

Vern was planing a board. The plane blade bit into the wood and a thin shaving curled up and dropped onto the floor. It landed like a question mark. He did not stop. His hand pushed the plane again and the shaving curled again, longer this time.

Biscuit appeared at the open door. He stood in the sunlight and sniffed. His nose worked once, twice. Then he saw Dou on the sill. She was watching him with her one white ear forward.

Biscuit turned. He did not bark. He did not growl. He simply turned and walked back to the bench outside, where the sun was warm on the old wood. He put his chin on his paws and closed his eyes.

Inside, Vern planed again. The shaving curled and fell. Dou took another step along the sill. Then another. Then she sat down, tucked her tail around her feet, and watched the wood curl.
