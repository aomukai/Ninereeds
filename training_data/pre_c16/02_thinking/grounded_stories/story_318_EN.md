Mei lifted the apple from the basket. She turned it in her hand. The skin was red and smooth. She placed it at the front of the stall, on the left side, in a small neat row.

Emma watched. She picked up another apple. It was bigger, with a patch of green near the stem.

"Where does this one go?" she said.

"Behind," said Mei. "Small ones at the front. Big ones at the back."

Emma placed it in the second row. She moved her hand along the line. The big apples were round and heavy. The small ones were tight and neat.

Taro had the carrots. He pulled a bunch from the basket. The green tops were long and drooping. He set the bunch on the stall with the tops pointing to the right. He looked at it. Then he moved it so the tops all pointed the same way.

Mei put a jar of honey in the corner. Emma arranged the jars beside it, from smallest to tallest. Taro straightened the carrot bunches one more time.

Yun stood at the side of the stall. She watched. She did not say anything.

The stall was ready.
