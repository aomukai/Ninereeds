Emma stood still on the meadow path. Biscuit sat beside her, tongue out, watching.

"Close your eyes," Gran said.

Emma closed them. The light went away. She felt the sun on her arms, the grass tickling her ankles, and something else.

Something held under her nose.

She breathed in. Sharp. Green. Like the garden after rain, like the stone trough by the kitchen door.

"Rosemary," she said.

She opened her eyes. Gran was holding a sprig of rosemary, the needles dark and thin.

"Good," Gran said. "Again."

Emma closed her eyes. Another smell came. Lighter. Almost sweet. She knew it from the herb trough too, the one with the small leaves.

"Thyme," she said.

She opened her eyes. Gran nodded.

One more time. Emma closed her eyes. The smell was different. Damp. Dark. The way the garden smelled when Gran turned the soil over. Earth.

"The garden," Emma said. "Damp earth."

She opened her eyes. Gran was holding a handful of soil in her palm, dark and crumbly.

Biscuit sniffed at it and sneezed.
