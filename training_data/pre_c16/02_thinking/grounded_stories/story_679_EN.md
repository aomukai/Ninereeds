Mei stood at the bottom of the apple tree and looked up.

Dou was in the fork. Very still. Her grey tail hung down like a piece of rope.

"Dou," Mei said.

Dou looked down. Her one white ear moved. She did not come.

Mei put her hand on the trunk. The bark was warm from the sun. She shook it a little, not hard. The leaves rustled. A small green apple fell and landed in the grass.

Dou did not move.

Mei looked up again. The cat was high. The fork was wide. The leaves were thick and green all around her. She sat as if she had always sat there.

Mei walked to the stone bench and sat down.

The garden was quiet. A bee went past. The apple tree stood still in the afternoon light.

Mei watched the fork. She could see Dou's shape between the leaves. The cat was not asleep. She was just sitting, looking down at the garden, at the bench, at Mei.

Mei waited.

She did not call again. She sat with her hands on her knees and watched the tree.

Above her, Dou did not move either.
