Clara stood at the blackboard. She held a piece of chalk.

"Who knows the answer?" she said.

Emma looked at the numbers on the board. She was not sure.

Then she saw Mei's hand go up. It was a small hand, but it was high in the air.

Clara saw it. She waited.

"Yes, Mei?"

Mei's voice was quiet at first. It was like a leaf falling — small and soft.

"Seven," she said.

Clara nodded. She turned to the board. The chalk made a thin, clear sound as she wrote. The number came out neat and round.

"Seven is correct," Clara said.

Mei lowered her hand. She did not look around. She looked straight ahead at the board, at her answer sitting there in white.

Emma looked at the number. Then she looked at Mei. Mei had known. Mei had not needed to be loud about it.

The chalk dust settled on the ledge. The room was quiet again. Clara was already writing the next question.
