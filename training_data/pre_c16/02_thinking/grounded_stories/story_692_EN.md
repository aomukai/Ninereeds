Taro sat on the flat stone at the edge of the pond. The water was still and green.

Biscuit did not sit. He crashed straight in. His front legs hit first, then his whole body. Water splashed up in a wide sheet. The ripples spread all the way to the reeds.

Bello stood at the edge. He put one paw in. Then another. He did not go deeper. He stood there with his front feet wet and his back feet dry, watching Biscuit swim in circles.

Then Dou came.

She did not come from the meadow path. She came from the reeds, where no one had seen her. She walked along the edge of the pond. Her tail was straight up, the white ear pointing forward. She kept her feet dry. She walked past the dogs as if they were stones.

She reached the flat stone. Taro moved his legs to give her room. She stepped onto the stone. She sat down.

For a moment she did nothing. She looked at the water. Then she reached out one paw and touched the surface. One touch. The water made a small ring.

She pulled her paw back. She shook it once. A single drop hit Taro's arm.

Then she stood, stepped off the stone, and walked back into the reeds. She did not look at the dogs again.

Biscuit shook himself on the bank. Bello shook himself at the edge. The ripples on the pond grew smaller and smaller, and then they were gone.
