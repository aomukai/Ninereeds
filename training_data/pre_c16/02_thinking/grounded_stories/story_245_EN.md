The mill stood dark against the white field. The wheel was still.

Mei pushed the door. It opened slowly, heavy on its hinges. Inside, the air was cold and still. The dust had settled on everything — on the stone floor, on the wooden hopper, on the iron fittings of the millstones. Nothing moved.

Gran stood in the doorway. She did not step inside.

Mei touched the edge of the hopper. Her finger left a line through the dust. "It's quiet," she said.

"It will wait for spring," Gran said.

Biscuit pushed past them into the room. His claws clicked on the stone. He sniffed the base of the millstones, then the corner where the chute came down. Nothing interested him. He sneeced once and sat down near the door.

Mei looked up at the wheel through the small window. Ice sat on the paddles, thick and white. The stream outside was silent.

"Everything sleeps," Mei said.

Gran nodded. "The mill rests. It changes. In spring it will turn again."

Mei stood beside her in the doorway, looking into the dark, quiet room. The dust lay still. The wheel did not move.
