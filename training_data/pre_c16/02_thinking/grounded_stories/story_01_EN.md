Biscuit sniffed at the base of the oak. His tail wagged. Emma crouched beside him and saw the small hole between the roots. "Squirrel," she said.  

Taro jumped for the low branch. He pulled himself up, scraping his knees on the rough bark. The oak creaked under him.  

Something small and brown fell. It landed with a soft *plop* in the leaves. Emma picked it up. The acorn was smooth in her palm, its cap rough like the tree. A tiny crack ran down one side.  

"Look," she said. Taro swung down. Biscuit nudged her hand with his nose.  

Taro took the acorn and turned it over. "It's a seed," he said. He poked at the crack.  

Biscuit barked at the squirrel hole. A rustling came from inside.  

Emma held out her hand. Taro dropped the acorn back into it. The wind shook the branches. Another nut fell, then a dry leaf, spinning.  

Biscuit pawed at the hole. The children looked up. The oak stood quiet, its roots deep, its branches full of secrets.
