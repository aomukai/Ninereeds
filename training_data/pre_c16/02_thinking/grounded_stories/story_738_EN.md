The kitchen was still.

Emma sat at the wooden table. A yellow square of light lay on the wood in front of her. It was sharp at the edges, warm where it touched her hand.

She watched it.

The square was not still. It moved. Very slowly, the yellow shape crept across the table. It touched the salt cellar. It touched the knife. It reached the edge of the bread board.

Gran came to the table. She set a loaf down. The light fell on the bread for a moment, then the bread was in shadow and the light had moved past.

“It is climbing,” Emma said.

Gran wiped her hands on her apron. “The sun is climbing.”

Emma looked at the window. The light came in at a different angle than before. The square on the floor was longer now, stretching toward the stove.

She put her hand where the square had been. The wood was still warm.

The clock on the shelf ticked. The light kept moving. The kitchen smelled of bread and morning.
