The rain fell all night. Emma heard it on the roof above her bed, loud and steady. In the morning, the air was still wet.

They walked to the pond. The meadow path was soft under their boots. Biscuit ran ahead, then came back, then ran ahead again.

The pond was different.

It was higher. Much higher. The water had climbed past the flat stone where they usually sat. Now the stone was underwater. A thin brown line marked where the water had been before.

"It's full," Emma said.

Taro stepped in. The water came up to his knees. "It used to be this deep," he said, holding his hand at his ankle.

The reeds were half underwater. Their tops stuck out, but their stems were hidden. A twig floated past, spinning slowly. A leaf followed it, riding the current toward the overflow stream at the far end.

Biscuit splashed after the twig. The water splashed up, dark and muddy.

Emma stood at the edge. She did not wade in. She watched the pond being full and new and different, and the water reaching places it did not usually reach.
