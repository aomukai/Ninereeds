The pond was different now.

Yellow leaves lay flat on the water. Brown leaves curled at the edges. They covered the surface like a patchwork quilt. The water was so still that the leaves did not move at all.

The reeds stood dry and brown along the edge. Their heads had gone soft and white.

Gran walked to the bench under the willow. She wiped the damp wood with her sleeve before she sat. She did not say anything.

Biscuit sniffed at the pond's edge. He put one paw in, then pulled it back. The water was cold. He shook his paw and stepped away.

Emma picked up a leaf from the grass. It was yellow with brown spots. She held it by the stem and walked to the flat stone. She leaned over and let it fall.

The leaf landed on top of the water. It sat on the surface and did not move. It lay among the other leaves as if it had always been there.

Taro stood beside her. "The ducks are gone," he said.

Emma nodded. The pond felt different without them. It felt quieter. Colder.

She looked back at the bench. Gran was sitting very still, watching the water. Her hands were folded in her lap.

Biscuit came and sat beside Emma. He did not bark. He just watched the leaves with her.
