Riku's bicycle was heavy today. The canvas bag on the side was full, and on top of it sat a brown parcel tied with string.

He rode slowly along the village street. The parcel did not wobble. He had tied it well.

At the post box he stopped. He pulled a letter from his bag and pushed it through the slot. The metal flap clanked shut.

Then he turned into the lane. The bicycle bumped over the stones. The parcel stayed where it was.

He stopped at Gran's gate. He lifted the bicycle onto its stand. The parcel was heavier than it looked. He carried it in both hands.

He knocked at the cottage door.

Gran opened it. She was wiping her hands on her apron.

"Riku," she said.

"Morning," he said. He held out the parcel. "For you."

She took it. The brown paper crackled. The string was tight around the middle.

She looked at the address. "Ah," she said. She said her own name, reading it from the label.

Riku nodded. He turned and walked back to his bicycle.

Emma came to the door. She looked at the parcel in Gran's hands.

"It came," she said.

Gran turned it over. She did not open it yet. She held it and felt the weight.
