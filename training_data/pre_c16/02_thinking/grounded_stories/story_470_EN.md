Emma stopped at the mill door. The wheel was turning. The noise came through the floor first — a low rumble she felt in her shoes.

Mei stood beside her. "It's the stone," she said. "Grinding."

Biscuit had stopped too. He stood a few paces back, ears forward, head low. His nose worked — sniff, sniff, sniff — but he did not move forward.

"Come on," Emma said.

Biscuit took one step. Then he stopped. The floor trembled under his paws. He looked at the wheel. It was large and dark and it turned and turned and he could not make sense of it. He stepped back.

Emma looked at Mei. "He won't come in."

Mei watched the dog. "He feels the vibration," she said. "He doesn't know what it is."

Biscuit circled once, slowly. He sniffed the doorway again. Then he sat down just outside, facing the mill, and did not move.

Emma shrugged. "He'll wait."

Inside, the wheel kept turning. The stone kept grinding. The noise went through the floor and out into the yard where Biscuit sat watching, still not sure.
