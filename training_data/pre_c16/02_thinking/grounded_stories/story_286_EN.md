Emma held the side piece against the front board. It did not sit flat. A gap showed along the edge—thin as a fingernail, but there.

She pressed harder. The gap stayed.

Vern came over. He did not take the piece from her. He put his finger on the spot where the wood was high. "There," he said. "Feel it."

Emma ran her thumb along the edge. It caught. A tiny ridge, barely there.

She picked up the sanding block. The paper was rough and grey. She rubbed it along the high place—short strokes, light. Sawdust gathered on her fingers.

She blew the dust off. Tried again.

The piece slid closer. The gap was smaller, but not gone.

"Once more," Vern said.

She sanded again. Slower this time. She checked with her thumb after every few strokes. The ridge flattened. The edge felt smooth.

She fit the piece against the front board. It sat flush. No gap. No tilt.

Taro leaned in. "Is it square?"

Vern held the small carpenter's square against the corner. The metal rested flat. The wood met it exactly.

"Correct," he said.

Emma set the piece down. The fit was tight. It would hold.
