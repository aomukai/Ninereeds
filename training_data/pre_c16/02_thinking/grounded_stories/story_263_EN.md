Vern’s workshop smelled of sawdust and oil. Light fell through the open door in a wide yellow square.

Vern held a nail between his thumb and finger. He set it on a block of wood.

“Try,” he said.

Taro took the hammer. He lifted it high and swung.

The nail bent sideways with a soft *ping*. It leaned like a broken tooth.

Vern did not say anything. He reached for the hammer. He turned it around and slid the claw under the bent nail. He pulled. The nail came out with a small squeak.

He held a new nail between his fingers. He tapped it once, gently, with the hammer face. The nail stood straight in the wood, held by its own point.

“Start it light,” Vern said. “Then drive it.”

He handed the hammer back.

Taro took a breath. He tapped. The nail stayed straight. He tapped again. It went deeper. He hit harder. The nail moved down smooth and even.

Emma watched from the doorway. The hammer made a clean sound now — *thock*, *thock*, *thock*.

The nail sank until its head was level with the wood.

Taro lowered the hammer. He looked at Vern.

Vern nodded once.
