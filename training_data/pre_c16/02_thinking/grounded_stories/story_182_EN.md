The pond was still. The last light clung to the willow leaves overhead. Emma sat on the flat stone, her toes just touching the water. Taro crouched beside her, peering into the dark.  

"Listen," Gran said from the bench.  

The frogs had been quiet all afternoon. Now one called, then another, then a dozen at once. Their voices filled the air like pebbles dropped into water. Something small and quick flitted over the pond — a bat, maybe, though Emma couldn’t see it properly. The water, blue all day, was black now except where the sky touched it.  

"It’s different," Taro whispered.  

Gran nodded. "This is how it is when we’re not here."  

The frogs kept calling. The bat came back, or another like it. The air smelled of wet grass and something else — something night had brought with it. Emma held very still. The pond was quiet but full, as if the dark had turned it inside out.  

Gran stood. "Time to go."  

They walked back through the hush, the frogs loud behind them.
