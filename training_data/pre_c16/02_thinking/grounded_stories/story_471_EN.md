Gran lifted the iron bar. The cellar door swung open, slow and heavy. A cold breath came up from below — the smell of earth, of apples stored in crates, of something dark and still.

Bello pushed his nose into the gap before anyone could stop him. He sniffed once, hard. Then he sneezed. The sneeze made him pull back, ears flat. But in a moment he pushed forward again, nose working, tail still.

Taro stood behind him. "He's going to bolt in," he said.

Gran did not answer. She was already reaching for the lamp.

Bello sniffed the top step. Then the second. His nose twitched at the cool air rising. He sneezed again — a small sharp sneeze — and then he stopped.

He turned away.

Biscuit was already at the garden wall, sniffing the place where Dou sometimes sat. Bello trotted over to join him, tail up, done with the cellar.

Gran lit the lamp. The flame caught and steadied.

"Coming down?" she said.

Taro looked at the dark steps, then at the dogs. The dogs had lost interest. He stepped carefully around the open door and followed Gran down.
