Emma put her foot on the low branch. The bark was rough under her palm. She pulled herself up.

From the ground, the fork in the oak looked like a tangle. From up here, she could see the nest.

It sat in the crotch of two branches, tucked in tight. Dry grass and moss, woven together. A few feathers stuck out from the rim.

Emma leaned forward. She held the branch with both hands.

Inside, three eggs.

Blue. A soft blue, like the sky after rain. Small. Smooth. She could see the curve of them, the way they sat together in the hollow.

She did not touch.

Above her, a robin called. Short, sharp sounds. It landed on a higher branch and watched her. Its head turned.

Mei stood below, looking up. Her hands were in her pockets.

"How many?" she said.

"Three," Emma said.

She looked at them a moment longer. The shells had no spots. Just the blue, even and quiet.

Then she put her foot down, found the next branch, and climbed back to the ground.

The robin stopped calling.
