The robin flew into the oak tree. Emma stopped walking. She put her hand out to stop Mei.

"Look," she whispered. "Up there."

The bird landed in the fork of a high branch. It had something in its beak. A twig, or maybe a piece of moss. It disappeared into the tangle of branches.

Mei tilted her head back. "Is that the nest?"

They moved closer. Very slowly. Very quietly.

From below they could see the edge of it — a cup of dry grass and mud, wedged into the fork where the branch split in two. And inside the cup, something blue.

Three eggs. Small. Blue. The colour of the sky on a clear morning, but softer.

Bello trotted up and circled the trunk once, twice. His ears stood up. He looked at the tree, then at the children, then at the tree again.

Biscuit lay down on the roots. He put his nose on his paws and watched.

The robin landed on a lower branch. She did not sing. She sat very still, watching them back.

No one moved.

The eggs stayed blue. The nest stayed quiet. The robin watched.
