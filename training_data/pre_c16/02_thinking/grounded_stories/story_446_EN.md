Bello pushed through the garden gate with his nose. The wood swung wide, and he stepped through — then stopped.

His front foot hung in the air. His tail, which had been high and waving, went down. Just a little.

Dou sat on the wall. Still as stone. One white ear turned forward.

Emma crouched beside Bello. She put her hand on his back. She could feel him holding very still.

“It’s all right,” she said, very soft. “That’s just Dou.”

Bello did not look at Emma. He did not take his eyes off the cat.

Dou blinked. Slow. One eye, then the other.

Bello’s tail dropped another inch.

Emma stayed low beside him. She kept her voice quiet and even. “She won’t move,” she said. “She’s just looking.”

Bello’s ears went forward, then back. He did not bark. He did not step forward. He did not step back.

Dou turned her head. Just slightly. Her tail curled higher on the wall.

Bello’s whole body was tight, like a held breath.

Emma waited. She did not push him. She did not pull him away.

The garden was very quiet for a long moment.
