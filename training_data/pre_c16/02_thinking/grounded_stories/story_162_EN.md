Emma stood under the apple tree, looking up. Three birds perched on the lowest branch—one brown, two speckled. They pecked at something unseen.  

"One," she whispered, pointing. "Two. Three."  

A gust shook the leaves. The birds lifted as one, wings snapping open. They vanished over the garden wall.  

Emma stared at the empty branch. "None left."  

Gran knelt by the rosemary, fingers dusty with soil. She didn’t look up. "Three birds. Then none. Zero is still a number."  

Emma touched the rough bark. The branch still held the shape of their weight. She could almost see the dents where their claws had been.  

"Zero," she said to the air where they’d flown.  

The tree was quiet. Only the leaves moved, turning the same way the birds had gone.
