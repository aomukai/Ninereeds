The rain had been falling all morning. It fell on the meadow path and on the field beyond. It fell on Owen's flat cap until the cap was dark and heavy.

Emma walked with Gran. The rain tapped on Gran's coat. It dripped from the hedge leaves. When they reached the gate, Owen was there.

He leaned on the gate. His coat was wet. His sheep stood bunched against the hedge on the far side of the field. They did not move. They stood with their heads down and the rain running off their wool.

Gran stopped under the same tree. Emma stood beside her.

Owen looked at the sky. Then he looked at the field.

"It's wet," he said.

Gran nodded.

The rain dripped off the gate. It dripped off Owen's cap. A drop ran down his nose and fell.

Emma watched the sheep. They waited. Owen waited. The rain did not stop.

Owen said something about the weather. Then he said nothing else. He leaned on the gate and looked at his field.

Gran did not hurry. She stood under the tree. The rain kept falling, and the water ran down the gate and into the grass.
