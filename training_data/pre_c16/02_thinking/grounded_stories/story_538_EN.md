Emma carried the broken handle down the lane. It was split at one end, the wood rough and splintered.

Vern’s workshop door stood open. The smell of sawdust and linseed oil came out into the street.

Vern looked up from his bench. He did not say anything. He held out his hand.

Emma gave him the handle. He turned it over in his fingers. He ran his thumb along the split. He looked at the metal ring at the other end. Then he put it on the bench and picked up a piece of fresh wood.

He set the wood in the vise. He chose a plane from the wall. The blade caught the wood and a thin curl peeled away, pale and smooth. It fell onto the floor.

Emma watched the shavings pile up. Curly and thin. They smelled clean.

Vern measured with a small rule. He held the new piece against the old one. He trimmed a little more. Then he set the plane down.

He picked up the new handle and turned it in his hands the same way he had turned the broken one. He nodded once.
