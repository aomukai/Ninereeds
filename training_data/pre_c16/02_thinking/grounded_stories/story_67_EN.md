Taro lay on the sofa with his knees pulled up. The blanket was heavy over his shoulders, but his feet were cold. He pressed his hands flat against his stomach and waited.  

It came in waves. Between them, he could almost sit up. He could look at the clock on the wall and count the ticks. Then the next wave would rise, and he would curl tight again, very still, until it passed.  

Gran brought a glass of water. She held it where he could reach. "Small sips," she said.  

He took one, careful not to move too much. The water was cool. It helped, a little.  

Gran sat in the chair by the window. She had her mending basket, but she wasn’t sewing. She watched him over the top of her glasses.  

Another wave came. He shut his eyes. When he opened them, the light had changed. The clock ticked. Gran was still there.  

"Further apart now," she said.  

He nodded. The next wave was smaller. He uncurled, just a little, and let his feet slide toward the edge of the sofa. The floor was solid under them.
