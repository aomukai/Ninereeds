Mei stood to one side of the millstones. She was not near the chute where the flour came down.

Taro stood right in front of it. He wanted to see.

The flour dust puffed up. It went into his eyes.

He blinked hard. He rubbed his face with both hands.

Yun saw him from the stall table. She came over and took a corner of her apron. She wiped his face clean.

“There,” she said. “Keep your eyes shut a moment.”

Mei pointed to the floor on the side of the room. The dust did not settle there. The floor was clean in that spot.

“I stand here,” she said. “The dust goes past.”

Taro opened his eyes. He looked at the mark on the floor where Mei stood. It was lighter than the rest.

He moved to that place.

The millstones turned. The flour came down. The dust drifted past him and did not touch his face.

He stayed where the floor was clean. He knew the spot now.
