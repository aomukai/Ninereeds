Owen came up the lane with the pumpkin in his arms.

It was very big. Bigger than a bucket. Bigger than a stool. He had both arms wrapped around it, and his hands were pressed flat against the sides. His fingers did not meet.

Mei was at the stall with Yun. She saw Owen first.

"That is a big one," she said.

Owen set the pumpkin on the stall table. The table creaked. He lifted it onto the brass scale. The arm of the scale dropped to the bottom and stayed there. It did not move.

Yun shook her head.

"Too much," she said. "The scale cannot hold it."

Owen lifted the pumpkin off the scale. He carried it to the ground beside the stall and set it down. It sat on the stones, heavy and still.

Yun straightened the table. "I will sell it whole," she said. "Not by weight. By the piece."

Mei put her hand on the pumpkin. It was cool. The skin was ridged and hard. She could not tip it. She could not move it at all.

Owen wiped his hands on his coat. He looked at the pumpkin on the ground.

Then he nodded and walked back up the lane.
