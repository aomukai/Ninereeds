Emma stood at the bakery door. The heat came out in a wave.

Hana was at the big table. Her hands pushed the dough. Folded it. Pushed again. The dough was pale and soft. Hana's hands were fast.

"I'm putting them in now," Hana said.

She slid the loaves into the oven on a long wooden peel. The smell changed. It got warmer. Fuller. Emma breathed it in.

Gran gave Emma a coin. "A loaf for supper."

Emma put the coin on the counter. Hana took it. She wrapped the bread in brown paper. It was warm through the paper. Emma held it against her coat.

---

Further down the street, Vern's workshop door was open. He was not at his bench. He was kneeling beside a chair. The leg was loose.

"Fixing it," he said.

He took a small saw. Cut a wedge of wood. Tapped it into the joint with a wooden mallet. His hands knew where to tap. He ran a finger over the join. Nodded.

"There," he said. "Good as new."

Emma watched his hands. Hands that made dough rise. Hands that made wood hold.
