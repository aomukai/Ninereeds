Emma woke to stillness. The room was colder than usual. She pulled the blanket tighter and padded downstairs.

The sitting room was dim. The fire had burned low overnight — nothing left but grey ash and a faint orange glow in the centre. Emma crouched close. A single ember pulsed weakly, like a tiny heartbeat.

Gran came in carrying a log. "Morning," she said softly. She knelt beside Emma, her breath visible in the cold air. Carefully, she set the log on the ember. She leaned forward and blew gently. The ember flared, then dimmed. She blew again. A thread of smoke curled up.

Emma held her breath. Slowly, the log began to blacken at the edges. A small flame licked up, hesitant at first, then steadier. Gran sat back on her heels. "There we are."

The room filled with warmth and light. Emma stretched her hands toward the fire. The ash beneath the log crumbled, but the flames grew stronger, feeding on the new fuel.

Gran stood. "Breakfast?" she asked, already heading for the kitchen. Emma nodded, her eyes still on the fire.
