Mei stood in the corridor. She looked at the row of hooks. Each one had a card above it with a name.

Emma came out of the classroom. She saw Mei looking.

"There," Emma said. She pointed to the empty peg near the end. "That one has no card."

Mei did not say anything. She held her coat in her hands.

Clara came out behind them. She had a small white card and a pen. She wrote on the card in her careful letters. M-E-I.

"Here," Clara said. She handed the card to Mei. "Put it where it belongs."

Mei took the card. She held it flat in her palm. She looked at the letters, then at the empty peg.

Emma stepped closer. "You put it above the hook," she said. "Like this." She pointed to her own card. E-M-M-A.

Mei reached up. Her fingers found the small nail above the empty peg. She placed the card on it. It sat straight.

She let her hand drop. She hung her coat on the hook below.

"Good," Clara said, and went back into the classroom.

Mei touched the edge of the card once more. Then she followed.
