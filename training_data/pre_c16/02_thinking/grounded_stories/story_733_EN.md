Gran gave Emma a small brown seed. It was dry and hard, no bigger than her thumbnail.

"Poke it in there," Gran said, pointing to the soil.

Emma pushed her finger into the earth. The soil was dark and cool. She dropped the seed into the hole and covered it. Then she patted the spot flat.

Every few days she came back with Gran's watering can. The water made the soil darker. Nothing happened for a long time.

Then one morning a green tip had pushed through. It was so small she almost missed it. A leaf unfolded. Then another. The stem grew taller.

Gran pushed a cane into the ground beside it. Within three weeks the vine had found the cane and was climbing. Emma watered and watched.

A flower appeared. It was soft and pale. Then the flower fell away, and a small green thing was in its place.

In summer the green thing was a pod. It hung from the vine, fat and full.

Emma picked it. She held it in her palm. She pressed her thumb against the seam, and it opened.

Inside were more small seeds.
