Vern's workshop smelled of sawdust and wood. Three wooden bins stood against the wall. One was labelled OAK. One was labelled PINE. One was labelled ASH.

Vern put a pile of wood scraps on the workbench. "Sort these," he said.

Emma picked up the first piece. It was heavy and dark. She turned it over. The grain ran in dark lines, close together. She touched the surface. It was rough under her fingers.

She dropped it in the oak bin.

The next piece was lighter. She held it up. The colour was pale, almost yellow. The grain was wide and straight. The surface felt smooth, but slightly rough where the saw had cut.

Pine, she thought. She put it in the pine bin.

The third piece was different again. The grain was straight and pale, almost white. The wood felt hard and smooth. She ran her thumb along the edge. No splinters.

Ash.

She sorted the rest. Oak pieces were dark and heavy. Pine pieces were light and rough. Ash pieces were pale and straight-grained.

Vern watched. He did not say anything. When the last piece was in its bin, he nodded once.
