Emma had five coins in her pocket. Gran had said, “Bread for dinner.”

The market was busy. Emma walked past the baker’s smell. She walked past the post box. She stopped at Yun’s table.

Yun was weighing carrots on her brass scale. She said, “Hello, Emma.”

Emma said, “Hello.”

On the table, beside the carrots, beside the eggs, beside the dried herbs in paper bags — there was a jar of honey. It was small and golden. The light went through it.

Emma looked at the bread. It was on the far end of the table, wrapped in a cloth.

She looked at the honey.

She picked up the jar. It was warm from the sun. She held it in both hands. She could see the honey move inside, slow and heavy.

She wanted it.

She looked at the bread again.

She put the honey back.

She took the bread. It was heavy and round. She gave Yun the five coins. Yun put them in her apron pocket and nodded.

Emma walked home. The bread was warm under her arm. She held it tight.

She looked back once.

The honey was still on the table, catching the light.
