Emma stood at the garden gate. She pushed. The gate did not move.

She pushed harder. Her hands were flat against the wood. Her arms were straight. The gate stayed where it was.

She pulled instead. It rattled a little. It did not open.

Emma's face went red. She pushed again, hard, with her shoulder. The wood was warm from the sun. The gate did not care.

Gran was in the garden, pulling something green from between the beans. She looked up. She did not say anything.

Emma stopped. She looked at the gate. There was a metal latch. It was hooked over a metal loop. That was what held it.

She lifted the latch with her thumb. It came free. She pushed. The gate swung open.

Emma stood in the open doorway. She looked back at Gran.

Gran nodded once, and went back to the weeds.
