The oak looked wrong.

Emma stood at the base and looked up. She knew this tree. She had sat on the low branch. She had watched the robin in the fork. She knew how it stood against the sky.

But today it was different.

The top branch was gone. There was a space where it had been — a pale, raw end where the wood had broken.

Taro was already at the fallen branch. It lay on the ground, longer than he was tall. He touched the splintered end where it had torn from the trunk. The wood was rough and wet. A piece came away in his hand.

Owen came across the field. He leaned on the gate and looked up at the tree.

"It broke in the night," he said. "The wind caught it."

Emma kept looking up. The shape of the tree had changed. She could see more sky than before.

"Will it grow back?" she asked.

Owen shook his head. "Not that branch. But it will grow a new one. Trees do that."

Taro picked up a long splinter from the ground. He ran his thumb along the edge. Then he laid it beside the broken branch.

Emma looked at the tree again. It was still the oak. But it was not the same.
