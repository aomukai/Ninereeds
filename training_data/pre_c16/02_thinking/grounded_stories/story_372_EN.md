The hill was green. The sheep were spread across it like clouds that had come down to rest.

Emma stood at the bottom and watched. The sheep moved slowly, their heads down, eating. Their jaws moved from side to side.

Taro ran up the slope. He stopped. He started counting on his fingers.

"Six," he said. Then a sheep walked past another sheep. "Seven. No. Six."

The sheep kept moving. The shape of the flock changed.

Owen stood by the gate. His dog sat at his heel, watching the far edge of the slope.

"They don't stay still," Emma said.

"No," said Owen. "They don't."

A lamb bleated — a thin, high sound. It ran across the slope, fast on thin legs, back to its mother. The mother did not lift her head. The lamb pushed under her and stood still.

Biscuit sat beside Emma. His ears were up. His nose worked. He watched the sheep but he did not move.

Taro started counting again. He got to nine. Then a ewe turned and he lost count.

"I had it," he said.

The dog circled the far edge of the flock. The sheep shifted. They moved down the slope, eating as they went, slow and steady.
