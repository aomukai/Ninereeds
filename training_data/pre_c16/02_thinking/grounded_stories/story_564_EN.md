Emma carried the handle down the side street. The wooden end was split. The metal head had come loose.

Vern took it from her hands. He turned it over once. He set it on the bench.

"Oak," he said. "The grain ran out."

He walked to the wood stacked against the wall. He ran his hand along a piece near the door. He picked it up and carried it to the bench.

He set the new piece in the vise. The vise closed with a low creak. Vern picked up a plane. The blade was sharp and thin.

He pushed the plane along the wood. A curl of shaving lifted and fell onto the floor. Thin and pale. It curled like a ribbon.

Emma watched. The plane made a sound — a clean whisper. Another curl fell. The wood began to look like the old handle. Same shape. Same length.

Vern stopped. He took the piece out of the vise. He held it against the tool head. It fit.

He put the handle in the vise again. He picked up a small pot of glue. He brushed it into the hole in the tool head. Then he pushed the new handle in.

He wiped the extra glue with his thumb. He set the tool on the bench. "It needs to wait now," he said.

The clamp went on. The glue would hold.
