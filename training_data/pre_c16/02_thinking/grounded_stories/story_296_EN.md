Vern ran his hand along the plank. It was long and straight and pale.

He took a pencil from behind his ear. He marked the plank four times. Each mark was the same distance from the next.

Taro watched. "What are the marks for?"

"Legs," said Vern.

He carried the plank to the saw. The blade bit into the wood. Dust fell onto the floor in a fine line. He cut four pieces. Each one was the same length.

The rest of the plank was wide and flat. Vern turned it over in his hands. He set it on the workbench.

Emma reached out and touched the flat piece. "Is that the seat?"

Vern nodded. He picked up a chisel and a mallet. He cut a notch in each leg. Then he cut a notch in the seat. He fitted them together. The wood held without glue.

Taro leaned close. "It stands."

Vern set the stool on the floor. He pushed it with one finger. It did not wobble.

"Try it," he said.

Taro sat. The stool held his weight. It did not shift.

Mei ran her finger along the joint. "One plank made all of that."

Vern picked up the pencil and put it back behind his ear.
