Emma had coins in her hand. They were round and cool. She held them tight so they would not fall.

The market stall was tidy. Yun stood behind it. The brass scale sat at one end. The eggs were in a basket near her hand.

“I want eggs,” Emma said.

Yun nodded. She put four eggs into a paper bag, one at a time. The bag was small. The eggs were brown.

Emma opened her hand. The coins lay flat on her palm. She picked one up and put it into Yun’s hand.

“One,” she said.

She picked up the next one.

“Two.”

Yun’s hand stayed open. Her fingers did not move.

“Three.”

The last coin was warm from Emma’s palm. She set it on top of the others.

“Four,” she said.

Yun closed her fingers around the coins. She dropped them into her apron pocket. The pocket made a small clink.

“The last number tells how many,” Yun said. She pushed the paper bag toward Emma. “Four eggs. Four coins.”

Emma took the bag. It was light. She could feel the eggs inside, round and still.

“Thank you,” she said.

Yun was already straightening a row of jars. She nodded without looking up.
