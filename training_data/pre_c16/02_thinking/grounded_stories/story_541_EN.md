The bakery light was still on. Emma pushed the door. The bell rang.

Hana stood behind the counter. Her apron had flour on it, even now. She was wiping the wooden surface with a grey cloth. Slow circles.

The shelf beside the window was nearly empty. One loaf sat alone on the board. It was smaller than the morning loaves.

“Is that the last one?” Emma asked.

Hana nodded. She picked it up. It was still warm. She wrapped it in brown paper, folded the ends tight, and handed it over.

“Half price now,” Hana said. “End of the day.”

Emma felt the warmth through the paper. She put the loaf in her basket. The bakery smelled of bread and something sweet that had already gone.

Hana looked at the shelf. Empty. She wiped the counter one more time, then wrung the cloth out over the sink.

“Goodnight,” Emma said.

“Goodnight.”

The bell rang again as Emma stepped out into the lane. Through the window she saw Hana take off her apron. The room behind her was quiet. The shelf was bare.
