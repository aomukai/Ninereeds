Dou sat on the garden wall. She was washing one paw — slow, careful, her head tilted. The white ear flicked once.

Mei and Emma stood on the grass below, watching.

“She does that for a long time,” Emma said.

“She likes to be clean,” said Mei.

Gran was at the vegetable patch, picking beans into a bowl. She did not look up, but she said, “She’s watching the hens.”

The hens were scratching near the apple tree. Dust rose in small puffs. Dou’s tail moved — just the tip.

Then Dou stopped washing. She sat very still. Her eyes were on the hens.

Emma held her breath.

Dou jumped.

She did not jump toward the hens. She jumped down into the garden, onto the stone bench by the kitchen door. She landed without a sound. Her tail went up, straight and tall.

She sat on the bench. She did not look at the apple tree. She did not look at the garden gate where Biscuit and Bello were sleeping in the sun. She had picked her spot.

Gran straightened up and looked at the bench. “That’s where she wants to be,” she said.

Dou began to wash the other paw.
