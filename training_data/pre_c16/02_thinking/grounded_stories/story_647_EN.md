Gran pushed the fork into the earth near the potato plants. The soil was dark and crumbly. She leaned on the handle and lifted.

The potatoes came up with the roots — pale shapes against the brown earth. Some were large. Some were small. One rolled out and lay on its side in the sun.

Emma knelt down. She picked up the biggest one. It was smooth and pale, with a few thin roots still clinging to the skin. She brushed the dirt off with her fingers. The skin was thin and clean underneath.

She put it in the basket.

Then she found the smallest one. It was no bigger than her thumb. She turned it over in her hand. It was round and perfect, like a tiny egg.

She brushed that one too and added it to the basket.

Gran pushed the fork in again. More potatoes came up. Emma counted them as she picked them up. Four big ones. Six medium. Three small ones like the first.

The basket grew heavier. The earth around the plants was full of holes where the potatoes had been.

Emma looked at her hands. They were brown with dirt. She rubbed them together and the dry earth fell away.

Gran wiped the fork with a handful of grass. "That's enough for today," she said.

Emma picked up the basket. It was heavier than before.
