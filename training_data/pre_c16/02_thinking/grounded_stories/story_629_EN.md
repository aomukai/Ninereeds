The light was grey. Not the grey of rain or cloud, but the grey of dusk coming early.

Emma stood by the flat stone. The pond did not look like the pond. The edges were white and hard. Ice had crept in from the reeds, thin and still.

Taro crouched at the bank. He pressed one finger against the ice. It did not break. It was cold and quiet.

No frogs. No ducks. The reeds were dark shapes, stiff and bent. The willow hung bare, its branches black against the fading light.

Biscuit trotted to the edge. He sniffed the ice. He sniffed the frozen mud. Then he turned back and looked at Emma, his tail low.

"It is too cold," Taro said.

Emma nodded. The pond was not their pond now. It belonged to the ice and the shadows.

They turned and walked up the meadow path. Biscuit stayed close. The grass was stiff under their feet. The light was going fast.

Behind them, the pond grew darker. The reeds became one shadow. The ice held still.

Soon it would be dark.
