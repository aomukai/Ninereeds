The mill floor was stone, but not all of it was solid.

Mei walked ahead. Her feet knew where to go. At one board she stepped over it without looking down — just a small shift to the side, like she had done it a hundred times.

Taro did not see her step. He put his foot on the board.

The board tipped. The far end went up and banged against the stone floor. Taro jumped back.

"What was that?"

Mei pointed. A nail head stuck up from the edge of the board, rusted and sharp. "That one. I always miss it."

Emma came closer and looked. The board was loose at both ends. The nail had worked its way up over time. One wrong step and the board tipped.

"How do you remember?" Emma asked.

"I don't," Mei said. "My feet know. They step over before I think about it."

Taro looked at the board. Then he looked at the path across the floor. He took a step to the left, where the stone was solid, and walked past.

Mei nodded. "Good. Now you know it too."
