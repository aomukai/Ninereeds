Emma climbed onto the low branch. She held the bark with both hands and looked into the fork.

Three eggs. Blue. Small as her thumb.

She climbed down without speaking.

Three days later, she climbed up again. Mei stood below, one hand on the trunk.

The eggs were gone. Three small birds filled the nest, mouths open wide, pink and hungry.

"Look," Emma said, very quiet.

The parent robin came. A worm in its beak. It dropped the worm into one open mouth, then another, then the third. Then it flew away.

Mei watched from below. "They need so much food."

Emma checked every day. The birds grew. Feathers pushed through. The nest got crowded.

One morning Emma climbed up and the nest was empty.

She looked around. A fledgling sat on the low branch, not far from her hand. It had a red breast, soft and new. It blinked.

"Don't move," Mei whispered.

The bird sat very still.

Then it opened its wings and flew — a short, wobbling flight to the next branch. Then another.

Emma smiled.

"Wait," she said.

They waited. The parent robin landed beside the fledgling. It had a worm.
