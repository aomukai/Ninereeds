Mei stopped at the edge of the stream. She pointed down at the water.

"Look," she said.

Taro looked. The water split. Half of it ran straight ahead. The other half turned into a narrow stone channel, cut into the bank.

"Someone dug it," Mei said. "A long time ago. To make the mill wheel turn."

Taro knelt and put his hand in the wide part. The water was slow and cold. Then he touched the channel. The water there ran faster. It pulled at his fingers.

"It's narrower," he said.

Mei nodded. "So it goes quicker."

Biscuit pushed past them and put his whole nose into the wide stream. He drank in long, noisy laps.

Taro stood up and followed the channel. It ran straight beside the mill wall, then bent around the corner. He watched it turn. The water kept going, fast and smooth.

He walked further. The channel bent again, then opened out. The water spilled back into the main stream, slower again, joining the flow that had gone the long way around.

Taro looked back at the mill. Then down at the water.

"It goes in," he said, "and then it comes out again."

Mei was watching him watch the water. She did not say anything. She did not need to.
