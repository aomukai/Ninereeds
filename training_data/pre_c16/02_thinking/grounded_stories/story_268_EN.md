Mei sat on the stool at Vern's workbench. She held a piece of oak in one hand. It was heavy and dark, smooth from where Vern had planed it.

She pressed the tip of her knife into the wood. The blade skidded sideways. It did not bite. She tried again, harder. The knife left a faint scratch, nothing more. The oak resisted.

Vern watched from the other end of the bench. He did not say anything. He reached past her and picked up a different piece of wood — pale, light, almost white. He set it down in front of her.

"Try this," he said.

Mei put the knife to the pine. The blade sank in. A long curl lifted away from the wood, thin and smooth. The knife ran easily, without force. A shaving curled over her finger and dropped into her lap.

She looked at the oak again. Then at the pine.

"Different woods," Vern said, picking up his own knife. "Some resist. Some give."

Mei ran her thumb across the pine. It was soft. The oak was hard. The knife knew the difference before she did. She set the oak aside and turned the pine in her hands, planning where the next cut would go.
