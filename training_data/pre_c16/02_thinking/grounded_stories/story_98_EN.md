Emma stood on tiptoe under the oak, her neck craned. The nest was a dark smudge in the fork above, but if she tilted her head just so, the morning light caught the curve of one pale blue egg.  

"Same as yesterday," she said.  

Taro scrambled onto the low branch and peered. "Still three. Not yet."  

Gran knelt by the vegetable patch, pinching off pea shoots. "Give it time," she said. "Eggs know when to wait."  

Emma counted the days in her head. Four mornings now, checking early before school. The robin always flitted away as they approached, but returned when they left.  

"Tomorrow?" Taro asked, swinging his legs.  

"Or the day after." Gran dropped a handful of shoots into her basket. "Things happen when they're ready."  

Emma squinted up again. The egg gleamed, smooth and unchanging. But deep inside, she imagined something stirring, small and patient, keeping its own quiet time.  

"Come on," said Taro, jumping down. "We'll look again tomorrow."  

And they did.
