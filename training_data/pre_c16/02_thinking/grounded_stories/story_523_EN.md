Mei set the jar on the nature table. It was small, with a metal lid. Inside, something was very white and very still.

Clara came over. "What have you brought?"

Mei twisted the lid off. The jar was full to the top. She tilted it toward Emma.

Emma touched the top with one finger. The white stuff clung to her skin. It was soft and cool. She rubbed her thumb against it. The stuff felt like nothing she had held before — not sand, not salt, not sugar.

"It's flour," Mei said. "From the mill."

Clara took a piece of black paper from the drawer. She tipped a little flour onto it. The white mound sat on the black, very round at first. Then it spread a little, settling into a flatter shape, the edges soft and uneven.

Emma bent close. The flour smelled of grain — dry and dusty and somehow warm.

"Touch it again," Mei said.

Emma pressed her fingertip into the mound. The flour gave way. It clung to her skin in a fine white coat. She held her hand up. The flour dust caught the light from the window.

"It's so fine," she said. "Like powder."

Clara nodded. "That is what grinding does. Stone against stone, grain by grain, until it is this soft."

The flour sat on the paper, very white, very still, waiting.
