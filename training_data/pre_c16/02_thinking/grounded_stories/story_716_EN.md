Emma stood under the apple tree and tipped her head back.

The branches were covered in white blossom. Hundreds of small pink-and-white cups, each one open to the morning light. Bees moved from flower to flower, their legs heavy with yellow.

"I remember this tree in winter," she said.

Gran was on the stone bench, pulling the tops off beans. She did not stop working.

"Bare branches," Emma said. "No leaves at all. Just sticks."

"Same tree," Gran said.

Taro came out of the kitchen door with a piece of bread. He looked up at the tree and stopped.

"It doesn't look the same," he said.

Emma touched a low branch. The blossoms were soft and cool, and a few petals came away on her finger.

"Later," Gran said, "there will be small green apples. Hard ones."

Emma tried to imagine the blossoms gone, the green fruit in their place. She could not quite see it.

"And then big red ones," Taro said.

"And then winter again," said Emma.

She looked at the tree, trying to hold all of it at once: the winter branches, the spring flowers, the autumn fruit. But the tree only showed her what it was right now.
