Vern pulled a long plank from the stack by the wall. He ran his hand along it once, then set it on the bench.

"Four sides," he said. He measured with a small wooden rule and made a mark with a pencil. The pencil was flat on one side and left a thin grey line.

Taro stood close. He watched the saw blade bite into the wood. Dust fell in a fine line onto the bench.

Vern cut four pieces. They were the same width, the same length. He set them on the bench in a row.

"Hold these," he said to Taro.

Taro put his hands on the two end pieces. They were smooth. Mei picked up the piece for the bottom. She turned it over and set it down on the bench.

"It needs to be square," Vern said.

Mei looked at the edges. She touched the corner with her finger. Then she nodded.

Vern fitted the four sides together without glue. They stood up. The corners met. He turned the box in his hands and looked at each joint.

"Good," he said.

He took the sides apart. He spread glue along the edges with a small brush. The glue was pale and thick. He pressed the sides together and fitted them into the clamp. The clamp had wooden jaws. He turned the handle until the joints closed tight.

He set the box on the bench.

"Tomorrow," he said.

Taro touched the clamp. The wood was still wet at the seams. Mei stood back and looked at the box from the side.
