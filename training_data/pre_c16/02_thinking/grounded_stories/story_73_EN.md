Emma watched as Gran took the apple from the bowl. Its skin was smooth and red, cool under her fingers. The knife went in with a quiet crunch.  

Gran turned the halves apart. The inside was white, damp at the edges where the blade had passed. And there, at the centre—five small brown pips, pressed close in a star.  

Emma leaned in. "I didn’t know they were like that."  

Gran held one half toward her. "The tree keeps them safe."  

Emma touched a pip with her fingertip. It came away easily, leaving a shallow hollow in the flesh. The core was darker, a rough little tunnel where the stem had been. Outside, the skin shone. Inside, everything was quiet and secret.  

Gran wiped the knife on her apron. "One for the garden," she said, and set a pip aside on the windowsill. The rest went into the compost bowl with the cores.  

Emma took a bite. The flesh was sweet, the skin tart where her teeth broke through. She held her half up to the light, turning it so the star caught the sun.
