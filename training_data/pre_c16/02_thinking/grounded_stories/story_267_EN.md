Vern's workshop smelled of sawdust and oil. The door stood open. Spring air came in, but the room stayed cool.

Emma stood at the workbench. The wood was pale and smooth. Vern picked up a pencil and drew a rectangle on it. The line was straight and careful.

He took a chisel from the peg. The handle was dark, worn to the shape of his hand. The blade was flat and sharp.

"Watch," he said.

He set the chisel on the line. The edge stood straight up. He picked up the mallet — a short wooden hammer — and tapped.

*Tap.*

A small chip lifted from the wood.

He moved the chisel along the line and tapped again. Another chip. Then another. Each tap went deeper. Emma watched the recess grow.

Vern worked the chisel across the bottom, levering the wood loose. Chips fell onto the bench. The recess deepened. He checked the depth with his finger.

One more tap. Then he set the chisel down.

"Feel," he said.

Emma put her finger into the recess. The bottom was flat. The sides were straight. The depth was even all the way across.

Vern brushed the chips into his palm and dropped them into the bin.
