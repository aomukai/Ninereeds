The puddle was deep after the rain. Taro jumped in first, sending water splashing up to his knees. Emma stepped in more carefully, feeling the cool mud squeeze between her toes.  

Next morning, the puddle was smaller. The edges had pulled back, leaving a ring of wet earth. Taro crouched and skimmed his fingers across the surface. "Less to jump in," he said.  

The day after, only a dark patch remained, the ground damp but no water left to splash. Emma pressed her palm flat—warm, not wet.  

By the fourth day, the earth had cracked. The same place where they’d jumped was now dry lines and dust. Taro poked at a curled-up edge. "Gone," he said.  

Emma touched the cracks. The sun had been warm all week. The puddle hadn’t run away—it had just turned to nothing, bit by bit.  

Biscuit sniffed the spot, lost interest, and trotted on. The meadow path stretched ahead, dry and waiting.
