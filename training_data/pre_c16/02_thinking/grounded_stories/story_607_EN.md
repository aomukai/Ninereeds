Dou sat in the fork of the apple tree. Her grey fur blended with the bark. One white ear stood out against the red leaves.

Mei stood below and looked up. "Dou," she said.

Dou looked down. Her tail was curled around her paws. She did not move.

Emma came across the grass. She stopped beside Mei. "How did she get up there?"

"She climbed," Mei said.

Biscuit arrived last. He put his front paws on the tree trunk and whined once. A small, high sound.

Dou looked at him. She did not move.

There was an apple on the branch beside her. Red and round. She did not touch it. She sat very still and watched the garden.

Emma reached up. Her fingers fell short by a long stretch. "Can you reach her?"

"No," said Mei.

Biscuit whined again. Dou blinked. She turned her head very slowly and looked at the apple. Then she looked back down at the dog.

She did not climb higher. She did not come down. She sat in the fork, tail still, ears up, watching everything from where no one could reach.
