Biscuit followed Mei and Taro down the lane. He knew where they were going.

At the mill, Taro pushed the heavy door. The air smelled of dust and old stone.

Mei walked to the sluice. She put her hand on the iron lever. "Watch the wheel," she said.

She pulled.

At first nothing happened. Then a sound came from below — a deep, slow groan. The wheel began to turn. Water splashed. The wheel turned faster.

Biscuit stopped.

He did not put one paw forward. He stood very still. The noise came up through the floor. He felt it in his feet, in his chest, in his ribs.

Mei called him. "Biscuit. Come here."

He wagged once. Low. His tail did not leave his legs.

Taro crouched beside him. "It's just the wheel," he said. "It's just water."

Biscuit looked at the wheel. He looked at the door. He sat down just inside it, where the stone floor was dry and the noise was a little quieter.

Mei waved from beside the sluice. The wheel shook water into the channel. Biscuit watched and did not move.
