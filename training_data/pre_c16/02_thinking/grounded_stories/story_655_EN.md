Emma stood at the edge of the vegetable patch. She remembered when the soil was bare and brown, turned over by Gran's fork. Now the bean plants climbed the canes in tight green spirals.

She knelt and touched a leaf. It was soft and wide.

Gran was pulling weeds near the carrots. She tugged one up and tossed it onto the grass.

"Are the carrots ready?" Emma asked.

"Some are," Gran said. She did not look up. "Pull one and see."

Emma chose a thick green top and pulled. The carrot came out pale orange, damp with soil. It was as long as her hand.

She held it up. "This one was not here before."

"It was," Gran said. "It was just small."

Emma looked at the pumpkin patch. The leaves were huge and flat. Yellow flowers sat open on the stems. The pumpkins were still green and small.

"Next month," Gran said, "they will be orange and heavy."

Emma looked at the garden. The beans were climbing. The carrots were ready. The pumpkins were waiting. Every time she came out, something had changed.
