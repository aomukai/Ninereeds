Dou sat on the flat stone by the pond. Her grey tail curled around her paws. She stared at the water.

Bello trotted down the path. His ears stood up like little flags. He saw the cat and his tail went up too.

He stopped two paces away. He wagged his tail. He waited.

Dou did not move.

Bello whined. A small, high whine. He took one step forward. Then another.

Dou blinked. Very slowly. She turned her head toward the water. She watched a dragonfly skim the surface.

Taro laughed. “She does not care at all,” he said.

Mei smiled. “She never does.”

Bello whined again. He sat down. He looked at Dou. He looked at Taro. He looked back at Dou.

Dou yawned. Her pink tongue curled. She turned her head the other way.

Biscuit came splashing through the reeds. Water flew everywhere. He shook, and drops landed on the flat stone.

Dou stood up. She stepped off the stone. She walked along the edge of the pond without hurrying. Her tail was straight up. She did not look back.

Bello watched her go. His tail dropped. He sat in the wet grass and sneezed.

Taro laughed again. “She won,” he said.
