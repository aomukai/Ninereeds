Gran came to the workshop with something wrapped in a cloth. She set it on the bench and unfolded the corners.

It was a stool leg. The end was split — a long crack running up from the bottom, and a splinter loose at the side.

Vern picked it up. He turned it in his hands, slowly. He looked at the break from one side, then the other. Then he fitted the two halves together dry. They met. The crack closed.

He nodded.

He reached for the glue bottle. A small brown bottle with a brush in the cork. He set the leg on the bench, opened the bottle, and brushed glue into the crack. Then he pressed the pieces together and held them.

“Clamp,” he said.

Gran handed it to him.

He fitted the clamp over the leg and turned the screw. The jaws tightened. A bead of glue squeezed out at the seam.

Vern wiped it with his finger.

“Tomorrow,” he said.

Gran nodded. She looked at the leg in the clamp, then at the row of other things waiting on the shelf — a chair rung, a broken handle, a box with a split corner.

“Good,” she said.
