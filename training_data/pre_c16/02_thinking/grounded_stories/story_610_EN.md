The meadow path was dry. The grass on either side was tall and pale in the sun.

Taro walked ahead. Biscuit had stayed at the cottage with Bello. So Taro walked alone, kicking at the dust.

“Taro,” Gran said.

He stopped. She was standing still at the hedge gap. Not walking. Just looking.

He went back to see what she saw.

Through the gap the pond was visible. The willow hung over the water. The flat stone sat at the edge. The light caught the surface and made a glint that shifted when he moved his head.

He had passed this gap a hundred times. He had never stopped.

“It’s always there,” he said.

“Yes,” Gran said. “But you don’t always see it.”

Owen was in the field beyond the pond. He raised a hand. Gran raised hers back. No words. Just the hand going up and coming down.

Taro looked at the pond again. The water was still. The willow was still. The shape of it sat in the light like something placed there on purpose.

Then Gran turned. She walked on down the path. Taro looked one more time through the gap, and then he followed.
