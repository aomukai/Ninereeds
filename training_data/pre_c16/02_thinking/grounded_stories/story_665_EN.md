Mei carried Dou down the meadow path. The cat did not wriggle. She sat in Mei's arms with her tail tucked, her one white ear turned forward.

At the pond, Mei set her down on the grass.

Dou did not move. She stood very still, her front paws close together, her whiskers aimed at the water.

Emma sat on the flat stone and watched. Biscuit would have splashed in by now. Bello would have put his nose in and sneezed.

Dou took a step. Then another. Her paws went down silently, one at a time. She stopped at the very edge, where the reeds began, and sat.

She watched the water.

A frog jumped. A small one, from a lily pad into the deeper part. The ripple spread, slow and wide, and then the surface went still again.

Dou's ear flicked. That was all.

She leaned forward. Her front paw lifted, hovered, and came down very slowly into the water. The tip of her paw went under. She held it there for a count of three. Then she lifted it, shook it once — a quick, precise shake — and set it back on the dry grass.

She sat again. She watched the water.

"She is different," Emma said.

"Yes," said Mei. "She does not need to go in."

They sat together on the flat stone. Dou stayed at the edge, still as the reeds, watching the place where the frog had gone.
