The path to the mill was damp. Emma followed Mei along the edge of the stream. The water ran fast and clear over the stones.

Mei stopped where the bank widened. "Look," she said. She pointed.

Two streams flowed toward each other through the grass. One came from the left, thin and quick. The other came from the right, a little wider. Just ahead of where the girls stood, the two streams met.

The water joined. It curled and swirled for a moment, then ran on together, wider and faster than before.

Emma watched the place where they had become one. The current was stronger now. It pulled at the grass along the edges.

"That is why the wheel turns so well here," Mei said.

She led Emma further down the bank. The combined stream rushed toward the mill building. The water wheel stood against the east wall, dark with damp. Water poured over it, and the wheel turned steadily, slowly, with a low sound you felt through the ground.

Emma looked back up the stream. She could not see the place where the two had been separate anymore. The water was one now, and it did not look back.
