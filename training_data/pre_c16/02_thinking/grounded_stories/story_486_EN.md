Clara stood at the blackboard. The chalk made a soft tapping sound as she wrote.

3 + 5 = ?

The number three was round on one side and flat on the other. The number five had a belly and a hat. The question mark looked like a hook.

Taro looked at the board. He moved his lips but no sound came out.

Emma put her hands on the desk. She held up three fingers on her left hand. Then she held up five fingers on her right hand. She looked at them. She counted them.

"One, two, three," she said. Then she started again. "One, two, three, four, five."

She looked at her fingers and she looked at the board. The line under the question mark waited.

Taro leaned over. He pointed at her left hand. "Three," he said. Then he pointed at her right hand. "Five."

He counted them all together, touching each finger with his own. "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight."

Clara picked up the chalk again. She wrote an 8 after the equals sign.

3 + 5 = 8

The chalk dust fell in a small white line on the ledge. Clara wiped her fingers on her apron.
