Emma opened her hand on the nature table.

A brown leaf lay in her palm. It was dry. The edges curled inward like a tiny cup. She set it down carefully, afraid it might break.

Taro came to the table. He held a green leaf, still soft and flat.

“Where did you find it?” Clara asked.

“By the oak,” Taro said. “It was still on the tree.”

Clara put the two leaves side by side on the table. The green one lay flat. The brown one was curled and brittle.

Taro reached out. He touched the brown leaf with one finger. It crumbled. A piece fell off and landed on the wood.

“It’s dead,” Taro said.

Emma looked at the green leaf. She touched it. It did not break.

“This one is alive,” she said.

Clara nodded. She picked up the brown pieces and put them in a small dish.

“Bring more leaves tomorrow,” she said. “We can press them between paper.”

Emma looked at the brown crumb on the table. She brushed it into her hand. It was light and very thin.
