Snow had come in the night.

Emma stood at the edge of the field and looked at the oak. It was not the same tree.

The branches were white lines against the grey sky — thin at the tips, thick where they joined the trunk. The bark was hidden under a layer of soft white. The low branch that she sat on in summer held a ridge of snow along its top.

She walked closer. Her feet sank a little with each step. Behind her, her footprints marked a dark trail across the white.

Bello hopped through each print, placing his paws exactly where hers had been. He looked up when he reached the tree and sneezed.

The squirrel hole was gone. The roots were covered — smooth white slopes where the rough bark used to be. Emma bent down and brushed the snow away with her hand. The dark hole appeared underneath.

“Still there,” she said.

Taro came around the other side of the tree. “Look.”

He pointed at the ground. Small marks led from the trunk out into the field — two little dents side by side, then two more, then a space, then two more. A bird’s tracks, heading away.

They followed them a few steps. The tracks stopped under a low branch. The snow had fallen from the branch and covered them.

Biscuit pushed past, nose deep in the snow, and came up with a mouthful of white. He shook his head and sneezed twice.

The oak stood still and quiet. It was the same shape, but the snow had made it something else.
