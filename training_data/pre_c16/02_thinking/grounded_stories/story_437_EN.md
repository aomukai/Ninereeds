The root cellar door was propped open with the iron bar. The steps were cold under Emma’s feet.

Mei was already at the bottom, holding Gran’s metal lamp. The flame made her shadow move against the stone wall.

“There is one more,” Mei said.

She reached to the very back of the shelf, behind the empty jars. Her fingers found it.

The jar was small. The label was yellowed at the edges. In Gran’s careful writing: *September.*

“It is the last one,” Emma said.

They carried it up together. The glass was cool. The plums inside were dark—almost black against the light.

That evening, Gran twisted the lid. It did not open. She tapped the edge with the back of a knife. The seal broke with a soft pop.

She tipped the plums into a bowl. They were soft and dark, swimming in thick syrup.

Emma took a spoonful. The taste was sweet and deep, like late summer held still.

“It tastes like autumn,” she said.

Mei nodded. She took a smaller spoonful and said nothing.

Gran wiped the jar clean and set it on the counter. The shelf below was empty.

The last jar from autumn was gone. The cellar would wait until the new fruit came in.
