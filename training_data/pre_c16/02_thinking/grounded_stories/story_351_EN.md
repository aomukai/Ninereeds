Taro ran up the hill. Biscuit ran ahead, then circled back. Bello darted left and right, chasing something only he could see.

The grass was short and dry at the top. The sky was very wide.

Biscuit found the gate and pushed his nose under it. Bello jumped at a butterfly and missed. They ran in circles near the fence, panting.

Taro lay down on the grass. The ground was warm through his shirt. He put his hands behind his head and looked up at the clouds.

It was very still. No wind. Nothing moved.

Taro listened.

First he heard his own breathing. Then the dogs' paws on the grass. Then something else — faint, but clear.

A voice. Small and far away.

He knew it. Clara's voice. She was out in the schoolyard. He could not hear the words, but he could hear the shape of them. The way she called.

The sound carried all the way up the hill.

Biscuit came over and sniffed Taro's ear. Taro did not move. He kept listening.

The voice stopped. Then it came again, a little louder. Then it was gone.

Taro closed his eyes. The grass tickled his neck. The sun was warm on his face.

Bello barked at something down the slope. Biscuit trotted over to see what it was.

Taro opened his eyes and sat up. The village lay below, small and quiet. The school roof was a grey square. He could not see Clara, but he had heard her.

He stood up and ran after the dogs.
