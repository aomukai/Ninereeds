The cellar door was heavy. Gran propped it open with the iron bar. The steps were cold under Emma’s feet.

The lamp made the shadows move. Jars stood on the shelf in a row. Dark shapes inside them.

“Plums,” Taro said. He pointed. “I think there are ten.”

Emma looked. The shelf was deep. The jars were close together. “Seven,” she said. “Maybe seven.”

Gran came down the steps. The lamp light caught her hands. She put one finger on the first jar and moved it along.

One. Two. Three.

Taro leaned forward. Four. Five. Six.

Emma counted with her eyes. Seven. Eight.

Gran’s finger stopped. Nine jars. She lifted the last one and held it up. The plums inside were dark and still.

“Close,” she said.

She put the jar back. It settled against the others with a small sound.

Taro looked at the shelf again. “I was one off.”

“I was two,” Emma said.

Gran turned with the lamp. The light slid across the jars and then away. “Come,” she said. “The door is still open.”
