The daisies grew along the edge of the meadow path. White and yellow, thick as stars.

Emma knelt and picked one. She held it up and began to pull the petals away, one by one.

"Eight," she said.

Mei picked another. She sat down in the grass and counted her petals slowly, touching each one with her fingertip.

"Eleven," she said.

Emma looked at the two flowers. One was bigger. One was smaller. But the petals were different numbers.

She picked another. Mei picked another.

"Six," said Emma.

"Six," said Mei.

They looked at each other.

Emma picked a clover flower, purple and round. She counted the tiny petals. Mei picked a clover too.

"Twenty? No. More," Mei said.

They picked more daisies. Some had twelve. Some had nine. Two had ten. They laid them in a row on the path, petals scattered beside them like small white teeth.

Emma picked one more. It was small. She counted.

"Eight," she said.

Mei picked one from the same clump. She counted.

"Eight."

They did not know why. The path was long and the daisies went on. They picked another.
