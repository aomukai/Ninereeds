The air hung heavy over the pond. Not a leaf stirred. Emma crouched by the flat stone and saw her face in the water—not a wobble, not a blur, as if someone had laid a pane of glass over the surface. The sky stretched below just as it stretched above, cloud for cloud.  

Taro held his breath. A duck quacked, far off by the reeds, and the sound came sharp as if it were beside them.  

Gran stood at the bench, her hands resting on the back. "Listen," she said.  

They did. No rustling grass, no creaking branches. Only the high hum of a single fly, the plop of a fish rising somewhere out of sight.  

"Doesn't happen often," Gran said. She didn't explain. She didn't need to.  

Emma touched the water with one finger. A ring spread outward, slow and perfect, breaking the sky into pieces that wobbled and settled back into place. For a moment, the world was doubled, and whole, and still.
