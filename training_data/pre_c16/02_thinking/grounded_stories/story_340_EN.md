Stefan stopped at the stall. He walked his beat slowly, hands behind his back, and then he stopped.

Mei was at the table. She was stacking apples in a pyramid. Yun sat on a low stool behind the brass scale, tying a twist in a paper bag.

Stefan looked at the turnips. They were in a wooden box at the edge of the table. He leaned forward and picked one up.

It was heavy. The skin was smooth, pale at the top and darker toward the root. He turned it over in his hand. He felt the weight. He felt the slight give at the top where the leaves had been cut.

He set it down. He picked up another one. This one was rounder. He turned it over the same way.

Mei watched. She did not say anything.

Stefan put the round one on the table. He reached into his pocket and brought out a coin. He placed it beside the turnip.

Yun looked at the coin. She nodded once.

Stefan picked up the turnip. He put it in his coat pocket. It stuck out a little.

He walked on. His beat continued down the street. The turnip stayed in his pocket, heavy and round.
