The rain fell straight down. It tapped on the umbrella and bounced off the stones of the lane.

Emma held the umbrella low. Taro walked beside her with his hands in his pockets. Water ran in a small stream along the edge of the lane.

At the gate, Owen stood without moving.

His coat was dark with rain. Water dripped from the brim of his hat in a steady line. He did not seem to notice.

Emma stopped. Taro stopped too.

Beyond the gate, the field was soft and wet. The grass lay flat in places. Under the hedge, the sheep stood close together, heads low, backs dark with water.

Owen was looking at the field. Not at the sheep. Not at the children. At the field itself — the wet grass, the mud, the way the rain sat on everything.

"It is still coming down," Owen said.

Emma nodded. She did not say anything.

Taro looked at Owen's hat. The water dripped from the brim. Drip. Drip. Drip.

"Will the sheep be all right?" Taro asked.

Owen did not turn his head. "They know where to wait," he said.

The rain fell. Owen stood at the gate. Emma and Taro stood in the lane.

No one moved.
