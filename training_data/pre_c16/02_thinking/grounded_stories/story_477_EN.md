Gran reached up into the pear tree. Her fingers found the stem of a pear and twisted it gently. The pear came off in her hand.

Emma stood at the base of the tree and looked up. Dou was in the high fork, tucked into a place where the branch split. Her grey body was still. Her white ear caught the light.

“She does not want the pears,” Emma said.

“No,” said Gran. She put the pear into her basket and reached for another. “She wants the height.”

Dou did not look down. She blinked slowly. The leaves around her were small and yellow at the edges. The branch she sat on was flat and wide. She had found the warmest place in the garden.

Emma watched her. The cat did not move. She did not flick her tail. She was part of the tree.

“Will she come down?” Emma asked.

“When she is ready,” said Gran. “Not before.”

Gran picked another pear. The basket filled slowly. Dou stayed in the high fork, still and quiet, and the sun moved across her fur.
