Emma walked beside the fence. Wooden posts stood in a long line. Each one was the same.

She stopped at the first post. The wood was grey and rough. She took three steps to the next post. She counted as she went. One. Two. Three.

She did it again. One. Two. Three. The same number of steps between each post and the next.

Taro watched. Then he jumped. He landed between two posts, crouched, and jumped again. The gap was the same each time. He kept jumping, post to post, along the fence line.

Emma walked and counted. Taro jumped and landed. The pattern repeated — post, space, post, space — all the way to the corner of the field.

At the corner the fence stopped. Then it turned. Emma looked along the next side. More posts. More spaces. The same line, starting again.

Taro stood at the corner, breathing hard. "Does it go all the way around?"

Emma looked across the field. The posts marched on, one after another, each in its place.

"I think so," she said. "All the way to where we started."
