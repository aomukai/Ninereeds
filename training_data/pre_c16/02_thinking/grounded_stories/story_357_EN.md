Gran pulled the gate to the hill shut behind them. The grass at the top was short and cold underfoot.

Emma looked west. The clouds were low and dark, piled one on top of another like heavy blankets. They filled the sky where the sun had been that morning.

The wind came across the hilltop without anything to stop it. It pushed at Emma’s coat and pulled at her hair. She had to lean into it to stay still.

Taro stood with his hands in his pockets, watching the clouds move closer. “It’s coming fast,” he said.

Gran nodded. She did not take her eyes off the west. “It will be here soon.”

The first drops hit Emma’s cheek. Cold and sudden.

“Time to go down,” said Gran.

They turned together. The cottage was small below them, the chimney sending up a thin grey line of smoke. The wind pushed at their backs as they walked.

They reached the bottom of the hill. The oak stood dark against the field. The first real rain began to fall, tapping on the dry winter grass.
