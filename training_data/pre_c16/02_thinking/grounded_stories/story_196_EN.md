Mei put her hand on the iron lever. It was cold and smooth.

"This is the sluice," she said.

Taro stepped closer. The lever was as tall as his shoulder. A metal rod ran from it down into a narrow channel where the stream ran dark and fast.

Mei pulled. The lever moved with a heavy, grinding sound. Water began to run faster beneath the mill. The wheel above them groaned once, then began to turn. Faster. The noise changed — from a low creak to a deeper, steady rhythm. Water splashed. The whole building seemed to hum.

Taro watched the wheel. It was large and dark with damp, turning steadily now, throwing drops into the air.

"Can I try?" he said.

Mei stepped back. Taro took the lever with both hands. He pushed it closed. The grinding sound came again. The wheel slowed. The water in the channel grew quiet. The noise fell away until there was only the stream again, running under the stone.

Mei looked at the still wheel.

"Fast or slow," she said. "It depends on the lever."
