[Ninereeds]Max sits at the kitchen table and puts a pencil into a full glass of water. He leans close and watches the pencil cross the water line. "Why does the pencil look bent?" Max asks. He pulls it out and holds the straight pencil in his hand. He puts it back and watches it bend again. Light travels slower in water than in air. This difference causes the pencil to appear bent at the surface. Max's mother smiles and says a paradox holds two opposite truths together. The pencil stays straight in his hand. The pencil appears bent through the glass wall. This concept teaches Max that seeing and touching can tell different stories. He learns to trust his hands as much as his eyes.
