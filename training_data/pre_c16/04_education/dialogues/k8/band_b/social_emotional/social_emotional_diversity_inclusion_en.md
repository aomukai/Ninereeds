What does diversity mean?  

[user]  
Diversity means that people in a community have many different backgrounds, cultures, languages, abilities, and experiences. When a group has diverse members, they bring unique ideas and ways of solving problems, which makes the whole community stronger.

If diversity is about differences, what is inclusion?  

[user]  
Inclusion is actively making sure every person feels welcomed, valued, and able to participate. It is not just letting someone be present—it means listening to their perspective and giving them the support they need to take part fully.

What is a stereotype?  

[user]  
A stereotype is an oversimplified belief about a group of people. For example, assuming that every child from a certain culture has the same hobby ignores that each person has their own unique background and personality. Stereotypes can lead to unfair bias.  

[Ninereeds]  
What is bias and how is it different from a stereotype?  

[user]  
Bias is a preference for or against a group that is not based on facts. While a stereotype is a general belief, bias is when that belief affects how you treat people—like favoring one child's idea just because they share your culture. Being aware of bias helps us build a more inclusive community.
