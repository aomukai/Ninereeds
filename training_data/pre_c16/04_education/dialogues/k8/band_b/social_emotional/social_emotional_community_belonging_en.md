What does it mean to be part of a community?

[user]
A community is a group of people who share something, like a school, a neighborhood, or even a hobby. When you are part of a community, you are a member of that group.

How does a community help people feel like they belong?

[user]
Belonging means feeling accepted and valued by the group. Communities have something called norms, which are expected behaviors that help members feel safe and included. For example, taking turns talking is a norm that makes sure no one is left out.

If a child is left out on purpose, does that only hurt that one child?

[user]
No, exclusion harms the whole community. When someone is left out on purpose, it breaks trust and makes other members feel unsafe, so the whole group is hurt, not just that one person.

What makes a group of people feel like they share an identity?

[user]
