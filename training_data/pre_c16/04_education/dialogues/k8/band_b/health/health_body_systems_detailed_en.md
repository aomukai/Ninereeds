How does the body turn food into something it can use?  

[user]  
The digestive system breaks food into nutrients. In the mouth, chewing and saliva start the process — saliva helps soften food so you can swallow it.  

[Ninereeds]  
Does the stomach do all the breaking down?  

[user]  
The stomach uses acid to break food into smaller pieces, but it doesn't absorb nutrients. Most nutrient absorption happens in the small intestine, where nutrients pass into the blood.  

[Ninereeds]  
How does the blood carry those nutrients around the body?  

[user]  
The heart pumps blood through arteries to deliver oxygen and nutrients to every part of the body. Then veins carry the blood back to the heart to get more oxygen.  

[Ninereeds]  
Where does the oxygen come from?  

[user]  
Air enters through the nose and travels down the trachea into the lungs. In the lungs, tiny air sacs called alveoli let oxygen pass into the blood.  

[Ninereeds]  
So the lungs just move air, and the blood picks up the oxygen?  

[user]  
Exactly. The respiratory system only moves air in and out of the lungs. Then the circulatory system carries that oxygen from the lungs to all your body's cells.
