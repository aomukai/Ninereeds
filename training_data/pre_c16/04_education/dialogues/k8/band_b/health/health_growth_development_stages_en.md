Why do some kids in my class start growing taller much earlier than others?

That's a great question. The reason is that each person's body has its own internal schedule for releasing hormones, which are chemical messengers made by glands. These hormones trigger growth and changes, but they start working at different times for different people.

So hormones are like little signals that tell my body when to grow?

[user]
Exactly right. Hormones are chemical messengers that travel through your blood to different parts of your body, telling them when to start changing. One important one is called growth hormone, and it helps regulate how tall a person becomes.

Does everyone go through the same stages of life, or do some people skip a stage?

[user]
Everyone passes through the same stages of life, but not at the same speed. The stages are infancy, early childhood, middle childhood, adolescence, adulthood, and old age. Puberty is part of the adolescence stage, when the body matures toward adulthood.

What kind of changes happen during puberty besides getting taller?

[user]
During puberty, glands release hormones that cause many physical changes, like a growth spurt and voice deepening, as well as emotional changes. These changes are normal and happen because of the chemical messengers inside you, not because of anything you eat or do.
