What are the different kinds of voices in a choir?  

[user]  
In a standard choir, there are four voice parts from highest to lowest: soprano, alto, tenor, and bass. Some children think the alto is lower than the tenor because “alto” sounds like “low,” but alto is actually a higher voice than tenor—alto is a female voice and tenor is a male voice.

How is an orchestra put together?  

[user]  
An orchestra is divided into four sections: strings, woodwinds, brass, and percussion. For example, violins are in the string section, flutes are in the woodwinds, trumpets are in the brass, and drums are in the percussion section. Each section has its own family of instruments.

What does a composer actually do?  

[user]  
A composer is a person who writes music. For instance, a composer named Mozart wrote many symphonies and operas during a time called the Classical era, and his music is still performed by orchestras today.

Why do the different sections of an orchestra sound so different from each other?  

[user]  
The sound comes from how each section makes the sound. In the string section, players use bows to draw across the strings, while in the brass section, players buzz their lips into a mouthpiece. That difference in sound production gives each section its own unique tone.
