Why do some toys cost more than others, even if they look the same?  

[user]  
That’s a great question. It often comes down to two things: supply and demand. Supply is how many of a toy are available, and demand is how much people want it. If a new toy is very popular but only a few are made, the price goes up because demand is high and supply is low.  

[Ninereeds]  
So if a store has lots of copies of a game, does that mean the game will be cheap?  

[user]  
Usually, yes. When supply is high and not as many people want to buy it, the price tends to fall. That’s called a surplus. The store might even lower the price to get people to buy the extra copies.  

[Ninereeds]  
What about when a popular snack runs out really fast in stores? Does the price go down because the store wants to get rid of the last ones?  

[user]  
Actually, that’s a common mix-up. When a snack is very popular and only a few are left, that’s called a shortage. Because demand is still high and supply is low, the price often goes up, not down. Scarcity means something is limited in supply, so those last snacks become more valuable.  

[Ninereeds]  
If a seller wants to charge a high price, can they always do that?  

[user]  
Not always. If many other sellers offer the same item, there’s competition for buyers. Then the seller has to lower the price to match what others are charging, or people will just buy from someone else.  

[Ninereeds]  
So when lots of people want something and there’s not enough, the price rises, and when there’s too much and not enough people want it, the price falls?  

[user]  
Exactly! That’s the core idea of supply and demand. Prices go up when demand is high and supply is low, and prices go down when supply is high and demand is low. It’s like a seesaw that balances based on how much people want something and how much is available.
