[Ninereeds] Why did the British want colonies in North America?

[user] Britain wanted colonies because of an idea called mercantilism. Mercantilism says a country gets richer by selling more manufactured goods to other countries than it buys from them. So Britain passed tariffs, which are taxes on trade, to control what the colonies could sell and buy. The colonies had to send raw materials like timber and tobacco to Britain, and then buy expensive manufactured goods from Britain.

[Ninereeds] So did that make the colonies rich too?

[user] Actually, no. The mercantilist system was designed to make the mother country, Britain, rich, not the colonies. The colonies were forced to sell their raw materials at low prices and buy British goods at high prices. That kept the colonies poorer than Britain.

[Ninereeds] I’ve heard about something called the triangular trade. What was that?

[user] The triangular trade was a three-legged trade route linking Europe, Africa, and the Americas. On the first leg, European ships carried manufactured goods to Africa. On the second leg, they carried enslaved Africans across the Atlantic to the Americas. On the third leg, they carried colonial cash crops like cotton and sugar back to Europe. Each leg made money for European merchants.

[Ninereeds] So the triangular trade was just about moving goods?

[user] Many people think that, but the middle leg of the triangle was the most important: enslaved Africans were the key cargo. They were bought and sold as commodities and forced to work on plantations in the Americas. Without the slave trade, the triangular trade would not have been so profitable for European traders.

[Ninereeds] What is a cash crop?

[user] A cash crop is a crop grown specifically to be sold for profit, not for the farmer’s own use. In the colonies, tobacco, cotton, and sugar were cash crops. They were produced on large farms called plantations, and most of that work was done by enslaved Africans who were forced to labor without pay.

[Ninereeds] If the colonies had to sell raw materials cheap and buy British goods expensive, who got all the money?

[user] The British merchants and the mother country got the most money. The colonists, especially the small farmers, stayed poorer. But a few wealthy plantation owners in the colonies also made some profit because they used enslaved labor to produce cash crops. Still, the whole system was set up to benefit Britain, not the colonies.
