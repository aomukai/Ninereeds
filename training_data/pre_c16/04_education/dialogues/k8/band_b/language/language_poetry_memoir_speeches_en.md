Teacher, what's the difference between a simile and a metaphor?  

[user]  
A simile compares two things using "like" or "as," for example "as brave as a lion" — that's a simile and also a common saying. A metaphor says one thing is another without those words, so "Her smile is a ray of sunshine" is a metaphor, not a simile.

Is a memoir the same as an autobiography?  

[user]  
No, they’re different. A memoir is a true story about one specific part of the author’s own life, like a summer at the beach. An autobiography covers the whole life from birth onward. So a book about a writer’s childhood summer is a memoir, not a novel or a full autobiography.

Why do speakers repeat phrases in speeches?  

[user]  
Speeches often use repetition to persuade an audience. For example, Martin Luther King Jr. repeated "I have a dream" many times to make his message powerful and easy to remember. That’s a kind of rhetorical device—using language to move people or make them agree with you.

What's alliteration? Is it like rhyme?  

[user]  
Alliteration repeats the same beginning sound in nearby words, like "silly snake" or "Peter Piper picked." Rhyme repeats ending sounds, like "cat" and "hat." They’re different sound patterns, but both make phrases catchy and easier to remember.
