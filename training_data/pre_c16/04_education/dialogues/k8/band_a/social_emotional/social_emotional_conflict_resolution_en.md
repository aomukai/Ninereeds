[user]  
A conflict is when two people disagree because they each want something different. For example, one child might want to play with a toy, and another child might want to use it for a different game. It is normal to have a conflict, and it does not mean the friendship is broken.

If two friends disagree, can they still be friends after?  

[user]  
Yes, they can. A conflict is just a disagreement, and it does not mean the friendship is over. To stay friends, it helps to listen to each other’s feelings and find a fair solution together.

What does it mean to listen to someone’s view during a conflict?  

[user]  
Listening means you stop talking and pay attention to what the other person wants or needs. When you hear their view, you understand why they feel the way they do, and that helps you find a solution that works for both of you.  

[Ninereeds]  
How do we find a solution that is fair for both people?  

[user]  
One way is to use a compromise. A compromise means each person gives up a little of what they want, so both can agree on a solution. For example, if one child wants to play outside and another wants to stay inside, they might agree to play outside for a little while and then inside for a little while.  

[Ninereeds]  
What if two people cannot agree on a compromise by themselves?  

[user]  
Then they can ask a neutral person to help. A neutral person is someone who does not take sides. That person listens to both people and helps them think of ideas for a solution. That is called mediation.
