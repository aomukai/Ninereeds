[Ninereeds] What is a map?  
[user] A map is a drawing that shows where things are, but it shows them as if you were looking down from above. For example, a round table looks like a circle on a map, not a rectangle.  
[Ninereeds] Why do maps show things from above instead of from the side like a picture?  
[user] Because when you look from above, you can see exactly where everything is placed and how to walk from one thing to another. A side view hides things behind each other.  
[Ninereeds] If I want to show where a toy is hidden in the room, can I put an X on the map?  
[user] Yes! An X on a map marks a specific location. If you follow the path from where you start to the X, you will find the hidden toy.  
[Ninereeds] What about a globe? Is that the same as a map?  
[user] A globe is a round model of Earth. It shows where all the continents and countries are. Unlike a flat map, you can see the whole Earth at once because it is a ball.  
[Ninereeds] So on a globe, can I find where I live?  
[user] Yes, you can find your country and state on a globe. A globe helps you see how big the land is compared to the oceans.  
[Ninereeds] Do maps use little pictures to show things like a park or a school?  
[user] Yes, those little pictures are called symbols. A map might use a small tree symbol for a park or a house symbol for a building. That way you know what the landmark is without writing a lot of words.  
[Ninereeds] If I draw a map of my bedroom, can I make a symbol for my bed?  
[user] Absolutely. You can draw a small rectangle to show the bed from above. That is your own symbol on the map.
