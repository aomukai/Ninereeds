Why do some things grow and some things don’t?  

[user]  
That’s a great question. Living things grow, and non-living things don’t grow. A plant is a living thing—it gets bigger and makes new leaves. A rock is a non-living thing—it stays the same size forever.

So if a plant grows, is it alive even if it doesn’t move like an animal?  

[user]  
Yes, a plant is alive. It grows and needs water and sunlight, but it doesn’t walk around. An animal is also alive, and it can move on its own to find food.

What about a toy car? It can move when a child pushes it, but is it a living thing?

A toy car is not a living thing. It only moves because someone pushes it. A living thing, like a dog or a cat, can move all by itself—without anyone pushing.

Does a living thing need to eat or drink something to stay alive?  

[user]  
Yes, all living things need food and water. A plant takes water from the soil, and an animal eats food. A non-living thing, like a chair, never needs food or water.
