If C15 confirms the JP gap persists after full EDJC rotation, a JP-specific corpus block is the next intervention.

**Candidate sources:**
- https://imabi.org/ — comprehensive JP grammar reference, very detailed
- https://guidetojapanese.org/learn/ — Tae Kim's Guide; progressive grammar structure

**Why these work for BDH:** Pedagogical resources retain particles and subjects explicitly (more than colloquial speech would), which gives cleaner co-firing signal. Natural Japanese drops subjects, drops particles, and ellipts verbs when context is clear — minimising exactly the explicit co-occurrence BDH learns from.

**How to apply:** Build a [user]/[Ninereeds] corpus from grammar explanations, targeting particle usage, subject retention, and register. This is a targeted intervention, not a general JP expansion. Only pursue if C15 brain scans show JP after-hub similarity consistently below EN/DE/ZH even after EDJC rotation normalises positional exposure.

**Why JP may be structurally harder for BDH:** Pro-drop (missing subjects), particle omission in casual speech (りんご食べたい, うち帰る), and deep ellipsis (俺は車だ meaning "I came by car" — entire verb phrase omitted). The language offloads to shared context; BDH learns from explicit co-occurrence. These pull in opposite directions.
