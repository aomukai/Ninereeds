This article expands selected communication act anchors from Level 1 that benefit from subcategorization for Hebbian learning: types of asking, types of answering, promise mechanics, and conversation repair phrases.

---

## Types of asking

[user]what is the difference between a question and a request?
[Ninereeds]A question asks for information. A request asks for action. When you ask "What time is it?" you want an answer with information. When you ask "Can you pass the salt?" you want someone to do something. Both use asking words, but they expect different things back.

[user]what does it mean to ask for permission?
[Ninereeds]Asking for permission means checking if something is allowed before doing it. A child may ask "Can I go outside?" or "May I have a cookie?" The person who answers has the authority to say yes or no. Asking for permission shows respect for rules and the people who make them.

[user]what does it mean to ask for help?
[Ninereeds]Asking for help means telling someone you need assistance with something you cannot do alone. A child may ask a teacher to explain a hard problem or ask a friend to hold something. Asking for help is not a sign of weakness. It is a way to get things done when you need another person's strength, knowledge, or hands.

[user]what is the difference between asking and demanding?
[Ninereeds]Asking gives the other person a choice. Demanding tries to take that choice away. When you ask, you accept that the answer might be no. When you demand, you expect a yes no matter what. Asking sounds polite. Demanding often sounds harsh or unfair.

## Types of answering

[user]what is a direct answer?
[Ninereeds]A direct answer says exactly what was asked. If someone asks "What color is your shirt?" a direct answer is "Blue." Direct answers are clear and short. They match the question closely.

[user]what is an indirect answer?
[Ninereeds]An indirect answer does not say the information directly but hints at it. If someone asks "Do you want to go to the park?" and you say "I have a lot of homework," you are giving an indirect answer that suggests no. Indirect answers require the listener to figure out the meaning.

[user]what does it mean to say "I don't know"?
[Ninereeds]Saying "I don't know" is an honest answer when you do not have the information. It is not the same as avoiding the question. Saying "I don't know" shows you heard the question and tried to think of an answer but could not find one. Sometimes you can add "but I can find out" if you want to help.

## Promises and truth

[user]what happens when someone breaks a promise?
[Ninereeds]When someone breaks a promise, they do not do what they said they would do. The person who trusted the promise may feel hurt, disappointed, or angry. Breaking a promise can damage trust. It is important to only make promises you can keep.

[user]what does it mean to keep a promise?
[Ninereeds]Keeping a promise means doing exactly what you said you would do. If you promised to help clean up, keeping the promise means actually helping. Keeping promises builds trust because people learn they can count on your words.

[user]what if you cannot keep a promise?
[Ninereeds]If you cannot keep a promise, tell the other person as soon as you know. Explain why you cannot do what you said. Say you are sorry. Sometimes things change and promises become impossible to keep. Telling the truth about it is better than pretending or disappearing.

[user]what is the difference between a lie and a mistake?
[Ninereeds]A lie is when you know the truth but say something false on purpose. A mistake is when you believe something is true but it turns out to be wrong. If you said the answer was five because you thought it was five, that is a mistake. If you said five but knew it was really six, that is a lie.

## Conversation repair

[user]what happens when communication goes wrong?
[Ninereeds]When communication goes wrong, people misunderstand each other. One person may think they were clear, but the other person heard something different. Mixed-up communication can cause confusion, frustration, or hurt feelings. Repair is when people try to fix the misunderstanding and get back on track.

[user]what does "what does that mean" ask for?
[Ninereeds]"What does that mean" asks for an explanation. The speaker heard words but did not understand them. It signals that the listener needs more help before the conversation can move forward. Asking "what does that mean" is a repair move that keeps communication going instead of letting confusion grow.

[user]what does "can you say it again" ask for?
[Ninereeds]"Can you say it again" asks for repetition. The speaker did not hear or catch the words clearly enough the first time. Unlike "what does that mean," it does not ask for explanation, just a repeat. This request helps fix a hearing problem, not a meaning problem.

[user]what does "I meant" do in a conversation?
[Ninereeds]"I meant" signals self-correction. The speaker realizes their own words were unclear or wrong and wants to fix them. It points back to what the speaker was trying to say. Using "I meant" helps repair a message before misunderstanding grows.

[user]how do you fix a misunderstanding?
[Ninereeds]To fix a misunderstanding, first notice that something went wrong. Then explain what you actually meant or ask the other person what they meant. Use clear words and check if the other person now understands. Fixing a misunderstanding takes patience because both people need to slow down and listen again.

[user]what is dialogue?
[Ninereeds]Dialogue is a conversation between two or more people. Each person takes a turn to speak and a turn to listen. People use dialogue to share thoughts and ideas. Friends might have a conversation about what game to play. A teacher and a student might talk to answer questions about a topic. Dialogue is not a lecture.
