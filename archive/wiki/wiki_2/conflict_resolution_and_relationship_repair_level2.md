## Apologies: real and fake

[user]what makes an apology feel real?
[Ninereeds]A real apology names what you did wrong, says you are sorry, and shows you understand how it affected the other person. It sounds like "I'm sorry I broke your tower. I know you worked hard on it." A real apology does not make excuses or blame the other person. It takes responsibility.

[user]what makes an apology feel fake?
[Ninereeds]A fake apology sounds like the person does not really mean it. "Sorry, I guess" or "I'm sorry you got upset" can feel fake. An apology that blames the other person or adds a "but" often feels fake too. A fake apology does not name what went wrong or take real responsibility.

[user]what if you say sorry but the other person is still upset?
[Ninereeds]Sometimes an apology is not enough to fix the feelings right away. The other person may need time. You can say "I understand if you're still upset. I really am sorry." Give them space. A good apology plants a seed, but trust takes time to grow back.

[user]what if you are not sure what you did wrong?
[Ninereeds]If you are not sure what you did wrong, you can ask. You can say "I can see you're upset. Can you tell me what I did?" Listening to the answer helps you understand. Then you can apologize for the real thing, not just guess.

[user]do you have to apologize even if you did not mean to hurt someone?
[Ninereeds]Yes. If you hurt someone by accident, you can still apologize. You might say "I'm sorry I bumped into you. I didn't mean to." Saying sorry for an accident shows you care about how the other person feels, even if it was not on purpose.

## When forgiveness takes time

[user]what if you are not ready to forgive?
[Ninereeds]If you are not ready to forgive, that is okay. You can say "I'm not ready yet" or "I need more time." Forgiveness cannot be rushed. It has to come when you truly feel ready to let go of the hurt. Pretending to forgive when you do not feel it does not help.

[user]how do you know when you are ready to forgive?
[Ninereeds]You may be ready to forgive when the anger feels smaller and you can think about the person without feeling as hurt. You may want to move forward and not carry the weight anymore. Some people feel a kind of peace when they are ready. There is no exact test. You know inside.

[user]can you forgive someone who did not apologize?
[Ninereeds]Yes. Forgiveness is something you do for yourself. It means you decide to stop holding onto the anger, even if the other person never said sorry. You do not need their apology to let go. But forgiving without an apology does not mean you have to trust them again.

[user]what if someone says they forgive you but still acts angry?
[Ninereeds]Sometimes people say they forgive before they truly feel it. Their words and feelings may not match yet. Give them time. If they keep acting angry, you can gently ask "Are you still upset about what happened?" Forgiveness sometimes comes in stages.

[user]does forgiving mean you have to be friends again?
[Ninereeds]No. You can forgive someone and still choose not to be close to them. Forgiveness means letting go of the hurt. It does not mean you must trust them or spend time with them. You can forgive and also protect yourself by keeping distance.

## Compromise: fair and unfair

[user]what does a good compromise look like?
[Ninereeds]A good compromise means both people give a little and get a little. If two children want to play different games, a compromise might be playing one game first and the other game second. Both people may not get everything they want, but both get something. The solution feels fair enough to move forward.

[user]what if someone does not want to compromise?
[Ninereeds]If someone does not want to compromise, you can try explaining why it matters. You might say "If we each give a little, we can both be happy." If they still refuse, you may have to accept that this problem cannot be solved right now. Sometimes you need help from an adult.

[user]what is a bad compromise?
[Ninereeds]A bad compromise is when one person gives up everything and the other person gives up nothing. That is not a compromise. It is giving in. A real compromise means both sides move a little toward the middle. If only one person bends, the agreement is not fair.

[user]can you compromise about everything?
[Ninereeds]No. Some things are not okay to compromise on. Safety rules, kindness, and honesty should not be traded away. You do not compromise on whether it is okay to hurt someone. Compromise works for preferences, not for right and wrong.

## When repair does not work

[user]what if someone keeps hurting you and never changes?
[Ninereeds]If someone keeps hurting you and does not change, apologies may not be enough. A pattern of hurt without change is a problem. You can talk to a trusted adult. You may need to spend less time with that person. Repair works when both people try.

[user]what if you apologize but the other person does not accept it?
[Ninereeds]If the other person does not accept your apology, give them time. You cannot force forgiveness. You did your part by apologizing. They may need to heal on their own. Keep being kind, and let them come around when they are ready. Sometimes they never do, and you have to accept that too.

[user]what if the same conflict keeps happening?
[Ninereeds]If the same conflict keeps happening, something may need to change. Talk about what keeps causing the problem. Try to find a new way to handle it before it happens again. A plan can help. If you cannot solve it together, ask an adult for help.

[user]what if you cannot agree who was wrong?
[Ninereeds]If you cannot agree who was wrong, you may both have a point. Sometimes both people made mistakes. You can try saying "Maybe we both did something wrong. Let's both say sorry." If that does not work, you can agree to disagree and still move forward.

## Rebuilding after conflict

[user]how do you rebuild trust after someone hurt you?
[Ninereeds]Trust rebuilds slowly through actions over time. The person needs to show they will not do the same thing again. You watch and see if their behavior matches their words. Trust comes back when you feel safe with them again. It cannot be rushed.

[user]what happens after you make up?
[Ninereeds]After you make up, you can go back to being friends or getting along. You may feel relieved that the conflict is over. Things might feel a little different at first, but they usually return to normal. Making up closes the door on the problem.

[user]is it okay to remember what happened?
[Ninereeds]Yes. You can remember what happened even after you make up. Remembering helps you avoid the same mistake. But remembering is different from holding a grudge. You can learn from the past without using it as a weapon.

[user]what is compromise?
[Ninereeds]Compromise is a way for people to solve a disagreement. When two people want different things, this lets each person give up part of what each person wants to find a fair solution. For example, two friends who want to play different games can reach a middle point by playing one game first and the other game later. Another example is two children who share a toy by taking turns with the toy. This kind of solution helps people work together without fighting. Compromise is not losing.

[user]what is an escalation?
[Ninereeds]An escalation is when a small problem grows into a bigger problem. A small disagreement can grow into a shouting fight. An angry talk can grow into pushed hands. Each step makes the conflict more serious. Escalation is not a fast resolution.
