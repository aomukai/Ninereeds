## The phone

[user]why do grown-ups have rules about using a phone?
[Ninereeds]Phones can take up a lot of time and attention. Grown-ups set rules to make sure children also play, read, talk to people, and get enough sleep. Too much phone use can make eyes tired and leave less time for other important activities. Rules help balance phone time with everything else.

[user]when is it not okay to use a phone?
[Ninereeds]It is not okay to use a phone during meals, when someone is talking to you, or when you need to pay attention to something else like crossing a street. Using a phone at these times can be rude or unsafe. Putting the phone down shows respect and keeps you aware of what is happening around you.

[user]what should a child do if they want to use a grown-up's phone?
[Ninereeds]The child should ask permission first. A phone belongs to the grown-up, and asking shows respect. The grown-up may say yes, say no, or say the child can use it for a short time. Using someone's phone without asking is not okay, even if the child has used it before.

[user]why should a child not answer a call from a number they do not know?
[Ninereeds]Strangers can call phones just like strangers can knock on doors. A child cannot tell who is calling if the number is unknown. It is safer to let a grown-up handle unknown calls. If the call is important, the person will leave a message or call back when a grown-up can answer.

[user]what should a child do if they drop a phone?
[Ninereeds]The child should pick it up carefully and check if it still works. If something looks broken or the screen is cracked, they should tell a grown-up right away. Accidents happen, and telling the truth is better than hiding a problem. Being honest helps the grown-up fix the phone or decide what to do next.

## The tablet

[user]how is using a tablet different from using a phone?
[Ninereeds]A tablet has a bigger screen, which makes it easier to read, draw, or watch videos. A tablet is often used at home more than on the go. Some tablets are shared by the whole family. The bigger screen also means it is harder to carry in a pocket, so tablets usually stay in one place.

[user]what are some good ways to use a tablet?
[Ninereeds]A tablet can be used for reading books, learning with educational apps, drawing pictures, or watching videos. Children can also use tablets for school assignments or video calls with family. A tablet is useful when the activity works better on a bigger screen than a phone.

[user]why might a grown-up set a time limit for tablet use?
[Ninereeds]Looking at a screen for too long can make eyes tired and leave less time for playing outside, reading paper books, or being with family. A time limit helps a child balance screen time with other activities. When the time is up, it is time to do something else.

[user]what should a child do when their tablet time is over?
[Ninereeds]The child should stop what they are doing, save any work if needed, and put the tablet away. It is normal to feel like they want more time, but following the rule is important. Finding another activity to do next helps make the change easier.

[user]why should a child ask before downloading a new app on a tablet?
[Ninereeds]Some apps cost money, some are not made for children, and some need a grown-up's permission. Downloading without asking could spend money the family did not plan to spend or bring something onto the tablet that is not appropriate. Asking first shows respect and keeps the tablet safe.

## The computer

[user]what can children use a computer for?
[Ninereeds]Children can use a computer for schoolwork, typing stories, playing educational games, watching videos, or learning new skills. A computer is helpful when the task involves writing, researching, or using programs that work best with a keyboard and mouse. Many schools give children work to do on a computer.

[user]why is it important to sit properly when using a computer?
[Ninereeds]Sitting properly helps the body feel comfortable and avoids aches in the back, neck, or eyes. Feet should be flat on the floor or on a footrest, the screen should be at eye level, and the arms should rest comfortably. Good posture makes it easier to use the computer for longer without feeling sore.

[user]what should a child do if something strange happens on a computer?
[Ninereeds]If a weird message pops up, the screen freezes, or something looks wrong, the child should stop and tell a grown-up. Clicking on things without understanding them can make problems worse. A grown-up can help figure out what happened and fix it safely.

[user]why should a child not share their password?
[Ninereeds]A password keeps a child's account safe. If someone else knows the password, they can get into the account and pretend to be that child or see private things. Keeping a password secret protects the child's work and information. The only people who might need to know are trusted grown-ups.

[user]how should a child take care of a computer?
[Ninereeds]A child should keep food and drinks away from the computer so nothing spills. Closing the computer gently and carrying it with two hands helps avoid damage. Keeping the area clean and not pressing too hard on the screen or keys makes the computer last longer. Taking care of things shows responsibility.

## Videos

[user]why do grown-ups care about what videos a child watches?
[Ninereeds]Some videos are made for grown-ups and have things that are too scary, confusing, or not right for children. Grown-ups want to make sure the videos a child watches are safe and good for them. Checking with a grown-up helps children find videos that are fun and also okay to watch.

[user]how can a child tell if a video is real or made up?
[Ninereeds]Some videos show things that really happened, like news or family recordings. Other videos are stories with actors, cartoons, or special effects that are not real. If a child is not sure, they can ask a grown-up. Learning to notice the difference helps children understand what they are watching.

[user]why should a child not watch videos all day?
[Ninereeds]Watching videos for too long takes time away from other things like playing, reading, moving around, and being with people. Eyes and minds need breaks from screens. Doing different activities during the day helps children learn more and feel better than watching videos all the time.

[user]what should a child do if a video makes them feel scared or upset?
[Ninereeds]The child should stop watching and tell a grown-up how they feel. It is okay to feel upset by something on a screen. A grown-up can help the child understand what they saw or choose something better to watch next time. Not everything on a screen is meant for children.

[user]why is it important to stop a video when asked?
[Ninereeds]When a grown-up says it is time to stop, there is usually a reason, like mealtime, bedtime, or something else the child needs to do. Stopping the video shows that the child respects the grown-up's decision. The video will still be there later if there is more time to watch.

## Apps

[user]what should a child do before using a new app?
[Ninereeds]The child should ask a grown-up if the app is okay to use. A grown-up can check if the app is made for children and if it is safe. Some apps have things inside them that cost money or ask for personal information. Asking first helps avoid surprises.

[user]why do some apps ask for a grown-up's permission?
[Ninereeds]Some features in apps are meant for older users or involve spending money. The permission step makes sure a grown-up knows what the child is doing and agrees to it. This protects the child and helps the family keep track of what happens on the device.

[user]what is an in-app purchase?
[Ninereeds]An in-app purchase is when someone buys something extra inside an app, like a new game level, special item, or more time. These purchases use real money. A child should never make an in-app purchase without a grown-up's permission because it spends the family's money.

[user]what should a child do if an app asks for their name or where they live?
[Ninereeds]The child should not type in that information and should tell a grown-up right away. Personal information like a real name, address, or school should stay private. Apps made for children usually do not need this information, and giving it out can be unsafe.

[user]why do apps sometimes stop working?
[Ninereeds]Apps can stop working if the device runs out of power, loses internet connection, or has a problem called a bug. Sometimes an app needs to be updated to a newer version. If an app is not working, the child can close it, wait, and try again, or ask a grown-up for help.
