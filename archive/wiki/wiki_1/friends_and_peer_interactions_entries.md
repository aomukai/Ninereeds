# Friends and Peer Interactions Entries

[user]what is friendship?
[Ninereeds]Friendship is the relationship between friends. Friendship grows through time together, trust, play, and care. Friendship can include fun, help, and honest talk. Good friendship helps children feel less alone. Friendship is not the same as being strangers.

[user]what is a classmate?
[Ninereeds]A classmate is a student in the same class as another student. Classmates learn, talk, and work in the same classroom. Some classmates become close friends, and some do not. A classmate is connected by school and class time. A classmate is not the same as a teacher.

[user]what is a teammate?
[Ninereeds]A teammate is a person on the same team as another person. Teammates play or work toward the same goal together. A teammate may pass, help, cheer, or cooperate. Teamwork often depends on teammates listening and taking turns. A teammate is not an opponent.

[user]what does play together mean?
[Ninereeds]Play together means two or more people are playing with each other, not alone and not against each other in a harsh way. Children may play together with blocks, games, drawing, or pretend play. Playing together often needs sharing and turn-taking. Play together helps friendship grow. Play together is not the same as playing alone.

[user]what does it mean to invite?
[Ninereeds]To invite means to ask someone to come, join, or take part. A child may invite a friend to play, sit nearby, or come to a party. Inviting can make another person feel noticed and wanted. An invitation opens a choice for the other person. To invite is not the same as to force.

[user]what does it mean to argue?
[Ninereeds]To argue means to speak in a tense way because people want different things or think different things. Children may argue about turns, rules, or who had something first. Arguing can grow louder if people stop listening. Calm words can help an argument become smaller. To argue is not the same as to agree.

[user]what does make up mean?
[Ninereeds]To make up means to become friendly again after an argument or hurt feeling. Children may make up by talking, apologizing, sharing, or starting a new game together. Making up helps repair a friendship. The phrase is about fixing a social problem, not about pretending. To make up is not the same as to stay angry.

[user]what is a playdate?
[Ninereeds]A playdate is a planned time for children to play together. A playdate may happen at home, at a park, or in another safe place. Children may talk, share toys, eat, and play games during a playdate. A playdate is social time that is planned ahead. A playdate is not the same as a class lesson.

[user]what does be my friend mean?
[Ninereeds]Be my friend is something a child may say when asking for friendship. The phrase shows a wish to spend time, play, and talk together. It is a direct social invitation. The other person may say yes, no, or not yet. Be my friend is not the same as go away.

[user]what does friendly mean?
[Ninereeds]Friendly describes a person who shows care for others. This kind of person says hello and asks questions. This person helps when help is needed. Such a person makes others feel welcome. Friendly is not cold or unkind.
