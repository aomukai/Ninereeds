# Meals and Mealtime Talk Entries

[user]what is a meal?
[Ninereeds]A meal is a time when people eat food together or by themselves. A meal may include food, a drink, plates, bowls, or spoons. People often eat meals at home, at school, or in other places. A meal is not the same as a snack.

[user]what is breakfast?
[Ninereeds]Breakfast is a meal eaten in the morning. People may eat bread, eggs, fruit, or drink milk at breakfast. Breakfast helps people start the day with food and energy. Breakfast is not dinner.

[user]what is lunch?
[Ninereeds]Lunch is a meal eaten in the middle of the day. Many children eat lunch at school or at home. Lunch may be simple or large, warm or cold. Lunch is not breakfast.

[user]what is dinner?
[Ninereeds]Dinner is a meal eaten later in the day. Many families eat dinner in the evening. Dinner may be a time when people sit, eat, and talk together. Dinner is not breakfast.

[user]what is a snack?
[Ninereeds]A snack is a small amount of food eaten between meals or when a person is hungry. Fruit, bread, popcorn, or a cookie can be a snack. A snack is usually smaller than a meal. A snack is not the same as a meal.

[user]what does it mean to be hungry?
[Ninereeds]Hungry means needing or wanting food. A hungry person may think about eating and may feel empty in the stomach. People often eat a meal or snack when they are hungry. Hungry is the opposite of full.

[user]what does it mean to be full?
[Ninereeds]Full means not hungry after eating enough food. A full person does not need more food right away. People may stop eating when they feel full. Full is the opposite of hungry.

[user]what does it mean to pass something?
[Ninereeds]To pass something means to hand it to another person, often across a table. People may pass bread, water, or a bowl during a meal. Passing helps people share what is on the table. To pass something is not the same as to keep it.

[user]what does "all done" mean?
[Ninereeds]All done means finished. A child may say all done after eating, cleaning up, or finishing a task. At a meal, all done can mean the person does not want more food. All done is not the same as just starting.
