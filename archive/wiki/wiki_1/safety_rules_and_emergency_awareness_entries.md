# Safety, Rules, and Emergency Awareness Entries

[user]what is safety?
[Ninereeds]Safety is the state of being protected from danger or harm. Safety matters at home, at school, on roads, and during play. People use rules, care, and good choices to stay safe. Safety helps children move and learn with less risk. Safety is not the same as danger.

[user]what is danger?
[Ninereeds]Danger is a situation that may cause harm. Fire, fast traffic, deep water, or a sharp tool may bring danger. Danger means a person needs care, distance, or help. People need to notice danger early when they can. Danger is the opposite of safety.

[user]what does careful mean?
[Ninereeds]Careful means acting in a safe and watchful way. A careful child may walk slowly on wet ground or hold scissors the right way. Being careful helps prevent accidents. Careful action often means looking, thinking, and moving with control. Careful is not the same as careless.

[user]what is an emergency?
[Ninereeds]An emergency is a serious problem that needs help right away. A fire, a bad injury, or a person who cannot breathe may be an emergency. Emergencies need fast action from adults or helpers. A child should not try to solve a big emergency alone. An emergency is not the same as an ordinary problem.

[user]what is a trusted adult?
[Ninereeds]A trusted adult is a grown person a child can go to for help and safety. A trusted adult may be a parent, teacher, grandparent, or another safe helper. Children can talk to a trusted adult when something feels wrong, unsafe, or confusing. Trusted adults help children during problems and emergencies. A trusted adult is not the same as a stranger.

[user]what does call for help mean?
[Ninereeds]Call for help means use words or a phone to get help from another person. A child may call for help when someone is hurt, lost, or in danger. Calling for help is important when a problem is too big to solve alone. The words should be clear and quick. Call for help is not the same as staying silent.

[user]what does look both ways mean?
[Ninereeds]Look both ways means check in both directions before crossing a road or path with traffic. Children look both ways to notice cars, bikes, or other movement. The action helps a person cross more safely. Looking both ways is a road safety rule. Look both ways is not the same as running without looking.

[user]what is a helmet?
[Ninereeds]A helmet is hard protective gear worn on the head. Children may wear a helmet while riding a bike, scooter, or skates. A helmet helps protect the head during a fall or crash. Helmets should fit the head in the right way. A helmet is not the same as a hat.

[user]what is a seatbelt?
[Ninereeds]A seatbelt is a safety strap worn in a vehicle seat. A seatbelt helps hold the body in place during sudden stops or crashes. Children and adults wear seatbelts to lower harm in a car. A seatbelt is part of safe travel. A seatbelt is not the same as a backpack strap.

[user]what does not safe mean?
[Ninereeds]Not safe means something may lead to harm or danger. A child may hear not safe about climbing too high, touching fire, or going near a road alone. The words tell a person to stop, step back, or get help. Not safe is a warning about risk. Not safe is the opposite of safe.

[user]what is a crime?
[Ninereeds]Crime is an act that breaks a rule made by a society. One example is taking a thing that belongs to another person. Another example is harming a person on purpose. A police officer helps find a person who does a wrong act. A judge gives punishment for such acts. Crime is not a game.

[user]what is prevention?
[Ninereeds]Prevention is the act of stopping harm before harm happens. washing hands with soap stops illness in this way. wearing a seatbelt in a car keeps bodies safe during an accident. prevention is not a cure.

[user]what is protection?
[Ninereeds]Protection is a way of keeping something safe from harm. A coat gives protection from cold. A helmet protects a head. A seatbelt keeps a person safe in a car. A fence keeps a garden safe. Protection is not danger.
