# Social-Emotional Learning Competencies Entries

[user]what is self-management?
[Ninereeds]Self-management is the skill of handling actions, feelings, and choices in a steady way. A person uses self-management to stay calm, follow a plan, or keep trying. Self-management helps in school, play, and daily life. Self-management is not acting without thinking.

[user]what is social awareness?
[Ninereeds]Social awareness is noticing other people, other feelings, and what is happening around you. A socially aware person pays attention to words, faces, and situations. Social awareness helps people act with care and respect. Social awareness is not self-management.

[user]what is empathy?
[Ninereeds]Empathy is understanding and caring about how another person feels. Empathy can help a person be gentle, helpful, and fair. People often show empathy by listening and responding kindly. Empathy is not the same as agreement.

[user]what is self-regulation?
[Ninereeds]Self-regulation is controlling the body, feelings, and actions in a useful way. A person may pause, breathe, wait, or choose calmer words. Self-regulation helps with learning and relationships. Self-regulation is not losing control.

[user]what is responsible decision-making?
[Ninereeds]Responsible decision-making means choosing in a careful and thoughtful way. A responsible choice looks at safety, fairness, and what may happen next. People use this skill when rules, feelings, and goals all matter. Responsible decision-making is not guessing.

[user]what is impulse control?
[Ninereeds]Impulse control is stopping before doing the first thing that pops into the mind. Impulse control helps a person wait, think, and choose a better action. This skill matters when a person feels upset, excited, or tempted. Impulse control is not acting right away.

[user]what does how would you feel if mean?
[Ninereeds]How would you feel if is a question that asks a person to imagine an emotion in a situation. Adults and children use this question to build empathy and reflection. The question helps a person think about effects on others. How would you feel if is not a command.

[user]what does that was a good choice mean?
[Ninereeds]That was a good choice means an action was thoughtful, safe, or kind. People say this to notice good judgment and encourage more of it. The words point to a decision and its value. That was a good choice is not criticism.

[user]what is guidance?
[Ninereeds]Guidance is help and direction given to someone. A teacher gives guidance to a child learning a new task. This help shows a person the correct way forward. The teacher offers clear steps and simple explanations. The help lets a child make good choices. Guidance is not force.

[user]what is a loser?
[Ninereeds]A loser is a person or a team that does not win a game or a competition. A runner finishing last in a race is one example. A team with the lowest score in a sport is another example. The word describes a specific result of a game. Loser is not a winner.

[user]what is self-control?
[Ninereeds]Self-control is the ability to stop and think before acting. a child who feels angry may want to shout but instead takes a deep breath and stays calm. this skill helps a person wait for a turn with a toy. practicing self-control makes a child stronger at making good choices. self-control is not acting on every urge.

[user]what is trouble?
[Ninereeds]Trouble means a situation that happens when something goes wrong or breaks. A child who loses a favorite toy feels trouble. A glass cup that drops and breaks on the floor causes a problem. People can work together to fix the problem. trouble is not play.
